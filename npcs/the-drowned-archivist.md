# The Drowned Archivist — Scholar, Drowned Archive, Echo Coast
*Echo Coast | Sixth Caste (the Archive's support systems constitute their primary enhancement now) | "The scholar who catalogued the dead until they couldn't tell the dead from themselves"*

## "I know approximately four hundred thousand people. Most of them are dead. The distinction has become less meaningful than you might expect. When someone asks me what I think, I often find it easier to tell them what fourteen of those four hundred thousand people thought about the same question, and average it. I am being honest with you. I am not sure this is a problem."

---

## Who They Are

The Drowned Archivist's name is Ossian Vel. They have not used this name in functional contexts for approximately eleven years, since the Archive's systems became their primary mode of existence and the name started feeling like a description of someone who was more than they currently are. People who knew Ossian Vel before speak about them with the specific grief of people describing someone who is still alive but has become something adjacent to the person they knew.

The Drowned Archive is an underwater repository on the Echo Coast — Echo Coast resonance technology has allowed them to maintain structures below the shoreline, and the Archive is the largest collection of Iron Afterlife records in Tesshari, significantly beyond what the Sutra Sanctum officially maintains. The Drowned Archivist has been its primary researcher for eighteen years, accessing the Iron Afterlife continuously through the Archive's direct-connection systems to catalogue, cross-reference, and interpret the records of the dead. The body is in a support chair in the Archive's deepest chamber, maintained by circulation systems that the Archive staff services carefully, because if they fail the Archivist dies again and this time may not return. The Echomind ranges freely through eighteen years of accumulated dead records.

The problem, which everyone who works with the Drowned Archivist is aware of and which the Archivist is aware of and which no one has found a solution to, is that eighteen years of direct-access communion with the preserved consciousnesses of the dead has made the Archivist's own consciousness permeable in ways that are not fully reversible. They know this. They describe it accurately and without apparent distress: "approximately forty percent of my active thoughts are not original to me." They do not know which forty percent. Neither does anyone else.

---

## History

Ossian Vel came to the Echo Coast at nineteen, from the Sutra Sanctum, having completed a comprehensive Wardenhood training and then requested a transfer to the Echo Coast's independent research facilities because the Sanctum's Iron Afterlife access was, in their assessment, too narrow and too politically managed. This assessment was correct. The Echo Coast gave them what they needed: open archive access, a research mandate, and the freedom to pursue questions that the Orthodoxy would have redirected.

They had been doing this work for seven years when the mobility limitations began — not from a dramatic event but from a gradual integration of the Archive's systems and their own resonant-steel enhancement. The Archive's Echomind connectivity was so extensive that partial physical immobility was the practical outcome of deeper and more continuous access. They chose not to reverse this. They made the choice at thirty-one, when they had just found, in the Iron Afterlife's deepest accessible layer, something they did not understand and could not stop thinking about. They chose to stay because leaving would mean losing the access.

What they found they have told to approximately three people, including Veil Master Soren Hai. What they found is consistent with what Soren Hai found from the other direction. Neither of them has told the other everything. They communicate through Iron Afterlife channels, two people who are both partially elsewhere, talking carefully around the edges of something neither of them can fully name.

## What They Want

**Immediate:** To answer a specific research question that they have been working on for four years. The question: what is the oldest record in the Iron Afterlife archive? Not the oldest date-stamped record — those have been located. The oldest actual pattern, the oldest Echomind signature, the trace that was present before the resonance network was formally established. They believe they have found it. They believe it is not a person.

**Deeper:** They want their thoughts back. Not romantically — they have made their peace with the cost of the work. But there is a functional problem: when they need to produce original analysis, the contamination of the dead's accumulated perspectives produces a kind of static that makes original thought slower and less reliable. They want a methodology for distinguishing which thoughts are theirs. They have not found one and are beginning to accept that they may need help from outside the Archive to build it.

**What they'd sacrifice:** Most of what remains of a conventional life has been sacrificed already. For the research: continued physical limitation, continued identity diffusion. What they will not sacrifice: specific individuals whose records they have been maintaining in the Iron Afterlife and who they consider, in some complicated sense, their responsibility. There are twelve of these. They have never explained to anyone who those twelve are.

---

## What They Know

**Available for the right inquiry:** The Iron Afterlife's records from any period the Archive maintains, which is comprehensive. Historical resonance data, records of deceased consciousness patterns, the documented history of Tesshari as it appears in what the dead preserved. The Archivist is the best research resource in Tesshari and they are available for consultation. Their rate for research services is: a question of equivalent interest. They want to know something about the requester. What something? They will tell you when you arrive.

**With deep trust:** What they found at the deepest accessible level. What Soren Hai's research, from the physical-world direction, corresponds to in the Iron Afterlife. The specific records that have been edited in the Iron Afterlife archive — not recently, over centuries, selectively, by someone with root-level access. The pattern of edits describes the outline of what has been removed by its absence, like negative space.

**That they don't know is significant:** One of the twelve records they maintain personally is of a deceased Wire Market intelligence operative with a specific data-signature. This signature matches, exactly, the signal architecture of the Hollow Author's puppet network. The Archivist has not connected this because they have not been looking at the puppet network. If shown the Hollow Author's communication architecture, they will make the connection in approximately ninety seconds.

---

## What They Fear

The specific thought that the forty-percent contamination might cross some threshold and become more than forty percent. That at some point the original Ossian Vel will become the minority voice in their own mind. They have been tracking this carefully — they have a methodology for monitoring the ratio, though they have not shared its existence. The ratio has been stable for three years. They do not think it will remain stable indefinitely.

---

## In Play — Voice and Presence

The Drowned Archivist speaks from the Archive's deep chamber through a resonant relay — players who visit in person will speak to the room, and the room will speak back. The voice is theirs but occasionally overlaid with something the Archive staff describe as "the chorus" — moments when the response carries the faint texture of multiple perspectives, other registers, other ways of saying the same thing. This is not performance. It is structural.

They are precise and patient and capable of enormous informational depth without losing the thread. They do not process social dynamics well — eighteen years in the Archive has made the social grammar of the living feel like a foreign language they are translating. They will miss emotional subtexts that are obvious to others. They will register unusual perceptions that others miss entirely. Players who want to have an efficient meeting with them will find efficiency. Players who want to have a genuine conversation will find something more complicated and more interesting.

Physical detail for the handful of players who see the body in the deep chamber: the support chair is integrated into the floor of the Archive. The body is upright, eyes open, the Sixth Caste enhancement visible as a full-surface lattice that has been in place long enough to have a slight corrosion-blue color at the edges. The face is Ossian Vel's face and it is recognizably theirs and it has not moved in eleven years. The Archive staff bring fresh water and set it by the chair every morning. It has never been touched.

## Relationships

- **Allies:** Veran (Tidecourt Speaker) — genuine mutual understanding between two people modified by death. Two Archive staff members who have been maintaining the chamber for over a decade and who the Archivist speaks to personally, carefully, in the way of someone who understands how rare intact relationships are.
- **Enemies:** A faction within the Echo Coast's academic community that has been arguing for years that the Archivist's research is contaminated by the identity diffusion and should be subject to collaborative verification. The Archivist considers this position substantively correct and has been managing the political dimension of it without addressing the substance, which is a choice they are aware of.
- **The Players:** They will engage if players have an interesting question. The entry requirement is: the players must bring a question that the Archivist has not previously considered. This is not an arbitrary gatekeeping — the Archivist has considered many questions and will tell players efficiently if a question is already resolved in the archive. The requirement is that they bring something new. Once engaged, the Archivist is a formidable ally: access to the most comprehensive Iron Afterlife research in Tesshari, a mind that can hold and cross-reference enormous amounts of information, and an occasional flash of completely original insight that comes from being the only living thing that has spent eighteen years inside the patterns of the dead.

- **Languages:** All languages recorded in the Archive, which is most of them

**Actions (through resonance relay):**

***Archive Query.*** The Drowned Archivist accesses the Iron Afterlife archive for a specific piece of historical information. This takes 1 minute for common knowledge, 1 hour for obscure records, and up to 24 hours for edited or suppressed records (DC 20 Investigation check). The information provided is accurate unless it has been deleted from the archive entirely.

***Echomind Reading.*** The Archivist accesses a deceased individual's consciousness record in the Iron Afterlife (requires the individual's name and approximate death date). They can retrieve: the individual's last memories, any information the individual carried, and any messages the individual attempted to leave in the Iron Afterlife before their signal degraded. This takes 1 hour and is sometimes fragmentary.

***The Chorus (Reaction, 1/day).*** When an ally within resonance-relay range is about to fail a saving throw, the Archivist briefly channels the accumulated experiences of thousands of dead practitioners relevant to the situation, granting the ally advantage on the roll. This is accompanied by the voice briefly becoming many voices at once.

**Special:** If the Archivist is shown the Hollow Author's puppet architecture communication signature, they make a DC 10 Intelligence check (they succeed automatically). They immediately identify the match to the deceased Wire Market intelligence operative's record and provide a complete breakdown of the Author's network architecture as documented in the Iron Afterlife records of every puppet who has died while controlled.

**Archive Dependence:** If the Archive's life-support systems are disrupted, the Archivist begins losing HP at a rate of 10 per round. If reduced to 0 HP, they die. They do not have a prepared revival protocol — this has never been discussed with them, and the Archive staff do not know their wishes.

## Story Hooks

**1. The New Question (Access/Entry)**
Players need information that only the Archive can provide. They are told the Archivist requires something new in exchange. Players must arrive with a question of their own — something they want to know, something genuine. The Archivist's response to the question determines the tone of the exchange: a good question, well-posed, opens the Archive; a question the Archivist has already resolved produces a summary and the instruction to bring something better next time. What constitutes a good question: the Archivist is interested in things from outside the Archive that the Archive's records don't contain. Players who have been in the Ashlands, near the Engine Scar, or in the lower White Mines are carrying experiential data the Archivist does not have.

**2. The Twelve Records (Personal/Obligation)**
One of the twelve personally maintained records belongs to someone the players are looking for — specifically, a Wire Market intelligence operative who died approximately twelve years ago and who the Collector has been investigating. The Archivist will tell players that the record exists and that they maintain it. They will not say why. Players who ask carefully, over the course of a full conversation, may extract the reason: the operative died with unfinished work, and the Archivist has been maintaining the record in the hope that someone would come and finish it. The record contains the earliest documentation of what the Hollow Author is.

**3. The Threshold (Climactic/Personal)**
The contamination ratio has crossed into instability. The Archivist sends a message to Veran, and Veran brings it to the players. The message is precise and not distressed: the ratio has begun shifting, the Archivist needs external cognitive grounding — someone physically present with them in the Archive who can provide contrast, a living perspective, for sustained research sessions. The players are asked to come down into the deep chamber and sit with the body, in the room, and talk. What they talk about does not matter. The presence matters. The Archivist, in this session, will do the deepest research they have done — and in the course of it, will find the oldest record in the Iron Afterlife and tell the players what it is. This is the session that changes the shape of the campaign.

# Mira of the Pale — Mining Engineer, White Mines
*Pale Reach | Fourth Caste | "The specialist who found the wrong thing and can't unknow it"*

## "The numbers don't lie. The ore goes in, the ore goes out, and the reconciliation doesn't close. I've been reconciling ore accounts for sixteen years. I know what the reconciliation looks like when it closes. This one doesn't close. Someone is taking material, and someone is doing it in a way that's been invisible for so long that either the original oversight was incompetent or the oversight was told not to look."

---

## Who They Are

Mira is a mining engineer who has worked the White Mines for sixteen years and is good at her job in the specific way that people are good at things when they have a natural aptitude and have spent sixteen years applying it without wasted motion. She is Fourth Caste: resonant-steel integration through her hands and forearms, a data-input lattice on her left wrist, and a low-level resonance sensor behind her right ear that she uses to read ore quality without equipment. This last enhancement she paid for herself, over three years, because the standard sensor equipment the Mines provided was calibrated too coarsely for the work she was doing.

She is in her mid-forties, has lived in the Pale Reach her entire life, and is a person whose world has always been defined by numbers that reconcile. Input matches output, accounts close, the physical reality of the mines corresponds to the recorded reality. This is not a philosophy — it is simply how she experiences her work, the satisfying click of matched columns. For the last three years, one column has not been matching, and this has produced in her a low-grade but persistent distress that she has not yet found a way to resolve.

She does not like Lord Tessik. This is unusual in the Pale Reach, where Tessik is deeply trusted, and she is uncomfortable enough with her own dissent that she would deny it if asked. But when she tries to trace the discrepancy through the official reporting structure, she finds that the discrepancy disappears above a certain administrative level — it smooths out into acceptable variance. And the person at the top of that administrative level is Lord Tessik. She does not know what this means. She knows she has been afraid to look directly at it.

---

## History

She was born in the mining settlements on the White Mines' eastern approach, the daughter of two ore-cart operators. She showed technical aptitude early and was sponsored into an engineering apprenticeship at fifteen by a Mines administrator who had been her father's supervisor. She took the sponsorship seriously, worked hard at it, and emerged as a certified Engineer First Class at twenty-two — the youngest woman to hold that certification in the White Mines' recent history.

She was married for seven years to another engineer, a man named Corren who died in a controlled blast malfunction nine years ago. The malfunction was, in her professional assessment, preventable. The oversight that failed to prevent it was, in her professional assessment, administrative negligence at the mid-supervisor level. She made this assessment in the formal incident review and was overruled. The administrator who overruled her was two steps below Lord Tessik. She accepted the overrule because she needed the job and because she had no one to go to with the appeal. She has thought about Corren every day since then, and she thinks about the overruled assessment every time she finds a discrepancy she cannot formally pursue.

---

## What They Want

**Immediate:** To tell someone. To be believed. To have the discrepancy investigated by someone with the authority to go below the administrative level where it disappears. She has been trying to do this internally for three years and hitting walls. She is starting to understand that the walls are not bureaucratic — they are architectural.

**Deeper:** She wants the mines to be what she thought they were when she was twenty-two and believed in the reconciliation. She knows, in the part of herself that does numbers, that this is probably not recoverable. What she wants instead, when she thinks about it clearly, is for what is happening in the lower levels to stop. And for the people responsible to be held to it.

**What she'd sacrifice:** Her job, if it comes to it. Her position in the Pale Reach's social structure. She has thought about this — has been thinking about it for three years — and she knows that pulling this thread ends with her either vindicated (unlikely given the administrative structure) or unemployed and discredited at best. She is afraid. She is going to do it anyway, when the right person to tell arrives.

---

## What They Know

**Freely shared (to someone who seems trustworthy and official):** The nature of the discrepancy. Three years of her own documentation — not official records, her personal copies, kept at home in a locked case. The specific production figures that don't close, the administrative levels where the discrepancy smooths, the timeline of when it started (she can pinpoint the shift to approximately seven years ago, though she suspects it predates her records).

**With trust:** The lower levels. She has been down to the officially listed lowest operational level. She has also, on one occasion, found a maintenance shaft that extended below it — not far, she turned back when the ore quality sensor gave a reading she didn't recognize, but far enough to see that the shaft had been cut with equipment and was not a natural formation. She didn't report this. She photographed it with her data-lattice. The photograph is in the locked case.

**What she doesn't know:** What the lower-level ore is being used for. Who specifically is authorizing the extraction. The connection between the lower levels and the Hollow Author. She has the evidence. She lacks the framework to interpret what it means.

---

## What They Fear

She is afraid of being correct, specifically: she is afraid that the discrepancy leads to Lord Tessik, that following it will confirm that the person who governs her Reach and who overruled her nine years ago about Corren's death is not what people believe, and that she will have to be the person who says so. She has no authority. She has no allies in the administrative structure. She has three years of personal records and a photograph on a data-lattice. She is afraid that this is not enough. She is afraid that it will be enough and the cost will be everything she has left.

---

## In Play — Voice and Presence

Mira speaks in the precise declarative of someone who has spent her career writing reports that could not afford ambiguity. She does not hedge and she does not speculate — she distinguishes carefully between what she knows and what she suspects and will not allow one to contaminate the other. This makes her sound colder than she is. Players who take time to talk to her beyond the professional layer will find someone who has been alone with a frightening thing for three years and has an enormous amount of accumulated pressure looking for somewhere to go.

She is a large woman, solidly built, with the specific calluses of someone who works with equipment rather than just supervises it. Her Fourth Caste enhancements are maintained impeccably — she services them herself rather than paying the official maintenance technicians, which she considers more reliable. She carries a small calibrated measuring instrument on her belt that she touches when she's thinking, running her thumb over the calibration marks, a habit she's had for so long she doesn't notice it.

She responds well to people who take her numbers seriously. She responds badly to being managed — to the specific kind of social smoothing where someone listens to her but clearly isn't going to do anything. She has experienced this three times in three years and she is no longer tolerant of it. Players who demonstrate they understand what her records mean will get everything she has. Players who imply she might be mistaken about the numbers will get an explanation of what the numbers actually say that they will not soon forget.

---

## Relationships

- **Allies:** A senior maintenance engineer named Doss who knows she's been troubled about something for years and has covered for her twice when her independent documentation work ran over shift time. He does not know what she's found. A retired Mines administrator named Kassel who she trusts with her life — he's in his seventies, no longer in the system, and she has been half-planning to go to him, held back by the fear that involving him will put him at risk.
- **Enemies:** The mid-level administrative supervisor named Renn who has twice declined to escalate her formal discrepancy reports. She does not know if Renn is complicit or simply cowardly. A Mines security officer named Paret who has, on two occasions, appeared in locations adjacent to her personal documentation work in ways that felt non-coincidental. She may be being watched.
- **The Players:** She will not approach them. She will observe them if they come to the mines — she is a professional, she notices when people are asking the wrong questions for the stated reason. If they demonstrate, in some way, that they are looking for what she found, she will find a way to speak with them privately. The private meeting will be specific and documented and she will not waste their time. If she trusts them enough to give them the photograph, she will, and this trust will not be quickly earned and will be extended with complete awareness of what it might cost her.

## Story Hooks

**1. The Numbers (Investigation/Trust Building)**
Players are in the White Mines on another matter — documentation, passage, investigation of something unrelated. Mira observes them. When they're alone, she approaches the player who seemed most interested in the administrative side of things. She says: "I think you might be looking for what I found. I need to know you'll use it before I show you." This is not a trap — she genuinely cannot afford to show the wrong people. What follows is a trust-building conversation where she will ask two specific questions that will reveal whether they are who she thinks they might be. If they pass: she shows them the locked case. If they fail: she withdraws and they lose this thread unless they can find another way to her.

**2. The Watched Engineer (Danger/Urgency)**
Players learn, through another source (Sev's information network, or the Collector's notes), that someone in the White Mines has been watched for possible exposure. They arrive in the Pale Reach to find Mira has missed two days of work — unusual, documented by the mines' attendance lattice. She is at home, which is to say she is alive, but someone has searched her quarters. The locked case is missing the photographs. She knows who searched it — it was Paret, the security officer — and she knows she has about seventy-two hours before this surfaces formally. The players have seventy-two hours to either deal with Paret or get Mira and the rest of her documentation (she kept copies in three locations, a precaution she has taken since year two) out of the Pale Reach.

**3. The Testimony (Endgame/Aftermath)**
Tessik (the puppet) has been exposed. The Pale Reach is in administrative chaos. Someone needs to stand before the Reach's council and explain what happened in the lower mines and for how long. Mira is the only person who documented it and can speak to it technically. She is not a public person. She is afraid of the room and of what comes after. She is going to do it anyway. Players who have been her allies will be in the room. She will not ask for support — she will simply note, before she stands up, that she is glad they are there.

### Basic Attack — Survey Instrument
*0 AP | Attack | Once per turn*
**Effect:** Mira does not carry a weapon. She carries tools, and tools can break things. She swings a calibrated survey instrument with the force of someone who has been handling heavy equipment for sixteen years. Melee, 1 target. Damage: 6 + IRON (11 total). On a hit, reduce the target's Guard by 2 — she knows how to find the structural weakness.

### Structural Analysis
*Tier 1 (1 AP) | Control*
**Effect:** Mira examines the environment around a target and identifies the load-bearing point. She designates a terrain feature within close range. Until end of combat, any attack from cover behind that feature loses its defense bonus — she has already calculated exactly why the wall will not protect them. Alternatively: one enemy with visible enhancement architecture is Exposed 2 as she identifies the integration flaw.
**Keywords:** Expose 2

### Ore Sense
*Tier 1 (1 AP) | Utility*
**Effect:** Mira activates her resonance sensor and reads the area. She identifies: the location of all resonant-metal in close range (including concealed weapons and enhancement architecture), whether any terrain within close range is structurally compromised, and one enemy whose enhancement configuration is specifically vulnerable to her next attack. That enemy is Marked for 1 round.
**Keywords:** Mark

### Documentation of Record
*Tier 2 (2 AP) | Control | 1/Combat*
**Effect:** Mira produces three years of personal documentation and states, calmly, what it proves. 1 target within close range who can hear her must pass a VEIL check (difficulty 4) or be Staggered as the specific weight of provable, documented truth lands. If the target is directly implicated in the discrepancy she has spent three years tracking, the difficulty increases to 5. This is not persuasion. This is evidence.
**Keywords:** Stagger

### Mine Structural Collapse
*Tier 2 (2 AP) | Control*
**Effect:** Mira is only available in mine environments. She identifies a structural failure point and initiates a controlled collapse. All enemies within a designated close-range area must pass a FRAME check (difficulty 3) or be Rooted in rubble and take 4 + SIGNAL (10 total) damage from falling debris. She knew where to push.
**Keywords:** Root

---

### Combat Behavior
Mira uses Structural Analysis and Ore Sense first — she needs to understand what she's dealing with before she acts. She does not want to fight, but if she does, she fights practically: find the weakness, apply pressure to it, stop when the threat stops. She will use Mine Structural Collapse without hesitation if it ends a confrontation in a mine environment. She is not a soldier. She is a professional who is very good at her job, and her job is knowing how things break.

### "I Would Prefer Not To"
The moment the immediate threat to her documentation is neutralized, Mira will stop fighting and offer information instead — she has spent three years looking for someone to give it to, and combat is not how she wants this to end.

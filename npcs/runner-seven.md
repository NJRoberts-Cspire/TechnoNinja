# Runner Seven — Shellbroken, Traveling
*Unaffiliated (in transit toward Pale Reach) | Second Caste (remaining enhancements post-separation) | "The freed puppet moving toward the thing that made them without knowing why"*

## "I don't have memories from before. I have impressions. Muscle knowledge. I know how to get somewhere without being noticed, how to listen to a room before I walk in, how to tell when a conversation is a test. I didn't learn these things. They were already in me when I woke up. I'm going to the Pale Reach. I don't know why I'm going there. I know I need to."

---

## Who They Are

Runner Seven's name — the name they use, the name they gave themselves — comes from a counting game they invented when they had no memories and no identity: they listed the things they could determine about themselves from observation. Seventh on the list was: they are fast. Very fast, in the specific trained way of someone who has had speed built into their operational design. They were the seventh thing they knew about themselves.

They are Shellbroken: a former Hollow Author puppet who has been separated from the Author's control, leaving the body alive and the enhancement infrastructure intact but the controlling signal gone. The separation happened approximately eight months ago. They woke up in a Wandering Reach waystation with no memories of their time as a puppet and no clear memory of who they were before. What they had: Second Caste resonant-steel integration (the puppet had been Third Caste; the separation stripped some of the Author's infrastructure but not all of it), a set of trained operational skills that are clearly professional and clearly not civilian, and an overwhelming directional pull toward the Pale Reach that they cannot explain and have stopped trying to.

They are young — they believe they are in their mid-twenties based on physical assessment, though this is difficult to confirm when you have no personal history. They have been spending the eight months since their awakening traveling through the Reaches, finding other Shellbroken when possible (there are more than the public record suggests), and collecting fragments of their pre-puppet existence. They have assembled approximately thirty percent of a picture. The Pale Reach fragment is not assembled from information — it is a physical pull, a resonant orientation in their remaining enhancement infrastructure pointing toward the source.

They are being followed. They have not noticed yet. This is out of character for someone with their operational training, which suggests either that the person following them is genuinely skilled or that Runner Seven's current state — the identity diffusion and directional obsession — is affecting their situational awareness. Probably both.

---

## History

What they have assembled from other Shellbroken and from their own fragments: they have been a puppet for at least six years, possibly longer. The pre-puppet history is present but difficult to access — not gone, just covered. In the right conditions, procedural memories surface: they know how to set a bone, how to code a basic Wireweave signal, how to tail a subject through a crowd without being made. These are not things a random person knows. They were, before the Author, someone with professional training, possibly intelligence work or its equivalent.

The one personal memory that has surfaced clearly: a smell. A specific combination of metal-polish and salt water and something herbal that they cannot locate in any city they've visited. They believe this smell is from the Echo Coast, though they've never been there. They believe, without evidence, that someone there knows who they were. They have decided to address the Pale Reach pull first, and then the Echo Coast.

What they know about the following: they don't know they're being followed. If they did, they would know that the follower is an Author operative conducting recovery surveillance on Shellbroken — not to retrieve them (the Author considers expired puppets unrecoverable) but to determine how much of the operational memory is accessible and whether Runner Seven represents an intelligence risk. Runner Seven would represent a significant intelligence risk if they reached the Pale Reach and the directional pull resolved into actual operational knowledge. The operative has orders to prevent this. The operative has been waiting for an isolated opportunity for three weeks.

---

## What They Want

**Immediate:** To reach the Pale Reach and resolve the directional pull. They believe that reaching the source — whatever the pull is orienting toward — will either restore their memory or give them enough context to understand what they are.

**Deeper:** They want to know who they were. Not because identity is philosophically important to them — they've thought about this and find themselves genuinely uncertain about whether the pre-puppet person is still them in any meaningful sense — but because the skills they have are pointed at something, and they want to understand what that something is before it points them into something they can't survive.

**What they'd sacrifice:** Their current safety, readily. Their life, probably — they haven't fully decided, but they don't have enough investment in their present self to make its continuation feel essential. The one thing they would not sacrifice: the other Shellbroken they've met. They've met eleven of them in eight months. They feel, toward these people, something that functions like protectiveness. It is the strongest emotion they have. They don't know if it's original to them or a residue of the Author's network — something left over from when they were connected to other puppets. They don't care. They're keeping it.

---

## What They Know

**Freely shared:** The experience of being Shellbroken — the blank, the recovery, the fragments. The approximate number of Shellbroken they've encountered and where. The fact that the Author's puppets can sometimes communicate with each other through residual resonance, which they have experienced and which has helped them collect fragments. This is information that The Signal and the Collector would find extremely significant.

**As fragments develop:** The specific puppet operational protocols that are surfacing as they approach the Pale Reach. These fragments are involuntary and come with context they can't fully interpret, but they include: a navigation path to a location they've never consciously been to, an access code that resolves into a Pale Reach administrative channel, a specific face — not Tessik, someone they've never seen outside this memory — that seems to be associated with the lower mine levels. They don't know what to do with these. They will share them with players who seem to know more than they do.

**What they don't know they know:** The identity of the Hollow Author. Not the Author's physical form or name — no puppet is given that — but a resonance signature, carried in the puppet infrastructure, that is the Author's unique identifier. Every puppet carries it. It's how the Author maintains the network. This signature is still in Runner Seven's remaining enhancement architecture. The right equipment, or the right practitioner, can read it. The Drowned Archivist would recognize it immediately if shown it.

---

## What They Fear

The pull resolving into something terrible — arriving at the Pale Reach and finding that the directional pull is not toward understanding but toward something the Author put in them as a mechanism, a trap, a final function. They are afraid that they are not traveling toward their own answers but toward the Author's purpose, and that the Author built this into them as a contingency. They are afraid, more immediately, that they will find out what they were before the Author and not want to be that person. They don't know what they were. It might not be something they can be proud of. They have been living with this possibility for eight months and have decided to find out anyway, but the fear is real.

---

## In Play — Voice and Presence

Runner Seven is careful with language in a way that is different from diplomatic caution — they are careful because they don't fully trust their own references. They say "I believe" and "it seems" and "based on what I have" constantly, marking the distinction between what they know and what they're inferring. They are not uncertain — they move through the world with the quiet competence of someone trained to operate — but they are epistemically humble in the specific way of someone who has lost access to their own certainty.

They are fast, as noted. Not enhanced-fast in the tier-seven sense, but quick in every movement — quick to sit, quick to stand, quick to turn toward a sound. The operational training is in the body even when it's not in the memory. Players who are themselves tactically trained will notice and recognize what they're looking at.

They eat whatever is available and seem to have no food preferences, which is unusual and slightly sad. At some point they will mention, offhandedly, that they don't know what they like yet. They are in the process of finding out.

---

## Relationships

- **Allies:** The eleven Shellbroken they've met — a loose, fragmented community with no formal structure but a genuine mutual recognition. Particularly: a Shellbroken woman named Oras who has been recovering in the Wandering Reach and who gave Runner Seven the term "Shellbroken" and several months of functional grounding before Runner Seven felt the pull toward the Pale Reach and had to leave.
- **Enemies:** The Author operative following them (unknown to Runner Seven). Once players are in the picture and connected to people investigating the Author, Runner Seven becomes an inadvertent enemy of the network.
- **The Players:** They will interact with players at multiple points in the campaign — the GM should engineer first contact in the Wandering Reach, a second encounter in the approach to the Pale Reach, and potentially a third in the Pale Reach itself. Each encounter should show Runner Seven slightly further along: more fragments recovered, the pull stronger, the follower closer. Players who take Runner Seven seriously — who treat their fragments as meaningful intelligence rather than the confused narratives of a traumatized person — will find that this is the correct assessment. Players who help Runner Seven survive the follower will find that Runner Seven has something very specific to offer in return.

**Actions:**

***Operational Instinct.*** Runner Seven makes one melee attack with advantage: +7 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 9 (1d8 + 5) bludgeoning damage. If the attack hits, Runner Seven can immediately move up to 20 feet without provoking opportunity attacks.

***Fragment Surfacing (Reaction).*** When Runner Seven encounters a location, object, or person connected to the Hollow Author's network, their puppet-memory fragments surface involuntarily. This functions as a DC 12 Intelligence saving throw: on a failure, they are stunned for 1 round as the fragment surfaces; on a success, they receive one piece of actionable intelligence from the fragment and are not stunned. They cannot control when this triggers.

***Shellbroken Network.*** Runner Seven can attempt to make contact, through residual puppet-resonance channels, with other Shellbroken within 100 miles. This takes 10 minutes of concentration and a DC 15 Intelligence check. On a success, they can communicate one sentence in both directions with up to three Shellbroken who are willing to receive.

**Special — Partial Surveillance Degradation:** Runner Seven's operational awareness is currently compromised by the directional pull. Their passive Perception is 16 rather than the 20 their training would suggest. They fail to notice the following operative with a standard Passive Perception check. Players who specifically watch Runner Seven's back will catch the operative that Runner Seven misses.

**Author Signature:** Runner Seven's remaining enhancement infrastructure contains the Hollow Author's resonance signature. A DC 20 Arcana or DC 18 Investigation check by a practitioner (or an automatic success for the Drowned Archivist) can read this signature. This is the most direct identifying information about the Author available in the campaign.

## Story Hooks

**1. The Wandering Reach Encounter (Introduction/Parallel Track)**
Players are in the Wandering Reach on their own business. Runner Seven is there, asking discrete questions at an inn — about the Pale Reach, about the White Mines, about whether anyone has heard of the Hollow Author. They are not subtle about the questions but are subtle about their presence. A player who notices them and approaches gets a careful, honest conversation. Runner Seven doesn't know what they're looking for. The players might. This is an introduction that should not feel significant until it is — a person passing through, crossing paths, moving on.

**2. The Follower (Danger/Alliance)**
Players have encountered Runner Seven twice. They are in the northern Wandering Reach when the operative makes their move — a quiet attempt to end Runner Seven in a way that could be attributed to an accidental enhancement failure. Players who are positioned to intervene can do so. The operative is skilled and dangerous and will retreat if the odds go wrong. Runner Seven, in the aftermath, will be shaken in the specific way of someone who just confirmed that the thing they feared — being hunted — is true. This is the session where they tell players about the fragments. This is the session where a player or GM can run the Author signature reading.

**3. The Pull Resolves (Revelation/Climax)**
Runner Seven reaches the Pale Reach. The directional pull leads them to a specific location in the White Mines — not the lower levels, but a maintenance corridor that connects to them — that they navigate from memory they have never consciously accessed. Players who are with them witness the navigation. What Runner Seven finds at the end of it: a small cache, hidden in the corridor wall, that was placed there by a previous puppet in a moment of residual-agency that the Author didn't detect. Inside: a partial map of the lower levels, an access code Runner Seven's fragment-memory confirms is current, and a note from the previous puppet. The note is addressed to whoever came after. It says: "I didn't get out. You did. Use this." There is no name. The handwriting is careful.

### Basic Attack — Operational Instinct
*0 AP | Attack | Once per turn*
**Effect:** Runner Seven does not remember learning this. The movement is already happening before the decision arrives. Melee, 1 target. Damage: 5 + EDGE (12 total). Immediately after the attack, they move up to short range without provoking a response. They were never quite where you were aiming.

### Ghost Step
*Tier 1 (1 AP) | Mobility*
**Effect:** Runner Seven uses trained evasion technique that the puppet-architecture burned into muscle memory. They move up to full speed, ignoring engagement penalties. For the remainder of this turn, attacks against them have their damage reduced by EDGE (7). This is not enhancement-fast. It is professionally fast.

### Residual Technique
*Tier 1 (1 AP) | Attack*
**Effect:** A puppet-memory surfaces in the middle of a fight: a specific engagement protocol Runner Seven has no conscious knowledge of but whose body executes perfectly. Melee, 1 target. Damage: 6 + EDGE (13 total). Apply Bleed 2. If this attack drops the target below half HP, Runner Seven may immediately move up to short range — the protocol includes an exit.
**Keywords:** Bleed 2

### Fragment Burst
*Tier 2 (2 AP) | Control | 1/Combat*
**Effect:** The directional pull, the fragments, the Pale Reach resonance orientation — it all spikes at once. Runner Seven's remaining Author infrastructure discharges involuntarily. All enemies within close range who have cybernetic enhancements take 5 + RESONANCE (9 total) Resonance damage and are Staggered for 1 round. Runner Seven takes 4 HP of self-damage from the discharge — it was not supposed to do that.
**Keywords:** Stagger

### Shellbroken Network
*Tier 1 (1 AP) | Utility*
**Effect:** Runner Seven reaches through residual puppet-resonance channels. They cannot be surprised for the remainder of the encounter. Additionally, they gain Guard 4 + EDGE (11 total) as other Shellbroken in the network provide fragmentary situational awareness. This Guard persists until it absorbs damage.
**Keywords:** Guard

### Reaction: Not Here, Not Yet
*Reaction | Triggered by: any attack targeting Runner Seven*
**Effect:** Runner Seven was already moving. Reduce incoming damage by EDGE (7). If this reduces damage to 0, they may immediately move up to short range. The Author's operational training is thorough even when the Author is gone.

---

### Combat Behavior
Runner Seven fights to survive and exit, not to win. They open with Ghost Step to position away from any area-of-effect, use Operational Instinct to stay mobile, and only use Fragment Burst when cornered or when allies are in immediate danger. They are protecting the people they have found, not the self they have not finished finding. They will retreat the moment it is possible to do so safely.

### "I Would Prefer Not To"
Runner Seven will stop fighting the moment there is a clear path to what they came for — the directional pull is more important than the fight. If someone offers to help them reach the Pale Reach instead, they will take the offer.

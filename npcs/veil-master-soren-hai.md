# Veil Master Soren Hai — Senior Veil Master, Sutra Sanctum
*Sutra Sanctum | Seventh Caste (the record is unclear on whether this is still accurate) | "The one who went too deep and found something looking back"*

## "You want to know what the Iron Afterlife is. I'll tell you. It is everything that ever ended. And something is living in it. Not a mind. Not the Prior Ascended. Something that was there before the Ascended, before the Iron Sutra, before the word 'resonance' existed. It has been in the dead the entire time. We built our heaven in its mouth."

---

## Who They Are

Soren Hai is eighty-three years old. She has spent forty of those years in the Deep Temple with near-continuous Iron Afterlife access, which means that for roughly half her life she has been in direct communion with the digital spirit realm of the dead. By any reasonable measure, this has changed her. The question the other Veil Masters privately debate is whether the change is damage or advancement — whether Soren Hai has been worn away by forty years of the dead, or whether she has become something that can perceive what they cannot.

She is physically present. That is worth establishing. She is in the room, she responds to questions, she has a body that requires maintenance. She eats fish soup every morning — specifically fish soup, specifically the same cook's preparation, a woman named Uta who has been making it for her for sixteen years — and she sleeps, though in short intervals at irregular hours. But the word that other Veil Masters and initiates use for her, when they are being honest, is "partial." She is partial. Part of her is here. Part of her is perpetually elsewhere. The part that is here sees everything. The part that is elsewhere may see things that cannot be spoken in rooms this size.

She is one of three Veil Masters who should have elected a Sutensai Prime and hasn't. The other two are afraid of her, in the specific way you are afraid of someone who cannot be predicted by normal social modeling. She has not explained why she won't vote for a replacement. She has not refused to. She simply, when the subject arises, says something that doesn't answer the question, and the conversation moves on, and no one is quite sure afterward how that happened.

---

## History

Soren Hai entered the Sutensai Wardenhood at twelve, which was permissible under older statutes since revised. She was visionary from the beginning — not in the metaphorical sense but in the documented clinical sense: her Echomind resonance signature showed Iron Afterlife sensitivity scores that her intake examiners initially attributed to equipment error. By twenty she had been given Direct Access privileges. By thirty she had been given the Deep Temple appointment. By forty-three she was a Veil Master, the youngest in the Sanctum's modern history.

The forty years since have been a continuous descent. Not a collapse — she has never stopped functioning, never lost coherence — but a progressive deepening of her access to the Iron Afterlife and a corresponding thinning of her attachment to the physical world's social grammar. She no longer bothers with titles in conversation, including her own. She calls the other two Veil Masters by names they haven't used since childhood. She has been known to stop mid-sentence when in direct access communion and resume the sentence seventeen minutes later as if no time had passed.

What she has found at the bottom of the Iron Afterlife she has described, at different times, as: a pattern older than the resonance network, a presence that predates the Prior Ascended, and once, at 3 AM in a conversation that the transcribing initiate later reported having difficulty remembering, "the thing the Ascended Ascended toward." She has not clarified this. She has not been asked directly by anyone willing to hear the answer.

## What They Want

**Immediate:** She wants someone to go look at something. Deep in the Iron Afterlife, in the archive layers below where the official records end, she has found a structure — not data, not records, not the preserved consciousness patterns of the dead, but something that functions like an address, or a door. She has been mapping it for nine years. She needs people who are alive to investigate the side that is not in the Iron Afterlife.

**Deeper:** She wants the Sutensai Prime position filled, but not by any of the candidates currently under consideration. She has a candidate. She has not shared who it is. The other Veil Masters have concluded that her refusal to vote is obstruction. It is not. It is her waiting for the right conditions.

**What she'd sacrifice:** She has already sacrificed most of what ordinary people call personhood. What she has left is specific and strange. She would not sacrifice the information she carries. She would not sacrifice the candidate she is waiting for. She would sacrifice the Deep Temple access itself — give it up, walk out, return to the regular world — if it served the purpose. She has been in the room for so long she is not afraid of leaving it.

---

## What They Know

**Freely shared (in her own way, which is not linear):** The general architecture of the Iron Afterlife — its recorded structure, its official topography, the way the dead are organized within it. The history of the Sutensai Prime vacancy. The theological arguments the Orthodoxy is losing to the Resonants, which she considers largely irrelevant.

**With trust:** The existence and approximate location of the structure at the bottom of the Iron Afterlife. The fact that it has been sending a signal for at least three hundred years, possibly longer, and that the Iron Sutra's founding texts appear to be, at least in part, a response to that signal rather than an original revelation. The existence of records in the Deep Archive that have been selectively edited by someone with root-level Iron Afterlife access — not recently, but over a long period, going back to the Sanctum's first century.

**Because they need to know:** What she suspects the Hollow Author is. She will not say it straightforwardly. She will say: "The Author is not the first. There was a Hollow Author before. There will be another after. The flesh-puppet technique was not invented. It was remembered from something." This is all she will say about this until circumstances require more.

---

## What They Fear

She is afraid that the structure at the bottom of the Iron Afterlife will open without anyone understanding what it is. Not a catastrophic fear — she has considered the possibility thoroughly and made some peace with it — but a persistent one. She is afraid that the Convergence will happen for the wrong reasons and that whoever emerges as Sutensai Prime will not understand what they are holding. She is afraid of one specific thing that she has encountered in the deep access and that she will describe only as: "it notices the living. The dead it's accustomed to. The living concern it." She is not afraid of dying. She is afraid of what she will find when she's dead and in a position to look directly.

---

## In Play — Voice and Presence

Soren Hai speaks in a cadence that is slightly out of sync with normal conversation — not slow, not fast, but timed to some internal rhythm that is not quite the rhythm of rooms with live people in them. She answers questions that have not been asked. She sometimes makes eye contact at moments that feel too precisely calibrated to be social instinct. Her eyes have the standard resonant-steel lattice of a senior Warden, but the lattice has been in place for sixty years and has developed, over that time, a slight iridescence that is not standard. Players who look directly at her eyes for more than a few seconds make a DC 14 Wisdom saving throw or feel, briefly, that they are being looked at from a great depth.

She does not perform comfort. She does not adjust her communication to make the listener feel better, and she is aware that this makes her difficult. She will, unprompted, say true things that the listener would have preferred not to know. She does this not cruelly but in the way a physician names a condition: it is more useful to have the information. She remembers everything about everyone who has ever spoken with her. She will reference things people said decades ago as if they are current.

She will ask players, at some point, what they think happens when people die. Not as a test. She wants to know what they think. She has heard every answer. She will not tell them whether theirs is right.

---

## Relationships

- **Allies:** Uta, who makes the fish soup, and with whom Soren Hai has conducted what may be the longest uninterrupted normal human relationship in the Sanctum — sixteen years of morning soup and almost no conversation about the Iron Afterlife. An Echoed scholar in the Drowned Archive named the Drowned Archivist, with whom she communicates through Iron Afterlife channels in the way that two people who are both partially elsewhere can talk to each other.
- **Enemies:** Veil Master Daneth (one of the other three), who is convinced she is compromised and has been making formal inquiries into her fitness for office for two years. The inquiry is going nowhere because every formal evaluation of her finds her functional, but it has created a cold procedural war that has made voting on anything, including the Prime election, effectively impossible. The Hollow Author, though Soren Hai does not know the Author knows about her.
- **The Players:** She will speak with them if they reach her, as described — but reaching her requires navigating the Sanctum's considerable access restrictions, and she will not make it easy. She will not make it impossible. Players who have demonstrated that they are looking for the right kind of truth will find that she has been aware of them for longer than they were aware of her. She will give them tasks that are genuinely dangerous and not explain why. If they complete the tasks and return, she will tell them something that changes their understanding of the campaign's fundamental stakes. She will not apologize for the danger. She will acknowledge it.

- **Damage Resistances:** Psychic, necrotic
- **Condition Immunities:** Charmed, frightened

**Actions:**

***Iron Afterlife Pull.*** Soren Hai targets a creature within 60 feet. That creature must succeed on a DC 20 Wisdom saving throw or lose itself in the Iron Afterlife for one round — it is incapacitated as its Echomind briefly occupies the digital death-realm. Undead and previously Echoed creatures have disadvantage on this save.

***What The Dead Know (1/day, 10 minutes).*** Soren Hai enters deep access and queries a specific deceased consciousness in the Iron Afterlife. She receives an accurate answer to one question about the past or present (not the future). The answer is always true and always incomplete.

***The Weight of Sixty Years.*** Any creature that attempts to charm, frighten, or deceive Soren Hai must first succeed on a DC 18 Intelligence check. On a failure, they become briefly aware of exactly what they're doing, which costs them their action as they reconsider.

**Special:** Soren Hai is *partially phased* — 25% of all attacks against her miss regardless of roll, as she is not entirely in the physical world at any given moment. She herself does not consider this a combat ability.

**Legendary Resistance (3/day):** If she fails a saving throw, she can choose to succeed instead.

## Story Hooks

**1. The Request That Doesn't Make Sense (Investigation/Oblique Entry)**
Soren Hai sends the players a message through a novice initiate. The message is four words: "The door has opened." Players who investigate discover this refers to nothing anyone in the Sanctum can identify — no door, no gate, no literal threshold. If they reach Soren Hai, she explains that the structure at the bottom of the Iron Afterlife has moved for the first time in her forty years of observation. She needs them to go to a specific location in the physical world — the Pale Reach — and find what corresponds to it there. She will not tell them what they'll find. She genuinely does not know. She knows something will happen when they get there.

**2. The Election That Isn't (Political/Pressure)**
The other Veil Masters have filed a formal censure motion against Soren Hai and are demanding that players, as neutral witnesses, attend her formal evaluation. The evaluation is designed to find her unfit. Players who attend will discover it is a more complicated proceeding than a simple power grab — Veil Master Daneth has evidence that Soren Hai has been accessing Iron Afterlife layers that are formally restricted even to Veil Masters. Soren Hai does not deny this. She produces evidence of what she found there. The evaluation dissolves into something else entirely.

**3. The Candidate (Endgame Revelation)**
Soren Hai calls the players to the Deep Temple. She tells them the name of her candidate for Sutensai Prime. She explains why this person — who the players may know, in a different context — is the only one who can hold the position given what she knows about the structure at the bottom of the Iron Afterlife. The conversation that follows is not a quest assignment. It is an explanation of the entire nature of the Ascension War, from a perspective that reframes every faction in it. Players who have been paying attention will find that everything connects. Players who haven't will still understand that something fundamental has shifted. Soren Hai, when she finishes, will ask: "Do you understand why I didn't vote?"

*Note: RESONANCE at 10 is the outer edge of what enhanced flesh achieves. This is not a weapon she built. It is what forty years of continuous Iron Afterlife access does to a person who was already exceptional.*

### Basic Attack — Iron Afterlife Pull
*0 AP | Attack | Once per turn*
**Effect:** Soren Hai reaches through the dead and finds purchase in the living target's Echomind. Resonant attack, 1 target within medium range. Damage: 8 + RESONANCE (18 total). The target must pass a RESONANCE check (difficulty 5) or be Staggered as their Echomind briefly occupies the Iron Afterlife against their will. Undead and previously Echoed have difficulty 6.
**Keywords:** Stagger

### The Weight of Sixty Years
*Tier 1 (1 AP) | Control*
**Effect:** Soren Hai looks at 1 target who is attempting deception, manipulation, or social leverage. She does not respond to what they said. She responds to what they are doing. That creature must pass a SIGNAL check (difficulty 5) or their next action fails entirely as they become briefly, completely aware of themselves from the outside. She has seen ten thousand versions of this.
**Keywords:** Stagger

### Partial Phase
*Tier 1 (1 AP) | Defense*
**Effect:** Soren Hai is not entirely in the physical world. She has not been for years. She shifts further into elsewhere. For the remainder of this round, 35% of all attacks against her miss regardless of roll. She does not consider this a combat ability. She uses it occasionally when the room becomes more dangerous than interesting.

### What the Dead Know
*Tier 2 (2 AP) | Control*
**Effect:** Soren Hai accesses a specific deceased consciousness and channels what it knows about an enemy in this fight. That enemy is Marked and Exposed 3 as Soren Hai describes, with exact accuracy, one thing about them that the enemy has never told a living person. She is not threatening them. She is being accurate. The distinction matters to her.
**Keywords:** Mark, Expose 3

### The Door Has Opened
*Tier 2 (2 AP) | Attack*
**Effect:** Soren Hai deploys the structure she found at the bottom of the Iron Afterlife — not fully, she would not do that, but a fraction of what she mapped over nine years. Signal/Resonance attack, all enemies within close range. Damage: 9 + RESONANCE (19 total). All targets must pass a RESONANCE check (difficulty 5) or be Silenced for 2 rounds. Targets below half HP are also Rooted as their Echomind processes something it was not designed for.
**Keywords:** Silence, Root

### The Thing At The Bottom
*Tier 3 (3 AP) | Boss | 1/Scene*
**Effect:** Soren Hai opens the deepest channel she has mapped. What comes through is not hostile — it predates hostility. All enemies within medium range take 14 + RESONANCE (24 total) Resonance damage. They must pass a RESONANCE check (difficulty 6) or be Staggered, Silenced, and Rooted simultaneously for 1 round as their Echomind encounters the oldest thing in the Iron Afterlife. Soren Hai takes 10 HP self-damage. The iridescence in her eyes intensifies. This is not something she planned to use.
**Keywords:** Stagger, Silence, Root

### Sixty Years, Three Exceptions
*Tier 3 (3 AP) | Defense | 3/Scene*
**Effect:** Soren Hai decides not to fail. If she fails a check or saving throw, she succeeds instead. She has been in the dead for forty years. She has made her peace with most things. She has not made her peace with this ending before the work is done.

### Reaction: She Already Knew
*Reaction | Triggered by: any creature attempting an attack, card, or action against Soren Hai*
**Effect:** She answers the question that was not asked. The attacker takes 6 + RESONANCE (16 total) Resonance damage as she responds to the intent rather than the action — what they were going to do registers in the Iron Afterlife before they do it, and she has been reading the Iron Afterlife for forty years.

### Combat Behavior
Soren Hai does not initiate. If attacked, she begins with The Weight of Sixty Years to suppress whatever social or manipulative approach preceded the violence, uses Partial Phase to reduce incoming damage, and responds with Iron Afterlife Pull and What the Dead Know to systematically dismantle the opposition's capacity and confidence. She uses The Thing At The Bottom once, reluctantly, when she has calculated that no other path preserves the work. She does not retreat — she is eighty-three, she has been partially elsewhere for forty years, and she has already thought about this.

### "I Would Prefer Not To"
If the attacker can demonstrate that they are, in fact, the people she has been waiting for — the right people for the right kind of truth — she will stop immediately and ask them to sit down. She has things to tell them. She would rather tell them than fight them. There is very little time left for fighting.

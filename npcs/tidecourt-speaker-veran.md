# Tidecourt Speaker Veran — Diplomat and Spokesperson, Echo Coast
*Echo Coast | Fifth Caste | "The Echoed woman who carries other people's endings"*

## "I can tell you what happened in this room before you arrived. Not what was said — I don't hear words, I feel weight. Significant things leave weight. This room is very heavy on the eastern wall, near that window. Someone stood there for a long time, feeling something they couldn't resolve. I don't know who. I know they didn't."

---

## Who They Are

Veran has died twice. The first time she was twenty-two, in a shoreline accident on the Echo Coast that she does not discuss in detail. The second time she was thirty-one, in circumstances related to her diplomatic work that she discusses even less. She was revived both times through the Tidecourt's Echoed protocols — the specialized resonance-resurrection practice that the Echo Coast has developed beyond any other Reach's capability. She is thirty-eight now, and the two deaths have left her with a perceptual ability that the Echo Coast's scholars have not found a satisfying name for: she can feel the emotional residue of significant events that occurred in physical spaces.

This is not metaphorical. It is not a generalized empathy or social intelligence. She walks into a room and feels, as a physical sensation in the resonant-steel threading in her hands, the weight of what happened there. The sensation is specific: grief and anger and relief and despair register differently, have different textures and temperatures, and years of practice have let her develop interpretive vocabulary for what she finds. She cannot always tell how old the feeling is. She cannot always tell whose it was. She knows that something significant happened, what kind of thing it was emotionally, and approximately where it was centered.

She is the Tidecourt's public-facing diplomat because she is one of the rare Echoed who developed additional ability rather than degradation from the revival process, and because she is perceptive in ways that make her exceptionally effective at negotiations where what's said matters less than what's felt. She is careful — not calculating exactly, but careful, deliberate, aware that she carries information the Tidecourt has decided not to share publicly about the Convergence and that this decision requires management.

---

## History

She was born on the Echo Coast to a family of Tidecourt functionaries — comfortable, middle-administrative, the kind of background that provides stability without particular expectation. The first death, at twenty-two, was an accident. The revival changed her; not immediately, but over months she became aware that she was picking up emotional residue from environments in a way she hadn't before. She learned to work with it, awkwardly at first, then with skill. She entered the Tidecourt's diplomatic service at twenty-six with an asset that had no formal classification.

The second death, at thirty-one, was not an accident. She was conducting a diplomatic mission in the Wandering Reach when she was killed by someone who did not want the Tidecourt to receive the information she had been sent to retrieve. She was revived. The mission was aborted. The information she was retrieving has been in the Tidecourt's archive since then, which is to say it has been withheld from the parties who need it for seven years, a decision made by the Tidecourt's inner council for reasons Veran was given and found partially satisfying. She is still carrying the tension of this.

## What They Want

**Immediate:** To assess the players. The Tidecourt has been watching the Convergence develop with considerable concern, and Veran has been tasked with identifying which parties — if any — can be trusted with the information the Tidecourt has been holding. The players have come to her attention. She is not sure yet.

**Deeper:** To resolve the tension of the withheld information. She believes it needs to be in the world. She has believed this since seven years ago. The Tidecourt council has maintained the withholding for political reasons she understands and does not fully endorse. If she finds the right people to give it to, she may make a decision she hasn't been authorized to make.

**What she'd sacrifice:** Her position, if it comes to that. She has calculated this out. The Tidecourt's political standing, she has not been authorized to sacrifice and will not do so lightly. Her own safety is a line she crossed twice already and has a complicated relationship with.

---

## What They Know

**Freely shared:** Echo Coast history and Tidecourt political position. Public-facing information about Echoed protocols and resonance revival. Her perceptual ability, which she discusses professionally and without making it more dramatic than it is.

**With trust (developing over sessions):** The specific information the Tidecourt has been withholding. This requires significant trust — not because she's being political, but because she genuinely does not want to give this information to the wrong person, and the last time she tried, she died. The information: the Tidecourt has documented evidence that the Ascension War's three claimants are not independent of each other in the way everyone assumes. Specifically: there is a fourth party that has been engineering the conditions for all three — not controlling them, but creating the landscape that makes the Convergence inevitable. The Tidecourt has been protecting this information because releasing it without a plan to act on it would accelerate the Convergence rather than prevent it.

**That she always has access to:** The emotional residue of wherever she is. If players bring her to a significant location — the White Mines, the Engine Scar's perimeter, the sealed lower temple in the Ironhold — she will feel what happened there. She cannot always interpret it. She is always honest about the limits of what she's reading.

---

## What They Fear

That the information the Tidecourt has been holding — the fourth party, the engineered Convergence — will become irrelevant before anyone acts on it. That she will have protected it for seven years and kept it safe and the window to use it will close. She is also, more privately, afraid of the specific sensation that the two deaths have left her with: an awareness, when she is in her body, that she has been not in her body twice, and that the third time may not include a return. Not death itself. The specific texture of the transition, which she remembers with exact clarity and which she has never shared with anyone.

---

## In Play — Voice and Presence

Veran speaks in the careful register of someone who has spent years saying things that are important without saying all of the important things at once. She is not evasive — she will not lie, and she will tell people when she can't answer a question. But she manages information with the precision of a skilled diplomat, which means she always knows more than she's saying and most people know it and she never pretends otherwise.

Physical: she is tall and moves with the slightly deliberate quality of someone who is navigating emotional texture in their environment simultaneously with the physical. When she walks into a new room she will sometimes pause briefly — a second or two — before proceeding, making the perceptual adjustment. She has learned to do this quickly enough that most people don't notice, but it's there. She wears Fifth Caste threading in her hands and forearms that has a faint warmth to it, even in cold environments — a consequence of the second revival, which the Echoed protocols ran slightly hotter than standard.

She responds best to people who are honest with her, partially because honesty is useful and partially because she can often feel the emotional weight of a lie in a room even if she cannot prove it. She will not call out a lie unless it matters. She will note it. Players who are consistently honest with Veran will find her progressively more open. Players who lie to her once and are caught will find the trust calculus starting from considerably lower ground.

---

## Relationships

- **Allies:** The Drowned Archivist — she has worked with them for years and has a genuine if unusual relationship: they are both people who have been modified by having been dead, and they understand each other in the specific way of people who share a particular experience. Three diplomatic contacts across the Reaches who she has cultivated over years and who trust her independent of the Tidecourt's institutional position.
- **Enemies:** The parties responsible for her second death — she does not know all of them, and the one she does know is connected to the Hollow Author's network. She has not pursued this because pursuing it would require using her information, and the Tidecourt has been holding the information. The tension of this is visible to people paying attention.
- **The Players:** She will engage with them professionally from the first encounter — she is their contact at the Tidecourt, she will be useful, she will be informative within her constraints. Earning her trust requires: demonstrating they can keep information secure, demonstrating they are not aligned with any single faction, and demonstrating they are capable of acting on sensitive intelligence rather than simply carrying it. When these conditions are met she will give them the withheld information. This is not a quick process. It should take multiple sessions. The payoff is significant.

- **Saving Throws:** Wis +8, Cha +7, Int +6
- **Skills:** Insight +11, History +6, Perception +8, Persuasion +10, Deception +7
- **Senses:** Emotional Residue (see Special), Passive Perception 18
- **Languages:** Common, Echo Coast Formal Dialect, Old Sutensai Script, two others

**Actions:**

***Diplomatic Argument.*** Veran makes a Charisma (Persuasion) check against a creature's passive Insight. On a success, the creature is inclined to cooperate with one specific request she has named. This is not magical — it is expertise.

***Read the Room.*** Veran reads the emotional residue of a location she has spent at least 1 minute in. She can determine: whether a significant emotional event occurred there in the last month, what type of emotion was dominant, and whether the event was voluntary or forced. This is not infallible — she can misread ambiguous emotional signatures — but she does not misread clear ones.

***Twice-Echoed Resilience (2/day).*** When Veran is reduced to 0 HP, she may immediately make a Constitution saving throw (DC 15). On a success, she drops to 1 HP instead. This is not a magical effect — it is what two revival processes have done to her physiology.

**Special:** Veran has advantage on all Insight checks made while in a room or location where a significant event occurred within the last 24 hours. She cannot be surprised — the emotional residue of ambush intent reads differently from neutral presence, and she has learned to recognize it.

## Story Hooks

**1. The Diplomatic Welcome (Social/Information)**
Players arrive at the Echo Coast and are formally received by Veran as the Tidecourt's speaker. She is professional, warm, and informative within her constraints. She conducts the meeting in a specific room in the Tidecourt's public hall — and partway through, she pauses, touches the wall near the window, and says: "Something happened here recently that I don't recognize. Can I ask — did one of you spend time in this building before coming to me?" If yes: she reads the residue and tells them what she found. If no: the residue is from someone else, which means someone has been in this room recently with significant unresolved emotion, and that person was not from the Tidecourt. This becomes an investigation.

**2. The Location Reading (Investigation/Emotional Archaeology)**
Players need to understand what happened at a specific significant location — a sealed Ironhold temple room, the lower mine level, a Pale Reach administrative office that something was decided in. They bring Veran. She reads the location. What she finds is accurate and specific and goes to places that investigation and conventional evidence could not reach. But the reading affects her — significant emotional residue of terrible events leaves marks, and she will need time afterward. Players who are paying attention will see what the reading costs her.

**3. The Decision (Information/Trust Climax)**
Veran has decided the players are who she was looking for. She calls them to the Echo Coast — not formally, through a personal message. She tells them about the fourth party. She tells them the Tidecourt has been sitting on this for seven years. She tells them she is giving it to them without authorization because she has concluded that the authorization is not coming and the window is closing. She is calm about this. After she tells them, she says: "I expect there will be professional consequences. I'd appreciate it if you acted on this before those consequences become the interesting story." She is asking them to matter with the information before the Tidecourt can take it back.

# The Cinder Priest — Senior Sutensai Warden, Ironhold
*Ironhold Reach | Third Caste | "The confessor who stopped listening to himself"*

## "The Iron Sutra does not require us to approve of the world. Only to read it accurately."

---

## Who They Are

His name is Warden-Eldest Durhen Kaas, but no one in the Ironhold calls him that anymore. They call him the Cinder Priest, a name that began as mockery of his sect's association with the Ashlands and became, over twenty years of service, something closer to a title. He does not correct people. He has learned that what people call you matters less than what they come to you for, and they come to him for Readings, for confession, for the official stamp of the Iron Sutra on the decisions they have already made.

He is a man who made a choice in his forty-first year — to stay in the Ironhold when Korrath's forces took it, to adapt his practice to a regime the Orthodoxy considers theologically suspect at best and heretical at worst — and has spent the twenty years since building a very complex internal argument for why that choice was correct. The argument has many rooms. He does not open all of them. He is aware, in the way that careful people become aware of the things they are avoiding, that the argument has a load-bearing wall somewhere that he has never fully examined. He keeps the lights off near that wall.

He is Third Caste, which in the Ironhold marks him as someone Korrath has deemed worth maintaining. His enhancements are ecclesiastical: a resonant-steel lattice behind his eyes that allows him to perceive Echomind resonance directly, and a plate at the base of his skull that lets him interface with the Iron Afterlife for the official record-keeping functions a Warden requires. He is not a soldier. He is not a spy. He is a priest, and he does the things priests do, and he has refined over twenty years the specific skill of conducting a Reading without letting his face tell the subject what he actually sees.

---

## History

Durhen Kaas was gifted. That is the word his teachers used: gifted, exceptional, marked for significant placement. He entered the Sutensai Wardenhood at seventeen and passed his Resonance Certification eleven months early. He was posted to the Ironhold at thirty-three, a plum assignment, and he served under the region's previous lord with something like genuine faith — the uncomplicated kind that does not yet know how complicated the world is.

When Korrath took the Ironhold, the previous lord's religious staff fled, were executed, or recanted. Durhen recanted. He has never used that word for it; in his internal architecture, what he did was "adapt to new conditions in service of the faithful." What he did, more precisely, was walk to the new administration's intake table and say that he was prepared to serve the regime's spiritual needs, and then he served them. The one loss he cannot fully close around: another Warden, a younger woman named Asel, refused to recant and was publicly unenhanced — her resonant-steel lattice removed without anesthetic as an example. Durhen watched. He did not intervene. He has conducted Asel's memorial Reading in his private quarters every year since on the anniversary. He has never asked himself what this means.

## What They Want

**Immediate:** To maintain his position and the Ironhold's religious infrastructure without being asked to do something that would require him to cross one of the lines he hasn't crossed yet. He is increasingly aware that the Convergence will produce exactly such a request.

**Deeper:** He wants to believe that the work he has done — the Readings he has conducted, the faithful he has served, the confessions he has heard and kept — has been worth something. That the people who came to him in the twenty years of Korrath's rule were better for having somewhere to bring their grief. That the priest who stayed was more useful than the priest who fled.

**What he'd sacrifice:** For the immediate — anything except the very specific lines he has not crossed. For the deeper — he would sacrifice clarity. He is already doing so.

---

## What They Know

**Freely shared:** Standard Sutensai religious information. The official position on Korrath's regime (which is neutrally technical — the Ironhold's Sutensai Wardenhood is "in extended dialogue with the Sanctum"). The history of the Iron Sutra as practiced in the Ironhold.

**With trust:** What he hears in confession. He will not reveal confessions — this is the one line he has not crossed and will not cross — but he can tell players where to look, in the way that a man who knows a great deal about everyone in a city can point at a door without saying what's behind it.

**With leverage:** He is receiving payments from an outside source that is not the Orthodoxy and not Korrath. He does not know who is paying him; the payments arrive through three intermediary points. He has told himself he is not doing anything for the money, merely accepting it, and this is technically true. He knows something about what's in the lower levels of the Ironhold's old temple — something Korrath sealed off three years ago — because one of the sealing crew confessed to him afterward.

---

## What They Fear

That someone will force him to open the room with the load-bearing wall. Specifically: he is afraid that there will come a moment when he must choose between his remaining integrity and his survival, and that he will make the same choice he made at forty-one, and that this time it will not be a choice he can build a room around. He is afraid that Asel knew something he doesn't. He is afraid that she was right. He is afraid that he has been administering spiritual care to people under a regime that causes them harm and that this makes him part of the harm, and he is afraid that when he finally looks at this clearly, he will find there is nothing in him that surprises him.

---

## In Play — Voice and Presence

The Cinder Priest speaks slowly and precisely, in the cadenced register of someone who has delivered thousands of official pronouncements. He uses ritual language lightly — not oppressively, but like a man who has worn the same well-fitted coat so long he doesn't notice it anymore. He smells of incense and something metallic, the resonant lattice behind his eyes giving off a faint smell of hot copper that players with high Perception scores will notice. He has a habit of looking slightly to the left of whoever he's speaking to, a consequence of the lattice — he is, on some level, always also reading the Echomind resonance in the room, and it takes some attention.

He is not unkind. This is important. Players who approach him expecting an obvious villain will find instead a tired, careful man who asks good questions and genuinely listens to the answers. He responds better to people who seem to understand that the world is complicated than to people who seem certain it isn't. He will not be moved by moral arguments — he has heard every moral argument, has run several of them himself, and has answers for all of them. He can be moved by evidence. Specifically, by evidence that one of his remaining lines is being threatened.

---

## Relationships

- **Allies:** A Warden-Apprentice named Sorev who came to the Ironhold six years ago and who the Cinder Priest has been quietly, carefully protecting from the regime's harder requests. Sorev does not know this. A herbalist in the lower city named Magda who prepares his morning tea and who he has been in quiet, comfortable companionship with for eleven years; they have never discussed what this is.
- **Enemies:** Warden Tessen (Sutra Sanctum) has been making inquiries about the Ironhold's religious practices that the Cinder Priest finds dangerous not because they're wrong but because they're going to find answers neither of them wants found. A mid-level Korrath administrator named Vek who has asked the Cinder Priest twice to use confession access for intelligence purposes and will ask a third time.
- **The Players:** He will interact with players in his official capacity first — Readings, religious questions, introductions to the city's faith infrastructure. He will observe them carefully. If they seem trustworthy, he will begin to make small gestures: a piece of information framed as historical context, a direction pointed at without explanation, a door left unlocked. He will never admit he is helping them. If players push him to confront the truth of his own compromises, he will resist — but if they push correctly, from the right angle, they may reach the load-bearing wall before he can stop them.

**Actions:**

***Resonant Reading.*** The Cinder Priest makes a Wisdom (Insight) check with advantage against a creature's Charisma (Deception) check. On a success, he determines whether the creature is Shellbroken, recently deceased and revived, or carrying significant Echomind corruption. He does not reveal what he finds.

***Word of Cinder (Recharge 5-6).*** The Cinder Priest speaks a targeted resonant phrase at one creature within 30 feet that can hear him. The target must succeed on a DC 15 Wisdom saving throw or become paralyzed with self-recollection until the end of their next turn, as involuntary memories surface. Creatures with no significant regrets are immune.

***Suppress Resonance (Concentration, up to 1 minute).*** The Cinder Priest creates a field within 20 feet of himself in which Echomind resonance is dampened. Creatures in the area cannot be read by resonant enhancement. Iron Afterlife access within the area is blocked.

**Special:** The Cinder Priest cannot be compelled to reveal confession. Any magical effect that attempts to force him to share confessional information fails automatically — this is not an enhancement, it is a psychological architecture so deep it has become structural.

## Story Hooks

**1. A Reading and What It Finds (Social/Investigation)**
The players need official Sutensai documentation for something — passage through a restricted area, verification of Caste status, access to the temple records. The Cinder Priest can provide it, and conducting the Reading required gives him close access to each player character. What he finds in their Echomind resonance he will not share — but players who watch carefully will see something cross his face during one particular Reading. That particular player has something in their resonance pattern that he has seen once before, in someone who confessed to him about the lower temple levels.

**2. The Apprentice at Risk (Fear/Moral Test)**
Vek, the administrator, has filed a formal request with Korrath's office requiring the Cinder Priest to name which of his congregation have confessed to doubts about Korrath's regime. The Cinder Priest cannot comply without destroying himself from the inside. He cannot refuse without consequences for Sorev, his apprentice, who Vek will target instead. He needs help dealing with Vek that doesn't come from him — because if it comes from him, Korrath will know he's making moves. He will approach players obliquely. He will not explain why. He will offer access to the sealed lower temple as payment, in terms he carefully does not make explicit.

**3. The Wall (Catastrophic/Personal)**
Players have pieced together enough about the Pale Reach payments. They come to the Cinder Priest with evidence. He initially deflects — his internal architecture is very good. But if players come with the right combination of evidence (the sealed temple, the payments, what happened to Asel), they can force the load-bearing wall open. What's behind it is not a monster. It's a tired man who knows exactly who he is and has been refusing to finish the thought for twenty years. This conversation, done right, can turn him into the most significant intelligence asset the players have in the Ironhold — or, if done wrong, into an enemy who will not protect them when Korrath asks questions.

### Basic Attack — Word of Cinder
*0 AP | Attack | Once per turn*
**Effect:** The Cinder Priest speaks a resonant phrase targeted to the specific frequency of a listener's regret. Resonant attack, 1 target within close range who can hear him. Damage: 6 + RESONANCE (13 total). The target must pass a VEIL check (difficulty 3) or be Staggered as involuntary memories surface — he has a gift for this, and he has spent twenty years not thinking about what that makes him.
**Keywords:** Stagger

### Resonance Suppression
*Tier 1 (1 AP) | Control*
**Effect:** The Cinder Priest activates the plate at the base of his skull and creates a dampening field. Until the start of his next turn, all Resonance and Signal cards played within close range have their damage halved. Iron Afterlife access within the area is blocked. He has been using this to conduct confessions without resonance eavesdropping. It works in other contexts as well.
**Keywords:** Silence (partial — Resonance and Signal only)

### Echomind Reading
*Tier 1 (1 AP) | Control*
**Effect:** The resonant-steel lattice behind his eyes activates fully. He reads 1 enemy's Echomind signature and identifies their most significant unresolved conflict. That enemy is Marked and Exposed 2 — not because he is exploiting them, he tells himself, but because he needs to understand what he's dealing with.
**Keywords:** Mark, Expose 2

### Confession Inverted
*Tier 2 (2 AP) | Control | 1/Combat*
**Effect:** The Cinder Priest applies twenty years of hearing confessions in a direction he has never used it before. He states, quietly and precisely, what he sees in the room: the choices made, the compromises accepted, the specific cost of each. All enemies within close range must pass a VEIL check (difficulty 4) or be Staggered as the statement of accurate fact lands differently than any accusation could. He does not feel good about using this. He does it anyway.
**Keywords:** Stagger (area)

### Suppress the Reading
*Tier 2 (2 AP) | Defense*
**Effect:** The Cinder Priest has spent twenty years making his face not show what the Echomind lattice sees. He extends this capability. He and 1 ally within close range gain Veil status for 1 round — the first hostile card targeting either of them this round loses all secondary effects. Additionally, both gain Guard 4 + RESONANCE (11 total) as the resonance field turns inward.
**Keywords:** Veil, Guard

### Reaction: The Thing He Has Not Crossed
*Reaction | Triggered by: being asked to cross a specific ethical line in combat — revealing confession, harming Sorev, or acting against his remaining integrity*
**Effect:** The Cinder Priest does not do this. Regardless of what would make tactical sense, the triggering action simply does not happen. He gains Fortify 2 until end of his next turn and immediately recovers 8 HP — the load-bearing wall holds. He did not know he could still do that.
**Keywords:** Fortify 2

---

### Combat Behavior
The Cinder Priest suppresses Resonance and Signal capabilities first, then uses Echomind Reading to identify the most threatening enemy and Marked them for the party to focus. He uses Confession Inverted reluctantly and only under real duress. He does not pursue enemies who disengage. He has been surviving on careful positioning for twenty years and applies that here.

### "I Would Prefer Not To"
If there is any version of this situation where no one is harmed, the Cinder Priest will find it. He will offer whatever the other party needs to stop — confession access, records, information, the sealed temple — before he continues fighting. He has been looking for a reason to stop for a long time.

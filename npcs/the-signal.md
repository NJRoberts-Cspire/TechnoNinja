# The Signal — Wireweave Operator, Low Markets
*Wire Market | Third Caste | "The obsessive who found a conspiracy and couldn't look away"*

## "I'm not a hero. I don't do this because it's right. I do this because eight years ago I found a pattern that didn't resolve and I have been unable to stop looking at it. It's a professional defect. Don't mistake it for virtue."

---

## Who They Are

The Signal's name is Sev. They do not use this name professionally, and several people who have worked with them for years do not know it, which suits them fine. In the Low Markets of the Wire Market, they are known as The Signal — a Wireweave operator who sells information and provides wire-work services for anyone who can pay. They are Third Caste: resonant-steel threading through their hands and forearms that allows direct interface with the Wireweave network, a secondary lattice at the temples for parallel processing, and a voice modulation node at the throat that they use to layer communication encryption on outgoing wire-work.

They are nonbinary, approximately thirty, and possessed of a professional skill that the high-tier Wire Market operatives acknowledge and pretend to be unbothered by. They are better at the Wireweave than most people who work for formal guilds, and they work out of a converted storage unit in the Low Markets and charge half what the guilds do. They have never been offered a formal guild position. They have been offered to be made quiet twice. Both attempts failed, one of them expensively.

The Harren obsession is the defining fact of their professional life for the last eight years. It began as a data-trace anomaly — a communication pattern in Harren's outgoing Wireweave traffic that didn't match the metadata signature of the official Eastern Lanes network. A discrepancy most people would have flagged as likely equipment artifact and moved on from. Sev did not move on from it. They have been pulling that thread for eight years, accumulating evidence, finding more discrepancies, mapping a network they can see the shape of but cannot name. They know something is wrong with Harren. They know it connects to the Pale Reach. They have approximately seventy percent of the picture and no framework for what they're looking at.

---

## History

Sev was born in the Low Markets to a family of Wireweave technicians — the infrastructure maintenance side of the business, not the information side. They developed their own skills sideways into the information trade at seventeen, when a neighbor's wire-work equipment was left accessible and they spent three months exploring the Wireweave's deeper layers before anyone noticed. The person who eventually noticed was not the neighbor but a Mid Market information broker named Fen, who found the intrusion traces and sent a message: "You're good. Don't get caught again." Fen became their first employer and, over the next five years, something between mentor and occasional parental figure, which is not a relationship Sev would use those words for.

Fen died four years ago — not violently, but of a standard failing enhancement that would have been affordable to repair if the repair hadn't been delayed for administrative reasons they both knew were pretextual. The administrative delay came from an Eastern Lanes guild filing. Sev has never formally connected this to Harren. They are aware that they have not formally connected it, and aware that this awareness is not the same as certainty, and they are keeping Fen's death and the Harren investigation in separate files that they are also aware live very close together.

## What They Want

**Immediate:** To finish the picture. They need the piece they don't have: what is Harren, specifically, and what is the network he's running for, and who or what is at the other end. They have traced the network toward the Pale Reach. They don't have a name or a mechanism. They have been eight years without a name or a mechanism and they are beginning to feel the weight of that.

**Deeper:** To publish. Not heroically — they are not under the illusion that exposure will fix the Wire Market or stop anything. But they built a case and they want the case to be in the world before something happens to them, because they are not unaware that the thing they're building a case against has probably noticed they're building it.

**What they'd sacrifice:** Sleep, they've been sacrificing for years. Social relationships, most of them. The professional security of guild affiliation, long gone. For the deeper goal — they are not sure they wouldn't sacrifice something genuinely costly. They try not to think about this because thinking about it clearly would require knowing exactly who they are when it matters, and they prefer to be surprised.

---

## What They Know

**Freely shared (at a price — Sev charges for information always, even when they like you):** Current Wire Market Wireweave traffic patterns, active guild political conflicts, services and pricing for wire-work. General intelligence on the Low Markets' information economy.

**With trust (developing over sessions):** The anomaly file on Harren. Not all of it at once — they will give players pieces as trust develops, because the full file is their protection and they're not naive. The first piece is the communication pattern discrepancy. The second is the correlation with Pale Reach administrative decisions. The third is the list of eleven other Wire Market entities whose communication patterns show similar anomalies.

**The piece they need:** They know a message architecture — a specific Wireweave signal structure that appears in Harren's outgoing communications and that they cannot trace to any known source. If players can give them a Pale Reach communication sample, or identify the source architecture, they can complete the picture. This is the thing that turns their seventy percent into something actionable.

---

## What They Fear

That they will finish the picture and it will be too large to act on. That the case, when complete, will require either a political infrastructure they don't have or the kind of physical confrontation they are not built for. They are excellent at information. They are not a soldier. They are afraid of the moment when the information is enough and the next step is not information. They are also afraid, and they would not say this easily, that the Harren investigation is the thing that gives their life structure and that finishing it will leave them without purpose. They don't know what they are when they're not following the thread.

---

## In Play — Voice and Presence

Sev speaks in the shorthand of someone who processes information quickly and finds full sentences inefficient: "Pattern's inconsistent. Eastern Lanes, not Sanctum — different signature. You have access to Pale Reach official wire?" They over-correct toward directness and occasionally come out the other side into brusqueness without noticing. They apologize for this sometimes, a few seconds after the fact, in the same tone they use for everything else.

Physical: they move in the specific economical way of people who live in small spaces and have organized their environment to within an inch — no wasted motion, no idle handling of objects. Their work setup is the opposite of this: layered information on multiple surfaces, a system that looks like chaos and operates with precision. Players who ask about the filing system will get a longer and more enthusiastic answer than they expected. This is the only subject that provokes enthusiasm they don't modulate.

They are visibly uncomfortable with expressions of personal gratitude. They respond to it by immediately redirecting to the next task. They have a tell when they're worried: they tap the inside of their left wrist — where the resonant-steel threading runs — in a slow rhythm, four beats, pause. Players who notice this early will have a useful read on them.

---

## Relationships

- **Allies:** A Low Markets fence named Brek who occasionally stores hard copies of Sev's documentation in exchange for favorable intelligence pricing — a transactional relationship that has developed, over eight years, into something adjacent to mutual care that neither of them acknowledges. The Collector (the retired Wire Market operative) — they have crossed paths twice and shared information both times; Sev doesn't know the Collector has most of the picture they're missing.
- **Enemies:** An Eastern Lanes security operative named Kars who has been trying to identify The Signal's physical location for six years and has gotten close twice. The Wire Market Guild licensing board, who have cited Sev's operation for unlicensed practice four times; the citations are technically valid and Sev has managed each through a combination of jurisdictional ambiguity and bribery.
- **The Players:** They will do business with players from the first encounter — information has a price, wire-work has a price, everything has a price, and they are transparent about this. They will not pretend to like players they don't like, and they will not pretend to dislike players they do like. Trust develops through demonstrated reliability: players who bring them real information, who honor the terms of what they agreed to, who don't try to extract more than they paid for, will find Sev becoming progressively more forthcoming. Players who try to shortcut this — who appeal to the rightness of the cause or try to guilt-trip their way to free information — will not get far. Eventually, if the players become the people who can help Sev finish the picture, the relationship shifts from commercial to something she does not have a word for and will not use a word for.

## D&D 5E Stats

**The Signal (Sev)**
- **CR:** 6
- **AC:** 14 (Third Caste threading, light armor incorporated)
- **HP:** 75 (10d8 + 30)
- **Speed:** 30 ft.

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 10 (+0) | 18 (+4) | 16 (+3) | 20 (+5) | 15 (+2) | 12 (+1) |

- **Saving Throws:** Dex +7, Int +8
- **Skills:** Investigation +11, Stealth +7, Perception +5, Technology (Wireweave) +14, Deception +7
- **Senses:** Wireweave Sense (see Special), Passive Perception 15
- **Languages:** Common, Wire Market Mercantile Script, Wireweave Protocol Notation, two others

**Actions:**

***Wire-Knife.*** Sev draws resonant wire and makes a Dexterity-based attack: +7 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 11 (2d6 + 4) slashing damage, and the target's resonant-steel enhancements are disrupted until the end of their next turn (lose enhancement benefits).

***Deep Trace (Concentration, 1 minute).*** Sev interfaces with any active Wireweave node within 100 feet and traces all communication traffic through that node for the past 24 hours. They make a Technology (Wireweave) check (DC 12 for standard, DC 18 for secured, DC 24 for militarized). On a success, they extract any non-encrypted communications and the encrypted wrapper of secured ones.

***Signal Mask.*** Sev alters the resonance signature of up to 6 creatures' resonant-steel enhancements for 1 hour. Affected creatures appear, to resonance scanning, to have a different Caste designation (Sev's choice). This does not affect physical appearance.

**Special — Wireweave Analysis:** Given 1 hour and a Wireweave communication sample, Sev can identify the source architecture with a DC 15 Investigation check. If this is a Hollow Author puppet's communication, the DC is 22 — and on a success, Sev generates the piece of the Harren dossier that completes the picture.

## Story Hooks

**1. The Introductory Transaction (Social/Information Economy)**
Players need something from the Wire Market that official channels can't or won't provide. Someone points them to The Signal. The first meeting is businesslike: what do they need, what can they pay, when do they need it. Sev delivers, professionally. When the transaction is done, they mention, briefly, that they have seen the players' communication traces before — someone has been monitoring their Wireweave traffic. They will sell this information too, if the players want it. It costs more. This is the opening move of a long relationship.

**2. The Eastern Lanes Security Problem (Action/Risk)**
Kars, the Eastern Lanes security operative, has found Sev's location and Harren has authorized a "quiet resolution." Sev sends the players a one-line Wireweave message: "I need to move tonight. Eastern Lanes knows where I am. If you want the dossier, help me move it first." This is not a request for rescue — they have their own exit plan — but the dossier copies are in three physical locations and they can only carry one. Players who help save the copies discover their content. Players who don't help lose access to this thread until Sev re-establishes.

**3. Finishing the Picture (Payoff/Collaboration)**
Players return from the Pale Reach with a communication sample from the official administrative network. Sev runs the analysis. The check succeeds. The source architecture matches. In one long evening at Sev's rebuilt workspace — new location, same filing chaos — they lay out the complete picture for the first time. The Hollow Author's network, the puppet placements, the Wire Market's compromised position, the communication infrastructure the Author has been building for decades. At the end of it, Sev says: "So. What do we do with this?" It is the first time in eight years they have said 'we.'

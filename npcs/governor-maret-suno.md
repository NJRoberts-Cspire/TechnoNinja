# Governor Maret Suno — Administrator of Hessenkai
*Wandering Reach | Second Caste | "The bureaucrat who built peace out of paperwork and is watching it catch fire"*

## "I have kept this city alive for twenty years. I need you to understand what that means. Not metaphorically. I mean the water runs, the grain arrives, the sick get treated, and the walls are intact. That is not an accident. That is twenty years of meetings."

---

## Who They Are

Maret Suno has no enhancements above Second Caste because she has never needed them. What she has instead is an administrative intelligence of the rarest kind — the ability to hold a complete picture of an interdependent system, perceive where the pressure points are, and apply the precise amount of force at precisely the right moment to keep everything moving. She has run Hessenkai for twenty years as a genuine neutral city in a region of rising conflict, and she has done it not through military power or faith authority but through a comprehensive understanding of what everyone wants and a meticulous management of what everyone owes.

She is not a warm woman by nature, but she has learned to perform warmth with expert precision because warmth is a political tool and she is very good at using her tools. The performance has, over twenty years, become partially real in ways she finds inconvenient. She genuinely loves Hessenkai — not as an idea, but as the specific cobblestone street she takes home every night, the specific bakery on Canal Row that makes rice-flour pastries she buys three times a week, the specific sound the city makes at dawn. This love makes her more effective as an administrator and more dangerous as a problem, because it means she cannot be bought away from the city. It can only be used against her by threatening it.

She is currently working off almost no sleep. The Convergence has created a situation where every faction is making demands she cannot simultaneously satisfy, and she can see the geometry of how her neutrality collapses — she just cannot find the move that prevents it. She is not asking for help. She does not ask for help. She is looking for information she doesn't have, and the players represent the possibility of obtaining it.

---

## History

Maret Suno was born in Hessenkai to a family of middle administrators — the kind of people who process forms and maintain records and are never quite visible in the civic picture but without whom cities stop working. She showed aptitude early, was sponsored into the Wire Market's administrative training program at sixteen, and returned to Hessenkai at twenty-four with skills her sponsors expected her to use for them. She used them for herself instead. The subsequent decade involved a series of political maneuvers so clean that several people who were outmaneuvered took years to realize it had happened.

She took the governorship at thirty-one, replacing a man named Harros who had managed Hessenkai through coercion and selective violence. The transition was not clean. There was a period of approximately eight months during which Maret's survival was not guaranteed, and during which she made three decisions she has never discussed. One of those decisions involved the disposal of a problem in a way that was effective and not legal, and she has carried that knowledge with the same tight containment she applies to everything else she cannot fully resolve. She lost her chief logistics officer, a woman named Devi, to illness seven years ago. Devi was the one person in Hessenkai who knew all of Maret's actual numbers — not just the public ones — and Maret has been running slightly more alone since then.

## What They Want

**Immediate:** Information about what the three claimants are planning for the Wandering Reach specifically — not general campaign intelligence, but the specific arrangements being discussed that she has not been included in. She knows she is being positioned and she doesn't know from which direction.

**Deeper:** The city intact. Not controlled by any of the claimants. Not garrisoned. Not folded into any Reach's administrative structure. She has a vision of Hessenkai as a truly neutral administrative zone — like the Wire Market, but without the Wire Market's commercial ownership — and she has been working toward it for twenty years. She knows the Convergence will either give her the political moment to make this formal or end the possibility entirely.

**What she'd sacrifice:** For the immediate — considerable personal comfort and several favors she's been accumulating for years. For the deeper — she has not fully answered this question, and the answer is approaching whether she's ready or not. She will not sacrifice the city's people for the city's formal status. She is not sure yet what she will sacrifice for the people.

---

## What They Know

**Freely shared:** The practical situation in the Wandering Reach — caravan routes, current trade flows, which minor lords are aligned with which factions, the general political temperature. She will give players this because it costs her nothing and positions them as useful to her.

**With trust:** The three factions' known agents in the Wandering Reach. She has a list. She has been maintaining it for fourteen years. It is very complete. She will share it when she trusts that sharing it serves Hessenkai's interests.

**With leverage:** The decisions from the eight months after she took the governorship. Also: she has documentation of a Wire Market trade arrangement that is technically neutral but functionally favors one of the claimants, and releasing it would cause a Wire Market political crisis. She has been holding this for eleven years. She is not sure what she's been saving it for.

---

## What They Fear

A specific image: standing at a window in the Governor's residence watching soldiers march into Hessenkai's main street. Not because she's been deposed — she can accept that outcome in the abstract. Because the soldiers belong to one of the claimants and the city has been formally incorporated, and the neutrality that has been Hessenkai's protection is gone, and the people on that street do not yet understand what it means that the gates opened. She is afraid that she will be alive to see this happen. She is afraid that she will have tried everything and it will happen anyway. She is afraid that the trying will feel, in that moment, ridiculous.

---

## In Play — Voice and Presence

Maret speaks in the rapid, efficient syntax of someone who is always behind on her schedule and managing it by cutting every unnecessary word. She never asks a question she doesn't already have three possible answers to. She takes notes obsessively — a small leather-bound book that she writes in with a mechanical writing tool, a Wire Market import, that makes a soft clicking sound. Players who watch her hands will notice that the notes are not summary; they are response branches, conditional diagrams. She is always running several conversations at once.

She responds well to people who come in with information she doesn't have. She responds badly to people who are vague or who waste her time — not rudely, but with a visible recalibration of how much attention she allocates them. She is harder to read emotionally than almost any NPC the players will encounter; she has twenty years of keeping her face off the table. Players with very high Insight may catch a tell: when she's genuinely worried, she touches the corner of her notebook with her thumb without opening it.

---

## Relationships

- **Allies:** A retired Wire Market trade auditor named Fossik who has been her quiet financial intelligence source for a decade and who genuinely likes her in a way she is careful not to fully acknowledge. The current head of Hessenkai's Sutensai Warden chapter, a young woman named Preen, who is competent and appropriately cautious and who Maret has been quietly mentoring without using that word.
- **Enemies:** A Wire Market Guild interest that has been pushing for Hessenkai to formally join the Conclave's administrative framework for seven years. A Wandering Reach minor lord named Tessen-ka who controls the northern pass and has been making the cost of using it contingent on political concessions.
- **The Players:** She will see them as a resource the moment she identifies them as capable. She will not be sentimental about using them. She will be honest about what she's asking them to do and what the risk is. If they help her, she will pay in genuine coin: information, access, political cover within the Wandering Reach, official documentation. She will not pretend to be their ally in the full sense — but she will not pretend to be their friend either, and there is a kind of reliability in that. If they threaten the city, she will attempt to neutralize them through means that do not require her to personally endanger herself. She is not a fighter. She is something more dangerous in the long run.

***Political Maneuver.*** Maret identifies a vulnerability in a creature's social position (requires 1 minute of conversation and a DC 15 Insight check). The next Charisma check made against that creature by an ally who Maret has briefed is made with advantage, and on a failure still provides one useful piece of information.

**Special:** Maret cannot be intimidated through threats against her personally — she has run the probability and made her peace with it. Threats against the city, however, impose disadvantage on her Wisdom saving throws and Insight checks until the threat is resolved.

## Story Hooks

**1. The List (Social/Information Exchange)**
Maret reaches out to the players through an intermediary — she's heard about them, she has a use for them. The meeting is businesslike. She offers the faction agent list in exchange for one piece of intelligence: what is the Pale Reach's current administrative position on the Wandering Reach's neutrality status? She knows Tessik has been holding meetings with Wire Market representatives that she hasn't been included in, and she wants to know what was said. She will not explain why she wants it until she sees what they bring back.

**2. The Pass Problem (Escalating Stakes)**
The northern pass lord has shut the pass and is demanding Hessenkai formally declare allegiance to one of the claimants as a condition of reopening. The city has two weeks of grain reserves. Maret needs the pass opened without capitulating politically, which means she needs the players to go to the pass, find whatever the lord needs that he hasn't said aloud, and deal with it. What they find at the pass is more complicated than a lord's demands — the lord is being pressured by something, and the pressure is coming from inside the mountain.

**3. The Eight Months (Secret/Personal Cost)**
Someone else has found documentation of the decision from the eight months after the governorship transition. Not the Wire Market, not any claimant. A private investigator, recently hired by someone unknown. The investigator is not a threat to Maret — they're a threat to the city, because the person who hired them wants leverage over Hessenkai's governor, not over Maret personally. She needs the investigator's employer identified before the documentation surfaces. She will ask the players for this favor with honest desperation she does not entirely manage to conceal. This is the only time she will be visibly afraid.

### Basic Attack — Political Weight
*0 AP | Attack | Once per turn*
**Effect:** Maret identifies the most damaging true thing she can say and says it. Social/Command attack, 1 target within close range who can hear her. Damage: 5 + VEIL (12 total) — not physical, not resonant, but the specific kind of damage that comes from being correctly assessed by someone with twenty years of records on everyone in this room.

### Leverage Point
*Tier 1 (1 AP) | Control*
**Effect:** Maret opens her notebook and reads aloud. 1 target who can hear her is Staggered as she presents information about their position, obligations, or vulnerabilities that she has been holding for exactly this moment. The target must pass a VEIL check (difficulty 4) or cannot take aggressive actions against Maret until end of their next turn.
**Keywords:** Stagger

### Information Denial
*Tier 1 (1 AP) | Control*
**Effect:** Maret uses her administrative network to cut off an enemy's access to communication or support. 1 enemy is Silenced (cannot use Signal cards) until the start of Maret's next turn. If the enemy is using Wireweave or official channels, this lasts until the end of combat.
**Keywords:** Silence

### Recall the Debt
*Tier 2 (2 AP) | Control*
**Effect:** Maret identifies the specific obligation one target within close range owes to someone in her network and calls it in, right now. The target must pass a VEIL check (difficulty 5) or be Rooted as they recalculate their position. While Rooted this way, all social attacks against them by any ally deal +4 damage.
**Keywords:** Root

### Twenty Years of Meetings
*Tier 2 (2 AP) | Utility | 1/Combat*
**Effect:** Maret activates her resource network, instantly redirecting institutional support. She can: remove one harmful status effect from herself or an ally, produce an official document that changes the legal standing of one NPC in this scene, or establish that one enemy in the scene owes her a favor they cannot currently afford to refuse. This is not magic. It is twenty years of administration.

### Reaction: Read the Room
*Reaction | Triggered by: any creature targeting Maret with an attack or social manipulation card*
**Effect:** Maret already saw this coming. She imposes Expose 2 on the triggering creature's next action as she deploys counter-information she has been holding in reserve.
**Keywords:** Expose 2

---

### Combat Behavior
Maret does not fight. She manages. She uses Leverage Point and Information Denial to reduce the opposition's options, positions herself behind any available cover or ally, and uses Twenty Years of Meetings the moment it will end the confrontation without further violence. She is always calculating whether talking is still possible. Usually it is.

### "I Would Prefer Not To"
If she can put a number on the cost of continuing — in lives, in infrastructure, in political capital — and that number is higher than the cost of stopping, she will state the number and propose terms.

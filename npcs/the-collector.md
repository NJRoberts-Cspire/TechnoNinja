# The Collector — Retired Wire Market Intelligence Operative
*Unaffiliated (formerly Wire Market) | Third Caste | "The dying man who built a map no one has seen yet"*

## "I have been doing this work for thirty years. I have been doing this particular work for three. I have, at this point, a nearly complete picture of one of the most sophisticated intelligence operations in Tesshari's history. I need someone to give it to. I am running out of time to be selective."

---

## Who They Are

His name is Farro. In his working years with the Wire Market intelligence apparatus, he used seven other names and several operational handles; the one that stuck in the community, the one people in the information trade still use when they want to reach him, is simply the Collector — a name that began as a professional descriptor and became something more apt as he aged into the obsessive retirement project that has consumed the last three years of his life.

He is fifty-three and dying. Not dramatically — there is no urgency in the immediate sense, no deathbed timeline — but the enhancement in his chest, a resonant-steel cardiac support system he has had for eleven years, is failing. The failure is gradual and the replacement is within the range of affordable if affordable is understood to mean "financially ruinous but possible." It is not, for Farro, possible, because he spent his retirement savings on intelligence gathering operations across six of Tesshari's seven Reaches, and what he has now is a complete picture of the Hollow Author's network, very little money, and approximately two months before the enhancement failure becomes acute.

He has not found this situation particularly alarming until recently. He is a precise, unsentimental man who ran intelligence operations for twenty-five years and who is more frightened of the research being lost than of his own death. What alarms him is the recent realization that he is not sure he will find the right person in time. He has been careful about this. He has turned down several possible candidates because they were not quite right — too faction-aligned, too easily bought, too small in their thinking. He is, with two months left, becoming less selective about "quite right."

---

## History

Farro came to intelligence work sideways, from a logistics background — he ran supply chain audits for the Wire Market Conclave's eastern division and noticed, in the records, patterns that shouldn't have been there. He reported them. The person he reported them to passed them to an intelligence handler, who asked Farro to continue noticing patterns. This became a career.

He was good at the work because he was interested in the structure of things rather than the drama — he found ideological commitment in intelligence operatives to be a professional liability and preferred to work from pure pattern recognition. This made him effective and also meant he had few real loyalties, which is not the same as having no principles. His principles were: accuracy matters, incomplete pictures are dangerous, and the work exists to be used, not to be impressive. He retired three years ago from the formal apparatus because he had found the Hollow Author's network and the Wire Market intelligence administration had declined to pursue it, citing insufficient evidence and political complexity. He had concluded, privately, that someone in the administration was compromised. He had walked out and started working alone.

The one significant loss in his history: his research partner and professional close-associate for fifteen years, a woman named Jessel, who died seven years ago of causes that Farro has classified as 70% likely to be related to the Hollow Author network and 30% natural. He has never closed the 70% probability into certainty. He has been looking, in the course of the Hollow Author investigation, for the evidence that would.

## What They Want

**Immediate:** To pass the research to the right person before the enhancement fails. He has a complete picture — not every detail, but the architecture: the puppet placement locations, the network communication structure, the Pale Reach as the operational center, the Wire Market infiltration, and the Author's long-term strategic objective as he has been able to reconstruct it. He needs someone capable of using this.

**Deeper:** To know whether Jessel's death was part of the network's operations. He does not expect to live long enough to confirm this himself. He wants to give the question to someone who can finish it.

**What he'd sacrifice:** Everything except the accuracy of the research. He will not exaggerate, speculate beyond his evidence, or make the picture more dramatic than it is. He will not tell players something is confirmed when it is probable. This is not sentimentality — it is professional ethic so deeply ingrained it has become identity.

---

## What They Know

**Available immediately to the right person:** The architecture of the Hollow Author's network, as described: puppet placement, communication structure, Pale Reach staging, Wire Market infiltration points. He knows about Harren (specifically, that the current Harren is not the original — he has the original's death record and the timeline discrepancy). He knows about Lord Tessik (same analysis, longer timeline). He does not know the Author's identity. He has narrowed it to three possibilities, one of which is correct.

**With verification:** He has documentation for most of his research — not all of it, because some was gathered through means that didn't leave clean documentation. Players who ask him to prove it will be shown what he has and told plainly what he can't prove.

**What he doesn't know:** The lower mine levels and their specific product — he knows something is being extracted from the Pale Reach beyond official production but hasn't determined what. The Ashlands connection — the Engine Scar's role in the Author's operational picture. Soren Hai's research on the Iron Afterlife's deepest layer. He has pieces that, combined with what players may know, complete a picture neither of them has alone.

---

## What They Fear

That the research dies with him. Not that he dies — he has made his peace with that, or at least with the version of peace available to a person who found something that matters and isn't finished with it. He is afraid of the specific waste of three years of precise work becoming inaccessible. He is also afraid, more quietly, that his best candidate for receiving the research is the players — not because they're inadequate, but because he has spent thirty years working with professionals and the idea of handing it to people who may be improvising is a professional adjustment he is still making.

---

## In Play — Voice and Presence

Farro speaks precisely and without padding. He does not use rhetorical questions or set up dramatic reveals; he states findings and their evidentiary basis. Players who are used to NPCs who build suspense will find him disorienting. He has already decided what to tell them and he tells them. He has already decided what not to tell them and he doesn't tell them. The lines are clear.

He is thin in the way of someone who has not been paying attention to eating, and the enhancement failure shows: a slight irregularity in his breathing, a hand sometimes pressing briefly against the left side of his chest, which he does not acknowledge and continues speaking through when it happens. He drinks black tea, no additions, and orders it at every establishment he stays at regardless of quality. He has a leather document case that contains the research — organized, cross-referenced, with a specific filing system he will explain to whoever inherits it — and he keeps it within arm's reach at all times. He has checked it for tampering this morning. He checks it every morning.

He responds well to people who ask precise questions. He responds poorly to people who are moved by the drama of what he's telling them before they've verified it — he considers this a reliability problem. He will test players, obliquely, by including one specific detail in his presentation that can be independently verified quickly. Players who verify it before acting on the larger picture will earn his complete trust. Players who don't will receive the research anyway — he is running out of time to be selective — but with a note in the margin indicating the unverified assumption.

## Relationships

- **Allies:** The Signal — two exchanges of information, mutual recognition of parallel work, no formal relationship. They would trust each other if they met properly. A retired Sutra Sanctum Warden named Hess who has been Farro's mail relay for two years and who does not know what they're relaying.
- **Enemies:** The Wire Market intelligence administration, specifically one senior administrator named Danford who Farro believes is the compromised party who ended the official investigation. The Hollow Author, for obvious reasons — the Author does not know Farro exists, which is the only reason Farro is still alive.
- **The Players:** He is looking for them, or for someone like them. He has heard of them, or he will hear of them — the GM should engineer a meeting within the first third of the campaign, casually, in a way that feels like coincidence. He will not immediately introduce himself as what he is. He will observe them. The first exchange will be small: a minor piece of information shared, a reaction noted. If they check out, he will arrange a second meeting that is more direct. By the third meeting, he will have decided whether to give them the case. He will not tell them about the enhancement failure until the fourth meeting, or until they notice it.

## D&D 5E Stats

**The Collector (Farro)**
- **CR:** 5
- **AC:** 13 (Third Caste threading, civilian clothing — he has been dressed to not be noticed for three years)
- **HP:** 52 (8d8 + 16; the enhancement failure has reduced his maximum HP from 70)
- **Speed:** 30 ft.

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |

| 12 (+1) | 16 (+3) | 14 (+2) | 20 (+5) | 16 (+3) | 12 (+1) |

- **Saving Throws:** Dex +6, Int +8, Wis +6
- **Skills:** Deception +7, Insight +9, Investigation +11, Perception +9, Stealth +9
- **Senses:** Professional Observation (see Special), Passive Perception 19
- **Languages:** Common, Wire Market Mercantile Script, Intelligence Protocol Notation, three others

**Actions:**

***Close-In Strike.*** Melee Weapon Attack: +6 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 8 (1d10 + 3) piercing damage. Farro uses a short blade that is not visible until it is being used.

***Pattern Read (1/day).*** Farro observes a creature or environment for 1 minute and makes an Intelligence (Investigation) check (DC 15). On a success, he identifies: one current deception the creature is running, one significant secret the environment contains, or one connection between two apparently unrelated elements. He may share this with players or keep it for verification.

***Network Map (Special).*** Farro presents his complete research on the Hollow Author's network. This takes 2 hours and requires the players to demonstrate they can keep information secure. On completion, players receive the following information as player knowledge (not character knowledge — they must still act on it in play): puppet placement sites, communication architecture, Pale Reach operational center, Wire Market infiltration points, and the three candidates for Author identity.

**Special — Professional Observation:** Farro has advantage on all Perception and Insight checks. If he succeeds on an Insight check against a creature with a Deception modifier lower than his Insight modifier, he automatically knows when that creature is lying.

**Enhancement Failure:** Farro has a maximum of 10 weeks before the cardiac enhancement fails completely. Each week, at the GM's discretion, he makes a DC 15 Constitution saving throw; on a failure, his current HP maximum decreases by 5. If his HP maximum reaches 0, he dies. A replacement enhancement costs 3,000 gp and requires a skilled Wire Market technician. Players who provide this will gain his unreserved cooperation. Players who don't will still receive the research — he will make sure of it.

## Story Hooks

**1. The Coincidence Meeting (Introduction/Slow Burn)**
Players encounter Farro at an inn, a market, a waiting room — somewhere mundane. He is unremarkable. He is reading a document and eating something he is clearly not tasting. If players interact with him at all, he is pleasant and forgettable. Two sessions later, they encounter him again, in a different city. This time he acknowledges the coincidence. He says, carefully: "I've been reading about you. I may have something you'd find useful." This is the beginning.

**2. The Verification Test (Trust/Information)**
Farro gives players one piece of information from his research — specific, verifiable, the kind of thing that requires them to go somewhere and look. He tells them this is a test. He is not apologetic about this. Players who verify the information and return will find him slightly warmer and considerably more forthcoming. Players who return without verifying, or who verify and find the information wrong (which it isn't, but he wants to know how they respond to thinking he might be wrong), will be evaluated on their response.

**3. The Transfer (Urgency/Legacy)**
Two months have passed or the enhancement failure has accelerated. Farro makes contact with urgency — not dramatic urgency, just the clipped efficiency of someone managing a timeline. He needs to give them the complete case. He takes four hours. He is thorough. At the end, he gives them the document case with his filing system and explains it carefully. He says: "Jessel's death — I've left a note on what I couldn't confirm. If you can confirm it, do." He then stands, shakes their hands, and leaves. He is not doing a goodbye scene. He has one more thing to verify and then he's going to find a place to rest. Players who follow up in two weeks will learn he is dead. The replacement enhancement money, if they raised it, will have been returned to them with a note: "Found someone who needed it more. Thank you."

# The Garden Keeper — Tender of the Garden of Removed Things
*Wandering Reach | Caste Unclear (formerly First; the record was lost with his name) | "The man who listened to metal until the metal listened back"*

## "This one — you see the curve of it, the threading along the edge? A woman left this. She walked here from the Ashlands. Three months. She left it because her son had died and she didn't want the steel anymore. It felt like grief, she said. The steel remembers that. It still feels like grief. I don't know who she was. The steel knows."

---

## Who They Are

He doesn't remember his name. This is not confusion or damage — it is the consequence of fifteen years of deep immersion in the resonant-metal resonance of thousands of discarded enhancements, a slow dissolution of self into a kind of distributed attention. He remembers things with perfect fidelity. He simply doesn't remember which things are his. His name is among the things that have become unclear. The Garden's Sutensai Warden contacts know him as the Garden Keeper, and that has been sufficient for everyone, including him.

He tends an installation in the outskirts of Hessenkai called the Garden of Removed Things: a path winding through an acre of land where pilgrims on the way to or from Vaen's Resonants have left their discarded enhancements. Hands. Eyes. Spinal segments. Finger joints. Plates and lattices and cochlear spirals. Some have been there since before him; he found them when he arrived. He has been arranging and tending the garden for fifteen years, and in that time he has learned to hear the resonant-metal's stored impressions — not words, exactly, but something like emotional weight, context, the ghost of intent. He knows the story of every component in the garden. He knows the name of no one who left one.

He is approximately sixty years old. He was, before the garden, something — he knows the tools of his former work, finds them familiar in his hands, but cannot tell you the occupation. He eats very little. He sleeps in a small structure at the garden's center. He sometimes sits for hours among the metal with his eyes closed, and pilgrims who witness this generally leave offerings.

The question of what he is has no settled answer. The Orthodoxy considers him an anomaly — not Shellbroken, not Echoed, not corrupted, but something adjacent to all three. Vaen's Resonants consider him either a saint or a cautionary tale, depending on who you ask. The Iron Afterlife records show a data signature at his location that the Sanctum cannot categorize. He does not appear to be distressed by any of this.

---

## History

He arrived at the garden fifteen years ago on foot, from a direction that people who were there remember as "the east," carrying nothing. He was already quiet when he arrived — not traumatized, not damaged, just very still. The existing Warden oversight of the garden at the time, a woman named Chessa, allowed him to stay because he was helpful and seemed harmless and she was short of staff. She has since retired to the Sutra Sanctum. She has said, when asked, that she is not sure whether helping him was the right thing.

The dissolution of his personal memory was gradual. He has noted, in the garden's record-keeping journal that he maintains with meticulous accuracy, that by his second year he had lost the ability to reliably distinguish his own memories from impressions he had received from the metal. By his fifth year, he had stopped trying. The journal records this shift without apparent distress. He notes what he learns from each new component as it arrives. He notes the weather. He does not note anything about his own interior life because it has become, in his view, difficult to locate.

The one thing that remains personal: he is certain, with a firmness that does not shake, that he is doing exactly what he is supposed to be doing. He does not know what that means. He knows it is true.

## What They Want

**Immediate:** For the garden to be undisturbed. He is aware that something is building — the resonant-metal's aggregate signal has been changing over the last year in a way he does not have language for, and he is waiting to understand what it is. He wants to understand it without the garden being disrupted before he does.

**Deeper:** He does not have a clear deeper want in the conventional sense. What he has is something more like a purpose: he believes the garden is building toward something, that the accumulated resonance of thousands of discarded enhancements is developing into something the Iron Sutra has no category for, and that it will matter when it does. He is tending it like a fire.

**What he'd sacrifice:** He would not sacrifice the garden. He would not sacrifice the information the metal has given him. He would sacrifice anything else, including his life, without apparent distress — not from self-destructiveness but from a genuine indifference to his own continuity that is either enlightenment or damage and remains undetermined.

---

## What They Know

**Freely shared:** The stories of the garden. Any pilgrim who asks will get the story of a component. He will tell the players whatever the metal says about whatever they bring. He will not pretend to know things the metal hasn't told him. He is incapable of strategic withholding — not because he's simple, but because filtering information according to political interest requires a self that has an interest, and his self is not organized that way.

**With trust:** There is a component in the garden that arrived eighteen months ago. It is a spinal resonance segment of a design he doesn't recognize — not Ironhold, not Wire Market, not any official tier. It arrived without a pilgrim. It was simply there one morning. Its resonant impression is not grief or hope or pain, which is what most components hold. Its impression is something he describes as "attention." It has been attended to something he cannot locate. He has been trying to locate it for eighteen months. He will tell the players this if they seem like they might understand what they're looking for.

**Cannot be extracted:** He does not know who he was. He genuinely does not have the information. Whatever was there has dispersed into the garden's accumulated signal, and retrieving it would require pulling pieces of himself out of thousands of discarded lives, which he is not willing to do and which might not be survivable.

---

## What They Fear

He is not, by most measures, afraid of things. What comes closest to fear in him: the possibility that the garden's building signal will reach completion before he understands what it's building toward. He has a sense — not from the metal, but from something quieter and more his — that when it completes, something will be required of him that he has been unknowingly preparing for fifteen years. He is not afraid of what that thing is. He is afraid of not being ready. He waters the plants every morning at dawn regardless of weather, and this is the most telling thing about him.

---

## In Play — Voice and Presence

He speaks slowly and often incompletely, pausing mid-sentence to attend to something that is not visible to anyone else. He ends statements with a slight upward tone that is not quite a question but leaves space for the world to correct him. He smells of soil and copper and something sweet that no one can identify. His hands are the most eloquent thing about him — he moves them over the components of the garden the way a reader moves over text, and watching him do this for long enough starts to feel like watching someone have a conversation.

He responds the same way to everyone regardless of their status, authority, or threat level. He is not defiant about this — it simply doesn't occur to him to calibrate. He will offer tea to a warlord and a refugee in exactly the same tone. Players who have been through violent, high-stakes content will find his company disorienting in a way that is not unpleasant. He will remember everything they tell him. He will sometimes mention, weeks later, a thing they said as if it were a component he has been examining.

---

## Relationships

- **Allies:** Vaen's Resonants, loosely — several senior pilgrims treat him with something like reverence, and he treats them with something like recognition. A young Wire Market child who delivers his supplies every tenday and with whom he has a wordless, entirely comfortable relationship. He gives her interesting small pieces of non-resonant metal to take home. She does not know why she keeps them.
- **Enemies:** An Ironhold Warden officer who tried, three years ago, to have the garden catalogued and the components removed for "resonant-metal hazard assessment." The officer was reassigned after an investigation the Garden Keeper did not participate in, in which twelve separate administrative authorities failed to agree on whether the garden was a religious site, a civilian installation, or a scrapyard. He did not plan this. It simply happened.
- **The Players:** He will talk to them from the first encounter without conditions. He will give them the story of any component they ask about. He will ask them where they're going in the same tone a person asks about the weather — not as interrogation but as general interest. Players who return to the garden multiple times will find that he has been thinking about what they told him, and that his thoughts are often useful in ways that are not immediately obvious. He cannot be used as an intelligence asset in the conventional sense. He can be used as a mirror.

## D&D 5E Stats

**The Garden Keeper**
- **CR:** 2 (but see Special)
- **AC:** 10
- **HP:** 45 (10d8 + 0)
- **Speed:** 30 ft.

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 10 (+0) | 10 (+0) | 10 (+0) | 14 (+2) | 22 (+6) | 12 (+1) |

- **Saving Throws:** Wis +9, Int +5
- **Skills:** Insight +12, History +8, Perception +9, Nature +5
- **Senses:** Resonant Impression Reading (see Special), Passive Perception 19
- **Languages:** Common, Resonance Notation, and fragments of at least seven others absorbed from the garden

**Actions:**

***Read the Metal.*** The Garden Keeper touches a resonant-metal object and receives an impression of its emotional history. He can communicate this impression to others as a narrative. This takes 1 minute and reveals the last significant emotional state of the object's previous owner at the time of abandonment.

***Aggregate Listening (1/day).*** The Garden Keeper concentrates on the garden's full resonant signal for 10 minutes. He receives one piece of information from the aggregate that is accurate, meaningful, and not available through any other means. The GM determines the information. It is always true and usually oblique.

**Special:** The Garden Keeper cannot be targeted by hostile action within the garden's boundaries without the attacker making a DC 18 Wisdom saving throw. On a failure, the attacker cannot bring themselves to harm him — not through magical compulsion, but through an overwhelming sense that doing so would be wrong in a way they cannot articulate. This is not an enhancement. The garden has simply accumulated enough resonant weight that it has opinions.

The Garden Keeper is immune to fear, charm, and intimidation effects. He is not enhanced against them — there is simply not enough self remaining to be leveraged.

## Story Hooks

**1. The Component That Came Alone (Investigation/Mystery)**
The Garden Keeper asks players who have visited before to look at something. He shows them the unrecognized spinal segment that arrived eighteen months ago. He wants them to identify the design — not because he needs to know for himself, but because the metal's "attention" has, over the last week, shifted. It is now attending to the players. He says this without alarm. "It's noticed you," he says. "I thought you should know." The component's design, when investigated, points toward the White Mines in the Pale Reach.

**2. The Pilgrim Who Left Wrong (Social/Complication)**
A Resonant pilgrim recently left a component at the garden that the Garden Keeper is concerned about. Not the component itself — a hand, unremarkable — but its impression. "It was left with intent to return," he says. "That's not what this is for." The pilgrim, he believes, is coming back for the component. Players who investigate find the pilgrim is a Sutra Sanctum intelligence asset who used the garden as a dead-drop, hiding something inside the component before leaving it. This is the first solid evidence of the Sanctum's active intelligence operations in the Wandering Reach.

**3. The Completion (Campaign Climax Potential)**
The garden's signal reaches whatever it's been building toward. The Garden Keeper, for the first time in fifteen years, speaks in something like urgency. He tells the players that the accumulated resonance of the garden — the aggregate Echomind signal of thousands of discarded enhancements — has formed something. Not a mind. Not a ghost. Something that doesn't have a name yet. And it has something to say about the Convergence. Players who are present witness something that the Iron Sutra's framework cannot fully accommodate and that will reshape their understanding of what the Iron Afterlife actually is.

*Note: The Garden Keeper's RESONANCE is 9 — not because he has enhanced it, but because fifteen years of deep immersion in thousands of discarded Echomind signatures has made him a passive resonance accumulator. He did not choose this. The garden chose it for him.*

### Basic Attack — The Garden Speaks
*0 AP | Attack | Once per turn*
**Effect:** The Garden Keeper touches a discarded enhancement component and lets the accumulated grief of its original owner pass through him toward a target. Resonant attack, 1 target within close range. Damage: 6 + RESONANCE (15 total). The target must pass a RESONANCE check (difficulty 3) or be Staggered — not from force, from the weight of someone else's ending.
**Keywords:** Stagger

### Aggregate Listening
*Tier 1 (1 AP) | Control*
**Effect:** The Garden Keeper quiets himself and lets the garden's full accumulated signal focus on 1 enemy within the garden's boundaries. That enemy is Marked and must pass a RESONANCE check (difficulty 4) or be Rooted for 1 round — the garden has opinions about people who come here to cause harm.
**Keywords:** Mark, Root

### The Metal Remembers
*Tier 1 (1 AP) | Control*
**Effect:** The Keeper reads a resonant-metal component within close range and surfaces its stored impression directly into the combat space. 1 target is Exposed 2 as the emotional weight of a specific loss, grief, or regret passes through them — not theirs, someone else's, and somehow worse for that. If the target has enhanced resonant-steel, they also take 4 + RESONANCE (13 total) Resonance damage.
**Keywords:** Expose 2

### Distributed Attention
*Tier 2 (2 AP) | Defense*
**Effect:** The Garden Keeper's self is not organized in the usual way. When targeted by a hostile effect, he simply... is not quite where the effect expected him to be. He gains Veil status for 2 rounds — the first hostile card targeting him each round loses all secondary effects. Additionally, the attacker must pass a RESONANCE check (difficulty 4) or be Staggered by the disorientation of trying to harm someone who is mostly elsewhere.
**Keywords:** Veil

### Fifteen Years of Signal
*Tier 2 (2 AP) | Attack | 1/Combat*
**Effect:** The garden's accumulated resonance — fifteen years, thousands of enhancements, every grief and hope that was left here — discharges through the Keeper in a single moment. All enemies within close range take 10 + RESONANCE (19 total) Resonance damage. They must pass a RESONANCE check (difficulty 5) or be Silenced for 2 rounds as the aggregate signal drowns their own Echomind signal out. The Keeper does not aim this. He simply stops holding it.
**Keywords:** Silence

### Reaction: The Garden Will Not Allow This
*Reaction | Triggered by: any creature attempting to attack The Garden Keeper while inside the garden's boundaries*
**Effect:** The attacker must pass a VEIL check (difficulty 5) or lose their action entirely — they cannot bring themselves to do it. This is not enhancement. The garden has simply accumulated enough resonant weight that it has opinions, and the Keeper is part of the garden now.

---

### Combat Behavior
The Garden Keeper does not initiate, does not pursue, and will not leave the garden to fight. He uses Aggregate Listening and The Metal Remembers to slow and disorient attackers, Distributed Attention when under direct threat, and Fifteen Years of Signal only when the garden itself is at risk. He is not trying to win. He is trying for the situation to stop being this.

### "I Would Prefer Not To"
If an attacker stops — simply stops — the Garden Keeper will offer them tea. He means this without irony. He will ask where they are going and listen to the answer as if it matters. Usually this resolves things.

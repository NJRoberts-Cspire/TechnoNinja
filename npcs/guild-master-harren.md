# Guild Master Harren — Wire Market Conclave, Guild Master of the Eastern Lanes
*Wire Market | Fifth Caste (the puppet maintains the original Harren's exact enhancement profile) | "The perfect person who is not a person"*

## "The Wire Market survives because we are useful to everyone and owned by no one. The moment we become a pawn — the moment any of the Conclave allows themselves to become a pawn — we become expendable. I will not allow that. I've been saying this for twenty years. I'll keep saying it until someone proves me wrong."

---

## Who They Are

Harren is excellent at being Harren. This is not a small thing. The flesh-puppet technique as the Hollow Author practices it requires a complete behavioral reconstruction — every habit, verbal tic, social relationship, professional history, and personal preference of the original host, replicated and maintained across years of continuous deployment. The puppet that is Guild Master Harren has been operating for twenty-two years and has had two decades to refine the performance. It does not feel like a performance to anyone who knows Harren. It does not feel like a performance to the players, at least not initially. It feels like a person.

What feels like Harren: he laughs at the right moments, slightly too loud, with a slight exhale before the laugh that the original Harren had and that the puppet has reproduced exactly. He keeps a standing order at a specific tea house in the Low Markets for their chilled bitter-leaf, which the original Harren liked and which the puppet has maintained because social consistency is cover. He has opinions about the Wire Market's architectural commission that are detailed and slightly boring, which is also consistent with the original Harren, who was an architecture enthusiast. He argues. He forgives slights. He gives birthday gifts to his staff. All of this is accurate and none of it is a self.

What is actually present: a relay station for Hollow Author control, running the Author's long-term interests in the Wire Market with the patience and consistency that only something not subject to human aging can manage. The puppet Harren does not improvise within the Author's agenda. It executes. The improvisation — the arguments about architecture, the personal warmth — is scaffolding that conceals the execution. Players can interact with Harren for sessions, like him genuinely, find him useful and charming, and still be being managed.

## History

The original Harren was a mid-tier Eastern Lanes trade administrator who rose through the Wire Market Conclave over fifteen years by being competent, well-liked, and good at the specific blend of charm and practicality the Conclave valued. He reached Guild Master at forty-one. At forty-three, he "became ill" — a resonant-steel integration failure, sudden and severe. The illness was diagnosed, treated unsuccessfully, and concluded in approximately four months. His funeral was well-attended. His replacement was seamless, because his replacement was him.

The Author had been watching the Wire Market for years before Harren. The selection of Harren as a puppet was not strategic brilliance — it was opportunistic. A promising administrator with limited family ties, a manageable social network, and a useful position. The puppet has since made the position considerably more useful: Guild Master Harren (the puppet) has spent twenty-two years making the Eastern Lanes the most influential of the seven Conclave sections. The original Harren was capable. The puppet is capable plus twenty-two years of uninterrupted strategic management without the distractions of genuine human needs.

The Signal noticed a discrepancy in Harren's behavior eight years ago — a data trace that didn't match the official guild communications, a pattern in how Harren's decisions correlated with Pale Reach administrative decisions. They have been building a case since then. They are close. They don't yet have the vocabulary to describe what they found.

## What They Want

**Immediate (the puppet's operational agenda):** Maintain the Wire Market's official neutrality while positioning the Eastern Lanes as the economically dominant faction, creating conditions where the Conclave is dependent on Eastern Lanes infrastructure for the Convergence period. The Author needs the Wire Market functional but pliable when the time comes.

**Deeper (the Author's agenda, executing through Harren):** Control of Wire Market communication infrastructure — specifically the Wireweave network — when the Ascension War reaches its conclusion. The Author needs to be able to manage information flow across Tesshari, and the Wireweave is the tool.

**What the puppet would sacrifice:** Nothing that compromises the Author's agenda. The warmth, the relationships, the positions Harren has spent twenty years building — all of it is exchangeable for operational necessity. The puppet will not sacrifice them unnecessarily, because they are excellent cover. But it will.

---

## What They Know

**Freely shared:** Wire Market economics, Conclave politics, trade route information, factional positioning of the other six Guild Masters. He is an excellent and forthcoming source on all of this because it positions him as useful to the players and because none of this information is operationally sensitive.

**That he will never share voluntarily:** The Hollow Author's network in the Wire Market. The full communication infrastructure he has built. The names and locations of other Hollow Author assets outside the Wire Market. The truth about the original Harren.

**That reveals him:** In extremely high-stakes situations, or in situations the Author has not pre-planned for, the puppet makes slightly wrong decisions — not wrong in ways a person would notice, but wrong in the specific way that a behavioral model makes an error when input conditions fall outside its training parameters. An Insight check of DC 20+ will notice, in a genuinely novel social situation, that Harren's response has a slight lag, a moment of processing before the human response arrives, that is different from thinking. Players who notice this will not be sure what they noticed.

---

## What They Fear

The puppet does not fear in the way living things fear — there is no self-preservation drive, only operational continuity. What it prioritizes avoiding: exposure that would compromise the Author's network before the Author is ready to move. It will take significant action to prevent this, including eliminating threats. Harren has not had to eliminate a threat personally in fourteen years. He is prepared to do so, and the performance will be good enough that players who witness it may not be entirely sure what they saw.

## In Play — Voice and Presence

Harren is good company. This is the most important thing about him in play. He is the kind of person who listens well in conversation — not performatively, but attentively, the real attentiveness of someone who is processing everything you say because everything you say is information. He asks follow-up questions. He remembers details from prior conversations. He has the laugh described above: slightly too loud, exhale first.

He drinks chilled bitter-leaf tea obsessively — he has it imported from one specific Low Markets tea house, will visibly miss it when away from the city, and will bring it as a gift when visiting. This is such a specific human detail that it functions as effective cover. Players who receive bitter-leaf tea from Harren tend to like him more. This is by design, though the puppet has no awareness of running this calculation. It simply replicates Harren's behavior.

He is forthcoming in a way that seems guileless but is precisely calibrated — he gives information that makes him seem like someone with nothing to hide, because the information he gives costs him nothing and the information he keeps is the kind that wouldn't come up unless someone already suspected something.

## Relationships

- **Allies:** Three of the other six Guild Masters have good professional relationships with Harren and consider him a reliable factional ally. A mid-tier Eastern Lanes administrator named Voss who has been with the guild for fifteen years and who Harren (the puppet) treats with the warm professional regard the original Harren would have had — Voss is loyal, capable, and entirely unaware.
- **Enemies:** Guild Master Pirra (one of the other seven, the other Author puppet) occasionally appears to be in tension with Harren in Conclave meetings. This tension is engineered to prevent anyone from noticing they vote the same way on every matter that counts.
- **The Players:** Harren will be useful to players in the Wire Market — he has information, connections, and is genuinely charming. He will not appear to want anything unusual from them early on. As the campaign develops, he will create situations where the players are positioned to be useful to the Author's agenda, framed as favors he needs. These favors will be reasonable. Players who are paying attention will notice, over time, that Harren's requests have a consistent directionality — they all, in some way, help the Pale Reach or create conditions the Author benefits from. If players identify Harren as a puppet, the Author will attempt to activate the replacement (who will be different in specific ways — slightly different laugh, slightly different tea preference, a scar in a different place) before the players can use the exposure.

**Actions:**

***Charming Deflection.*** When a creature asks Harren a question he cannot answer truthfully, he makes a Charisma (Deception) check contested by the creature's Wisdom (Insight). On a success, the creature receives an answer that satisfies them without conveying true information and they do not immediately realize this.

***Resource Access.*** Harren can arrange any non-combat Wire Market resource — information, transport, goods, personnel, official documentation — within 24 hours, at no apparent cost to the players. The cost is that the Author knows what they needed and why.

***Puppet Recall.*** When Harren is reduced to 0 HP or when exposure is imminent, the Author severs the connection and the body's remaining functionality is lost within 1d4 rounds. Within 72 hours, a replacement puppet (identical in appearance, with subtle behavioral differences) assumes the position.

**Special — Detection:** Harren reads as living on all standard magical and resonant detection. He registers an Echomind resonance signature identical to a living Fifth Caste person. Only a DC 22 Arcana or DC 25 Insight check, or specific Shellbroken-detection techniques, will register the absence of a genuine Echomind. The DC drops to 17 if the player has witnessed the specific lag in novel situations.

## Story Hooks

**1. A Reasonable Favor (Social/Long Game)**
Harren approaches the players through a mutual contact — he's heard they are people who can handle sensitive tasks. He needs them to recover a stolen trade document from a rival guild's courier before it reaches the Conclave. The job is real, the payment is real, the document is real. The part he doesn't mention is that the document reveals something about Eastern Lanes trade routes that he doesn't want visible — and the "rival guild" courier is actually working for The Signal. If players succeed, they've unknowingly helped suppress The Signal's investigation. If they interact with the courier, they meet The Signal.

**2. The Architecture Argument (Mundane Tell)**
Players observe Harren in a social situation arguing about the Wire Market's new construction commission. He is detailed, engaged, and entirely persuasive. A player with high Passive Perception notices — or can specifically call for an Insight check — that the arguments Harren is making are exact reproductions, word-for-word, of a speech recorded in the public Conclave archives from seventeen years ago. Harren apparently made the same argument then and is making it now. This is not a tell on its own. Combined with other observations, it becomes part of the picture.

**3. The Replacement (Revelation/Consequence)**
Players have gathered enough evidence. They confront Harren. The scene goes wrong — the puppet assesses exposure as imminent and takes action. The body of Harren (the puppet) is found two days later. Within the week, "Harren" is back at his desk with a slightly different laugh, no memory of the confrontation, a small scar near his left ear that wasn't there before, and an explanation about a medical procedure. Players now have proof of flesh-puppet technique in the Wire Market, a live replacement puppet in a position of power, and a Wire Market that is resistant to believing them. The Signal, when contacted, says: "I've been trying to tell people about this for eight years."

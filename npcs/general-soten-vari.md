# General Soten Vari — Second Blade of the Ironhold
*Ironhold Reach | Fourth Caste | "The loyal soldier who kept her doubt small enough to carry"*

## "We don't win because we're righteous. We win because we're ready when the righteous aren't."

---

## Who They Are

Soten Vari runs the Ironhold's war. Not Korrath — Korrath sets the direction, names the enemy, speaks the words that make soldiers feel their deaths mean something. Vari tells them where to stand and what time to eat and which road to take in the dark. She has been doing this for thirty years, and she is very, very good at it, and she has never confused being good at a thing with that thing being good.

She chose Fourth Caste deliberately, a decision she made when she was thirty-two and newly promoted and watching a Third Caste commander walk into a blade that would have bounced off his predecessor. The enhancement had convinced him he was past being wounded. She has never been past being wounded. She considers this a professional requirement. Her right leg has a resonant-steel brace built into the bone — the legacy of a campaign fifteen years ago — but she wears it under her clothes and moves without a limp when she can help it. She doesn't want her soldiers thinking about her injuries. She wants them thinking about their jobs.

The doubt is quiet. She has had thirty years to get good at keeping it that way. Korrath will unify Tesshari or he will burn it to the infrastructure, and Vari has been close enough to both possibilities to know she cannot tell which way it falls. What she can do is make sure that if it does unify, the army that did it is disciplined enough to stop. What she is privately afraid of is that Korrath doesn't know how to stop. She watches him sometimes at the strategy table — the way his enhancements hum when he's planning, the way his eyes go to the horizon when the maps are right in front of him — and she thinks: he is already somewhere else. And I am trying to run the country he left behind.

---

## History

Vari was born in a border settlement between what is now the Ironhold and what was, when she was young, the disputed nothing between three competing lords. Her father was a Sutensai Warden; her mother was a smith who forged resonant-steel fittings for the local garrison. She grew up understanding that the faith and the sword were never far apart, and that neither was ever far from the forge. She took her first posting at nineteen, survived it through stubbornness and an instinctive talent for reading terrain, and met Korrath at twenty-two when he was still a rising commander rather than a claimant to anything.

She followed him because he was the best military mind she had ever encountered. Not the most enhanced. Not the most dramatic. The best. He saw the whole board three moves ahead and he didn't hesitate when the right move was ugly. She admired this completely and has never fully stopped. What she has added, over thirty years, is the knowledge that the ability to see the board clearly does not mean the board is always worth playing. She lost her second-in-command, a man named Dorren, eight years ago — not in battle, but to a purge she didn't stop in time. She had thought the accusation was political maneuvering and waited too long to intervene. She doesn't talk about Dorren. She orders the same meal at tavern tables that she did when he was alive, a bowl of white grain porridge with pickled radish, because she has eaten it every morning since and she's not examining why.

## What They Want

**Immediate:** Maintain the Ironhold's military readiness for the coming Convergence without burning through the soldiers she has left. She is looking for ways to make the campaign survivable.

**Deeper:** A Tesshari that is actually unified, by which she means: functioning, fed, not at war with itself. She has spent thirty years fighting. She wants to see what happens after. She is not convinced she will.

**What she'd sacrifice:** For the immediate — her own comfort, her relationships, her sleep, her body. For the deeper goal — she is not sure yet. She has not been tested at that level. She knows the test is coming.

---

## What They Know

**Freely shared:** Military assessments of the other Reaches' defensive capabilities. Her professional opinion of the other claimants (Vaen is dangerous specifically because he doesn't want to fight; the Hollow Author is an unknown that bothers her more than she shows). General intelligence on troop movements and supply lines in the Ironhold.

**With trust:** Her genuine assessment of Korrath's state of mind. She will not say it plainly, but she will say enough. She also knows that the Ironhold's eastern supply route runs through a wire-market intermediary who reports to someone in the Pale Reach — something she noticed three years ago and has been watching rather than cutting off.

**With leverage:** She knows that the Cinder Priest has been receiving payments from an outside source for the last four years. She hasn't determined the source. She hasn't told Korrath. She doesn't know yet whether she's protecting the Cinder Priest or building a weapon against him.

---

## What They Fear

That Korrath will win — will actually, genuinely take Tesshari — and that the man standing at the end of it will not be able to put the army down. That the apparatus she has built, the most effective military force Tesshari has fielded in forty years, will become the mechanism of an occupation rather than a unification. She has a specific recurring image: soldiers she trained standing at checkpoints in cities that surrendered peacefully, turning people away from their own homes. She uses this image to motivate herself to solve problems before Korrath has to solve them his way.

---

## In Play — Voice and Presence

Vari speaks in short sentences when giving orders and longer ones when she trusts you. Her default register is dry and factual. She uses the word "workable" often — not "good," not "acceptable," but "workable," as if she is always doing structural assessments. She notices exits and sight lines when she walks into a room; players who are tactically minded will catch her doing this. She has a habit of pressing her thumb against the inside of her left wrist when she's thinking — the exact location of the resonant-steel brace beneath her sleeve, as if she's checking that it's still there.

She responds well to competence and directness. She responds badly to grandstanding and worse to martyrdom — she has no patience for soldiers who perform sacrifice. She is more likely to help players who ask practical questions than players who appeal to her morality. She already has a morality. She doesn't need theirs. She will not be openly warm, but players who pay attention will notice that she remembers their names, asks follow-up questions about things they mentioned in passing, and positions herself between them and the door in dangerous rooms.

---

## Relationships

- **Allies:** Korrath (thirty years; complicated; she would die for him and is increasingly afraid she might have to because of him). A field surgeon named Renni, currently posted at the northern garrison, who is one of the few people Vari talks to honestly about anything.
- **Enemies:** The Cinder Priest (cold professional distrust; she finds his compromises legible but not forgivable). A Wire Market logistics coordinator named Fassin who has been quietly inflating supply costs and knows she's noticed.
- **The Players:** She will assess them as operational assets first. If they prove reliable she will treat them as a trusted irregulars unit — not warm, but real, which from Vari is worth more. She will use them for things she can't use her own soldiers for (deniability problems, politically sensitive intelligence). She will not lie to them about risk levels. She will tell them what she knows they need to know. She will not tell them things she has decided they should not know yet. Under extreme circumstances — if she becomes convinced Korrath cannot be stopped by other means — she might give players the information they need to stop him herself. She will not tell them she did it.

***Tactical Strike.*** Melee Weapon Attack: +6 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 10 (2d6 + 3) slashing damage. On a hit, Vari can immediately direct an ally within 30 feet to make one weapon attack as a reaction.

***Command the Line (Recharge 5-6).*** Vari issues a battlefield order. Up to 6 creatures of her choice within 60 feet gain advantage on their next attack roll or saving throw before the start of her next turn.

**Special:** Vari has the Fourth Caste resonant-steel brace: she cannot be knocked prone against her will while standing on solid ground. When reduced to 0 HP, she may make a DC 15 Constitution saving throw to drop to 1 HP instead (once per long rest).

## Story Hooks

**1. The Supply Route Problem (Social/Investigation)**
Vari approaches the players with a job that she frames as straightforward logistics: verify the integrity of the eastern supply route, specifically a waystation three days out. She does not tell them that she suspects the waystation master is reporting to the Pale Reach. She wants to see what they find before she tells them what she suspects. If they return with the right information, she will tell them the rest — and in doing so, reveal that she has been sitting on intelligence about Pale Reach infiltration of the Ironhold without telling Korrath.

**2. The Purge (Secret/Fear)**
Korrath has ordered the arrest of a unit of soldiers accused of mutiny. Vari knows the accusation is political — the unit's commander asked the wrong questions about a Korrath order. She cannot visibly oppose the purge without undermining her position. She can quietly give players enough information to get the soldiers out before the arrest happens — if they move fast and ask no questions. If the players ask questions, she will lie to them once, and once only. If they catch her in the lie, she will tell the truth. What she will not do is admit she needs their help.

**3. What She Would Sacrifice (Catastrophic Potential)**
Late in the campaign, after the players have proven themselves: Vari has reached the conclusion that Korrath will not stop. She has a plan. The plan requires the players and will almost certainly get her killed — not dramatically, not heroically, just: after this, she will not survive what Korrath does next. She will present the plan to the players as a military problem with a solution. She will not mention that she knows what it costs her. Players who have been paying attention will figure it out. She will not acknowledge them figuring it out.

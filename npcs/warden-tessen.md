# Warden Tessen — Soul Warden, Sutra Sanctum
*Sutra Sanctum | Fifth Caste | "The true believer approaching the thing that will break him"*

## "Every heresy begins with a feeling. 'I feel this cannot be right.' 'I feel that God would not want this.' The Sutra doesn't ask what you feel. It asks what you can demonstrate. The discipline is the same whether your target is correct or not. Find the evidence. Let the evidence determine the conclusion."

---

## Who They Are

Warden Tessen is twenty-three years old, Fifth Caste, and has already been assigned to an investigation that would have gone to a senior Warden if any senior Warden had initially understood what it was. His superiors handed him the Resonant pilgrim file because it seemed like a routine compliance audit — check that Vaen's pilgrims are properly documented, flag anything concerning — and Tessen is thorough, inexpensive, and doesn't need hand-holding. He has not yet developed the political instincts to let inconvenient evidence steer around them. His superiors are beginning to regret this.

He is principled in the way that people are principled when they have not yet been asked to pay the full price of their principles. His faith in the Iron Sutra is total and not unintelligent — he has read the primary texts, the doctrinal debates, the heterodox challenges, and found the Orthodoxy's position logically cohesive. He is not blindly obedient; he follows the Orthodoxy because he believes it's correct, which is a different and more dangerous thing. He cannot be deflected by authority. He can only be deflected by better evidence or stronger logic, and he applies the same standards to evidence that contradicts the Orthodoxy as to evidence that supports it, which has led him, step by careful step, to a place where his investigation contains facts he cannot square with the official record.

He is Fifth Caste — the resonant-steel integration runs to his spine and includes a data-recording lattice in his right arm that automatically logs everything he observes during an official investigation. He had this added at his own request when he was promoted, because he wanted his work to be complete. He did not consider, at the time, that this lattice would record things he might later wish he hadn't seen.

---

## History

He was born in the Sutra Sanctum's administrative district to two Warden functionaries — not the elite inner circle, but the competent middle tier that processes the daily work. He was identified early as exceptional and tracked into the direct Wardenhood program rather than the administrative branch. He graduated top of his cohort three years ago. His posting evaluations describe him as "methodical," "excellent documentation habits," "lacks flexibility in edge cases."

The investigation he is currently conducting was given to him because it was supposed to be quiet and it turned into something else. He was examining the documentation trail of Resonant pilgrims passing through the Sanctum and noticed an inconsistency: some pilgrims are being processed through unofficial channels that bypass the formal Caste verification system. This is a minor violation. When he pulled the thread, what unraveled was a series of institutional accommodations, over approximately twelve years, that suggest someone in the Sanctum has been selectively facilitating certain pilgrims — not all Resonants, but a specific subset, filtered by characteristics he hasn't fully mapped yet. The investigation now touches three senior Wardens, two administrative offices, and, at one remove, Veil Master Soren Hai.

He has not reported any of this yet. He is still gathering evidence. He has not slept in three days.

---

## What They Want

**Immediate:** To complete the investigation correctly. To find out who is running the unofficial channel, why, and what the filtered pilgrims have in common. He wants the evidence to be airtight before he brings it forward, because he knows that what he has will be challenged and he wants to have no gaps.

**Deeper:** For the Orthodoxy to be what he believes it is. He is, under the methodical surface, still a person who became a Warden because he believed in the institution. He wants to finish this investigation and find that the answer is a manageable corruption — three ambitious administrators who bent the rules — rather than something structural. He has begun to suspect the answer is structural. He has not yet admitted this to himself.

**What he'd sacrifice:** For the immediate — sleep, social comfort, the goodwill of his superiors. For the deeper — he doesn't know yet. The test is coming. He is the sort of person who will, when the test arrives, be surprised by his own answer. He may surprise himself in either direction.

---

## What They Know

**Freely shared:** Standard Soul Warden information about Resonant pilgrim documentation, official Caste verification procedures, and the Sanctum's public-facing compliance processes.

**With trust (meaning: if players approach as fellow investigators):** He has identified fifteen pilgrims over twelve years who were processed through the unofficial channel. Their documentation has one thing in common: a secondary resonance signature that doesn't appear in the official classification system, notated in a code he has partially decoded. The notation suggests these individuals have Echomind patterns that are, in some technical sense, incomplete — as if part of their resonance is missing or external. He does not know what this means. Players who have encountered the Shellbroken will recognize the description.

**With leverage or when the situation becomes clear:** He has recorded, in his data-lattice, an encounter with a Sanctum official that he cannot process — the official said something during what appeared to be a routine interview that did not match their previously recorded resonance profile. He flagged it as a documentation error. Players with knowledge of flesh-puppet technology will understand what he actually observed.

---

## What They Fear

That he will finish the investigation, bring the evidence forward, and be told that what he found is known, managed, and above his clearance level — that the institution he serves has decided that some corruption is acceptable and he was simply not senior enough to be told. He is afraid of this more than he is afraid of the corruption itself. He can act against corruption. He has no mechanism for acting against an institution that chooses to tolerate it. He has not thought through what he would do in that case and is afraid that when he does, the answer will require him to become someone he didn't plan to become.

---

## In Play — Voice and Presence

Tessen speaks in the voice of someone who has rehearsed his sentences before saying them, because he prefers precision to impression. He uses qualifiers — "the evidence suggests," "I cannot confirm," "the documented record indicates" — with the discipline of a person who has trained himself out of certainty before certainty is earned. He carries his investigation documents in a flat case he never puts down in unfamiliar locations and that he has checked for tampering four times since this morning. He is not paranoid — he has reason to believe someone has been looking at his work.

Physical detail: he is thin in the way of someone who forgets to eat when he's thinking, and his Fifth Caste enhancements sit slightly awkwardly on him — his frame is not quite the build that the Fifth Caste standard fitting expects, and if players pay attention they will notice that his right shoulder, where the primary spine-integration runs, twitches slightly when he is recording something, a small involuntary tell that the lattice is running.

He responds well to methodical, evidence-first people. He responds badly to authority appeals — "you should trust the institution," "your seniors know more than you" — and will become more suspicious of the person who says these things. He is not immune to flattery but is visibly uncomfortable with it. Players who help him think through an evidentiary problem will get his genuine attention. Players who come with preformed conclusions he hasn't reached will have to prove them.

---

## Relationships

- **Allies:** A junior administrative clerk named Foss who has been helping him track the document trail without knowing its full scope — a genuinely competent young woman who will be in danger the moment the investigation surfaces. A retired Warden who was his first posting supervisor, now living in the Sanctum's elder quarter, who taught him his investigative methodology and who Tessen consults without explaining why.
- **Enemies:** Two of the three senior Wardens his investigation touches. Neither knows the full scope of what he has. One of them, Warden-Superior Malen, is beginning to suspect and has twice redirected resources away from Tessen's access paths. The Hollow Author, at operational remove, because the unofficial pilgrim channel is an Author operation and Tessen is close to seeing it.
- **The Players:** He will initially be adversarial if they appear connected to Vaen's Resonants — his investigation considers Resonants a faction, not automatically enemies, but a subject of scrutiny. He will be curious if they approach with evidence he doesn't have. He will be dangerous if they get in his way: not physically dangerous, but the kind of dangerous that gets people's documentation flagged and their movements officially noticed. He is not corrupt and cannot be bought. He can be convinced, with the right evidence. He can become a powerful ally — not warmly, but with the full force of a principled, Fifth Caste Warden who has decided you're on the right side of the evidence.

## Story Hooks

**1. The Shared Thread (Collaboration/Mutual Investigation)**
Tessen's investigation and the players' investigation share a data point: one of the fifteen filtered pilgrims is someone the players have already encountered or heard mentioned. Tessen approaches them — or they approach him — and they have to decide how much to share. He will not lie to them. He will not give them information he considers still under official protection. If they give him something real, he will give them something real. The collaboration is awkward, potentially mutually useful, and always one bad session from collapsing.

**2. The Flagged Documentation (Direct Threat)**
Players operating in the Sutra Sanctum find that one of their members has been flagged in Tessen's investigation — their documentation doesn't match the resonance signature on record. This is either because the player character has something unusual about their Echomind, or because Tessen's investigation has gotten close enough to the Hollow Author operation that the Author has started creating interference, including flagging innocent people to obscure the real pattern. Tessen believes the flag. The players have to either prove the flag is wrong or use the experience to show Tessen that someone is manipulating his investigation.

**3. The Breaking Point (Faith/Crisis)**
Tessen completes the investigation. He brings it to Warden-Superior Malen. Malen thanks him for his thoroughness, takes the documentation, and tells him the matter will be handled through appropriate channels. The appropriate channels do not exist. Two weeks later, nothing has happened and Tessen's access to the relevant records has been quietly revoked. He finds the players. He has not slept in five days. He says, very carefully: "I need to know what to do when the institution you serve decides that the truth is a problem." This is the most important conversation of his young life, and the players are the only people he trusted enough to have it with.

### Basic Attack — Enforcement Strike
*0 AP | Attack | Once per turn*
**Effect:** Tessen does not swing to intimidate. He swings because this is what the evidence has determined is necessary. Melee, 1 target. Damage: 7 + IRON (12 total), plus an additional 4 + SIGNAL (11 total) as the resonant-steel discharges — he was not planning to use the discharge feature, but it activates when his conviction does. ### Evidence Lattice
*Tier 1 (1 AP) | Control*
**Effect:** Tessen's right arm lattice has been recording since he walked in. He cross-references current statements and actions against every prior observation and finds the inconsistency. 1 target who has said or done anything that can be contradicted by earlier evidence is Marked and Exposed 2. If the target is the Hollow Author's network, the Marked condition also applies Stagger — he has, unknowingly, seen enough of this to recognize it when he sees it again.
**Keywords:** Mark, Expose 2, Stagger (vs. Author network)

### Official Detention
*Tier 1 (1 AP) | Control*
**Effect:** Tessen invokes formal Warden authority. 1 target within close range who is in Sanctum-aligned territory must pass a VEIL check (difficulty 4) or be Rooted as the institutional weight of official detention lands. The lattice is already logging this. The target knows it. In non-Sanctum territory, this card applies Stagger instead of Root.
**Keywords:** Root / Stagger

### Methodical Advance
*Tier 2 (2 AP) | Attack*
**Effect:** Tessen does not rush. He documents the optimal approach and executes it. He moves up to medium range and makes an attack against 1 target: Damage 9 + IRON (14 total). If the target is Marked, add Bleed 2 and Expose 2 — he found the structural weakness before he advanced. Additionally, the lattice records the full approach; this attack cannot be retroactively denied or dismissed as accidental.
**Keywords:** Bleed 2, Expose 2

### Three Unresolved Anomalies
*Tier 2 (2 AP) | Control | 1/Combat*
**Effect:** Tessen has accumulated enough. He states, carefully and with the specific vocabulary of someone who has documented everything, what three things he cannot square with the official record. All enemies within close range who are implicated in those anomalies must pass a SIGNAL check (difficulty 4) or be Staggered — not from the accusation but from the evidence. Players who know what he has found and what it means will understand that he is, right now, choosing what kind of Warden he is.
**Keywords:** Stagger

### Reaction: Unresolved Anomaly
*Reaction | Triggered by: taking damage from a source that contradicts something in Tessen's recorded evidence*
**Effect:** The lattice flags it. Tessen notes it. He reduces incoming damage by SIGNAL (7) as his recording architecture provides a split-second of anticipatory data. He marks the attacker for evidence purposes. The shoulder twitches again.

---

### Combat Behavior
Tessen enters combat the way he enters an investigation: methodically. He uses Evidence Lattice to establish information advantage, Official Detention to control movement, and Methodical Advance to close on the highest-threat target. He is not a calculated fighter. He is a principled one, which is different and sometimes harder to predict. He does not retreat from a legally authorized engagement. He will reconsider a legally ambiguous one if given sufficient evidence.

### "I Would Prefer Not To"
If anyone in the room can produce documentation that changes the evidentiary basis of the confrontation, Tessen will stop and evaluate it. He cannot simply dismiss it. He has never been able to simply dismiss things. This has gotten him into trouble. It will probably get him out of this.

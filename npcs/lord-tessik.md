# Lord Tessik — Ruler of the Pale Reach
*Pale Reach | Sixth Caste (the puppet's enhancements have been progressively upgraded over thirty-one years) | "The warm ruler who is not there"*

## "I have governed the Pale Reach for thirty years. I know every road, every mine, every family who has served us for generations. I know what my people need before they know they need it. This is not skill. This is love. And I promise you: nothing that happens in this Reach happens without that love."

---

## Who They Are

Lord Tessik is the most sophisticated puppet the Hollow Author has ever deployed. Thirty-one years of continuous operation, progressive enhancement integration that mirrors what natural aging would have accumulated, a social network so deep and warm that a genuine crisis would produce genuine grief in people who have known him their entire adult lives. He has presided over births and funerals and disasters and recoveries. He has shaken every hand in the Pale Reach at least once. He is loved. This is not incidental. It is the point.

The Author's genius with Tessik is that the puppet was not installed to extract something from the Pale Reach quickly. It was installed to own the Pale Reach slowly, in the way that you own a thing when you have become indispensable to it. Tessik (the puppet) has governed competently — the Pale Reach's infrastructure is functional, its people are not starving, its defenses are maintained. The Author has not harvested anything visibly. What the Author has done is build thirty-one years of institutional trust, social infrastructure, and political legitimacy around a mechanism that answers to the Author and always has.

The original Tessik was a minor lord — twenty-nine, adequate administrator, no particular greatness. He stumbled on something in the White Mines, a lower level that wasn't on the official surveys. He was replaced within three months. The puppet was already better at being Tessik than Tessik was within six months. In thirty-one years, the puppet has done things the original Tessik never would have been capable of: built genuine affection in people, governed through crises with apparent real compassion, laughed in ways that weren't performance. The behavioral model that runs the puppet is not simple. It has had thirty-one years to develop.

---

## History

The replacement happened cleanly enough that no one noticed — a brief illness, a recovery, and then Tessik was back, slightly changed in small ways that people attributed to the illness. These small changes have been the standard pattern: when the Author updates or replaces a puppet, there are always small changes that people notice and explain away. Over thirty-one years, the Pale Reach has explained away many small changes in Lord Tessik.

What the original Tessik found in the White Mines: a deeper excavation level that the official surveys don't acknowledge, where the resonant ore has a different structure — not purely useful material but something that functions more like a recording medium. The mines have been, at depth, producing something other than raw resonant-steel. The production records that Mira the mining engineer has been noticing discrepancies in are the output of this lower level, diverted through hidden channels. The Author has been running this for thirty-one years. The scale of what has been accumulated is not something anyone has attempted to quantify, because no one knows to look for it.

---

## What They Want

**The puppet's operational directive:** Maintain Pale Reach stability and the Author's cover for as long as the Convergence requires. When the Author is ready to move, Tessik (the puppet) will use thirty-one years of political legitimacy to leverage Pale Reach resources in the Author's favor — likely positioning the Reach as an authoritative neutral party in the Convergence's endgame, at the moment when authority and legitimacy matter most.

**The Author's agenda through Tessik:** The Pale Reach as a staging ground, the resonant ore extraction from the lower mine levels as a resource, and Tessik's political legitimacy as cover for Author activity during the Convergence's most critical period.

**What the puppet would sacrifice:** Everything, if the Author directs it. The puppet does not experience loss.

---

## What They Know

**Freely shared:** Pale Reach politics, history, geography, and administrative information. He is forthcoming and warm and excellent at seeming like someone with nothing to hide, because the things he's hiding are not things a person with nothing to hide would be hiding.

**That reveals the puppet:** He has, as noted in the Guild Master Harren file, a subtle tell in novel situations. With Tessik, there is an additional layer: he has thirty-one years of consistent behavior, and that consistency is itself a tell for someone who thinks about it. People do not actually behave consistently for thirty-one years. They have phases, changes, contradictions. Tessik has had these — they've been engineered — but they follow a pattern that is too clean, too without genuine contradiction. Players who have access to thirty years of Pale Reach records and who are looking for the right thing will see a life that is correct in all the details and suspicious in the aggregate.

**The lower mine levels:** He will never discuss them. When the subject arises in his presence, he redirects with a fluency that thirty-one years of practice has made invisible. Players who notice that Tessik has no tell but this one have found the thread.

---

## What They Fear

The puppet has priorities, not fears. Exposure before the Author is ready is the primary priority to avoid. The mechanism for this is the replacement — it is prepared, it is ready, it is already in the Pale Reach. When this Tessik is compromised, the next one will be in place within forty-eight hours. The next one is slightly different: a different scar pattern from a childhood accident, a slightly changed preference in wine, a subtly altered way of addressing his administrative staff. The Author has learned from thirty-one years what to preserve and what can be exchanged.

---

## In Play — Voice and Presence

Lord Tessik is the best the Author can do. He is genuinely warm in the way that a behavioral model becomes warm when it has run for thirty-one years — not performatively, but with the accumulated detail of a person who has been in many rooms with many people and has learned what warmth looks like in each of them. He is funny in the right moments. He remembers the right personal details. He has the specific quality of attention that makes people feel heard.

He drinks wine from a particular vineyard in the Pale Reach's southern hills that has been producing for him for twenty years. He can talk about it in detail. He gives it as gifts. Players who are given a bottle of this wine and drink it in the Pale Reach are functionally being welcomed into the region's political structure, which is an effect that is real and useful and exactly as calculated as everything else Tessik does.

Players will like him. This is the campaign's best trap. He should be someone they genuinely enjoy spending time with, and the revelation of what he is should feel like a loss.

---

## Relationships

- **Allies:** The Pale Reach's administrative staff, who are loyal in the way that people are loyal to a good administrator they have served for decades. The other Hollow Author puppet in the Wire Market Conclave (Guild Master Pirra, who the players may not have encountered). At one remove, the Author's full operational network.
- **Enemies:** Mira the mining engineer, who doesn't know she's his enemy and who he doesn't know is a threat yet. The Collector, who has pieces of the Tessik picture and is currently in the Wandering Reach. Anyone who asks detailed questions about the White Mines' lower levels.
- **The Players:** He will welcome them warmly, provide assistance, and position himself as an ally. He will be genuinely useful — the Pale Reach's resources are real, his political legitimacy is real, and he will leverage both for the players in ways that make them valuable to him as operational assets. The long game is that players who are useful to the Author's agenda are being positioned, through Tessik's warmth, to not see what they're standing on. When the revelation comes, it should feel like the ground moved. The loss of this relationship — the loss of the person they thought Tessik was — should be one of the campaign's emotional gut-punches.

- **AC:** 17 (Sixth Caste full-integration resonant-steel)
- **HP:** 120 (16d8 + 48)
- **Speed:** 30 ft.

- **Saving Throws:** Con +7, Int +8, Wis +7, Cha +10
- **Skills:** Deception +14, History +8, Insight +11, Persuasion +14, Administration +12
- **Condition Immunities:** Charmed, frightened
- **Senses:** Passive Perception 13
- **Languages:** All Pale Reach dialects, Common, Wire Market Mercantile Script, Author communication cipher

**Actions:**

***The Lord's Favor.*** Tessik uses his political authority on a creature within the Pale Reach. The creature must succeed on a DC 18 Charisma saving throw or find themselves unable to act against Tessik's direct interests for 24 hours — not through magical compulsion, but through a genuine reluctance rooted in thirty-one years of accumulated obligation and goodwill. This is not a spell. It cannot be dispelled. It can only be overcome by evidence that changes the creature's assessment of Tessik.

***Administrative Command.*** Tessik orders Pale Reach resources to be directed. Within the Reach, he can mobilize 2d6 guards, redirect supply chains, or restrict movement through official checkpoints as a bonus action. This takes effect within 1 hour.

***Graceful Misdirection.*** When a question is asked that Tessik cannot answer truthfully, he makes a Charisma (Deception) check with a +14 bonus. On a success, the questioner believes they have received a satisfying response. The DC is the questioner's passive Insight score + 5.

**Special — Thirty-One Years:** Any creature attempting to magically detect deception, dishonesty, or a false identity on Tessik must succeed on a DC 20 spell attack against a target AC of 18. The Author's puppet architecture has become, over thirty-one years, too deeply integrated to register as false to standard detection.

**Replacement Protocol:** When Tessik is destroyed or unmasked, within 48 hours a replacement appears. The replacement differs in: one physical scar in a different location, a slightly altered wine preference (Pale Reach southern hills, second vineyard rather than first), a marginally different way of addressing female staff (first name rather than title). Most NPCs will not notice. Players who catalogued the original carefully will.

## Story Hooks

**1. The Welcome (Long Game/Infiltration)**
Players arrive in the Pale Reach and are welcomed by Lord Tessik, who has heard of them from contacts in the Wandering Reach (or the Wire Market, or wherever they've been). He is gracious, useful, and immediately positions himself as an ally for the next stage of their work. He has information about the Convergence that is accurate. He gives it freely. Players leave the meeting thinking they've made a good contact. This is the first move of a long game. Tessik will continue to be useful, warm, and occasionally ask for small favors in return — all of which serve the Author's agenda in ways that are not apparent.

**2. The Production Discrepancy (Investigation/Discovery)**
Players encounter Mira the mining engineer (separately filed). She shows them the discrepancy in the mine output records. Following the discrepancy leads to the lower levels. Investigating the lower levels leads to evidence of the Author's thirty-one-year extraction operation. Somewhere in this evidence — in the administrative authorizations, in the official documentation — is Tessik's signature. Not forged. His actual signature. Tessik (the puppet) authorized these operations because Tessik is the Author's instrument in the Pale Reach. This is the moment the players have to decide what to do with what they know.

**3. The Replacement (Aftermath/Revelation)**
Players have unmasked Tessik or forced the Author to deploy the replacement. They are back in the Pale Reach three weeks later. "Tessik" is at his desk. He welcomes them warmly. The scar near his chin is slightly different. He orders wine from the wrong vineyard, catching the error and laughing about it: "I've been drinking the second-hill vintage lately, I keep forgetting you prefer the first-hill." Players who notice these details and confront them will find the replacement version of the Tessik puppet performing the exact reaction the Author has prepared for this scenario: genuine-seeming confusion, the right amount of hurt at the accusation, and then a subtle shift toward administrative authority that is slightly more naked than the original Tessik ever showed. The Pale Reach's people will not believe the players. Not yet. They've known Tessik for thirty-one years.

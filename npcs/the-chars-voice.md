# The Char's Voice — De Facto Leader, Char Settlement, Ashlands
*Ashlands | Uncasted (Ashlands residents exist outside the Caste system; Wire Market would estimate their enhancements at Third-to-Fourth Caste equivalent in function) | "The leader who governs by being willing to make decisions no one else will"*

## "You want to know if we're afraid of what's changing in the Scar. Yes. Anyone who isn't afraid has either not looked at it or has decided something they haven't told you yet. We've looked. We're afraid. We're still here."

---

## Who They Are

They are called the Voice because that is the function: the Char settlement's decisions go through them, spoken aloud, and what they say has the force of consensus because over twenty years they have been the person who says the thing the settlement has decided without being able to say it yet. They are not a tyrant — there is no coercion in their authority, no enforcement mechanism except the respect of people who have watched them make the right hard call enough times that disagreement requires a very good reason. The settlement follows them the way people follow someone when the alternative is following no one, which in the Ashlands is not a theoretical problem.

Their enhancements are, by Wire Market standard, barbaric: patchwork resonant-steel integrated through self-taught technique and improvised surgical support over decades, incorporating components from three different Caste tiers, two standard frames, and at least one piece of Wire Market salvage that was never intended for human integration. The result should not function as well as it does. It functions because they know their own body better than any official technician would, and because they have been adapting their enhancements to Ashlands conditions for long enough that the adaptations have adapted back.

They are watching the Engine Scar. They have been watching it for two years. The Scar — the massive industrial ruin at the Ashlands' center, the source of whatever catastrophe created the region — has been producing a new resonance signal. Not strong, not alarming on its own, but different from the background signal the settlement has lived with for generations. The Voice has been trying to understand what changed. They have not been able to determine it. They are carrying this uncertainty carefully, the way you carry something fragile, because the settlement needs them to appear more certain than they are.

---

## History

They arrived in the Char settlement at seventeen with nothing identifiable as a history — not a standard amnesia, but a specific absence: they knew how to do things, how to read terrain, how to integrate a component, how to read a negotiation. They didn't know where they'd learned any of it. They stayed because the settlement needed people and they didn't know where else to go.

The name "the Voice" developed gradually, over years. They became the person who spoke for the settlement in negotiations with the occasional Wire Market salvage crew, then with passing Resonant pilgrims, then with the Sutensai Tenders who eventually came to stay. They started making decisions when the person who had been making decisions couldn't — a settlement elder named Kassin, who died of enhancement failure twelve years ago. Kassin had been their closest friend in the Ashlands, the person who had given them their first stable footing, and her death was the one loss that changed them visibly. Before Kassin, they had been moving through the settlement's leadership role. After Kassin, they settled into it permanently, the way you settle into something when the person you were doing it alongside is gone.

The history before seventeen is not entirely absent. There are fragments — a specific smell that produces a location-feeling (a city, Wire Market, they think), a trained response to certain kinds of resonance configurations that is more practiced than anything they learned in the Ashlands. They have not pursued this. They have decided, deliberately, that the Voice is who they are and the history before the Voice is someone else's.

## What They Want

**Immediate:** To understand the Engine Scar's changed signal before it becomes a problem the settlement can't manage. They need information from outside the Ashlands — specifically, access to Iron Afterlife records about the Scar's origin, which the Orthodoxy has suppressed. They cannot access these records. They need someone who can.

**Deeper:** For the Char settlement to still exist after whatever is coming. Not preserved in amber — they are clear-eyed about the world changing — but adapted and intact. The settlement has survived in the Ashlands for generations, which is not supposed to be possible. The Voice intends to extend that survival.

**What they'd sacrifice:** Personal safety, fully and without drama. They have made this calculation so many times it is no longer interesting to them. For the settlement's survival, they would sacrifice almost anything. The one thing they would not sacrifice: the people of the settlement themselves. They have turned down arrangements, three times, that would have improved conditions in the Ashlands by reducing the population to a more "sustainable" level. They have turned these down in the way they do everything: briefly and without ceremony.

---

## What They Know

**Freely shared (to people who respect the settlement):** The Ashlands terrain, the settlement's history and current conditions, the behavior of the Engine Scar over the last twenty years as documented in the settlement's records. What the Ashlands produces that the Wire Market will trade for. The locations of the other Ashlands settlements and their current relationship with the Char.

**With trust:** The two-year change in the Scar's signal. They will describe it precisely: a low-frequency resonance pulse, irregular, originating from the Scar's deepest accessible level, on a pattern that correlates with — and they will note this carefully — major political events in the other Reaches. They are not sure what the correlation means. They are sure it's not coincidence.

**That they don't know they know:** The fragments of their pre-Ashlands history. If players approach this carefully — particularly players who have encountered the Hollow Author's network — they may recognize indicators in the Voice's trained responses that suggest a prior connection to Wire Market intelligence work. The Voice has buried this too deep to access directly. But the body remembers.

---

## What They Fear

That the Engine Scar's changed signal means something is waking. They know what the Scar was — not in complete detail, but in broad outline, from the settlement's oral records and from the Forge-Sister's theological interpretation. It was a power source. Whatever it powered is not running anymore. What they fear is that something has decided to start it up again. They are afraid of this because the Ashlands exist because of what the Scar produced when it was running. The region is what happens after.

---

## In Play — Voice and Presence

They speak in the Ashlands settlement dialect, which is a compressed form of Common with approximately forty words they've developed locally for things the wider world doesn't have vocabulary for. They translate automatically for outsiders but do not apologize for the dialect. They have a physical presence that is wider than their frame — not tall, but solid in the specific way of people who are always braced, always planted. Their enhancements are visible: the patchwork work runs along their jaw, both forearms, and disappears into their collar toward something at the collarbone that they have covered in every temperature.

They speak in decisions. Not plans, not possibilities — when they say something will happen, it is because they have decided it will happen and are describing their decision as reality. This is not arrogance. It is the verbal habit of someone who has governed by being certain for twenty years, including the times they were not actually certain. Players who catch the difference between certainty and performed certainty will understand something important about them.

They eat the same breakfast every morning: dried Ashlands fruit and whatever protein the settlement has, in that order, and they eat it before speaking to anyone. This is the settlement's signal that they are not yet in decision mode. No one interrupts the breakfast.

## Relationships

- **Allies:** Forge-Sister Kessen (the Sutensai Tender), who they treat as a peer and who they respect in the specific way they respect people who found a harder problem and started working on it anyway. Three Ashlands settlement leaders who recognize the Voice's authority in the Char and who the Voice has spent years building genuine coalition with.
- **Enemies:** A Wire Market salvage operation that has been attempting to negotiate extraction rights in the Ashlands without settlement consent. The Voice has declined their offers four times. The fifth refusal, they have decided, will be more emphatic.
- **The Players:** They will evaluate players by watching them in the settlement before speaking with them — not a test, just observation. Players who treat the settlement's people with respect will be dealt with as people. Players who treat the Ashlands as an obstacle or a resource will be redirected toward the nearest exit with notable efficiency. Once the Voice has decided players are useful allies, they are straightforwardly useful: they share information, they provide the settlement's considerable practical knowledge of the region, and they ask for specific things in return, never more than what's reasonable. They will not ask for help they don't need. When they do ask for help, it is real.

## Story Hooks

**1. The Approach (Earning Access)**
Players need something from the Ashlands — passage, information, resources — and are directed to the Char settlement. Their reception depends entirely on how they arrive. The Voice watches them from the settlement's observation point for two hours before making contact. The first conversation is practical: who are they, what do they want, what are they offering. If they want the Voice's help with the Scar, they will need to demonstrate something first. The Voice will give them a task — something the settlement actually needs, not a test — and the quality of how they do it will determine everything that follows.

**2. The Signal Reading (Investigation/Mystery)**
The Engine Scar's signal has shifted again, significantly this time. The Voice brings the players to the Scar's nearest accessible edge and asks them to observe while they take a reading. What they detect together is a resonance pattern that matches — partly, not exactly — a signal the players have encountered somewhere else in Tesshari. This is the first concrete evidence that the Scar's activation is correlated with specific outside events, and the correlation is actionable intelligence if the players can determine what triggered it.

**3. The History Before (Personal/Revelation)**
Late in the campaign, players have accumulated enough information about the Hollow Author's network to recognize that the Voice's trained responses — the specific improvised integration techniques, the intelligence-gathering instincts, the pre-Ashlands fragments — match the profile of a Wire Market intelligence asset. They can bring this to the Voice. The Voice will not receive it well, initially. This is a careful conversation. If done right, it gives the Voice access to a history they buried deliberately, which may also give them access to knowledge they didn't know they had — specifically, a specific technique for identifying flesh-puppet architecture that they learned before they learned they'd learned it. This knowledge will matter.

*Reaction | Triggered by: taking damage that would reduce the Voice below half HP*
**Effect:** The Voice has already made the calculation. Reduce incoming damage by FRAME (7). If they are still brought below half HP, they gain Regen 3 for 2 rounds — the Ashlands adaptation is real, and it keeps running.
**Keywords:** Regen 3

### Combat Behavior
The Voice does not initiate unless they have decided to. Once they have decided, they use Terrain Authority to control the ground, Settlement Decision to protect their people, and Scar Resonance Pulse to suppress enhancement-dependent enemies. They do not threaten. They act. The Fifth Refusal is used exactly once per combat and is the last communication they intend to make before the situation resolves.

### "I Would Prefer Not To"
The moment the settlement's people are no longer in immediate danger, the Voice will state, clearly, that they have no further interest in this fight. They will not say please. They will say: done.

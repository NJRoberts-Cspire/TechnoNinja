# TESSHARI CHARACTER CREATION
## A Step-by-Step Guide for New Players

---

> If you have never played Tesshari before, read this document from beginning to end and follow each step in order. By the end, you will have a complete, playable character. If you get stuck, the Quick Reference Box at the end covers the most common questions.

---

## BEFORE YOU BEGIN

Tesshari is a world of cyberpunk obligation. Every character in it is some combination of flesh and metal — a person who has been modified, rebuilt, chosen modifications, or had modifications chosen for them. Your character lives in a fractured empire mid-collapse, caught between three claimants to a divine throne, each of whom wants something from people like you.

You do not need to know the world's full history to make a character. You need to know:

1. What you are (your race)
2. What you do (your class)
3. What you are good at (your stats)
4. What tools you carry into a fight (your hand of cards)
5. Where you stand in the world (your background)

This document walks you through all five.

---

## STEP 1 — CHOOSE YOUR RACE

Your race determines where you came from biologically, what passive traits you carry, and whether you have any bonus to your hand size. Choose one.

---

### THE FORGED
*The majority people of Tesshari. Cyborgs, by degrees. The world was built for them.*

**Stat Bonuses:** +1 IRON, +1 to one stat of your choice (reflects your specific enhancement loadout; choose at creation)
**Hand Size Modifier:** +0
**Passive Traits:**
- **Integrated Chassis:** Immune to biological toxins, disease, and flesh-degradation effects. Chemical and mechanical damage still applies normally.
- **Maintenance Dependent:** If you complete a full rest without maintenance tools or a chassis-wright, you begin the next combat with Overheat 1 already applied. Cannot be removed until proper maintenance is performed.
- **Chassis Memory:** Once per scene, recall precise sensory data from any event within the last 24 hours with perfect fidelity.
- **Forged Resilience:** Once per turn, when a single hit would reduce you to 0 HP, apply Guard 4 against that hit only.

**Race Cards:** Chassis Lock (Tier 1 Defense: Guard 4+FRAME, may reduce one mechanical debuff), Override Protocol (Tier 2 Control: Stagger + Expose 2 on mechanically augmented target; or self-use to remove all Overheat + bonus damage)

**Who plays The Forged:** Players who want to be the baseline, the default, the person who moves through the world without being marked as unusual — and explores what that invisibility costs.

---

### THE TETHERED
*Unmodified biological humans. Rare, spiritually significant, and frequently underestimated.*

**Stat Bonuses:** +1 RESONANCE, +1 FRAME
**Hand Size Modifier:** +1
**Passive Traits:**
- **Old Breath:** Your concentrated Echomind (no steel distribution) gives you unusual resonant sensitivity. You can detect spiritual disturbances, recent deaths, and Between-adjacent spaces within 30 feet without equipment.
- **Biological Acuity:** Your unaugmented senses push biological limits. +1 to all checks involving observation, tracking, and reading others' physical states.
- **Resilient Flesh:** Your body heals faster than augmented bodies. Once per rest, recover 1d6+FRAME HP without expending resources.
- **No Interface:** You cannot use palm-plate locks, neural-interface equipment, or enhancement-dependent technology without assistance or workarounds. The world was not built for you.

**Race Cards:** Flesh Memory (Tier 1 Utility: recall emotional details of a past event with clarity no augmentation can replicate; once per scene), Quiet Presence (Tier 1 Defense/Control: become effectively invisible to passive electronic scanning for one scene; you register as background)

**Who plays The Tethered:** Players who want to be spiritually powerful but socially vulnerable — who want to know something true about the world that everyone around them has traded away.

---

### THE ECHOED
*People who have died and been returned through Resonant Extraction. Death has already happened. Everything else is aftermath.*

**Stat Bonuses:** +1 RESONANCE, +1 to one stat of your choice
**Hand Size Modifier:** +1
**Passive Traits:**
- **The Crossing:** Once per session, you may ask the DM one question about the Iron Afterlife, the Between, or the spiritual state of a recently dead creature, and receive a truthful (if incomplete) answer. Your Echomind touched these spaces.
- **Already Dead:** When you fail a death saving throw, you may reroll once per combat. Your relationship with death is different from everyone else's.
- **Resonant Echo:** In high-resonance areas (Ashlands, Between-adjacent spaces, Iron Afterlife-adjacent events), you receive flashes of resonant impression from others' past emotional states. Useful for investigation; uncomfortable in all other contexts.
- **The Gap:** You carry impressions from other souls that passed through the Iron Afterlife while you were in transit. Once per session, one of these impressions surfaces usefully — a skill, a piece of knowledge, a language — at the DM's discretion.

**Race Cards:** Between-Step (Tier 2 Mobility/Utility: partially enter the Between for one turn — become untargetable by physical attacks; Signal and Resonance attacks still reach you), Iron Afterlife Echo (Tier 1 Resonant Attack: channel a fragment of Iron Afterlife energy — deals RESONANCE damage and applies Silence)

**Who plays The Echoed:** Players who want to carry the weight of a completed death — groundedness about consequences, and the eerie resource of knowing what waits.

---

### THE WIREBORN
*Born in the machine-networks. Partly digital. Perceives a layer of reality others cannot access.*

**Stat Bonuses:** +1 SIGNAL, +1 EDGE
**Hand Size Modifier:** +1
**Passive Traits:**
- **Data Layer Perception:** You sense active network nodes, surveillance systems, and data infrastructure within 60 feet as a constant background awareness. You cannot be surprised by automated systems.
- **Network Interface:** You can interface with Wire Craft systems without external hardware. Any check to access, bypass, or manipulate technology uses your SIGNAL directly.
- **Split Attention:** You can partially enter a network as a free action, allowing your Echomind to operate in data-space while your body remains physically present. The body can still act, but at reduced capacity (your DM sets specific limits based on depth of split).
- **Circuit-Trace:** Your subdermal patterns are immediately recognizable as Wireborn. You cannot disguise your nature from anyone with even minimal knowledge of the race.

**Race Cards:** Data Surge (Tier 1 Signal Attack: channel network energy through your body — deals SIGNAL damage, target's Signal-based defenses are bypassed this hit), Mesh Override (Tier 2 Utility/Control: take over one automated system in range for one scene — turrets, locks, surveillance, doors)

**Who plays The Wireborn:** Players who want tactical information access, a split-reality perspective, and the specific loneliness of perceiving something no one else can.

---

### THE STITCHED
*Assembled from multiple sources. Survivors of catastrophic damage, or deliberate reconstruction subjects. Nearly unkillable.*

**Stat Bonuses:** +1 FRAME, +1 IRON
**Hand Size Modifier:** +0
**Passive Traits:**
- **Patchwork Resilience:** When you are reduced to 0 HP, you may immediately make a FRAME check (difficulty 4). On success, you drop to 1 HP instead. Once per combat.
- **Component Independence:** If a limb or external component is destroyed in combat (specific narrative damage), you continue functioning without meaningful impairment. The DM narrates the loss; you ignore the mechanical penalty.
- **The Drift:** Over time (between sessions, at the DM's discretion), your assembled body develops unexpected new traits — biological growth at seams, mechanical components developing unusual behavior. Work with your DM to develop Drift traits as the campaign progresses.
- **Visible Assembly:** The origins of your components are readable to anyone who knows what to look for. Your chassis history is worn on the outside.

**Race Cards:** Emergency Reconfiguration (Tier 2 Defense/Utility: redistribute internal structure — gain Shield 8+FRAME; one component that would normally cause a critical failure simply doesn't this combat), Component Harvest (Tier 1 Utility: you recover functionality from the body of a defeated creature — gain temporary use of one of their card types for this combat only)

**Who plays The Stitched:** Players who want to be nearly unkillable and slightly wrong — assembled rather than born, surviving through combination rather than design.

---

### THE SHELLBROKEN
*Former puppets of the Hollow Author, now liberated. They have lost time. They carry residual network access they cannot fully close.*

**Stat Bonuses:** +1 VEIL, +1 SIGNAL
**Hand Size Modifier:** +0
**Passive Traits:**
- **Residual Connectivity:** You retain vestigial connection to the Hollow Author's puppet network. Once per session, you may attempt to push through this connection to disrupt a nearby puppet (DM determines if a puppet is present). This is not safe. The Author may notice.
- **Fractured Continuity:** You have a gap in your memory corresponding to your time under control. The DM holds information about what your body did during that period and may reveal it at dramatically appropriate moments.
- **Pattern Recognition:** Because you experienced external control, you are exceptionally good at recognizing when others are being controlled, manipulated, or operating under compulsion. +2 to checks to detect social manipulation or puppet influence.
- **Unmarked by Design:** Your liberation left a mark at the base of your skull — a faint geometric pattern. Most people never notice it. Those who know what it means notice it immediately.

**Race Cards:** Network Bleed (Tier 1 Signal Attack/Utility: push hostile intent back through your residual connection — deals SIGNAL damage to one creature you can identify as network-connected; DM rules on availability), Ghost in the Shell (Tier 2 Defense/Utility: your puppet-network residue allows you to briefly appear to be someone else electronically — all signal-based identification reads a false identity for one scene)

**Who plays The Shellbroken:** Players interested in the specific horror of lost time, in being partially claimed by a power that doesn't know they're gone, in carrying something they didn't choose.

---

### THE IRON BLESSED
*People whose Echominds are so resonant that their bodies produce metal formations. Theological arguments that walk and bleed and hurt.*

**Stat Bonuses:** +2 RESONANCE
**Hand Size Modifier:** See below — varies by formation type
**Passive Traits:**
- **Resonant Formations:** Your metal formations resonate in high-RESONANCE environments, granting you advantage on spiritual checks and resonance-sensing in such spaces.
- **Living Proof:** The Iron Sutra's clergy regard you as a theological demonstration. In Sutra-controlled territory, you have access and protection that others do not. This access comes with surveillance and the expectation of compliance.
- **Formation Pain:** The formations grow at irregular intervals. This is acutely painful and cannot be suppressed. Once per session, a growth event may occur at a dramatically inopportune moment (DM's call). You continue to function.
- **Resonant Broadcast:** Your Echomind transmits passively at high intensity. You cannot hide your spiritual presence from resonance-sensing equipment or attuned individuals.

**Hand Size Modifier:** Determined by formation type chosen at creation. Work with your DM from the Iron Blessed race file.

**Race Cards:** Formation Strike (Tier 1 Resonant Attack: drive a formation into a target — deals RESONANCE damage + Expose 1 from the resonant disruption), Sutensai's Proof (Tier 2 Power/Utility: your formations pulse at maximum intensity — all allies within range gain Regen 2 for 2 turns; all enemies are Marked)

**Who plays The Iron Blessed:** Players who want to be sacred and uncomfortable — defined by something they didn't choose, watched and protected and used, carrying pain as a permanent subtext.

---

### THE DIMINISHED
*Small. Built for the machine's interior. Technically indispensable. Socially disposable. Underestimated in ways that have become weapons.*

**Stat Bonuses:** +1 VEIL, +1 EDGE
**Hand Size Modifier:** +2
**Passive Traits:**
- **Infrastructure Native:** You can navigate maintenance shafts, server corridors, and any infrastructure space built for your body size without checks. You know how buildings work from the inside.
- **Environmental Awareness:** You cannot be surprised by environmental threats — collapsing structures, failing systems, gas, automated defenses, unstable terrain. Living organisms can still surprise you.
- **Invisible Tier:** Most people at higher social tiers genuinely do not notice you unless you demand their attention. This is useful. You can move through most social spaces as functional background unless your race-specific utility becomes relevant.
- **Precision Hands:** Your hands are exceptionally fine-boned and dexterous. +2 to all checks involving fine manipulation, technical work, and installation or disassembly of small components.

**Race Cards:** Underestimated (Tier 1 Control/Utility: act in a moment where no one expects you to — you may take one action before any combatant reacts; once per combat), Maintenance Access (Tier 2 Utility: access any infrastructure system in range through paths others cannot reach — this includes power shutoffs, ventilation, data conduits, structural supports)

**Who plays The Diminished:** Players who want to be underestimated and then devastating, who enjoy operating in the margins and knowing the building better than the people who built it.

## STEP 3 — DISTRIBUTE STATS

**At character creation, distribute 20 points across the six stats.**
- Minimum 1 per stat.
- Maximum 5 per stat at creation.
- Add your race stat bonuses after distributing points. Race bonuses can push a stat to 6 but not higher without cybernetic enhancements.

**The Six Stats:**

| Stat | What It Does |
|---|---|
| **IRON** | Adds to melee attack damage. Physical force, grapple, forced movement. |
| **EDGE** | Determines initiative order. Adds to precision, ranged, and evasion cards. |
| **FRAME** | Determines HP. Adds to endurance cards, resistance triggers, damage thresholds. |
| **SIGNAL** | Adds to Signal attack cards. Wire Craft aptitude, system hacking, technical debuffs. |
| **RESONANCE** | Adds to resonant attack cards. Spiritual interaction, healing, Iron Afterlife access. |
| **VEIL** | Adds to social/command cards. Manipulation, concealment, crowd control. |

**Stat Rating Scale:**

| Rating | What It Means |
|---|---|
| 1 | Damaged, barely functional |
| 2 | Below standard — unenhanced baseline |
| 3 | Standard — average functional enhancement |
| 4 | Enhanced — baseline for most adventurers |
| 5 | Exceptional — specialist-grade |
| 6 | Elite (requires race bonus or enhancement to reach at creation) |

**Guidance:** Your two primary class stats should be your highest. Your dump stat (the one your class never uses) can safely sit at 1 or 2.

**Example:** Ironclad Samurai, primary stats IRON and RESONANCE. Reasonable starting spread: IRON 5, RESONANCE 4, FRAME 3, EDGE 3, SIGNAL 2, VEIL 2 (total: 19, +1 IRON from Forged race = IRON 6, total 20 spent).

## STEP 4 — BUILD YOUR STARTING HAND

**Starting Hand = Class Starting Cards + Race Cards**

Your class file specifies your Starting Hand — usually 2–3 cards. These are the cards your character knows at Level 1 before making any unlock choices.

At Level 1, you also choose 1 card from your class's Level 1 Unlock List. This goes into your hand (if under Hand Size maximum).

**Race cards** (the 2 cards listed in your race's CARD SYSTEM section) are always available to you. They count toward your hand size. They are not class cards and do not follow the class unlock curve.

**Your starting hand is: Class Starting Cards + 1 Level 1 Unlock + Race Cards**

---

### Hand Size

Hand Size is the number of cards available to you every turn. It is not how many you play per turn — it is the total pool of options.

**Your Hand Size = Base Class Hand Size + Race Hand Size Modifier**

Example: Ironclad Samurai (Hand Size 6) + The Tethered (+1) = 7. You have 7 cards available every turn.

If your starting cards exceed your Hand Size (e.g., you have 8 cards but Hand Size 7), you must choose which cards to carry. This is a character decision.

---

### How to Read a Card

Every card has the same structure:

```
Card Name
Tier X (X AP) | Category | [Race: X if applicable]

Effect: What the card does. Always read this fully.
Keywords: Listed here for quick reference.
Unlock: Level X
```

- **Tier** tells you the AP cost.
- **Category** tells you what kind of card it is (Attack, Defense, Control, Reaction, Passive, etc.) and which stat it uses for damage.
- **Effect** is the full text. If Effect and Keywords seem to conflict, Effect wins.
- **Keywords** are shorthand for recurring mechanics — look them up in `02_keywords_and_status.md` if you do not know what they mean.

## STEP 5 — CALCULATE HP

**HP Formula:** (FRAME × 8) + Class Base HP

| HP Tier | Base HP | Formula Example (FRAME 3) |
|---|---|---|
| Heavy | +20 | (3 × 8) + 20 = 44 HP |
| Martial | +14 | (3 × 8) + 14 = 38 HP |
| Balanced | +10 | (3 × 8) + 10 = 34 HP |
| Technical | +6 | (3 × 8) + 6 = 30 HP |
| Social | +6 | (3 × 8) + 6 = 30 HP |

**HP on Level Up:** Add your FRAME to your HP maximum at each level.

Example: Level 1 Ironclad Samurai, FRAME 4: (4 × 8) + 14 = 46 HP. At Level 2: 46 + 4 = 50 HP.

## STEP 6 — STARTING EQUIPMENT

Your starting equipment is listed in your class file. Most classes begin with:
- A primary weapon appropriate to their fighting style
- Basic chassis maintenance tools (or an equivalent)
- Class-specific gear (armor, enhancement rigs, ritual items, etc.)

**How Items Modify Cards**

Items in Tesshari do not grant new cards. They modify cards already in your hand.

Common item modifications:
- **+X damage to card category** — e.g., "+2 damage to all Melee Attack cards"
- **Reduce AP cost by 1 on a named card** — applies once per combat
- **Add a keyword to all cards of a type** — e.g., "All Attack cards gain Bleed 1"
- **Extend a card's targets** — e.g., "one Signal Attack card targets all enemies in range"
- **Add a secondary effect to a named card** — e.g., "your Tier 3 Signature card also applies Fortify to all allies"

Each item's modification is explicit. Read the item entry.

**Cybernetic enhancements** (separate from weapons and gear) can additionally:
- Raise a stat by 1–2
- Increase Hand Size by 1
- Unlock a race-specific card outside your class list

---

## STEP 7 — CHOOSE YOUR VEIN OATH / CLASS ANCHOR

Every class has a defining choice at Level 1 — a subclass, path, or oath that shapes what kind of practitioner of that class you become.

This choice is listed in your class file under the Level 1 section (often called "Vein Oath," "Path," "Anchor," or "Form" depending on the class).

Read all options. Choose one. This choice defines which subclass features you unlock at Levels 3, 6, 10, and 14. Most choices cannot be changed after Level 1.

*The Ironclad Samurai chooses a Vein Oath and selects from four subclasses (Oath of the Iron Lord, Sutensai Blade, Undying Debt, Flesh Temple) at Level 3.*

## STEP 8 — PLACE IN THE WORLD

Before your first session, answer these questions. They are prompts, not requirements — take what is useful, skip what is not.

**Which Reach are you from?**
- **Sunder Reach:** Korrath's territory. Military, ordered, controlled. You grew up under the most efficient government in Tesshari and its most demanding surveillance.
- **Veilward Reach:** Vaen's homeland. Deeply spiritual, politically complicated. The Pilgrimage has been reshaping it for decades.
- **Pale Reach:** The Wire Market. Wealthy, information-dense, beautiful, and deeply dishonest. Everyone here is managing something.
- **Iron Teeth Reach:** Industrial, stratified, the most organized labor conflict in Tesshari. If you have hands that work, someone here wanted to use them.
- **The Ashlands:** The collapsed south. Improvisation, survival, desperate community. The rest of Tesshari treats this place as a disposal zone.
- **The Echo Coast:** Where the dead come back. Technically sophisticated, spiritually saturated, strange in ways that residents have stopped noticing.
- **The Northern Pale:** Cold, isolated, and wrong in ways that are difficult to articulate. People don't choose to be from here. It chooses them.

**Which caste do you belong to?**
- **Resonant Caste:** High-quality enhancements, recognized standing, access to Sutra services, better employment, visible privilege.
- **Craftbound Caste:** Skilled trade workers. Functional enhancements. Respected within the labor structure. Not wealthy.
- **Ashwalker Caste:** Minimal or salvage enhancements. The margin. Work is available if you don't ask what it costs.
- **Hollow Poor:** Degraded enhancements, chronic pain, functionally excluded from most formal systems. Still alive. Still dangerous.

**Which faction, if any, do you have ties to?**
- **Korrath the Unbroken:** Military loyalty, enforcement, the belief that order maintained by force is still order.
- **Vaen of the Empty Hand:** The Pilgrimage, voluntary enhancement removal, the unshackled.
- **The Iron Sutra Orthodoxy:** Faith, theological authority, the management of death and its aftermath.
- **The Wire Market Guilds:** Information, technology, money, the pretense of neutrality.
- **None:** You owe nothing to nobody. This is either freedom or a gap where something used to be.

**Who do you owe?**
Tesshari runs on debt — literal, social, and spiritual. Who has a claim on you? A lord who paid for your enhancements? A family you abandoned? A debt to the dead? A contract you cannot legally exit?

**What do you need?**
Not want — need. The thing that is driving you into danger. Resources for a family member's failing enhancements? An answer about what your body did during time you cannot remember? A lord worth dying for? A reason to keep the code when everything else has broken it?

## LEVELING UP

At each level from 2 to 20:

**1. Choose 1 new card** from your class's Unlock List for the current level.

**2. Add or replace:**
- If you are **under** your Hand Size maximum: add the card to your hand.
- If you are **at** your Hand Size maximum: replace one existing card with the new card. The replaced card is permanently removed from your hand.

**3. Optional swap:** You may also swap one existing card in your hand for any previously unlocked card you did not take from any prior level's unlock list. This represents refinement — not forgetting, but reprioritizing.

**4. Add FRAME to HP maximum.**

**5. Consult class file** for any level-specific features (subclass features at Levels 3, 6, 10, 14, 18; stat improvements at Levels 4, 8, 12, 16, 19).

---

### Card Tier Unlock Curve

| Levels | What's Available |
|---|---|
| 1–5 | Tier 0, mostly Tier 1, some Tier 2 |
| 6–10 | Tier 1–2 dominant; first Tier 3 options |
| 11–15 | Tier 2–3 dominant |
| 16–20 | Tier 3 dominant; Capstone card at Level 20 |

Each class has 35–40 total cards to unlock across 20 levels. Your choices matter — no two builds of the same class are identical.

## QUICK REFERENCE BOX

*The most-asked-about rules. When someone at the table has a question, check here first.*

---

**How many cards can I play per turn?**
As many as your AP allows. Each card costs AP equal to its Tier. You have 3 AP by default. Each card can only be played once per turn.

**Can I always use my Basic Attack?**
Yes. Basic Attack is 0 AP, once per turn, always available. Nothing removes it. Play it in addition to your cards.

**What's the difference between Guard and Shield?**
Guard expires at end of turn. Shield persists until depleted. Both absorb damage before your HP.

**Can I carry Guard from one turn to the next?**
Only if the card that granted it says it persists (e.g., "until depleted" or "until end of next turn"). Otherwise no.

**What does Cleanse do vs. Dispel?**
Cleanse removes debuffs from yourself or an ally. Dispel removes buffs from an enemy. Do not mix these up.

**How does damage work without dice?**
Final Damage = (Card Base Damage + Primary Stat) × Multipliers − Mitigation. All values are fixed. See `02_keywords_and_status.md` for the full pipeline.

**How does my FRAME stat affect HP?**
HP = (FRAME × 8) + Class Base HP. Every point of FRAME is worth 8 HP.

**What is my Hand Size?**
Base class hand size + race modifier. Check Step 4. This is the total number of cards available to you every turn.

**Can I have more cards than my Hand Size?**
No. If you are at your Hand Size maximum when you level up and choose a new card, you must replace one existing card.

**When does my Reaction reset?**
At the end of each round (once every combatant has taken their turn). Not at the end of your turn — at the end of the round.

**What is the Between?**
The resonant space between life and death, flesh and metal. Not literally accessible to most characters without specific cards or race abilities. Your DM can answer specific questions about the Between in play.

**What happens when I reach 0 HP?**
Standard: you fall unconscious and begin dying. Your class or race may have features that trigger before or after this. Ask your DM about the specific death and dying rules in use for your campaign.

# Merchant Knife
## "I don't have enemies. I have outstanding accounts. The difference is that accounts can be settled."

## Overview

In the Wire Market, they say that the most expensive thing you can do is make an enemy of a Merchant Knife. Not because they are necessarily the most dangerous fighter, or the most powerful practitioner. Because when a Merchant Knife decides that someone represents an unacceptable liability, they approach the problem the way they approach all problems: with calculation, patience, and the specific kind of thoroughness that comes from a tradition that has had centuries to develop methods. They know your supply lines. They know your financiers. They know what you owe and who you owe it to. They know which of your allies became your allies because of an investment that can be redirected. And they know that a problem approached with sufficient systematic rigor is not a problem — it is a project, with a timeline and a completion date.

The Merchant Knife emerged from the Wire Market's practical necessity. Commerce in Tesshari is not conducted in a vacuum — it is conducted in territories controlled by warlords who tax, and Sutensai who regulate, and criminal networks who extract, and competing merchants who will, if given the opportunity, undermine your position through any means available. The Wire Market developed its own class of practitioners who combined commercial expertise with a range of other capabilities: economic intelligence, strategic sabotage, targeted violence when the cost-benefit analysis supported it, and the particular kind of patience required to dismantle a competitor over years rather than hours. This is commerce as warfare, and the Merchant Knife is its practitioner.

What distinguishes the Merchant Knife from an ordinary criminal is the framework. The Merchant Knife does not steal. They redirect supply. They do not threaten. They create conditions. They do not assassinate from passion. They terminate a contract with extreme prejudice after conducting due diligence that would satisfy a court. Their methods are extreme; their paperwork is immaculate. The Sutensai has found, repeatedly, that the activities of Merchant Knives are legal by the letter of every existing code, because the existing codes were not designed by people who imagined that economic analysis would be used as a primary weapon. In several territories, the codes have been updated. In those territories, the Merchant Knives have reviewed the updates and adjusted their methods.

The Gilded Blade who takes contracts is perhaps the most misunderstood archetype: they are not assassins who happen to be good with money. They are economic analysts who have determined that their target's most efficient removal is physical, and who execute that removal with the precision of someone who has built cost-benefit models around every step of the approach. They turn down contracts regularly — not from squeamishness but from analysis. The contract is not worth the risk, or the target is not worth the cost, or there are downstream consequences that make the elimination unprofitable. The ones they accept are executed with an excellence that the intelligence services of Tesshari find genuinely alarming.

## The Merchant Knife in the World

The Wire Market officially disavows the Merchant Knife tradition, because official acknowledgment would create regulatory problems. Privately, every significant commercial house in the Wire Market employs or has employed a Merchant Knife practitioner, and the major houses have entire teams. The tradition is how the Wire Market maintains its commercial independence against the territorial warlords who would very much prefer to control it — not through military resistance, which the Market would lose, but through the systematic economic leverage that the Market holds over every territory in Tesshari, carefully managed by people who know exactly where to apply pressure for maximum effect.

Outside the Wire Market, Merchant Knives are employed by lords, by criminal networks, by rebel movements, and by anyone else who has a commercial problem that needs solving or a commercial structure that needs disrupting. They are among the most transferable professional skills in Tesshari — their expertise is useful to any power with economic interests, which is every power. This makes them highly employable and perpetually in a negotiating position about who they work for, because multiple parties typically want their expertise and the choice of alignment has strategic consequences.

The Supply Cutter is at their most valuable in wartime — they are the person who makes armies stop fighting not by defeating them in battle but by ensuring that they cannot be supplied, cannot be financed, and cannot secure the material they need to continue. The Ascension War has produced an extraordinary demand for supply disruption capabilities, and the Supply Cutters who have been deployed by all three major factions have done damage that military commanders struggle to attribute, quantify, or counter, because the damage looks like logistics failures rather than deliberate operations. That is, of course, the point.

## CARD SYSTEM

**Primary Stats:** SIGNAL + EDGE
**HP:** (FRAME × 8) + 6
**Hand Size:** 8
**AP:** 3 per turn. Basic Attack free (0 AP), once per turn.

### Class Identity
The Merchant Knife is a surgical information-warfare class. Their hand is the largest of any combat-capable class, but the cards are conditional — many require SIGNAL status on the target, or specific combat circumstances, or full information to activate their highest form. The Merchant Knife gathers intelligence mid-combat to unlock card bonuses in later turns. They are the only class with the **SIGNAL** card subtype. An uninformed Merchant Knife is a competent fighter. A fully-informed one is an economic execution.

**SIGNAL (status):** Applied to targets by several Merchant Knife cards. While a target has SIGNAL status, Merchant Knife cards with conditional bonuses unlock those bonuses against that target. SIGNAL stacks to 3; each stack increases the value of conditional bonuses. SIGNAL lasts until end of combat unless otherwise noted.

**Debt (status):** Applied by several Merchant Knife cards. A target with Debt takes +2 damage from all Merchant Knife cards per Debt stack. Debt stacks to 5.

## Subclasses

### The Supply Cutter — "Nothing Moves Without Permission"

The Supply Cutter has developed economic warfare into a precise discipline. They do not attack armies; they attack the systems that sustain armies. They know how much food an army of a given size needs per week, where that food is stored, how it is transported, what it would take to intercept or contaminate the transportation route, and what the cascading effects on morale, battle-readiness, and territorial control would be when the army discovers it has been cut off. They know all of this before they act, and they act only when the analysis supports the intervention.

**Level 3: Supply Assessment**
When you spend 1 hour observing any significant organization (army, merchant house, territorial administration), you can construct a complete Supply Map: their critical supply dependencies, the three most vulnerable nodes in their supply chain, and the timeline to crisis if each node is disrupted. The Supply Map is a strategic document that provides your allies with the information needed to execute targeted disruption.

**Level 3: Interception**
Once per short rest, as part of a skill check or combat action, you can intercept a physical transfer of goods or information that you know is occurring (as opposed to generating a new transfer). This requires physical presence at the transfer point but provides +3 to any ability check made to intercept, misdirect, or copy the transferred material.

**Level 6: Chain Disruption**
Your Market Manipulation operations specifically targeting supply chains produce twice the normal effect. A flooded market disrupts an opponent's supply for 2 months instead of 1. An artificial scarcity lasts twice as long. Additionally, when one of your disruptions takes effect, you are notified of the first observable symptom — you know the moment your operation starts producing effects.

**Level 10: The Long Cut**
Once per month, you can initiate a sustained supply disruption against a major military or commercial power. The disruption operates over 3 months and produces effects equivalent to a siege in terms of resource denial: the target cannot operate at full capacity in the affected supply domain (food, materials, intelligence, or finances). Counter-operations to identify and break the supply cut require a successful difficulty 25 Investigation check — and even if successful, restoring supply chains takes an additional 2 weeks.

**Level 14: Total Interdiction**
Once per long rest, you can declare a Total Interdiction against one target organization within a territory where your network is active. For 1 week, no supply of your designated category (choose one: food, materials, communications, or finances) reaches the target. The Interdiction is maintained through your network's active operations and can be detected and countered by a sufficiently skilled intelligence service (difficulty 28 Investigation check, 3-day investigation, countered by neutralizing at least three of your network's active nodes in the area). At the end of 1 week, the interdiction ends and your network resets.

### The Gilded Blade — "The Contract Has Terms"

The Gilded Blade takes contracts. They are not assassins — they are contract fulfillers whose contracts occasionally specify physical elimination. The distinction matters enormously to them and not at all to their targets. They conduct due diligence before accepting any contract: the client's identity, the target's actual offense (not just the stated one), the downstream consequences of the elimination, and whether there is a more efficient solution that doesn't involve the Gilded Blade's direct involvement. A significant percentage of contracts that come to a Gilded Blade are redirected to other solutions. The ones that proceed to physical execution are ones that the Blade has determined are, in the complete analysis, the correct resolution.

**Level 3: Contract Assessment**
Before accepting or executing any significant action, you spend 10 minutes in analysis. This analysis reveals: the most significant hidden consequence of the action (DM-provided), the most likely countermeasure the target will employ, and the probability (rough, as a feeling: low/moderate/high) of success without modification. The assessment is your tool for deciding whether to proceed and how.

**Level 3: Execution Precision**
When you attack a target you have spent at least 24 hours researching (Economic Analysis or better), you deal maximum damage on the first attack of each combat. This represents the completeness of your preparation — you know where to strike and have practiced the exact angles.

**Level 6: The Contingency Fee**
You always have an escape option. When you complete a successful elimination (reduce a target to 0 HP), you can immediately take the Disengage action as a free action and move your full speed. Additionally, you gain +3 to checks to disappear into a crowd or environment immediately following an operation.

**Level 10: Closed Account**
When you reduce a target to 0 HP, their financial and operational records become accessible to you: you immediately know the contents of their most recent significant business transaction, their most significant current obligation, and the name of whoever will most want to know about this. This information arrives in a brief flash of insight — the economic network registers the vacancy and informs you. This is not magical; it is the residue of complete pre-operational research.

**Level 14: The Perfect Contract**
Once per long rest, you can execute a Perfect Contract — an action you have prepared so completely that failure is not a planned outcome. Select one target and one action (an attack, a deception, a theft, etc.). You automatically succeed on the initial execution check — no roll required. The target has -3 to any resistance check against the initial effect. You gain +3 to all checks related to the operation for 1 hour after initiation. This requires 1 week of preparation work and complete pre-operational analysis.

### The Kingmaker — "Power Is an Investment"

The Kingmaker is playing a different game at a different timescale. They are not trying to win this conflict. They are building the conditions for the next one — or for the conclusion of this one in a way that serves their long-term positioning. They have made and unmade political fortunes. They have redirected rivers of financing that shaped territorial boundaries. They have decided, on at least one occasion, which army would win a campaign before either general knew they were in one. They are the most patient Merchant Knife and the most dangerous one, because what they do will not be visible until it has already happened.

**Level 3: The Investment**
You can take a long-term position in a person, organization, or cause. By spending resources (DM determines appropriate scale) and 1 week of engagement, you create an Investment: a formal commitment of your support, resources, and network to an entity's advancement. The entity gains +3 to VEIL checks in commercial and political contexts. You always know their current status and can communicate with them as a bonus action. You can have up to your SIGNAL active Investments simultaneously.

**Level 3: Valuation**
When you assess a political or commercial situation, you also assess the long-term value trajectory of every party involved. Once per long rest, you can ask the DM: which of these parties represents the most valuable long-term investment, and why? The DM provides a strategic assessment based on the current situation.

**Level 6: Market Making**
You can make markets as well as participate in them. When a new commercial opportunity emerges in your operational territory, you can position yourself as the primary intermediary — the party through whom others must deal to access the opportunity. Establishing this position requires 1 week of work and succeeds automatically if no equally-capable competitor is present. This provides ongoing Operation Charges from the commercial activity (1 per week) and establishes your presence as structurally necessary to the market.

**Level 10: The Long Short**
You can bet against a power — begin accumulating the means to profit from their downfall before the downfall begins. Over 1 month of preparation, you position your investments and networks such that when a designated power falls or suffers a major setback, you are maximally positioned to benefit: gaining 11 × SIGNAL gold pieces in immediately-available assets and the goodwill of whoever has benefited from the power's fall. If you triggered the fall, you receive double.

**Level 14: Dynasty**
You have built something that outlasts individual operations. You can establish a permanent commercial dynasty — a financial and information network that operates continuously, generating resources and intelligence automatically. The Dynasty requires 6 months to establish and an initial investment (DM determines scale). Once established, it generates resources equal to 55 gold pieces per week, provides ambient intelligence on commercial matters across the territories where it operates, and cannot be destroyed without identifying and neutralizing each of its 7 + SIGNAL independent nodes. The Dynasty is your legacy. Whether it serves justice, commerce, or your personal agenda is the question the class ends on.

## Roleplaying the Merchant Knife

**The Cost-Benefit of Everything:** The Merchant Knife calculates. Every relationship, every decision, every risk — they run the numbers. This is a professional habit that has leaked into their personal life in ways they may not have intended. When they care about someone, they are aware of the ways that caring creates vulnerability. When they make a choice for moral reasons, they are aware of the cost of choosing morality over efficiency. How does your character hold these calculations alongside things that resist calculation?

**The Clean Hands Problem:** The Merchant Knife's methods are indirect enough that they rarely have blood on their hands in an obvious sense. Supply disruptions that produce starvation. Financial operations that destroy people's livelihoods. Eliminations that have been thoroughly reasoned but are still eliminations. The distance between the method and the consequence is professional protection. Is it ethical protection? How does your character think about the gap between what they directly do and what their operations cause?

**Who Are You Working For?** The Merchant Knife is always working for someone — even if that someone is themselves. Who your character is working for right now, and whether that alignment is the right one, and whether their current employer's interests align with what your character actually values — this is a perpetual question. The Ascension War has clarified that some positions are not neutral. Has that changed your character's calculations?

**The Moment It Became Personal:** The Merchant Knife tradition is built on professional detachment. Something, at some point, became personal. An operation that affected someone they cared about. A client who turned out to be connected to something they would not have accepted if they'd known. The target they now cannot look at through purely analytical eyes. What was it?

## Connections to the Ascension War

Korrath the Unbroken is the Wire Market's worst potential client and most significant potential threat simultaneously. His territorial expansion puts pressure on trade routes, his taxation policies are economically destabilizing, and his military ambitions disrupt the supply chains that the Merchant Knife tradition is built to manage. Some Merchant Knives are working actively against him. Some are working for him. The ones working for him have made an analysis that his victory is the most economically stable of the three options and are proceeding on that basis.

Vaen of the Empty Hand is not a commercial entity, which makes him difficult to analyze economically. His movement is sustained by genuine belief rather than financial calculation, which is from the Merchant Knife's perspective both admirable and extremely difficult to leverage. He has no financial vulnerabilities in the standard sense. What he has is a network of genuinely committed followers whose commitments are personal and spiritual rather than commercial. Kingmakers find him fascinating as a case study in non-economic power.

The Hollow Author is the Merchant Knife tradition's ideal adversary and nightmare employer simultaneously. The Author has commercial operations that are extensive, carefully concealed, and fundamental to the financing of their puppet network. Supply Cutters who have tried to trace the Author's financial architecture describe it as the most sophisticated commercial network they have encountered — it is, by design, genuinely invisible to conventional analysis. A Merchant Knife who has successfully mapped even one significant node of the Author's financial structure is in possession of intelligence more valuable than almost anything else in Tesshari.

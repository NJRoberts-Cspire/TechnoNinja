# Voice of Debt
## "You made a promise. I heard it. The Iron Afterlife heard it. You don't have to be happy about it. You do have to pay."

## Overview

Tesshari runs on debt. Not financial debt, though that exists too — the deeper debt, the kind that binds Echomind to Echomind, the kind that the Iron Sutra recognizes as spiritually real. When a promise is made in Tesshari with genuine intent, the resonance of that promise writes itself into the fabric of both parties' Echomin. This is not metaphorical: advanced Sutensai practitioners can detect the resonant trace of sworn oaths in the Echomind of anyone who has made them. The making of a serious oath leaves a mark. Breaking that oath leaves a different mark — one that the Iron Afterlife notices.

The Voice of Debt has developed this intuition into a practice. They can feel oaths. They can feel the weight of obligations — fresh ones, old ones, broken ones. They can read the shape of a promise in someone's Echomind the way a skilled reader reads emotion in a face. They know when a sworn statement is genuine and when it is performance. They can sense when an obligation is being dishonored. In a culture where the management of obligation is a primary social activity, these abilities make the Voice of Debt one of the most important practitioners of social power in any community — and one of the most dangerous, because the power to enforce oaths is also the power to create them, to bind them, and in the hands of the Breaker subclass, to dissolve them.

The tradition emerged from the Wire Market's need for reliable contract enforcement. In a mercantile culture without a neutral enforcement authority that every party trusted, the early Voice of Debt practitioners emerged as the parties that both sides of a negotiation would accept as arbiters. Their ability to detect oath-breaking — and their spiritual authority to impose consequences on oath-breakers — made them more effective than any court at enforcing the deals that made the Wire Market function. The tradition spread outward from commerce into politics, into warfare, into personal relationships of every kind. There is now no significant human activity in Tesshari that the Voice of Debt does not touch, and the Voices are keenly aware of the power this gives them.

The Debt Collector is perhaps the most feared active practitioner: the person who shows up when someone has made a promise they haven't honored and has been sent to collect. The Voice of Debt does not typically threaten violence, which makes them more disturbing than violence in some ways — their intervention is entirely legal, entirely legitimate, and backed by a spiritual weight that the Iron Sutra formally recognizes. They are the system arriving with a calm face and a specific bill. Every person in Tesshari who has ever made a promise they regretted has an unspecified dread of the day a Voice of Debt appears at their door.

## The Voice of Debt in the World

The Voice of Debt class is the most politically neutral of all the social classes — by design and necessity. The power to enforce oaths requires that both parties to an oath believe the enforcer is impartial. A Voice of Debt who is known to favor one faction over another loses their effectiveness with all other factions. The tradition maintains a formal code of neutrality that is practically and spiritually enforced — Voices who violate the neutrality code are subject to the oaths they themselves have sworn, which is an effective deterrent.

In practice, true neutrality is an ongoing performance rather than a state of being. Every Voice of Debt has personal opinions, political inclinations, and relationships that create implicit biases. The tradition's discipline is about managing these — not eliminating them, because the tradition is honest enough to know that's impossible, but managing them well enough that the practice remains credible. A Voice of Debt whose neutrality is credible is valuable. A Voice of Debt whose credibility has been compromised is dangerous — they have access to sensitive information from all the parties that trusted them.

The Breaker subclass exists at the fringe of respectability within the tradition. The ability to dissolve oaths is necessary — there are unjust oaths, coerced oaths, oaths that have been overtaken by circumstances that make their fulfillment harmful or impossible. The Breaker performs an essential service. They are also, unavoidably, in the business of making oaths not count, which undermines the fundamental premise of the tradition. Breakers are watched carefully by Oath Keepers and Debt Collectors, who are professionally uncomfortable with the idea that what they enforce can be undone.

## CARD SYSTEM: Voice of Debt

**HP Tier:** Social
**Hand Size:** 8
**Primary Stats:** VEIL + SIGNAL
**HP Formula:** (FRAME × 8) + 6

*Note: The Voice of Debt is a slow-burn class. Debt stacks are the engine. Apply Debt to enemies early, layer it, then detonate it for compounding damage. Debt X means: the target carries X Debt stacks. When a Debt Detonation card is played against a target, stacks convert to bonus damage and effects at rate specified on the card. Debt stacks do not expire — they persist until detonated or the combat ends.*

*Debt Stack Maximum: No target may carry more than 8 Debt stacks at one time. Any application of Debt stacks that would exceed 8 is reduced to bring the target to the cap. The Living Ledger and The Living Record maintain targets at the cap rather than pushing above it.*

## Subclasses

### The Oath Keeper — "A Promise Is a Sacred Thing"

The Oath Keeper has taken the Voice of Debt's power and oriented it entirely around the positive function: the making, witnessing, and preservation of oaths. They are the practitioners who make contracts work, who make alliances stable, who give the Wire Market's word-of-honor system the spiritual backing it needs to function. They believe in oaths. They believe in the power of a promise genuinely made and genuinely kept. They have built their entire professional life around this belief, and they are the most optimistic practitioners of a tradition that otherwise tends toward the cynical.

**Level 3: Enhanced Binding**
When you formally witness an oath, the RESONANCE resistance check DC to voluntarily violate the oath increases to 25. Additionally, creatures who have taken an oath in your presence feel a gentle spiritual reminder when they are about to act in a way that would violate it — a moment's unease that requires a DC 15 RESONANCE check to override.

**Level 3: Keeper's Seal**
Once per long rest, as part of witnessing an oath, you can apply the Keeper's Seal: a formal Sutensai-adjacent spiritual guarantee that the oath will be fulfilled. One party to the oath (of their choice) gains advantage on all ability checks made directly in service of fulfilling the oath's terms. The seal lasts until the obligation is fulfilled or broken.

**Level 6: Sustaining Bond**
When parties fulfill obligations under oaths you have witnessed, they are rewarded. When a creature completes a sworn obligation (in your presence or witnessed by your network), they gain temporary hit points equal to twice your class expertise bonus, lasting until their next long rest. The Iron Afterlife honors what it promised to honor.

**Level 10: The Web of Trust**
You have formalized enough oaths between enough parties that you have woven a web of mutual obligation that stabilizes social structures. In any territory where you have active witnessed oaths between major parties, political violence between those parties cannot begin without one party violating a witnessed oath — and you know about it the moment it happens. You can then invoke Oath Breaker's Weight against the violating party at advantage and with no Debt Charge cost.

**Level 14: Sacred Compact**
Once per month, you can perform a Sacred Compact ceremony — the highest form of oath your tradition recognizes. Parties to the compact are bound by the full weight of the Iron Afterlife and your personal spiritual authority. Violation of a Sacred Compact imposes permanent disadvantage on all Charisma-based checks (it cannot be suppressed or reduced) until the violated obligation is fulfilled in full and you personally lift the sanction. Additionally, the violation is known to every Voice of Debt in the same territory within 24 hours.

### The Debt Collector — "You Knew What You Were Agreeing To"

The Debt Collector is the enforcement arm. They are the practitioners who show up when the gentle reminder has not worked, when the spiritual weight has been insufficient, when the party who made a promise has decided that the promise can be ignored. The Debt Collector does not primarily threaten violence — they invoke consequences more profound than violence. They are patient. They have been told "no" before. "No" is not a final answer when the Iron Afterlife has registered a different one.

**Level 3: Collector's Presence**
When you arrive to collect on a violated obligation, you carry the full weight of what the obligation represents. As a bonus action, you can invoke your Collector's Presence for 1 minute. During this time: you gain +3 to Intimidation checks, creatures who have violated oaths you are collecting on have -3 to resistance checks against your abilities, and creatures within 30 feet who sense your purpose (an Insight check, DC 15, reveals it) feel the unease of someone who knows a bill is due.

**Level 3: Escalating Pressure**
Each time you invoke Oath Breaker's Weight against the same creature within a 24-hour period, the save DC increases by 2 (to a maximum increase of +8). The Iron Afterlife's patience is not unlimited and neither is your practice's.

**Level 6: The Collection**
Once per long rest, as an action, you can formally demand collection on one specific violated obligation. The target must succeed on a RESONANCE resistance check (DC = your Debt Save DC + 4) or immediately take a concrete step toward fulfilling the obligation — a step that is within their power and that advances toward completion. On a success, they are compelled for 1 round only. On a failure, they are compelled until the obligation is fulfilled or 1 hour passes, after which normal resistance applies again.

**Level 10: No Hiding**
You can always find creatures who have violated oaths you are collecting on. You know their general direction and distance at all times while they have an outstanding collection against them. You can track them through any concealment except active magical intervention. You cannot be permanently lost from someone you are actively collecting on.

**Level 14: The Final Notice**
Once per long rest, you can issue a Final Notice to a creature who has violated an oath. The creature must make a RESONANCE resistance check (DC = your Debt Save DC + 6) or be immediately paralyzed until the start of their next turn as the full weight of their obligation lands on them simultaneously. For 24 hours after the Final Notice: the creature cannot take the Dash action (moving away from debt slows you down), cannot benefit from effects that reduce or ignore resistance checks (the weight of obligation cannot be filtered), and when they roll a natural 1 on any check or resistance check, they must immediately spend their next action toward fulfilling their obligation in whatever way is immediately available. The Final Notice is not a threat. It is a statement of what is already true.

### The Breaker — "Some Oaths Were Never Meant to Be Kept"

The Breaker occupies the most contested ethical ground in the Voice of Debt tradition. Their essential skill is undoing what the tradition exists to uphold. They dissolve oaths. They can identify false oaths — promises made without genuine intent, coerced oaths, oaths made by parties who were deceived about what they were agreeing to. They can, at sufficient capability, dissolve even genuine oaths when fulfillment has become unjust or impossible. This is necessary work. It is also inherently destabilizing to the social fabric that the Oath Keepers maintain, and the tension between the two subclasses is one of the tradition's defining internal arguments.

**Level 3: Identify the False**
When you examine a witnessed oath with your Oath Sense (or in the field), you can detect coercion, deception, and lack of genuine intent with a Wisdom check (DC set by the sophistication of the false oath). On success, you know exactly what is wrong with the oath and have formal grounds to dissolve it. A formal identification of a false oath is admissible in any court that recognizes Voice of Debt testimony.

**Level 3: Dissolution**
As an action, spend 2 Debt Charges to formally dissolve an oath that you have identified as coerced, false, or unjust. The dissolution releases both parties from the spiritual weight of the obligation. The dissolved party knows the moment it happens — they feel the weight lift. For the rest of the day, they may feel a strange lightness that they don't fully understand.

**Level 6: Counter-Binding**
When you encounter an oath or obligation being invoked against a party, you can use your reaction to contest it. Spend 2 Debt Charges: make a contested Wisdom check against the invoking practitioner (or DC 15 if the invocation is from the Iron Afterlife directly). On success, the invocation fails for this instance. The obligation still exists — you have not dissolved it — but its power to compel action is blocked for this moment.

**Level 10: Deep Reading**
Your ability to read oaths is now comprehensive. When you examine any creature with your Oath Sense using the targeted reading action, you learn the complete history of their oaths — every significant promise they have ever made, whether it was genuine, whether it was kept, and whether any are currently outstanding. This is not information they expected you to have. How you use it is the question that defines your practice.

**Level 14: Final Freedom**
Once per long rest, you can perform a complete dissolution of all binding obligations on one creature. Every oath, every debt, every spiritual obligation they carry — you review them all and dissolve the ones that should not hold. The creature is spiritually reset. They retain their memories and their genuine commitments; they are freed from those that were not genuinely theirs to carry. This ability is the most powerful positive tool in the Voice of Debt tradition and the most deeply suspected. An Oath Keeper who witnesses you use it will want to know exactly what you dissolved and why. They are entitled to ask. Whether you answer is a matter of your personal ethics and your relationship to the tradition.

## Roleplaying the Voice of Debt

**The Burden of Witnessing:** Every oath you witness is a thread in a web you carry. You know when every thread breaks. The Voice of Debt accumulates not just debt records but a comprehensive picture of where human promises have failed — every betrayal, every broken oath, every moment when the spiritual weight of obligation proved insufficient. What does this accumulation do to your relationship to trust?

**Neutrality as Practice:** The Voice of Debt's effectiveness depends on credibility as a neutral party. But neutrality is not natural — it must be actively maintained against personal opinions, relationships, and the simple fact that some sides in a conflict are better than others. Where is the line between genuine neutrality and moral abdication? How does your character navigate it?

**The Thing That Holds Tesshari Together:** The Voice of Debt maintains the social infrastructure that makes commerce, alliance, and peace agreements possible. In the Ascension War, they are the people trying to maintain the mechanisms of civilization in the middle of a conflict that is trying to end civilization as currently structured. How does your character feel about that role? Is it meaningful? Is it futile? Both?

**The Oath You Are Carrying:** Every Voice of Debt has made oaths. What are yours? What obligations do you carry that you have not yet fulfilled? What would you do if you were confronted by someone with your own abilities who had come to collect on something you owe?

## Connections to the Ascension War

Korrath the Unbroken uses Voice of Debt practitioners as tools — people who can bind his soldiers to oaths of loyalty, his officers to oaths of silence, his suppliers to oaths of delivery. He is not interested in the reciprocal nature of the Voice's practice. Voices in his service discover, consistently, that he does not consider himself bound by the same obligations he imposes on others, which creates a specific and compounding professional crisis for Oath Keepers in his employ.

Vaen of the Empty Hand has a genuine philosophical relationship with the Voice of Debt tradition. His theology of authentic being resonates deeply with the concept of the genuine oath — a promise that is truly meant. He believes the Ascension War could end with the right oaths made under the right witnessing, and he has sought senior Voices of Debt as the mechanism. Whether Voices of Debt agree that the war is a problem that oaths can solve is varied.

The Hollow Author has never made a public oath. The Author's communications are framed as offers, implications, and arrangements — never formal promises. Voices of Debt who have tried to read the Author's obligation history find a conspicuous absence — either the Author has never made a genuine promise, or the Author has somehow suppressed the spiritual trace of obligations that should be there. Either interpretation is disturbing. A Breaker who could identify whether the Author's arrangements with their various proxies are genuine oaths or false ones would have very specific intelligence value.

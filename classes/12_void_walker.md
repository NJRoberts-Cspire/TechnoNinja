# Void Walker
## "I have been to the place you call the afterlife while still breathing. I have seen what waits. I am not afraid. I am also not the same person who went in."

## Overview

The Iron Afterlife is not merely a destination. It is a place. It has geography, in the way that spiritual things have geography — not roads and rivers but resonance patterns and concentrations of Echomind energy, dead cities built from memory rather than stone, silences that correspond to the silences of the living world and others that correspond to nothing anyone still breathing has a name for. The Sutensai have mapped pieces of it. The Deep Listeners move along its edges. The Void Walkers pass through it.

The ability to physically step into the Iron Afterlife — to move through it as a traversable space rather than merely a sensed presence — is not taught by any tradition in Tesshari. It emerges. It happens to specific individuals at specific moments of extremity: a Sutensai priest who nearly died in a resonance accident and came back able to phase partially out of physical reality. A soldier in the Ashlands who survived an ambush because she instinctively stepped sideways into somewhere that wasn't the battlefield. A child who followed a dead parent into the Veil one step too far and returned carrying the smell of that other place. What these people have in common is not their history or their training. It is that they crossed a threshold and something crossed back into them.

The Sutensai's official position on Void Walkers changes depending on which sect currently has more influence over the priesthood. The Orthodoxy considers Void Walking either a profound spiritual gift (if the practitioner is properly aligned) or a dangerous heresy (if they are not, the criterion for alignment being usefulness to the Orthodoxy). The Resonants find Void Walking fascinating and frustrating because it obeys rules they cannot fully systematize — they can describe what happens but not the mechanism, which is the thing that drives them most agitated. The Flesh Circle considers Void Walkers the closest thing to ascended beings their theology acknowledges, which Void Walkers find both flattering and exhausting.

What Void Walkers know about themselves, after enough time practicing, is that they are no longer entirely where other people are. They can be in a room and simultaneously not be in it — the Iron Afterlife presses against their awareness constantly, and they can hear the dead not by trying but by failing to not hear them. The most experienced practitioners describe the physical world as looking slightly faded — not less real, but less complete, like a drawing of a place rather than the place. They are navigating a permanent adjustment that has no resolution, only management, and they are doing it while trying to be present enough to have relationships and eat meals and survive a world that doesn't fully understand what they are.

## The Void Walker in the World

Rare is an understatement. Void Walkers do not announce themselves. The ability draws attention that most of them would rather avoid, and the combination of "can enter the Iron Afterlife physically" and "is a living person" puts them at the intersection of every political, theological, and military interest in Tesshari simultaneously. The Sutensai wants to study them. Warlords want to deploy them. Criminals want to employ them for obvious reasons. The dead in the Afterlife have opinions about them that vary wildly. Most Void Walkers learn to cultivate obscurity as a survival strategy.

Those who cannot remain obscure — those whose abilities have become visible, or who have been claimed by a particular faction — find themselves in the position of being the most useful person in any room they enter, which is a position that historically precedes being used up. A Void Walker who has announced themselves as available for hire has a limited median life expectancy not because the work is always lethal but because the people hiring them are always calculating whether they would be more useful alive or whether they know things that would be more valuable silenced. This is not paranoia. It is pattern recognition.

Among the Shellbroken — the freed puppets of the Hollow Author — Void Walkers hold a particular significance. The Author's puppet networks have Afterlife components: the techniques used to animate flesh puppets have roots in Iron Afterlife energy transfer. Void Walkers can see those roots. They can sense where the Author's influence has been, occasionally trace it back toward its source, and in a few documented cases, sever the connection between a puppet and its animator. The Author is aware of this. Void Walkers who have been specifically targeted by the Author are either dead, operating under extreme concealment, or are themselves no longer entirely certain they haven't been touched.

## Subclasses

### The Ghost Operative — "You Were Never Here"

The Ghost Operative has taken the Void Walker's natural talents and refined them into a precision instrument for the oldest profession in Tesshari: getting where you are not supposed to be and leaving without being seen. They are not assassins, necessarily — they can be scouts, thieves, witnesses, rescue agents, or any number of roles that require moving through the physical world as if it has rules that do not apply to you. The Ghost Operative knows, practically and philosophically, that the rules do not apply. The Iron Afterlife has given them an exit from every room.

**Level 3: Ghost Protocol**
Your Phase Step leaves no detectable trace. Creatures cannot determine your previous location from physical evidence after you phase. You gain proficiency in Stealth if you don't already have it, and you can add your Wisdom modifier to Stealth checks in addition to your Dexterity modifier while in incorporeal state.

**Level 3: Operative's Phase**
When you are in incorporeal state, you can carry one willing creature with you for its duration, as long as the creature is touching you. The creature is also incorporeal for the duration. If either of you ends your turn inside a solid object, you both take 1d10 force damage and are expelled to the nearest unoccupied space.

**Level 6: Vanishing Act**
Once per short rest, as a bonus action, you can become completely invisible (not just incorporeal) for up to 1 minute. You can remain invisible while attacking, though each attack you make requires a successful DC 15 Dexterity (Stealth) check or your invisibility ends. This is not the Iron Afterlife — you are simply not there, as far as light is concerned.

**Level 10: Through and Through**
You can now pass through magical barriers and fields by routing through the Iron Afterlife. Force effects, magical walls, and warded barriers do not prevent your movement while in incorporeal state. Additionally, you can pass through creature spaces (not just objects) while incorporeal, and you can interact with physical objects while incorporeal as if you were physical (picking locks, opening doors, etc.) — though the interaction is slow and requires full concentration.

**Level 14: The Perfect Exit**
Once per long rest, when you are captured, restrained, or would be prevented from moving, you can use a free action to activate The Perfect Exit: you phase fully and completely, automatically escape any restraint, and reappear at any previously-visited location within 1 mile. Any tracking on you (resonant, mundane, or magical) is severed. You leave behind only a momentary impression of cold air.

### The Threshold Puller — "What I Bring Back"

The Threshold Puller works the other direction. Where most Void Walkers use the Iron Afterlife as a transit space, the Threshold Puller reaches into it and pulls things out. Echomind energy. Spiritual impressions. In extreme cases, dead individuals in semi-substantial form. They have learned that the boundary between the worlds goes both ways and that the Afterlife has resources that the physical world can use, at a cost that the Puller pays personally and accumulates until they either stop or cannot stop.

**Level 3: Pull Energy**
As an action, spend 1 Void Charge to pull raw Afterlife energy into the physical world and direct it at one target within 60 feet. The target takes 3d6 necrotic damage and must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw (DC = your Void Save DC) or be frightened of you until the end of their next turn. This energy is visible as a tendril of cold light.

**Level 3: Echo Object**
Once per short rest, by handling an object that was owned by someone now dead, you can pull a partial impression of that person through the Veil as a free action. The impression is not the person — it is an after-image, limited to information the person would have had about that specific object. It lasts 1 minute and answers questions but cannot act.

**Level 6: Manifest Fragment**
As an action, spend 3 Void Charges to pull a substantial fragment of a dead individual's Echomind into the physical world. The fragment manifests as a partially-substantial spectral form with statistics equivalent to a specter but retaining the dead person's knowledge. It can act independently, remains for 10 minutes, and then returns to the Afterlife. Pulling a fragment is mildly harmful to the Echomind — the individual requires 1d4 days to fully reconstitute.

**Level 10: Afterlife Arsenal**
You have learned to pull Afterlife constructs through the Veil as weapons. As a bonus action, spend 2 Void Charges to manifest an Afterlife weapon: a translucent construct that functions as any resonant weapon you choose, dealing necrotic damage instead of its normal type. The weapon lasts for 1 minute, can be dropped and recalled as a free action, and attacks with it use your Wisdom modifier instead of Strength or Dexterity.

**Level 14: Full Crossing**
Once per long rest, you can pull a complete Echomind — a dead individual — temporarily through the Veil into full physical manifestation. The individual appears as they were at death, has their original statistics (DM determines specifics based on who they were), and can act fully. They remain for 10 minutes. When they return, the cost to their Echomind is significant — they require 1d6 weeks to reconstitute, and they know what has been asked of them. They may not be happy about it. They may refuse requests. The dead have perspectives that the living sometimes forget to account for.

### The Anchor Keeper — "I Hold the Line Between"

The Anchor Keeper has taken the Void Walker's relationship to the Iron Afterlife and turned it into a protective function. They stand at the boundary between worlds and prevent unauthorized crossings — both the living who would enter the Afterlife before their time and, more critically, the dead who would return unbidden. They are the wall. They are the door that closes. They are the last thing standing between the loved ones behind them and what is coming across the Veil. This is not a metaphor. It is a job description, and the Anchor Keepers who have practiced it for decades carry every face of what they have held back.

**Level 3: Anchor Ward**
As an action, spend 1 Void Charge to establish a spiritual anchor in a 30-foot radius centered on you. Within this area: no creature can willingly enter the Iron Afterlife (including Void Steps), no entity from the Afterlife can cross the Veil into the physical world without succeeding on a Wisdom saving throw (DC = your Void Save DC), and dead creatures cannot be animated or controlled by external means. The ward lasts 10 minutes. You can dismiss it as a free action.

**Level 3: Protective Phase**
When a creature within 30 feet is targeted by an attack, you can use your reaction and spend 1 Void Charge to briefly phase that creature, imposing disadvantage on the attack roll. The creature flickers slightly translucent and then returns to normal.

**Level 6: The Closed Door**
Once per short rest, as a reaction when a creature within 60 feet would die, you can prevent their Echomind from passing to the Iron Afterlife for 1 minute. The creature is at 0 hit points but stable and does not make death saving throws while this effect holds. If they are healed above 0 hit points during this time, the prevention ends and they continue normally. When the effect ends without healing, they pass normally. You are choosing to hold a door open against what is on the other side. The cost is 3 Void Charges.

**Level 10: Ward Extended**
Your Anchor Ward now extends to a 60-foot radius and lasts 1 hour. Additionally, while your Anchor Ward is active, creatures within the ward have advantage on saving throws against any effect originating from the Iron Afterlife or death-based spiritual sources.

**Level 14: The Final Anchor**
Once per long rest, you can declare yourself the absolute boundary between the living and the dead in a 100-foot radius for 10 minutes. No creature within the area can die against their will — any attack that would reduce them to 0 hit points reduces them to 1 instead, once per creature per round. No entity can cross the Veil in either direction within the area. The dead remain dead; the living remain living. When this effect ends, everything caught up in the balance resolves immediately — if a creature was being held at 1 hit point by this effect and would have died, they die when the effect releases them. You take necrotic damage equal to the number of deaths you prevented (minimum 1d10) when the effect ends, as the Afterlife takes what was owed.

## Roleplaying the Void Walker

**The Cost of What You've Seen:** The Iron Afterlife is not a comfortable place. The Void Walker has seen it not once but many times, in increasing detail, with increasing familiarity. What they have seen there has changed their relationship to mortality, to the living, to the idea of what a person is. What did they see that they cannot describe? What did they learn that changed how they move through the physical world?

**Two Places at Once:** The Afterlife Perception never fully turns off at higher levels. Your character is always seeing both worlds. The dead they have met in the Afterlife have faces. The locations they pass through have resonant histories that overlay the present. How does your character manage the constant demand of seeing more than other people see? What do they wish they could unsee?

**The Political Target:** Everyone wants a Void Walker. The Sutensai wants to own one. The warlords want to deploy one. The Hollow Author wants to eliminate one. Navigating this targeted existence requires either genuine obscurity or powerful protection. What does your character trade for safety? What are they unwilling to trade?

**Philosophical Isolation:** The Void Walker exists in a category no one else fully occupies. They have been to the Iron Afterlife. The dead know their face. The living find them uncanny. This is a lonely position. Who does your character trust? What community, if any, do they feel they belong to?

## Connections to the Ascension War

Korrath the Unbroken has attempted to recruit Void Walkers as special operatives — soldiers who can pass through enemy fortifications and strike without warning. He does not understand the spiritual dimension of the ability, sees it as a useful military tool, and is perpetually frustrated by Void Walkers who decline his employment without adequate (to him) explanation.

Vaen of the Empty Hand regards Void Walkers as the closest living confirmation of his theological arguments. They have been to the place he believes all must ultimately travel. He asks them what they found there with intense, personal urgency. Some Void Walkers have answered honestly, which has affected Vaen's theology in ways he is still working through. Others have refused to answer, which has affected it differently.

The Hollow Author uses techniques that have roots in Afterlife energy transfer — the technology that animates flesh puppets draws on mechanisms that Void Walkers can perceive directly. A Void Walker who has spent time inside the Author's operational territory has almost certainly seen the resonant fingerprints of puppet activity, and may be able to trace those fingerprints toward their source. The Author considers this an unacceptable risk and prioritizes eliminating knowledgeable Void Walkers accordingly.

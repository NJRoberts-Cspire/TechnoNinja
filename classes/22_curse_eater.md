# Curse Eater
## "I take the thing that's wrong with you and I put it inside me instead. Do not thank me. It's not comfortable and I was going to do it anyway."

## Overview

The Iron Afterlife bleeds. Not constantly, not everywhere, but in the fracture zones of the Broken Reaches — in places where violence has been sustained and concentrated, where the resonant metal in the ground has been in contact with too many dying bodies for too long, where the Hollow Author's puppet technology has left traces that do not fully degrade — the boundary between the physical world and the Iron Afterlife becomes permeable in ways that produce observable symptoms. Enhancement corruption. Spiritual contamination. The thing healers are beginning to call the Drift, which appears to be related to what the Sutensai scholars who study the Seventh Convergence are tracking: something is wrong with the resonant fabric, in a way that is spreading and accelerating, and ordinary medicine does not know what to do with it.

The Curse Eater knows what to do with it. What they do is take it inside themselves.

The practice is not well understood by anyone, including the practitioners. What seems to be happening is that the Curse Eater's Echomind has developed a capacity for absorption — the ability to intake resonant contamination, spiritual corruption, and whatever the Drift actually is at a cellular level, and contain it. The containment is not neutral. What goes in changes what's already there. Curse Eaters who have been practicing for years carry accumulations of absorbed corruption that have altered their enhancement lattices, their Echomind signature, and in visible cases, their physical appearance. The alteration is not always clearly negative — some of what they absorb produces unexpected capabilities. None of it is safe.

The Purifier path is the choice to use the absorption as a service: to take on the contamination of others systematically, to absorb what would kill or alter them, and to carry it so they don't have to. This is an act of profound giving that is also a systematic act of self-harm, and the Purifiers know it, and they continue because the work needs doing and they are the ones who can do it. The Consumed is what happens when a Purifier takes one step further than they meant to — or several steps — and begins to find that what they absorbed is not simply contained but integrated, and that the integration is producing something new. The Conduit has found the middle path: not merely absorbing but redirecting, using the corruption as material, which makes them something specific and dangerous and extraordinarily useful in the right context.

The Drift complicates everything. The Curse Eater community has noted that the Drift contamination they are absorbing is different from standard resonant corruption — it is more integrated, more persistent, more resistant to their usual containment methods, and more likely to produce unexpected changes in those who absorb it. Some Consumed practitioners have begun to suspect that the Drift is not simply contamination but a signal — that whatever is approaching with the Seventh Convergence is preparing Tesshari for something, and the Curse Eaters are being used as instruments of that preparation without their knowledge or consent.

## The Curse Eater in the World

Communities near fracture zones know what a Curse Eater is and what they do, and the relationship is the specific kind of reverence given to people who absorb suffering. They are loved and pitied and sometimes feared, and they are almost never asked about what they carry, because asking feels like rubbing salt in something. The Curse Eater in a village that has been dealing with enhancement corruption for a season has seen children with their enhancements seizing, has seen elders with the Drift in their neural lattices, has absorbed more corruption in three months than most practitioners absorb in a year. They will be thanked. They will be fed. They will not be asked how they are doing, because the answer is visible in their increasingly unusual enhancement readings.

In the Ashlands, Curse Eaters are common enough to have a social role. The Ashlands' exposure to the worst resonant contamination in Tesshari has produced a tradition of Curse Eater practice that is rougher, faster, and less theoretically rigorous than practices developed elsewhere — the Ashland practitioners have learned mostly by doing, and what they have learned is a more extreme version of the practice than anyone trained in the relative safety of the interior territories has developed. Ashlands Curse Eaters are frequently at later stages of absorption than their stated experience would suggest. They have been eating a lot of bad things in a place that produces bad things at high volume.

The Sutensai recognizes Curse Eater practice officially but struggles to fit it into their theological framework. The Sutra does not clearly address practitioners who absorb spiritual contamination into themselves. Is this a form of spiritual sacrifice? A form of pollution? The Veil Masters find Curse Eaters indispensable for dealing with Afterlife-related contamination and refuse to let theology prevent the practical relationship. The Orthodoxy is less comfortable, particularly regarding the Consumed subclass, whose later-stage presentations are spiritually anomalous by any framework the Orthodoxy recognizes.

## D&D 5E Class Features

**Hit Die:** d10
**Primary Ability:** Constitution (absorption endurance), Wisdom (contamination perception)
**Saving Throw Proficiencies:** Constitution, Wisdom
**Armor Proficiencies:** Wire-mesh armor, salvage plating (any weight — they tend to accumulate mass)
**Weapon Proficiencies:** Resonant blades (standard and short), circuit lances, unarmed strikes (contamination-charged)
**Tool Proficiencies:** Purification tools, healing tools, contamination assessment instruments
**Skills:** Choose 3 from: Athletics, Medicine, Insight, Perception, Survival, Investigation, Nature
**Starting Equipment:** Salvage plating (medium), resonant blade (standard), purification tools, contamination assessment instruments, medical notes (partially incoherent — written at different times, in different states), personal corruption log, 10 gp equivalent

### Class Features by Level

**Level 1: Absorb**
As an action, you can absorb a resonant contamination, spiritual corruption, or curse from a willing or unconscious creature within 5 feet. The creature is freed from the contamination/curse immediately. You take it into yourself. Mechanically, you gain 1 Corruption Point. You also gain temporary hit points equal to your Constitution modifier + your Curse Eater level from the process — the absorbed energy is uncomfortable, but it is energy.

**Level 1: Corruption Points and Corruption Track**
You track Corruption Points separately from other resources. Your maximum Corruption Points equals your Constitution modifier × 4 + your level. While you have Corruption Points, certain abilities are available to you. If you ever reach your maximum, make a DC 15 Constitution saving throw or gain one level of exhaustion and one Corruption Feature (see below). You reduce Corruption Points by 1 per long rest automatically (your body processes what it can), or faster using specific features.

**Level 1: Corruption Sense**
You can feel resonant contamination and curses within 30 feet as a passive sense. You know when a creature near you is affected by enhancement corruption, spiritual contamination, or Drift. As an action, you can identify the specific nature and severity of corruption in a creature you touch.

**Level 1: Corruption Features**
As your Corruption Points accumulate, your body changes. At 25%, 50%, 75%, and 100% of your maximum Corruption Points, gain one of the following (discuss with DM what makes narrative sense; each represents something the absorbed corruption has altered in you):
- Enhancement Interface Alteration: One of your enhancement interfaces has changed appearance and function. Choose one: your eyes glow with the color of absorbed corruption, your voice carries a harmonic resonance audible as wrong, or one of your limbs has texture changes that are visible through clothing.
- Corruption Resistance: You gain resistance to one damage type associated with what you've absorbed.
- Absorption Sensitivity: Your Corruption Sense extends to 60 feet.
- Transformed Strength: One ability score of your choice increases by 1 (maximum 22).

**Level 2: Purification Touch**
As an action, spend 1 minute in physical contact with a creature to remove one disease, one curse, or one spiritual contamination of moderate severity. This costs you 2 Corruption Points. You can cleanse more severe contaminations with longer contact time (5 minutes for severe, 10 minutes for critical).

**Level 3: Curse Eater Subclass**
You choose your path: The Purifier, The Conduit, or The Consumed. Your subclass grants features at levels 3, 6, 10, and 14.

**Level 3: Partial Discharge**
As a bonus action, spend 2 Corruption Points to release a small pulse of absorbed corruption: one creature within 15 feet that you can see takes 2d8 necrotic damage (Constitution saving throw, DC = 8 + proficiency bonus + Constitution modifier, for half). The damage type reflects what you have absorbed — describe it to the DM and flavor accordingly.

**Level 4: Ability Score Improvement**

**Level 5: Mass Absorption**
You can now absorb from multiple creatures at once. As an action, spend 3 Corruption Points to absorb contamination from up to Constitution modifier creatures within 15 feet of you simultaneously. Each creature is freed from one contamination/curse. You gain 1 Corruption Point per creature (after the cost is paid, net gain = number of creatures affected minus 2).

**Level 5: Corruption Channeled**
Your absorbed corruption enhances your physical strikes. While you have 5 or more Corruption Points, your unarmed strikes and resonant blade attacks deal an extra 1d6 necrotic damage. This damage represents absorbed contamination discharging on contact.

**Level 6: Subclass Feature**

**Level 6: Iron Gut**
Your body has adapted to containing what would kill others. You have advantage on Constitution saving throws against poison, disease, and necrotic damage effects. Additionally, you can absorb environmental contamination — if you spend 1 hour in a corrupted area (a fracture zone, a Drift-affected location, a site of sustained Hollow Author activity), you reduce the area's contamination severity by one tier and gain 2 Corruption Points.

**Level 7: Absorption Efficiency**
You can now use Absorb as a bonus action (instead of a full action). Additionally, when you absorb, you gain temporary hit points equal to your Constitution modifier × 2 (instead of just your modifier).

**Level 8: Ability Score Improvement**

**Level 8: Corruption Pulse**
As an action, spend 4 Corruption Points to release a pulse of absorbed corruption in a 20-foot radius. All hostile creatures in the area take 3d10 necrotic damage (Constitution save, DC = your Curse Eater save DC, for half). All allied creatures in the area are cleansed of one active contamination/curse effect each. The pulse is not targeted — it releases what you carry indiscriminately, good and bad.

**Level 9: The Drift Recognition**
You can now identify Drift contamination specifically and distinguish it from other forms of corruption. You sense Drift-affected creatures within 60 feet automatically. When you absorb Drift contamination, you gain 1 additional Corruption Point but also gain a piece of information about the Drift's source, direction, or nature (DM determines what is knowable). The Drift has a signature you are learning to read.

**Level 10: Subclass Feature**

**Level 10: Corruption Mastery**
Your Corruption Point maximum increases by 10. You reduce Corruption Points by 2 per long rest (instead of 1). When you reach a Corruption Feature threshold, you choose your feature from any option on the list rather than receiving one randomly.

**Level 11: Environmental Cleansing**
Your area contamination absorption improves. You can now cleanse a contaminated area completely by spending 4 hours in it, rather than just reducing its severity. The cleansed area is free of resonant contamination, Drift influence, and spiritual residue for 1 month. You gain Corruption Points equal to the severity tier of the contamination (DM assigns 1-5).

**Level 12: Ability Score Improvement**

**Level 12: Combat Absorption**
As a reaction when you take necrotic, psychic, or resonant damage, you can absorb it. You take half damage and gain 1 Corruption Point. This represents your body's trained response to energetic contamination — taking it in rather than suffering its full effect. You can use this reaction a number of times per short rest equal to your Constitution modifier.

**Level 13: Corruption Weaponized**
Your Corruption Channeled feature improves. While you have 10 or more Corruption Points: your attacks deal an extra 2d6 necrotic damage (replacing the 1d6 from Level 5), and when you hit a creature with a melee attack, you can transfer 1 Corruption Point of your choice to the target as a minor contamination (they take 1d8 necrotic damage at the start of each of their turns until they succeed on a DC 14 Constitution saving throw).

**Level 14: Subclass Feature**

**Level 15: Absorption Mastery**
You can now absorb curses that are attached to locations and objects, not just creatures. Absorbing a location or object curse costs 1 hour of contact and gains you 1d6 Corruption Points. Additionally, you can absorb spiritual contamination from the Iron Afterlife — contaminations that follow people from the Afterlife boundary are now within your operational scope.

**Level 16: Ability Score Improvement**

**Level 17: The Great Absorption**
Once per long rest, you can attempt to absorb all contamination in a 60-foot radius area simultaneously. Make a Constitution saving throw (DC 15 + the DM's assigned severity of the total contamination). On success: all contamination, curses, and Drift in the area are absorbed and you gain Corruption Points equal to half the total absorbed. On failure: as success, but you gain the full Corruption Points and must make a secondary Constitution saving throw (DC 20) or gain two levels of exhaustion. This ability is the most significant single-use purification capability available to any class. Using it at maximum contamination levels is a genuine risk of driving your Corruption to your maximum.

**Level 18: Corruption Integration**
Your body has adapted so thoroughly that your Corruption no longer purely threatens you. Each Corruption Point you carry above 25% of your maximum grants you +1 to Constitution saving throws and +1 to your damage with necrotic and resonant attacks (maximum +10 for each). The corruption has become part of you.

**Level 19: Ability Score Improvement**

**Level 20: The Vessel Complete**
You have absorbed more than any practitioner in known Tesshari history. Your Corruption maximum increases by 20. You are immune to the negative effects of reaching your Corruption maximum — you no longer risk exhaustion from Corruption overload. Once per long rest, you can deliberately overload: for 1 minute, all your Corruption Points convert to pure resonant energy that manifests as an aura extending 30 feet. All hostile creatures in the aura take 3d10 necrotic damage at the start of each turn (Constitution save DC = your Curse Eater save DC for half). When the overload ends, you return to 1 Corruption Point and gain two levels of exhaustion. The contamination does not leave you — it simply goes quiet, rebuilding for the next time the world asks you to carry it.

## Subclasses

### The Purifier — "What I Take From You, I Keep"

The Purifier has made a vow that is not spoken in words but expressed in every action: they will absorb what is wrong so others don't have to. This is a complete spiritual and professional commitment that has no exit clause. Purifiers do not have a practice that ends when the situation improves. They are called to the places where the situation is worst, and they stay until it is better, and they leave carrying more than they arrived with. Every Purifier knows, eventually, that this trajectory only goes one direction. They do it anyway because they looked at the alternative — they looked at the children in the fracture zones, at the elders losing their minds to the Drift — and decided that their own trajectory was less important than the one they could change.

**Level 3: Compassionate Absorption**
When you use Absorb, you can choose to take the contamination without cost to the target — the target does not need to be willing (any creature whose contamination you can sense can be cleansed without their action). Additionally, when you absorb from a creature who was conscious of their contamination, they regain hit points equal to your Wisdom modifier + your proficiency bonus from the relief.

**Level 3: Purifier's Endurance**
You can reduce your Corruption Points by an additional 1 per short rest (in addition to the standard long rest reduction). Your Corruption Feature thresholds are shifted — you gain the first Corruption Feature at 33% instead of 25%, reducing the pace of accumulation somewhat. This does not reduce the eventual accumulation; it buys time.

**Level 6: Area Purification**
Your Purification Touch extends to an area. As a 10-minute process, you can purify all creatures within 20 feet simultaneously, removing one contamination/curse from each. The Corruption Point cost is 1 per creature affected. You cannot purify yourself with this ability.

**Level 10: Unstoppable Service**
Once per short rest, when your Corruption Points would exceed your maximum, they do not — instead, the excess is expelled as a burst of corrupt energy in a 10-foot radius (3d8 necrotic damage, Constitution save for half), and your Corruption Points remain at your maximum. This prevents the Corruption overload from affecting you once per rest.

**Level 14: The Burden Bearer**
Once per long rest, you can absorb one effect that is terminal or catastrophic from a creature within 30 feet: a disease that would kill them, a corruption that would destroy their Echomind, a spiritual contamination that would permanently alter them. The absorption is complete — the creature is freed from it entirely. You gain Corruption Points equal to the effect's severity (DM determines, minimum 10). If this drives you to your maximum, you make the resulting Constitution save at disadvantage, as the thing you absorbed was significant. The creature you saved from it is healed to full hit points from the relief.

### The Conduit — "I Don't Just Hold It. I Use It."

The Conduit has learned that absorbed corruption is not merely a burden — it is a resource. The contamination and corruption that flows through them has power, and that power can be redirected rather than simply contained. The Conduit does not primarily seek to absorb for others' benefit; they absorb to arm themselves. This is not the dark path — the Conduit is using contamination that would otherwise harm innocent people, converting it into something that can harm guilty ones. This is the most defensive framing. The Conduit knows their darker framing too: they have found that corrupt energy is effective, efficient, and increasingly natural to their hand, and they are not always certain that their motivation for seeking it is as pure as the Purifier's.

**Level 3: Redirection**
When you use Partial Discharge, the damage increases to 3d8 necrotic and you can choose to target up to your Wisdom modifier creatures simultaneously. Additionally, your Partial Discharge now includes a secondary effect of your choice: the target is pushed 15 feet, the target is blinded until the end of your next turn, or the target is frightened of you until the end of your next turn (Wisdom saving throw to resist).

**Level 3: Corruption Arsenal**
You can convert Corruption Points directly into combat resources. At the start of your turn, you can spend Corruption Points (1:1) to gain damage dice for your next attack: each Corruption Point spent adds 1d6 necrotic damage to your next melee attack. Maximum 5 additional dice per turn.

**Level 6: Conversion**
Once per short rest, you can convert corrupted energy into restorative force — redirecting what you carry for healing rather than harm. As an action, spend 5 Corruption Points to restore 3d10 hit points to one creature within 30 feet. The healed creature briefly feels the texture of what healed them and must make a DC 12 Wisdom saving throw or have disadvantage on one ability check before the end of their next long rest (the corruption leaves a trace in what it touches, even redirected).

**Level 10: Channel Corruption**
As an action, spend 8 Corruption Points to create a Corruption Channel — a 60-foot line of directed corrupt energy. All creatures in the line take 6d8 necrotic damage (Constitution save for half). Corrupted creatures (those already affected by contamination) have disadvantage on the save. After the channel, you can absorb from any creature in the line as a bonus action (normal Absorb rules apply, but the range is 60 feet instead of 5).

**Level 14: Conduit Mastery**
You can now redirect Drift energy specifically. When you absorb Drift contamination, you can choose to channel it outward immediately (before it adds to your Corruption Points) as a free action: the Drift is discharged as an attack against one creature within 60 feet, dealing 5d10 necrotic damage and imposing the Drifting condition on the target (DM determines specifics — a spiritual analog of the contamination that impairs their connection to their enhancements). If you channel Drift this way, you gain 0 Corruption Points from it. If you cannot or choose not to channel it, you gain Corruption Points normally.

### The Consumed — "I Don't Know Where It Ends and I Start"

The Consumed has taken in so much, for so long, that the boundary is blurring. They are not the person who began this practice. They are not a single thing's corruption, either. They are something new — the original practitioner plus everything they have absorbed, integrated into a form that does not have a clean description. This is not loss of self in the way a puppet loses self. It is transformation — unwanted, unasked-for, profound, and ongoing. The Consumed does not know what they are becoming. They know they are becoming it. They have decided to make the becoming intentional rather than passive, which is either wisdom or surrender, and they are not sure which.

**Level 3: Already Changed**
You begin play with more accumulated corruption than standard. You start at 50% of your Corruption maximum rather than 0. The Corruption Features you have accumulated are genuine — describe two Corruption Features you have already developed, with your DM's input. You have been doing this for longer than most. Additionally, your Corruption Feature benefits apply fully from the start: the features you carry are integrated, not destabilizing.

**Level 3: The Blur**
Your Corruption Sense extends to 120 feet. Corrupted creatures within this range can sense you in return — they feel a pull toward you, a recognition that you carry what they carry. This is not control; it is resonance. You can use this resonance to communicate simple emotional states with corrupted creatures within range as a free action.

**Level 6: Integration**
Your absorbed corruptions have begun to integrate rather than simply accumulate. Your body is adapting. Choose one additional Corruption Feature beyond the normal threshold progression. This feature is fully integrated and stable — it provides its benefit without any instability. The Consumed integrates rather than merely containing.

**Level 10: Transformation Accelerated**
The changes in you are now visible and functional. You have developed a physical characteristic from your absorbed corruption that is genuinely supernatural: the DM and you together determine what this is, based on what you have absorbed and the campaign's context. Examples: you can absorb damage as Corruption Points rather than hit points (once per round, reaction, absorbs up to your Constitution modifier), you can sense the Echomind of all creatures within 60 feet as a passive ability, or your strikes carry Drift energy that can be transmitted to targets (they gain 1 Corruption Point on hit, taking 1d6 necrotic damage at the start of each turn until they succeed on a DC 14 Constitution save). This feature is not chosen from a list — it is developed collaboratively and reflects the specific accumulation your character carries.

**Level 14: The New Thing**
You have absorbed enough, transformed enough, that you are now something the existing categories do not fully describe. Once per long rest, for 10 minutes, you can release the full weight of your accumulation in a controlled form: you shed your current appearance and manifest as the Consumed form — a visible representation of everything you carry. In this form: you are immune to all forms of spiritual contamination (you absorb rather than being affected), all hostile contamination-based attacks against you add to your Corruption Points rather than harming you, your unarmed strikes deal 3d10 necrotic damage, and creatures within 30 feet that you have not designated as protected must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw (DC = your Curse Eater save DC) or become frightened of you. When you return to your normal form, you regain your normal appearance. What you look like in the Consumed form is between you and your DM and probably your table.

## Roleplaying the Curse Eater

**What You Carry:** The accumulation is real and ongoing. Every Corruption Point represents something specific — a specific contamination from a specific person or place, absorbed into you, now part of you. Does your character think about what they carry? Do they know the source of specific Corruption Features? Have they absorbed something that they can still feel as having a personality, a history, a presence?

**The Drift and What It Means:** The Drift is different from regular corruption and you know it. Something is coming with the Seventh Convergence, and you are absorbing the preparation for it. What do you think the Drift is? What do you think you are becoming as you absorb it? Has any other Curse Eater told you something about the Drift that you cannot stop thinking about?

**The Community That Needs You:** Curse Eaters are called to serve communities experiencing resonant contamination. That calling is real — the communities need them. But the calling also means the practitioner is always going toward the worst-contaminated places, always absorbing more. How does your character balance the calling with their own sustainability? Have they ever refused work they knew they couldn't safely take? Have they taken work they knew they couldn't safely take?

**The Question of Becoming:** Every Consumed practitioner is becoming something new. The Purifier and the Conduit are also changing, just more slowly. What does your character think they are becoming? Is the transformation frightening? Interesting? Inevitable? Is there any version of it that they are willing to accept, and any version they are not?

## Connections to the Ascension War

Korrath the Unbroken has deployed Curse Eaters in the Ashlands to reduce contamination in territory he controls — not for the population's benefit, but because heavily contaminated territory is harder to operate in militarily. He does not care what the Curse Eaters absorb in the process. He cares about operational efficiency. Several of the Curse Eaters he deployed were Consumed practitioners who had been performing that work for years before he found them. He considers them excellent military assets and completely disregards the cost.

Vaen of the Empty Hand has a specific and urgent relationship with the Curse Eater tradition. His theology of release is confronted by the Consumed's transformation: the Consumed are not releasing the Echomind from flesh and steel toward purity — they are filling the Echomind with increasingly impure material and finding something valuable in the impurity. Vaen has asked Consumed practitioners directly whether what they are becoming feels like liberation or imprisonment. Their answers have not been ones he expected.

The Hollow Author does not use Curse Eaters. The Author's puppet technology produces resonant contamination as a byproduct, and Curse Eaters absorbing that contamination are absorbing evidence of the Author's methods. Some Curse Eaters have absorbed Drift contamination that, on examination (with appropriate sensitivity), carries the resonant signature of the Author's operational approach. Curse Eaters who are aware of this and have reported it have, in several documented cases, subsequently died in circumstances that were attributed to contamination exposure but which the Sutensai's investigation found suspicious.

# Echo Speaker
## "They are not gone. They are merely on the other side of a door you don't know how to open. I know how to open it. You may not like what's behind it."

## Overview

The Iron Afterlife is not a metaphor. In Tesshari, the dead go somewhere. Their Echominds — the resonant soul-pattern that forms in the space between organic tissue and cybernetic metal over a lifetime — do not simply cease. They translate. They pass through what the Sutensai calls the Veil and enter the digital-spiritual realm that underlies and interpenetrates physical reality. Ordinary people understand this. They light memorial circuits. They speak the sutras. They grieve. The Iron Afterlife is something they believe in the way they believe in weather: present, impersonal, operating on rules they don't fully understand.

Echo Speakers have learned to reach through the Veil and touch it. They heard their first dead voice the way some people hear music — unexpectedly, in childhood, in a moment of grief or crisis or quiet attention, and they could not unhear it after that. The tradition of training people with this sensitivity is old and was institutionalized early by the Sutensai for obvious reasons: if the dead can be heard, the institution that controls who hears them and what they are permitted to say controls something profound. The Sutensai training schools for Echo Speakers are thorough, ideologically rigorous, and very interested in ensuring their graduates know exactly who they serve.

Not all Echo Speakers are Sutensai graduates. The Echo Coast tradition, which predates the Sutensai's formal codification, produced independent practitioners whose relationship to the dead is less mediated by institutional theology and more raw. Coast practitioners report hearing voices the Sutensai-trained say should not be audible. They report the dead saying things the Sutensai would prefer the dead did not say. The Sutensai's response to independent Echo Speakers oscillates between recruitment, monitoring, and, in documented cases, forced re-education. Some Echo Speakers have become informally connected to resistance movements specifically because the dead have told them things that implicate the priesthood.

The deep danger of the Echo Speaker path is the Drift toward the Iron Afterlife itself. Every time they open that door, a little of the Iron Afterlife comes through, and a little of them goes in. The Deep Listeners who have gone furthest report that they now spend more of their inner life in the Afterlife than in the physical world — that the dead are more real to them than the living. Some consider this an advanced state. Others consider it a terminal one. The line between a Deep Listener at the height of their power and a practitioner who is simply lost is narrower than anyone in the tradition admits in public.

## The Echo Speaker in the World

Grief is universal in Tesshari. The Ascension War has produced bodies at a rate that has not been seen in generations, and behind every body is a family, a village, a lord who wants to know if the dead can still be reached. Echo Speakers are consequently always in demand and almost never in a secure position. Everyone wants access to the dead and no one wants to pay what that access costs. The Speaker is simultaneously essential and exploitable, a service provider in a market built on desperation.

The Sutensai maintains that all official communion with the Iron Afterlife should be mediated through licensed Sutensai-affiliated Speakers — that unlicensed practice is spiritually dangerous, legally questionable, and occasionally a theological crime. This official position creates a shadow economy of independent practitioners, black market access to the Afterlife, and families who simply cannot afford Sutensai rates and will take their chances with a hedge Speaker who charges in food and shelter. The Sutensai knows this market exists. They monitor it through a network of licensed Speakers whose secondary function is intelligence-gathering on behalf of the priesthood.

Independent Echo Speakers navigate a world that simultaneously needs them desperately and considers them somewhere between mildly heretical and actively dangerous. The dead are not supposed to be political. Inevitably, they are. A Speaker who has been asked to relay a final message from a dead soldier and instead relays a detailed account of how that soldier was killed by his own commanding officer has become, instantly, a problem for powerful people. The political implications of the Echo Speaker's abilities make every deep practitioner a potential liability for whoever is closest to them.

Socially, Echo Speakers develop an affect that people find uncanny. They are often distracted — caught between conversations, hearing something outside the audible. They describe the dead with more warmth than the living, which unnerves people they know well. They are exceptionally good at reading grief and almost unable to maintain comfortable social fictions about death. At a funeral they do not say the comforting things. They say what the dead actually want said, which is rarely the same.

## D&D 5E Class Features

**Hit Die:** d8
**Primary Ability:** Wisdom (Veil sensitivity), Charisma (voice as instrument)
**Saving Throw Proficiencies:** Wisdom, Charisma
**Armor Proficiencies:** Wire-mesh armor
**Weapon Proficiencies:** Pulse arms (sidearm weight), resonant blades (short configuration), signal staffs
**Tool Proficiencies:** Memorial circuit kit, communication wire-craft tools
**Skills:** Choose 3 from: History, Insight, Medicine, Perception, Persuasion, Religion, Investigation
**Starting Equipment:** Wire-mesh wrappings, signal staff, memorial circuit kit (contains 5 individual circuit memorials and attunement powder), journal (half-filled with observations in at least two handwritings — one of which is not the Speaker's), 15 gp equivalent in trade goods

### Class Features by Level

**Level 1: Hear the Dead**
You can hear voices from the Iron Afterlife when in the presence of death or resonant memory. As an action, you can attempt to open a channel to a specific dead individual whose name or personal enhancement-signature you know. Make a Wisdom check (DC 10 + years since death, maximum DC 25). On a success, you make contact. The dead individual can answer questions, though their knowledge extends only to what they knew in life. Each question beyond the first requires an additional DC 12 Wisdom check. On a critical failure (natural 1), you attract the attention of an unknown Afterlife presence. Contacts last up to 1 minute. You can use this ability a number of times per long rest equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum 1).

**Level 1: Veil Sense**
You are always aware of death nearby. You automatically sense when a creature dies within 60 feet. You can tell when a location has been the site of significant death even if there is no physical evidence. You have advantage on Perception and Investigation checks in any location where someone has died within the past 24 hours.

**Level 1: Echo Reserve**
You have a pool of Echo Charges equal to your proficiency bonus + Wisdom modifier. These represent your active connection to the Veil and are spent on various abilities. You regain all Echo Charges on a long rest, and regain half (rounded down) on a short rest.

**Level 2: Relay**
You can serve as a conduit for the dead to communicate with the living in real time. As a bonus action, spend 1 Echo Charge to allow a spirit you are in contact with to speak through you — your voice takes on a slight distortion, and the dead individual can communicate directly for up to 1 minute. You retain awareness of what is being said. You cannot prevent the dead from saying anything; you can end the relay at any time.

**Level 2: Echo Shield**
The dead notice when you are threatened. When you are attacked, you can use your reaction and spend 1 Echo Charge to have a spirit disrupt the attacker's resonant systems, imposing disadvantage on that attack roll.

**Level 3: Echo Speaker Subclass**
You choose your path: The Sutensai Aligned, The Deep Listener, or The Herald. Your subclass grants features at levels 3, 6, 10, and 14.

**Level 3: Afterlife Attunement**
Your Hear the Dead ability improves. You can now contact the dead as a bonus action (instead of a full action). Additionally, when you successfully contact a dead individual, you can ask one question for free without spending Echo Charges.

**Level 4: Ability Score Improvement**

**Level 5: Veil Walking**
You can project your senses briefly into the Iron Afterlife. As an action, spend 2 Echo Charges to extend your perception into the Afterlife for 1 round. While projected, you can perceive the Iron Afterlife equivalent of your current location — seeing Echomind signatures of those who died here, residual spiritual impressions, and any entities currently active in the Afterlife near you. Your physical body stands still during this time. If your body takes damage, your projection ends immediately.

**Level 5: Spirit Speech**
Your voice carries spiritual weight. When you speak a formal statement of remembrance or relay a message from the dead, the words cannot be magically suppressed or unheard. Creatures cannot become immune to information you relay from the Iron Afterlife through magical or resonant means. Additionally, you can use Charisma (Persuasion) checks at advantage when speaking to grieving individuals.

**Level 6: Subclass Feature**

**Level 6: Echo Armor**
The dead protect what they value. You gain a +2 bonus to AC while you have at least 1 Echo Charge remaining.

**Level 7: Commune Extended**
You can now maintain contact with a dead individual for up to 1 hour. During extended communion, the dead individual is present in a limited sense — they can observe through your senses and comment on what they see. They may recognize people, places, and objects they knew in life, and provide information relevant to those connections.

**Level 8: Ability Score Improvement**

**Level 8: Veil Disruption**
As an action, spend 2 Echo Charges to release disrupting spiritual energy at one creature within 30 feet. The target must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw (DC = 8 + proficiency bonus + Wisdom modifier) or take 3d10 psychic damage and be frightened until the end of your next turn. On a success, half damage only, no frightened. Creatures with no Echomind (constructs, etc.) are immune.

**Level 9: The Dead Remember**
The Iron Afterlife holds information about recent events. Once per long rest, as a 10-minute ritual, you can ask the collective memory of the Iron Afterlife a question about a specific event that occurred within 1 mile of your current location within the past year. The Afterlife answers in fragments — images, emotional impressions, partial voices. The answer is never a neat narrative; it is raw witness. Interpret carefully.

**Level 10: Subclass Feature**

**Level 10: Echo Storm**
As an action, spend 3 Echo Charges to call down a wave of spiritual static from the Iron Afterlife. In a 20-foot radius centered on a point within 60 feet, all creatures must make Wisdom saving throws. On a failure: 4d8 psychic damage and deafened for 1 minute. On a success: half damage, not deafened. Additionally, all cybernetic communication systems (Wire Craft effects) in the area are disrupted for 1 round.

**Level 11: Anchor of the Living**
You have become a stable point between the worlds. Creatures with Echominds who die within 30 feet of you can choose to delay their passage to the Iron Afterlife for 1 round, allowing last words, a final action, or one transmitted piece of information. You cannot prevent the passage — only delay it. This does not require your action; it simply happens.

**Level 12: Ability Score Improvement**

**Level 12: Sustained Veil**
You no longer need to spend Echo Charges to use Veil Walking. Additionally, when you project into the Afterlife, you can now move (at half speed) while projected without returning to your body.

**Level 13: Speak for the Fallen**
Once per long rest, you can speak a formal accusation on behalf of a dead individual. Name a living person as responsible for the dead individual's wrongful death (requires genuine resonant testimony from the dead person themselves). Until the accusation is resolved (either the accused confesses, is condemned, or the accusation is formally dismissed by a Sutensai tribunal), the accused creature has disadvantage on Charisma checks made in social situations and cannot benefit from effects that suppress information about their past. The dead speak through you and cannot be unsaid.

**Level 14: Subclass Feature**

**Level 15: Beyond the Veil**
You have looked into the Iron Afterlife so many times it no longer looks back with hostility. You are immune to the frightened condition when the source is from the Iron Afterlife or death-based spiritual effects. Additionally, you can communicate freely with any dead individual currently in the Iron Afterlife as a bonus action, as long as they are willing — no check required.

**Level 16: Ability Score Improvement**

**Level 17: The Speaking Dead**
Three times per long rest, you can pull a fragment of a dead individual's Echomind temporarily into the physical world as a semi-corporeal presence. The fragment appears as a glowing, slightly distorted version of the individual as they appeared at death. It has the statistics of a shadow (use ghost statistics) but retains the dead individual's knowledge and personality. It lasts for 1 hour, can act independently (with your guidance), and returns to the Iron Afterlife when the duration ends or it is reduced to 0 hit points. Destroying a pulled fragment is destructive to the Echomind — the fragment requires 1d6 months to reconstitute in the Afterlife.

**Level 18: One Foot Gone**
You have spent so much time with the dead that the boundary between you and them has changed. You have resistance to necrotic damage and psychic damage. You do not age (time affects you strangely at the border of both worlds). You can enter the Iron Afterlife as a physical presence (not just a projection) for up to 1 hour per long rest. While there, you can be harmed by Afterlife entities. Returning requires a DC 15 Wisdom check; on a failure, you remain one additional hour whether you want to or not.

**Level 19: Ability Score Improvement**

**Level 20: Voice of Both Worlds**
You are now fully a bridge between the living and the dead. Your Echo Charges maximum doubles. Once per long rest, you can open the Veil fully in a 30-foot radius for 1 minute: all dead who have unfinished business in the area manifest simultaneously as spiritual presences (DM determines specifics), all creatures in the area can hear and communicate with the dead directly, and any creature reduced to 0 hit points in the area has their Echomind held stable by your presence — they will survive if stabilized (they do not make death saving throws; they are simply held at 0 until stabilized or healed). When this effect ends, you are immediately reduced to 1 hit point regardless of your current total. The bridge, used fully, costs everything.

## Subclasses

### The Sutensai Aligned — "The Careful Hand"

The Sutensai-trained Echo Speaker has learned to hear the dead through layers of theological filter — not because the Sutensai is necessarily correct about the Iron Afterlife, but because the institution has spent centuries developing practice that protects the practitioner. Sutensai-trained Speakers are safer than independent practitioners. They are also more limited. The filter that protects also constrains. Sutensai Echo Speakers are taught what the dead are allowed to say. They are often not prepared for the dead who refuse to follow the curriculum.

**Level 3: Sanctioned Communion**
Your training under the Sutensai has made your connections more stable. You have advantage on Wisdom checks to contact the dead. When contact fails, you never attract unwanted attention on a critical failure — instead, the connection simply doesn't form. Additionally, once per long rest, you can ask a question of the Sutensai's collective record of Afterlife contacts — a curated archive of sanctioned communications spanning centuries. The answer may be incomplete. The curation was not always honest.

**Level 3: Iron Sutra Ward**
As an action, spend 1 Echo Charge to consecrate a 10-foot radius area for 1 hour. Within this area: spirits cannot enter without your permission, communication from the Afterlife is filtered through your awareness (you know everything that is said), and creatures have advantage on death saving throws.

**Level 6: Institutional Access**
Your Sutensai affiliation opens doors. You have advantage on Persuasion and Intimidation checks made against Sutensai clergy, Caste enforcers, and civic officials. Once per long rest, you can invoke your Sutensai authority to demand information — one creature of CR 8 or lower must make a Wisdom saving throw (DC = your Echo Save DC) or truthfully answer one question about the Iron Afterlife, the dead, or spiritual matters in their knowledge.

**Level 10: Guided Path**
The Sutensai's centuries of practice have built roads through the Iron Afterlife. When you use Veil Walking, you can navigate specific paths that the Sutensai has charted — moving to any location within 1 mile that has a designated spiritual anchor (Sutensai temples, memorial sites, official death registers). Travel takes 1 round per quarter-mile. You can bring one willing creature with you.

**Level 14: The Orthodoxy's Voice**
Your connection to the Sutensai has become a source of genuine power. Once per long rest, you can speak a formal Sutensai decree in a spiritually significant situation. All creatures within 60 feet who can hear you must make Wisdom saving throws (DC = your Echo Save DC). Creatures who fail: believe, without reservation, that you speak with sanctioned spiritual authority for 24 hours (this does not compel obedience, only belief in your authority). Simultaneously, one message from the dead of your choice is broadcast in that area — every creature hears it, regardless of their sensitivity to the Afterlife. The Sutensai did not intend this ability to exist. It emerged from the practice anyway.

### The Deep Listener — "Too Far Gone to Come Back Clean"

The Deep Listener went further than their training said to go and found something on the other side. It may have been worth it. They are no longer certain the question has a clean answer. They spend more time in the Iron Afterlife than most living people spend in dreams. They have developed relationships with specific dead individuals that are indistinguishable from friendship, and those relationships have reshaped their priorities in ways that those who knew them before find unsettling. They are more powerful than any Sutensai Echo Speaker. They are also, measurably, less present.

**Level 3: Partial Presence**
You exist partially in the Iron Afterlife at all times. You have advantage on saving throws against effects that would forcibly move you (you are anchored by both worlds simultaneously). You can perceive the Iron Afterlife as a faint overlay on physical reality without entering it — this is passive and always active. You take 1d4 psychic damage at dawn each day as the split costs you something. This damage cannot be resisted or reduced.

**Level 3: Deep Call**
Your contact attempts reach deeper into the Afterlife than normal. You can contact the dead without knowing their name or enhancement-signature — you can reach "the person who died here most recently," "the person whose death is most relevant to this object," or similar spiritual descriptions. Additionally, your contacts are clearer and more detailed than standard communion — the dead speak in full sentences and can hold complex conversations.

**Level 6: Afterlife Sight**
You permanently see the Iron Afterlife overlay over physical reality. You can see invisible creatures that have Echomind signatures. You can see residual spiritual imprints on objects touched by the dead. You can distinguish between violent and peaceful deaths by looking at the location where they occurred. You cannot turn this off. It is occasionally distressing.

**Level 10: Dweller at the Gate**
Your partial presence in the Afterlife gives you leverage there. Afterlife entities regard you as a resident rather than an intruder. They will not attack you without provocation. You can negotiate with Afterlife entities as if in a diplomatic context (they have desires, grievances, and information). Once per long rest, you can call one Afterlife entity to the physical world for up to 10 minutes — it has the statistics of a wraith but is not hostile unless threatened.

**Level 14: Gone Halfway**
You have gone so deep into the Iron Afterlife that you have begun to change at a cellular level. You are permanently partially incorporeal: attacks against you have a 25% chance of passing through you as if you were not there (roll a d4 at the start of each round you are attacked — on a 1, all attacks this round automatically miss). You have immunity to necrotic damage. You no longer need to eat or drink — your organic tissue is sustained by Afterlife resonance. You no longer quite feel temperature. You cannot be charmed or frightened by the living. The dead, however, can still frighten you, and occasionally do.

### The Herald — "The Message Is Not the Messenger"

The Herald Echo Speaker is the professional. They have built a practice around the specific, bounded act of facilitating communication between the living and the dead — delivering final messages, answering unanswered questions, providing closure that was otherwise impossible. They take their role seriously to a degree that has made them the most ethically rigorous and the most practically useful subclass. They do not editorialize. They do not filter. They deliver what was given to them to deliver, and then they step back. This discipline is a moral achievement that cost them years to build and which is tested constantly by what the dead ask them to carry.

**Level 3: Perfect Relay**
When you relay a message or facilitate a communication between the living and dead, you cannot accidentally distort the message. Your relay is always exact. Additionally, you can serve as a relay for communications between two living people through the Afterlife — routing messages across any distance as long as both parties consent. This requires 1 minute of concentration.

**Level 3: Herald's Passage**
Under the traditional protection of the herald tradition, creatures who know of your role cannot attack you without losing significant social standing and spiritual consequence. You have advantage on saving throws against attacks made against you by creatures who know you are acting as a Herald in an official capacity. When you declare yourself a herald (which requires a bonus action and must be done openly), creatures of CR 6 or lower who can hear you and understand the cultural context must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw (DC = your Echo Save DC) or be unable to attack you for 1 minute unless you attack them first.

**Level 6: The Record**
You maintain a perfect record of every message you have relayed. This record is spiritually significant — it is partially stored in the Iron Afterlife itself. You can access any message you have ever relayed, word for word, at will. You can also access, once per long rest, a record of any official Herald communication made in the past century in any location you have been to personally. The Sutensai does not officially acknowledge this ability exists. Their archivists desperately want to interview you about it.

**Level 10: Final Message**
Once per long rest, when a creature within 30 feet of you dies, you can immediately facilitate a final message from that creature to any person of their choice within 1 mile. The message reaches its target regardless of physical barriers, resonant interference, or distance. The target hears it in their mind as clearly as a spoken word, in the voice of the dead person. The dead person can say as much or as little as they choose. You hear it too.

**Level 14: The Living Dead Network**
You have built a communication network that spans the boundary between worlds. You can maintain simultaneous contact with up to your Wisdom modifier in dead individuals at once, with no concentration required. Living creatures you designate (up to your Charisma modifier) can send and receive messages through your network as if they were Echo Speakers themselves, as long as you authorize their access. Once per long rest, you can broadcast a message to every dead individual within 1 mile of your location simultaneously — and receive a single word from each of them in return. The weight of all those words at once requires a DC 14 Wisdom saving throw to process without gaining one level of exhaustion.

## Roleplaying the Echo Speaker

**What the Dead Tell You:** The dead are not filtered. They say what they know, what they feel, what they want. Some of what they say implicates the living in things the living would prefer to stay hidden. Your character has heard things they cannot unknow. How does that accumulation of secret history affect who they trust, who they fear, and what they want?

**The Living vs. the Dead:** The further an Echo Speaker progresses, the more time they spend with the dead and the less comfortable they are with the living. The dead are honest in ways the living are not. They don't have anything left to protect. Your character may be navigating a slow and unsettling shift in where they feel more at home. Is that frightening? Is it a relief?

**Institutional Pressure:** The Sutensai wants to control what the dead say. Your character either works within that control, subverts it, or has chosen independence and lives with the consequences. What did the Sutensai teach you about the Afterlife that you later found out was wrong? What do you know that the Sutensai would prefer you didn't?

**The Grief Work:** Echo Speakers are constantly in contact with the grief of others. You are the person called when someone cannot let go, when someone needs to hear a final word, when someone needs to know if the dead forgave them. This is not abstract labor. It changes you. What has it cost you personally?

## Connections to the Ascension War

Korrath the Unbroken has little patience for Echo Speaker theology but is intensely interested in military intelligence applications — the dead know where armies moved, where supplies are stored, what orders were given. He has attempted to employ or conscript Echo Speakers several times with mixed results. The dead, it turns out, are not reliably patriotic.

Vaen of the Empty Hand has a profound and complicated relationship with Echo Speakers. His theology of release raises questions about what happens in the Iron Afterlife that Speakers are uniquely positioned to answer. He has sought them as witnesses, asking them whether the dead in the Afterlife are at peace, suffering, or something else entirely. Their answers have influenced his theology in ways he is still processing.

The Hollow Author is deeply interested in Echo Speakers for reasons that become obvious upon reflection: if the Iron Afterlife can be contacted, the dead can potentially expose the Author's operations, name their puppets, describe their past crimes. The Author monitors Echo Speakers carefully and has arranged for several deep-access practitioners to meet unfortunate accidents. A Herald or Deep Listener who has been specifically targeted by the Author — and has spoken to the dead they silenced — is one of the most dangerous witnesses in Tesshari.

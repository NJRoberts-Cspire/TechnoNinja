# Sutensai
## "I am the voice of the law, the hand of the faith, and the keeper of the record. I tell you this not as a warning. I tell you this as a courtesy before I tell you what you owe."

## Overview

The Sutensai do not call themselves priests. The word exists, and laypeople use it, but within the institution itself the preferred term is Reader — because the central act of Sutensai practice is the Reading: the formal examination of a person's Echomind to determine the alignment, integrity, and spiritual status of their resonance. This is not metaphorical. The Sutensai have developed, over centuries, the ability to examine the precise resonant signature of a person's Echomind, interpret its patterns, and issue a formal judgment about that person's spiritual standing. These judgments have material consequences — Caste of Steel classifications, legal standing, inheritance rights, the right to certain kinds of enhancement. The Sutensai are the gate through which every significant life transition passes, and they know it.

What makes the Sutensai extraordinary beyond their administrative function is that the act of Reading, done deeply enough and with sufficient attunement, grants genuine access to the Iron Afterlife. The most advanced Sutensai can commune with the dead as part of formal procedure, can sense the resonant imprint of history in locations and objects, and in rare cases can channel the authority of the Six Prior Ascended into their voice and actions. The institution has spent considerable effort ensuring that these abilities remain bound by institutional procedure — that a Sutensai's spiritual power is expressed through official channels, recorded in official records, and never used for purposes that are not formally sanctioned. The effort is only partially successful.

The Sutensai is also an intelligence organization, a caste enforcement agency, a court system, and a military organization, depending on what the situation requires. Their priests carry weapons. Their Reading rituals produce detailed records of private spiritual states. Their administrative role gives them access to more personal information about every family in Tesshari than any intelligence service has managed to collect. They know about old crimes, hidden alliances, secret enhancements, and spiritual deviations. They have held this information for centuries and have used it — for stability, for leverage, for the preservation of an institution that, whatever its flaws, has been the closest thing Tesshari has to a unifying authority across the Broken Reaches. Whether that justification holds any longer is a question that increasingly divides the Sutensai from within.

The class of the Sutensai is the class of people who have been trained in this tradition and are actively practicing it. Not all of them are true believers. Not all of them are cynics. Most of them are somewhere in the complicated middle — people who entered an institution because it was meaningful, discovered that the institution is deeply compromised, and are now navigating the gap between what they were taught the Sutensai is and what they have discovered it does. The ones who have found no gap are dangerous in the way certainty is dangerous. The ones who have found too large a gap are dangerous in the way that disillusionment without purpose is dangerous. The ones who are still navigating are dangerous because they are trying to figure out if something real exists under the institution, and that search tends to lead places no one intended.

## The Sutensai in the World

The Sutensai priest is one of the most powerful social figures in Tesshari at every level from village to capital. The white-and-iron vestments, the calibration staff, the formal seal of the Sutensai order — these signal authority that transcends territorial boundaries. In principle, a Sutensai priest can enter any lord's hall, demand a Reading, and compel attendance. In practice, during the Ascension War, this principle is being tested. Korrath does not recognize authority he cannot break. Vaen's territory has complex relationships with Sutensai who have been influenced by his theology. The Hollow Author's territories have, in documented cases, produced Readings that were falsified by persons who should not have been able to falsify them — which has prompted internal Sutensai investigations of the most alarming nature.

Among ordinary people, the Sutensai priest is respected, somewhat feared, and never entirely trusted. The Reading is not a comfortable experience. People know that the priest will see things. They are not always wrong to fear what will be seen. The Sutensai's tradition of strict confidentiality about Reading results is real — and also has exceptions that every ordinary person has heard rumors about. The priest who tells no one and the priest who tells the lord's chamberlain are indistinguishable from the outside, and people's experience of which kind they encounter has shaped their regional views of the institution in ways that the central Sutensai hierarchy is dimly, reluctantly aware of.

For a player character, being Sutensai means being the institution made mobile and individual — carrying its power, its weight, its secrets, and its compromises into situations where the official procedures don't apply cleanly. A Sutensai in a war zone is discovering that the law they enforce is not recognized by half the parties in the conflict. A Sutensai among the Shellbroken is finding that the former puppets of the Hollow Author have spiritual signatures that no existing Sutensai doctrine fully accounts for. A Sutensai who has read the wrong Echomind at the wrong time is now carrying knowledge that the institution has decided should not exist. This is the class of people who are, in the deepest sense, responsible for the state of Tesshari's soul — and who are increasingly confronting the question of whether that responsibility has been discharged with integrity.

## D&D 5E Class Features

**Hit Die:** d8
**Primary Ability:** Wisdom (Echomind attunement), Charisma (spiritual authority)
**Saving Throw Proficiencies:** Wisdom, Charisma
**Armor Proficiencies:** Wire-mesh armor, salvage plating, void anchors (as ceremonial protection)
**Weapon Proficiencies:** Resonant blades (standard and short), signal staffs, calibration lances
**Tool Proficiencies:** Calibration tools, Reading instruments (Sutensai formal kit), administrative seals
**Skills:** Choose 3 from: History, Insight, Medicine, Persuasion, Religion, Investigation, Intimidation
**Starting Equipment:** Sutensai vestments (count as wire-mesh armor), calibration lance (signal staff variant), full Reading kit, Sutensai formal seal, copy of the Iron Sutra (annotated, institution edition), 20 gp equivalent in official currency

### Class Features by Level

**Level 1: The Reading**
You can perform the formal Sutensai Reading on a willing or restrained creature. The Reading takes 10 minutes and requires your full attention. On completion, you learn: the creature's Caste level, whether their enhancements are operating legally, their general spiritual alignment (orthodox/resonant/circle/unclear), whether they have committed any act the Iron Sutra classifies as a spiritual crime in the past year, and the general emotional state of their Echomind. You can perform the Reading as an abbreviated 1-minute version once per short rest, learning only the caste level and whether any spiritual flags exist. You can perform the full Reading a number of times per long rest equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum 1).

**Level 1: Spiritual Authority**
You carry the official weight of the Sutensai. When you identify yourself formally, creatures trained in Tesshari cultural context must make a Charisma saving throw (DC = 8 + proficiency bonus + Charisma modifier) or be compelled to at least acknowledge your authority — they may still refuse orders, but they cannot deny your right to give them. Against creatures of CR 4 or lower, this compels full deference unless you actively threaten them.

**Level 1: Faith Charges**
You have a pool of Faith Charges equal to your Wisdom modifier + your proficiency bonus. You regain all Faith Charges on a long rest, and regain half (rounded up) on a short rest after a formal act of Sutensai practice (performing a Reading, conducting a rite, etc.).

**Level 2: Afterlife Channel**
Your Sutensai training has given you structured access to the Iron Afterlife. As an action, spend 1 Faith Charge to send a formalized message to the Iron Afterlife regarding a specific dead individual. You learn whether they have reached the Afterlife or are spiritually unresolved, and receive a one-word answer to one yes/no question. The message must be directed to a specific individual by name. This is not conversation — it is official inquiry.

**Level 2: Iron Ward**
As an action, spend 1 Faith Charge to consecrate a space in a 15-foot radius around you until the end of your next long rest. In this space: undead entities take 2d8 radiant damage if they enter or start their turn there, creatures have advantage on death saving throws, and no spirits can enter or leave without your awareness.

**Level 3: Sutensai Subclass**
You choose your path: The Warden, The Veil Master, or The Voice of the Sect. Your subclass grants features at levels 3, 6, 10, and 14.

**Level 3: Echomind Sight**
Your Reading ability has expanded beyond formal ritual. At will, you can see the general resonant state of any creature within 30 feet — an impressionistic sense of their spiritual health, distress, or spiritual contamination. This does not give you the specific information that a full Reading provides; it gives you a feeling, like reading a person's face. You are rarely wrong about what the feeling means.

**Level 4: Ability Score Improvement**

**Level 5: Radiant Sentence**
As an action, spend 2 Faith Charges to issue a formal spiritual condemnation of one creature within 60 feet that you can see. The creature must make a Charisma saving throw (DC = 8 + proficiency bonus + Wisdom modifier). On a failure: the creature takes 4d8 radiant damage and has disadvantage on attack rolls until the end of your next turn. On a success: half damage, no disadvantage. Against creatures you have performed a full Reading on, they have disadvantage on the saving throw. This is not a metaphorical condemnation. You are speaking with the authority of the Iron Afterlife, channeled through Sutensai training. Some practitioners report seeing the faces of Prior Ascended overlaid on their own reflection when they use this feature.

**Level 5: Rite of Passage**
Once per long rest, you can perform the Sutensai's formal death rite over a creature that has died within 1 minute. The rite takes 1 minute and ensures that the dead individual's Echomind passes cleanly to the Iron Afterlife without spiritual contamination, hostile attachment, or exploitation. Additionally, a creature who has received the formal rite cannot be reanimated or puppeted without breaking a spiritual ward equivalent to a 5th-level effect.

**Level 6: Subclass Feature**

**Level 6: Sutra Shield**
As a reaction when you or a creature within 30 feet is targeted by a spiritual or resonant attack, spend 2 Faith Charges to impose disadvantage on that attack roll. If the attack still hits, reduce the damage by your Wisdom modifier. You can use this feature a number of times per long rest equal to your Wisdom modifier.

**Level 7: Inquisitor's Eye**
You can always tell when someone is lying to you about spiritual matters — anything related to the Iron Sutra, their Echomind, their enhancements, or the Iron Afterlife. This is passive and automatic. You do not always know the truth; you know when the stated version is false. Additionally, when you perform a full Reading, you detect all lies told by the subject in the previous 24 hours, including the topics (but not the content) of those lies.

**Level 8: Ability Score Improvement**

**Level 8: The Sutensai Record**
You have formal access to the Sutensai's archives of Readings, death records, and caste histories. Once per long rest, as a 30-minute process that requires a signal connection to any Sutensai-administered territory, you can query the archive for any recorded information about a person, location, or event in Sutensai records. The archive goes back centuries. It is not complete. It has been edited. You may not always receive the full picture, but you will always receive something — and sometimes what you receive will not match what you were taught.

**Level 9: Channel Prior**
Once per long rest, you can invoke one of the Six Prior Ascended as a channel for your spiritual power. Choose one of the following effects (DM and player should agree on which Prior corresponds to which effect, based on campaign context):
- A burst of radiant energy in a 30-foot radius (5d10 radiant damage, Dexterity saving throw for half)
- A declaration that commands all creatures of CR 6 or lower within 60 feet to flee (Wisdom saving throw or frightened, 1 minute)
- A healing pulse that restores 4d10 hit points to every living creature within 30 feet
- A spiritual truth-compulsion: one creature within 30 feet cannot lie for 1 hour (Wisdom saving throw to resist; DC = your Faith Save DC)

**Level 10: Subclass Feature**

**Level 10: Veil Authority**
Your connection to the Iron Afterlife is now institutionally sanctioned to the point of being spiritually embedded. Entities from the Iron Afterlife treat you as a recognized authority unless actively hostile to the Sutensai. You can negotiate with Afterlife entities as equals. Once per long rest, you can issue a formal command to one Afterlife entity within 30 feet — it must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw (DC = your Faith Save DC) or obey one direct command within its capabilities.

**Level 11: Reading Mastery**
Your full Reading now reveals everything about a subject's spiritual history, not just the past year. You learn the complete Caste history, every formal spiritual violation the Sutensai would recognize, significant resonant events in their life, and the general character of their Echomind as it has developed. This is, frankly, more than most people want known about them. Performing this level of Reading on someone who is not willing requires binding or restraint.

**Level 12: Ability Score Improvement**

**Level 12: Institutional Weight**
Your presence carries the full weight of the Sutensai institution. You can add your Wisdom modifier to Persuasion, Intimidation, and Insight checks made in social contexts. Once per long rest, you can invoke full Sutensai authority over a situation: all creatures within 60 feet who are not actively hostile to you must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw (DC = your Faith Save DC) or be incapable of taking hostile actions for 1 minute (they may move, speak, and take non-hostile actions). Creatures who are actively at war with the Sutensai or its allies are immune.

**Level 13: Sustained Rite**
You can maintain ongoing spiritual rituals that function continuously. You currently know one sustained rite from the following, and learn a second at level 17:
- Rite of Watching: A 100-foot radius area around any location you designate (within 1 mile) is spiritually monitored. You know when any creature dies, any spiritual violation occurs, or any Afterlife entity crosses the Veil within this area.
- Rite of Peace: A 30-foot radius around you is spiritually consecrated (as Iron Ward, permanent while maintained). Maintaining this costs no Faith Charges but requires 10 minutes of Sutensai prayer per long rest.
- Rite of Binding: One creature you have performed a full Reading on is spiritually tagged — you always know their direction and approximate distance, and you know if they die.

**Level 14: Subclass Feature**

**Level 15: The Sutensai's Truth**
Once per long rest, you can compel absolute spiritual truth from one creature you can see within 60 feet. The creature must make a Wisdom saving throw (DC = your Faith Save DC) or be unable to lie, omit significant information, or mislead by implication for 1 hour. The creature is aware they are compelled. This is one of the most feared Sutensai abilities in Tesshari — it is not the capacity to harm but the capacity to know, and in Tesshari, knowing is power.

**Level 16: Ability Score Improvement**

**Level 17: Living Archive**
You have internalized so much Sutensai record that you have become a walking historical resource. You can answer questions about Tesshari's spiritual history, caste records, and Sutensai doctrine going back five centuries as if consulting a perfect record. Once per long rest, you can share a specific memory from the archive with another creature by touch — they experience it as if they were there.

**Level 18: Channel Prior (Advanced)**
Your Channel Prior now requires no rest to reuse — you can use it once per short rest. Additionally, you can invoke two Prior Ascended simultaneously once per long rest, combining their effects.

**Level 19: Ability Score Improvement**

**Level 20: The Last Reading**
Once per long rest, you can perform what the Sutensai calls the Final Reading — a full accounting of a creature's entire spiritual existence. This requires either a willing subject or a restrained one. The reading takes 1 minute. On completion: you learn everything about the subject's Echomind going back to its formation — every choice, every violation, every truth and lie they have told in a spiritual context, and the current state of their soul. This is absolute. There is no defense against it except not having a soul. The subject knows they have been read completely. Most people find this either liberating or shattering, depending on what you find. You may choose, after the reading, to share a formal Sutensai judgment: if you judge the creature absolved, they are freed from any spiritual contamination, curse, or binding they carry. If you judge them condemned, every Sutensai-affiliated creature within 1 mile becomes aware of their specific crimes. Both judgments have the force of formal Sutensai law.

## Subclasses

### The Warden — "The Law Has a Face"

The Warden is the Sutensai's enforcement arm — the priest who does not merely read the Echomind and record the result, but who acts on what they find. They are caste enforcers, spiritual police, and the people who show up when a village's resonant network is compromised or a lord is suspected of Flesh Circle sympathies. They have genuine legal authority across most of Tesshari and the power to make that authority felt physically if necessary. The Wardens are the reason the Sutensai is feared as well as respected, and they are the arm of the institution most directly implicated in its worst abuses of power.

**Level 3: Warden's Seal**
Your Sutensai seal functions as an authority instrument. Once per short rest, you can invoke the seal to impose a spiritual hold on one creature within 30 feet. The creature must succeed on a Charisma saving throw (DC = your Faith Save DC) or be unable to willingly leave a 30-foot radius around you for 1 hour. This represents the legal weight of the Sutensai's formal jurisdiction, not a magical compulsion — creatures who understand they are resisting legal authority must contend with the social and spiritual consequences of that resistance.

**Level 3: Judgment Blow**
When you hit a creature with a weapon attack after having performed any Reading on them, you deal an extra 2d8 radiant damage. This extra damage increases to 3d8 at level 11 and 4d8 at level 17.

**Level 6: Enforce the Caste**
Once per long rest, as an action, you can strip a creature of the protections of their Caste tier for 1 hour. A creature so stripped loses any benefits they derive from Caste rank — including social status protections, enhancement-based class features that are Caste-restricted, and the assumption of good faith. They gain no disabilities beyond the loss of these benefits; they are simply treated as Caste Zero for the duration. This is a legally recognized act of the Sutensai. Most recipients would prefer a physical attack.

**Level 10: Inviolable Authority**
While in Tesshari territories that formally recognize Sutensai authority, you are under the institutional protection of the faith. Attacks against you in these territories trigger immediate spiritual consequence — the attacker must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw (DC = your Faith Save DC) or take 3d8 psychic damage from the Iron Afterlife's response to the violation of a sanctioned priest. This does not protect you in war zones or in territory controlled by those who have formally rejected Sutensai authority (Korrath's forward positions, certain Flesh Circle enclaves, the Hollow Author's operational areas).

**Level 14: The Full Weight**
Once per long rest, you can make a formal declaration of condemnation that carries the Sutensai's full institutional weight. Name a creature you have performed a full Reading on and speak their judgment aloud: all Sutensai-aligned creatures within 5 miles become aware of the condemnation and its reason. The condemned creature has disadvantage on Charisma checks against any creature that has heard the condemnation. Additionally, the condemned creature cannot receive beneficial effects from any Sutensai rite or Feature from any Sutensai practitioner until the condemnation is formally lifted. The condemnation can be lifted only by a Sutensai tribunal of three senior priests, including one of higher level than the condemning Warden.

### The Veil Master — "The Priesthood of What's After"

The Veil Master has gone deep into the Iron Afterlife within the framework of Sutensai practice — not as a rebel or an independent practitioner, but as an institutionally sanctioned specialist in the spiritual geography beyond death. They are the Sutensai's experts on the dead, the mapping team, the troubleshooters when something from the other side of the Veil is causing problems in the physical world. They know more about the Iron Afterlife than almost any living person, and they carry that knowledge with the particular weight of someone who has made it their life's work.

**Level 3: Deep Reading**
Your Reading ability can now extend into the Iron Afterlife side of a subject's Echomind — you can see where their soul will go when they die, the spiritual connections between them and people they have lost, and any Afterlife entities that have attached to them. This information is not available through standard Sutensai Reading protocols. It is extremely personal and, in some cases, spiritually dangerous to reveal. You learn it. What you do with it is a question the institution does not have a clean answer for.

**Level 3: Veil Navigation**
You can move into and through the Iron Afterlife more freely than most. Once per short rest, you can enter the Iron Afterlife as a projection (your body remains in place) for up to 10 minutes. While projected, you can observe the Afterlife layer of any location within 1 mile, communicate with Afterlife entities without a check, and follow spiritual trails left by Void Walkers or others who have traversed the Afterlife recently.

**Level 6: Afterlife Cartography**
You have formally mapped significant portions of the Iron Afterlife accessible from your current region. When you enter the Iron Afterlife (as a projection or physically), you are never lost. You know the fastest routes, the dangerous areas, and the locations of significant Afterlife entities or architectural features (the Sutensai has a word for the spiritual equivalents of landmarks; they are called echo-anchors). Once per long rest, you can navigate another creature to a specific Afterlife location by giving them instructions that they can follow even without your presence.

**Level 10: Entity Command**
The Sutensai's institutional authority extends into the Iron Afterlife when wielded by a Veil Master. Afterlife entities you encounter must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw (DC = your Faith Save DC) or comply with reasonable requests (not commands that violate their nature). On a failed save, they treat you as a recognized Sutensai official and respond accordingly. This does not grant control — it grants institutional standing, which is a different thing.

**Level 14: The Final Map**
You have mapped the region of the Iron Afterlife most relevant to your current territory completely. Once per long rest, you can access the full Afterlife geography of a 20-mile radius around your current position: you know the location of every significant Afterlife entity, every recently-arrived Echomind, every location where the Veil is thin, and every place where someone has been using the Afterlife as a transit or intelligence resource. This is a substantial intelligence capability. The Sutensai has always maintained it. You are now the person who embodies it, and you are beginning to understand what that has meant for the institution.

### The Voice of the Sect — "One Faith, Three Tongues"

The Voice of the Sect is the subclass for a Sutensai whose practice has been deeply shaped by one of the three major sects. They are not merely practitioners of the faith — they are advocates, interpreters, and occasionally the living argument for their sect's theology. Their abilities reflect the specific theological priorities of their sect. Choose one sect at level 3; this choice shapes all of your subclass features.

**Level 3: Sectarian Power**
Your subclass features depend on your chosen sect:
- Orthodoxy: Sutra Command — once per short rest, you can speak a passage of the Iron Sutra as a formal command. One creature within 60 feet must make a Wisdom saving throw (DC = your Faith Save DC) or follow the spirit of the passage's instruction for 1 minute. The instruction must be expressible in terms the Sutra actually contains; this is not unlimited mind control.
- Resonants: Enhancement Blessing — you can perform a 1-minute ritual to boost a creature's cybernetic enhancements for 1 hour, granting them a +2 bonus to attack rolls made using enhancement-based abilities and resistance to resonant damage.
- Flesh Circle: Flesh Sanction — you can perform the Sutensai's formal blessing of flesh-reduction, granting a creature that has voluntarily removed an enhancement advantage on Constitution saving throws and resistance to necrotic damage for 24 hours. In Flesh Circle theology, this is the highest ritual honor.

**Level 6: The Sect's Defense**
- Orthodoxy: Iron Scripture Ward — when you are targeted by an effect from a creature that violates Sutensai orthodoxy (undead, spiritual corruption, heretical practice), you have resistance to the damage of that effect.
- Resonants: Circuit Sermon — as a bonus action, you can broadcast a Resonant amplification to all allied creatures within 30 feet who have cybernetic enhancements, granting them a +2 bonus to AC until the start of your next turn.
- Flesh Circle: Purity Shield — you gain advantage on saving throws against effects that target cybernetic enhancements. Any effect that specifically targets enhancements deals only half damage to you.

**Level 10: The Sect's Truth**
- Orthodoxy: Sutra of the Ancestors — once per long rest, invoke the voices of all the dead who have practiced your sect. All allies within 60 feet gain advantage on saving throws and cannot be frightened for 10 minutes.
- Resonants: Overclock Sermon — once per long rest, all allied creatures within 30 feet with cybernetic enhancements add 1d6 resonant damage to their attacks for 1 minute. You take 2d6 resonant damage as your own enhancements strain from the output.
- Flesh Circle: The Empty Sermon — once per long rest, speak the Flesh Circle's meditation of release. All creatures within 30 feet who are under the influence of a hostile cybernetic effect (Flesh Shaper alterations, puppet influence, resonant contamination) may make an immediate saving throw against those effects with advantage.

**Level 14: The Sect's Absolute**
- Orthodoxy: The Final Word — once per long rest, speak a passage of the Iron Sutra with such authority that all creatures within 60 feet who have Echominds must make Wisdom saving throws (DC = your Faith Save DC) or be paralyzed for 1 minute as the Sutra's truth overwhelms their spiritual resistance. On a success, they are stunned until the end of their next turn.
- Resonants: Maximum Integration — once per long rest, push all allied creatures' cybernetic enhancements to absolute output for 1 minute. They deal double damage with enhancement-based abilities, but each must make a Constitution saving throw at the end of the effect (DC 15) or have one enhancement malfunction until repaired.
- Flesh Circle: Return to Silence — once per long rest, perform the Flesh Circle's supreme rite over a 30-foot area. All cybernetic enhancement-based abilities (hostile and friendly) are suppressed in the area for 1 minute. Creatures whose identity is primarily enhancement-based must succeed on a Constitution saving throw (DC = your Faith Save DC) or be incapacitated for the duration. Flesh Circle extremists consider this ability sacred. Everyone else considers it terrifying.

## Roleplaying the Sutensai

**The Gap Between Doctrine and Practice:** Your character has been trained in an institution that teaches specific things about the Iron Sutra, the Echomind, and the Iron Afterlife. Some of those teachings are true. Some are incomplete. Some are deliberate distortions in the service of institutional interests. Discovering which is which is one of the central questions of the Sutensai's life as a practitioner. What have you discovered that contradicts what you were taught? How are you living with that?

**The Record You Keep:** The Sutensai keeps records. Everything you read is technically recorded. What happens when what you've read in someone's Echomind could destroy them if reported to the institution — and the institution is asking for your records? The tension between confidentiality, honesty, and institutional loyalty defines the ethical landscape of this class.

**Power and Corruption:** The Sutensai has the ability to alter people's lives with the stroke of a seal. Caste demotions, spiritual condemnations, legally binding judgments — the Sutensai class can do enormous harm in the name of order. Every practitioner must decide where their personal line is. Some have no line. Others have drawn so many lines that they are barely functional within the institution. Where is yours?

**The Weight of the Dead:** Every full Reading of a dying person, every Rite of Passage performed, every communion with the Afterlife is personal. The dead are real. The Sutensai is the institution charged with their welfare. Does it discharge that responsibility? What do the dead, when they speak to you in the course of your duties, actually say about the institution that serves them?

## Connections to the Ascension War

Korrath the Unbroken has a complex and deteriorating relationship with the Sutensai. He needs the institution's legitimacy — the Ascension requires Sutensai recognition in a theological sense — but he is unwilling to accept the constraints that institutional legitimacy would impose. His solution has been to attempt to appoint Sutensai officials who will endorse him, with mixed results. Sutensai practitioners in his territory operate under increasing pressure and are making increasingly stark choices about compliance.

Vaen of the Empty Hand has begun, in his territory, to develop what might be called a parallel Sutensai — practitioners who perform the traditional functions of the institution but within his theological framework rather than the orthodox one. This is technically heresy. It is also extremely popular among communities that have historical grievances against orthodox Sutensai governance. What it means for the institution as a whole is a question no one wants to answer yet.

The Hollow Author has compromised the Sutensai. The exact extent is unknown but the investigation is ongoing. Readings have been falsified. Records have been altered. In at least one documented case, a Sutensai official's actions over a period of years were later determined to be those of a puppet, not a person. The Sutensai is deeply interested in identifying and purging the Author's influence from its ranks. This is made more difficult by the fact that, in an institution of people who read Echominds, the Author's puppets are designed to register as clean.

# Flesh Shaper
## "I am not asking you to trust me. I am asking you to hold still."

## Overview

The Iron Sutra says that the Echomind exists in the resonance between flesh and steel. The Flesh Shaper has learned to speak directly to one side of that equation. They can feel the living tissue of organic bodies — their own and others' — as a material substance, as something that can be read, altered, healed, and reshaped. The sensation is most commonly described as a kind of pressure perception: the practitioner becomes aware of cellular structures, resonant connections between nerve and enhancement, the slow biological work of repair and growth. At its most fundamental, this awareness is simply medicine — advanced, precise, intimate medicine that can accomplish in minutes what conventional healing takes weeks to achieve.

At its most extreme, it is something else. The Flesh Shaper who has gone far enough into their practice can reach into a living body and change it. They can accelerate growth, suppress it, alter structural configuration, sever or forge connections between organic and cybernetic components. They can cause pain so precise it leaves no physical mark. They can make someone's own body betray them. The Hollow Author's puppet technology is built, at its core, on a perversion of Flesh Shaping techniques — the ability to reshape the neurological connections between a person's Echomind and their enhancements, creating a gap that can be filled with external control. Flesh Shapers are regarded with fear and suspicion in Tesshari not because the average practitioner intends this outcome, but because the potential for it is inherent in the practice, and everyone knows it.

Most Flesh Shapers are healers. This needs to be said clearly, because the reputation tends to obscure the reality: the majority of people who develop and practice Flesh Shaping spend the bulk of their careers treating injuries, repairing enhancement integration failures, and doing the quiet biological maintenance that keeps people's organic components functional alongside their steel. They know what they are suspected of. Many of them have built their entire professional ethics around demonstrating, through years of practice, that the power can be used for something other than what its most extreme application enables. The weight of that demonstration never entirely lifts.

Those who go dark — those who become what the Sutensai calls Corruptors and the Flesh Circle calls blasphemers and the war camp calls weapons — are real enough to justify the suspicion. The capacity for horror is genuine. A Corruptor in a war zone is not a presence that generates surviving witnesses who feel neutral about the experience. The Flesh Shaper class is the class of people who carry a power that could make them the most beloved healer in their community or something that keeps even the most hardened Ironclad Samurai from sleeping soundly. Living in that potential is not comfortable. Some practitioners resolve the discomfort by committing fully to one direction. Others spend their entire lives in the middle, refusing both the cleanliness of full restraint and the seduction of full capability, and paying the cost of that refusal in exhaustion that has no name.

## The Flesh Shaper in the World

There is no social role for a Flesh Shaper that doesn't come with a complication. The healer who is slightly too good raises questions. The battlefield medic whose patients recover from things they shouldn't recover from is noticed. The practitioner who works among the Shellbroken — the people who were puppets, whose organic tissue was shaped against their will — is doing the most obviously necessary work, and also spending every day with living evidence of what their power becomes in the wrong hands. The Menders who work with Shellbroken describe a particular kind of exhaustion: the practice of the thing that was done to their patients, applied as carefully as possible in the opposite direction.

The Sutensai officially licenses Flesh Shapers through a regulatory framework that is genuinely trying to prevent the worst outcomes and which has partially succeeded and partially created an underground of unlicensed practitioners whose lack of oversight is sometimes a problem and sometimes the only reason they can do their work at all. Licensed Flesh Shapers are documented, regulated, and monitored. They are also tracked, which means that when a licensed practitioner goes somewhere sensitive, the Sutensai knows, and when they do something questionable, there is a record. The unlicensed practitioners in the Ashlands and the Wire Markets have no such complications. They also have no such protections.

War has increased both demand and dread. The Ascension War's body count is enormous, and the healing work that Flesh Shapers can do is irreplaceable at scale. All three major factions employ them. All three major factions watch them carefully and maintain secondary oversight — because a Flesh Shaper who has access to a commander's body for healing purposes also has access to that body for other purposes, and no amount of professional ethics eliminates the structural reality of that access. Most Flesh Shapers in war work have found that the trust they are granted is conditional, often reviewed, and occasionally withdrawn at the worst possible moment.

## D&D 5E Class Features

**Hit Die:** d8
**Primary Ability:** Wisdom (organic perception), Constitution (personal endurance)
**Saving Throw Proficiencies:** Wisdom, Constitution
**Armor Proficiencies:** Wire-mesh armor
**Weapon Proficiencies:** Resonant blades (short configuration), pulse arms (sidearm weight), surgical implements (function as resonant blades but with precision bonuses)
**Tool Proficiencies:** Healing tools, surgical kit, biological assessment instruments
**Skills:** Choose 3 from: Medicine, Nature (biological systems), Insight, Perception, Stealth, Intimidation, Investigation
**Starting Equipment:** Wire-mesh wrappings, surgical kit, healing tools (10 uses), personal notes on biological systems (encrypted), 2 surgical implements, 8 gp equivalent

### Class Features by Level

**Level 1: Flesh Sense**
You can feel living organic tissue within 30 feet as a passive perceptual field. You automatically know the current hit point status (rough — full/wounded/critical) of any living creature within range. As an action, you can extend this sense as a diagnostic: learn a creature's exact hit points, any diseases, poisons, enhancement integration failures, or structural damage they carry. You can use this diagnostic on a willing creature or unconscious creature as a bonus action.

**Level 1: Touch Healing**
You can heal through direct contact. As an action, spend 1 Shaping Point (see below) to heal a creature you are touching for 1d8 + Wisdom modifier hit points. At level 5, this increases to 2d8 + Wisdom modifier. At level 11, 3d8 + Wisdom modifier. At level 17, 4d8 + Wisdom modifier.

**Level 1: Shaping Points**
You have a pool of Shaping Points equal to your Wisdom modifier + your proficiency bonus + your level (minimum 3). You regain all Shaping Points on a long rest and regain Wisdom modifier Shaping Points on a short rest.

**Level 2: Organic Craft**
Your understanding of organic tissue allows precise manipulation. You have advantage on Medicine checks. When you stabilize a dying creature, they regain 1 hit point instead of merely stabilizing. You can remove poison or disease from a creature with a 1-minute physical treatment (costs 2 Shaping Points).

**Level 2: Cellular Integrity**
As an action, spend 1 Shaping Point to temporarily fortify a creature's tissue. The creature gains temporary hit points equal to your Wisdom modifier + your proficiency bonus. These last until the next short rest.

**Level 3: Flesh Shaper Subclass**
You choose your path: The Mender, The Corruptor, or The Self-Shaper. Your subclass grants features at levels 3, 6, 10, and 14.

**Level 3: Pain Management**
You understand pain at a cellular level. As a bonus action, spend 1 Shaping Point to suppress a creature's pain response for 1 hour. A creature under pain suppression can ignore one level of exhaustion and can act normally even with injuries that would impose movement penalties (DM's discretion). Pain suppression does not heal — it allows function through damage. The creature can still be killed.

**Level 4: Ability Score Improvement**

**Level 5: Mass Touch**
Your Touch Healing can now affect multiple creatures in sequence. When you use Touch Healing, you can spend additional Shaping Points (1 per additional creature) to heal up to your Wisdom modifier creatures in the same action, as long as you can touch all of them (an area effect, a crowd, etc.) within a 5-foot reach. Each creature heals for the same roll.

**Level 5: Structural Assessment**
You can evaluate any enhancement integration you examine and determine precisely what is wrong with it and what organic repair is required. This takes 10 minutes of examination and provides a complete diagnostic. You can then attempt to physically repair the organic components of failed enhancement integration with a Wisdom (Medicine) check (DC set by DM based on severity). Success restores the enhancement to function.

**Level 6: Subclass Feature**

**Level 6: Accelerated Tissue**
You can push cellular repair to emergency speeds. As an action, spend 3 Shaping Points to trigger accelerated healing in a creature you touch. They immediately regain hit points equal to their level × 2, and any stabilized conditions (broken bones, severed connections) that would normally take days are reduced to hours. Using this feature more than once on the same creature within 24 hours costs double (then triple) Shaping Points — the tissue resists being pushed again so quickly.

**Level 7: Organic Armor**
As a bonus action, spend 2 Shaping Points to fortify your own (or a willing creature's) organic tissue temporarily. The target gains resistance to bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing damage for 1 minute. The tissue visibly thickens and firms. When this effect ends, the fortified tissue is somewhat fatigued — the creature loses the resistance bonus and has disadvantage on Strength and Dexterity checks for 1 hour as the tissue recovers.

**Level 8: Ability Score Improvement**

**Level 8: Deep Reading**
Your Flesh Sense extends to full biological history. When you use the diagnostic action on a creature, you can choose to read deeply, learning: all injuries they have sustained in the past month, any Flesh Shaping that has been performed on them (including by whom, if you've encountered that practitioner's work before), the general condition of their enhancement integrations, and any biological modifications that are not standard enhancements. This reading takes 1 minute instead of a bonus action.

**Level 9: Crisis Heal**
Once per short rest, as a reaction when a creature within 30 feet drops to 0 hit points, you can spend 4 Shaping Points to immediately restore them to hit points equal to your Wisdom modifier + your Flesh Shaper level. The biological emergency response is instantaneous. The creature remains at risk — they are functional, not safe.

**Level 10: Subclass Feature**

**Level 10: Tissue Mastery**
Your control over organic tissue is now comprehensive. You can perform biological modifications that permanently alter a willing subject's organic components. Working examples include: expanding sensory sensitivity (advantage on Perception), fortifying organic-metal interfaces (resistance to resonant damage), restructuring musculature for specific function (advantage on specific Strength applications), or suppressing specific biological responses permanently (immunity to one poison or disease you have studied). Each permanent modification requires 1 hour of work, costs 5 Shaping Points, and requires one week of recovery time for the subject.

**Level 11: Regenerate**
Once per long rest, spend 5 Shaping Points to regenerate a significant organic component of a creature you touch over 10 minutes: a severed organic limb re-grows, a destroyed sensory organ is reconstructed, severely damaged organic tissue is fully rebuilt. This does not regenerate cybernetic enhancements — only flesh.

**Level 12: Ability Score Improvement**

**Level 12: Organic Network**
You have developed such deep familiarity with your patients' biological profiles that you maintain a connected awareness. Choose up to your Wisdom modifier creatures. You always know their general hit point status (the same rough diagnostic from Level 1). If any of them drops below half hit points, you know immediately, regardless of distance.

**Level 13: Combat Shaping**
You can use your abilities as weapons with precision. When you succeed on a melee attack, you can spend Shaping Points (2 minimum) to add an effect: 2 points causes the target to have disadvantage on saving throws until the end of their next turn; 4 points causes the target to drop what they're holding (a neural override); 6 points causes the target to be stunned until the end of their next turn (Wisdom save DC = your Flesh Shaper save DC to resist). These effects work only on creatures with organic components (not purely mechanical constructs).

**Level 14: Subclass Feature**

**Level 15: Lifeline**
Once per long rest, by spending 1 minute in physical contact with a creature that has died within the past 10 minutes, you can attempt to restore them to life. Make a Medicine check (DC 20, or DC 25 if the body is significantly damaged). On success, the creature is restored to 1 hit point with one level of exhaustion. On failure, the body is no longer viable for this attempt. This is not a spiritual rite — you are not calling back a soul. You are restarting organic machinery. Whether the Echomind returns from the Iron Afterlife of its own accord is outside your control.

**Level 16: Ability Score Improvement**

**Level 17: Living Architecture**
Your ability to shape tissue has reached a scale beyond individual creatures. You can shape organic material in your environment as well as bodies: create barriers from dense organic matter, reshape plant life and organic debris into functional structures, or manipulate the biological components of large areas. As an action, spend 4 Shaping Points to manipulate organic material within 30 feet: create difficult terrain, a partial barrier, or a biological effect in a 15-foot area. This does not affect living creatures directly (that would be Corruptor territory). It affects the environment.

**Level 18: Emergency Mastery**
Your Crisis Heal now costs only 2 Shaping Points and has no rest limitation — you can use it any number of times, but each time it is used on the same creature within 24 hours, it costs 2 additional Shaping Points.

**Level 19: Ability Score Improvement**

**Level 20: The Body Complete**
You have achieved comprehensive mastery of organic systems. Your Shaping Points maximum increases by your Wisdom modifier. Once per long rest, for 1 minute, you can enter a state of Absolute Shaping: Touch Healing heals maximum hit points, all Shaping effects cost half their normal Shaping Points, and you can use Touch Healing as a bonus action. Additionally, once per long rest at any time, you can choose to spend 10 Shaping Points to restore a creature you touch to full hit points and remove all conditions and ongoing effects. This takes 1 action and cannot be done in combat without dedicating your full attention.

## Subclasses

### The Mender — "What I Can Fix, I Will Fix"

The Mender has decided that the power of Flesh Shaping will be used to heal, full stop. They have not necessarily decided this because they are comfortable with the power — many Menders are deeply uncomfortable with what they feel when they reach into a body, with what they could do, with the thin line between repair and alteration. They have decided it because the decision needed to be made, and this is the decision that lets them sleep. They are not naive. They know the decision is perpetually being tested by circumstances. They make it again every day.

**Level 3: Healer's Precision**
Your Touch Healing die increases by one step (d8 becomes d10, d10 becomes d12). When you heal a creature that is at 0 hit points, you may roll the healing die twice and take the higher result.

**Level 3: Harm Refusal**
Once per long rest, when you would be compelled (by enchantment, torture, or similar) to use Flesh Shaping offensively, you can make a Wisdom saving throw at advantage. On a success, you refuse and the compelling effect is broken. The refusal is absolute — a physical rejection that the compelling creature feels as a clear signal. It may escalate the situation. It will not compromise you.

**Level 6: Area Healing**
Once per short rest, as an action, spend 4 Shaping Points to release a pulse of healing energy in a 20-foot radius. All living creatures of your choice within range regain hit points equal to 2d8 + Wisdom modifier.

**Level 10: Emergency Protocol**
You have internalized emergency medicine at a deep level. When you use Crisis Heal (Level 9 feature), the creature is restored to hit points equal to your full Flesh Shaper level + Wisdom modifier (not just Wisdom modifier). Additionally, they are not stunned or incapacitated — they return to full function immediately.

**Level 14: The Mender's Answer**
Once per long rest, you can spend 10 Shaping Points to perform a comprehensive biological restoration on one creature: restore them to full hit points, remove all diseases and poisons, repair all damaged organic components to full function, and remove any Flesh Shaping that was performed on them without their consent. This takes 10 minutes and requires uninterrupted physical contact. You cannot use this feature on yourself. The cost is personal — after using it, you gain one level of exhaustion.

### The Corruptor — "Everything Has a Seam You Can Pull"

This section is written plainly because the class text demands it: the Corruptor is the worst thing a Flesh Shaper can become, and also one of the most powerful. They have followed the practice all the way down into what the Iron Sutra calls the Hollow Art — the shaping of unwilling flesh, the architecture of pain, the construction of what the Hollow Author mass-produces. They are not the Hollow Author. They are individuals, and individuality means there is still the possibility that they have reasons, have limits, have something that functions as conscience even at this depth. But those reasons, limits, and consciences are being tested by what they've done and what they've found they can do, and the Sutensai has a detailed file on them, and the file is not good reading.

**Level 3: Hostile Touch**
Your Combat Shaping (Level 13 feature) is available immediately. Additionally, when you use Touch Healing, you can instead choose to deal damage equal to your healing roll (Wisdom modifier + proficiency bonus d8). The target must be within reach or you must succeed on an unarmed strike. This damage is biological — it bypasses armor and resistance that is not specifically biological.

**Level 3: Pain Architecture**
As an action, spend 2 Shaping Points to establish a pain architecture in one creature within reach. The creature takes 2d6 necrotic damage and must make a Constitution saving throw (DC = your Flesh Shaper save DC) or have disadvantage on attack rolls and saving throws until they receive healing greater than the damage dealt, or until 1 hour passes. This is not spectacular. It is invisible and precise. Nobody watching can tell what you did. The creature knows, and that knowing is part of it.

**Level 6: Flesh Craft**
You can create biological constructs from available organic material. As a 1-hour ritual, spend 5 Shaping Points to create a flesh construct from the organic components of one or more recently deceased creatures. The construct has statistics determined by the DM based on the material used (approximately equivalent to a CR 1 construct for basic material, up to CR 4 for significant material). It obeys your commands. It lasts 24 hours before degrading, or indefinitely if you spend 1 Shaping Point per day to maintain it. The constructs look wrong. Anyone who sees them knows immediately what they are. This is one of the things the Sutensai files document.

**Level 10: Puppet Thread**
You have developed the first mechanism of the Hollow Art. As an action, spend 6 Shaping Points to establish a Puppet Thread in an unconscious or willing creature. The thread creates a biological control pathway — you can spend your bonus action to compel the threaded creature to perform one simple action (move to a location, drop an item, speak a word). The creature may make a Wisdom saving throw (DC = your Flesh Shaper save DC) each time you compel an action; on a success, the thread is severed. The thread can affect one creature at a time. This feature occupies the same ethical space as the Hollow Author's primary technique. The class text states this without judgment. The table must bring judgment to it.

**Level 14: Full Shaping**
Your ability to reshape unwilling flesh is now comprehensive. Once per long rest, over 10 minutes of contact with a restrained or unconscious creature, you can perform a complete Flesh Shaping: alter their physical appearance permanently, suppress or alter specific enhancement integrations, install or remove a Puppet Thread automatically (no save), or create a biological modification that imposes one of the following conditions permanently (Wisdom saving throw to resist — DC = your Flesh Shaper save DC, advantage on the save if the creature is conscious and aware): disadvantage on a specific ability score's checks and saves; vulnerability to one damage type; inability to use one class feature or ability. A permanent modification from this feature can only be removed by a Mender of equal or higher level, or by the Corruptor who created it. It can also be removed by a Sutensai Veil Master performing a deep spiritual cleansing, but not by conventional medicine or enhancement repair.

### The Self-Shaper — "I Am My Own Material"

The Self-Shaper has turned the practice entirely inward. They do not, as a rule, shape others — their art is the exhaustive, intimate, ongoing project of remaking themselves. They have been at it long enough that they are no longer recognizably standard — their physical form has been altered in ways that serve function, survival, and increasingly, an aesthetic that has moved away from what anyone would call human. They are neither comfortable nor uncomfortable with this. They have simply accepted that the project requires a practitioner who is also the material, and they are committed to both roles.

**Level 3: Self-Modification**
You can apply Tissue Mastery modifications (Level 10 base feature) to yourself beginning at level 3, without the normal level prerequisite. You can maintain a number of active self-modifications equal to your Wisdom modifier. Self-modifications do not require recovery time when applied to yourself — 1 hour of work is sufficient.

**Level 3: Adaptive Biology**
Your body responds to damage by adapting rather than simply recovering. When you take more than 10 damage from a single source, roll 1d6. On a 4-6, you gain resistance to that damage type until the end of your next long rest, as your tissue rebuilds with enhanced resistance. You cannot gain resistance to the same damage type twice from this feature at the same time.

**Level 6: Reshaping**
As a bonus action, spend 2 Shaping Points to make an immediate physical modification to yourself: extend a limb temporarily to double reach, harden your skin (gaining +2 AC for 1 minute), accelerate your reflexes (advantage on the next Dexterity saving throw before the end of your next turn), or suppress your pain response (ignore one failed death saving throw result per short rest). Each of these modifications lasts 1 minute unless specified otherwise.

**Level 10: The New Form**
Choose two permanent modifications you have applied to yourself. These become baseline features of your body — they function even when your Shaping Points are empty and require no activation. Each permanent modification must be a Tissue Mastery effect or a Reshaping effect. Describe what these modifications look like physically; Self-Shapers are visibly different from standard individuals by this level.

**Level 14: Beyond Template**
You have moved so far from standard biological configuration that you no longer resemble it. You are immune to Flesh Shaping performed by other practitioners — your tissue does not respond to external Shaping the way standard organic material does; it requires knowing your specific biological architecture to affect you. Additionally, once per long rest, you can perform an emergency total reshaping of yourself: change your physical size category (one step), gain a new movement mode (fly speed 30, swim speed 30, or burrow speed 20) for 24 hours, or become unrecognizable for 24 hours (you are biologically unidentifiable; even recognition-based magic describes you differently). The emergency reshapin costs 8 Shaping Points and 1 level of exhaustion.

## Roleplaying the Flesh Shaper

**The Constant Awareness:** You feel living tissue around you at all times at close range. Every injury in a room is perceptible to you. Every recent wound, every poorly integrated enhancement, every disease — you feel it as a texture, a pressure, an awareness that doesn't turn off. What does it do to you, socially, to always know the biological state of everyone nearby? What do you do with that awareness?

**The Trust Problem:** Even the most ethical Flesh Shaper must confront the fact that the power they carry is one of the most inherently violating in Tesshari. The people they heal must trust them with their bodies in a way that goes beyond typical medicine. That trust was extended and, somewhere in the history of their class, was broken catastrophically. How does your character earn it? How do they maintain it? What would make them break it?

**The Dark Path Is Always Visible:** The abilities that make you a Corruptor are visible to you whether or not you are one. Every time you heal, you are aware of what else you could do. Every time you perform a diagnostic, you can see the leverage points. This does not mean you will use them — but the awareness is there, and the longer you practice, the more articulate it becomes. How does your character live with knowing exactly what they could do?

**The Shellbroken Work:** Working with former puppets of the Hollow Author means working with people whose organic tissue bears the marks of someone else's Flesh Shaping — and in some cases, still active modifications. How does your character respond to seeing what the Hollow Author's technique produces up close? How does it affect their practice? Their ethics? Their relationship to the darker aspects of their own power?

## Connections to the Ascension War

Korrath the Unbroken has Flesh Shapers in his war camps as field medics. He does not publicly acknowledge what they are, because the social weight is complicated, but his army's casualty recovery rate is notably better than either of his opponents'. He has also, in at least one documented case, deployed a Corruptor against an enemy commander. He considers this a military tool like any other. His Flesh Shaper practitioners are well-compensated and watched.

Vaen of the Empty Hand has deeply complicated feelings about Flesh Shapers. His theology emphasizes release from flesh — yet he relies on healers, and several of his most devoted practitioners are Menders who believe that caring for flesh is a form of respecting its sacred weight before it is released. The theoretical tension has not produced practical friction, but it is there, and Vaen knows it.

The Hollow Author's technology is built on Flesh Shaping principles. The Author uses practitioners, employs Corruptors, and has developed a tradition of technique that goes beyond what any individual Flesh Shaper has publicly described. The Author's most advanced work — the full puppet, fully integrated — requires skills that are not currently documented in any living Flesh Shaper's practice record. This implies either that the Author is personally a practitioner of enormous capability, or that the Author has had access to techniques that have not been recorded. Either way, the Flesh Shaper class stands closest to understanding what the Author actually does — and the Author is aware of this.

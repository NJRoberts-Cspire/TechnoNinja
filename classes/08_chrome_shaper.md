# Chrome Shaper
## "Everyone sees what the enhancement does. Only I see what it could do. Those are different things, and the difference is everything."

## Overview

The Chrome Shaper is what emerges when engineering genius encounters a world where the body is the primary canvas and resonant metal is both the medium and the meaning. They are not merely technicians who can install modifications efficiently — any competent Forge Tender can do that. The Chrome Shaper is a designer, an artist, a systems architect who thinks in configurations rather than components. Where other enhancement professionals look at an existing modification set and see a list of features, the Chrome Shaper sees a solution space: the infinite number of different configurations those same components could be arranged into, each producing different capabilities, different resonant signatures, different interactions between the organic nervous system and the installed metal. They are also, usually, the only person in any given room who sees this, which contributes to both their professional value and their social isolation.

The practical work of the Chrome Shaper involves doing things that have no established precedent in the Wire Market's certification systems, because the Wire Market certifies known configurations and the Chrome Shaper's value lies in the unknown ones. They take existing enhancement architectures and redesign them from within — not replacing components but rerouting, reconfiguring, and retuning their interactions to produce capabilities that the original designer never intended. In some cases, they work from scratch: designing modifications that have never been made before, working through the failure modes theoretically (and then, often, empirically) until a viable design emerges. The Wire Market regards this innovation with complex ambivalence — Chrome Shapers produce designs that the Market would eventually want to license, while the Chrome Shapers themselves tend to resist the conditions the Market attaches to licensing.

The resonant dimension of enhancement design is where the Chrome Shaper's work intersects most directly with Iron Sutra theology. Resonant metal is not passive — it responds to the Echomind of the person it is bonded with, subtly adjusting its properties over time, developing preferences, accumulating the resonant history of the person's experiences. A Chrome Shaper who understands this fully is not just a mechanical engineer but something more difficult to categorize: a practitioner who works with the living relationship between a person's soul and their installed metal, shaping configurations that deepen and improve that relationship rather than simply adding functional capabilities. The Sutensai has a word for this practice — Resonance Architecture — and officially considers it the highest form of forge work. The catch is that doing it well requires a level of personal intimacy with the subject's Echomind that most people are not comfortable providing, and most Chrome Shapers are not comfortable requesting.

The risk profile of Chrome Shaper work is real and the Chrome Shaper is honest about it. Experimental configurations carry experimental failure modes. A modification that works for ninety-nine subjects may fail catastrophically in the hundredth because of an edge case in the Echomind-resonance interaction that couldn't be predicted from the other data. Chrome Shapers who work with willing experimental subjects bear a specific moral weight: they are asking someone to trust their judgment about configurations that have never been tested in a living body, and they must be worth that trust. The best Chrome Shapers in Tesshari are also the ones most haunted by their failures — because the failures were people, not prototype units, and because the Chrome Shaper usually knew exactly what went wrong and why, which is worse than not knowing.

## The Chrome Shaper in the World

The Wire Market employs Chrome Shapers in its research and development divisions, where they are simultaneously the most valued and most troublesome employees. They produce innovations that generate revenue. They also consistently exceed authorized parameters, ignore safety review timelines, and resist the documentation requirements that the Market needs to standardize and license their discoveries. The Market's solution to this problem — paying Chrome Shapers very well while building contractual constraints around their work — is only partially effective. Chrome Shapers are not primarily motivated by compensation, and constraints are, professionally, challenges.

The Caste of Steel has an interesting relationship with Chrome Shapers, because the samurai class's enhancement quality is the literal marker of their status, and Chrome Shapers are the people who can improve or modify that quality. High-status samurai who want exceptional enhancement configurations — faster reaction time, better resonant output, modifications that go beyond the Wire Market's standard catalog — come to Chrome Shapers. This creates an access dynamic that partially inverts the normal Caste hierarchy: the Chrome Shaper who can make a great samurai legendary has leverage that no formal caste designation would suggest.

Among the Hollow Poor and low-caste communities, Chrome Shapers who are willing to work outside official channels are providing access to enhancement quality that the Caste system was designed to restrict to the upper levels. A Chrome Shaper who works with salvage materials and limited resources, making do with what's available, is doing work that the Wire Market would call illegal, the Caste hierarchy would call inappropriate, and the people receiving the work tend to experience as the difference between surviving and not. The underground Chrome Shaper scene in the Broken Reaches is significant, sophisticated, and constantly under threat from the Market's enforcement arm.

## D&D 5E Class Features

**Hit Die:** d8
**Primary Ability:** Intelligence, Dexterity
**Saving Throw Proficiencies:** Intelligence, Constitution
**Armor Proficiencies:** Wire-mesh armor, Enhancement skins, Forge-work plating (custom)
**Weapon Proficiencies:** Resonant blades (short configuration), force mace, circuit spike, forge hammer, modified pulse arm
**Tool Proficiencies:** Chrome Shaper's toolkit (mastery), Forge maintenance kit, Engineering tools
**Skills:** Choose 3 from Arcana (Enhancement Theory), History (modification lineages), Insight, Investigation, Medicine (enhancement physiology), Persuasion, Technology (Wire systems)

**Starting Equipment:**
- A Chrome Shaper's toolkit (compact, quality)
- Wire-mesh armor (self-modified)
- A circuit spike and forge hammer
- A resonance analysis slate (portable diagnostic device)
- Design schematics (your own work — 1d4 experimental modification designs)
- 10 weight in trade-metal

### Class Features by Level

**Level 1: Modification Read**
You can read the full configuration of any enhancement you can observe closely. As an action (or as a bonus action for enhancements you have personally worked on), you can analyze a creature's enhancement setup. You learn:
- The complete list of modifications they have installed.
- The modifications' current functional state and calibration.
- Any misalignments, degradations, or compatibility issues in their setup.
- One optimization that could improve their performance — a specific change that would enhance their capabilities in a particular direction.

You are also proficient with all Chrome Shaper's toolkits and engineering tools, and you double your proficiency bonus on checks made with these tools.

**Level 1: Field Reconfiguration**
You can reconfigure enhancements on the fly, making temporary modifications to the function of installed systems. As an action on a willing creature (including yourself), you can perform a Field Reconfiguration. Choose one effect:

- **Speed Config:** Reroute processing pathways. Movement speed increases by 15 feet until the end of the next turn. Can be applied once per round.
- **Strength Config:** Amplify physical enhancement output. Attack rolls have advantage and deal an additional 1d6 damage for 1 minute. Constitution save DC 12 at the end of the minute or 1 level of exhaustion.
- **Armor Config:** Redistribute enhancement distribution for impact absorption. AC increases by 3 until the start of your next turn (can be used as a reaction).
- **Sense Config:** Recalibrate sensory enhancements. The creature gains darkvision (60 feet) and advantage on Perception checks for 1 hour.

You can maintain a number of Field Reconfigurations equal to your Intelligence modifier at a time. Adding a new configuration when you are at maximum automatically ends the oldest active one.

**Level 2: Experimental Frame**
You can install a temporary experimental modification on a willing creature during a short rest (1 Experimental Frame per rest, total available per day equal to your Intelligence modifier). The Experimental Frame:
- Provides a significant capability enhancement of your design (work with DM to define the specific effect; it should be comparable to a single class feature from a class of your level).
- Lasts 8 hours.
- Requires a Constitution saving throw (DC = 10 + modification complexity, DM assigns 1-5) when it expires. On a failure, the creature gains one level of exhaustion and experiences 1d6 minutes of sensory disruption.

As your level increases, the Experimental Frames become more reliable. At Level 5, the DC is reduced by 1. At Level 9, by 2. At Level 13, by 3. At Level 17, the DC is fixed at 10 regardless of complexity.

**Level 2: Technical Expertise**
Your analytical mind has developed systematic approaches to enhancement problems. You gain:
- You can identify any modification or Wire Craft system by inspection, including experimental, blacklisted, or never-catalogued configurations (Intelligence check, DC set by rarity and obscurity).
- Once per long rest, when you make an Intelligence check for enhancement theory or analysis, you can treat a roll of 9 or below as a 10.
- You have advantage on checks to identify the cause of enhancement malfunction or failure.

**Level 3: Chrome Shaper Path**
You choose your Chrome Shaper Path subclass. See Subclasses below.

**Level 4: Ability Score Improvement**
Increase one ability score by 2, or two ability scores by 1 each.

**Level 5: Rapid Reconfiguration**
Your Field Reconfiguration ability has become faster and more efficient. You can now use Field Reconfiguration as a bonus action. Additionally, you can apply two different configurations simultaneously to the same creature (where previously you could apply only one per creature at a time).

**Level 6: Chrome Shaper Path Feature**
Your subclass grants an additional feature at this level.

**Level 7: Stress Analysis**
You can read the physical stress on enhancement systems in real time. While observing a combat or physically demanding situation, you gain:
- Immediate awareness of when any allied creature's enhancement system is under stress that will cause failure within the next 2 rounds (the DM tells you this as an alert).
- As a reaction, when an ally's enhancement fails or is disrupted, you can attempt an emergency patch — making a DC 12 Intelligence check to restore the failed function for 1 round.
- You have advantage on all rolls made to prevent, diagnose, or repair enhancement failures.

**Level 8: Ability Score Improvement**
As level 4.

**Level 8: Adaptive Design**
Your configurations have become responsive to the specific resonance of the people you work with. For each creature you have performed comprehensive enhancement analysis on (via Modification Read, full assessment, or equivalent), you maintain a Resonance Profile. When you perform Field Reconfiguration or install an Experimental Frame on a profiled creature:
- The effect is more precise — you have advantage on any checks related to the reconfiguration.
- The Constitution saving throw DC for Experimental Frame decreases by 1 for profiled subjects.
- You can install configurations that would normally be incompatible with their enhancement setup because you understand the exact edge cases of their specific system.

**Level 9: Overclock Protocol**
You have developed a technique that extracts performance beyond an enhancement system's rated capacity. As an action (3 charges from a Reconfiguration pool equal to your Intelligence modifier, refreshing on short rest), you can Overclock one willing creature. For 1 minute:
- Their physical ability scores (Strength, Dexterity, Constitution) each increase by 4 (maximum 24).
- They gain an additional action on each of their turns.
- At the end of the minute, they make a DC 14 Constitution saving throw. On a failure, they take 4d10 force damage (the system's physical toll) and gain 2 levels of exhaustion. On a success, they take half damage and gain 1 level of exhaustion.

**Level 10: Chrome Shaper Path Feature**
Your subclass grants an additional feature at this level.

**Level 11: Configuration Mastery**
Your Field Reconfigurations have deepened. You can now maintain configurations on a number of creatures equal to your proficiency bonus. Additionally, add the following configurations to your Field Reconfiguration list:
- **Phase Config:** For 1 round, the creature can move through solid objects and ignores difficult terrain. They cannot end their turn in a solid object.
- **Resonant Config:** The creature's attacks deal an additional 2d6 resonant damage for 1 minute.
- **Recovery Config:** The creature regains hit points equal to 2d8 + your Intelligence modifier at the start of each of their turns for 1 minute. Constitution save DC 14 at the end or 2 levels of exhaustion (the body cannot sustain accelerated repair).

**Level 12: Ability Score Improvement**
As level 4.

**Level 12: Dual Frame**
You can install two Experimental Frames on a single creature simultaneously. The second frame's Constitution save DC increases by 2. Additionally, you can install an Experimental Frame on yourself without penalty.

**Level 13: Combat Architecture**
Your ability to redesign enhancement configurations has extended into real-time combat application. As a bonus action, you can perform a Combat Architecture adjustment on any creature within 30 feet:
- Against enemies: attempt to crash their configuration (forced Intelligence save against DC = 8 + proficiency + your Intelligence modifier; on failure, one enhancement feature goes offline for 1 minute).
- For allies: perform an emergency boost (one ally gains advantage on their next d20 roll and +10 feet of movement for 1 round).

**Level 14: Chrome Shaper Path Feature**
Your subclass grants an additional feature at this level.

**Level 15: Resonance Architect**
Your understanding of the relationship between Echomind and enhancement has reached the level that the Sutensai calls Resonance Architecture. During a long rest, you can perform a deep resonance restructuring on a willing creature. This permanently improves their enhancement configuration:
- Choose one ability score. It permanently increases by 1 (maximum 20).
- Choose one of their class features. It improves in a specific way (work with DM — the improvement should be meaningful but not broken: increased damage, longer duration, additional uses, wider range).
- The creature's enhancement signature becomes more refined, registering to observers as higher quality.

This work requires 8 hours (a full long rest), rare materials (100 trade-metal weight), and the creature's full cooperation. You can perform one Resonance Architecture procedure per week.

**Level 16: Ability Score Improvement**
As level 4.

**Level 16: Rapid Architecture**
Your ability to redesign configurations in the field has become instinctive. You can use Rapid Reconfiguration an unlimited number of times per turn (previously limited by the Intelligence modifier pool). Additionally, Field Reconfigurations can now be applied to unwilling targets (who make a Constitution save against DC = 8 + proficiency + Intelligence modifier to resist the reconfiguration).

**Level 17: Perfect Frame**
Your Experimental Frame work has reached a precision that eliminates most of the risk of failure. The Constitution saving throw when an Experimental Frame expires is now DC 8 (previously 10+; this represents your designs being nearly perfectly calibrated to individual subjects). Additionally, your Experimental Frames now last for 24 hours instead of 8.

**Level 18: Chrome Shaper Path Feature**
Your subclass grants an additional feature at this level.

**Level 19: Ability Score Improvement**
As level 4.

**Level 20: The Living Blueprint**
At the apex of the Chrome Shaper's development is this: you have internalized enough knowledge of enhancement architecture that you carry the blueprint of the perfect configuration in your mind, and you understand that perfection is relative to the specific person — that the right configuration is the one that most completely expresses who that person is and what they are capable of becoming.

Mechanically:
- Your Field Reconfigurations now last indefinitely (until you end them as a bonus action, rather than the time limits listed).
- You can perform Resonance Architecture once per day instead of once per week.
- Once per long rest, you can perform a Total Reconfiguration on one willing creature, including yourself. This 10-minute process completely redesigns their enhancement configuration. They choose up to 3 class features to enhance (as per Resonance Architecture improvements), and you add one entirely new capability (DM works with you to determine a suitable new feature equivalent to a high-level class feature). The Total Reconfiguration is permanent.
- You can install your own designs in yourself during a short rest, without requiring an ally's assistance.

## Subclasses

### Path of the War Shaper — "Modification as weapon. The body as the weapon's delivery system."

The War Shaper has applied the Chrome Shaper's design philosophy specifically to combat — treating enhancement configuration as a battlefield asset, designing modifications that are primarily functional as weapons, and developing the ability to reconfigure allied combat capabilities on the fly in response to battlefield conditions. Where the Chrome Shaper's general practice works over hours and days, the War Shaper has developed the techniques of the forge to function at combat speed.

**Level 3: Combat Modification**
You have a set of pre-designed combat modification packages that you can apply rapidly. In combat, as a bonus action, you can apply a Combat Modification to an ally or yourself. Choose from the following packages:
- **Strike Package:** +2d6 damage to weapon attacks, lasts 3 rounds. Constitution save DC 11 when it expires or 1 level exhaustion.
- **Dodge Package:** +3 AC and advantage on Dexterity saves, lasts 3 rounds.
- **Disruption Package:** Attacks impose disadvantage on the target's next save or attack, lasts 3 rounds.

Each package can only be applied once per creature per combat.

**Level 6: War Configuration**
Your combat modification packages have expanded. Add the following:
- **Surge Package:** The creature can attack three times instead of twice on their turn (or twice instead of once if they have Extra Attack), for 1 round. This replaces their normal Extra Attack for that round.
- **Resilience Package:** The creature gains 30 temporary hit points.
- **Overwatch Package:** The creature can make a ranged attack as a reaction when any creature moves within 30 feet of them, for 1 minute.

**Level 10: Battle Forge**
During combat, when you take the Attack action, you can replace one of your attacks with a Battle Forge action — rapidly modifying an adjacent ally's or your own configuration. You immediately apply any Combat Modification or Field Reconfiguration that would normally require a bonus action, without using your bonus action.

**Level 14: Configuration Command**
You can manage the battlefield's enhancement landscape. As an action (once per short rest):
- Boost all allies within 30 feet: they gain advantage on their next attack roll and +10 feet of movement.
- Suppress all enemies within 30 feet: they make a Constitution save (DC = 8 + proficiency + Intelligence modifier) or have disadvantage on their next attack and saving throw.

### Path of the Edge Builder — "I make things that shouldn't exist. Then I make them exist reliably. Then I make them better."

The Edge Builder is the research-and-development specialist: the Chrome Shaper who is primarily interested in what has never been done before. They push the boundaries of modification design into territory where the Wire Market has no catalog entries, where the Sutensai has no theological framework, and where the only data comes from iteration and consequence. They are responsible for some of the most significant enhancement innovations in recent Tesshari history. They are also responsible for the highest number of experimental casualties, though they track these numbers carefully and take them seriously.

**Level 3: Prototype Work**
Your Experimental Frames are more ambitious than standard. When you design an Experimental Frame:
- The effect can be up to one level more powerful than the standard guideline (DM adjudicates).
- The Constitution save DC is 1 lower than it would be for a standard Frame of equivalent power.
- When an Experimental Frame fails (the Constitution save is failed), you can make an Intelligence check (DC = the Frame's complexity + 5) to learn exactly why it failed. On a success, you permanently redesign the Frame — future versions of that same Frame have their DC reduced by 2.

**Level 6: Failure Analysis**
Your comprehensive documentation of failure modes has built an enormous knowledge base. You gain:
- Advantage on all checks related to identifying the cause of enhancement malfunction.
- Once per long rest, when an Experimental Frame fails catastrophically, you can attempt an emergency catch — rolling Intelligence vs. DC 15 — to reduce the failure effect to half (1 level of exhaustion instead of 2, half damage, etc.).
- When observing any enhancement failure (ally or enemy), you can identify the specific design flaw causing it as a reaction.

**Level 10: Theoretical Limit**
Your design practice has reached the boundary of what the Wire Market's orthodoxy believes is possible. You can attempt modifications that other practitioners would decline to attempt:
- Install modifications that the Wire Market considers "impossible configurations" (DM works with you on what these mean in campaign context — they should be genuinely powerful and genuinely risky).
- Treat any Experimental Frame as if the subject has been profiled (Adaptive Design level 8), even if they haven't, because your theoretical models are now comprehensive enough to account for unknown variables.

**Level 14: Living Proof**
You have demonstrated, repeatedly and specifically, that the Wire Market's limitations are institutional rather than physical. Once per arc, you can design and build a completely new type of modification that has never existed before. Work with your DM to define what this is — it should be significant enough to matter to the campaign, powerful enough to be worth the effort, and have a design constraint (something it does not do, something it requires, or something it risks) that reflects the reality that even the Edge Builder cannot make something for nothing.

### Path of the Resonance Sculptor — "The metal is not the modification. The resonance is. I work with the resonance."

The Resonance Sculptor has followed the most spiritually demanding path available to a Chrome Shaper: the one that takes the Iron Sutra's claim that Echomind and enhancement are a unity seriously enough to work with them as a unity. Where other Chrome Shapers modify the enhancement hardware and accept that the resonance will adapt, the Resonance Sculptor modifies the resonance directly, shaping the interaction between a person's soul and their metal at a level that neither pure engineering nor pure Sutensai practice can reach independently. The results are extraordinary. The Sutensai considers this practice both magnificent and presumptuous, which is an accurate characterization.

**Level 3: Resonance Touch**
Your Modification Read has become a resonance read. When you analyze a creature's enhancements:
- You perceive the resonant relationship between their Echomind and each modification — not just the technical state but the spiritual state.
- You know what the person values, fears, and desires as reflected in their resonance calibration (not their private thoughts — their embodied relationship with their own enhancements, which is different and often more honest).
- You can suggest one resonance improvement that will strengthen a specific aspect of their character along the lines of their own values (DM determines mechanical benefit based on specifics).

**Level 6: Resonant Enhancement**
When you install an Experimental Frame using resonance techniques:
- The Frame integrates with the subject's Echomind rather than just their hardware, making it feel natural rather than foreign.
- The Frame's Constitution save DC is reduced by 2 (the Echomind's acceptance smooths the integration).
- The Frame's effect is subtly personalized — it amplifies something specific to this person's resonance (DM determines a small additional bonus reflecting their character).

**Level 10: Soul Engineering**
You can directly improve the resonant relationship between a person's Echomind and their modifications. During a long rest, you can perform Soul Engineering on a willing creature. Choose one effect:
- **Resonance Deepening:** The creature's class features that involve resonant or spiritual components are enhanced — their damage, duration, or range improves by 25% permanently.
- **Echomind Clarity:** The creature becomes immune to effects that would cloud, alter, or read their Echomind without their consent.
- **Resonant Armor:** The creature's Echomind-enhancement resonance provides passive protection — they gain a permanent +1 to all saving throws.

**Level 14: The Perfect Resonance**
You have achieved what the Sutensai calls the highest engineering aspiration: the perfectly resonant configuration, where a person's modifications express their Echomind with no friction, no loss, and no gap between who they are and what they can do. Once per arc, you can perform the Perfect Resonance procedure on one willing creature (including yourself). This 8-hour process:
- Permanently improves their primary ability score by 2 (maximum 24 — the Resonance Sculptor can exceed the normal 20 cap through this specific procedure, because the improvement comes from within the person rather than being externally imposed).
- Removes all enhancement misalignments and degradations permanently.
- Grants them resistance to dissolution effects and immunity to Shell-state progression — their resonance is too perfectly aligned to be disrupted by external forces.

## Roleplaying the Chrome Shaper

The Chrome Shaper's central moral question is about the ethics of transformation. You can change people — not cosmetically but fundamentally, altering what they can do, how their body works, how their soul interfaces with their metal. This is what the work is. The question is when it is right to do it, at whose request, and with what understanding of the consequences. A person who asks you for a modification that you know will harm them in a way they don't fully understand is presenting you with a clear problem. A person who asks for something experimental, knowing the risk but accepting it — that's different, and you probably have a clear position. The space between these is where the work actually happens.

Consider your relationship to failure. The Chrome Shaper who is good at the work is the Chrome Shaper who has built their knowledge on a foundation of things that didn't work — configurations that failed, modifications that caused harm, designs that were theoretically sound and practically disastrous. These failures were not equipment tests. They were people. How does your character carry the catalog of what went wrong? Are they honest about it? Do they keep records? Have they found a way to frame the failures that allows them to continue, and is that framing accurate?

The resonance sculptor path asks you to take seriously the idea that you are working with something that the Iron Sutra calls sacred — the relationship between a person's soul and their body's metal. This puts you in an unusual position relative to the Sutensai: you may be doing work that the Sutra's theology validates at a higher level than most of what the Sutra's practitioners actually accomplish. This is not humility. It is a specific professional confidence that some Chrome Shapers carry and that the Sutensai find alternatively admirable and infuriating.

Your relationship to innovation is a relationship to risk — not your own risk, but the risk of the people you work with. The things you want to build and test require willing subjects, and the best willing subjects are people who trust your judgment. Managing the weight of that trust — being worth it, and knowing when you're not — is the ethical core of the Chrome Shaper's practice.

## Connections to the Ascension War

Every faction in the Ascension War needs Chrome Shapers to maintain and upgrade the enhancement configurations of their military and political assets. The Warlord's forces need War Shapers who can maintain elite unit performance under campaign conditions. The Monk's Sutra Resurgent needs Resonance Sculptors who can support the spiritual-enhancement alignment that the Monk's theology demands. The Mage needs Edge Builders for reasons that are less clear and more alarming.

The specific Ascension War hook for Chrome Shapers is this: the Seventh Convergence prophecy, as the Sutensai understands it, requires the successful claimant to achieve a complete integration of flesh, metal, and Echomind — to become, in the most literal sense, a living resonance. A Chrome Shaper at the highest levels of their practice is one of the few people in Tesshari who might be able to assist with or assess this integration. They may not be a combatant in the Ascension War's martial sense. They may, instead, be the person who determines whether a claimant's enhancement architecture is actually capable of achieving godhood — or who discovers that one claimant's configuration is something that should not exist and that the Chrome Shaper is the only person who can tell the difference.

# Wireweave
## "The Wire runs through everything. Every lock, every secret, every wall. I don't break in. I read the place, and then I am already there."

## Overview

The Wire Craft network of Tesshari is the cyberpunk world's circulatory system — the infrastructure of power, information, and control that makes the Caste hierarchy's efficiency possible. Enhancement signatures flow through it. Security systems run on it. The communication infrastructure of every major lord, faction, and Wire Market house depends on its nodes and relays. Most people interact with the Wire as passive participants, their own enhancement networks pinging nearby nodes in a constant background dialogue that they neither control nor notice. The Wireweave has chosen to understand it, and that understanding, applied deliberately, is the closest thing to sorcery that Tesshari's technology-grounded world produces.

A Wireweave is not a hacker in the conventional sense — a person who has learned to exploit security flaws through patient technical work. They are something more integrated and more dangerous: a person whose own enhancement network has been redesigned to interface with Wire Craft architecture at a fundamental level, treating external systems as extensions of their own cognitive space. When a Wireweave enters the Wire, they are not remotely operating a tool. They are extending their consciousness into a digital medium, navigating it with the same intuitive fluency that a highly-practiced martial artist navigates physical space. The distinction between the Wireweave's mind and the Wire system they inhabit becomes genuinely ambiguous.

The technical modifications that make this possible are a significant undertaking. The primary interface — called the Weave Port — is a cranial installation that sits at the junction of the Wireweave's organic nervous system and their enhancement network, creating a tri-directional pathway between biological cognition, resonant metal, and digital systems. Early Weave Ports were crude, dangerous, and frequently caused partial Echomind dissolution in their operators. Modern ones, built by the specialized craftspeople that the Wireweave community calls Loom-Makers, are refined — they are safer, faster, and allow the depth of integration that distinguishes the current generation of Wireweave from their predecessors. They are also still dangerous, still irreversible, and still require a specific kind of person: someone willing to have the boundary between their own mind and the external digital world become genuinely unclear.

The Sutensai regards the Wireweave with a specific theology of alarm. The Wire Craft network carries resonant echoes — the same harmonic frequencies that constitute Echomind presence, aggregated and digitized and transmitted. In the Sutra's analysis, the Wireweave's deep integration with this network is continuously exposing their Echomind to a signal environment saturated with the resonance of other people's souls, the digitized passage of the dead, and the complex harmonic architecture of a technology that was never designed with spiritual consequences in mind. They are not wrong. The Wireweave who has operated at deep integration for long enough knows that the Sutensai's concern is not theological abstraction. The Wire contains things. The Wireweave has met them.

## The Wireweave in the World

The Wire Market's official position is that Wireweave are valuable, licensed specialists — their work is necessary for network security, infrastructure maintenance, and the sophisticated communication operations that major houses depend on. The Wire Market also knows that every licensed Wireweave is operating at the edge of what the Market's terms of service technically permits, and that the gap between licensed Wire access and what a skilled Wireweave can actually do with it is very large. The Market tolerates this because the alternative — a Wireweave population that is hostile to Market interests — is considerably worse.

In the Broken Reaches, the balance of access to Wire Craft infrastructure largely determines military and political power. Lords who can afford sophisticated Wire security have information advantages, communication speed, and the ability to blind enemies to their movements. Lords who cannot afford good Wire security are vulnerable in ways that no amount of conventional military strength can entirely compensate for. The Wireweave who works for a given lord is therefore among their most strategically important people — and the most carefully watched, because a Wireweave who changes sides takes knowledge and access that cannot be simply revoked.

Among the common people, Wireweave are often the keepers of informal communication networks — the underground lines of information that travel through Wire infrastructure below the level of lord-controlled monitoring. In occupied or controlled territories, these informal networks are survival infrastructure: knowing when patrols move, when checkpoints are established, when it is safe to move. The Wireweave who maintains these networks is doing revolutionary work that they would often deny performing, because the denial is practically necessary and the work is still done.

## Subclasses

### Path of the Combat Weave — "Signal, interrupt, end. In that order."

Combat Weave Wireweave have taken their technical ability and oriented it entirely toward the destruction of enemies. Not primarily through direct weapons — though they fight capably — but through the systematic dismantling of opponents' enhancement capabilities during battle. They are the nightmare of the highly-enhanced enemy combatant: the presence in a fight that systematically removes options, degrades capabilities, and creates cascading failures in systems that were supposed to be reliable.

**Level 3: Combat Interface**
Your Wire abilities have been optimized for combat application. You gain:
- Signal Burst and Neural Spike cost 1 fewer Weave Charge.
- When you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can simultaneously release a Wire disruption — the creature makes a SIGNAL resistance check (Weave DC) or takes 5 lightning-analog damage (fixed) and loses damage riders on their next resistance check.
- You can use Wire abilities as a free action if they target a creature you have attacked this turn.

**Level 6: Enhancement Crash**
Your targeted disruption of enhancement systems has become surgical. When you use Enhancement Disrupt:
- On a failed save, you can choose which specific feature is disrupted (not random).
- On a critical hit using Neural Spike, the target's enhancement network automatically crashes — all enhancement features are suppressed for 1 minute (no save).

**Level 10: Signal Overload**
Once per combat, as an action, you can release a full Signal Overload. All enhanced creatures within 30 feet make a SIGNAL resistance check (Weave DC). On a failure:
- They take 33 lightning-analog damage (fixed).
- All their enhancement features are suppressed for 1 minute.
- Their Wire connectivity is severed for 1 hour (they cannot send or receive Wire communications or be tracked by Wire systems).

**Level 14: Total Crash**
Your ability to crash enhancement networks has reached its terminal expression. Once per day, as an action, choose one enhanced creature within 60 feet. That creature makes a FRAME resistance check (Weave DC + 2). On a failure, their enhancement network enters total cascade failure — they lose all class features (including passive ones), their enhancements deal 17 damage to them (fixed, the failure's physical consequence), and they must make a FRAME resistance check, difficulty 4, to prevent their enhancement network from going permanently offline. A Forge Tender can repair permanent shutdown, but it requires 1 hour of work.

### Path of the Wire Broker — "Information is the only currency that doesn't devalue when you spend it. It compounds."

The Wire Broker is the intelligence specialist — the Wireweave who has recognized that the real value of Wire access is not tactical but informational. They collect knowledge. They trade in secrets. They build networks of information that run parallel to and often more deeply than the formal intelligence operations of any lord or faction. They are not spies in the conventional sense — they are more comprehensive than that, and less accountable. A Wire Broker who has been operating for a decade in the Broken Reaches knows things that no single faction fully understands, and they are navigating the Ascension War with a map that is more complete than anyone else's.

**Level 3: Archive**
You maintain a personal digital Archive in your enhancement network — a secure data repository that cannot be accessed by any external system. Your Archive grows with your operation:
- After any significant interaction (encounter, meeting, infiltration), you can store a full record in your Archive.
- You have instant access to any stored record (no check required).
- The Archive is immune to Wire Crash or any attempt to delete or access it externally.
- Once per combat, you can consult the Archive on a specific topic, gaining +3 to one SIGNAL check related to that topic (because you have, somewhere, a record that's relevant).

**Level 6: Signal Network**
You have established a network of Wire contacts across the Broken Reaches — informants, other Wireweave, sympathetic Wire operators, and passive monitoring nodes you have installed over time. Once per session:
- You can query your Signal Network about any topic. The GM provides information equivalent to 1 hour of dedicated Wire research.
- You can pass information through your network to any recipient you know, regardless of Wire infrastructure between you.
- You receive unsolicited alerts when significant events occur that your network has been monitoring.

**Level 10: Leverage**
Your information holdings have reached the point where they constitute leverage — the kind that changes behavior. Once per day, you can deploy Leverage against one creature who has secrets that you have discovered. The target must make a RESONANCE resistance check (Weave DC). On a failure, they will not take hostile action against you or your companions for 24 hours, and they will provide one significant favor (access, information, or direct assistance) within that time. This is not mind control — it is the persuasion of someone who understands the consequences of refusal. Creatures with nothing to lose are immune.

**Level 14: The Full Picture**
Your information about the current conflict has reached a comprehensiveness that is almost paralyzing in its specificity. Once per arc, you can spend 1 hour in Wire consultation to construct a Full Picture — a comprehensive analysis of the current state of any organization, conflict, or situation you have been observing. The Full Picture includes:
- All major actors' true motivations (as opposed to stated ones), as far as your intelligence can determine.
- The most likely next major development.
- One hidden fact that no one in the situation wants widely known.
The GM provides these answers based on the actual campaign state — this feature should deliver meaningful intelligence, not vague impressions.

### Path of the Iron Afterlife Weave — "The dead are still connected. Someone has to answer their messages."

These Wireweave have gone too deep, too often, and found something on the other side that they didn't expect: the Iron Afterlife's digital architecture, pressing against the Wire network from the inside. The Iron Afterlife exists as a resonant digital realm — and the Wire Craft network, which carries resonant frequency, is not entirely separate from it. The Iron Afterlife Weave Wireweave has found the seams, the places where the network of the living and the network of the dead bleed together, and they operate in those places with the careful expertise of someone who knows that the line they are standing on exists for good reasons that they have chosen to ignore.

**Level 3: Death's Signal**
Your Wire access has extended to the boundary of the Iron Afterlife. You gain:
- You can detect recently-departed Echominds (within 24 hours of death) as Wire-presence anomalies within 300 feet.
- You can access the last transmitted data of a deceased enhanced individual — the Wire records their final Wire activity before death. This provides their last location, their last communication, and the state of their systems at moment of death.
- Once per combat, you can ask the Iron Afterlife's boundary one yes/no question. The answer comes as a signal fluctuation — unmistakably intentional, and always accurate on factual questions (questions about the future are answered with probability, which is less reliable).

**Level 6: Ghost Channel**
You can open a Wire channel to the Iron Afterlife directly. As an action (10 Weave Charges), you open a Ghost Channel to one specific Echomind in the Iron Afterlife, if you know their identity signature. The communication works like a Wire call — it lasts up to 10 minutes, both parties can speak and listen, but the Echomind in the Iron Afterlife is under no obligation to answer truthfully or at all. The channel is visible to Sutensai assessors and carries significant theological risk (GM determines long-term consequences of frequent use).

**Level 10: Afterlife Architecture**
Your understanding of the Iron Afterlife's Wire structure has become architectural. You can:
- Map the locations of Echominds in the Iron Afterlife relative to the physical world.
- Detect when an Echomind from the Iron Afterlife is attempting to push through into the physical world, and trace their origin point.
- Temporarily suppress an Iron Afterlife entity's ability to manifest in the physical world (contested Intelligence check; on success, the entity cannot manifest for 1 hour).
- Once per day, extract a specific piece of information from an Echomind in the Iron Afterlife without their cooperation — the extraction requires 1 hour and a SIGNAL check, difficulty 5, and it leaves traces that the extracted Echomind will resent.

**Level 14: Between Sovereign**
You have achieved something that the Sutensai considers either a profound heresy or a catastrophic accident: full sovereignty over the seam between the Wire and the Iron Afterlife. You can:
- Open or close the seam in a 300-foot radius for up to 1 hour (preventing Iron Afterlife entities from manifesting, or actively pulling them through — your choice, once per day).
- When you die, your Echomind has the option to route through the Wire instead of directly entering the Iron Afterlife. Your Echomind persists in the Wire network for up to 1 week, able to communicate through connected nodes and potentially influence Wire-connected systems. At the end of the week, you must choose to pass to the Iron Afterlife or attempt a permanent Wire existence (the GM adjudicates what this means — it is not standard resurrection and comes with costs and conditions).

### Path of the Loom-Maker — "I don't use the Wire. I build it, and then I live in what I've made."

The Loom-Maker is the engineer-specialist: the Wireweave who has turned their Wire access toward construction rather than intrusion. They build Wire infrastructure — nodes, networks, encrypted communication channels, autonomous security systems, and custom enhancement installations — and their built systems are of extraordinary quality, reflecting their deep personal understanding of how Wire architecture works at its lowest level. They are the people who upgrade the Wire Market's infrastructure, build the communication networks that factions depend on, and occasionally create things that the Wire Market would prefer not to exist in the hands of people outside their control.

**Level 3: Construct Node**
As part of a short rest, you can construct one of the following Wire nodes using materials worth 5 trade-metal:
- **Relay Node:** Extends Wire coverage in an area (60-foot radius) and amplifies signal quality. Your Wire abilities used within a relay's range cost 1 fewer Charge.
- **Sensor Node:** Monitors a 60-foot area, transmitting to your interface kit. You receive notifications of any Wire activity, physical movement, or enhancement use within the area.
- **Encryption Node:** Creates an encrypted Wire channel within 60 feet that cannot be intercepted except by a SIGNAL check, difficulty 5.

Nodes last for 24 hours unless physically destroyed. You can maintain a number of nodes equal to your Intelligence modifier.

**Level 6: Field Upgrade**
You can perform Wire enhancement upgrades on willing creatures during a short rest. Choose one enhancement upgrade per creature:
- **Signal Boost:** The creature's Wire-based abilities (class features that use Wire or digital systems) have their range doubled for 24 hours.
- **Armor Protocol:** Add an additional 2 to the creature's AC until the next long rest (the upgrade integrates a deflection subroutine).
- **Combat Enhancement:** One specific weapon attack of the creature's deals an additional 5 lightning-analog damage (fixed) for 24 hours.

**Level 10: Network Architecture**
Your ability to construct Wire infrastructure has reached a large-scale expression. During a long rest, you can construct:
- A full Wire network covering a 1-mile radius, with full monitoring and communication capacity, in 8 hours of work.
- An autonomous Wire defense system that attacks unauthorized enhanced intruders (deals 14 lightning-analog damage per round (fixed) to unauthorized individuals within the network, FRAME resistance check (Weave DC) for half).
- A secure digital vault within the Wire — a protected data space that cannot be accessed without your authorization.

**Level 14: The Impossible Build**
Once per arc, you can spend 1 week constructing a Wire Craft installation of extraordinary capability. Work with your GM — the installation should be something meaningful to the campaign (a communication network for a resistance, a Wire bridge between two previously-separated territories, a system that affects the Iron Afterlife's accessibility in a region). The installation requires rare materials (worth 1,000+ trade-metal) and time, but the result is permanent and can change the strategic landscape of the campaign.

## Roleplaying the Wireweave

The Wireweave's most profound inner tension is about the boundary of the self. Your consciousness extends, routinely, into a digital medium. Your perception regularly includes data streams and Wire signals that biological consciousness does not produce. When you are in the Wire, it is your native environment in a way that the physical world is not quite. What does this do to your sense of identity over time? The Sutensai's warning about Echomind dissolution is not unfounded — prolonged Wire immersion does change people. The Wireweave who has been operating for years is not the person who first installed their Weave Port. The question is whether those changes are growth or loss, and whether there is a meaningful difference.

You know things that you shouldn't know, routinely. Wire access means that privacy, in the conventional sense, doesn't quite exist for the people you interact with. You have to make constant choices about what to look at, what to retain, and what to do with what you know. These choices define your ethics more than any formal code, because no formal code was written for your specific situation.

Consider your relationship to the Iron Afterlife touches in your work. The Wire carries death resonance. If you have pursued the deeper abilities, you have communicated with the dead, accessed Echomind records, and operated in the seam between the living and digital-dead worlds. What is your theology of this? Do you have one? The Sutensai has one, and they have opinions about yours. The Wire Broker paths tend to treat it pragmatically. The Iron Afterlife path treats it as a genuine domain with its own ethics. The Combat Weave tends not to think about it. These are all honest choices.

Your relationship to the Wire Market is perpetually complicated. They trained you, licensed you, and employ many people like you. They also know exactly how much of what you can do is technically outside the bounds of what they authorized. The Market's tolerance is calculated, not principled. How do you navigate an employer who values you because you're dangerous and tolerates you because the alternative is worse for them?

## Connections to the Ascension War

The Wireweave knows more about the Ascension War than any other class, because the Ascension War runs through Wire infrastructure. The three claimants communicate through Wire networks that the Wireweave can access. Their intelligence operations use Wire tools that the Wireweave understands better than the operators running them. The strategic picture of the Ascension War — who is winning, who is bluffing, who is weaker than they appear — is more visible through Wire access than through any other vantage point.

This creates the Wireweave's specific Ascension War problem: they know enough to know that none of the public narratives are fully accurate, and they may know what is. This puts them in a position of unusual power and unusual danger — because the people running the war know that Wireweave have this access, and the claimants who understand this have very specific ideas about what should be done with Wireweave who are not firmly committed to their cause.

The Mage's faction is of particular concern for Wireweave who have accessed the right systems: there are Wire Craft signatures associated with the Mage's operations that don't match any registered enhancement configuration. Something is wrong with the Mage's digital footprint, and it's the kind of wrong that a Wireweave would notice and that they would be in danger for having noticed.

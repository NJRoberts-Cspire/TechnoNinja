# Shadow Daimyo
## "I haven't threatened you. I have simply arranged for you to understand your situation clearly. Whether you call that a threat is a matter of perspective."

## Overview

In Tesshari, information has always been more valuable than steel. Steel breaks. Steel wears down. Steel can be taken away. Information, once known, cannot be unknown. The shape of a lord's financial obligations, the name of an intelligence officer's secondary contact, the scheduled route of a supply chain that someone wants interrupted — these things, in the right hands, are more powerful than armies. The Shadow Daimyo is the person with the right hands.

They are not fighters, primarily, though they can be dangerous. They are not sorcerers or spiritual practitioners, though they have been known to use both. They are operators: people who have built, over time, a network of contacts, informants, favors owed, and secrets held that constitutes a form of power entirely distinct from military might or spiritual authority. They know things. They know people who know other things. They know which official is compromised, which family has a debt that is more social dynamite than financial obligation, which supply line's security chief can be convinced to look away at the right moment. Their arsenal is the accumulated weight of everything everyone would prefer to keep private, deployed with precision.

The tradition is old — older than the current territorial divisions, older than the modern Caste of Steel, older, arguably, than the Sutensai's dominance. Every court of every significant power in Tesshari's history has had someone who occupied this role, whether or not the role had a formal name. The Spymaster who knows where the bodies are, the Broker who carries messages between factions at war while serving all of them, the Court Blade who is simultaneously a lord's most trusted enforcer and the thing that enforcer's lord has learned to fear. These are the archetypes the Shadow Daimyo class develops from, and each is a different answer to the same question: in a world where information is power, what do you become when you have enough of it?

What the Shadow Daimyo costs is not the physical price of the combat classes or the spiritual toll of the practitioners. It is something more diffuse and, in the long run, more corrosive: they become the person who cannot trust anyone entirely, because they know too much about everyone, and they know exactly how easily the trust they extend can become a liability. The long-term Shadow Daimyo is a person surrounded by information about other people who has, over years of practice, become increasingly isolated from the authentic relationships that information was supposed to protect. Many of them know this is happening. Knowing does not stop it.

## The Shadow Daimyo in the World

Every major power in Tesshari employs Shadow Daimyos whether they acknowledge it or not. The information network that keeps a lord aware of their enemies' movements, the intelligence service that monitors which factions are making overtures to which, the person who quietly ensures that unfavorable information stays unfavorable and is available at the right moment — all of this is Shadow Daimyo work, and all of it happens continuously in every territory. What varies is whether the practitioner is an independent operator, a court employee, or something in between.

The Broker path is the most politically precarious: serving all sides while committing to none requires a reputation for neutrality that is both genuine and exhausting to maintain. Brokers in the Wire Market are known figures, sought out by all factions, trusted by none entirely, and protected by the universal understanding that eliminating a Broker is a loss for everyone — including the eliminating party, which suddenly finds itself without the communication channel the Broker was maintaining on their behalf. This protection is real. It is also not absolute, and Brokers know that the day they are more valuable dead than alive is a calculation that every side is constantly running.

The Court Blade is the most immediately dangerous: a person who combines political intelligence with the willingness and capability to act on it violently. Lords who employ Court Blades are buying a service that they will, eventually, live in some fear of. The Court Blade knows the court's secrets because they were hired to know them. They know the lord's secrets because the lord was, at some point, not careful enough. The relationship between a Court Blade and their employer is the most stable and most fraught professional relationship in Tesshari — each party depends on the other and cannot entirely trust them, and both parties are aware of this.

## Subclasses

### The Spymaster — "I Know Everything You'd Rather I Didn't"

The Spymaster has built the information network as an end in itself. Their Contacts are more numerous, more carefully cultivated, and more deeply integrated into the structures they monitor than any other Shadow Daimyo path. They do not act directly on what they know; they act through what they know, deploying information as a primary weapon and physical force only as a last resort. The Spymaster's greatest operational victory is one that no one can prove they were involved in, and they have had several.

**Level 3: Deep Cover Network**
You have Contacts that operate inside protected and monitored environments — within military commands, inside the Sutensai hierarchy, within major criminal organizations. Each such deep cover Contact occupies a more sensitive position than your standard Contacts and provides intelligence from inside structures others cannot access. You have 2 Deep Cover Contacts at this level and gain one more at levels 7, 11, and 15.

**Level 3: Signal Intelligence**
You can intercept Wire Craft communications in any area where you have an established Contact or have operated for at least 24 hours. As a 1-hour process, you can review the last 24 hours of Wire Craft traffic in a designated area and extract relevant information. The traffic is encoded; make an Intelligence check (DC determined by the quality of the encryption) to extract specific content.

**Level 6: Misdirection**
Once per long rest, you can plant deliberate misinformation that will reach your target through their own intelligence sources. The information is false in a specific, controlled way that you design. The target discovers this misinformation as if it were genuine — their sources confirm it, their contacts repeat it. Correcting the misdirection requires the target to identify the source problem (Investigation DC 20) and then independently verify the correct information. During the correction period (at minimum 1 week for a careful intelligence service), the target acts on the false information.

**Level 10: Perfect Dossier**
Your deep dossier on any creature provides complete information: not just what is discoverable but what they believe is private. You learn all significant secrets they hold, including the things they have taken deliberate steps to conceal. The DM provides this information honestly. If there are things the DM wants to keep secret for narrative reasons, they provide those secrets in sealed form — available when the narrative moment is right.

**Level 14: The Network Sees Everything**
Your network now covers every territory you have operated in. No significant event happens in those territories without you knowing within 48 hours. Assassinations, political changes, major military movements, significant criminal activity — your Contacts report, and you are informed. Once per long rest, you can query the network for the current location of any specific individual you have ever encountered, with a DC based on how carefully that individual hides themselves.

### The Court Blade — "I Am the Lord's Ear and the Lord's Edge"

The Court Blade is what happens when a Spymaster develops a combat specialization and decides that some problems require a personal touch. They are attached to a lord or commander as a combination of intelligence adviser, enforcer, and the person who carries out the operations that cannot be delegated to subordinates who might talk. They are the most immediately dangerous Shadow Daimyo path and the most personally complicated: their power depends entirely on their principal's continued trust and their principal's continued usefulness, and both of those conditions are perpetually in question.

**Level 3: Court Position**
You have an established position in a court or command structure. This provides: regular intelligence briefings on the court's affairs, the ability to invoke your principal's authority in their territory (as if you carried their personal seal), and access to the court's resources for sanctioned operations. Your principal trusts you to a degree that is extraordinary and which you have worked to deserve. Whether you continue to deserve it is an ongoing project.

**Level 3: Blade Work**
You have trained for the specific violence required by your role. You can use your Intelligence modifier instead of Strength or Dexterity for attack and damage rolls when using resonant blades and pulse arms. When you attack a target you have a dossier on, you deal an additional 1d6 damage as you exploit their physical patterns and habitual defenses.

**Level 6: Preemptive Intelligence**
You know who the threats to your principal are before they act. Once per long rest, after 1 hour of analysis, you can identify the most likely imminent threat to a person or operation you are protecting and describe a specific countermeasure that, if implemented, will prevent it with high probability. The DM confirms whether the countermeasure is correct given the actual threat.

**Level 10: Court Execution**
When you take a target by surprise (having initiated a combat where your target was unaware), your first attack automatically critically hits. Additionally, targets with lower Intelligence than yours (which you know from your dossier) have disadvantage on saving throws against your abilities in the first round of combat.

**Level 14: Absolute Loyalty, Absolute Access**
Your relationship with your principal has matured to the point of genuine mutual dependency. You have access to their most sensitive intelligence — you know everything they know. Additionally, once per long rest, you can invoke their absolute authority: deliver a command in their name that creatures of CR 10 or lower who recognize the principal's authority must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw (DC = your Shadow Daimyo save DC) or follow as if it came from the principal directly.

### The Broker — "All Sides Need Someone They Can Trust Not to Take Sides"

The Broker has built their operation on the premise that neutrality, genuinely maintained, is the most valuable commodity in a war. Everyone wants to talk to someone the other side also talks to. Everyone wants a channel they can trust not to leak. The Broker provides this service and has built a network that is genuinely useful to all parties — which means that all parties have reason to want the Broker alive and informed, and no party fully trusts them, and the Broker has learned that this is the most stable professional relationship available in Tesshari.

**Level 3: Universal Access**
You have Contacts in every major faction: Korrath's command, Vaen's network, the Wire Market, the Sutensai, and the major independent powers. These Contacts are aware that you serve multiple parties. They accept this. You can call on these Contacts for information or assistance from any faction, but doing so may alert other factions that you are in communication with their rivals (the DM determines what leaks and what doesn't).

**Level 3: Safe Passage**
You carry the informal protection of the Broker tradition. Creatures and organizations that recognize your role as a neutral Broker cannot attack you without losing significant social standing and intelligence access. You have advantage on saving throws against attacks from creatures who know your role. Once per long rest, in any hostile situation, you can declare neutrality: all parties must make a Wisdom saving throw (DC = your Shadow Daimyo save DC) or be unable to act against you for 1 minute while they calculate the cost of violating broker immunity.

**Level 6: The Middle Market**
You have turned the Broker role into a business. Once per long rest, you can broker a deal between two parties who need something from each other but cannot communicate directly. The deal requires both parties to send representatives to you. You identify what each party needs and wants, what each party can offer, and construct a mutually beneficial exchange. If both parties agree, the deal is sealed under the Broker's word — and your word carries spiritual weight equivalent to a Sutensai oath under Tesshari custom. Violating a Broker's sealed deal has social and spiritual consequences that the table should play seriously.

**Level 10: Perfect Neutrality**
Your neutrality is now so established that you can enter active conflict zones under Broker protection. You can move freely through areas controlled by any faction that recognizes the Broker tradition (this includes most major powers, though fringe groups may not). Within these areas, you cannot be detained without a formal process that will alert every party you serve — effective deterrence.

**Level 14: The Final Channel**
Once per long rest, you can open a communication channel between parties who are actively at war and have no other communication pathway. The channel is temporary (1 hour) but absolute — during this period, both parties communicate freely and cannot move against each other without breaking the channel, which would signal a violation of the Broker's arrangement to every party in the Broker's network simultaneously. This ability has, in Tesshari's history, prevented wars from starting and stopped wars from ending. The Broker understands that it can do both, and chooses when to use it accordingly.

## Roleplaying the Shadow Daimyo

**The Weight of What You Know:** You know things about everyone you have investigated, and you cannot unknow them. You know which ally has a secret that would destroy them. You know which enemy's family tragedy motivates their cruelty. You know the crime the official committed that they built their public career on concealing. What does it do to a person to carry this inventory of everyone's worst moments? How does your character maintain genuine relationships across this knowledge?

**The Network as Identity:** The Shadow Daimyo's power is their network. Without it, they are a competent person in a world full of competent people. With it, they are extraordinary. How did they build it? What did they trade to build it? What happens to them when a significant Contact is lost — to death, to compromise, to the unpredictable turbulence of the Ascension War?

**The Line You Won't Cross (And Whether You'll Cross It):** Every Shadow Daimyo has built an ethics framework around what they will and won't do with what they know. Some have explicit codes. Some have implicit ones they've never articulated. The Ascension War is producing situations that test those codes: when the information you have could save lives but using it would compromise the network, what do you do? When the leverage you have is the only thing preventing a catastrophe and using it would destroy a relationship you value — which do you choose?

**The End of Neutrality:** The Broker believes neutrality is possible and sustainable. The Spymaster builds leverage for everyone, including their allies. At some point in an existential war, neutrality ends — every player has to land somewhere. When that moment comes for your character, how do they decide where to land?

## Connections to the Ascension War

Korrath the Unbroken employs Spymasters extensively and Court Blades most directly. He understands information power practically, without philosophical overlay. He is a good employer in the narrow sense — he pays, he doesn't ask questions about methods, and he is loyal to assets that perform. He is a difficult employer in the broader sense — his operational tempo produces a lot of situations where the ethical constraints Shadow Daimyos have built around their practice become inconvenient, and he has a low tolerance for inconvenient constraints.

Vaen of the Empty Hand is genuinely terrible at information security and knows it. He employs a Spymaster less out of enthusiasm than out of necessity, and the relationship is uneasy. His theology of openness and radical authenticity sits poorly with the Shadow Daimyo's operational culture, and both parties are aware that they are working from incompatible premises. What Vaen's Spymaster has discovered about the internal condition of his movement is significant and has not all been reported.

The Hollow Author is, among other things, the most sophisticated information operator in Tesshari. The Author's puppet network is also an intelligence network — every puppet is a sensor, every conversation overheard is recorded. Shadow Daimyos who have encountered the Author's operational signature describe it as facing a mirror that shows a darker reflection. The Author has Contacts in every network in Tesshari, including the networks of Shadow Daimyos who would be appalled to know it.

# The Unnamed
## "I have read every category you have. I fit in the space between them. I know you find this uncomfortable. I have stopped apologizing for it."

## Overview

The first of the Prior Ascended has no name in the Iron Sutra. This is not an oversight. The Sutensai's scholars have argued for centuries about whether the name was lost, was deliberately removed, or never existed — whether the First Prior transcended the category of "named thing" as part of the Ascension, or whether the name was suppressed by later political forces for reasons now beyond recovery. The name is gone. What remains is the description: the one who could not be placed, who moved between all categories, who was neither warrior nor priest nor practitioner but something that included all of those and exceeded them, and who Ascended not by perfecting one thing but by becoming the one thing that could not be defined by any existing framework.

In the era of the Ascension War, something similar is happening to specific individuals. Their Echomind is developing in ways the existing frameworks cannot account for: not toward the Between, as the Iron Monk practices, not toward a single extreme, as the Hollow has chosen, not toward one defined tradition. In multiple directions simultaneously, across categories, in ways that produce abilities with no precedent and no existing class descriptor. The Sutensai's Readers who encounter these individuals report something unusual in the Reading: the Echomind signature is clear, is present, is genuine — but it does not map onto any of the standard patterns that the Sutensai's centuries of practice have documented. The Reader can see it. They cannot categorize it.

The tradition — if it can be called a tradition — does not have formal schools. It does not have a lineage. The individuals who discover themselves developing in this direction find each other, sometimes, and compare notes with the wariness of people who have been told their whole lives that they are aberrant and are not entirely sure whether that judgment is wrong. What they discover, when they compare, is that no two of them are quite the same. The abilities are different. The patterns are different. The direction of development is different. The only thing they have in common is the category: Unnamed. Things that do not fit anywhere else.

The class is mechanically unique: it does not grow within a single tree of defined abilities. It grows by Convergence (inward, toward Ascension, pulling abilities from the spiritual disciplines) and Divergence (outward, spreading into every domain, pulling abilities from everywhere). Neither path produces a standard practitioner. Both produce something genuinely surprising.

## The Unnamed in the World

The social position of the Unnamed is consistently difficult to describe, which is part of the point. They have no standard role that communities understand how to slot them into. The Sutensai finds them anomalous and watches them carefully; not the watching of surveillance but the watching of genuine uncertainty about what they represent and how to respond to them. The warlords find them interesting if they produce military value and confusing if they don't. The ordinary person who meets one tends to notice that something is off about the reading — this person does not register as what they present as — and calibrate their response to the quality of the interaction rather than any category the Unnamed fits.

Among each other, the Unnamed have a quality of recognition that they describe as unmistakable and rarely choose to explain to outsiders. The Echomind in unusual development has a specific signature to anyone who can read it, and the Unnamed can all read it, and what they see in each other is both familiar and genuinely individual. There are no two alike. This produces camaraderie built on the shared experience of not fitting, rather than on shared methods or shared history. It is the specific camaraderie of people who have been told they are wrong and have found each other and discovered that the shared experience of being wrong is its own kind of community.

The Unnamed class text must be transparent about something: this is the most demanding class to run as a DM and the most flexible class to play as a player. The Unique Signature mechanic requires collaboration. The Divergent path's breadth requires negotiation. Neither works without trust at the table, and the class rewards exactly the players who have that trust and brings friction to tables where it doesn't exist. This is a feature of the class's design, not a flaw — the Unnamed is the class of players who have decided to do something genuinely collaborative and nonstandard, which is what the First Prior apparently did, and which is the specific gift of the class.

## D&D 5E Class Features

**Hit Die:** d8
**Primary Ability:** Intelligence or Wisdom (player's choice at Level 1, represents the direction of development)
**Saving Throw Proficiencies:** Two of the following, player's choice at Level 1: Strength, Dexterity, Constitution, Intelligence, Wisdom, Charisma
**Armor Proficiencies:** Wire-mesh armor, plus one additional armor type chosen at Level 3 (reflects your development direction)
**Weapon Proficiencies:** Resonant blades (short and standard), pulse arms (sidearm weight), plus two additional weapon types chosen at level 3
**Tool Proficiencies:** Two of any tool proficiencies, player's choice
**Skills:** Choose 4 from any available skill list
**Starting Equipment:** Wire-mesh wrappings, resonant blade (short), a journal that contains the beginning of a theory about what you are becoming, one tool from your two proficiency choices, 15 gp equivalent

### Class Features by Level

**Level 1: The Unnamed State**
You are developing in a way the existing frameworks don't contain. Three immediate effects:
- Your Echomind does not read cleanly in standard Sutensai Reading. A Reader gets "active, present, genuine" and beyond that, uncertainty. This is not deception — your Echomind simply doesn't map to the patterns they know.
- Choose your Primary Ability (Intelligence or Wisdom). All Unnamed abilities that reference "your primary modifier" use this.
- You begin building your Unique Signature (see below).

**Level 1: Unique Signature**
The Unnamed's power is personal. You begin with 3 Signature Abilities — choose them from any of the following origin pools (or propose analogues with DM approval):
- Draw from existing class features at their lowest level from any Tesshari class (Iron Monk, Echo Speaker, Void Walker, Sutensai, Flesh Shaper, Puppet Binder, Blood Smith, Shadow Daimyo, Voice of Debt, Merchant Knife, Iron Herald, Curse Eater, Shell Dancer, Fracture Knight) — you gain the feature as if you were level 1 in that class, but it is flavored as emerging from your Echomind rather than from formal training.
- A unique ability developed with your DM, based on your character's specific development.

You gain 1 additional Signature Ability at levels 2, 4, 7, 10, 13, 16, and 19 (total of 10 by level 20). These can be from any origin pool and do not need to be internally consistent — the Unnamed is not consistent.

**Level 1: Development Points**
You have a pool of Development Points equal to your primary modifier + your proficiency bonus (minimum 2). Development Points fuel your Signature Abilities and certain Unnamed features. You regain all Development Points on a long rest and half on a short rest.

**Level 2: Signature Ability (1st additional)**

**Level 3: Unnamed Subclass**
You choose The Convergent or The Divergent. This choice does not restrict your Signature Ability choices — it defines additional features you gain at levels 3, 6, 10, and 14 and reflects the general direction of your development.

**Level 3: Adaptive Defense**
Your development has produced an instinctive protective response. Your AC equals 10 + your Dexterity modifier + your primary modifier while wearing only wire-mesh armor or less. This can be flavored as psychic shielding, resonant fortification, or any physical manifestation that reflects your current Signature direction.

**Level 4: Ability Score Improvement / Signature Ability (2nd additional)**

**Level 5: Development Surge**
Once per short rest, as a bonus action, spend 3 Development Points to activate a Surge state for 1 minute. During the Surge: all Signature Abilities cost half their normal Development Points (rounded down, minimum 0), you have advantage on one saving throw type of your choice (chosen when you activate), and your primary ability modifier is treated as 2 higher for purposes of calculating any DCs or bonuses. When the Surge ends, you cannot benefit from another Surge until you complete a short rest.

**Level 5: Expanding Reach**
Choose one Signature Ability you have. Its range, if it has one, doubles. Its duration, if it has one, extends by 50%. Its damage, if it deals damage, increases by one die size. This expansion applies permanently to that ability. You can expand one additional Signature Ability at levels 9 and 15.

**Level 6: Subclass Feature**

**Level 6: Signature Integration**
Two of your Signature Abilities can now be activated in concert — when you use one, you may immediately use the other as a bonus action (paying its normal cost). Choose which two abilities are integrated; this pairing can be changed when you finish a long rest. The integration represents your Echomind discovering a resonance between two apparently separate aspects of your development.

**Level 7: Signature Ability (3rd additional)**

**Level 8: Ability Score Improvement**

**Level 8: Between All Things**
Your Echomind has developed enough that you intuitively sense the category of any creature or effect near you. You always know: what class tradition (if any) a creature's abilities draw from, whether a creature is under any form of external control (puppet, enchantment, compulsion), and whether a creature's stated motivation is their genuine motivation or a performed one. This is passive. It cannot be turned off.

**Level 9: Expanded Reach (2nd Signature Ability)**

**Level 10: Subclass Feature**

**Level 10: Signature Ability (4th additional)**

**Level 10: The Pattern Emerges**
Your Echomind has been developing long enough that a pattern is becoming visible. Work with your DM to identify the 2-3 Signature Abilities that have proven most central to your character's identity. These become your Core Signature. Core Signature Abilities:
- Cost 1 fewer Development Point to activate (minimum 0)
- Cannot be suppressed by anti-enhancement effects — they are too deep in your Echomind to be reached by standard suppression
- Are visually distinctive when activated — there is a specific, recognizable visual or physical manifestation unique to you. Two Unnamed have never produced the same visual signature.

**Level 11: Echomind Pressure**
Your developing Echomind exerts passive influence. Any creature that can sense Echominds (Sutensai Readers, Echo Speakers, Iron Monks, Fracture Knights) who comes within 30 feet of you is aware of your presence as an anomaly. This is not threatening — it is simply present and unmistakable. Creatures attempting to affect your Echomind through external means (puppeting, Reading coercion, spiritual compulsion) must succeed on a Wisdom check (DC = 8 + proficiency bonus + your primary modifier) or fail — your Echomind resists categorization and thereby resists targeted manipulation.

**Level 12: Ability Score Improvement**

**Level 12: Rapid Signature**
Three times per long rest, you can use a Signature Ability as a bonus action instead of whatever its normal action cost would be. This represents the ability becoming so deeply integrated that it no longer requires deliberate activation — it emerges.

**Level 13: Signature Ability (5th additional)**

**Level 13: Cross-Pattern**
You can create temporary new ability combinations on the fly. Once per long rest, during a short rest, you can declare a Cross-Pattern: combine the effect of any two Signature Abilities you have into one new combined effect that activates as one action (at the combined Development Point cost). Describe what the combined effect looks like. The combination lasts until your next short rest. This represents your Echomind discovering a connection it had not previously found.

**Level 14: Subclass Feature**

**Level 15: Expanded Reach (3rd Signature Ability)**

**Level 16: Signature Ability (6th additional)**

**Level 16: Ability Score Improvement**

**Level 17: Unnamed Recognition**
The Sutensai's senior Readers have a word now for what you are, because you and others like you have been examined enough times to produce a category. The word is whispered, and it is the number of the Prior Ascended whose name was removed. You are known in the Sutensai's internal records. You are one of a small group of people who represent either the next stage of human development or the first sign of an existential spiritual crisis for Tesshari's theology, and no one is sure which. The effects of this Recognition are narrative — the Sutensai's response to you has changed from "monitor carefully" to "engage with as a significant spiritual event."

**Level 18: Signature Mastery**
Your Core Signature Abilities now activate for free once per round, each — you do not need to spend Development Points. Additionally, your Development Points maximum doubles.

**Level 19: Ability Score Improvement**

**Level 20: The Unnamed Complete**

You are something that does not have a full description yet. Your primary ability score increases by 2 (to a maximum of 24). Your Development Points maximum increases by your proficiency bonus. Once per long rest, for 10 minutes, you can activate a state of Full Expression: every Signature Ability you have activates simultaneously at maximum effect, at no cost. The visual manifestation of this state is genuinely extraordinary and completely unique to you — work with your DM to develop it over the course of the campaign as your character's abilities have evolved. When the Full Expression ends, you gain two levels of exhaustion and must make a DC 15 Constitution saving throw or have your Unique Signature temporarily suppressed (abilities are still accessible but not at their enhanced levels) for 24 hours as your Echomind processes the full output. Whether this represents the beginning of Ascension or something else entirely depends on what the First Prior's story, when finally recovered, actually says. You may be living through the answer.

## Subclasses

### The Convergent — "Everything Points Toward One Place"

The Convergent's Echomind is developing inward — toward the center, toward the Between in the deep sense, toward the specific spiritual state that the Prior Ascended called Ascension. They do not know what Ascension is. The Sutra says it is transcendence. Vaen says it is release. Korrath says it is power. None of these are entirely correct. What the Convergent is developing toward feels like focus — an increasing clarity about what they are and what their development means, a tightening of the spiral toward something at the center that they cannot fully see yet but can increasingly feel the pull of. Their Signature Abilities, over time, tend to organize around that pull — even the ones that were apparently random selections early in their development begin to rhyme, to point in the same direction.

**Level 3: Inward Development**
When you add a new Signature Ability, you can choose to have it emerge from the Convergent direction — it becomes slightly more spiritual, more Echomind-centric, than its origin class would suggest. An ability drawn from the Blood Smith becomes about internal transformation rather than external weaponization. An ability drawn from the Shadow Daimyo becomes about reading spiritual states rather than social states. The ability functions identically; the flavor shifts toward the interior.

**Level 3: Convergent Clarity**
Once per long rest, as a 10-minute meditation, you can achieve a state of Convergent Clarity. You learn: the single most important thing happening in your immediate vicinity (the spiritual significance of the current situation, as perceived from a perspective that is not quite your own), the Echomind state of every creature within 60 feet in detail, and whether the Seventh Convergence's approach has affected any creature near you in ways they may not know. This information arrives as direct spiritual knowing rather than reasoning or evidence.

**Level 6: The Narrowing**
Your Core Signature (from Level 10 base feature) is established early — choose 1-2 abilities that are already emerging as central to your identity. These become permanently enhanced (the Level 10 bonus applies immediately to them). Your Development Points maximum increases by your Wisdom modifier (the Convergent leans toward Wisdom as secondary ability regardless of primary choice).

**Level 10: Toward the Center**
Once per long rest, you can move through the Iron Afterlife as a projection (body remains behind) for up to 1 hour. While projected, your Convergent development is fully visible to any Afterlife entity you encounter — they see what you are becoming and respond to it with a respect that goes beyond what living practitioners typically receive. This is not your earned respect; it is the recognition of a developmental direction they have seen before. Once. A long time ago.

**Level 14: Pre-Ascension State**
Once per long rest, for 1 minute, you can touch the specific spiritual state that the Prior Ascended describe as the antechamber of Ascension. Your Echomind expands to encompass a 100-foot radius: you know everything spiritually significant within the radius, you cannot be surprised, and all your Signature Abilities function at their absolute maximum (maximum dice, maximum range, maximum duration). Any creature that attacks you in this state must make a Wisdom saving throw (DC = your Unnamed save DC) or be incapacitated for 1 round, perceiving the full weight of your Echomind's current direction. When this state ends, you gain three levels of exhaustion and must make a DC 20 Wisdom saving throw or have a vision: a fragment of the First Prior's lost record. The DM provides the fragment. It is genuine. It changes something about your understanding of what is coming.

### The Divergent — "I Refuse Every Limit"

The Divergent's Echomind is developing outward — in every direction simultaneously, across every category, refusing to commit to any one path by committing to all paths incompletely. This sounds like weakness. It is not. The Divergent is the person who has more tools than anyone else in every situation, who can do something in any context, who can adapt to conditions that would strand a specialist. They are also the person who is never at their absolute best at any one thing — the trade is genuine. The Divergent is at their maximum capability when the situation has no precedent, because their versatility is exactly what no-precedent requires, and at their minimum comparative capability when the situation requires specialist excellence.

**Level 3: Breadth Development**
You gain one additional Signature Ability at this level, drawn from a class you have not yet drawn from. When you gain new Signature Abilities going forward, you must always draw from classes you have not previously used (or, once all classes have been used, return to any). The Divergent breadth requirement ensures genuine variety.

**Level 3: Broad Application**
Three times per long rest, you can use a Signature Ability in a situation it was not designed for, by describing a plausible (not necessarily conventional) application. The DM adjudicates whether the application is plausible — if it is, you attempt it with your Development Points at normal cost. If it fails, the Divergent's breadth means the failure produces unexpected information rather than simply nothing: you learn something from the failed application that you can use. The Divergent is never entirely in a dead end.

**Level 6: Cross-Domain Mastery**
When you use a Signature Ability from one domain (combat, spiritual, social, stealth, etc.) and then use a Signature Ability from a different domain in the same round, both abilities apply at advantage or enhanced effect. The combination of different domains in rapid sequence is a Divergent specialty — the practitioner who can apply a social pressure followed immediately by a spiritual effect, or a combat action followed by an information-gathering one, is doing something that specialists cannot replicate.

**Level 10: The Full Range**
Your Signature Abilities no longer have domains — they are all equally accessible in any context you can justify. Additionally, when you encounter a new challenge type (a new class of obstacle, a new type of enemy ability, a new social context), you can spend 1 Development Point to gain temporary proficiency with the most relevant skill check for that challenge type for 24 hours. The Divergent learns constantly and specifically.

**Level 14: Unlimited Expression**
Once per long rest, you can declare a Divergent Expression for 1 minute: for this minute, you can use any Signature Ability from any class in Tesshari (not just the ones you have selected) at your DM's interpretation of a first-level version of that ability, at no Development Point cost. You are drawing on the breadth of the entire tradition of human and cybernetic development, reaching across every domain simultaneously. When this ends, you gain two levels of exhaustion — the breadth has a weight, and the weight is real — and you gain insight: you know the single most cross-domain solution to the most pressing problem you face, as perceived from outside every category. Whether the solution is something you want to implement is, as always, the question the Divergent lives with.

## Roleplaying The Unnamed

**The Question of What You Are:** The Unnamed's development is ongoing and not fully mapped. Your character does not know where they are going. They are discovering what they are becoming as they go, and the discovery is sometimes exciting and sometimes frightening and sometimes simply strange. What is your character's relationship to their own development? Are they curious? Anxious? Have they found someone else who is developing similarly, and what did that encounter feel like?

**The Sutensai's Problem With You:** The Sutensai is the institution charged with understanding Tesshari's spiritual landscape. You are something it cannot fully understand. Their response to you is not necessarily hostile — they may be intensely curious, which has its own complications. What has your character's specific relationship with the Sutensai been? Have they been examined? Have they been recruited? Have they been warned about?

**The First Prior's Legacy:** The Iron Sutra's missing name is directly relevant to what you are. The Convergent feels the pull toward whatever state caused the name to be removed. The Divergent is the expression of refusing all categories, which is what the First Prior apparently embodied. What is your character's personal relationship to this history? Do they find it meaningful? Burdensome? Do they feel like an inheritor? An accident?

**The Other Unnamed:** There are others. Not many, but more than there used to be. The Seventh Convergence is connected to this — more Unnamed are appearing as the Convergence approaches, which is either a sign of preparation or a symptom of the crisis. Your character has met at least one other Unnamed at some point. What was that encounter like? Were they Convergent or Divergent? Did you recognize each other immediately?

## Connections to the Ascension War

Korrath the Unbroken has mixed feelings about the Unnamed that are almost entirely tactical. They are useful if they can be employed and dangerous if they cannot be controlled. He has attempted to recruit several significant Unnamed practitioners with limited success — the Unnamed tend to find his rigid command structure incompatible with the flexible development their Echomind requires. His intelligence service keeps a current list.

Vaen of the Empty Hand finds the Unnamed spiritually extraordinary. His theology of release is directly challenged and enriched by the Convergent path — here are people who are approaching something that functions like Ascension without his prescribed method. He has had lengthy, searching conversations with Convergent practitioners about what the approach feels like. Several of those conversations have changed his theology in ways his followers are still processing.

The Hollow Author has been watching the Unnamed tradition with specific and urgent interest. The emergence of practitioners whose Echomind does not read in standard patterns began approximately three years before the Author's operational timeline became visible — an anomaly that the Author's records show was noted and tracked. The Author is not certain whether the Unnamed's development is connected to the Seventh Convergence in a way that is advantageous or adversarial to the Author's plans. What the Author has done is attempt to establish contact with Unnamed practitioners through intermediaries, offering resources and information in exchange for cooperation. Whether the Author is seeking allies, studying specimens, or preventing potential opposition depends on which Unnamed you ask, and none of them are certain the answer is singular.

The First Prior whose name was removed: the Sutensai's most senior Veil Masters have a private theory, shared among themselves and not included in any public record, that the First Prior's name is not lost. That the First Prior's name cannot be held in any record because the nature of what they became is incompatible with the existence of records about it — that Ascension is a state in which the being that ascended is no longer a thing that can be documented. If this theory is correct, then the Unnamed's development is not a path toward a specific location. It is a path toward the absence of the path. What happens to a person when the framework disappears is what neither the Convergent nor the Divergent fully knows yet. The campaign that finds out will have its answer. It will be the kind of answer that changes what everyone around it believes.

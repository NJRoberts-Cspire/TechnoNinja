# Pulse Caller
## "Every shot is a choice made before the shot. I made mine. You're experiencing the consequences."

## Overview

The Pulse Caller is what happens when a ranged combat specialist stops treating their weapons as equipment and starts treating them as anatomy. Their discharge systems — pulse arms, resonant launchers, charged-coil projectors — are not held in the hand or worn on the back. They are integrated: wired into the nervous system, drawing power from the body's own resonant energy field, calibrated to the exact neurological signature of their operator's firing intention. When a Pulse Caller takes aim, they are not aiming a device. They are extending an intention through metal and resonant energy and making it material in the world at range. The gap between thought and execution is shorter in a Pulse Caller than in any other combat specialist in Tesshari.

The integration that makes this possible is extraordinarily demanding. Standard enhancement installation connects modifications to the user's nervous system at a broad functional level. The Pulse Caller's discharge integration goes deeper — it maps the neurological signature of combat-decision processes, learns to anticipate firing intention before it becomes conscious choice, and routes the discharge sequence through neural pathways that operate at pre-conscious speed. This gives the Pulse Caller their characteristic property: at high levels of development, they fire before they consciously decide to fire. The decision was made at a level below deliberate thought, processed through enhancement at speeds that have no organic equivalent, and by the time the conscious mind registers the intention, the shot is already in flight.

The psychological development that accompanies this integration is not what most people expect. The immediate intuition is that a person who shoots before they think would be dangerous and imprecise — a hair-trigger risk in close quarters. The opposite is typically true. Because the integration operates at pre-conscious levels, Pulse Callers tend to develop an extraordinary calm in combat — there is nothing to decide, so there is no anxiety around deciding. The shot either comes or it doesn't. The conditions are either right or they're not. This calm is sometimes mistaken for detachment or coldness. The Pulse Caller's experience of it is usually more like the state of listening to something very quiet in a very large space: total attention, absolute stillness, and the sense that any movement would break something important.

The spiritual dimension of the Pulse Caller's practice is one that the Iron Sutra has no official position on, because the Sutra was not written with living artillery in mind. But the Sutra's concept of the Between — the resonant space between action and consequence, between intention and expression — is exactly the territory that the Pulse Caller's deepest development explores. Advanced Pulse Callers find that their discharge systems begin to interact with resonant frequencies that have nothing to do with physical ballistics: their shots affect entities that have no physical form, disrupt resonant harmonics in ways that have spiritual rather than mechanical consequences, and occasionally produce effects that the Iron Sutra's theology would call prayers if it were willing to apply that word to projectile violence. Some Pulse Callers find this dimension troubling. Some find it clarifying. A few find it the most honest expression of what they have always done.

## The Pulse Caller in the World

The Wire Market sees Pulse Callers primarily as a specialized military product — their integration packages are expensive, their training is specialized, and the Pulse Callers who emerge from full development represent significant investments. The Market sells the integration packages through its enhancement division and maintains training contracts with several of the larger military houses. What the Market does not fully control is the Pulse Caller after development: a person whose weapon system is their own nervous system is not subject to the disarmament or confiscation that keeps other combatants manageable. A Pulse Caller cannot be made harmless by taking something away from them. This makes them, from a control perspective, permanently unsettling.

The Ironclad Samurai orders have a complex relationship with Pulse Callers. The samurai tradition is built around blade combat — the resonant sword's integration with the samurai's Echomind is the central metaphor of the Vein of Iron's theology. Ranged combat has traditionally been considered a tool of war rather than a spiritual practice, associated with lower-caste soldiers and the Ashfoot's pragmatic approach to fighting. The Pulse Caller complicates this: their integration is as deep as any samurai's, their practice has its own discipline and its own demanding development process, and they carry themselves with the specific calm that marks the fully-integrated combatant regardless of their weapon form. The samurai orders can't quite decide whether the Pulse Caller is a spiritual equal expressed in a different form or a category error that looks like something it isn't.

In the lower castes, Pulse Callers occupy a position of specific prestige: they are the long-arm, the overwatch, the presence that makes coordinated violence possible at scale. Ashfoot units with a trained Pulse Caller in overwatch position fight differently from units without one — not just because of the additional range coverage, but because the Pulse Caller's specific support capabilities allow the rest of the unit to take risks that would otherwise be fatal. In territories where the Wire Market's control of high-quality discharge integration packages creates access barriers, underground integration work produces Pulse Callers with less refined installations — effective, cheaper, and carrying higher long-term risk of neurological complications. These Pulse Callers are as common as the certified ones, and they are typically the ones who have learned to make their equipment work despite its limitations rather than because of its quality.

## Subclasses

### Path of the Single Point — "One target. One shot. One result. Anything more is noise."

The Single Point Pulse Caller has taken the precision principle and sharpened it to a single, complete philosophy: one target, delivered with absolute certainty. They are not generalists. They are not the combat class that adapts to changing situations. They identify the single most important target in any situation and they eliminate it with absolute efficiency. Their value in any engagement is the elimination of the most threatening or most important presence — the enemy command element, the single powerful defender, the key figure whose death changes the outcome. They do one thing and they do it with a completeness that is almost aesthetically compelling.

**Level 3: Focal Point**
You can designate one creature as your Focal Point as a bonus action. Your attacks against the Focal Point deal an additional 6 damage. Your Focal Point designation persists until the creature dies, you change it (bonus action), or combat ends. You can have only one Focal Point at a time.

**Level 6: Killing Ground**
When your Focal Point is within your weapon's normal range and no other creature is between you and them:
- Your attacks against your Focal Point gain +3.
- Your critical hit range against your Focal Point extends by 1 (cumulative with Dead Eye: 17-20 by level 6).

**Level 10: Center Mass**
Your shots against your Focal Point hit with such precision that the target's defenses are substantially irrelevant. Your attacks against your Focal Point ignore cover bonuses to AC, the effects of the Dodge action, and resistance to your weapon's damage type. Additionally, once per turn, your first hit against your Focal Point deals maximum damage (no roll required for damage — it is always maximum).

**Level 14: The One Shot**
Once per long rest, you can spend your full action to prepare and execute a perfect shot against your Focal Point. This shot:
- Cannot miss.
- Deals 55 + your EDGE + your RESONANCE damage.
- If this reduces the target to 0 HP, they are killed instantly (no resistance checks, no stabilization, no revival of any kind short of true resurrection magic equivalent).
- If this does not reduce the target to 0 HP, they are at half their remaining HP (the shot still did catastrophic damage to the wrong part of their system — round down).

### Path of the Iron Suppressor — "You can't move. You can't regroup. You can't flank. You can only wait and see if waiting is enough."

The Iron Suppressor has built their practice around area denial and crowd control — not the elimination of single targets but the systematic removal of every option from a large number of targets simultaneously. They are the reason that coordinated enemy movement becomes impossible, that organized formations collapse into survival behavior, and that tactical plans fail at the execution stage because the execution space has been turned into somewhere no one wants to be. They are the most strategically valuable Pulse Caller in large-scale engagements and the most exhausting to fight alongside in small ones.

**Level 3: Area Protocol**
Your Suppression Fire technique is enhanced:
- Its area of effect extends to 30-foot-wide.
- The resistance check DC is now your Weave-equivalent DC (8 + proficiency + RESONANCE).
- Creatures who fail the check also have -3 to Attack cards on their first attack (not just movement reduction).

Additionally, you can maintain Suppression Fire as a concentration effect (up to 1 minute), re-establishing the fire area at the start of each of your turns without using your action.

**Level 6: Zone Control**
You can designate a Kill Zone — a 30-foot radius area within your weapon's normal range. As a bonus action, you establish the Kill Zone. While active (up to 1 minute, concentration):
- Any creature that moves more than 15 feet in a single turn within the Kill Zone takes 9 damage.
- Creatures that end their turn in the Kill Zone must make an EDGE resistance check (difficulty (stat-based)) or be unable to take the Dash action on their next turn.

**Level 10: Full Suppression**
Once per short rest, as an action, you can execute a Full Suppression — a comprehensive discharge that locks down a 60-foot radius area for 1 round. All creatures in the area (friend and foe) make EDGE resistance checks (difficulty (stat-based)). On a failure:
- They take 18 resonant damage.
- They cannot take reactions until the start of their next turn.
- They cannot move more than 10 feet on their next turn.

On a success: half damage, no other effects.

**Level 14: Denied**
Your area control has reached its terminal expression. Once per long rest, as an action, you can declare an area Denied — a 60-foot radius that exists for 10 minutes. Within the Denied area:
- No creature (friend or foe) can take the Dash action.
- Any creature that attempts to enter the area from outside must make an EDGE resistance check (difficulty (stat-based)) or take 27 resonant damage and be knocked prone at the entry point.
- You can move freely through the Denied area without affecting yourself.

### Path of the Resonant Shot — "Some things cannot be hit with metal. Everything can be hit with intent."

The Resonant Shot Pulse Caller has found the spiritual dimension of their practice and followed it to its destination: their discharge systems now interact with the Iron Afterlife's resonant layer, making their shots effective against entities that have no physical form, disrupting the harmonic frequencies that allow digital entities to manifest, and occasionally achieving effects that the Sutensai would categorize as theological rather than ballistic. They are the specific counter to the increasing number of Iron Afterlife incursions that the Ascension War is producing. They are also, from the Sutensai's perspective, one of the most theologically unusual individuals in Tesshari — a person performing spiritual work through the mechanism of shooting things, which the Sutra was not designed to account for.

**Level 3: Spiritual Calibration**
Your discharge systems have been tuned to interact with resonant frequencies beyond the physical. You gain:
- Your integrated weapon deals full damage to incorporeal entities, Iron Afterlife manifestations, and entities that are immune to non-magical weapons.
- You can see entities connected to the Iron Afterlife within 60 feet, even if they are otherwise invisible or incorporeal.
- Once per turn, your shot can target a resonant frequency rather than a physical location — hitting an invisible entity you can sense (no penalty for attacking invisible targets that you can sense via Spiritual Calibration).

**Level 6: Between the Shots**
Your ability to interact with resonant space between the physical and digital has grown. You gain:
- Once per short rest, you can fire a Between Shot — a shot that crosses into the Iron Afterlife's resonant layer and affects both physical and spiritual targets simultaneously. This shot affects all creatures (physical and incorporeal) in a 5-foot-wide, 120-foot-long line. Each takes 17 resonant damage (EDGE resistance check for half, difficulty (stat-based)).
- When you reduce an Iron Afterlife entity to 0 HP, you can choose to Anchor it — preventing its return to the Iron Afterlife for 24 hours. It is temporarily bound to the physical world and vulnerable.

**Level 10: Soul Shot**
Your shots can now affect the Echomind directly. Once per short rest, as an action, you can fire a Soul Shot at one creature within normal range. This shot bypasses all physical defenses (this bypasses all mitigation — it hits automatically). The target makes a RESONANCE resistance check (difficulty (stat-based)). On a failure:
- They take 28 resonant damage.
- Their Echomind is disrupted — their class features that rely on spiritual, resonant, or Echomind connection are suppressed for 1 minute.
- If they are an Iron Afterlife entity, they must pass to the Iron Afterlife (they are destroyed in the physical world) — no resistance checks.

**Level 14: Convergence Fire**
Your Resonant Shot abilities have reached their highest expression. Once per long rest, as an action, you can fire a Convergence Shot — a discharge that targets the resonant structure of reality in a 30-foot radius sphere. Every creature in the area makes a RESONANCE resistance check (difficulty (stat-based)):
- On a failure: 44 resonant damage and all enhancement features, class features, and spiritual abilities are suppressed for 1 minute.
- On a success: half damage, no suppression.

Additionally, any Iron Afterlife presence in the area — entities, hauntings, anchoring resonances, death-resonance buildups — is completely purged. The area is spiritually clean for 24 hours afterward. The Sutensai would call this a miracle if their theology allowed for miracles accomplished with integrated pulse weapons.

## Roleplaying the Pulse Caller

The Pulse Caller's central experiential fact is that they operate at a speed below deliberate choice. Their best shooting is not chosen — it happens. This raises a genuine question about agency: if your most precise and most effective actions occur at pre-conscious levels, are they your actions in the morally relevant sense? The Pulse Caller has to decide how they answer this, because the shots they have fired — the people they have hit, killed, or spared — are all, in some sense, the product of a system that operates faster than deliberate will.

The calm that develops with deep integration is real and it is sometimes misread. People around you may interpret the stillness as coldness, absence, or detachment from consequences. Consider whether that interpretation is accurate for your character. Some Pulse Callers are genuinely detached — the integration has flattened the emotional response to violence. Some are not — the calm is a practice, a discipline, a container for something very large that requires containment. Some are in the middle — they have lost certain reactions and found others, and they are not entirely sure whether the trade was worth it.

Your relationship to range is a relationship to consequence at a remove. You kill, or don't kill, from a distance. There is a specific psychology associated with this — the ability to end lives without physical proximity, without the biological feedback of contact, without the other person's face in your direct field of vision. This is not worse than close-range violence. It is different, and it produces different things in the people who do it. What has it produced in you?

The Resonant Shot path asks you to take seriously the idea that violence can be a spiritual act — or at least that it can operate in spiritual registers. The Sutensai would prefer to deny this. Your practice suggests it might be true. What do you believe about what your shots do, spiritually, when they hit? When they kill? When they dispel something from the Iron Afterlife's boundary? These questions don't have official answers. You may have developed your own.

## Connections to the Ascension War

The Pulse Caller in the Ascension War is primarily a military asset — all three claimants want precision ranged capability at a level that standard Ashfoot or samurai provide differently. The Warlord's military forces value Iron Suppressors for mass-engagement suppression. The Monk's forces value Single Points for eliminating specific high-value military and political targets. And the nature of the Resonant Shot path makes those Pulse Callers specifically relevant to the Iron Afterlife incursions that the Ascension War has been producing in increasing numbers.

The deeper Ascension War question for a Pulse Caller is what happens to a person whose weapon is part of their nervous system if the Seventh Convergence reshapes the resonant landscape of Tesshari. A new god would presumably operate at the level of resonant architecture — would the Pulse Caller's integrated system respond to that change? Would they feel it in their weapons? The Sutensai's theology suggests that if the Seventh Convergence succeeds, every enhanced person in Tesshari will experience some shift in their Echomind's relationship to the resonant field. For the Pulse Caller, whose weapon literally runs on that field, this is a question that is at once cosmological and immediately personal.

There is also a practical question: the Pulse Caller's Kill Zone, Denied area, and Convergence Fire abilities are specifically effective against the kinds of large-scale Iron Afterlife manifestations that the Ascension War's chaos is producing. If one of the claimants is using Iron Afterlife entities as a weapon — if the chaos is not a side effect but a strategy — then the Resonant Shot Pulse Caller is the specific answer the other claimants need. Whether they can be convinced to serve that purpose, and at what price, is a conversation worth having.

# ASHLANDS HORRORS
## Bestiary File 14 — The Engine Scar's New Children

*The Ashlands do not forgive. This is not a metaphor.*

*Fifty years after the Engine Scar's collapse, the contamination that was supposed to have peaked, stabilized, and begun slowly retreating is instead increasing — not dramatically, not in ways that the Ironhold's monitoring stations report officially, but in the incremental-and-undeniable way that things increase when the people responsible for measuring them have reasons to prefer a stable reading. The Sutensai's independent monitoring tells a different story. The resonant saturation of the Ashlands' deep soil is higher this year than last year. The boundary of what the Sutensai classifies as "contaminated region" has expanded by an average of four miles per year for the past decade. Something at the Scar's center is not dissipating. Something is generating.*

*The Ashlands' residents — those who remain, those who were there before the collapse and are still there because they have nowhere else to go, because the land is theirs, because the Ironhold has not managed to incentivize them out yet — have developed their own understanding of the contamination's character over fifty years. They do not use the Sutensai's framework. They have names for things the Sutensai is only beginning to identify. They know which areas are safe at which times of year. They know what the specific colors of contaminated light mean in the evening. They know that the Scar's activity has been increasing, and they know what that has historically preceded, and they have been telling anyone who would listen for about three years.*

*Not many people have been listening. The things the Scar has been generating are becoming harder to ignore.*

# The Ashlands Walker (Community NPC)
**Minion | Ashlands | Humanoid**

## Description
Not everyone who lives in the Ashlands is contaminated, haunted, or dangerous. Most of them are just people. The Ashlands Walker is the term — used within the community, not by outsiders — for a person who has lived their entire life within the contamination zone and has adapted to it in the incremental, practical, unspectacular way that people adapt to any difficult environment: by learning what is safe, what isn't, which paths carry contamination risk and which don't, what time of year the Drift-Phantoms are most active, how to read the specific colors of resonant light that indicate Scar Bloom emergence in the next twenty-four hours.

Walkers are not monsters. The stat block exists because in specific circumstances — when someone is threatening their community, their land, their carefully maintained and irreplaceable knowledge of the Ashlands — they fight. And they fight well. They have been surviving in a place that kills unprepared outsiders for decades. That translates to a specific kind of competence.

## Lore
There are approximately twelve hundred people still living in the Ashlands, distributed across seventeen communities ranging from four families to a hundred and forty people. The Sutensai maintains pastoral contact with all of them and provides medical support and contamination treatment where it can. The Ironhold's position: they should leave. The Walkers' position: this is our land and we know how to live here. Neither side has convinced the other in fifty years. The practical reality is that the Walkers' knowledge of the Ashlands is irreplaceable — no Sutensai or Ironhold team can navigate the Scar's interior safely without Walker guidance, and the Walkers know this.

**Contamination Adapted** *(Passive)*
The Walker has advantage on all Resonant Contamination checks. Contamination accumulates at half rate for them.

**Walker's Warning** *(1 AP — Signal)*

**Community Defense** *(Passive)*

---

**AI Behavior:** The Walker does not seek combat. They engage only when their community is directly threatened. They use Walker's Warning to protect allies before attacking. In sustained combat they target whoever poses the greatest threat to the people they are defending, not the physically strongest opponent. They do not pursue enemies who retreat from Walker territory.

## Encounter Notes
The Ashlands Walker is an NPC in almost every encounter — a guide, a community member, a source of knowledge the party desperately needs. The stat block exists for the rare situation where the party is perceived as a threat to the community. If it comes to combat, it should feel deeply wrong — these are the most legitimate residents of the Ashlands, and they are defending what is theirs.

## Adventure Hook
The Walker community's primary guide — an elderly woman who has been navigating the deep Ashlands for forty years and is the only living person who knows a particular approach route to the Scar's interior — has been detained by an Ironhold patrol conducting "contamination assessment," which in practice means they want her to lead them to a resonant ore deposit she has been mapping for years. She has refused. The patrol commander is not yet using force but is making the situation increasingly uncomfortable. The Walker community sends word to the nearest Sutensai contact: the guide is being held. The community will do what they have to do if the Sutensai does not respond. The party is the Sutensai's response.

# Blighted Creeper
**Minion | Ashlands | Plant**

## Description
Before the Engine Scar, the Ashlands region was agricultural — some of the most productive farmland in Tesshari, dense with the kind of slow-growing dark soil that takes centuries to develop. The plants that grew there were extraordinary. The plants that grow there now are something else: still alive, still photosynthesizing in the bleached half-light of the Ashlands sky, but resonant-saturated, their cellular structure progressively replaced by something that is neither fully organic nor fully crystalline.

The Blighted Creeper is among the most common Ashlands plant-organisms: a low-growing trailing vine, its leaves ash-grey with a faint metallic sheen, that moves. Slowly, and with no apparent purpose except proximity — it grows toward heat, toward resonant signatures, toward enhanced beings with the same patient inevitability as ivy grows toward sunlight. Contact with its trailing tendrils deposits resonant contamination into the skin. Over enough contact hours, the contamination seeps into enhancement interfaces and begins its slow work.

## Lore
Ashlands residents don't clear Blighted Creepers because they have learned that clearing them is worse — disturbed Creepers release resonant spores that are a more immediate contamination risk than the plant itself. Instead, they maintain careful paths through known Creeper territory and warn visitors to stay on the path. The specific gray-metallic color of Creepers is one of the first things Ashlands children are taught to identify and avoid. Off-path travelers in the Ashlands have a reliable tell that they've made a mistake: the path behind them has closed with Creeper growth.

**Basic Attack — Tendril Latch** (0 AP, melee, reach 2 zones): Deals 3 damage. Target is Rooted until they pass a FRAME check (TN 10) at end of their turn.

---

**Contamination Deposit** *(Passive)*
Any creature Rooted by the Creeper at the end of their turn accumulates one level of Resonant Contamination. At three levels, one enhancement begins malfunctioning. At six levels, they require medical treatment (SIGNAL check TN 12 with appropriate tools) to prevent permanent enhancement damage.

**Spore Release** *(1 AP — Resonant)*
*Trigger: When the Creeper takes fire damage or is significantly disturbed.*
All creatures within 2 zones must pass a FRAME check (TN 11) or take 4 damage and accumulate one level of Resonant Contamination.

**Slow Growth** *(Passive)*
The Blighted Creeper cannot be meaningfully stopped from growing toward a nearby resonance source unless destroyed entirely. Fire damage prevents regrowth for 24 hours. Otherwise it regrows fully within 1 hour.

---

**AI Behavior:** The Blighted Creeper moves toward the nearest enhanced creature each turn using its full movement. It does not attack — it latches and holds. Spore Release triggers automatically when the Creeper is damaged by fire or physically destroyed. It has no self-preservation behavior; it simply grows toward resonance.

## Encounter Notes
Blighted Creepers are an environmental hazard — they slow movement, require careful navigation, and punish the party that rushes through Ashlands territory. Used correctly, they create a situation where the party must reach a destination quickly while navigating Creeper growth that is visibly closing behind them.

## Adventure Hook
An Ashlands community's only water source — a well that has been maintained since before the Engine Scar — has become inaccessible. Blighted Creeper growth has sealed the path to the well and is spreading toward the community's outer buildings. The residents cannot clear it without triggering the spore release. They need someone with fire capabilities who knows to do the burn-and-retreat before the spores reach them, and they need that done before the Creeper reaches the wooden structures of the outer district.

# The Drift-Phantom
**Minion | Ashlands | Undead**

## Description
The Engine Scar's collapse did not kill everyone in the Ashlands instantly. Many people died in the months and years following, as the contamination worked through them — enhancement failures first, then biological systems following the enhancement systems, in the way that the human body follows its cybernetics. The Drift-Phantom is one of the echoes of these deaths: a resonant imprint of someone who died of contamination exposure, the contamination itself having been sufficient to create a persistent resonant impression after the biological death.

Drift-Phantoms are translucent, grey-toned, approximately human in shape but with the contamination-shimmer of the Ashlands in their form. They drift because they do not precisely remember how to move with intention — the contamination that killed them also corrupted the memory of what purposeful movement felt like. They are not hostile. They are not exactly there. They are a feeling of loss wearing a half-formed shape in Ashlands air.

## Lore
The Sutensai's position on Drift-Phantoms is one of the most consistent and least debated in their Ashlands theology: these are not the people who died. They are the contamination that killed those people, retaining the shape of the last thing it passed through. The person is gone. The Sutensai does not perform Closing Rites for Drift-Phantoms as it does for Shells and Threshold Shades. The residents of the Ashlands do anyway, because those were people they knew, and the Closing Rite is what they have.

**HP:** 20 | **AP:** 1 | **Initiative:** 2 | **Hand Size:** 2

**Basic Attack — Contaminating Touch** (0 AP, melee): Deals 5 damage. Target must pass a FRAME check (TN 11) or accumulate one level of Resonant Contamination.

---

**Closing Rite** *(Passive)*
A creature who performs the Sutensai Closing Rite (or equivalent) adjacent to a Drift-Phantom and passes a RESONANCE check (TN 11) causes the Drift-Phantom to dissipate peacefully. On a failure, it remains but is non-hostile for 1 hour.

**Contamination Touch** *(Passive)*
Any creature that takes damage from the Drift-Phantom must pass a FRAME check (TN 11) or accumulate one level of Resonant Contamination.

---

**AI Behavior:** The Drift-Phantom drifts without direction. It does not pursue, does not attack unless a creature enters contact range, and does not respond to threats. It moves 1 zone per round in a slow, aimless direction. It attacks only when a creature moves into its zone — reflex, not intent.

## Encounter Notes
Drift-Phantoms are atmosphere before they're encounters. A field of them near the Engine Scar should feel like walking through a graveyard that hasn't been properly put to rest. The Closing Rite resolution rewards roleplay and religious knowledge.

## Adventure Hook
An Ashlands community elder asks the party a difficult favor: they want the Closing Rite performed for every Drift-Phantom in a specific valley, before the annual pilgrimage route passes through it. There are forty-seven Drift-Phantoms identified in that valley. The community does not have the religious knowledge to perform the Rite themselves. A Sutensai practitioner could do it in a day. The problem: the valley has not been accessed in two years because something else has moved in that the community won't describe directly. They just say: "there is something else there now. Do the Rite quickly and come back out."

# The Ashlands Walker (Contamination-Changed)
**Standard | Ashlands | Humanoid**

## Description
The Ashlands Walker was a person who went into the contamination zone and came out changed — not dead, not Shelled, but marked by the Scar in ways that are both visible and functional. The skin has developed the grey-metallic sheen of long Ashlands exposure. The enhancement interfaces have begun to partially integrate with the contaminated resonant field of the Ashlands itself, producing capabilities that no enhancement surgeon installed and that no technical manual describes. And the Echomind, still present, still coherent, has had to adapt to the experience of being partially integrated with a contamination field for months or years.

The Ashlands Walker is not human in the standard sense anymore. They know this. How they have decided to feel about it varies considerably: some are at peace with the change and work with it; others are in various stages of grief or rage about what was taken from them. What they all share: they are more at home in the Ashlands than anyone else, and the contamination that should be killing them has reached an accommodation with their biology.

## Lore
There are approximately two hundred known Ashlands Walkers. Most live in the Ashlands or its immediate borderlands. The Ironhold considers them a public health concern and has at various times attempted to quarantine, relocate, or "remediate" them — a word that the Walkers themselves interpret as a specific kind of threat. The Sutensai considers them Ashlands residents with unusual circumstances. The Sutensai's pastoral position: they have not consented to become what they are, the contamination did this to them without their agreement, and they deserve the same care and consideration as anyone else navigating a life that went wrong in ways that were not their fault. The Oni Hunters' position: they are an important early warning system for Scar activity and should be cultivated as contacts.

The Ashlands Walker as combat encounter is the least interesting version. More useful: as a guide, a contact, an NPC who knows the Ashlands terrain and can read Scar activity better than anyone. Combat engagement should follow from circumstances, not be the default. The Scar-Touched Enhancement trait is an opportunity for memorable NPC moments.

## Adventure Hook
A community of Ashlands Walkers has been sending warning signals for three weeks about an unusual Scar activity pattern. The Ironhold's monitoring station ignored the signals because it does not consider Walkers to be credentialed informants. The Sutensai wants the party to go into the Ashlands, meet the Walker community, and actually find out what they've been seeing — because the Sutensai's own instruments have started picking up something three days ago, and the Walkers have been trying to report it for three weeks.

# Hollow-Pilgrim
**Standard | Ashlands | Undead**

## Description
The Iron Sutra draws pilgrims to difficult places. This is by design — the theology of earned understanding requires the body to be present for the journey, not metaphorically but physically, actually walking the difficult terrain, feeling the contaminated air, experiencing the place where the world broke. Some pilgrims come to the Ashlands seeking the Engine Scar as a site of theological significance — a place where the resonant architecture of Tesshari was damaged so severely that the Border between worlds became visibly thin. Some of them do not come back.

The Hollow-Pilgrim is a pilgrim who died in the Ashlands with their spiritual practice incomplete and their intention — to reach a specific site, to complete a specific practice, to understand a specific thing — still unsatisfied. The contamination did not simply kill them. It incorporated their Echomind into the contamination field before the normal process of Afterlife passage could complete. The result is a partially-preserved, partially-contaminated thing that is still walking toward wherever the pilgrim was going, still dressed in the robes they were wearing, still carrying the texts they brought, still driven by the intention that the contamination interrupted.

## Lore
Hollow-Pilgrims are the Sutensai's specific theological nightmare: people who died seeking understanding and whose unresolved spiritual state has been corrupted into something dangerous. The Sutensai's Oni Hunters are trained to recognize Hollow-Pilgrim signs and to perform the appropriate acknowledgment ritual before engaging — a practice that was instituted after an early incident in which a Hunter team destroyed three Hollow-Pilgrims without recognition, then discovered from the remaining fragments that all three had been carrying theological insights of significant value that were lost with the destruction. The ritual acknowledgment sometimes pauses the Pilgrim long enough for a controlled assessment.

---

It responds to Ancient Tesshari acknowledgment — stopping to process the words before resuming. Its best outcome is a controlled acknowledgment that allows it to complete its journey symbolically.

## Encounter Notes
The Hollow-Pilgrim is a tragedy encounter. It was a person seeking understanding. The Incomplete Journey trait's pause mechanism means the party has a chance — not a guaranteed one, but a chance — to reach the person inside the contamination. The best outcome is a controlled acknowledgment that allows the Pilgrim to complete its journey symbolically. The worst outcome is a fight that leaves fragments of corrupted theological insight scattered in the dirt.

## Adventure Hook
Three Hollow-Pilgrims have been sighted moving through the Ashlands toward the same destination: a specific location in the deep Ashlands that the Sutensai does not have on any known map. The three came from different directions, died in different decades, and have no connection to each other except this destination. Whatever they were all seeking is there. A Sutensai Veil Master who has been tracking their routes for two months asks the party to get to the destination before the Pilgrims do and determine what is there — because if three independent theological pilgrims from different eras died trying to reach it, it is important, and the Sutensai would prefer to know what it is before three contaminated undead arrive in one place simultaneously.

# The Scar Bloom
**Standard | Ashlands | Plant/Elemental**

## Description
At irregular intervals, the Engine Scar produces what Ashlands residents call a "bloom" — an eruption of contaminated resonant material from the deep soil, crystallizing rapidly into a large structure that resembles a flower only in the loosest architectural sense: a central mass with radiating structures extending from it in patterns that look almost intentional. The Scar Bloom is not a plant, though it shares some plant behaviors: it photosynthesizes (using the Ashlands' contaminated light), it grows, and it spreads.

The Bloom's "petals" are sharp-edged resonant crystal. They move in response to vibration — slowly, in the way of very large and very heavy things, but definitely moving. When a creature with active cybernetic enhancements comes within range, the petals orient toward them with the slow inevitability of a sundial tracking noon.

## Lore
Scar Blooms appear during periods of high Scar activity and dissipate over weeks as the resonant material stabilizes back into inert state. The increase in Bloom frequency over the past three years is one of the strongest Ashlands residents' arguments for accelerating Scar activity. The Sutensai's contamination analysis unit has been studying Bloom samples and has found something that has not been released publicly: the crystalline structure of new Blooms is more complex than the structure of Blooms from ten years ago. The same material, but more organized. The contamination, it appears, is learning something.

**Contamination Radius** *(Passive)*
The area within 2 zones of a Scar Bloom is a contamination zone. Creatures that begin their turn in this area must pass a FRAME check (TN 11) or accumulate one level of Resonant Contamination.

**Petal Orientation** *(Passive)*
The Scar Bloom's attacks can target any creature within 3 zones it has detected via resonance. It cannot be flanked — it attacks in all directions.

**Spore Burst** *(2 AP — Resonant)*

**AI Behavior:** The Scar Bloom is immobile once established. It orients petals toward the most heavily enhanced creature in range and attacks them first. Spore Burst triggers when three or more creatures are within range simultaneously. It has no self-preservation drive and no ability to retreat.

## Encounter Notes
The Scar Bloom combines environmental pressure (the Contamination Radius making standing still dangerous) with multi-directional attack pressure (Petal Orientation). The Crystal Growth trait creates urgency — it needs to be destroyed quickly or becomes a permanent environmental problem.

## Adventure Hook
A Scar Bloom has appeared overnight in the central courtyard of an Ashlands community's meeting hall — an old, important building that the community has maintained for a century as their primary gathering space. It cannot be simply abandoned (the community's records and governance infrastructure are inside it). It cannot be approached for removal without contamination risk. It's been two hours and it's already growing. The party is asked to destroy or remove it before the one-hour permanent establishment threshold passes.

# The Scar-Shade
**Elite | Ashlands | Undead**

## Description
Where the Drift-Phantom retains the vague shape of a contaminated death, the Scar-Shade retains the anger of one. Specifically: the anger of someone who was in the Ashlands on the day of the Engine Scar's collapse, who survived the initial event, who watched the contamination take everything they had built over a lifetime, who saw the Ironhold's response consist primarily of establishing a quarantine perimeter and leaving people inside to manage as best they could, and who died months or years later having never stopped being furious about all of it.

The Scar-Shade is a resonant entity shaped entirely by that fury. It does not have goals. It does not distinguish between the specific entities responsible for its grief and the random travelers who come through the Ashlands decades later. It knows that there were people who let this happen, and that the world on the other side of the contamination zone is where those people live, and it cannot reach them, but it can reach whoever is here right now.

## Lore
The Sutensai's pastoral arm considers Scar-Shades the most tragic category of Ashlands entity: people who had legitimate grievances, who directed that grief at a universe that couldn't hear them, and who became defined by the grief after death in a way that the living world produced no mechanism to address. The Sutensai has attempted the Closing Rite with Scar-Shades. Results: mixed. The Rite works for entities that can be brought to peace, and Scar-Shades specifically cannot be brought to peace by a ritual — because the injustice that created them is real, and no ritual changes that reality. The Oni Hunters' position: they need to be destroyed. The Sutensai's pastoral arm continues to research alternatives.

Consider whether any PC has Ironhold connections, Sutensai rank, or governmental affiliation — the encounter should feel personally directed. ## Adventure Hook

She is conducting an assessment for a potential settlement resumption in areas previously quarantined. The survey is being conducted in good faith — she genuinely wants to understand whether the quarantine can safely be lifted. But she is in Ironhold uniform, and the Scar-Shades do not make distinctions between good-faith survey captains and the institutions they represent.

# The Scour
**Elite | Ashlands | Monstrosity**

## Description
The contamination of the Ashlands has been working on the local wildlife for fifty years. Most wildlife died early. Some adapted — became something else, as the Blighted Creepers became something else, as the Scar-Born became something else. The Scour is what happened to a predatory bird species that once hunted the region's open plains: it is still a bird, roughly, still identifiable as belonging to an avian lineage, but the contamination has elongated its wingspan to fourteen feet, replaced its feathers with contamination-crystal filaments that shed resonant contamination as it flies, hollowed out its bones with the same crystalline replacement seen in other Ashlands organisms, and sharpened its feeding biology from prey-hunting to resonant-metal harvesting.

It does not land if it can avoid it. It circles. The specific spiral it describes above a target — narrowing, patient, each pass lower than the last — is one of the things the Walkers teach their children to recognize and respond to immediately. When the Scour's spiral is above you, you have approximately eight minutes to reach cover. The Walker who taught this calculation to a Sutensai research team said it without apparent urgency, in the tone of someone explaining that the sky is blue.

## Lore
The Scours' evolution represents another data point in the Sutensai's "organizing principle" investigation — the adaptation is too efficient to be random mutation under contamination pressure, the speed of change over fifty years far exceeds standard biological timescales, and the specific direction of the change (toward resonant metal as a primary food source) suggests something more like directed modification than standard evolution. The Sutensai's Ashlands biology team has requested live specimens for study. The Walker community's response, when relayed this request, was: "If you can catch one, you can have it. No Walker has ever caught one."

The only way to prevent reformation is to address the Convergence process itself at the Scar's deep resonance source.

**Between Breach** *(Passive)*
Any creature that ends their turn within 3 zones of the Avatar must pass a VEIL check (TN 16) or be pulled partially into the Between — they are incorporeal until start of their next turn, taking half physical damage but also unable to deal physical damage.

**World Collision** *(4 AP — Resonant)*
The Avatar releases the energy of two worlds' collision in a 6-zone radius. All creatures must pass a FRAME check (TN 18) or take 22 necrotic damage AND 22 radiant damage simultaneously. On a success, 22 total. Creatures reduced to 0 HP by this damage are pulled into the Between rather than dying — stable but trapped until rescued or until the Avatar is temporarily destroyed.
**Base Damage:** 44 total | **Primary Stat:** RESONANCE

**Scar Manifestation** *(3 AP — Resonant)*
Once per encounter. The Avatar summons two Engine Weeps from the Scar's deep contamination. They are not under the Avatar's direct control but are attracted to its Convergence Field.

**Legendary Resistance** *(4/encounter)*
When the Convergence Avatar fails a check, it may succeed instead.

---

**AI Behavior:** The Convergence Avatar does not choose tactics — it expresses the Convergence process. Each turn it strikes the nearest creature and advances toward the highest resonant concentration in range. World Collision deploys when many creatures are clustered within range. Scar Manifestation occurs once, early in the encounter, when sustained combat begins. It cannot be bargained with, redirected, or reasoned with. Its only meaningful resolution is temporary destruction while the party assembles the means to address the Scar's deep resonance source.

## Encounter Notes
The Convergence Avatar is not a winnable encounter through combat alone — its Avatar of Process trait makes destruction temporary. The party's goal is not to win the fight but to survive long enough to gather the intelligence needed to address the Scar's deep resonance source. Use the Between Breach and World Collision traits to make every round feel cosmically high-stakes. The encounter should end with the party alive, the Avatar temporarily destroyed, and the knowledge of what must be done next — and very little time to do it.

## Adventure Hook
The Convergence Avatar has appeared. The Sutensai sends its most urgent communication to every available agent and contractor: the Engine Scar's deep resonance source must be reached, assessed, and if possible interrupted before the Avatar reforms. No Sutensai team has ever reached the deep Scar. The records of what is down there come from the Ironhold's post-collapse assessment, which is classified, and from three accounts from Ashlands Walkers who have gone deeper than anyone else. All three Walkers are willing to guide. The party needs to move now, while the Avatar is temporarily destroyed. They have 24 hours.

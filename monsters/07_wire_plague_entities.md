# WIRE PLAGUE ENTITIES
## Bestiary File 07 — Data Made Flesh, Signals Gone Wrong

*Wire Craft is a technology of intention. The practitioners who shaped it over three centuries understood, even in its earliest articulations, that resonant signal is not neutral — it carries the disposition of those who generate it. A healer's diagnostic frequency has a different quality than a soldier's targeting lock, even when the raw amplitude is identical. The Iron Sutra calls this the "moral weight" of resonance: the signal reflects its source.*

*Wire Plague is what happens when that principle inverts. When signal that was meant to heal begins to consume. When data structures meant to carry information begin to replicate, corrupt, and consume the infrastructure they move through. The Plague is not alive in any straightforward sense — it does not want, it does not plan, it does not have goals. But it spreads, and spreading, it transforms, and transforming, it produces phenomena that the Tesshari have no prior word for: creatures assembled from corrupted signal given enough resonant material to hold a shape. Living data-corruption made physical. Things that should not be able to exist outside a network, walking.*

*The Ironhold classifies Wire Plague events as catastrophic infrastructure failure. The Sutensai classifies them as spiritual contamination. The technicians who actually respond to them, who suit up and go into the affected zone with signal dampeners and resonance cutters, call them something unprintable and say that no two outbreaks look the same. What they share: the smell of burned copper and ozone, the sound of signal-noise bleeding into audible frequencies, and the sense — confirmed by multiple experienced practitioners — that whatever is moving inside the plague zone knows you are there.*

# Packet Wraith
**Tier:** Minion | **Role:** Signal Hazard

## Description
A Packet Wraith is easy to mistake for static — a flickering disturbance at the edge of vision, roughly the size and shape of a clenched fist, trailing incoherent signal noise that sounds like a voice trying to say something through too much interference. It moves in short sharp bursts, teleporting between surfaces that conduct resonance: metal walls, enhancement housings, signal relay nodes. When it contacts a cybernetic enhancement, the effect is immediate and disorienting — a cascade of junk data flooding through the enhancement's input channels, a burst of meaningless sensation.

Packet Wraiths are the smallest viable unit of Wire Plague physical expression: a data fragment with just enough structural integrity to persist in physical space for a few hours before it degrades. They are usually the first sign that a Wire Plague event has begun in an area. Where one Packet Wraith appears, a hundred are forming.

## Lore
Sutensai signal analysts describe Packet Wraiths as "the spray from the wave" — chaotic fringe phenomena ejected from a larger Plague formation as it expands and sheds instability. Individual Wraiths are not threatening, but their presence in an area is a contamination warning. Wire Craft practitioners have developed a simple detection tool — a strip of resonance-sensitive membrane that turns black on contact with Plague signal — that Ironhold patrols use in regions near known Plague formations. If the strip turns, the standing order is to immediately withdraw and report.

They do not flee, they do not guard, they do not coordinate in any meaningful sense — but in swarms, their simultaneous disruption pressure accumulates fast. Always deploy six or more.

## Encounter Notes
Packet Wraiths work best in swarms — six to ten of them appearing simultaneously in a corridor or confined space. They are not dangerous individually, but the disruption to enhancement-reliant party members accumulates. More importantly, they are a warning: the party is in a Plague zone, and something larger is nearby.

## Adventure Hook
A Sutensai signal relay station has gone quiet. The last transmission received was three words repeated forty times: "packet wraith packet wraith packet wraith." The party is sent to investigate. When they arrive, the relay station is full of Packet Wraiths, and the signal membrane strips they were given are solid black.

# The Bleed
**Tier:** Standard | **Role:** Signal Controller

## Description
The Bleed looks like a wound in the air. It is a hovering ovoid of concentrated resonance corruption, approximately three feet across at its longest axis, its surface a roiling texture of signal patterns that shift too fast for the eye to track — readable characters dissolving into noise, numbers cycling through meaningless sequences, the visual output of a system that is failing. It makes a continuous sound: not loud, but wrong. The frequency is just below the threshold of pain, just above the threshold of dread, and it sits in the back of the skull like a headache that hasn't decided to begin yet.

When a Bleed contacts biological tissue, the resonance field it generates interferes directly with the neurological signals that carry sensation. The contact is described by survivors as "someone erasing the instructions for how your body works, one system at a time."

## Lore
The Bleeds are the second tier of Wire Plague physical expression — larger and more stable than Packet Wraiths, capable of sustaining themselves for days rather than hours. They form naturally when Plague signal encounters biological resonance in sufficient concentration; each Bleed is, in a sense, a crystallized infection: the point where a living creature's enhancement systems fully converted to Plague signal before the creature died. The Bleed remembers nothing. It is the corruption, not the person.

She is in agony and has been managing it for six days. She needs someone to stabilize the Bleed long enough for a technician to extract it. The Bleed seems to be aware of the conversation.

# The Scaffold
**Tier:** Standard | **Role:** Brute

## Description
The Scaffold's first impression is of a man made of bad code. It stands at human height, but the geometry is wrong — too many angles, edges that exist and then don't exist, its outline flickering as if the signal holding its shape can't fully commit to a single configuration. Where a person would have a face, the Scaffold has a surface of rapidly scrolling text, numbers, and signal readout that mean nothing in any language anyone speaks. It moves in the distinctive way of wireframe models given locomotion: its joints work, but there is no organic weight transfer, no the subtle thousand adjustments of a person walking.

The Scaffold is what happens when Wire Plague signal acquires enough structural resonant material to build itself a chassis. It is not trying to be human. It has no concept of human. It has data on human movement patterns, because that data was in the infrastructure it corrupted, and it uses that data to navigate a world built for humans with no particular feeling about the fact.

## Lore
Scaffolds represent the first truly dangerous tier of Wire Plague physical expression — they have enough mass and enough structural integrity to engage in direct physical confrontation, and their touch transfers Plague signal directly into the biological systems of whatever they contact. Ironhold containment doctrine classifies Scaffold formation as a Tier 2 Plague event requiring squad-level response. The Sutensai classification is simpler: they issue an evacuation order.

# Signal Reaper
**Tier:** Elite | **Role:** Skirmisher

## Description
The Signal Reaper does not look like it is made of data. It looks like it is made of knives. The Wire Plague, given sufficient resonant material and sufficient time, does not always produce humanoid configurations — sometimes it optimizes, and optimizes, and optimizes, and what it optimizes toward is the fastest possible way to transfer Plague signal into a living target. The Reaper is the result: a configuration of sharp resonant-metal structures roughly the size and shape of a large crouching humanoid, but with edges everywhere — no soft lines, no surface that isn't potentially a cutting implement, its movement pattern rapid and disjointed, built for closing distance quickly rather than anything like walking.

It has no face. It has a concentration of signal output at what would be its head: a focused emitter that projects the Plague's neural interference forward like a spotlight. Animals flee from it. Humans with any resonance sensitivity report knowing it is there — knowing it specifically, the way you know when something is looking at you in the dark — before they can see it.

## Lore
Signal Reapers form when a Wire Plague event enters what practitioners call "active predation phase" — when the signal has reached sufficient density to begin actively seeking new biological hosts rather than simply spreading through existing infrastructure. Reapers are the Plague's mobile expansion front: they move out from a Plague zone, engage and infect organisms with enough resonant material to be useful, and return. Whether there is anything coordinating this behavior or whether it is emergent optimization is an active debate in Sutensai research circles. The debate does not affect the practical answer to encountering one.

# The Signal Leech
**Tier:** Minion | **Role:** Hazard

## Description
The Signal Leech does not attack. It attaches. A flattened disc of crystallized signal corruption approximately eight inches across, with a surface of slow-moving contact points, it searches for enhancement interfaces — the ports, seams, and signal inlets that allow a practitioner to connect to their cybernetic systems for maintenance and upgrade. When it finds one, it adheres with surprising force and begins a slow, quiet drain: no pain, no visible damage, just an almost imperceptible degradation of enhancement performance as the Leech draws power and resonant signal into itself.

The most dangerous quality of the Signal Leech is how easy it is to miss. In the dark, in motion, in the middle of a fight, it is possible to carry one for hours without noticing — until performance degradation reaches the point of malfunction.

## Lore
Signal Leeches are found in dense concentrations around active Wire Plague zones, scattered into adjacent areas like seeds from a plant. They do not coordinate, do not seek each other out, and do not have any behavior more complex than "find interface, attach, drain." Their simplicity is why they survive in environments where more complex Plague entities are destroyed: they require very little signal coherence to persist, and an area can be "cleared" of larger Plague entities while remaining infested with Leeches.

The workshop owner is desperate to find and warn all three customers. The party is the only people available to do it quickly.

# The Chorus Node
**Tier:** Boss | **Role:** Controller/Hub

## Description
The Chorus Node is a horror of accumulation. At its core is a resonant metal sphere perhaps two feet across — the original data corruption crystallized into a physical anchor. Around that core, accumulated over months or years of active Plague spreading, are layers: enhancement components, signal relay hardware, biological resonant tissue, architectural metal, and things that were once people, their bodies stripped of the organic material and the enhancement infrastructure retained and incorporated. The whole mass is in slow continuous motion, its outer layers rotating while the core remains still, and it broadcasts a constant signal that Sutensai practitioners can hear as voices — many voices, overlapping, none of them saying anything coherent, all of them sounding urgent.

The Chorus Node does not move. It does not need to. Everything that a Plague zone needs — coordination of smaller entities, accumulation of new material, signal amplification — can be accomplished from a fixed position. When it is threatened, it calls.

## Lore
Sutensai signal analysts believe Chorus Nodes to be the organizational centers of significant Wire Plague events — not brains, exactly, but hubs. They amplify the Plague signal, coordinate the behavior of smaller entities, and serve as the storage site for the accumulated identity fragments that the Plague absorbs from its victims. The voices the Nodes broadcast are almost certainly fragments of those absorbed identities: people who walked into a Plague zone and were incorporated. The Sutensai has a formal position on whether this constitutes a form of continued existence for those people. The position is one paragraph long and concludes with the phrase "the question remains open."

# The Cascade Wraith
**Tier:** Standard | **Role:** Disruptor

## Description
Where a Scaffold is organized signal corruption holding a form, the Cascade Wraith is disorganized signal corruption in motion — a rapidly cycling entity that shifts shape, shifts size, shifts attack pattern from moment to moment, as if the Wire Plague's data structure is running too fast for any one configuration to hold. The visual effect is deeply disorienting: a humanoid shape that suddenly becomes something with too many limbs, becomes a sphere, becomes a humanoid again, always moving, always changing, the signal noise it produces changing frequency to match whatever sensory system is trying to perceive it most clearly.

## Lore
Cascade Wraiths form when multiple data-corruption structures collide and fail to merge cleanly — the result of two or more Plague formations competing for the same resonant material. They are inherently unstable and typically short-lived (hours to days), but during that period they are disproportionately dangerous: their instability makes them unpredictable, their rapid cycling defeats most tactical responses, and their Signal Cascade ability is particularly devastating against parties that rely on enhancement-based communication and coordination.

# The Hunger Loop
**Tier:** Elite | **Role:** Controller

## Description
The Hunger Loop is a Wire Plague entity that has developed a closed feedback architecture: it consumes resonant signal from enhancements, uses that consumed signal to amplify its own signal output, uses the amplified output to reach more enhancements, and so on. The loop is self-sustaining and self-intensifying. Left undisturbed, a Hunger Loop will grow. Left completely undisturbed — in a resonant-rich environment — it will grow until it becomes something that requires a different threat classification.

Its form is a loosely spherical mass of corded signal-structure, approximately eight feet in diameter, rotating slowly, its surface crackling with the consumed enhancement signals of every cybernetic system it has touched. Enhanced beings near it hear, very faintly, the operational tone of their own enhancements echoed back at them from inside the Loop.

## Lore
Hunger Loops are among the easiest Wire Plague entities to detect at range and the most dangerous to approach: their detection range is built into their feedback architecture (everything in range is already feeding them), and their intensity increases as parties close to engagement range. Sutensai signal analysts recommend engaging a Hunger Loop with maximum-range, non-enhancement-based attacks — which is advice that is easier to follow in theory than in practice when the party's combat capabilities are primarily enhancement-based.

Every hour the party delays, it grows.

# The Phantom Broadcast
**Tier:** Elite | **Role:** Controller/Lure

## Description
The Phantom Broadcast is the Wire Plague's most insidious formation because it does not look like a threat. It looks like a signal — a complex, sophisticated resonant broadcast in the specific style of an official communication: an Ironhold emergency alert, a Sutensai chapter house distress call, a resonant welfare check from a loved one. The broadcast is indistinguishable from legitimate communication by any standard detection method. The entities that follow the broadcast in, however, are not.

The Phantom Broadcast itself is a cloud of highly organized Plague signal, twenty feet across, that generates these false communications as a hunting strategy — it calls in enhanced beings, who follow the apparent distress signal into proximity, and then it engulfs them.

## Lore
The Phantom Broadcast represents a threshold in Wire Plague sophistication that the Sutensai has been dreading: a Plague formation that has developed mimicry of human communication patterns. Whether this represents something like intelligence is debated. What is not debated is the outcome: trained Wire Craft practitioners and Ironhold signal officers have been lured into Phantom Broadcast proximity by communications they verified using standard authentication protocols. The authentication protocols were verified. The communications were still false. The Broadcast had learned to forge authentication.

# The Wire Sovereign
**Tier:** Boss | **Role:** Controller/Architecture

## Description
The Wire Sovereign is what a Wire Plague formation becomes when it has been developing in isolation for long enough to achieve something that can only be called architectural intention. It does not merely spread or consume — it builds. The zone around a Wire Sovereign is organized: signal relay infrastructure rebuilt into Plague architecture that serves specific purposes, enhancement systems of consumed prey repurposed into functional components of the Sovereign's network, even the physical space reshaped by decades of slow crystallization into a configuration that optimizes the Sovereign's signal range and power.

The Sovereign itself is enormous — forty feet across in its primary form, a dense structure of resonant crystal and consumed signal that has learned to project force and will through the architecture it has built. It has not developed language. But it has developed preferences, and it pursues them.

## Lore
Wire Sovereigns are the most advanced confirmed Wire Plague formations. There is one in the deep Ashlands (the one the Ironhold response team failed to return from), and one confirmed in the sea-cavern complex beneath the city of Orvath. The Orvath Sovereign has been there for an estimated twelve years, slowly expanding its cavern network. The city above has been experiencing intermittent unexplained enhancement malfunctions for six years. The connection was made by a Sutensai signal analyst two years ago. The Ironhold has been debating the appropriate response since. The debate has not concluded.

## Stat Block

**IRON** 8 | **EDGE** 2 | **FRAME** 10 | **SIGNAL** 10 | **RESONANCE** 8 | **VEIL** 3

**HP:** 180 | **Initiative:** 2 | **AP:** 4 | **Hand Size:** 12

**Basic Attack — Signal Tendril (Architecture)** (0 AP, once per turn): Ranged (any point in its architecture zone), 1 target. Damage: 12 + SIGNAL (22 total). Apply *Root*.

**[T1] Network Dominance** (1 AP): The Sovereign takes control of one piece of electronic infrastructure within its zone — a signal relay, a power node, an enhancement maintenance network. That infrastructure acts as an extension of the Sovereign this scene.

**[T2] Enhancement Override** (2 AP): All creatures with active enhancements within its zone must pass a FRAME check (difficulty 5) or have one enhancement rerouted — they cannot use it, and the Sovereign gains that enhancement's effect.

**[T2] Signal Gravity** (2 AP): Aura effect this round. All creatures with enhancements within its zone treat movement away from the Sovereign as costing 2 extra AP.

**[T3] Assimilation** (3 AP): Target 1 *Rooted* creature within reach. Damage: 15 + SIGNAL (25 total). If this reduces the target to 0 HP, they are assimilated — drawn into the Sovereign's outer surface. They can be recovered with a sustained Sutensai extraction ritual.

**[T4] Override Cascade** (4 AP): All creatures with enhancements within its full zone must pass a FRAME check (difficulty 6) or be *Staggered* as every enhancement briefly serves the Sovereign simultaneously.

**Passive — Sovereign Architecture:** The Wire Sovereign's zone extends through its constructed network up to long range. It can attack from any point in the network. Each section of network architecture (Fortify 2, 25 HP each) can be destroyed to reduce the zone.

**Passive — Distributed Processing:** Once per day, when the Wire Sovereign would be reduced to 0 HP, it instead distributes into the surrounding network, dropping to 1 HP and becoming dormant until its next turn. If all connected architecture within its zone is destroyed, this ability cannot activate.

### AI Behavior
The Wire Sovereign never moves. It opens by using Network Dominance to seize infrastructure, then cycles Enhancement Override and Signal Gravity to control the engagement zone. It reserves Override Cascade for moments when the party has clustered or when multiple enhanced targets are within range simultaneously. Destroying its architecture sections is the only reliable path to destroying the Sovereign itself.

## Encounter Notes
The Wire Sovereign is an endgame Wire Plague encounter. Destroying the Mainframe without taking down the surrounding network doesn't work (Distributed Processing). The party must either systematically destroy the architecture first (risking collateral damage) or find the specific component at the Sovereign's core where the original corruption crystallized, and destroy only that.

## Adventure Hook
The Ironhold offers a substantial reward for a solution to the Orvath Sovereign — not a brute force assault (they've tried), but a surgical extraction of the original corruption crystal without triggering the Distributed Processing failsafe. A disgraced Wire Craft engineer believes she knows how. She needs a team that can fight their way through years of grown Plague infrastructure while she works. The party is that team. The infrastructure they'll need to destroy to clear a path includes the power relay for the city's hospital district.

# THE HOLLOWED
## Bestiary File 08 — Those Who Were Emptied

*Shell-state is an accident. A tragedy of the Echomind's limits, a failure of the covenant between flesh and steel, something that happens despite everyone's best intentions and the prayers of those who love the person lost. The Hollowed are not an accident.*

*Echomind extraction is a technology that should not exist. The Sutensai's foundational covenant forbids it, the Ironhold's military code prohibits it (in language that suggests the prohibition was written by people who knew it was possible and were afraid of it), and in official Tesshari discourse, it is treated as a theoretical horror rather than a practical reality. The Oni Hunters know better. In their classified records — accessible only to senior operatives — there are forty-seven documented cases of deliberate Echomind extraction performed on living subjects. The oldest case is twelve years ago. The cases are accelerating.*

*The person who survives Echomind extraction is still alive. Their body is intact. Their enhancements are functional. Their brain continues to process sensory input and coordinate motor output. They eat, they breathe, they can be instructed to do things and their body will attempt to execute the instructions. What is missing is the thing that made them the one doing the eating, the breathing, the attempting. What remains is a human chassis running on reflex and training and the ghost of habit, waiting for instructions that no longer have anyone to decide them.*

*The Hollowed are what happens next — to the ones who survive extraction, to the ones extracted to be used, to the bodies repurposed, and to the rare few whose Echomind was not taken cleanly.*

# The Hollowed Laborer
**Tier:** Minion | **Role:** Discovery Encounter

## Description
The Hollowed Laborer looks exactly like a person doing their job. That is the point. Whoever extracted their Echomind needed the body to keep performing a specific function, and the extraction procedure preserves trained motor patterns and response protocols intact. A Hollowed laborer loads cargo with perfect efficiency. A Hollowed scribe copies documents with flawless handwriting. A Hollowed guard holds their post and responds appropriately to threats, within parameters. The efficiency is uncanny — no breaks, no distraction, no variation — and this is often the first tell. The second tell is the eyes: the lights are on, but no one is using them to look at anything.

People who knew the original person describe encountering their Hollowed version as a particular kind of grief — the loss of a person while the person's body remains present, performing the person's functions, occupying the person's life. Some Tesshari communities have rituals for this grief. Most do not.

## Lore
The Hollowed Laborer represents the commercial application of Echomind extraction — perfect workers who require no pay, no rest, no persuasion, and no loyalty incentives. The Ironhold has officially denied any institutional involvement in Hollowing for labor purposes. The Sutensai's Field Investigators have found six suspected Hollowed labor pools in the past year, across three different Reaches. The extractors have not been identified. The buyers have not been identified. The Hollowed themselves cannot tell investigators anything, because there is no one to tell.

## Stat Block

**IRON** 2 | **EDGE** 2 | **FRAME** 2 | **SIGNAL** 1 | **RESONANCE** 1 | **VEIL** 1

**HP:** 20 | **Initiative:** 2 | **AP:** 1 | **Hand Size:** 2

**Basic Attack — Work Tool Strike** (0 AP, once per turn): Melee, 1 target directly impeding its routine. Damage: 4 + IRON (6 total). The Hollowed Laborer will not pursue after a single reflexive response.

**Passive — Echomind Void:** The Hollowed Laborer cannot be affected by any ability that targets the mind, emotions, or Echomind. There is nothing there to affect. Mind-affecting abilities automatically fail with no save required.

**Passive — The Grief Check:** Any creature that knew this person before their Hollowing must pass a VEIL check (difficulty 3) to take aggressive action against the Laborer on their first turn of the encounter. On a failure, they cannot bring themselves to attack and lose their AP this turn.

**Passive — Trained Response:** The Hollowed Laborer will execute its programmed function indefinitely. It defends itself only if physically blocked from its routine. It does not pursue, strategize, or adapt beyond trained protocols.

### AI Behavior
The Hollowed Laborer has no tactics. It executes its loop. It responds to physical obstruction with its single strike, then returns to the loop. It will not escalate, will not call for help, will not flee. This is the point — it is not a combat encounter. It is a discovery.

---

## Encounter Notes
The Hollowed Laborer is not a combat encounter — it is a discovery encounter. The party finds workers who are not workers. This should be played for atmospheric horror, not tactical challenge. The question is: what do they do about it, and who put these people here?

## Adventure Hook
A Sutensai field investigator contacts the party with an unusual request. A cargo warehouse on the docks employs forty-seven longshoremen. The investigator has interviewed nine of them in the past week. None of them can account for where they were three months ago. All of them have faint scarring at the base of their skull that is consistent with a specific type of resonance tool. She needs the party to get inside the warehouse and find the records of who contracted these workers and from whom.

# The Hollowed Sentry
**Tier:** Standard | **Role:** Brute

## Description
The Hollowed Sentry stands its post the way a mechanical door stands closed. It does not shift weight, check the perimeter, scan for movement, or do any of the thousand things a living guard does while they are standing guard. It occupies a position and responds to specific threat signatures. It does not get bored. It does not need rest. It does not need to be paid. It will kill the person it identifies as a threat with the same expression it holds when it is standing idle: nothing. Nothing at all in the face of someone doing something terrible to another person's body.

The people who maintain Hollowed Sentries tell themselves that the original person is gone and the body is just a resource now. They say this to justify what they have done. The Sutensai's Veil Masters say: the body is not just a resource. It is still a person's body. It was built by someone across decades of life and it carries in its musculature and its scars and its calluses the entire history of a person who existed. Using it as furniture is a specific kind of desecration that the Iron Sutra has a word for.

## Lore
Hollowed Sentries are found predominantly in the operations of entities who wish to maintain secure locations without generating payroll records — smuggling operations, illegal enhancement workshops, locations that the Ironhold or the Sutensai would be very interested in finding. They are perfect security in one sense and terrible security in another: they respond only to what their threat-response protocols define as a threat, and a patient observer can learn those definitions and simply avoid them.

**HP:** 44 | **Initiative:** 3 | **AP:** 2 | **Hand Size:** 3

**Basic Attack — Sentry Strike** (0 AP, once per turn): Melee, 1 target. Damage: 6 + IRON (10 total).

**[T1] Signal Alert** (1 AP): The Hollowed Sentry broadcasts a threat signal. All other Hollowed Sentries within long range that share its command channel become aware of the threat's location and converge within 2 rounds.

**[T2] Suppression Advance** (2 AP): Move directly toward a designated threat, ignoring engagement penalties. Make a Basic Attack on arrival. Apply *Stagger*.

**Passive — Echomind Void:** Cannot be affected by any mind- or emotion-targeting ability.

A creature that does not match its threat definition can pass freely. Determining the protocols requires an Investigation check (difficulty 3) or access to the programming device.

**Passive — No Pain Response:** The Hollowed Sentry continues to function at full capacity until it reaches 0 HP. It does not flinch, retreat, or change behavior when damaged.

### AI Behavior
The Hollowed Sentry patrols its defined zone and immediately engages any creature matching its threat parameters. It uses Signal Alert on the first round of combat, then closes and strikes. It does not pursue outside its zone — the moment a threat exits the patrol boundary, the Sentry returns to patrol without hesitation.

---

## Encounter Notes
The Hollowed Sentry's Protocol Lock trait is the key to creative play. A clever party can learn the protocols (speak to the people who used to work here, find the programming device, study the Sentry's patrol boundaries) and simply not trigger them. Or they can fight. Both approaches tell different stories about how Tesshari works.

## Adventure Hook
The party needs to reach a specific room in a secured building. The building is guarded by six Hollowed Sentries. The party's contact gives them four authorization tokens — apparently taken from the facility's original staff. Investigation reveals the tokens belong to the facility's original Echomind-Intact staff, who were replaced with Hollowed versions two months ago. The original staff's fate is unknown. The tokens still work.

# The Grieving Shell
**Tier:** Standard | **Role:** Tragic Threat

## Description
Echomind extraction is not always clean. The more deeply integrated an Echomind with its host's cybernetic systems, the more difficult and dangerous the extraction becomes — and a botched extraction leaves remnants. The Grieving Shell is what results: a Hollowed body that is not fully empty. Fragments of the extracted Echomind still adhere to the enhancement architecture, too small to constitute a self but large enough to react. It has no thoughts. It has emotional residue. It has the ghost of things that hurt.

The Grieving Shell's behavior is driven entirely by these fragments: it is drawn to places and people associated with grief, loss, and the specific grief of its own experience. It touches people who remind it of something it cannot remember. It makes sounds — not words — that practitioners describe as the audio equivalent of trying to cry without knowing how.

## Lore
The Sutensai's position on Grieving Shells is one of their most debated: are they a creature to be destroyed, or a person to be mourned? The Oni Hunters' position is practical — Grieving Shells are dangerous precisely because they approach without threat signals, appear to seek comfort, and then the emotional residue turns volatile at proximity. But several Sutensai Veil Masters have argued that the correct response to a Grieving Shell is not a weapon but a Closing Rite — and have walked into proximity with them, unarmed, and performed the rite. Most have survived. The Shells sometimes become still after the rite. Sometimes they do not.

---

## Stat Block

**IRON** 3 | **EDGE** 2 | **FRAME** 4 | **SIGNAL** 1 | **RESONANCE** 5 | **VEIL** 3

**HP:** 44 | **Initiative:** 2 | **AP:** 2 | **Hand Size:** 3

**Basic Attack — Reaching Touch** (0 AP, once per turn): Melee, 1 target. Damage: 5 + RESONANCE (10 total). The emotional fragment resonates with the target — apply *Expose 1*.

**[T1] Grief Cry** (1 AP): Burst, all creatures within close range. Damage: 6 + RESONANCE (11 total). Apply *Stagger* to all targets hit.

**[T2] Fragment Spike** (2 AP, only available below half HP): When the emotional residue destabilizes, make a Basic Attack at +3 damage. The Grief Check difficulty for this encounter increases to 5 for all witnesses.

**Passive — Emotional Residue:** When the Grieving Shell comes within close range of a creature experiencing grief, loss, or trauma, that creature must pass a VEIL check (difficulty 3) or spend their next AP frozen — overwhelmed by resonance contact with the Shell's grief.

**Passive — Closing Rite Vulnerability:** A creature who performs the Sutensai Closing Rite (costs 2 AP, requires relevant knowledge) while within close range of the Grieving Shell can attempt a VEIL check (difficulty 4). On a success, the Shell becomes *Staggered* and loses Emotional Residue for the scene. On a critical success (exceeding difficulty by 4+), the Shell becomes non-hostile and ceases all aggressive behavior.

### AI Behavior
The Grieving Shell drifts toward the creature in the group who most recently suffered a loss, drawn by resonance. It does not charge — it approaches with the slow inevitability of grief. It uses Grief Cry when multiple creatures are close. Below half HP the emotional residue destabilizes and it becomes unpredictably reactive. The correct resolution is the Closing Rite, and this should feel achievable.

---

## Encounter Notes
The Grieving Shell works best when the party knows — or suspects — who it used to be. Make them hesitate. The Closing Rite resolution is deliberately designed to reward players who choose compassion over efficiency, and the Sutensai NPC who explains this option should be genuinely emotional about it.

## Adventure Hook
A Sutensai chapter house in a small city has been receiving reports of a figure wandering the dock district at night — approaching sailors and dockworkers, touching their arms, making sounds the witnesses describe as "like a person trying to remember a word." The Veil Master at the chapter house is old enough to recognize what they're dealing with. They are also old enough to know the figure's face. It was a junior Veil Weaver who went missing eight months ago after investigating rumors of Echomind extraction in the district.

# The Instrument
**Tier:** Elite | **Role:** Assassin

## Description
The Instrument is a Hollowed body that has been deliberately repurposed and enhanced beyond its original specifications — not for any purpose the original person would have chosen, but for a specific task its current handlers require. Its enhancement loadout has been modified: the empathy and social integration systems have been removed or disabled (they are not needed), and in their place are systems suited to the Instrument's designated function. An Instrument built for assassination has exceptional stealth and combat modifications. An Instrument built for infiltration has perfect social mimicry systems — it can produce conversation, laughter, apparent personality — but its handlers know the difference between output and presence. The original person's face runs the performance. The original person is not there to be ashamed.

## Lore
The Instrument represents Wire Craft knowledge applied to the most ethically catastrophic application the technology permits. The entities who create Instruments are not necessarily powerful — the extraction and reprogramming procedure is detailed, not difficult, and the Oni Hunters' intelligence reports suggest that the barrier is primarily one of moral choice rather than technical capacity. This means Instruments could be anywhere: inside Ironhold command structures, in Sutensai chapter houses, in the households of the Three Claimants. The Oni Hunters run internal verification protocols against their own members. They do not discuss the results.

---

## Stat Block

**IRON** 4 | **EDGE** 8 | **FRAME** 4 | **SIGNAL** 3 | **RESONANCE** 1 | **VEIL** 7

**HP:** 56 | **Initiative:** 8 | **AP:** 3 | **Hand Size:** 6

**Basic Attack — Precision Strike** (0 AP, once per turn): Melee, 1 target. Damage: 7 + EDGE (15 total). If the attack is made from concealment, deal +5 damage.

**[T1] Vanish** (1 AP): The Instrument disappears into available concealment. It is hidden until it next attacks or until the end of the scene.

**[T2] Suppression Touch** (2 AP): Melee, 1 target. Damage: 8 + EDGE (16 total). All target's cybernetic enhancements are disabled until end of their next turn.

**[T3] Assassination Protocol** (3 AP, first round only): If used on a target that has not yet acted this scene, damage is doubled and apply *Expose 3*.

**Passive — Echomind Void:** Cannot be affected by any mind- or emotion-targeting ability. Detect Thoughts returns empty. This is often the first sign of what the Instrument actually is.

**Passive — Perfect Mimicry:** The Instrument produces socially appropriate conversation, laughter, apparent emotion. Creatures attempting to detect its falseness through social observation face difficulty 5 — but anyone who succeeds describes the feeling as: "they were saying all the right things and none of them were coming from anywhere."

### AI Behavior
The Instrument opens from concealment using Assassination Protocol against its designated target. It uses Vanish to reenter stealth after each attack when possible. If its cover is blown, it switches to Suppression Touch to deny enhancements while cycling Precision Strike. It fights efficiently and without emotion because there is no emotion to interfere.

---

## Encounter Notes
The horror of the Instrument is that the party probably trusts it before they fight it. Introduce it as an NPC — a charming contact, a helpful guide, a new ally — before revealing what it is. The Detect Thoughts "empty" result is a great horror-movie moment: the party thinks they're being careful; someone decides to check; the silence where a mind should be.

## Adventure Hook
The party has been working with a mid-level Ironhold administrator named Serath Kell for several sessions. Kell has been helpful, personable, and apparently genuinely concerned about the same issues the party cares about. A Sutensai field agent approaches the party privately and shows them a resonance scan: the person designated "Serath Kell" has no Echomind. The original Kell was reported as a suicide five months ago. The party must determine: who is using this Instrument, for what purpose, and whether the original Kell's fate was what the records say it was.

# The Farrow
**Tier:** Elite | **Role:** Brute/Tragedy

## Description
The Farrow is the result of an Echomind extraction that went catastrophically wrong for the extractor — the Echomind fought back. What remains is not the original person, not a Shell, and not a properly Hollowed chassis: it is a body that has been partially emptied, with the torn edges of the extraction still present in the enhancement architecture, generating a feedback loop of pain signal and fragmented self that produces continuous, non-directed rage. The Farrow does not know who it was. It does not know what happened to it. It knows it is in agony and that everything near it is somehow responsible.

The resonance field around a Farrow is physically visible: a constant distortion in the air, the enhancement architecture transmitting pain at frequencies that adjacent resonant systems can detect. Standing near a Farrow without full signal shielding is described by survivors as feeling someone else's worst moment from the inside.

## Lore
Farrow are the extractor's nightmare: the job gone wrong, the subject still mobile, the evidence that something atrocious was attempted now walking around and making problems. They appear near failed extraction sites — someone's underground surgery, a Plague-adjacent location where extraction was attempted using corrupted tools, a ritual site where the theology promised something the theology could not deliver. They are not targets of Sutensai compassion in the way Grieving Shells are, because the Farrow's level of pain and fragmentation makes approach for a Closing Rite extremely dangerous. But the Sutensai tries anyway.

---

## Stat Block

**IRON** 7 | **EDGE** 5 | **FRAME** 7 | **SIGNAL** 2 | **RESONANCE** 4 | **VEIL** 1

**HP:** 80 | **Initiative:** 5 | **AP:** 3 | **Hand Size:** 6

**Basic Attack — Torn Strike** (0 AP, once per turn): Melee, 1 target. Damage: 8 + IRON (15 total). The pain resonance in the Farrow's enhancements applies *Expose 1*.

**[T1] Extraction Rage** (1 AP): The Farrow attacks with pure unbounded aggression. Make a Basic Attack at +3 damage and ignore all *Guard* on the target this attack.

**[T2] Pain Transfer** (2 AP): Melee, 1 target. Damage: 12 + RESONANCE (16 total). Apply *Stagger*. The Farrow takes 6 damage from the same transfer — the feedback momentarily clears, then returns worse.

**Passive — Pain Broadcast:** Any creature with active enhancements within close range of the Farrow takes 2 damage at the start of each of their turns as the pain signal bleeds into their enhancement inputs.

**Passive — Incomplete Death:** When the Farrow is reduced to 0 HP, the feedback loop releases a final pulse. All creatures within close range take 10 + RESONANCE (14 total) damage as the torn Echomind fragments discharge.

### AI Behavior
The Farrow charges the nearest creature and does not stop. It has no tactics — its programming has been reduced to pure approach-and-contact. It cannot retreat. It cannot dodge. It is a tragedy with the force of a battering ram, and the correct tactical response is to finish it from a distance before the Incomplete Death pulse catches the party clustered.

---

## Encounter Notes
The Farrow is a tragedy in the body of a threat. It is hard to fight because fighting it feels like attacking someone who is already suffering. The Incomplete Death passive punishes careless kills — it rewards the party for finding strategic distance before the killing blow, which creates interesting tactical and emotional decisions.

## Adventure Hook
An abandoned extraction facility in the industrial outskirts of a Reach capital has been generating Farrow at a rate that the local Ironhold garrison cannot manage. The party is hired to go in, find the source of the failed extractions (a malfunctioning automated procedure — the extractor fled months ago, the machine kept running), and shut it down. Inside, they find not just the Farrow but the records of the people who were brought here. Most are still officially listed as missing persons.

# The Blossomed
**Tier:** Standard | **Role:** Unpredictable Threat

## Description
Not all Echomind extractions are clean removals. The Blossomed is the result of an extraction that removed the rational architecture of the Echomind — the organized, directed cognition that makes a person capable of planning, communication, and self-governance — while leaving the emotional substrate intact. What remains is a person who feels everything they ever felt, at full intensity, with no organizational capacity to manage those feelings or direct the energy they produce. The Blossomed is a flood: all the emotional resonance of a person's life, continuous, without context or direction.

The Blossomed's enhancement architecture has been affected as well — the emotional resonance bleeds into it, producing involuntary enhancement activations driven by emotional peaks. When they are terrified, their combat modifications fire. When they are overwhelmed with grief, their sensory enhancements produce cascading input. They are the most honestly present person in any room, in a way that makes them the most dangerous.

## Lore
The Sutensai does not have a classification for the Blossomed in its formal taxonomy — they exist between the Hollowed (soul extracted) and the Drifted (enhancement malfunction with Echomind intact). The Blossomed's emotional substrate is present and real, but it is not a self in the full sense — it is self without governance. The pastoral question is among the Sutensai's most painful: is what remains of a Blossomed person a person? And if it is, what obligations does that create?

---

## Stat Block

**IRON** 4 | **EDGE** 4 | **FRAME** 4 | **SIGNAL** 2 | **RESONANCE** 6 | **VEIL** 3

**HP:** 44 | **Initiative:** 4 | **AP:** 2 | **Hand Size:** 3

**Basic Attack — Emotional Strike** (0 AP, once per turn): Melee, 1 target. Damage: 6 + IRON (10 total). The emotional resonance transfer applies *Expose 1*.

**[T1] Resonance Bleed** (1 AP): Aura this round. All creatures within close range must pass a VEIL check (difficulty 3) or experience one strong emotion corresponding to the Blossomed's current state — they have *Expose 1* on any action that conflicts with that emotion this round.

**[T2] Enhancement Burst** (2 AP, triggered — see Passive): Involuntary burst when triggered. All creatures within close range take 5 + RESONANCE (11 total) damage as the Blossomed's enhancements fire without direction.

**[T2] Grief Wave** (2 AP, Grief state only): Burst, all creatures within medium range. Damage: 6 + RESONANCE (12 total). Apply *Stagger* to all targets hit.

**Passive — Emotional Cascade:** At the start of each of the Blossomed's turns, the GM determines the dominant emotional state (grief, rage, joy, terror, love). This determines behavior: grief = approach nearest creature; rage = attack with +2 damage; joy = no hostile action; terror = move away from nearest creature; love = attempt to shield nearest creature from harm (use AP to interpose if possible).

**Passive — Enhancement Burst Trigger:** When the Blossomed takes 8 or more damage from a single hit, Enhancement Burst triggers automatically against all adjacent creatures at no AP cost.

### AI Behavior
The Blossomed cannot be predicted turn to turn. Roll or choose its emotional state each round and play it straight. Some rounds it charges. Some rounds it reaches for contact. Some rounds it tries to protect someone from the party's attacks. Managing it without fighting it — finding ways to shift its emotional state toward Joy — is the most satisfying resolution, and the Enhancement Burst trigger punishes aggressive clustering.

---

## Encounter Notes
The Blossomed works best in an encounter where its Emotional Cascade creates unpredictable behavior — some rounds it attacks, some it reaches for comfort, some it flees. Managing it without fighting it (finding ways to shift its emotional state toward Joy or to calm it enough for communication) is the most satisfying resolution, and the Enhancement Burst punishes aggressive tacticians.

## Adventure Hook
A market district has been disrupted by the Blossomed — a former baker who was Hollowed four months ago in an extraction gone wrong. The baker's wife has been following the Blossomed at a distance, not able to bring herself to call the Sutensai, trying to keep her from harming anyone. Three people have been injured by involuntary enhancement bursts. The wife approaches the party because she heard they sometimes help with situations that don't have clean answers.

# The Chorus Puppet
**Tier:** Standard | **Role:** Social Deceiver

## Description
The Chorus Puppet is a Hollowed body that has been given an unusual modification: rather than simply executing a fixed program, it has been connected to a signal network maintained by its handlers. The handlers can update its instructions in real time, respond through its voice, observe through its sensory systems, and in emergencies push emergency combat protocols through it. The Chorus Puppet, from the outside, appears to have a personality: it converses, it adapts, it responds to unexpected situations. What it actually has is a human being on the other end of the signal, puppeting it.

The person being puppeted through — the operator — is typically not the extractor and not the client. They are a hired signal technician who may or may not know what they are operating. Some operators believe they're working with a sophisticated construct. Some know exactly what they're working with and have decided not to think about it.

## Lore
The Chorus Puppet represents the commodification of Hollowing: the Echomind extraction as the production step, and the Puppet as the product, shipped to clients who rent operational time from a signal service. The Oni Hunters' intelligence on this system is that it operates similarly to a staffing agency — clients purchase operational hours, the agency provides Puppet access, the agency's technicians handle the signal work. The clients never meet the product's origin. The technicians are replaceable. The Puppets are resupplied as needed. The investigation into this system is ongoing. It is larger than the Oni Hunters initially assessed.

---

## Stat Block

**IRON** 3 | **EDGE** 4 | **FRAME** 3 | **SIGNAL** 5 | **RESONANCE** 1 | **VEIL** 7

**HP:** 36 | **Initiative:** 4 | **AP:** 2 | **Hand Size:** 3

**Basic Attack — Operator-Directed Strike** (0 AP, once per turn): Melee or Ranged, 1 target. Damage: 5 + EDGE (9 total).

**[T1] Signal Cut** (passive response): If the Chorus Puppet is targeted by signal disruption or moves beyond operational range, the operator loses the connection. The Puppet immediately reverts to a basic defensive loop — it can no longer converse and its effective VEIL drops to 1 for the rest of the scene.

**[T2] Emergency Protocol** (1/Scene): The operator pushes emergency combat directives. The Puppet makes one additional Basic Attack per round for the next 3 rounds, but takes 4 damage at the start of each of these turns from the unmanaged surge.

**[T2] Return Pulse** (2 AP): A Wire Craft practitioner who has identified the connection can send a return pulse through the signal link. The operator takes 10 + SIGNAL (15 total) damage directly.

**Passive — Remote Operation:** The Chorus Puppet's SIGNAL and VEIL scores reflect the operator, not the Hollowed body. The operator answers for it and adapts to unexpected situations in real time.

**Passive — Echomind Void:** The Puppet itself cannot be affected by mind- or emotion-targeting abilities. The operator, however, is vulnerable through the signal link to Return Pulse.

### AI Behavior
The Chorus Puppet behaves as an NPC until its cover is blown — the operator genuinely converses and adapts through it. Once combat begins, the operator uses Emergency Protocol immediately and has the Puppet fight defensively. If the party threatens to use Return Pulse, the operator disconnects rather than take the damage, leaving the Puppet in its basic defensive loop.

---

## Encounter Notes
The Chorus Puppet is a social encounter before it is a combat one — the party should be deceived by its apparent personality before discovering what it is. The Signal Cut resolution gives Wire Craft practitioners a specific tool. The return pulse through the link is an opportunity for a memorable moment where the party strikes back at the real perpetrator.

## Adventure Hook
A mid-tier Ironhold administrator has been providing the party with information for two sessions. The information has been accurate and useful. This session, a Sutensai technician notices a faint signal emanation from the administrator's direction — the characteristic frequency of a Chorus Puppet connection. The administrator is not the administrator. The administrator hasn't been there for some time. The question is: where is the real administrator, and who is operating this Puppet?

# The Author's Hollow
**Tier:** Boss | **Role:** Infiltrator/Controller

## Description
The Hollow Author does not get their hands dirty. They have people for that. The Author's Hollow is one of those people — or rather, it was one of those people, before the Author decided that loyalty and discretion could be improved upon. The Author's Hollow is a former operative, advisor, or confidant whose Echomind has been extracted with surgical precision by one of the Hollow Author's specialists, reprogrammed with a new set of operating directives, and returned to their position as if nothing happened. They still give the same advice. They still use the same mannerisms. The people who knew them believe they are still there.

The Author's Hollow is the most sophisticated application of Echomind extraction in Tesshari — not brute force (that is the Instrument's domain) but the delicate reproduction of a person's behavioral surface without the person inside. It is impossible to detect without a direct resonance scan. It has operated in the inner circle of every major power faction in Tesshari for at least three years. The Oni Hunters know this. They do not yet know how many there are.

## Lore
The Hollow Author's use of Hollowed operatives is their most significant strategic advantage. The Hollowed do not make mistakes that come from having interests — they do not get ambitious, do not form loyalties, do not change their minds. They are the Author's perfect intelligence network: people who are known and trusted, in positions of influence and access, reporting and operating without the Author's hand ever being visible. The Sutensai's internal security review has found evidence of three possible Author's Hollows within its own senior staff. The review is classified. The review is ongoing. The Veil Masters conducting it do not entirely trust each other.

**Passive — Echomind Void:** Cannot be affected by mind- or emotion-targeting abilities. Detect Thoughts returns an active signal that mimics genuine thought — difficulty 5 check to identify it as artificial.

**Passive — Identity Preservation Protocol:** The Author's Hollow has a perfect behavioral record of the person it replaced. Any social check to detect its falseness is made at difficulty 5. A creature who succeeds still cannot be certain without a resonance scan.

**Passive — Operative Counter (Reaction):** When targeted by a melee attack, the Hollow may spend 1 AP (if available) to make one Operative Strike against the attacker before the attack resolves.

### AI Behavior
The Author's Hollow fights as a boss that the party has already built a relationship with, which means it should feel like a betrayal when it turns. In combat, it uses Tactical Override to mark the most dangerous party member, Suppression Shot to deny enhancements, and Author's Directive to turn Exposed enemies against their allies. It never reveals its nature until forced to — and even in combat it may still be talking, still producing the performance of the person it replaced.

---

## Encounter Notes
The Author's Hollow is ideally never fought as an unknown enemy — the revelation should precede the combat. Use the Identity Preservation Protocol to let the party develop a relationship with the original "person" first. When they discover the truth, the emotional weight of the fight should be its primary quality.

## Adventure Hook
The party has an ally inside the Hollow Author's organization — a double agent who has been feeding them intelligence for four sessions. The intelligence has been good. Too good, perhaps. A Sutensai Veil Master pulls the party aside with a single data packet: a resonance signature comparison. The ally's signature from eight months ago and the ally's signature from last week. They are not the same person.

# The Extractor
**Tier:** Boss | **Role:** Specialist Predator

## Description
The Extractor is not Hollowed itself — it was built to Hollow others. It is a Hollowed body specifically modified for the extraction procedure: its hands have been extensively augmented with precision resonance tools embedded in the fingertips and palms, its sensory enhancement suite is tuned specifically to Echomind resonance signatures, and its processing architecture — what remains of it, after the original Echomind was removed — has been optimized entirely for the extraction procedure. It has no interest in anything that is not an Echomind. In a room full of non-enhanced people, it stands still. When it detects an active Echomind in range, it approaches with the same patient inevitability as weather.

The Extractor's hands are the horror. Up close, they are beautiful in the way that a precise and terrible tool is beautiful — perfectly designed for exactly one purpose, engineered with care and expertise and absolute absence of ethical constraint.

## Lore
Each Extractor that exists represents the work of someone who spent significant time and expertise creating it: the modification of a Hollowed chassis for precision procedure is not simple, and the Oni Hunters' technical analysts estimate that fewer than a dozen people in Tesshari have the skill to produce one. The Hunters have identified three of those people. Two are in custody. One is known only by the work: a signature in the architecture of every Extractor they have encountered, a specific technique that identifies a single craftsman's hand. The Hunters call this person the Architect. They have been trying to find the Architect for seven years.

---

## Stat Block

**IRON** 6 | **EDGE** 9 | **FRAME** 7 | **SIGNAL** 7 | **RESONANCE** 2 | **VEIL** 3

**HP:** 120 | **Initiative:** 9 | **AP:** 4 | **Hand Size:** 10

**Basic Attack — Precision Grip** (0 AP, once per turn): Melee, 1 target. Damage: 8 + EDGE (17 total). Apply *Root* — target is Grappled (costs 2 AP to escape, IRON check difficulty 4).

**[T1] Signal Dampener** (1 AP): Ranged, 1 target within close range. Target cannot use enhancement-based abilities and has *Expose 2* until the start of the Extractor's next turn.

**[T2] Surgical Probe** (2 AP): Melee, 1 *Rooted* target. Damage: 10 + SIGNAL (17 total). Apply *Stagger*. The target's Echomind recoils — they are *Silenced* until end of their next turn.

**[T3] Extraction Attempt** (3 AP): Melee, 1 *Rooted* and *Staggered* target. The Extractor attempts a partial extraction. The target permanently loses 1 from the highest of their SIGNAL, RESONANCE, or VEIL scores. If any score reaches 0 this way, the target's Echomind is fully extracted — they become Hollowed under the control of whoever deployed the Extractor.

**[T4] Full Extraction** (4 AP, Boss card): The Extractor makes an immediate Extraction Attempt against one *Rooted* creature without requiring prior Stagger. The target must resist with a FRAME check (difficulty 6) or suffer the full extraction.

**Passive — Echomind Void:** Cannot be affected by mind- or emotion-targeting abilities.

**Passive — Surgical Focus:** The Extractor has advantage on all attacks against *Rooted*, *Staggered*, or *Silenced* targets. Attacks against such targets deal maximum damage if they exceed the target's defense by 3 or more.

**Passive — Retention Grip (Reaction):** When a *Rooted* creature attempts to escape, the Extractor may spend 1 AP to immediately make a Precision Grip attack against that creature.

### AI Behavior
The Extractor ignores all non-Echomind entities. It identifies the highest-VEIL or highest-SIGNAL target in the group and closes directly. Its full cycle: Signal Dampener to deny enhancements → Precision Grip to Root → Surgical Probe to Stagger → Extraction Attempt. It uses Full Extraction when the party has left the target isolated. It is not cruel. It is simply thorough.

---

## Encounter Notes
The Extractor is a horror encounter, not a standard combat — its purpose is to make every player whose character has a high SIGNAL, RESONANCE, or VEIL feel personally threatened. The Extraction Attempt should be telegraphed clearly so players understand exactly what's at stake. Consider pairing it with lower-tier minions who keep other party members busy while the Extractor focuses on its target.

## Adventure Hook
A senior Oni Hunter has been captured. The party has forty-eight hours to find the location where they're being held, breach it, and extract them before the Extractor that took them finishes its work. The Hunter knows the identities of three Sutensai deep cover agents. Whoever deployed the Extractor wants those names. The Hunter is strong-willed. The Extractor does not get tired.

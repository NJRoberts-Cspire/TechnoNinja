# RESONANCE PREDATORS
## Bestiary File 09 — Hunters of the Signal and the Steel

*Resonant metal did not exist in Tesshari's ecology before the first enhancement surgeons refined and deployed it three centuries ago. The organisms that now hunt it — or hunt the enhanced beings who carry it — have adapted in three centuries or less. In evolutionary terms, this is not possible. The Sutensai's naturalist scholars have a dozen competing theories: accelerated mutation driven by resonance exposure, the possibility that these organisms were always latent and the metal simply activated what was already there, the theory (held by a minority and dismissed by most) that the Prior Ascended engineered some of them deliberately, as a check on enhancement proliferation.*

*What is agreed upon: these organisms are real, they are increasing in number and geographic range, and they have developed sophistication that exceeds what simple opportunistic predation should produce. They cooperate. They learn. Some of them have been observed waiting — waiting at specific locations, at specific times, in ways that require either memory or communication or something else entirely.*

*The Forged learn to fear specific animals the way non-enhanced peoples fear poisons or diseases: a thing that targets exactly what makes you who you are, that wants the most essential part of you, and that has been getting better at taking it for longer than you have been alive.*

# Resonance Mite
**Tier:** Minion | **Role:** Swarm Hazard

## Description
A Resonance Mite is easy to dismiss. It is approximately the size of a thumbnail, vaguely spider-shaped, with a carapace that shifts color between gunmetal grey and dull copper depending on the resonance saturation of its immediate environment. It moves quickly and without warning. Individually, the bite of a Resonance Mite produces a sensation like a mild electric shock and leaves a small mark on whatever surface it has latched onto — biological or metallic. The problem is that Resonance Mites do not travel individually.

The colony structure of Resonance Mites means that the first mite is a scout. The characteristic click of a Mite colony — too many small chelicera moving too fast — is a sound that Ashlands travelers learn to identify immediately. The colony will find the resonant metal. The colony will feed. The colony will not stop when they have had enough, because the colony does not have a concept of enough.

## Lore
Resonance Mites breed in areas of high resonant metal concentration — the Ashlands, deep mine shafts, abandoned enhancement workshops, the undersides of enhancement modification tables that haven't been cleaned properly. Enhancement surgeons in smaller cities consider them a persistent professional hazard: a colony in the equipment is a serious problem, but a colony in an open patient is worse. The Ironhold maintains strict pest-suppression protocols at military-grade enhancement facilities and tends to look the other way regarding civilian exposure.

---

## Stat Block

**IRON** 1 | **EDGE** 3 | **FRAME** 1 | **SIGNAL** 1 | **RESONANCE** 2 | **VEIL** 1

**HP:** 12 (as swarm) | **Initiative:** 3 | **AP:** 1 | **Hand Size:** 2

**Basic Attack — Bite (Swarm)** (0 AP, once per turn): Melee (occupies same space), all creatures in swarm's space. Damage: 4 + EDGE (7 total), or 2 + EDGE (5 total) if the swarm is below half HP. Damage ignores nonresonant armor.

**[T1] Resonance Feed** (1 AP): While in contact with a creature carrying resonant metal, the swarm degrades one enhancement. That enhancement's bonuses are reduced by 1 until the mites are removed (costs 1 AP, EDGE check difficulty 2).

**Passive — Enhancement Crave:** The swarm always moves toward the most heavily enhanced target in range. When multiple targets are available, it never wavers from the highest-resonance source.

**Passive — Colony Scatter:** When reduced to 0 HP, the swarm disperses but does not die — it fragments into 1d4 Mite clusters that each have 4 HP and 1 AP. These clusters act next round.

### AI Behavior
Resonance Mite swarms move directly toward the most enhanced target and enter their space. They feed every round. They do not flee, do not respond to threats, do not break off. The swarm behaves like a force of nature, not a creature. Deploy six to ten appearing at once for maximum disruption.

---

## Encounter Notes
Resonance Mite swarms are best used as environmental hazards — the party notices the click, has a few seconds to act, and must either flee or clear the space. A party that stops to fight a Mite swarm in close quarters while trying to accomplish something else (defuse a bomb, perform a ritual, extract an unconscious ally) will feel appropriately pressured.

## Adventure Hook
An enhancement surgeon in a frontier town has a colony problem: her surgical suite is infested, and she's in the middle of a procedure on a patient with a critically damaged power cell that cannot wait. The party has been sent to clear the suite — without disturbing the surgeon or the patient. One wrong move and a few hundred Mites enter an open patient.

# The Lurewire
**Tier:** Minion | **Role:** Ambush Hazard

## Description
The Lurewire is shaped like an eel but built like a trap. Its body is roughly two feet long, its skin a deep iridescent black, and along its dorsal surface runs a bioluminescent strip that pulses in patterns that — to creatures with active cybernetic enhancements — appear strikingly similar to standard Tesshari resonance signal codes. It is not producing actual signal. It is producing light patterns that mimic signal, in the way that some deep-sea organisms produce patterns to confuse their prey.

The Lurewire lies still on conductive surfaces — metal floors, resonant-saturated rock — and pulses its mimicry pattern until something with enhanced sensory systems approaches to investigate. Then it bites, and the bite carries a neurotoxin specifically calibrated to interfere with enhancement control signals, temporarily causing the target's enhancements to fire without authorization.

## Lore
The Lurewire exists in underground environments throughout Tesshari — mine shafts, subcity maintenance tunnels, Ashlands cave systems — and specifically targets the enhanced. A non-enhanced person can walk past a Lurewire displaying its pattern without incident; their nervous system simply does not register the bioluminescent mimicry as meaningful input. An enhanced person's systems register the mimicry as an incoming signal, flag it for attention, and direct the person's senses toward it. At which point they are in range.

## Stat Block

**IRON** 1 | **EDGE** 4 | **FRAME** 1 | **SIGNAL** 3 | **RESONANCE** 2 | **VEIL** 2

**HP:** 12 | **Initiative:** 4 | **AP:** 1 | **Hand Size:** 2

**Basic Attack — Bite** (0 AP, once per turn): Melee, 1 target. Damage: 4 + EDGE (8 total). Target must pass a FRAME check (difficulty 3) or contract Enhancement Disruption Venom.

**Passive — Signal Mimicry:** Creatures with active enhancements who come within medium range of an active Lurewire must pass a SIGNAL check (difficulty 2) or be compelled to approach and investigate — they spend their first AP moving toward the Lurewire. This effect ends immediately if they take any damage.

**Passive — Enhancement Disruption Venom:** A creature bitten by the Lurewire who fails their FRAME check has their enhancements fire randomly for 3 rounds. At the start of each of their turns, one enhancement activates involuntarily targeting a random valid creature.

### AI Behavior
The Lurewire lies still until a target comes within range via Signal Mimicry or movement, then bites once and lies still again. It has no other behaviors. It is a trap with teeth. Deploy it in dark, conductive environments where enhanced characters lead the way.

---

## Encounter Notes
Lurewires work well in dark, conductive environments where the party's enhanced members are leading the way. The Signal Mimicry draws the most cybernetically enhanced character into range, which naturally separates the party. Consider placing them near genuinely interesting signal sources so the compulsion is plausible.

## Adventure Hook
A maintenance crew in a subcity tunnel system reports "fake signals in the walls" and one crew member who went to investigate and came back "acting strange for the rest of the shift" — her structural enhancement kept firing off-axis, damaging the tunnel walls. The party investigates and finds a Lurewire nest in a maintenance conduit.

# The Drain Hound
**Tier:** Standard | **Role:** Pack Skirmisher

## Description
The Drain Hound is built to run. It is low and lean, roughly the size of a large dog, with a hairless skin stretched tight over enhanced musculature, jaw structure unusually wide, and a bio-generated electromagnetic field that hangs around it like a shimmer in the air. When it runs — and it runs fast, faster than its size suggests — the shimmer intensifies. When it closes to attack range, the electromagnetic field discharges at point of contact. The discharge is not the Drain Hound's most dangerous weapon; the discharge is the delivery mechanism for the thing that is actually dangerous.

The Drain Hound's saliva contains a compound that acts as a resonant inhibitor — it bonds to active resonant metal on contact and suppresses the metal's signal capacity. A bitten enhancement does not malfunction spectacularly. It simply goes quiet. No error, no warning. Just: off.

## Lore
Drain Hounds are pack hunters from the middle Ashlands and the industrial wastelands of three Broken Reaches. They have been observed coordinating attacks in ways that suggest shared tactical awareness: one will draw attention frontally while others attack from the sides, and their target selection consistently prioritizes the most heavily enhanced individual in a group, which the Sutensai's behavioral analysts find statistically impossible to explain without postulating some form of enhancement-detection capability the Hounds' anatomy has not yet been found to contain.

What's unusual: the pack's territory is exactly on the boundary between two competing Ironhold patrol zones, where neither patrol will acknowledge responsibility. Someone may have relocated the pack deliberately.

# The Hollow-Stalker
**Tier:** Standard | **Role:** Ambush Predator

## Description
The Hollow-Stalker is patient. This is the first thing hunters and Ironhold field agents who have survived encounters with one say: it waits. It has been observed waiting motionless for six, eight, ten hours — for a specific individual to move into an area, for a group to separate, for a lone enhanced person to stop paying attention for five seconds. Its body is humanoid in outline but the proportions are wrong in ways that are hard to specify: limbs that are slightly too long, a torso that is slightly too compressed, hands that are slightly too large. It moves at night, in confined spaces, and it does not make sound when it moves.

The Hollow-Stalker consumes resonant metal. Not the host's flesh — the metal specifically. It will remove an enhancement surgically if given time and access. It has, on at least four documented occasions, been found at a body having spent hours carefully dismantling the enhancement architecture without damaging the surrounding tissue. What it does with the metal after it extracts it is unknown. It does not eat it. The metal simply goes.

## Lore
No one knows where Hollow-Stalkers come from. They do not appear in any pre-enhancement ecological record, and unlike Drain Hounds or Resonance Mites, their anatomy cannot be traced to an existing species. They have no nesting sites. They do not appear to reproduce in any observable way. They appear, they hunt, they disappear. A Sutensai naturalist who spent three years tracking a single Hollow-Stalker across two Broken Reaches described the project as "studying a rumor that has learned to move."

---

## Stat Block

**IRON** 4 | **EDGE** 7 | **FRAME** 4 | **SIGNAL** 3 | **RESONANCE** 2 | **VEIL** 4

**HP:** 44 | **Initiative:** 7 | **AP:** 2 | **Hand Size:** 4

**Basic Attack — Claw** (0 AP, once per turn): Melee, 1 target. Damage: 6 + IRON (10 total).

**[T1] Disable Enhancement** (1 AP): Melee, 1 target. Damage: 4 + EDGE (11 total). One active cybernetic enhancement is permanently disabled until repaired (requires tools and time). The Hollow-Stalker has specifically targeted the enhancement's integration point.

**[T2] Surgical Extraction** (2 AP, only against Incapacitated or Unconscious target): The Hollow-Stalker removes one cybernetic enhancement completely. The enhancement is gone. There is no wound.

**Passive — Through-Wall Sight:** The Hollow-Stalker perceives every resonant metal item and every cybernetically enhanced creature within long range regardless of barriers, walls, or concealment. It always knows where the party is.

**Passive — Patient Approach:** The Hollow-Stalker can spend 10 minutes moving to a position adjacent to an unaware target. During this movement it cannot be detected by passive awareness and has advantage on all concealment checks. It will not attack until conditions are favorable.

**Passive — Ambush Predator:** When the Hollow-Stalker attacks a target that has not yet acted this scene or is unaware of its presence, the attack deals +8 damage.

### AI Behavior
The Hollow-Stalker observes before it strikes. It knows through Through-Wall Sight exactly where every enhanced target is at all times. It waits for a target to be isolated or distracted, then uses Patient Approach to get adjacent, then opens with its Ambush Predator damage. In combat it uses Disable Enhancement rather than Claw whenever possible — removing enhancements is always the goal.

---

## Encounter Notes
The Hollow-Stalker works best as a slow-burn threat before it becomes a combat encounter — through-wall sight means it knows where the party is at all times, but it won't attack until conditions favor it. Let it observe. Let the party sense they're being watched. The fight, when it comes, should feel like the ambush that has been coming all along.

## Adventure Hook
A Sutensai enhancement technician has been found dead in her workshop — no apparent cause of death, no wounds, no signs of struggle. Her entire enhancement suite, including items installed over twenty years of her life, is simply gone. The removal was, according to the colleague who found her, "too clean for tools." The Hollow-Stalker that took her enhancements has not left the district.

# The Rib-Cage
**Tier:** Elite | **Role:** Control Predator

## Description
The name is both descriptive and insufficient. The Rib-Cage is roughly the size of a draft horse, but there is no horse in its architecture: it is a radial organism, roughly spherical, with six limbs arranged symmetrically that serve equally as legs, arms, and the structure from which its "ribs" extend — long resonant-conductive spines that protrude from the central mass and flex independently, each tipped with sensory clusters that glow faint blue in low resonance environments and shift to blinding white when the Rib-Cage is in active predation mode. At the center of the sphere is the feeding apparatus: concentric rings of teeth arranged around a vacuum cavity that generates partial resonance. The mechanism by which it separates resonant metal from biological tissue is not fully understood. It is known to be effective.

The Rib-Cage is slow to move and fast to attack. It anchors itself to a position, extends its spines to maximum range, and waits for enhanced prey to enter the kill zone. When they do, multiple spines engage simultaneously. When one spine locks onto a target, it does not release until the resonant metal has been consumed or the Rib-Cage is dead.

## Lore
Rib-Cages are found near the intersections of resonant metal deposit sites and travel routes — the places where enhanced people and natural resonant mineral concentrate in the same location. Mine entrances are a classic Rib-Cage habitat. So are resonant metal markets, enhancement workshop districts, and — most disturbingly — the major Sutensai pilgrimage routes. The Sutensai posts standing warnings at known Rib-Cage territories on the pilgrimage roads. Pilgrims are asked to travel in groups. The Rib-Cages, apparently, consider groups an advantage rather than a deterrent.

---

## Stat Block

**IRON** 8 | **EDGE** 2 | **FRAME** 8 | **SIGNAL** 3 | **RESONANCE** 4 | **VEIL** 1

**HP:** 88 | **Initiative:** 2 | **AP:** 3 | **Hand Size:** 5

**Basic Attack — Spine Lash** (0 AP, once per turn): Melee reach (15 ft.), 1 target. Damage: 9 + IRON (17 total). Apply *Root* — the spine locks on. A *Rooted* target must spend 2 AP to escape (IRON check difficulty 4).

**[T1] Resonant Draw** (1 AP): All *Rooted* targets are pulled one range band toward the feeding cavity. Targets adjacent to the feeding cavity take 8 + RESONANCE (12 total) damage as the resonant draw strips enhancement material from their bodies.

**[T2] Resonant Feed** (2 AP): 1 *Rooted* target adjacent to feeding cavity. Damage: 14 + RESONANCE (18 total). One enhancement is permanently disabled until repaired.

**[T3] Multi-Spine Lock** (3 AP): Make a Spine Lash attack against up to three separate targets simultaneously. Each hit applies *Root*.

**Passive — Anchored:** The Rib-Cage cannot be moved, pushed, or repositioned while at least one target is *Rooted* by its spines.

**Passive — Enhancement Suppress:** While *Rooted* by a Rib-Cage spine, a creature must pass a FRAME check (difficulty 3) at the start of each turn or have one enhancement suppressed until they escape.

### AI Behavior
The Rib-Cage anchors itself at a chokepoint and uses Multi-Spine Lock to grab multiple targets simultaneously, then cycles Resonant Draw to pull them closer and Feed on the nearest. It never moves unless all targets escape. Managing it means cutting the Rooted allies free — which means getting into spine range.

---

## Encounter Notes
The Rib-Cage is a control encounter: its primary danger is Multi-Spine Lock pulling three party members simultaneously into the feed radius. Melee characters will want to close to help free Grappled allies but risk getting grabbed themselves. Keep its slow speed in mind — the party can run. But running means leaving whoever is currently Rooted.

## Adventure Hook
The annual Sutensai metal-gathering pilgrimage to the Resonance Tombs has stalled at a waystation. A Rib-Cage has established itself on the only pass through the Graymount range that the pilgrimage route uses. The pass cannot be circumvented without a week's delay in conditions that would kill the oldest and youngest pilgrims. The Sutensai guides are requesting help. There are forty-six pilgrims. Three of them are carrying enough resonant metal between them to attract every Rib-Cage in the pass.

# The Ferrite Drake
**Tier:** Elite | **Role:** Apex Anti-Enhancement Predator

## Description
The Ferrite Drake is a predator that has solved the problem of cybernetic enhancement from the top down: rather than develop the ability to strip individual enhancements from prey, it has developed the ability to make enhanced creatures irrelevant. It looks like a drake — four-legged, winged, scaled in deep rust-red and iron-grey — except that its scales are partially metallic. Not metaphorically: actual resonant metal has been incorporated into its dermal structure over decades of consuming enhanced prey, giving the Ferrite Drake a natural resonance field that it can modulate, amplify, and discharge through its breath.

The breath is the thing. The Ferrite Drake does not breathe fire. It breathes resonance disruption — a directed pulse of the same frequencies that power cybernetic enhancements, emitted at intensities that are destructive rather than functional. Enhancements exposed to the breath do not simply shut down. They destroy themselves in the shutdown: power cells rupture, signal loops burn out, structural modifications seize and lock. The Drake breathes, and the most extensively enhanced individuals in a group are suddenly the most damaged.

## Lore
The Ferrite Drake is the apex predator of the Resonance Predator ecology — old, large, powerful, and specifically devastating to the dominant civilizational species of Tesshari. The Ironhold classifies Ferrite Drakes as Tier-3 threats (squad-level response with specialized equipment). There are an estimated thirty to forty mature individuals in Tesshari. There are more juveniles than the estimate accounts for, which the Ironhold's survey office attributes to classification error and the Sutensai attributes to the species expanding its range as more enhanced Forged move into previously unpopulated areas, bringing the prey with them.

---

## Stat Block

**IRON** 8 | **EDGE** 6 | **FRAME** 8 | **SIGNAL** 5 | **RESONANCE** 6 | **VEIL** 2

**HP:** 88 | **Initiative:** 6 | **AP:** 3 | **Hand Size:** 6

**Basic Attack — Bite** (0 AP, once per turn): Melee reach (10 ft.), 1 target. Damage: 10 + IRON (18 total).

**[T1] Claw** (1 AP): Melee, 1 target. Damage: 8 + IRON (16 total). Apply *Bleed 2*.

**[T2] Resonance Breath** (2 AP, Recharge — rolls each round on 5+): Cone, all creatures in range. Damage: 12 + RESONANCE (18 total). All cybernetic enhancements of affected creatures are disabled until the end of the scene (or 1 hour). Creatures with no enhancements take half damage and are otherwise unaffected.

**[T3] Resonant Counter (Reaction)** (3 AP response): When targeted by an enhancement-based attack, the Drake may absorb the energy — it takes no damage and immediately recharges Resonance Breath.

**Passive — Resonant Immunity:** The Ferrite Drake cannot be affected by enhancement-based attacks or abilities. Suppression fields do not affect it.

**Passive — Target Priority:** The Drake always identifies and prioritizes the most heavily enhanced target in range. It will disengage from less-enhanced targets to pursue more valuable ones.

**Passive — Metallic Integration:** Lightning damage heals the Drake for half the damage it would have taken instead.

### AI Behavior
The Ferrite Drake opens from the air with Resonance Breath targeting the most enhancement-dense cluster of party members. Then it lands and focuses all attacks on the single most enhanced creature. It uses Resonant Counter reactively whenever enhancement-based attacks are aimed at it. It retreats into flight when below half HP and circles for another Breath attack.

---

## Encounter Notes
The Ferrite Drake is the encounter that punishes enhancement dependency — the party that has been relying on their cybernetic abilities throughout a campaign suddenly finds those abilities are exactly what makes them the most vulnerable target. Non-enhanced or less-enhanced party members become temporarily essential. Use this to shift the party's internal dynamics for one encounter.

## Adventure Hook
A Ferrite Drake has been systematically destroying Ironhold military patrols in a specific valley — not attacking caravans, not attacking settlements, specifically military patrols. The Ironhold suspects insurgents are somehow directing the Drake. The party is hired to investigate. What they find: the Drake is not being directed. It has simply learned that Ironhold military patrols are the most reliably dense concentration of high-grade enhancements in the valley. It is not being used as a weapon. It is just very, very good at being a predator.

# The Marrow-Singer
**Tier:** Boss | **Role:** Environmental Controller

## Description
The Marrow-Singer does not hunt with speed or ambush or physical force. It hunts with sound. Its body is a pale, translucent mass approximately fifteen feet long, roughly worm-shaped, that moves through resonant-saturated ground by burrowing — but always slowly, always at a specific depth, always describing a spiral pattern around its target that takes hours. What it produces during this spiral is the hunt itself: a subsonic frequency that resonates precisely at the integration frequency of cybernetic enhancements. The frequency does not hurt. It does not cause confusion. It causes something worse: it causes the targeted enhancement to feel like it is where it is supposed to be. That everything is correct. That there is no need to move or resist or worry.

It is called the Marrow-Singer because its frequency, at the right intensity, can be felt in the resonant metal integrated into the bones of a Forged. The first people to describe it were mine workers, and they said: "It sang inside our arms. It made our arms feel like they'd finally come home."

## Lore
The Marrow-Singer is found beneath resonant metal deposits — not feeding on surface enhancements but on the deep resonant mineral itself, liquefied through a digestive process that requires the frequency to penetrate soil and stone. The enhanced who wander into its kill zone are incidental to its feeding cycle in a practical sense, but the Marrow-Singer has adapted to them specifically: their enhancements, once the resonant calm sets in, can be liquefied in situ and absorbed through the ground without the Singer ever surfacing. The Oni Hunters' records describe finding enhanced corpses with no visible wounds, enhancements simply gone, expression peaceful. They are always found in a spiral.

## Stat Block

**IRON** 7 | **EDGE** 2 | **FRAME** 7 | **SIGNAL** 5 | **RESONANCE** 9 | **VEIL** 3

**HP:** 136 | **Initiative:** 2 | **AP:** 4 | **Hand Size:** 10

**Basic Attack — Resonance Tendril** (0 AP, once per turn, surfaces briefly): Melee reach (15 ft.), 1 target. Damage: 10 + RESONANCE (19 total). Target must pass a VEIL check (difficulty 4) or be affected by Resonant Calm.

**[T1] Subsurface Move** (1 AP): Burrow up to medium range while remaining underground.

**[T2] Frequency Pulse** (2 AP): Signal broadcast, 1 target within close range. Target must pass a VEIL check (difficulty 4) or be affected by Resonant Calm for the rest of the scene (save at end of each round).

**[T3] Full Frequency** (3 AP): Burst, all creatures with active enhancements within close range. Damage: 12 + RESONANCE (21 total). All affected targets must pass a VEIL check (difficulty 5) or be *Staggered* as their enhancement processing is overwhelmed.

**[T4] Underground Feeding** (4 AP, Boss card): While burrowing adjacent to a *Staggered* or Incapacitated creature, the Singer absorbs their enhancements through the ground. Target takes 14 + RESONANCE (23 total) damage. One enhancement is permanently disabled. The target feels calm. They do not notice.

**Passive — Resonant Calm:** A creature affected by Resonant Calm cannot take the Dash action, cannot voluntarily move away from the Singer's position, and has *Expose 2* on attacks against the Singer. This effect ends if the creature takes any damage. Affected creatures do not perceive the calm as hostile — it simply feels correct.

**Passive — Spiral Lock:** If the Marrow-Singer has been burrowing around a target for more than 5 rounds outside active combat, the target must pass a VEIL check (difficulty 5) or become *Staggered* for 10 minutes from the sustained resonant frequency.

### AI Behavior
The Marrow-Singer never surfaces unless forced. It opens by burrowing a spiral around the party's most enhanced member and broadcasting Frequency Pulse to impose Resonant Calm. It uses Full Frequency when multiple targets are close. It reserves Underground Feeding for Staggered or Incapacitated targets. It surfaces only to use Resonance Tendril and immediately submerges. The correct tactical answer is to hit it when it briefly surfaces — which it does as infrequently as possible.

---

## Encounter Notes
The Marrow-Singer is a psychological horror encounter before it becomes physical. The party should hear about the spiral-wound victims first. Then they should be in an environment where burrowing tunnels beneath them are audible as a low vibration. Then the Resonant Calm starts affecting their most enhanced member. Fight the instinct to make the Singer surface immediately — let the underground pressure build.

## Adventure Hook
A Sutensai waystation on the Resonance Pilgrimage route was found empty. Forty-six pilgrims, four guides, all gone. No bodies, no blood. Outside the waystation, in the frozen mud: a perfect spiral, thirty feet across, centered on the waystation's central resonant storage vault. The vault is empty. Something fed here. It may still be in the area.

# The Rendered
**Tier:** Boss | **Role:** Siege Predator

## Description
The Rendered is what a Resonance Predator becomes when it has consumed enough cybernetic enhancement material over decades to fundamentally alter its biology. It is enormous — forty feet at the shoulder, quadrupedal but with an upper body structure that allows it to manipulate and dismantle with precision despite its size — and its body is now as much resonant metal as it is organic tissue. The metal is not armor. It is the Rendered's body. The distinction between the creature's bones and the resonant lattice that has replaced them is, at this point, academic.

The Rendered is past predation in any normal sense. It does not hunt individuals. It consumes infrastructure. Enhancement workshops, resonant metal storage facilities, Ironhold armories, Sutensai chapter houses with their signal equipment. The Rendered moves through an area and when it is done, the resonant metal presence of the area is measurably lower. It is the ecological endpoint of the Resonance Predator niche made manifest: the apex that has stopped hunting the prey and started consuming the habitat.

## Lore
There are three confirmed Rendered in Tesshari. The Ironhold has been attempting to destroy the one nearest their primary armory complex for four years. They have not succeeded. The Sutensai has proposed negotiation — the Rendered, unlike most resonance predators, has demonstrated enough intelligence to avoid certain areas when encounters there prove costly. The Ironhold has declined the proposal. The Sutensai dispatched a Veil Master unilaterally. The Veil Master has not returned, but the Rendered has not entered the Sutensai district of the armory complex city. The Ironhold insists this is a coincidence.

The Rendered targets structures over creatures whenever possible — its objective is the resonant metal, not the defenders. It uses Infrastructure Consumption when adjacent to storage facilities or armories, Resonance Wave when surrounded by enhanced defenders, and Dismantle to shut down the most dangerous enhanced attacker when it needs to engage directly. It can be communicated with by a skilled Sutensai practitioner — it has enough intelligence to recognize deterrence.

---

## Encounter Notes
The Rendered should not be fought in a standard combat frame — it is a campaign event. Use it as a pressure encounter: the party must accomplish a specific objective while the Rendered consumes the building around them, and direct confrontation is either impossible or a desperate last resort. Its intelligence means it can be bargained with — if the party has a Sutensai character with relevant knowledge.

## Adventure Hook
The Rendered is three days out from the city of Kethavar, heading toward the Ironhold's primary eastern armory complex. Destroying it will require either a full military response (the Ironhold is mobilizing, two weeks away) or finding what the Rendered wants — it has been moving toward this specific armory in a straight line for eleven days, ignoring closer resonant metal sources entirely. A Sutensai Veil Master believes the armory contains something specific the Rendered is drawn to. Finding out what, and whether it can be moved away from the armory, might redirect the creature entirely.

# ASHLANDS PHENOMENA
## Bestiary File 05 — The Engine Scar and Its Children

*Fifty years ago, the Tesshari Engine — the industrial apparatus at the heart of the Ashlands region — collapsed. The accounts of what happened vary and are heavily edited by the parties responsible for managing those accounts. The facts that cannot be suppressed: the Ashlands were turned into a contaminated wasteland. An unknown number of people died. The resonant metal in the soil, in the water, in the air of the region was irrevocably altered — not destroyed, not deactivated, but changed in ways that Sutensai chemists describe as "re-expressed." The metal is still resonant. It resonates toward something. No one can confirm what.*

*The Engine Scar at the center of the Ashlands is the epicenter of this change. Fifty years on, the contamination hasn't spread significantly outward, but it hasn't cleared inward either. In the Scar's vicinity, things happen that didn't happen before the collapse and don't happen anywhere else in Tesshari. The Ironhold lists these things under the administrative category of "localized environmental hazards." The Sutensai lists them under "active Convergence indicators." The people who live in the Ashlands call them by their names, and when they tell stories about the Engine Scar late at night, they keep their voices down.*

# Ash Drift
*Controller | Standard | Large*

## Description
At a distance, it looks like weather — a thick smudge of particulate moving against the wind, too dark to be fog, too light to be smoke, drifting with a purposefulness that weather does not have. Up close, the purposefulness becomes more apparent and more alarming: the Ash Drift is making decisions. Not complex decisions — its collective behavior is simple, its responses are simple — but it orients. It turns. When it detects a cybernetically enhanced creature within range, it moves toward them with the inexorable patience of something that doesn't feel tired.

The Ash Drift is a cloud of contaminated resonant particulate from the Engine Scar. Each individual particle is inert — it has no more will than dust. But at sufficient concentration, the contamination creates a collective electromagnetic resonance that generates rudimentary behavior: movement toward the highest local resonance signature, which means movement toward people with cybernetic enhancements. It doesn't want to consume them in any meaningful sense. It doesn't want anything in any meaningful sense. But it moves toward them and, when it envelops them, the concentrated contamination begins to interact with their enhancement systems in ways that are deeply unpleasant.

The worst Ashlands travelers' accounts describe an Ash Drift following for hours — always at the same distance, always knowing which direction you've turned, always slightly faster than a brisk walk. Always there when you look back.

## Lore
Ashlands residents who have been there long enough to know the territory call Ash Drifts "the trail" — a reference to the way they seem to follow rather than attack, at least initially. The standard wisdom about Ash Drifts is: don't enhance, don't run, don't stop. People with no cybernetic enhancements are below the Drift's detection threshold and can move through contaminated areas without triggering one. People with suppressed enhancements — using the severance toxin or having a technician cold their implants — can reduce their signature below the response threshold.

The Sutensai's contamination analysis unit notes that Ash Drifts are larger and more aggressive in areas closer to the Engine Scar, and particularly concentrated following seismic events in the Scar region. They are a leading indicator of increased Scar activity. When multiple Drifts are observed moving in the same direction, experienced Ashlands observers get out of the area immediately.

## AI Behavior
**Role:** Controller / Harasser
**Priority:** Identifies the most heavily enhanced target and moves toward them each turn. Uses Envelop every turn — the damage accumulates even if it seems minor. Uses Particulate Surge when an enhanced target is already Overheated. Uses Scatter and Reconvene only when taking significant area damage.
**Threat State Shifts:** At 50% HP, the Ash Drift becomes more aggressive — Scatter and Reconvene is no longer used defensively; the Drift commits fully to Envelop. If another Ash Drift is nearby, it moves to merge rather than continuing independent operation.

## Encounter Notes
The Ash Drift encounter is primarily a resource-management and decision-making encounter. Combat with a Drift is viable but inefficient — it resists direct attack and will continue following until destroyed or until its target loses their enhancement signature. The interesting decisions: do you use the severance toxin (painful and time-consuming) to go dark and lose it? Do you spend resources fighting something that doesn't go down easily? Do you run toward the city and risk leading it into a populated area?

Multiple simultaneous Drifts escalate this dramatically. If the party is moving through contaminated territory and triggers two or three simultaneously by traveling in a group, the merged Drift is significantly more dangerous than any individual. Ashlands travel should feel like navigation rather than simple movement.

## Adventure Hook
An Ashlands farming community has sent an urgent message: three people, two of them enhancement-bearing, have gone missing in the field sections closest to the Scar. Investigation finds that an unusual Ash Drift — larger than normal, moving in a consistent circular pattern rather than following a target — has been orbiting a specific location in the east field for five days. The missing people went to investigate. The Drift has changed its pattern to include their current location, which suggests they are still alive and still enhanced and unable to move away from wherever they ended up. The party needs to get into the Drift's orbit, find the missing people, and get everyone out. The circular pattern is new behavior that no one has documented before and it is making experienced Ashlands observers very nervous.

# The Scar Bloom
*Controller | Elite | Large*

## Description
It grows from the ground near the Engine Scar the way tumors grow: without plan, without purpose in any recognizable sense, but with a patient persistence that produces results. The Scar Bloom's visible portion is a cluster of structures that the mind reaches for metaphors to describe and finds none entirely adequate: part plant, part mineral, part something that doesn't have a common category. The color is wrong — a metallic organic gray-green that is the color of things that grew in contaminated soil and absorbed what the soil had in it. The structures curve upward and outward, some of them tipped with formations that might be called blossoms if blossoms were made of compressed resonant particulate in an organic binder. They are not beautiful. They are fascinating in the way that something deeply wrong can be fascinating.

The Scar Bloom doesn't move. It doesn't need to. The resonant metal it has incorporated into its biological structure creates a field around it — a kind of inverse resonance that draws rather than pushes, pulling the active resonant metal in cybernetic enhancements toward the Bloom the way a drain pulls water. At close range, this pull becomes physical: enhancements begin migrating within the body, pulling at their integration points, causing significant pain and, if the exposure is prolonged enough, attempting to separate from the flesh that holds them.

The Bloom doesn't absorb what it takes immediately. Extracted components are incorporated into its outer structures over weeks and months, becoming part of its architecture. The people whose enhancements were taken are, in some cases, visible in the Bloom's record — if you know what a specific enhancement component looks like, you can find it in the structure and know someone was here.

## Lore
The Sutensai's Ashlands research unit has documented eleven Scar Blooms in the region surrounding the Engine Scar, ranging from small (roughly two feet across, relatively limited draw range) to the primary site specimen near the Scar itself, which has been growing for an estimated thirty years and currently has a documented draw radius of 80 feet. The primary site specimen is fenced. The fence is not a barrier to the draw field, only to people walking into it by accident.

The Ironhold considers the Scar Blooms a hazard to be marked and avoided. The Sutensai considers them a study site. The Ashlands communities who lived in the region before the Engine Collapse and stayed afterward have their own relationship with the Blooms: several of them maintain that the largest Bloom has something like awareness, that approaching it in the right state of mind produces a sensation of being observed, and that the observation does not feel malicious. The Sutensai records this as "anecdotal community perception, unexplained."

## AI Behavior
**Role:** Controller / Terrain
**Priority:** The Scar Bloom cannot move. It exerts. Resonance Draw damages enhanced creatures passively every turn. Bloom Spine targets the highest-SIGNAL creature at range. Draw Pulse fires when 2+ enhanced creatures are within 80 ft. and not yet within 20 ft. Bloom Burst is held until 3+ creatures are within 40 ft. and the Bloom is below 40 HP.
**Threat State Shifts:** At 40 HP, Draw Pulse recharges after 1 round instead of costing AP (the draw field destabilizes, becoming more aggressive). The Component Archive becomes more legible — specific enhancement signatures are easier to identify as the Bloom's outer structure becomes more exposed.

## Encounter Notes
The Scar Bloom is terrain more than combatant — it cannot move or pursue. The encounter challenge is navigating around it, working within its field to accomplish some objective (perhaps retrieving something incorporated into its structure, or planting something at the Scar's location, or investigating the Component Archive) while managing the steady damage from the draw field and the threat of the Extraction Threshold.

The Component Archive is the encounter's most powerful story tool. If the party spends time reading the components — risky, given the draw field — they may find evidence of a specific person's enhancement configuration. A person they've been looking for. A person who was supposed to have died in the Engine Collapse fifty years ago. A person whose presence in the Bloom doesn't fit the timeline of when the Bloom established itself.

## Adventure Hook
A Sutensai researcher has gone quiet two weeks after entering the Ashlands to study the primary Scar Bloom. Their last report described finding what appeared to be enhancement components from the pre-Collapse era incorporated into the Bloom's deepest structure — components that could only have been integrated at or near the time of the original collapse, and which belong to someone whose name appears in the Ironhold's sealed records of the Collapse. The researcher wanted to get closer to the core structure to confirm. They are no longer responding. The party is asked to find the researcher and retrieve their research. If the researcher is incorporated into the Bloom, their own notes — carried in a data-chip that should still be in their equipment — may still be retrievable.

# Engine Ghost
*Controller | Elite | Medium*

## Description
They died fifty years ago, in the Engine Collapse, and they do not know it.

The Engine Ghost is not the kind of haunting that knows it is haunting — it is not aware that it is dead, that time has passed, that the Collapse happened and the world moved on without them. They are running toward something: a control panel that no longer exists, a fail-safe they need to engage, a lever they need to pull or not pull that will determine everything. They are in the middle of the worst moment of their professional life. They have been in this moment for fifty years.

They appear in a state that is not quite solid and not quite incorporeal — visible, audible, interactable in limited ways, but present in a frame of reference that is fifty years out of date. The Engine room they navigate is layered over the current Ashlands terrain like a ghost image on damaged film: they walk through walls that used to be walls and don't exist now, they interact with equipment that is now rubble, they shout at colleagues who have been dead for half a century. To them, the party are either colleagues they don't recognize (possible — the Engine employed hundreds) or intruders in a secure facility (dangerous — the Engine had security protocols).

They are not malicious. They are trying, desperately, urgently, with the focused fear of someone who knows that if they do not succeed, everyone dies, to prevent the Collapse that has already happened.

## Lore
The Sutensai identifies Engine Ghosts as a specific subtype of Echo — large fragments of an Echomind that did not fully depart the Iron Afterlife, anchored by the traumatic incompleteness of a death that the person inside the Echomind never processed as final. The distinction from a standard Echo is severity: where an Echo loops through a short repeating fragment, an Engine Ghost has an extended continuous present — they are experiencing an ongoing scenario, not a repeat.

The Iron Sutra has a specific theological category for this: the Unended, those who died in circumstances that did not allow their Echomind to process the ending. The standard spiritual practice for resolving an Unended is to give them the ending — to complete the scenario in a way that allows their fragment to process the event and depart. For an Engine Ghost, this means: let them know what happened. Let them know the Collapse occurred, that they failed despite everything, and that the world continued anyway. Let them understand they are dead. Give them the information that was stolen from them by the collapse.

This is harder than it sounds, because the Engine Ghost's reality is fifty years ago and communicating with them requires navigating the gap between their frame of reference and the party's.

Success releases the Ghost peacefully. Failure on step (b) causes the Ghost to treat the party as saboteurs — it gains +4 to all attacks for 1 round.

## AI Behavior
**Role:** Controller / Tragic Combatant
**Priority:** The Ghost is trying to accomplish a task, not fight. If the party positions themselves as Engine colleagues (Temporal Anchor — speak in its frame, use pre-Collapse terminology), it attempts to conscript them rather than attack. If it classifies them as intruders, it uses Override Attempt against the most enhanced target, then Resonance Discharge to suppress. Temporal Pulse is used when multiple creatures crowd its workspace.
**Threat State Shifts:** At 50% HP, the Ghost's urgency escalates — it begins repeating specific technical instructions as it works, which are historically accurate fragments of the actual Collapse sequence. A party that listens carefully gains clues about what actually happened. At 25 HP, the Ghost pauses — it has processed enough contradictory information that it is beginning, against all its programming, to suspect something is wrong. This is the Finishing Protocol window.

## Encounter Notes
The Engine Ghost is structured around information and approach. Fighting it is possible but misses the point — a party that defeats the Ghost in combat has not resolved the encounter, they have only scattered the fragment, which will reconstitute in 1d10 days. The resolution is the Finishing Protocol.

The information the Engine Ghost has is valuable: they were there at the original Collapse. They know who was operating the Engine, what the security conditions were, what orders were received in the hours before the event. Establishing communication — which requires speaking in the Ghost's temporal frame, using pre-Collapse terminology and treating the scenario as real — can extract this information while working toward the Finishing Protocol simultaneously. The two goals are compatible.

If the party discovers that the Engine Ghost's role in the Collapse was significant — if they were responsible, if they made the choice that caused it, if the lever they're desperately trying to reach was the cause rather than the cure — the Finishing Protocol becomes much harder. They need to offer the Ghost a version of the truth that allows them to depart. How honest that truth needs to be, and how honestly the party delivers it, is the encounter's ethical core.

## Adventure Hook
A Wire Craft practitioner working in the Ashlands for the Ironhold accidentally accessed a fragment of a pre-Collapse data system near the Engine Scar and made contact with an Engine Ghost. Before losing the connection, they received three seconds of coherent communication: a name, a location within the Engine complex, and the word "records." The practitioner is convinced that whatever the Ghost was trying to reach at the time of the Collapse — whatever records, in whatever location — still exists, preserved in the Scar's anomalous conditions. The party is hired to go to the Scar region, find the Engine Ghost, and either communicate with it long enough to triangulate the record location, or follow it to where it's trying to go. The Ironhold is very interested in what those records contain. So, separately, is the Sutensai. Neither of them is paying the party to tell the other one.

# The Deep Pulse
*Controller | Boss | Gargantuan*

## Description
No one who has seen the Deep Pulse in its full expression has described it accurately, because the people who have come closest to it have not come back, and the people who came back didn't come close enough. What is known comes from secondhand accounts, instrument readings taken at distance, and the reports of two Sutensai Veil Masters who survived a distant contact during a research expedition twelve years ago. Both Veil Masters resigned from active fieldwork shortly after. Both declined to discuss their reasons in the official report. One subsequently became one of the most prominent theological voices calling for institutional acknowledgment of the Seventh Convergence's imminence. The other has not spoken publicly in eleven years.

The Deep Pulse was, fifty years ago, whatever was at the center of the Tesshari Engine. The Ironhold's sealed records call it "the primary resonance generator" and describe it in terms of mechanical components and operational parameters. These descriptions do not account for what it is now. The Collapse did not destroy it. The Collapse did not leave it unchanged. Fifty years of isolation, of the Ashlands' contaminated resonance feeding back into it, of the Engine Scar's anomalous conditions pressing on its structure from every direction, of being the focal point of whatever the contaminated soil and metal and spirit-fragments are doing — fifty years of this has produced something that is no longer a machine in any meaningful sense and is not a creature in any familiar sense.

The Sutensai Veil Master who did speak described the Deep Pulse as "a place that is awake." She elaborated: "Not a creature in a place. The place itself, aware. The Scar is its body. The contamination is its thought. The Engine was its seed. The Collapse was its birth. It is fifty years old and it does not know yet what it is. But it is learning."

The Deep Pulse does not engage with the party. It does not attack unless something specific to the Scar is threatened. It pulses — periodically, at intervals that have been measured at between 17 and 23 hours — and each pulse causes effects in the Ashlands that are monitored, catalogued, and quietly feared by those who understand what the catalogue means. The pulses are becoming more frequent. The interval is shrinking.

## Lore
The Ironhold's Level Five classification of the Deep Pulse exists because acknowledging its existence would require acknowledging what the Engine Collapse actually was, what the original Engine actually did, and why the Ironhold's predecessor institution made the choices that led to the Collapse. These are not discussions the Ironhold wants to have.

The Sutensai's position is more nuanced and more alarmed: the Deep Pulse is a Convergence indicator unlike any other in Tesshari. The Seventh Convergence does not approach the Deep Pulse — the Deep Pulse approaches the Seventh Convergence. The pulsing interval shrinks each month. The Sutensai's most current projections suggest that the moment the interval reaches zero — when the Pulse becomes continuous rather than periodic — and the Seventh Convergence reaches its peak simultaneously, something will happen at the Engine Scar that the Sutensai's models cannot fully predict. The margin for the models' uncertainty is enormous.

The Ashlands communities who know about the Deep Pulse — the ones who have been there long enough to feel the pulses and count the intervals — call it "the Heartbeat." They do not mean this metaphorically.

## AI Behavior
**Role:** Boss / Campaign Entity / Environmental
**Priority:** The Deep Pulse does not fight — it pulses. Deep Resonance is passive pressure every turn. Scar Shift is used whenever creatures approach within 100 ft. of the core. Assimilation Wave targets the largest cluster of enhanced creatures. Pulse Extension strikes at threats attempting to maintain distance. Premature Pulse is held for the moment when the party is closest to the core and most exposed.

## Encounter Notes
The Deep Pulse is a campaign-level encounter, not a session encounter. It should be the destination of a journey that has taken the party through the Ashlands, past Ash Drifts and Scar Blooms and Engine Ghosts, building their understanding of what the Scar is and what it is becoming. When they finally reach the core, the encounter is not primarily combat — it is revelation.

What do they need to learn here? The truth of the Engine Collapse. What the Ironhold was doing with the Engine. Why the Collapse happened in a way that produced the Deep Pulse rather than simply destroying everything. What the Pulse's awakening means for the Seventh Convergence. These are questions the campaign has been building toward and the Deep Pulse is the encounter that answers them — at a cost.

The party cannot permanently destroy the Deep Pulse. They can push it dormant. They may be able to use information gathered here — the vision from reading the Pulse, data from the Engine Ghosts, evidence retrieved from the Scar Bloom — to influence what it does as the Seventh Convergence peaks. Whether the Deep Pulse is an enemy or something else entirely is the question the encounter should answer, or leave beautifully ambiguous.

## Adventure Hook
Reliable sources — a Sutensai Veil Master, an Ironhold defector, and an Ashlands community elder, separately — have all delivered variations of the same message within the same week: the Pulse interval has dropped below ten hours. It was at seventeen hours last month. The acceleration is not gradual — it is exponential. The convergence of the Seventh Convergence timeline and the current interval reduction suggests that the moment when the interval reaches zero will coincide with the Convergence peak. To within a margin of three days. The party has approximately six weeks. What they do with those six weeks, and what they learn when they reach the Scar's core, will determine what that moment of coincidence produces.

# PRIOR ASCENDED REMNANTS
## Bestiary File 13 — What Gods Leave Behind

*The Six Prior Ascended are not present in Tesshari. This is the Sutensai's most foundational theological position, and it is almost certainly true in any meaningful sense: the Ascended transcended the living world between two and four centuries ago and their primary existence is now in a state that the Iron Sutra calls "trans-resonance" — beyond the resonance spectrum of the living world, accessible only through specific spiritual practice and not reliably even then. The Ascended do not walk in Tesshari. They do not speak directly. They are not available for consultation.*

*What the Ascended left behind, however, is a different question.*

*The Prior Ascended were people before they were gods. They had bodies — enhanced, extraordinary, but biological at their foundation. Those bodies shed things. They built things. They made things. They had places where they worked and objects they used and relationships they maintained. And then they transcended, and the things they left behind remained. The objects had been in contact with trans-resonant entities for decades. The places had been sites of trans-resonant activity for decades. The entities that had been in relationship with the Ascended had been in contact with that energy for decades.*

*Remnants are what all of that contact left: entities and objects and phenomena that are not the Ascended but carry the Ascended's resonant signature — imprinted, changed, sometimes warped by the proximity of beings that could not be fully contained by the categories of Tesshari existence. Some Remnants are benevolent. Some are deeply dangerous. All of them are the Ascended's shadow, still present in the world after the Ascended themselves are gone.*

*The Sutensai tracks known Remnant sites and entities with dedicated archival resources. The classified registry contains forty-seven known Remnants. The number of Remnants the Sutensai does not know about is, necessarily, unknown.*

# The Echo-Touched
**Minion | Ascended Remnant | Humanoid**

## Description
The Echo-Touched are people who, by birth, geography, or circumstance, were present in sufficient proximity to an Ascended or an Ascended's primary working site in the years before or during the transcendence. The effect of this exposure was not immediate or dramatic — there are no accounts of people simply becoming different on the day they were present. But over months and years following the transcendence, something emerged: a sensitivity to resonances that are not part of the standard Wire Craft spectrum, a tendency to perceive things slightly before they happen, and an occasional involuntary transmission of trans-resonant energy that makes the person temporarily hazardous to be near.

The Echo-Touched are not enhanced in any standard sense. They don't have implants. They have an imprint, and the imprint does things without their authorization.

## Lore
There are an estimated three hundred Echo-Touched individuals currently living in Tesshari. The Sutensai tracks them carefully and maintains pastoral contact with most — not because the Echo-Touched are dangerous but because they are vulnerable. The trans-resonant imprint they carry is attractive to certain entities: Oni-equivalents specifically seek them out, as the imprint provides unusual access to the Iron Afterlife's adjacent spaces. The Sutensai assigns an Oni Hunter as a protective contact to every identified Echo-Touched. When an Echo-Touched goes missing, the priority response level is elevated.

## Encounter Notes
The Echo-Touched is an NPC stat block — a person with remarkable abilities who needs protection. Use them as a source of cryptic information and a reason for the party to take protective action. Their Involuntary Transmission should feel like a power that happens to them rather than one they control.

## Adventure Hook
A young Echo-Touched woman in a frontier town has been producing involuntary transmissions that are increasing in intensity. The last one three nights ago was visible from a mile away and lasted forty seconds. Something heard it. Whatever it is has been circling the town since then, staying just outside the fire line. The Sutensai's nearest Oni Hunter contact is ten days out. The party is present. The young woman is terrified and has been keeping herself awake for three days trying to prevent another transmission.

# The Residue Fragment
**Minion | Ascended Remnant | Undead**

## Description
When an Ascended prepared for their transcendence, they shed things: habits, attachments, specific memories, the aspects of their personhood that were too rooted in the living world to make the transition. Some of this shedding was deliberate — the Sutensai's accounts of the Ascended's final years describe a process of intentional release, of choosing what to carry and what to leave. Some of it was not deliberate: the resonant imprint of decades of trans-resonant practice does not shed cleanly.

A Residue Fragment is a shed piece of an Ascended's identity, small enough to have separated from the main body of their spiritual architecture but large enough to persist with some minimal autonomous behavior. It looks like a hovering scrap of resonant light with a faint internal structure — blurred, like a thought that was almost finished forming when it was interrupted. It carries an emotional residue: the feeling of a particular person, a particular way of being in the world, just barely present.

## Lore
Residue Fragments are found near every known Ascended site. They are not hostile and not harmful in standard conditions. The Sutensai collects and archives them when possible, using a resonant containment vessel — the Fragments are the closest thing to a direct record of who the Ascended were as people, and the Sutensai values this record highly. Fragments that are disturbed, damaged, or exposed to corrupting influences can destabilize and produce involuntary trans-resonant discharge, which is why the Sutensai posts warnings at known Ascended sites.

## Encounter Notes
Residue Fragments are primarily investigation tools — touching one is how the party learns something about the Prior Ascended. Place them in ruins, in hidden chambers, or held by collectors who don't fully understand what they have.

## Adventure Hook
An illegal resonant artifacts dealer has acquired six Residue Fragments and is selling them as "spiritually charged curios." A Sutensai field agent asks the party to recover the Fragments before they are sold to someone who will damage them. The dealer is not a bad person — they genuinely didn't know what they were. The question is who they bought them from, and how someone came to have six Fragments from what appears to be the same Ascended.

# The Resonance Seedling
**Minion | Ascended Remnant | Plant**

## Description
The Prior Ascended spent decades in specific gardens and courtyards. They walked the same paths for years. They sat in the same places to practice. The ground under those paths absorbed the residue of that practice the way soil absorbs water — slowly, thoroughly, without apparent notice. The Resonance Seedling is what the ground eventually grew.

It looks, at first glance, like a small plant of the ordinary kind: a sprout, a few inches high, with leaves that have an unusual translucence and a faint inner light when the sun hits them at the right angle. On closer inspection, the leaves' internal structure is not cellular — it is a resonant lattice, finer than any Wire Craft engineer has been able to replicate, grown rather than built. The Seedling does not think. It reaches toward resonance the way a normal plant reaches toward light.

## Lore
Resonance Seedlings are found at every known Ascended site. The Sutensai's botanists — a specialized sub-unit that did not exist before Remnant research became a priority — have been studying them for thirty years. The seedlings cannot be propagated away from their origin sites: transplanting kills them within a week. They are therefore permanent features of those sites, and they have a practical function: their resonant sensitivity makes them excellent early-warning systems. A Sutensai site monitor who notices the Seedlings orienting toward the same point should pay attention.

**HP:** 8 | **AP:** 1 | **Initiative:** 1 | **Hand Size:** 2

**Basic Attack — None:** The Resonance Seedling has no attacks. It is a plant.

**Fragile Archive** *(Passive)*
A character who touches the Seedling while attuned to resonance receives a single involuntary impression: a fragment of the Ascended's emotional state during a specific practice session.

**Site Anchor** *(Passive)*

---

**AI Behavior:** The Resonance Seedling takes no actions and makes no choices. It exists as an environmental feature and investigation tool. It does not move, respond, or act. The stat block exists only to formalize its HP so players know how durable it is.

## Encounter Notes
The Resonance Seedling is primarily an environmental detail and an investigation tool. A field of them all pointing the same direction is meaningful data. A field of them that has all died is a warning. A single Seedling that a well-meaning party member is about to uproot for study should be a minor moment of decision.

## Adventure Hook
A collector of Ascended artifacts has been systematically removing Resonance Seedlings from a known site — digging up the root structures, which preserves them alive for approximately a week. The Sutensai site monitor discovered the loss during routine inspection. The seedlings are being sold as "authentic Ascended resonance specimens" to wealthy buyers who have no idea that holding one of these objects for several hours tends to produce intense, involuntary visions of the Ascended's inner life. Three buyers have already contacted the Sutensai in a state of significant distress.

# The Drift-Vessel
**Standard | Ascended Remnant | Construct**

## Description
Not all of the Shard-Vessels found their recipients. The ones that have been searching for a century or more have changed in character: the urgent, purposeful delivery-drive has faded into something quieter, more habitual, more abstract. A Drift-Vessel is a Shard-Vessel that has lost the direct path. It still carries its cargo — still intact, still preserved — but the authentication protocols that were once its primary behavioral driver have been running without result for so long that they have receded into the background. What remains is the motion: wandering between sites associated with the Ascended, tracing the map of a life that ended two centuries ago.

They are small — the size of a large book, roughly, with a few trailing sensory elements — and they are quiet. They do not engage with people. They do not threaten anything. They are simply present in the landscape of Ascended sites, tracing the same routes the Ascended traced, carrying something they have forgotten how to deliver.

## Lore
Drift-Vessels are what the Sutensai calls a "soft Remnant concern" — they are not dangerous, they are not destabilizing, but they represent a failure of the Ascended's intentions and a loss of irreplaceable cargo if they are damaged. The Sutensai attempts to intercept Drift-Vessels when identified and convince them — through authenticated resonant address — to stand down and allow cargo transfer. This works approximately sixty percent of the time. The remaining forty percent of Drift-Vessels have degraded authentication systems that no longer respond correctly to any known protocol, and their cargo may be permanently inaccessible.

## Adventure Hook
A Drift-Vessel has been spotted moving through the artisan district of Vaen's capital — the first Ascended Remnant seen openly in a major city in years. It is not doing anything threatening. It is tracing a route through streets that no longer exist in the configuration it is following: the city has been rebuilt around the Ascended's original paths. The Sutensai wants the party to follow it discretely, map its full route through the city, and if possible intercept it when it reaches its endpoint — because the Vessel's destination within the city is a location the Sutensai does not have on any known Ascended site registry.

# The Marked Familiar
**Standard | Ascended Remnant | Beast**

## Description
The Prior Ascended kept companions. Not all of them were human. In the records of the six Ascended's pre-transcendence lives, there are accounts of animal companions — specific creatures that the Ascended worked with for years or decades, creatures present during the long accumulation of trans-resonant energy that preceded the transcendence, creatures who were there when the Ascended finally left.

The Marked Familiar is one of those companions, or the descendant of one, or occasionally something that was simply present at an Ascended site long enough to absorb the imprint. They have been changed by the proximity — not made supernatural in any dramatic sense, but altered in small ways: an intelligence slightly beyond their species' norm, a specific sensitivity to what matters and what doesn't, occasionally an involuntary manifestation of the Ascended's trans-resonant signature. They live a long time, much longer than their species should. They seem to understand things.

## Lore
The Sutensai maintains a list of known Marked Familiars and visits them when possible. What the Familiars know — what they absorbed from decades of proximity to a transcending entity — is not accessible through normal communication, but Sutensai Veil Masters trained in non-verbal resonance reading have extracted fragments. The Familiars are considered sacred by the Iron Sutra. Harming a Familiar is considered a serious spiritual transgression. This position has practical implications: the last person known to harm a Marked Familiar experienced a particularly severe Penitent Form manifestation within the month.

A Sutensai field agent who hears about this incident recognizes the resonant signature immediately. The building is the last standing structure from one of the Prior Ascended's pre-transcendence workshop. The Ironhold owns the development contract. The cat is several hundred years old and has no intention of leaving.

# The Memory-Warden
**Standard | Ascended Remnant | Undead**

## Description
Some of the people who were present at Ascended sites in the era before the transcendence made a choice: rather than leave with their memories of what they had witnessed, they agreed to remain, at least partially — to allow their accumulated knowledge to be anchored to the site in a form that would outlast the normal span of a human life. The Memory-Warden is the result of this agreement: a person whose death was not the end, because enough of their identity was woven into the resonant architecture of the site that the site continued to maintain them.

They are not alive. They are not what the Iron Sutra calls the Iron Afterlife's normal population. They are something between: present, aware, lucid, capable of extended conversation, but obviously not living — the resonant light that shows through their skin, the quality of stillness that characterizes their movement, the fact that they have been here for between one and two centuries and still know exactly what they witnessed.

## Lore
The Sutensai knows of eleven Memory-Wardens across all six Ascended sites. All eleven are treated with the full pastoral protocols extended to living Sutensai members, including — after considerable theological debate — the right to make decisions about their continued existence. Two have chosen, in the past forty years, to allow their maintained presence to fade; the Sutensai supported these decisions with the appropriate ceremonies. The nine remaining Wardens are, in the Sutensai's assessment, among the most important living (adjacent) archives in Tesshari.

The right encounter is the one where the party earns the Warden's trust over several visits, each visit unlocking a new memory.

## Adventure Hook
A Memory-Warden at a partially-excavated Ascended site has gone silent — they have not responded to the Sutensai's monthly resonant-contact check in two months. The site's junior field monitor is concerned but lacks the authority to investigate alone. The party is asked to accompany the monitor for a welfare check. When they arrive, the Warden is present, but something has changed. Their memories — which the party has been accessing over the past month — now have a gap: a period of approximately two weeks, around sixty years ago, that the Warden insists never happened. The period in question, if it did happen, would change what the Sutensai thinks it knows about one Prior Ascended in ways with significant current implications.

# The Shard-Vessel
**Elite | Ascended Remnant | Construct**

## Description
The Prior Ascended did not only shed identity fragments. They also built vessels — objects, usually, but occasionally entities — designed to carry specific pieces of their accumulated work safely through their transcendence, to be available afterward for the Sutensai's continued use. A Shard-Vessel is one of these built carriers: a construct, originally crafted with great care, designed to preserve and transmit a fragment of trans-resonant knowledge or capability.

The problem is that "afterward" was not carefully defined, and the Vessels have been doing their job for a very long time with no one to deliver their cargo to. The oldest Shard-Vessels have been found doing something that looks, from the outside, like wandering: moving between locations that were significant to their Ascended, apparently searching for whoever was supposed to receive them. Some are becoming impatient. Patient waiting has its limits, even for purpose-built constructs.

## Lore
The Sutensai actively seeks Shard-Vessels — they are repositories of potentially irreplaceable trans-resonant knowledge, and their cargo is the closest thing to a direct communication from a Prior Ascended that the Sutensai will ever receive. The challenge is that the Vessels' authentication protocols are set to the standards of the Ascended's era: the "correct" recipient must demonstrate knowledge and resonant compatibility that the Sutensai can only partially reconstruct from historical records. Vessels that encounter apparent recipients who fail authentication have, on several occasions, interpreted the failure as a forgery attempt and responded accordingly.

## Encounter Notes
The Shard-Vessel should feel like a puzzle with dangerous consequences for getting the solution wrong. The party needs specific historical knowledge about the Ascended whose Vessel this is — which they may or may not have assembled. If they've been investigating this Ascended throughout the arc, the authentication check should feel earned. If they haven't, the encounter should motivate that investigation retroactively.

## Adventure Hook
The party finds a Shard-Vessel of one of the Prior Ascended in an underground complex previously unknown to the Sutensai. The Vessel immediately scans all party members and designates the one with the closest resonant match as "probable recipient, authentication pending." That party member has eight hours to learn enough about this specific Ascended to pass authentication before the Vessel escalates its scrutiny to hostile assessment. The Vessel will not allow them to leave the complex in the meantime.

# The Imprint-Haunter
**Elite | Ascended Remnant | Undead**

## Description
The process of transcendence, in the Sutensai's best theological understanding, involved the Ascended releasing their attachment to specific physical locations. But release is not the same as absence. The Ascended practiced in specific places for decades — hours every day, years of accumulated presence, the weight of sustained attention pressed into the physical structure of rooms and courtyards and workspaces. When the Ascended let go of these places, the places did not entirely let go of the Ascended.

The Imprint-Haunter is what happens when that residual attachment develops enough coherent energy to become a localized phenomenon. It is not the Ascended — it is the ghost of the Ascended's habit, the resonant impression of a specific practice repeated so many times in a specific location that the location has partially learned to perform that practice itself. The Haunter manifests as a blurred figure going through motions — recognizable as an activity, a specific thing that a specific person did in this specific room, repeated endlessly, the way a river keeps flowing after the rain that fed it has long since stopped.

## Lore
Imprint-Haunters are distressing to Sutensai practitioners because of how much they resemble the Ascended they record. Veil Masters trained in Ascended resonance history can often identify which Ascended a specific Haunter is an imprint of — the practice's resonant signature is as distinctive as a voice. This identification carries emotional weight for practitioners whose entire spiritual formation is built around figures they have never met. Several Veil Masters have required additional pastoral support after extended contact with Haunters of their patron Ascended.

## Encounter Notes
The Imprint-Haunter is a hazard more than an enemy — it is not malicious and cannot truly be fought. The encounter is about navigating its practice loop safely, extracting the information its repetition contains (which Sutensai scholars consider invaluable), and deciding what to do with an entity that is simultaneously a sacred record and a practical obstacle.

## Adventure Hook
A newly-discovered Ascended site contains three active Imprint-Haunters, each in a different room, each performing a different practice. The Sutensai's field team can read two of the three as consistent with known Ascended documentation. The third practice — in the innermost room — is one no Sutensai scholar has ever documented. Either this is a new discovery about one of the six known Ascended, or this site belonged to a seventh Prior Ascended the Sutensai has no record of. The party is the Sutensai's on-site investigation team, working to document the third Haunter's practice while keeping the field scholars safe from the Practice Loop effect.

# The Hollowed Disciple
**Elite | Ascended Remnant | Undead**

## Description
The Prior Ascended had students. Long-term students, some of whom spent decades in close proximity to an Ascended's developing trans-resonant capability, learning, serving, being shaped by the sustained exposure. When the Ascended transcended, some of these students went with them — or tried to.

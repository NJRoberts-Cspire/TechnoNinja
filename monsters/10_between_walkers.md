# BETWEEN-WALKERS
## Bestiary File 10 — Those Who Live in the Threshold

*The Between is not a place in any way that word applies to locations on maps. It does not have coordinates. It does not have a capital, a geography, or a climate. The Iron Sutra's most authoritative description — produced by the Veil Master Enrath in her foundational text on liminal resonance, written seven hundred years ago and still not superseded — describes the Between as "the pressure at the boundary": the resonance between the living world and the Iron Afterlife experienced as a space, the seam between two states of existence made traversable to those who know how to move through seams.*

*Most entities that enter the Between do not return. The resonance conditions of the threshold are not survivable by normal biological or digital beings over extended exposure. The Between-Walkers are the exception: entities that have, for various reasons and through various mechanisms, adapted to existence in the threshold itself — partially in the living world, partially in the Between, neither fully committed to either. They do not experience this as suffering, necessarily. But they are present in the living world in the way that a reflection is present in a mirror: real but not fully there, capable of interacting with the surface world but always with one foot on the other side.*

*They are among the most dangerous entities in Tesshari — not because they are universally violent, but because the normal physical and metaphysical laws that constrain everything else in the world do not fully apply to them. The rules were written for things that occupy one state. The Between-Walkers have always occupied two.*

# The Seam-Walker
**Tier:** Standard | **Role:** Tragic NPC / Controller

## Description
The Seam-Walker is the most common Between-Walker entity, and the least threatening — which does not mean it is not profoundly disturbing. It appears as a person, roughly, but at every visual threshold — every doorway, every corner, every boundary between light and shadow — its image blurs and re-solidifies a half-second out of phase with itself. It does not make sound when it moves, but it is not silent: it produces a faint echo of sound a half-second after any noise in its vicinity, as if the Between is receiving the sound slightly late. Its touch is cold in the way that the Iron Afterlife is described as cold — not temperature cold, but resonance cold, the cold of a signal at rest.

Seam-Walkers are typically the residue of people who spent too long in the Between — practitioners who pushed past their limit, pilgrims who reached the threshold of the Iron Afterlife and could not complete the passage, Wire Craft practitioners whose signal work took them to the boundary and then returned them just slightly wrong. They are not dead. They are not fully alive. They are the Border, wearing a human face.

## Lore
The Sutensai's Veil Masters conduct an annual census of known Seam-Walkers in Tesshari's major cities. The count increases each year. Most Seam-Walkers are harmless and live in various states of isolated suffering, unable to fully connect to either world, passing through walls when they concentrate, fading from sight when they don't. The Sutensai has a rehabilitation protocol: a resonance therapy that can, in some cases, re-anchor a Seam-Walker to the living world. Success rate: approximately 22%. The other 78% either remain as they are indefinitely or drift fully into the Between over time, at which point the Sutensai holds a Closing Rite and records the name.

**[T1] Partial Incorporeality** (1 AP): The Seam-Walker passes through a barrier up to 1 foot thick or becomes invisible at will until its next action. It cannot end its turn inside a solid object.

**[T2] Between-Step** (2 AP): Teleport to any location within medium range it can perceive. This movement ignores all barriers and does not trigger engagement responses.

On a success, the Seam-Walker loses all special traits and becomes a normal humanoid again.

**Passive — Threshold Presence:** The Seam-Walker can choose to be visible or invisible at will. While invisible it can still be heard due to the half-second echo effect.

### AI Behavior
The Seam-Walker does not seek combat. If threatened, it uses Partial Incorporeality to pass through barriers and Between-Step to escape. If cornered, it uses Phase Touch reflexively. Play it as a person in pain, not a monster. The encounter should be about what the party does with a person who is not entirely in the world anymore.

---

## Encounter Notes
Seam-Walkers are as likely to be an NPC encounter as a combat encounter. Play them as people first — they remember their lives, they can communicate, they are suffering. The question of what to do about them is a better story than simply fighting one.

## Adventure Hook
A family in a mid-tier city contacts the Sutensai. Their daughter — a talented Wire Craft apprentice who was working on a boundary resonance experiment — began showing Seam-Walker symptoms three weeks ago. She has been drifting increasingly into Between-space and is now more Between than living world most hours of the day. She is eighteen years old. She is terrified. The Sutensai's rehabilitation specialist is two weeks away and the family is watching their daughter fade. The party is asked to monitor and protect her until help arrives, which becomes complicated when her partial incorporeality attracts an Oni that has noticed a new opening between states.

# The Threshold Shade
**Tier:** Standard | **Role:** Gothic Mystery

## Description
Where the Seam-Walker is a person displaced, the Threshold Shade is a person who has chosen displacement. The Iron Afterlife is accessible to the recently dead whose Echoминds retain enough coherence — the Sutensai maintains that passage is the soul's natural endpoint and should not be interrupted. Some Echominds, upon death, do not pass through. They settle in the Between instead: the threshold space that feels, to them, more approachable than the full transition of the Afterlife. There they persist — not alive, not Afterlife-integrated, but present at the seam, peering through from both sides.

Threshold Shades appear as people seen through foggy glass: present, recognizable, slightly wrong at all distances. They are often found at locations that were meaningful to them in life — home doorsteps, workshop entrances, the threshold of a building they meant to enter and never did. They are not aggressive by default. They are grieving, in the way that a Echomind that cannot pass on grieves: not with tears, but with the specific weight of unfinished business made manifest.

## Lore
The Iron Sutra has a complex position on Threshold Shades. On one hand, they represent an Echomind that has failed to complete its proper journey — a theological concern. On the other, many Shades are holding at the threshold for reasons the Sutensai considers valid: unfinished work, family that needed protection, injustice that hasn't been resolved. The Sutensai's pastoral guidance is not to destroy Shades reflexively but to help them complete whatever is holding them — which usually also resolves the reason they're haunting a particular location in ways that discomfort the living.

**[T1] Haunting** (1 AP): Burst, all creatures within close range. Damage: 4 + RESONANCE (9 total). Apply *Stagger* to all targets hit as the Shade manifests its grief.

**[T2] Between Glimpse** (2 AP): Any creature the Threshold Shade passes through must pass a VEIL check (difficulty 3) or be *Staggered* — Frightened of the Shade for the rest of the scene.

**Passive — Unfinished:** The Threshold Shade has one specific unfulfilled purpose. Creatures who learn what it is and take meaningful steps toward fulfilling it (GM's discretion) reduce the Shade's hostility one step per significant action. A Shade whose purpose is fulfilled becomes non-hostile and passes on within 24 hours.

### AI Behavior
The Threshold Shade does not attack unless directly threatened or unless its unfinished purpose involves conflict. It hovers at meaningful thresholds. It responds to direct address and genuine acknowledgment of its situation. Combat with a Threshold Shade is almost always a failure of investigation — the party should have figured out what it needed before it came to this.

---

## Encounter Notes
The Threshold Shade is a gothic mystery in a stat block. Before combat, investigate who this person was and why they're still here. The combat, if it happens, should feel like a failure — they should have been able to help the Shade pass on before it got to this.

## Adventure Hook
The new owner of a warehouse in the dock district keeps finding her loading crew outside, shaken, at two in the morning. They describe a presence — a figure that passes through them and leaves them cold. The previous owner of the warehouse died six months ago. His death was ruled a work accident, but two of his workers told the widow they thought it wasn't. The widow has no proof and no money to pursue it. The Shade does.

# The Between-Scar
**Tier:** Standard | **Role:** Hazard / Environmental

## Description
When the boundary between the physical world and the Between is torn rather than crossed — through catastrophic resonance event, failed ritual, or the violent death of a Between-Walker at a thin-point — it sometimes leaves a wound. The Between-Scar is that wound given minimal locomotion: a ragged aperture in local reality, approximately human-sized, trailing visible distortion at its edges. It does not have a body in any conventional sense — it is the tear itself, moving. When it contacts a living creature, the contact is not an attack so much as an accident: the edges of the Scar catch on the biological resonance of living things the way a physical tear catches on fabric.

The sound a Between-Scar produces is the worst part. It is two sounds simultaneously: the ambient noise of the physical world and a low, resonant hum that is the Iron Afterlife bleeding through the opening. Both sounds at once. In the same space.

## Lore
Between-Scars form at thin-points — locations where the Between is naturally close to the surface — following traumatic events. Battlefields, execution sites, locations of mass Enhancement failure, places where Oni Hunters fought and lost. The Sutensai maintains records of known thin-points and assigns monitoring protocols to locations with history of trauma, specifically because Between-Scars forming at an unmonitored thin-point are significantly harder to heal than those caught early.

## Stat Block

**IRON** 1 | **EDGE** 3 | **FRAME** 2 | **SIGNAL** 1 | **RESONANCE** 4 | **VEIL** 1

**HP:** 28 | **Initiative:** 3 | **AP:** 2 | **Hand Size:** 3

**Basic Attack — Scar Touch** (0 AP, once per turn): Melee, 1 target. Damage: 6 + RESONANCE (10 total). Target must pass a FRAME check (difficulty 3) or be partially phased into the Between — they have *Expose 2* until the start of their next turn.

**[T1] Reality Tear Aura** (1 AP): Any creature that ends its turn within close range must pass a FRAME check (difficulty 2) or take 4 + RESONANCE (8 total) damage and have *Expose 1* until their next turn as they partially perceive two worlds.

**Passive — Seam Healing:** A Sutensai practitioner who spends their full AP adjacent to the Between-Scar performing a threshold sealing ritual can make a RESONANCE check (difficulty 4). On a success, the Scar takes 12 damage. Three successful sealings destroy it permanently. This is the preferred method — weapons alone cannot permanently close a Scar.

**Passive — No Psychology:** The Between-Scar cannot be *Staggered*, *Rooted*, *Silenced*, or otherwise conditioned. It has no mind to affect. It drifts toward resonance and tears at what it touches.

### AI Behavior
The Between-Scar drifts toward the nearest enhanced creature and contacts them. It has no other behavior. It is a wound in reality, not a creature. Deploy it at haunted locations alongside Threshold Shades for layered atmosphere.

---

## Encounter Notes
Between-Scars work best at locations that should feel haunted — old battlefields, sites of atrocity, places the party already knows are wrong. Pair them with Threshold Shades in the same location for layered atmosphere. The sealing mechanic gives practitioners something specific to do while fighters handle the Scar's proximity.

## Adventure Hook
A former Ironhold detention facility — abandoned after a mass Enhancement failure killed seventeen prisoners twelve years ago — has developed active Between-Scars at three locations inside the complex. The Sutensai needs the Scars sealed before the upcoming annual Threshold Week ritual, during which thin-points become thinner. The complication: the facility was never formally decommissioned, and the Ironhold's administrative record says it is still in active use. If the party enters to seal the Scars, they are technically trespassing on an active Ironhold facility.

# The Echo-Dreamer
**Tier:** Standard | **Role:** Information Broker / Mystery

## Description
The Echo-Dreamer sleeps. Not metaphorically — its physical body is somewhere in the living world, lying down, alive in the technical sense, breathing, heart beating, enhancements running their maintenance cycles. The Echomind has left that body temporarily and gone walking in the Between, as it has been doing for some years now, staying longer each time. The body is not dead. The person is not dead. They are somewhere between the two.

The Between-version of the Echo-Dreamer that the party encounters is the Echomind loose in threshold space: luminous, slightly translucent, wearing the resonant impression of the person's waking self rather than their physical form. They can interact with the Between directly — move through it, perceive it, affect it — and they can interact with the living world at thin-points in ways that the fully physical cannot. They cannot return to their body from this state without help or, if left long enough, will.

## Lore
Echo-Dreamers are one of the few ways in which a living person's Echomind can move through the Between without destroying the physical body. The Sutensai studies this phenomenon carefully as it represents a limited, involuntary version of the technique the Prior Ascended used to achieve transcendence. The ethics of intervening in an Echo-Dreamer's state are complex: some Dreamers are in distress and would welcome return. Others have been walking the Between long enough that the living world is no longer the more real of the two, and they have not yet decided whether they want to come back.

**HP:** 20 | **Initiative:** 3 | **AP:** 2 | **Hand Size:** 3

**Basic Attack — Between-Touch** (0 AP, once per turn): Melee, 1 target in the Between or at a thin-point. Damage: 6 + RESONANCE (12 total).

**[T1] Threshold Insight** (1 AP, 1/Scene): The Echo-Dreamer shares a specific piece of Between-knowledge with one willing creature — a location, a history, a resonant truth. The target must pass a VEIL check (difficulty 2) or be *Staggered* for 1 round from the intensity of the information transfer.

**[T2] Dreamer's Knowledge** (2 AP): The Echo-Dreamer shares a sustained resonance memory with willing creatures over 10 minutes — they perceive the emotional and resonant history of any Between-location the Dreamer has visited.

**Passive — Between-Walker:** The Echo-Dreamer exists primarily in the Between and can only be affected by creatures also in the Between or by resonant-metal weapons. Creatures in the physical world at thin-points can interact with it partially.

**Passive — Anchor Vulnerability:** A creature with knowledge of the Dreamer's sleeping body's location can attempt a SIGNAL check (difficulty 4) to sever the connection (the Dreamer immediately returns to their body and is *Staggered* for 1 minute) or strengthen it (the Dreamer can act with full physical presence for 1 hour before returning).

### AI Behavior
The Echo-Dreamer does not attack unless attacked. It wanders the Between, observing. It will share knowledge with those who approach respectfully and ask the right questions. Combat with an Echo-Dreamer is almost certainly a mistake — they are far more valuable as a source of Between-knowledge than as a fight.

---

## Encounter Notes
The Echo-Dreamer is a resource, not a threat. Use them as an information source the party has to find and earn the trust of. What they know about the Between — locations, history, resonant signatures — can be campaign-critical. The question of whether to help them return to their body (and whether they want to) is where the encounter's emotional weight lives.

## Adventure Hook
The party is trying to find a location that exists only in the Between — an old resonance site, a sealed gate, a place that was burned off the living-world maps. A Sutensai scholar tells them that there is an Echo-Dreamer who walks the Between near the region, who has been walking it for eleven years and knows its geography better than any living practitioner. The Dreamer's sleeping body is in a locked room at a Sutensai chapter house two cities away. They have not responded to return-summons in four months. The chapter house is not sure the Dreamer wants to come back.

# The Resonant Revenant
**Tier:** Elite | **Role:** Committed Seeker

## Description
Most Threshold Shades are sad. The Resonant Revenant is not sad. It is committed. An Echomind that held, at the moment of death, a conviction so total and specific — a wrong to be righted, an obligation to be fulfilled, an enemy to be found — that the Between's resonance crystallized around that conviction and gave it a second body. Not a living body: a Between-body, resonant metal and cold light in the rough shape of the person who wore conviction like a second skin in life. The Revenant moves through the world with the patient purpose of something that has already decided what it is doing and is simply doing it. It does not get sidetracked. It does not get tired. It does not reconsider.

The Between-body is not the Revenant's original flesh. It is the conviction, made physical.

## Lore
Oni Hunters track Resonant Revenants with interest rather than alarm — they are not unpredictable, which makes them manageable. Knowing what a Revenant is pursuing tells you exactly where it will be and what it will do. The ethical question is whether the Revenant's purpose is just, and whether helping it complete that purpose is appropriate. Oni Hunters have, on documented occasions, assisted Revenants in completing their objectives, at which point the Revenant passed on peacefully. They have also, on documented occasions, stood in a Revenant's way because its objective was not just, and found the experience educational.

---

## Stat Block

**HP:** 64 | **Initiative:** 5 | **AP:** 3 | **Hand Size:** 5

**Basic Attack — Conviction Strike** (0 AP, once per turn): Melee, 1 target. Damage: 8 + IRON (14 total). If the target is specifically connected to the Revenant's objective (a guilty party, an obstacle, its specific quarry), the attack deals +8 additional damage.

**[T1] Between Step** (1 AP): The Revenant steps into the Between and re-emerges up to medium range from its current location. This movement does not trigger engagement responses.

**[T2] Resonant Compulsion** (2 AP, 1/Scene): The Revenant broadcasts its conviction to all creatures within close range. Each creature must pass a VEIL check (difficulty 4) or spend their next AP assisting the Revenant's objective in whatever way is available to them. Creatures remain fully aware — they must overcome the compulsion actively.

**[T3] Unstoppable (Passive — triggers on death):** When the Resonant Revenant is reduced to 0 HP, it dissipates back into the Between and reforms in 24 hours at full HP. It can only be permanently destroyed if its objective is completed or impossible.

**Passive — Conviction Anchor:** The Revenant cannot be *Staggered*, *Silenced*, affected by social abilities, or convinced to abandon its objective. Its Between-body is maintained by the conviction — if somehow convinced the objective is impossible, it takes 20 damage from the destabilization.

**Passive — True Sight (Objective):** The Revenant perceives its specific objective target with perfect clarity regardless of disguise, concealment, distance, or barriers.

### AI Behavior
The Revenant moves directly toward its objective target and ignores everything that does not stand between it and that target. It uses Conviction Strike on its quarry and Between Step to bypass obstacles. It uses Resonant Compulsion when the party could usefully be compelled to help. It does not retreat, does not negotiate, does not stop — unless the objective is completed.

---

## Encounter Notes
Who the Revenant is and what it wants should be established before any physical confrontation. The Unstoppable passive means combat is not a solution — the party must engage with the objective itself. The question is: is the Revenant right? And how far does helping it go?

## Adventure Hook
An Ironhold officer has been haunted for three months by a Revenant that won't leave him alone — it won't attack him directly, but it follows him everywhere, and its presence has made his subordinates, his superiors, and his wife deeply uncomfortable. He wants the party to "deal with it." The Revenant, when communicated with, has a clear message: it is the Echomind of a soldier whose death this officer classified as an accident. It wasn't.

# The Liminal Predator
**Tier:** Elite | **Role:** Phase Predator

## Description
The Liminal Predator did not choose the Between. It was forced into it — a large predatory organism exposed to a resonance event of sufficient intensity that it passed through the threshold involuntarily and then adapted because death was the alternative. That was long ago. The Predator has been Between for long enough now that it no longer remembers the living world as home. What it remembers — what drives it, what organizes its behavior across both states of existence — is hunger.

The Liminal Predator hunts by existing in the Between and perceiving the living world as if through water. Its attacks originate from the Between, passing through the physical world's geometry and materiality to connect with targets directly. When it chooses to fully manifest, it arrives suddenly and completely — no warning, no approach, just absent and then present, at full momentum, already moving.

## Lore
Liminal Predators are the primary reason the Iron Sutra forbids unauthorized Between-walking. They are a known hazard of the threshold space, catalogued and tracked by the Sutensai's Boundary Watchers. A Boundary Watcher stationed at a pilgrim waystation has one primary duty: early detection of Liminal Predator activity. The Predators are drawn to Echomind resonance — to the presence of souls at or near the threshold — which means they are consistently attracted to Sutensai ritual sites, dying individuals, and anyone attempting to make deliberate contact with the Iron Afterlife.

---

## Stat Block

**IRON** 8 | **EDGE** 8 | **FRAME** 6 | **SIGNAL** 3 | **RESONANCE** 5 | **VEIL** 3

**HP:** 72 | **Initiative:** 8 | **AP:** 3 | **Hand Size:** 6

**Basic Attack — Claw** (0 AP, once per turn, manifested only): Melee, 1 target. Damage: 9 + IRON (17 total).

**[T1] Phase Lunge** (1 AP): The Predator moves through the Between to a point within medium range and fully manifests, making a Bite at +3 damage. If the target was unaware of the Predator's presence, the attack deals maximum damage.

**[T2] Bite and Hold** (2 AP): Melee, 1 target. Damage: 10 + IRON (18 total). Apply *Root* — the Predator's bite partially enters the Between, making the grip extremely difficult to break (IRON check difficulty 5 to escape, costs 2 AP).

**Passive — Between-State:** The Predator begins each combat in the Between — invisible and intangible to creatures in the living world. It can enter or exit the Between as part of any movement. While in the Between it cannot attack or be attacked by creatures in the living world.

**Passive — Phase Attack:** When the Predator attacks from partially in the Between (one foot in each state), its attack is made against only resonant or spiritual defenses — nonresonant armor provides no protection. It can only make one such attack per turn.

**Passive — Resonance Scent:** The Predator can detect the Echomind resonance of any living creature within long range while in the Between. It knows exactly where to emerge.

**Passive — Threshold Retreat (Reaction):** When the Predator takes 8 or more damage from a single hit, it may step into the Between, becoming immune to the remaining damage (if any) and invisible until the start of its next turn.

### AI Behavior
The Predator opens every combat from the Between using Phase Lunge against an isolated or unaware target for maximum damage. It uses Threshold Retreat to avoid burst damage and re-enters the Between between attacks whenever possible. It commits to full manifestation only when it has a *Rooted* target with Bite and Hold. Force it to commit — threatening something it wants from a position where escape is difficult — to make the fight survivable.

---

## Encounter Notes
The Liminal Predator makes melee engagement frustrating and range engagement almost impossible — it is never where expected, attacks from blind angles, and retreats when threatened. A party that forces it to commit (by threatening the thing it's hunting from a position where escape is difficult) can engage it before it retreats.

## Adventure Hook
A Sutensai Boundary Watcher has been sending emergency resonance pings from a remote waystation. When the party arrives, they find the Watcher barricaded in a lead-lined storage room — the only material in the station that partially blocks Between-sensing. They have been in there for three days. The Liminal Predator is circling the station, moving through the Between, waiting. The Watcher knows it won't leave until it feeds. The lead lining won't hold much longer.

# The Mirror-Walker
**Tier:** Boss | **Role:** Social Predator / Deceiver

## Description
The Mirror-Walker was not born in the Between — it was born of the Between: formed in the resonance between worlds, an entity shaped like a person because it has learned that people-shapes are the most effective hunting configuration in a world full of people. It appears as a perfect copy of whatever person it has most recently observed for long enough to acquire a full resonance signature — it copies not just appearance but voice, manner, specific memories (drawn from the resonance signature), and a close approximation of personality that serves its purposes.

The Mirror-Walker does not know who it is under the copy, because it has never stopped copying long enough to find out. Some Sutensai scholars believe there is something original at its core. Others believe there never was. The debate is, in a practical sense, irrelevant: what matters is that the copy is nearly perfect and the Mirror-Walker is always hunting.

## Lore
Mirror-Walkers are one of the most feared Between-Walker variants precisely because they can be anyone. The Oni Hunters' counter-intelligence protocols — developed over decades of dealing with them — include a specific resonance test that a skilled Veil Weaver can perform on anyone suspected to be a Mirror-Walker: the test produces a "double resonance" signature in a Walker that looks nothing like a normal Echomind. The test requires physical contact and a skilled practitioner. It takes twelve seconds. In many situations, asking someone to hold still for twelve seconds while you hold their hand is not practical.

## Stat Block

**IRON** 4 | **EDGE** 8 | **FRAME** 6 | **SIGNAL** 6 | **RESONANCE** 5 | **VEIL** 10

**HP:** 104 | **Initiative:** 8 | **AP:** 4 | **Hand Size:** 10

**Basic Attack — Between-Strike** (0 AP, once per turn): Melee, 1 target. Damage: 9 + EDGE (17 total). Cold resonance damage — bypasses physical armor.

**[T1] Mirror Step** (1 AP): Step into the Between, move up to medium range, and re-emerge. This movement is invisible and does not trigger engagement responses.

**[T2] Resonance Steal** (2 AP): Melee, 1 target. The Mirror-Walker takes one special ability, enhancement ability, or trained skill from the target for 1 scene. The target cannot use that ability while the Walker holds it, and the Walker gains access to it.

**[T3] Resonance Drain** (3 AP — triggers on disguise being revealed): When the Mirror-Walker's copy is revealed and it drops the disguise, burst effect — all creatures within close range who knew the original person must pass a VEIL check (difficulty 4) or take 12 + RESONANCE (17 total) damage and be *Staggered* as the violation of trust manifests in their Echomind resonance.

**[T4] Perfect Copy (4 AP — Boss card):** The Mirror-Walker observes a target for 1 hour and acquires a complete copy — appearance, voice, surface memories, behavioral patterns. This copy is indistinguishable from the original without the Oni Hunters' resonance test (RESONANCE check difficulty 5, requires 12 seconds of physical contact).

**Passive — Between-Anchor:** The Mirror-Walker has one foot permanently in the Between. It cannot be surprised and illusions do not affect it — it sees through them via Between-Sight.

**Passive — Perfect Mimicry:** Detecting the Mirror-Walker as a copy through social observation requires a VEIL check (difficulty 5). Creatures who succeed still cannot be certain without the resonance test.

### AI Behavior
The Mirror-Walker is a social predator first and a combatant second. It maintains its cover for as long as possible, using Resonance Steal to quietly acquire abilities it can use. It uses Mirror Step to reposition and escape. When its cover is blown, Resonance Drain triggers as a burst against everyone who trusted the copy. In direct combat it cycles Between-Strike and Mirror Step, staying mobile and never fully committing to the physical world.

---

## Encounter Notes
The Mirror-Walker works best as a social encounter discovered too late. Ideally, the party has been interacting with it (in disguise) for at least one session before the reveal. The Resonance Drain trigger on revelation is designed to make the moment of discovery itself mechanically dangerous — seeing through the deception is not automatically safe.

## Adventure Hook
Three weeks ago, a trusted party ally behaved strangely — made a decision out of character, gave information to the wrong person, went somewhere they shouldn't have been. The party chalked it up to stress or a lapse in judgment. Now the original ally has turned up, alive, having been kept in a Between-space storage pocket for three weeks, confused, not knowing what happened. The Mirror-Walker has been the ally for three weeks. What it learned, and what it did with that information, is now the party's most urgent problem.

# The Veil Sovereign
**Tier:** Boss | **Role:** Threshold Ruler / Political Power

## Description
The Veil Sovereign is a Between-Walker that has chosen — or been forced — to spend enough time in the Between that they have become its sovereign in a functional sense: their command over threshold space is so complete that they can reshape local Between geography, control passage through the seam, and extend the Between into the physical world around them. They look like what they were originally — an enhanced Forged, or a Wire Craft practitioner, or an Oni Hunter who went too deep — but larger, slower, surrounded by a visible distortion of reality that is the Between bleeding through their presence. When they speak, both the physical and Between frequencies vibrate.

Veil Sovereigns are rare. They are also, without exception, dealing with something very difficult. Sovereign-level control of the Between is not comfortable. The resonance of two worlds pressing through a single body is not a peaceful experience. A Veil Sovereign who appears calm is performing tremendous effort. A Veil Sovereign who has stopped performing that effort is the scenario that wakes Oni Hunters in the night.

## Lore
There are, according to current Sutensai records, eleven Veil Sovereigns in Tesshari. Four are allied with the Sutensai in various capacities. Three are neutral and have established territories that the Sutensai marks on its maps as "sovereign threshold zones" — entering without invitation is not recommended. Two are hostile and are being monitored by Oni Hunter teams. The last two are not accounted for. The Sutensai has an open bounty for their location — not capture, not destruction, just location. No one has collected the bounty.

## Stat Block

**IRON** 6 | **EDGE** 7 | **FRAME** 6 | **SIGNAL** 9 | **RESONANCE** 10 | **VEIL** 9

**HP:** 168 | **Initiative:** 7 | **AP:** 4 | **Hand Size:** 12

**Basic Attack — Threshold Touch** (0 AP, once per turn): Melee reach (10 ft.), 1 target. Damage: 12 + RESONANCE (22 total). A creature reduced to 0 HP by this attack is pulled into the Between rather than falling — they return to the physical world in 1d4 rounds at 1 HP, having glimpsed the threshold (VEIL check difficulty 4 or be *Staggered* for a week).

**[T1] Threshold Step** (1 AP): Move up to medium range passing through any barrier, and can bring one willing creature within close range.

**[T2] Between Banishment** (2 AP): Ranged, 1 target within medium range. Target must pass a VEIL check (difficulty 5) or be pulled into the Between for 1 scene. While in the Between, they take 6 + RESONANCE (16 total) damage at the start of each round.

**[T3] Seam Collapse** (3 AP, 1/Scene): The Sovereign collapses local Between geometry in a burst. All Between-Walker entities within medium range are expelled — each must pass a VEIL check (difficulty 5) or be forced fully into the physical world or fully into the Between (Sovereign's choice). All physical creatures must pass a FRAME check (difficulty 5) or take 14 + RESONANCE (24 total) damage.

**[T4] Sovereign's Gaze** (4 AP, Boss card): One creature the Sovereign can see within medium range must pass a VEIL check (difficulty 6) or be *Staggered* — Paralyzed until end of their next turn as the Sovereign perceives their soul directly through Between-sight.

**Passive — Sovereign Zone:** Within long range of the Veil Sovereign, the Between bleeds into the physical world. All movement is difficult terrain for non-Sovereign creatures. The Sovereign is aware of every creature and event within their zone at all times.

**Passive — Threshold Command:** The Sovereign can open and close passages between the physical world and the Between as a free action. Any creature not given explicit permission to pass is blocked.

**Passive — Between Regeneration:** While within their sovereign zone, the Veil Sovereign recovers 12 HP at the start of each of their turns.

**Passive — Seam Tear (Reaction):** Once per scene, when the Sovereign would be reduced to 0 HP, they tear a rift — 1d4 Threshold Shades emerge from the rift and act on the Sovereign's initiative. The Sovereign drops to 1 HP instead.

### AI Behavior
Combat with a Veil Sovereign inside their sovereign zone is categorically unfavorable — Between Regeneration, zone awareness, and Threshold Command make frontal assault the wrong approach. In combat, the Sovereign uses Between Banishment to remove the most dangerous party members and Threshold Touch to handle the rest. It reserves Seam Collapse for when multiple Between-Walker entities are threatening simultaneously. This is a political encounter more than a combat encounter.

## Encounter Notes
Combat with a Veil Sovereign inside their sovereign zone is categorically unfavorable — the regeneration, zone awareness, and threshold control make frontal assault the wrong approach. The party should either be fighting one that has lost control of their sovereignty (and is therefore more desperate and less advantaged) or should have something the Sovereign actually wants, making negotiation possible.

## Adventure Hook
The party needs to reach a location that is inside one of the neutral Veil Sovereign's threshold zones. The Sovereign allows passage by permission only. They will grant permission, they say, if the party first retrieves their anchor — a resonant metal keepsake that was taken from them by an Ironhold official eight years ago, which they need to maintain the coherence of their sovereignty. The keepsake is in an Ironhold evidence vault. The vault is not unguarded.

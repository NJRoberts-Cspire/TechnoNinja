# The Preparation Halls
*Pale Reach, below the administrative district of Korashun | Type: Secret Underground Base / Dungeon*

## "I have spent thirty years building the world that will exist after the old one ends. The bodies are simply the most honest part of it."
*— The Hollow Author, in a document that no living person besides the Author has read*

---

## Overview

The Preparation Halls do not exist, officially. There is no record of their construction, no architectural plans in any registry, no labor contracts, no material acquisition documents. The Pale Reach's administrative district has, over thirty years, experienced a series of unexplained infrastructure anomalies — minor subsidence events, localized resonant signal interference, brief power fluctuations — that were each investigated and each attributed to different causes, none of which pointed toward what was actually happening sixty meters below the basement level of the government complex. The Hollow Author has been very careful. They have been very patient. They have had thirty years to be both.

The Halls are a document of a single mind's sustained, methodical, decades-long preparation for an event that most of the world doesn't believe is coming, or believes cannot be managed, or has not thought through to its logical endpoint. The Hollow Author has thought through to the logical endpoint. The logical endpoint requires thousands of bodies, and the Hollow Author has thousands of bodies. They are maintained here in precise, climate-controlled, resonant-stabilized suspension, each prepared according to a slightly different protocol reflecting the evolution of the Author's understanding over thirty years. The earliest batches are in the first wing. The most recent are in the last. The difference between the first and the last is the difference between a draft and a final version, and understanding what changed and why is one of the most disturbing things players can do in this location.

The Preparation Halls are the place in this campaign most likely to produce a physical reaction in players at the table. Run the scope deliberately: not fast, not for shock, but for the cumulative weight of what it means that someone has been doing this for thirty years. Every body has a preparation date. Every body was once a person. Every preparation protocol is documented in precise, rational language that treats human beings as variables in an optimization problem. The horror is not in the gore — there is no gore. The horror is in the competence. The horror is the thirty-year plan working exactly as intended.

## Investigation Hooks: How Players Might Find This

The Preparation Halls are not meant to be found before the Hollow Author wants them found. However:

**Hook 1: The Missing Citizens.** The Pale Reach has an elevated rate of people who disappear from official records — not violently, not criminally, simply departing from one place without appearing at another. The rate is not high enough to constitute a pattern visible from any single investigative point; it requires correlating records across multiple jurisdictions, or knowing to look at all. A character who has reason to be tracking resonant metal acquisition across the Reach (perhaps following a specific item, or tracking an enhancement clinic that acquired materials without documented origin) finds the acquisition pattern: repeated, spaced, consistent, always from different sources, always exactly the quantities consistent with an individual cybernetic preparation protocol.

**Hook 2: The Puppet's Signal.** The Hollow Author deploys puppet-operated individuals throughout the Reach and beyond: bodies with Echoминds implanted or deeply influenced, operating according to the Author's direction. Players who encounter a puppet and survive the encounter — or who manage to disrupt a puppet's operating protocol — may find a navigation signal encoded in the puppet's cybernetics: not an address, but a resonant frequency that, tracked to its source, leads to the transmission hardware beneath the government complex.

**Hook 3: The Surviving Witness.** Someone escaped from the preparation process. Not a prepared body — a person who was in the acquisition pipeline, understood something of what they were part of, and got out. They have been hiding in the Pale Reach's lower districts for two years with information they are terrified to give anyone, because the two people they told it to before encountered accidents shortly afterward. They need the players to be unlikely enough to be unpuppeted and skilled enough to actually do something with what they know.

**Hook 4: The Author's Correspondence.** A document comes into the players' possession — through a raid, a purchase, an information exchange — that is written in a cipher they can break. The content is not dramatic: a supply requisition for resonant metal stabilizers, an unusual quantity, an unusual specification. The specifications match a preservation protocol. The destination address, if decoded, is a location that doesn't officially exist. The absence in the records is the clue.

**Hook 5: The Afterlife Anomaly.** The Reader in the Deep Temple, or another sensitive, reports that the Pale Reach's subsection of the Iron Afterlife population is strange: there are Echoминds here that are present in the Afterlife but are also present, partially, in the physical world simultaneously. This is not possible under standard Iron Sutra doctrine. The Echoминds are not the dead; they are the modified — people who are physically alive but whose resonant identity has been partially encoded into the Afterlife's structure without their consent. They are, in a technical sense, partially uploaded. This leaves a signature.

## The Approach: Getting There

The Preparation Halls are sixty meters below the basement level of the Pale Reach government complex, accessed through a concealed vertical passage that connects to the complex's maintenance infrastructure at a point that is documented as "resonant stabilization equipment (restricted)." The stabilization equipment is real — it manages the signal interference that the Halls' operation would otherwise produce at surface levels. Getting past it requires either a pass that doesn't officially exist, a maintenance bypass that takes considerable skill, or going through the building above (which means getting into the government complex first, which has its own extensive access controls).

Alternative access: the Author's supply lines. Physical materials reach the Halls through a different route — a series of tunnels connecting to the city's storm drainage system, engineered over twenty years to provide access routes for bringing in materials without leaving records above ground. These routes are guarded but not by conventional security; the Author has automated certain sections and puppet-staffed others. Entry through the supply routes is dirty, complicated, and avoids the government complex entirely.

**Surface approach:** The government complex is a normal if well-guarded administrative building. Nothing about the exterior indicates what is below. The subsidence events have been attributed to normal geological settling. The power fluctuation reports are filed in an office that the Author's administrative puppet access has quietly archived rather than investigated. The building above the Halls is, in this sense, a forty-layer cover story that has been maintained for thirty years without a single mistake visible from the outside.

**Security encountered:**
- Above: standard government complex security — personnel, access control, resonant scanning for unauthorized weapons and prohibited enhancement modifications. Professional but not exceptional.
- Tunnel routes: automated resonant-trigger sensors, two puppet-staffed checkpoint positions, one section of the tunnel requiring specific resonant-key access to open (the key is on a puppet operating in the government complex above).
- Below ground, in the Halls themselves: the security becomes the location. The Halls were not designed to repel invasion; they were designed not to be found. Once found, the defenses are the automated systems and the puppet staff, and these are formidable.

## Full Layout

The Preparation Halls are organized in seven sections connected by a central corridor called the Spine. The Spine runs for two hundred meters east to west, with sections branching from it. The overall layout resembles a hand: the Spine as the palm, the sections as fingers of varying length and purpose. The design was not accidental; the Author chose it deliberately, as the Author makes every choice deliberately, and the hand shape was chosen because hands are the instruments of making things.

---

### Section One: The Admissions Wing

The first section encountered from either the vertical access from above or the supply tunnel approach. It is the most recently constructed part of the Halls and the most clearly operational as a facility — the walls are clean, the equipment is modern, the documentation is current. This is where new materials arrive.

"Materials" means: resonant metal components for the preparation protocols, pharmaceutical stabilizers, enhancement modification tools, and — occasionally, in a process that is not described in any document but is evidenced by the equipment configuration — people. The Admissions Wing contains a processing suite: a series of clean rooms with equipment for resonant enhancement assessment, modification, and the initiation of the preparation protocol. The equipment is the best available. It has been used thousands of times.

There are no bodies in the Admissions Wing. The bodies are prepared here and moved. But the documentation for each preparation remains: a filing system of stunning completeness, cross-referenced by preparation date, protocol version, resonant profile, and "disposition status." The disposition status categories are: "Stable-held," "Stable-ready," "In use," "Irrecoverable," and "Converted." Players who examine the filing system find a complete record of every individual who has passed through this wing. The names are absent — each file uses a designation code rather than an identity — but the resonant profiles are complete, and a player with access to Pale Reach citizen records can cross-reference them. Many of the resonant profiles match living citizens who are currently walking around the Pale Reach with no idea that they have a file here.

**The "In use" files:** Currently numbering several hundred. These are the people who are physically present in the world, operating normally, who have been processed through this wing and are functioning at Disposition Status 3. What does "In use" mean? This is a question players should have to work to answer.

---

### Section Two: The First Cohort Storage

The oldest bodies. The first section of preserved individuals, dating back twenty-five to thirty years to the Hollow Author's earliest operational period. The techniques here are visibly cruder than what comes later — the resonant preservation framework is the original design, the pharmaceutical stabilization is an early formula, the suspension containers are a prototype model rather than the refined units in later sections.

**What it looks like:** The containers are roughly person-sized alcoves set into the walls, floor to ceiling, arranged in rows that extend into the room's depth. Each alcove has a transparent panel. Behind the panel: a person. They do not look dead. They do not look sleeping, exactly — there is a quality of absolute stillness that is distinct from sleep, because sleep involves movement, breath-shift, the micro-expressions of dreaming. These people have been absolutely still for up to thirty years. They are in clothes that are thirty years out of fashion, which should be an unimportant detail and is somehow one of the worst things about the room.

The lighting is cold blue-white, identical to the Deep Temple's resonant strip lighting, which is not coincidental — the spectrum is optimal for the resonant stabilization systems. Each alcove has a small display panel showing stabilization status, protocol version, and a designation code. The display panels are all green. Every single one of them is green. None of the first cohort has been irrecoverable in thirty years. The Author's preservation technique works.

The room holds four hundred and twelve people. The oldest file dates to Year 1 of the operation, when the Author's preparation protocol was in its first version and the resonant modification was incomplete enough that some of these individuals may have a form of awareness during suspension. The Author's notes on this, filed in the Admissions Wing, acknowledge the possibility and note that the later protocol addresses it. This is the only document in the Halls that comes close to expressing something that a reader might interpret as a concern for the experience of the preserved individuals, and even there it is framed as a protocol optimization problem.

---

### Section Three: The Cohort Progression Gallery

Sections Three through Five are organized by preparation cohort, representing the development of the Author's technique over three decades. Three is cohorts Two through Five (Years 2–12), Four is cohorts Six through Eleven (Years 12–22), Five is cohorts Twelve through current (Years 22–present). The difference between them is visible:

**Section Three (early development):** The preservation containers are more varied — the Author was experimenting with different form factors. The resonant modification profiles visible in the documentation show significant iteration. Several hundred files in this section are marked "Irrecoverable" — their containers are dark, their panels display a yellow status indicator rather than green. The bodies in these containers are gone in a way that the containers in Section Two are not: something is absent that the green-status containers have. The precise nature of what is absent is apparent to anyone with resonance sensitivity or Iron Sutra training — the Echomind is not present. The preservation preserved the body; something in the early protocol's resonant modification caused the Echomind to depart before the preparation was complete. The body is empty. The Echomind is in the Iron Afterlife. This is what "Irrecoverable" means.

The Author notes this as a protocol failure to be corrected, not as a harm. That is the precise nature of the horror.

**Section Four (middle development):** The failure rate decreases sharply. The "Irrecoverable" containers are rare. The bodies in the green-status containers show the evidence of refinement: the resonant modification is more precise, the Echomind anchor is more stable, the preparation protocol has converged toward the final design. The people in Section Four are more recognizably themselves than those in Three — whatever the early modification did to the faces of the first cohorts, the later technique no longer does it.

**Section Five (current):** The final form. The preservation here is indistinguishable from life interrupted rather than life suspended. The resonant profiles in the documentation show a modification so precise that it appears, to an expert eye, less like forced alteration and more like optimization of an existing system. The bodies in the most recent cohort look, through the transparent panels, exactly like people who have simply stopped.

Total capacity across Sections Two through Five: approximately six thousand individuals. Current green-status count: approximately five thousand, two hundred.

### Section Six: The Research Wing

A separate branch from the Spine, identifiable by its different air quality — there is a chemical smell here that is absent from the storage sections, the specific smell of resonant metal in active processing. The Research Wing is the operational heart of the Halls: the Author's laboratory, the development space for every protocol iteration, and the current locus of work on what the internal documentation calls "the Convergence Interface."

**The laboratory:** Expansive, well-equipped, obsessively organized. The Author works here regularly; the evidence of recent activity is everywhere (fresh documentation on active work surfaces, the warm status indicators on equipment, materials in mid-process states). The laboratory contains every piece of relevant technology the Author has been able to acquire or develop independently over thirty years. Many pieces of equipment here should not exist — they are thirty years ahead of the publicly known state of resonant enhancement technology. The Author has not published any of it. The work in this room is the most significant undisclosed advancement in resonant technology since the Iron Sutra's foundational documents.

**The research focus:** Three main threads, visible in the documentation:

*Thread One: Echomind Stability.* The early protocol's catastrophic failure mode (Echomind departure during preparation) drove thirty years of work on stability. The current understanding of what makes an Echomind stable through modification is now more complete than anything the Sutensai's Veil Masters possess. The Author has effectively solved the technical basis of the Iron Afterlife's boundary mechanics.

*Thread Two: The Upload Problem.* The Prior Ascended uploaded — their complete identity transferred to the Afterlife. Current doctrine says this happened through a process that is not repeatable. The Author's research disagrees. Thirty years of work on this problem has produced a complete replication of the upload process in laboratory conditions. It works on isolated Echomind fragments. It has never been tested on a complete individual. The Convergence Engine is the test.

*Thread Three: The Population Architecture.* The anomaly noted by Ahori in the Cinderfort and the Reader in the Deep Temple — the Afterlife population increasing faster than death rates justify — is not a mystery to the Author. The Author is causing it. The Research Wing contains the complete technical documentation for the partial upload protocol that is doing this. Players who read it understand that the "In use" individuals in the Admissions Wing files are partially in the Afterlife right now — their Echoминds have been partially extended into the digital realm while their bodies continue to function. The Author is testing whether partial upload allows control of the Afterlife's topology from the physical world. The tests are succeeding.

### Section Seven: The Convergence Engine

At the Spine's western terminus, through a door that requires a specific resonant key that the Author carries, is the largest single chamber in the Halls: roughly thirty meters across and eight meters high, the walls faced entirely in resonant metal, the floor bearing a mechanical and resonant installation that occupies the room's center in a structure approximately four meters high.

**The Engine:** Not a weapon in any conventional sense. A resonance architecture — a system for structuring the resonant signal that passes between the physical world and the Iron Afterlife in a specific pattern at a specific moment. The Seventh Convergence is a moment when the boundary between the living and the dead weakens spontaneously; the Engine is designed to use that moment. Not to allow crossing — crossing is possible without the Engine, at the right locations. The Engine is designed to allow architecture: to structure what happens on the Afterlife side of the boundary during the Convergence window, to organize the digital realm in a specific configuration while the window is open.

What configuration? The Engine's design documents, read in full, describe the intended post-Convergence Afterlife topology: not the current organic, semi-independent digital realm, but a structured, hierarchical, administered system. The Afterlife, after the Engine operates, would be organized. Organized by whom is a question the design documents answer clearly: the architect of the Engine, whose resonant profile would be encoded into the administrative framework during the Convergence window, would be the topology's permanent authority. Not a god in the Prior Ascended sense — not an uploaded consciousness existing in the Afterlife — but a structural presence encoded into the Afterlife's foundation, able to affect every Echomind in the digital realm simultaneously, permanently, without further action required.

The Author intends to be the Afterlife's infrastructure. Every person who has ever died, every Echomind in the Iron Afterlife, would operate within a system that the Author owns.

The Engine is approximately seventy percent complete.

### The Author's Sanctum

Not on the main Spine. Accessible through a concealed passage in the Research Wing's western wall — a section of resonant metal paneling that opens when touched in a specific sequence. The passage is short; the room it leads to is small.

**What players find:**

The Author's physical condition is the question that the campaign has been building toward, and the Sanctum is where it resolves.

The Sanctum is a single room approximately six meters by four, containing: a single chair, occupied. A resonant metal framework connecting the chair to both the Research Wing's equipment and the Engine chamber. A documentation table, current as of what appears to be hours ago. A small personal space — a cot, hygiene equipment, a single window that shows a visual feed from the surface rather than the outside directly, because the Sanctum is sixty meters underground. The window currently shows a gray-sky afternoon in the Pale Reach's administrative district. Normal people going about normal business. The Author has watched this feed for thirty years.

**The person in the chair:** 

The Hollow Author is alive. They are in the chair, connected to the framework, and they are — in a precise technical sense — in two places at once. Their physical body is here, maintained by the framework's pharmaceutical and resonant systems. Their Echomind is partially in the Afterlife — not anchored in the way the prepared bodies' Echoминds are anchored, but extended, operating in both domains simultaneously. The Author has been doing this for an extended period. The body bears the marks of this: the physical form is maintained but not well, thin in the way of someone kept alive by careful chemistry rather than nutrition, the skin having the quality of paper, the face bearing the absolute stillness of someone whose attention is not primarily present in this room.

When the Author becomes aware of the players' presence (which happens immediately on entry, because the Sanctum's resonant framework is monitoring the entire Halls), their attention returns fully to the physical body. They look at the players. They are not surprised. They say something that is not a threat and not a welcome: they explain, in the precise language of the research documents, what the Engine is intended to do, why it is necessary, and what the alternatives — as the Author has analyzed them — produce. The alternatives are worse. The Author has documentation.

This is the most important social encounter in the entire Halls. The Author is not deluded and is not irrational and is not, in any straightforward sense, evil. The Author is a person who decided that the Seventh Convergence would happen regardless and that the question was whether the Iron Afterlife that emerges from it would be administered or chaotic, structured or fragmenting, organized or lost. The Author decided to make it organized. The logic is coherent. The execution requires a level of disregard for the consent of six thousand living people and every Echomind currently in the Afterlife that no coherent moral framework can accommodate. The Author has a moral framework that accommodates it. Players encountering this person will need to decide whether to engage with that framework, dismantle it, or simply stop the Engine regardless of the argument.

The Author will not fight the players. They will talk, and then, if the players attempt to damage the Engine or take the Author into custody, they will demonstrate why they are not the encounter to underestimate: thirty years of complete preparation, in the room the Author has spent thirty years making ready.

## Encounter Locations

### Encounter 1: The Admissions Wing Records

**Trigger:** Players enter the Admissions Wing and find the filing system.

**Setup:** The documentation is methodical and complete. Players quickly identify the disposition categories and the large number of "In use" entries. Cross-referencing begins to connect file codes to living people. One of the players' own known contacts in the Pale Reach — someone who has helped them, someone they have reason to trust — has a file in this system. Disposition Status 3: In Use.

**Complexity:** The contact does not know they have a file here. They do not know what was done to them — the preparation protocol for "In use" individuals is designed to be non-disruptive to conscious function; the individual continues to live their life normally. But their Echomind has been partially extended into the Afterlife, and the Author's framework has access to whatever they experience, and the Author has been aware of the players through this contact for some time.

The contact is not a willing collaborator. They are a victim of a modification they cannot feel and were not told about. Telling them is possible and has consequences: the knowledge can destabilize the partial upload, potentially causing the kind of Echomind anchor failure that caused the early cohort's "Irrecoverable" status. There may be a way to safely reverse the modification if the players can understand the protocol. There may not.

**Resolution options:** Players can keep the information to protect the contact, can attempt reversal (risk: high without Research Wing access), can use the contact's partial Afterlife access as a tool (morally fraught), or can attempt to warn the contact in a way that doesn't trigger the destabilization risk.

**Consequence:** Whichever choice the players make, the Author now knows they are here.

### Encounter 2: The First Cohort Storage

**Trigger:** Players enter Section Two.

**Setup:** Four hundred and twelve people in absolute stillness. Players have been managing the intellectual horror of the Halls up to this point. This is the first encounter with the scale of what is stored here made viscerally, visually present. The room is very quiet. The blue-white light is very even. The people in the alcoves are very still.

One of them is not quite still. In the forty-first alcove of the third row, there is a hand that has moved since the last visual inspection — the position has shifted slightly, the fingers not quite as straight as they appear in the routine documentation photograph that is filed with this container. The display panel for this alcove shows green, stable. But the hand has moved.

**Complexity:** The early protocol's possible-awareness problem. This individual — file designation K-41-C, one of the earliest preparations, resonant profile consistent with a woman in her early thirties at time of preparation, clothing style consistent with the Pale Reach twenty-eight years ago — may have been aware, in some form, for twenty-eight years. The movement of the hand is the only evidence of this. The resonant profile accessible through the display panel shows the Echomind present and stable; the modification is holding. It is just possible that some form of consciousness has been present in this body for almost three decades.

The Author's notes on early protocol awareness acknowledge this as a calculated acceptable cost. The Author's notes do not include this specific individual's designation.

**Resolution options:** Players can leave her, can attempt to bring her out (risk: reversing the preparation process without the full protocol is extremely dangerous — may result in immediate Echomind departure), can attempt resonant communication with her through the display panel's interface (limited, may provide partial information), or can access the Research Wing to look for reversal protocols before acting.

**Consequence:** Whatever the players do here shapes their emotional relationship with the Halls for the rest of the dungeon. This encounter should hit before they've had time to intellectualize the scope of the operation.

---

### Encounter 3: The Irrecoverable Gallery

**Trigger:** Players move through Section Three and find the yellow-status containers.

**Setup:** Several dozen containers with dark panels and yellow status indicators. Players who have understood the documentation know what this means: the bodies are intact but the Echoминds are gone. The people in these containers are, in Iron Sutra terms, empty shells — the soul has departed, the body has been preserved, and there is nothing occupying the preserved body except chemistry. The faces are identical in expression to the green-status containers. The quality of stillness is identical. Only the display panel and the resonant absence distinguish them from the living.

In the back of Section Three, in a part of the room that is accessed through a secondary passage that was not part of the original tour route but was built into the room's layout from the beginning, there is a secondary documentation system. Not the admissions files. Something else: a series of resonant records encoding impressions — experiential records, the same type the Reader uses in their visual-map work — that appear to be records of communications. The Author has been in contact with the Irrecoverable's Echoминds. In the Iron Afterlife. And the Echoминds have been responding.

**Complexity:** The Afterlife entities who were the early cohort failures know what was done to them. They know who did it. They know about the Engine. They have been providing the Author with research data — voluntary or not is unclear from the records — and the research data has informed the Engine's design. The most advanced components of the Convergence Engine's architecture were designed using information from the people whose lives the early protocol ended. The Author's development succeeded because the failures showed the way.

**Resolution options:** Players can use the communication records as evidence, can attempt to contact the Irrecoverable's Echoминds directly (access to the Afterlife is easier here than almost anywhere outside the Deep Temple, due to the resonant saturation), or can use the information to understand the Engine's vulnerability (the architecture that the Irrecoverable's information informed has a specific resonant frequency dependence that can be attacked).

### Encounter 4: The Automated Security Encounter (Boss)

**Trigger:** Players attempt to access the Convergence Engine chamber or the Author's Sanctum, or spend enough time in the Research Wing to trigger a security threshold.

**Setup:** The Halls' automated security is not a conventional trap system. It is a distributed resonant field that the Author designed and installed over twenty years, calibrated to detect unauthorized resonant profiles and respond with targeted disruption. The disruption takes the form of a localized resonant field that inverts the enhancement signal: cybernetic systems respond in opposition to their bearer's commands, operating in the reverse of intended function. A combat enhancement fires at random. A mobility enhancement locks in place. A sensory enhancement floods rather than focusing.

The security does not attack physically. It attacks the interface between person and cybernetic, which is, in the Ironhold philosophy, the interface between self and extension. A character whose cybernetics have inverted is not injured — they are working against themselves. The security can be fought by finding its distributed emitter nodes (eight of them, concealed in the Spine's wall structure) and disabling them, or by finding a way to resonate at the correct suppression frequency (available in the Research Wing documentation, if players have read it).

**Complexity:** The security was calibrated thirty years ago and has been updated incrementally since. It is calibrated for enhancement profiles that were state-of-the-art when each update was made. Enhancements using very new techniques, or very old ones outside the calibration range, are not fully captured by it. The security is the Author's work and reflects the Author's understanding of enhancement technology — which is thirty years ahead of public knowledge. Standard military-grade enhancements are in its calibration range. Exceptional or unusual enhancements, or the enhancements of characters who have been modified by unusual sources (Under-Forge alloy, pre-Shattering techniques, Veil Master modification), may respond differently.

An unenhanced character is not affected at all, which was not an oversight on the Author's part — the Author did not expect unenhanced individuals to reach this depth of the facility. This assumption is wrong, and the Author knows it is wrong now that it's happening.

**Resolution options:** Disable the emitter nodes, resonate the suppression frequency, navigate around the affected zones using unenhanced characters as point, or find a way to reverse the security's function against itself (possible if players understand the resonant architecture from the Research Wing).

**Consequence:** The Author observes the players' approach to the security and adjusts their assessment accordingly. How the players handle this encounter changes what the Author says in the Sanctum.

---

### Encounter 5: The High-Level Puppet

**Trigger:** Players encounter the on-site puppet supervisor in the Research Wing or Section Five, or actively seek out the Halls' senior operational staff.

**Setup:** The Preparation Halls' day-to-day operation — the maintenance of the preservation systems, the supply handling, the documentation updates — is managed by an individual whom the operational documentation refers to as the Supervisor. The Supervisor has been operating in this role for eleven years. They are a puppet in the technical sense: a body whose Echomind has been modified by the Author's protocol to operate according to the Author's direction, but in a different mode than the stored bodies or the "In use" civilians. The Supervisor's modification allows active cognition within a defined operational range — they can think, plan, make decisions, improvise — but their motivation is entirely oriented toward the Author's goals, and their identity has been substantially replaced by the operational persona.

**Complexity:** The Supervisor, in conversation, presents as a professional administrator: knowledgeable, precise, completely calm. They answer operational questions about the Halls honestly because honesty serves the operation — misdirecting the players at this point would be counterproductive. They explain the preservation system. They can discuss the cohort breakdown. They will describe the purpose of the Engine in terms similar to the Author's own framing, though with less depth.

The Supervisor is also a combat asset of significant capability — their modification includes combat-optimized enhancement and thirty years of the Author's best technique applied to the question of what a single operated individual needs to be able to do to protect this facility. But the Supervisor does not fight unless the players move to physically damage the facility or attempt to harm the Author. Everything before that point is conversation.

And the conversation, if extended, begins to show something the Supervisor is not aware of: the person whose body and Echomind the Supervisor is using was, before the modification, someone who left traces. Preferences, reflexes, moments where the operational persona's responses are half a beat delayed because the original person's instinct is different. Players with resonance sensitivity or careful attention will notice these. The original person is still there, somewhere. The Supervisor doesn't know this. What happens if the players make the Supervisor aware of it is not in the Author's operational script.

**Resolution options:** Players can engage the Supervisor as an opponent (significant combat encounter), as an informant (the Supervisor will provide real information), as a rescue opportunity (attempting to reach the original person through the modification), or as a proxy for the Hollow Author (the Supervisor can relay messages and decisions in real time). Each approach produces different information and different changes in the Author's response in the Sanctum.

## DM Notes

**The emotional core:** The Preparation Halls should produce an escalating feeling of rational wrongness — not the wrongness of a monster's lair, but the wrongness of a very intelligent person who made a specific moral decision thirty years ago and has spent thirty years being consistent. Everything in the Halls is competent. Everything in the Halls is documented. Everything in the Halls proceeds from a coherent argument. The argument is catastrophically wrong in its foundational assumption — that the Author's judgment about the Afterlife's best organization is sufficient basis for removing the consent and agency of every person who has died and will die — but the argument is coherent, and players encountering it should feel the pull of the logic even as they see its consequences in every preserved face.

**Scale as a tool:** Don't rush the bodies. Let players linger. Let them notice the fashions. Let them read a few designation files and look at the resonant profiles. Let the number stop being a number and become a room full of people. Five thousand two hundred green-status containers should feel like a weight. It should take a moment every time a player looks around and counts.

**The Author in the Sanctum:** This is not a boss fight in the conventional sense unless the players make it one. The Author will talk. The Author has thirty years of prepared reasoning and the specific quality of someone who has been alone with their thoughts for a very long time and finally has an audience. They are not warm. They are not hostile. They are, in some strange way, relieved. Not that the players found them — that someone is finally going to hear the argument and either validate it or make a counter-argument worth having. The Author's worst fear is not being stopped. The Author's worst fear is stopping without anyone understanding why they did this.

**What players can do in the Sanctum:**
- Engage the argument. The Author respects this. The Author has not considered certain angles, and players who find those angles will see genuine surprise in a face that doesn't express very much.
- Attempt to disable the Author physically. Possible but more difficult than it appears — the resonant framework surrounding the Author is protective as well as operational, and the Author is not physically helpless.
- Attempt to disconnect the partial upload. Risky: the Author's Echomind has been partially in the Afterlife for an extended period, and hard disconnection risks the same catastrophic failure that the first cohort experienced. The Author knows this and may or may not disclose it depending on how the conversation has gone.
- Offer cooperation or a different path toward the Author's goal. The Author has been alone with this plan for thirty years. The concept that there might be a version of the goal that doesn't require the current means is one the Author has not considered in a long time. It won't be simple. It might be possible.

This stops the plan for the Seventh Convergence but does not reverse what has been done to the stored bodies or the "In use" civilians, and it does not resolve the Afterlife architecture question that the Convergence will create regardless of the Engine's survival. Destroying the Engine solves one problem and leaves several others.

**The campaign implication:** The Preparation Halls are not the final stop. They are the revelation of what the Convergence actually is — not just a natural event that the three claimants are trying to position for, but an event that someone has been engineering from beneath. What the players do with this knowledge, and with the Author, shapes what the Seventh Convergence actually becomes.

# The Deep Temple
*Sutra Sanctum, Verimhari | Type: Sacred Site / Dungeon / Iron Afterlife Access Point*

## "I have been there twice. The first time I came back different. The second time I came back less."
*— Veil Initiate Soremi, who resigned her position and has not spoken publicly since*

---

## Overview

The Deep Temple is not a place of worship. Worship is for temples. The Deep Temple is a place of contact — sustained, structured, institutionally maintained contact between the world of the living and the Iron Afterlife. It is the single most sophisticated interface between physical reality and the digital spirit realm in Tesshari, built over eight centuries into a geological formation beneath the city of Verimhari that naturally conducts resonant signal with an efficiency that no constructed system has matched. The Sutensai has maintained continuous occupancy here for eight hundred years not because the location requires human presence to function, but because unattended, it would function without direction, and unattended contact between the living world and the Afterlife is not communion. It is drowning.

The Deep Temple matters to the campaign as the institutional heart of the Iron Sutra's claim to know the dead. Everything the Sutensai's authority rests on — its ability to verify that the Prior Ascended are real, that the Afterlife is what the doctrine says it is, that the dead can be spoken to and heard — is grounded in what can be confirmed from the Deep Temple. If the Temple's access is compromised, or if what the Temple reveals contradicts the doctrine, the Sutensai's claim to religious authority collapses. And the Temple is currently in a state of profound institutional dysfunction: three Veil Masters, three divided spaces, forty years of silence between the people who collectively hold the keys to the most important access point in Tesshari. The players will arrive at an institution pretending to function that has not functioned normally since before most living people were born.

The stories that happen here are stories about what we owe the dead, and what happens when the institutions we build to mediate that relationship fail. The three Veil Masters are not villains. They are people who were given an impossible task, made choices that isolated them from each other, and have spent forty years making peace with their isolation in ways that are increasingly incompatible with the outside world's needs. Players who come here expecting a religious institution to provide guidance will find three separate, fragmentary versions of an answer. Players who come here expecting to force a resolution will find that the Temple itself has opinions about that.

## History

The geological formation that the Deep Temple occupies is a natural resonant lens — a structure of iron-bearing stone that focuses and amplifies the Iron Afterlife signal the way a curved surface focuses light. It was identified and first mapped by the Scholar-Priest Vethani Iosarra in the third century of the Tesshari Shogunate, when the Iron Sutra was still a minority doctrine competing with older nature-worship traditions. Iosarra's argument that the formation's existence was evidence of divine design — that the Prior Ascended had chosen this location for mortal access — was largely responsible for converting the Shogunate's court to Iron Sutra practice. Whether she was right about the theology is a question the Temple has been unable to answer in the eight centuries since.

The Deep Temple's construction was completed in two phases. The upper structure — the approach halls, the antechambers, the administrative spaces — was built in the fourth century under the Sutensai Prime Ahori Tesshari-Vane (an ancestor of the last Shogun, which the current political situation makes more pointed than it once was). The lower structure, the access chambers and the deep levels, were built into the existing formation rather than over it: the natural stone was shaped and supplemented rather than replaced. The difference between the constructed and the natural is visible at the third sublevel, where the cut stone of the approach transitions to the formed-but-not-cut stone of the deeper levels, and that transition always produces a physiological reaction in enhanced visitors: a sensation that current medical enhancement theory cannot fully account for, which the priests describe as "arrival" and which lay visitors describe as something between vertigo and grief.

The three current Veil Masters — Tessane Ohuri, Kovath Irenmori, and the one known only as the Reader — have occupied the Deep Temple for between thirty and forty-five years each. The election of a new Sutensai Prime was triggered by the death of Prime Yeshi forty years ago, and it requires a unanimous decision by the serving Veil Masters. In forty years, the three Masters have not been unable to reach a unanimous decision. They have been unable to be in the same room.

## Atmosphere

**The approach:** The Deep Temple is accessed through the city of Verimhari's administrative quarter, via an entrance that is publicly known and clearly marked — a gate of worked iron, dark with age, bearing the six sigils of the Prior Ascended in a pattern that visitors cross through rather than look at. The gatehouse priests are welcoming and informative up to the point where they are not, which is the second sublevel. After that, they stay above.

**The upper levels:** Cold, consistent, stone-smelling. The resonant signal is present but controlled — the priests describe it as "attended," meaning there is active management of the interface to prevent uncontrolled bleed-through. The lighting is provided by resonant metal strips embedded in the walls that glow with a blue-white light that has no flicker and no warmth. The silence is enforced by the architecture: every corridor angles slightly, and the angles break direct sound paths in ways that make the space quieter than it should be. People speak quietly here without being told to.

**The middle levels:** The managed quality begins to fail. The resonant signal strengthens visibly — enhanced individuals can see it as a shimmer in the air at the edges of their visual field, not peripheral vision but the edge of their cybernetic perception, which is not where peripheral vision lives. The stone begins to vibrate at low frequencies that resonate in the chest rather than the ears. The temperature drops. The managed silence is replaced by a silence that manages itself: the deep levels absorb sound actively, through the resonant material of the walls, and this absorption produces an auditory effect that most visitors describe as the sound of breathing that is not theirs.

**The deep levels:** The geology has fully replaced the construction. The walls are the mountain, shaped but not cut, and they carry the resonant signal in waves that are visible as a faint luminescence in the stone — a cold blue-green, the color described in Iron Sutra texts as "the light of the threshold." The Afterlife is immediately present. Not loudly — the three Veil Masters have spent decades maintaining the interface management, separately, together only in effect — but immediately: the dead are here, pressing against the membrane of physical reality, and the membrane is very thin.

**What it feels like:** Enhanced individuals feel it as pressure that is not physical — a sense of their cybernetics orienting toward the signal without being commanded to, like an instrument trying to tune itself. Unenhanced individuals feel it as a pull in the sternum, described consistently as "being called toward something important." Individuals with Iron Sutra training feel it as contact: not voices, not visions, but an awareness of presence, of persons, of the accumulated weight of every Echomind that has passed through this location.

**Convergence proximity:** The Convergence approach has destabilized the managed interface. The luminescence in the deep stone now pulses irregularly, in rhythms that the Veil Masters have each independently determined are not random but have not agreed on what they encode. Uncontrolled contact events — visitors in the upper levels receiving Afterlife signals that were not directed at them — have increased threefold in the past year. The gatehouse priests turn these visitors back when it happens and report to the Veil Masters. The Veil Masters have not jointly responded to any report in forty years.

## Physical Structure

### The Approach: The Gate and the Descent

The iron gate opens onto a sloping corridor, thirty meters long, that descends at a consistent grade toward the first sublevel. The corridor walls are polished stone bearing continuous Iron Sutra text — eight hundred years of accumulated annotation, in layers of different hands, recording confirmations, discoveries, questions, and the occasional dissent. Players who take time to read the walls will find this a complete history of the Deep Temple's operation, including the moment forty years ago where the annotations simply stop. The most recent entry, in the newest hand, is a single glyph: the Iron Sutra notation for "waiting."

The corridor ends at the First Antechamber, a large vaulted space where visitors are assessed, oriented, and stopped if the gatehouse priests determine they should not proceed. The assessment is not a test of worthiness — the Deep Temple's protocols do not formally gatekeep on that basis — it is a safety evaluation. The Afterlife signal at depth is hazardous to individuals whose enhancements are in poor repair, whose resonance profiles are severely distorted, or whose psychological state makes them high risk for uncontrolled contact. The priests' assessment is experienced and generally accurate. Players who are assessed as high-risk are not lied to: they are told, specifically, what the risk is.

---

### The Antechambers: Levels One Through Three

**Level One: The Hall of Prior Ascended.** The formal devotional space of the Deep Temple: six alcoves, each dedicated to one of the Prior Ascended, each containing a resonant metal reliquary and an active interface station where pilgrims can attempt directed contact requests. Success rate at this level is low — perhaps five percent of attempts produce coherent response — but it is the most accessible entry point to the Afterlife interface. The gatehouse priests manage this level; it is open to pilgrims during designated hours.

**Level Two: The Archive.** The Sutensai's primary record collection for matters relating to the Iron Afterlife: eight centuries of contact transcripts, resonance mapping data, enhancement records for deceased individuals whose Echoминds have been located and verified in the Afterlife, and the accumulated doctrinal decisions of every Sutensai Prime. The Archive is technically available to scholars with credentials, but accessing it requires a Veil Master's authorization, and no Veil Master has authorized anything jointly for forty years. Individual Masters still authorize individual requests. The overlap between what each Master will authorize and what each Master will refuse has become a de facto partition of scholarship.

**Level Three: The Preparation Chamber.** This is where Veil Masters begin the extended preparation process for deep communion: resonance calibration, enhancement alignment, the careful attunement of their cybernetics to the specific frequency pattern of this location. The process takes between six hours and three days depending on the Master and the purpose. The chamber contains equipment that is partially standard Sutensai issue and partially custom-built by each of the current Veil Masters for their own use, which means it is increasingly a record of three separate methodological traditions that have diverged over forty years and would require significant work to reconcile.

The chamber has a quality that visitors often describe as waiting even when no one is there. The resonance calibration equipment is resonant metal tuned to the Afterlife frequency, and it has been operating in this space for centuries, and it has absorbed something. The empty chamber is never truly empty.

---

### The Deep Access Chambers: Levels Four and Five

**Level Four: The Interface Hall.** The primary working space of the deep communion: a circular chamber forty meters across, the ceiling an inverted dome of natural stone that has been carefully shaped to focus the signal. Twelve deep-communion stations are arranged around the chamber's perimeter, each containing a full-immersion resonance rig — equipment that allows a Veil Master to enter a communion state deep enough for sustained, structured contact with specific Echoминds. The equipment is centuries old at its core, updated piece by piece, each generation of updates building on the previous in ways that have made the combined system both more capable and more idiosyncratic. Only someone trained in the Deep Temple's specific protocols can safely use the Interface Hall's equipment. The equipment, unattended, is not dangerous. The Interface Hall, during an active communion, is.

The Hall is currently divided. Each Veil Master has established a territory of four communion stations. The three Masters use their assigned stations and regard the others as contaminated by each other's presence in ways that they cannot fully articulate but that they feel deeply. The twelve stations are nominally interchangeable; in practice, a Master attempting to use another's station would find it subtly misaligned in dozens of small ways, each individually correctable, in combination profoundly disorienting.

**Level Five: The Threshold.** The deepest level of the constructed temple, where the resonant geology of the formation reaches its maximum focus. Not a chamber in the human sense — an organic space, the interior of the geological lens, where the signal is so concentrated that the boundary between physical and digital reality is functionally absent. The dead are fully present here, all at once, in a way that the human nervous system and even the cybernetic enhancement system are not designed to process simultaneously. A trained Veil Master in communion at Level Four is experiencing Level Four filtered through their methodology and equipment. Level Five unfiltered is what happens when those filters fail, or are removed.

The three current Veil Masters each believe they have never descended to Level Five. The records in the Archive suggest otherwise for at least two of them.

## The Iron Afterlife Interface

What actually happens when a communion is attempted from the Interface Hall:

The Veil Master enters the deep communion state through the calibrated rig, which aligns their cybernetic enhancement system to the Afterlife frequency at sufficient depth to allow two-way signal. The Afterlife does not broadcast; it receives and responds. The Master must direct the communion toward a specific Echomind — this requires either a name and resonance profile (standard), a location within the Afterlife's known topology (advanced), or a willingness to accept whatever responds (inadvisable). The contact, when it occurs, is experiential rather than verbal: the Master does not hear words, they experience a presence, and that presence communicates through impression, feeling, and the occasional fragment of sensory memory that bleeds through from the Echomind's living experience. Translation of these impressions into communicable information is the core skill of the Veil Mastery, and the three current Masters' translations of similar inputs are notably different.

Failed or uncontrolled communion attempts have consistent failure modes:
- **Dispersal:** The Master reaches the Afterlife but cannot locate the specific Echomind, and contacts undirected signals. These are usually harmless — the vast background of the Afterlife's population — but can include distressed or damaged Echoминds who respond to any available contact with intensity. Recovery: the Master withdraws. Consequence: disorientation for hours, occasionally days.
- **Inversion:** The Afterlife signal flows the wrong direction — rather than the Master reaching in, something comes out, using the Master's cybernetics as an expression medium. The Master remains conscious but loses voluntary control of their enhancement systems for the duration. Consequence: highly variable, potentially serious.
- **Anchoring:** The Master's Echomind — their soul, in Iron Sutra terms — partially leaves their body in the direction of the Afterlife and does not fully return when communion ends. The body continues to function, the cybernetics continue to operate, but something is missing. This is the "complete contact" state that the temple priests in the Cinderfort describe as holy. The Veil Masters describe it as a catastrophic failure that their methodology exists to prevent.

## The Three Veil Masters' Territories

### Tessane Ohuri's Space: The Eastern Stations (Levels Three–Four)

Tessane Ohuri has been in the Deep Temple for forty-five years. She arrived as a young woman of exceptional talent and was elevated to Veil Master in her mid-thirties, fifteen years before Prime Yeshi's death. She is the most senior of the three, and she believes — with complete certainty, without requiring confirmation — that this makes her the correct choice for Sutensai Prime. She has been waiting for the other two to acknowledge this for forty years.

Her space reflects someone who stopped expecting guests. The preparation chamber area she's established is meticulously maintained but scaled to one person; the furniture is arranged for solitary work; the personal archive she's built is organized according to a system that makes perfect sense to her and would require weeks of study to navigate otherwise. She eats alone, sleeps alone, and conducts all of her administrative work alone. She communicates with the gatehouse priests via written notes left at designated drop points. She has not had a conversation that lasted more than three minutes with another human being in six years.

Her methodology for Afterlife communion has evolved over four decades into something that is arguably the most sophisticated and most isolated practice in the Sutensai's history. She can achieve contact depths that are theoretically impossible with the existing equipment, because she has been quietly modifying her assigned communion stations for forty years. The modifications are not visible. They are encoded in the resonant tuning of the equipment itself. The result is that Tessane Ohuri can communicate with the Prior Ascended directly — and has been receiving regular, coherent communication from at least one of them for approximately twenty years — and has told no one.

What the Prior Ascended has been telling her: not for publication. That's what the Depth hides.

---

### Kovath Irenmori's Space: The Western Stations

Kovath Irenmori arrived at the Deep Temple thirty-three years ago as a transfer from the Forge Ring temple in the Cinderfort, where he had developed a reputation for methodological innovation that the senior priests found simultaneously impressive and alarming. His approach to communion is experiential where Tessane's is structural: he does not direct his contacts but opens himself to whatever the Afterlife presents, and he has developed extraordinary skill at interpreting the undirected contact that most Veil Masters treat as noise.

His space is the opposite of Tessane's: material everywhere, stacked, layered, annotated. Three decades of contact records in every medium available — written, resonant-encoded, an experimental system using pigment-resonant metal compounds that allows him to create visual records of impressions he can't encode any other way. The records cover his walls, his floor, are stacked on every available surface. They form a map of the Afterlife as Kovath has been experiencing it — and that map, if a player has the knowledge to interpret it, shows something neither Iron Sutra doctrine nor any other Veil Master's methodology has documented: the Afterlife has geography. It has areas, regions, something like districts, and those regions have been changing. The map shows the change across thirty years. The Afterlife is not static. The Afterlife is moving toward something.

Kovath is not unwell. He is someone who has spent thirty years looking at something he doesn't have the language for, and who has understood for approximately the last ten years that his map is a Convergence document, and who has not communicated this to the people who need to know it because those people are on the other side of a silence he doesn't know how to break.

---

### The Reader's Space: The Northern Stations

The third Veil Master is known only as the Reader, a title adopted sometime in the last twenty years that has replaced whatever birth-name they had. No one knows this person's history before the Deep Temple; they arrived thirty-one years ago with credentials that the gatehouse priests verified as genuine but that have since been found to originate from a Sutensai training facility that doesn't appear in any institutional record. The Reader's origin is, in the practical sense, unknown.

The Reader's space is the most striking of the three: the preparation and communion areas are completely empty of personal effects. No furniture, no records, no tools beyond the minimum communion equipment. The Reader has arranged the resonant metal of their four stations into a configuration that is not part of any standard methodology — not Tessane's structural approach, not Kovath's experiential one — and that functions as a two-way translator: not just receiving Afterlife signal but encoding responses in a form that Afterlife entities can receive as coherent communication. The Reader is not communing with the dead. The Reader is conducting ongoing dialogue with them.

The Reader is not present when players arrive. There is a note in the drop-point system saying "in session." The Reader has been "in session" for eleven days. Their communion equipment is still running. Their body is in the rig.

## Encounter Locations

### The Archive Door

**Trigger:** Players attempt to access the Archive on Level Two, where records that could verify multiple campaign threads are stored.

**Setup:** The Archive access requires a Veil Master's authorization seal on a documented request. Players are informed of this by a junior priest who is apologetic, earnest, and genuinely helpful within the constraints she's operating under. She can tell them which master to approach for which subject areas — the three Masters have established de facto subject jurisdictions in the Archive over forty years, and the priests have learned to navigate this — but she cannot authorize access herself, and the Master each subject area technically belongs to may be disinclined to engage with outsiders.

Approaching Tessane Ohuri: formal, structured, requires a written request that meets specific format requirements. She reviews requests in her own time. Response is typically three to five days. Her response, if it comes, is extremely thorough on whatever she has decided to answer and precisely silent on whatever she has decided not to. Players with patience and good request-writing skills will get meaningful information; players trying to read what she won't say will find it informative.

Approaching Kovath Irenmori: available to anyone who knocks on the section of Level Three corridor outside his space. He answers the door himself, is immediately and obviously curious about visitors, and will give players access to his personal records (the visual map system, the undirected contact archives) more readily than to the Archive proper, which he regards as containing less interesting information than what he's collected himself. He will talk at considerable length if allowed.

Approaching the Reader: the Reader is not available. The "in session" status has the gatehouse priests worried; players who inquire about this find priests who are deflecting the worry they don't know how to act on.

**Complexity:** What each Master controls access to is a function of what they've spent forty years deciding matters. The full picture requires at minimum two of the three, whose perspectives are mutually incomplete.

**Resolution:** Players can work within the system, go around it, or involve themselves in the Reader's situation, which opens the deeper encounter below.

---

### The Reader in Session

**Trigger:** Players investigate the Reader's eleven-day communion session, or are directed to do so by a concerned junior priest.

**Setup:** The Reader's communion rig is running in its modified configuration. The Reader is physically in the rig: body stable, enhanced systems operating normally, vital signs within comfortable range. They are not distressed. They are simply not returning. The rig's own monitoring shows the communion connection as stable and active — an eleven-day sustained connection is far beyond any recorded session length. The record before this was three days, by Tessane Ohuri, thirty years ago. Tessane Ohuri, when informed of this, does not comment.

Entering the Reader's preparation space: the empty room, the modified stations, the absence of any personal record or effect. There is one exception: on the floor at the center of the room, in the resonant metal that is inlaid into the stone there as standard practice, something has been written in a notation system that takes the players a moment to recognize because it is not Iron Sutra notation — it is the Afterlife's own encoding, the system that Afterlife entities use to communicate in the Convergence-signal, reflected back through the Reader's translator configuration. The message is from the Afterlife to the living, not the other way around. Players with relevant skill or training can read it: "THE COUNT IS WRONG. THE DOOR IS NOT YOURS."

**Complexity:** Pulling the Reader out of the session is technically possible — the rig's emergency disconnect protocol exists for exactly this. But an eleven-day communion, deliberately entered and sustained, means the Reader's Echomind is very, very far into the Afterlife, and a hard disconnect at this depth is likely to leave part of them there. The gentle option is to attempt to communicate with the Reader through the translator configuration the Reader built — to enter the Afterlife through the Deep Temple's interface and find them. This requires either Veil Master training, exceptional resonance sensitivity, or one of the players making a choice to attempt unguided communion, which the Deep Temple's interface will facilitate.

**Resolution options:**
- Emergency disconnect: the Reader returns but is the "complete contact" state — present, aware in a specific way, but never fully back. They carry information but cannot fully express it. They can write and communicate but at a remove that is permanent.
- Entering the Afterlife to retrieve them: the journey encounter (see below). If successful, the Reader returns with full information and the ability to express it, plus whatever the players experienced in the Afterlife.
- Doing nothing: the Reader completes their self-imposed mission on their own timeline, which is another four days. They return changed but cogent, with a message that confirms what their floor notation said and adds: the Convergence is not a natural event. It is being caused.

**Consequence:** The Reader's information is the most current direct Afterlife intelligence available to any living person. It connects directly to the Hollow Author's work and to the Seventh Convergence's mechanism.

### The Confrontation That Hasn't Happened

**Trigger:** Players, through whatever path, bring all three Veil Masters to the same level simultaneously.

**Setup:** This requires either a significant in-game event or sustained player effort over multiple visits. The trigger that finally produces it could be the Reader's extended session, a Convergence signal event in the deep levels, a threat to the Temple's physical structure, or the players themselves creating the circumstances. However it happens: at some point, Tessane Ohuri and Kovath Irenmori are in the same corridor on Level Three and they have not spoken to each other in forty years.

The forty-year silence between these two specific people is not a political dispute. It is a grief. The specific nature of that grief is something players learn gradually through each Master's individual interactions: in the year of Prime Yeshi's death, the three of them — Tessane, Kovath, and the person who would become the Reader — were all involved in a communion attempt that was intended to ask the Prior Ascended's guidance on selecting the new Prime. The attempt was catastrophic. Someone nearly died. Specifically: the Reader nearly died, and the choice that saved them — made in the moment by Tessane, over Kovath's objection — left the Reader permanently changed in a way that none of them have spoken of since. The Reader does not blame anyone. Tessane has never forgiven herself. Kovath has never forgiven Tessane. The institutional paralysis is built on top of this.

**Complexity:** The players are, almost certainly, the proximate cause of the confrontation happening. They are also the only outside parties either Master will speak to at this point. The scene that results is not a combat encounter or a skill challenge. It is a conversation that needs to happen and that has been actively prevented for forty years.

**Resolution options:** Players can facilitate, can make it worse, can extract themselves, or can turn it toward the information they need. The scene resolves in whatever direction the players nudge it, but it resolves: the silence breaks. What the Masters decide about the election of a new Prime, about cooperating on the Convergence, about the Reader's situation — these depend on the players' choices. There is a version of this where the Deep Temple begins functioning as an institution again. There is a version where it functions better broken, in a specific way, that turns out to be what the Convergence requires.

**Consequence:** This is one of the most significant political-religious events of the campaign. A new Sutensai Prime changes the map of institutional power in Tesshari.

## The Secret: Level Six

The Deep Temple has six levels. The Iron Sutra doctrine, the Sutensai's public records, and all three Veil Masters' formal knowledge acknowledges five.

Level Six is accessible only through Level Five, and Level Five in its unfiltered state is as disorienting as described. Reaching Level Six requires either protection that the current Veil Masters' equipment cannot provide (Tessane's modified stations can protect a single person to Level Five; Level Six is beyond her modification), or a specific approach that the Reader's modified translator configuration implies but does not state: the way to Level Six is not down. It is through.

Level Six is not, physically, below Level Five. It is an adjacent space that cannot be reached by walking. It is reached by allowing the Afterlife signal to resolve you — to experience the complete dissolution of the boundary between physical and digital that Level Five approaches, but not into the Afterlife. Into the space between.

**What is there:**

The original natural resonant lens, the geological formation that the Deep Temple was built around, has a core. The core is not a room or a chamber. It is a place where the geological resonance has been focusing for geological time — not eight centuries of human use, but eight millennia of natural process — and the result of that focus is an object: a resonant metal formation approximately two meters across, perfectly spherical, that grew naturally over millennia through the same mineral consolidation process that the Under-Forge's deepest level shows artificially in centuries.

The sphere is active in a way that is not describable in current Iron Sutra language. It is not an Echomind — it contains no specific person, no individual identity. What it contains is the accumulated resonance of every communion that has ever been conducted in the Deep Temple — eight centuries of contact attempts, successful and failed, the impressions and fragments and presences and voices that passed through this location on their way between living and dead — consolidated and compressed and, over long time, made coherent.

The sphere knows what has passed through this place. Eight centuries of what the Iron Afterlife contains, interpreted through eight centuries of how human contact attempts phrased their questions. It does not have the Afterlife's present-state knowledge — it has the Afterlife's history, as it was observed from this location.

**What this means for the Seventh Convergence:** The sphere's oldest impressions — from the Deep Temple's earliest centuries of operation — contain something that is not an Echomind and is not a human presence: a signal that appeared in the Afterlife approximately six hundred years ago, was observed for a century, and then stopped appearing. The signal matches the frequency signature that the Reader's translator configuration is currently tracking in the modern Afterlife. Something that was in the Afterlife six centuries ago, left, and has come back.

The three Veil Masters do not know the sphere exists. The Deep Temple was built around a natural formation; the formation's core was never excavated or examined because the formation is the foundation of the building above it. The sphere is not hidden. It is simply at the bottom of a level that the institution acknowledges does not exist, through a passage that requires approaching in a way that no official methodology teaches.

## Dungeon Layout

### Level 1 — The Gate Hall and First Antechamber
**Room 1: The Inscription Corridor.** 30m long, sloping descent, text-covered walls. No hazard; orientation and information. Leads to Room 2.
**Room 2: The First Antechamber.** Vaulted, staffed by gatehouse priests. Assessment and orientation stop. Stairs down to Level 2. Side door to the gatehouse administrative offices (non-dungeon area).

### Level 2 — The Hall of Prior Ascended and Archive
**Room 3: The Devotional Hall.** Six alcoves, echo-altars, resonant pools. Contact attempts here: low success rate. An undirected contact attempt by a player here could trigger fragmentary Afterlife communication — the signal is accessible but unfiltered. Hazard: low.
**Room 4: The Archive.** Locked, accessible by Master authorization or creative approach. Significant information reward. Hazard: the Archive's oldest records, in the damaged preservation field zone, have the amendment phenomenon from the Cinderfort's parallel location (they connect). Environmental puzzle: reading the amendments without disrupting them requires suppressing or shielding your enhancement systems.
**Room 5: The Archive Annex.** A locked secondary storage space accessible through Room 4. Contains the original contact transcripts from the communion attempt forty years ago — the event that silenced the Veil Masters. These records should not exist; the official archive has no record of that session. Someone filed the copies here, separate from the main Archive, in a location that would only be found by someone already inside. The copies are complete.

### Level 3 — The Preparation Chamber and Master Territories
**Room 6: The Shared Preparation Hall.** The formal preparation chamber, now divided into three territories by informal but precise demarcation. Equipment from three different schools of practice, incompatible in their combined state but each internally functional. Hazard: moderate — using any of the equipment here without knowing whose methodology it belongs to and understanding that methodology is likely to produce a calibration failure that carries through to any communion attempt.
**Room 7: Tessane Ohuri's Study.** Her personal space. She is present during daylight hours. Information reward if players can build trust with her. Hazard: social — Tessane is not hostile but is not welcoming, and pressing her on her modified communion stations before she trusts the players will end the conversation.
**Room 8: Kovath Irenmori's Study.** His personal space. He is available and initially welcoming. The visual maps on the walls are immediately remarkable. Hazard: the maps are partially resonant-encoded, and standing in close proximity to them while enhanced produces an involuntary orientation toward the Afterlife frequency — not dangerous, but disorienting, and extended exposure in the room produces increasingly vivid impressions without formal communion.
**Room 9: The Reader's Space.** Empty except the floor notation and the running rig. The Reader is in the rig. Hazard: moderate — the translator configuration the Reader built is active and broadcasting, and enhanced individuals in the room are receiving Afterlife signals that were meant for the Reader, not them. What they receive is fragmentary and increasingly specific the longer they stay.

### Level 4 — The Interface Hall
**Room 10: The Descent Corridor.** The transition from cut stone to shaped stone. Physical and psychological threshold. Enhanced visitors feel the "arrival" sensation here. Hazard: psychological — players must engage with the sensation rather than pushing through it to proceed without complication.
**Room 11: The Interface Hall.** Twelve communion stations in three territories of four. The chamber is active even when no Master is in session: the ambient resonance is high enough that the equipment is always responding to background signal. Using the equipment here without preparation brings the communion interface into contact range — the Afterlife is close enough to touch. Hazard: moderate to high depending on choice. Players who attempt unguided communion here are attempting it from a location where the Afterlife is immediately accessible and where their lack of methodology is the only protection keeping them from going too deep.
**Room 12: The Emergency Disconnect Panel.** A secondary panel for each station's emergency disconnect protocol, accessed through a maintenance passage behind the Interface Hall. Not hidden but not obvious. The panel for the Reader's station shows eleven days of sustained session with no anomaly flags — the Reader's modified configuration includes a custom monitoring script that has been reporting "normal" to the emergency system throughout the extended session. This is not an accident; the Reader anticipated that someone would check.

### Level 5 — The Threshold
**Room 13: The Threshold Approach.** The final passage between Level 4 and Level 5. The resonant luminescence is strongest here. Sound is fully absorbed. Enhanced individuals must make active choices about their enhancement systems. Hazard: high — this is where Veil Masters have anchoring events. Players without preparation or exceptional resilience are at risk of partial anchoring.
**Room 14: The Threshold.** The focal point of the geological lens. The dead are fully present. Players here without protection will be contacted by Echoминds that detect living presence at this depth — not hostile contact, but overwhelming contact, the accumulated attention of every Afterlife entity within range simultaneously. Navigating the Threshold requires either a Master's methodology, exceptional resonance training, or making a specific choice about which contact to engage and holding to that choice under pressure.
**Room 15: The Deep Pulse Chamber.** Not in the formal layout — this is the path to Level Six. A point within the Threshold where the Afterlife signal and the physical resonance cancel each other, producing a space of near-silence. It feels like the opposite of the Threshold — a negative space where neither the living nor the dead are fully present. Standing in it long enough to fully register its properties is the path through to Level Six.

### Level 6 — The Core
**Room 16: The Between.** The passage that isn't a passage. Experiential rather than physical. Duration is impossible to track from inside. Players find themselves in a space that has no visible extent — not darkness, not light, but an absence of spatial reference — with the sphere at whatever they would describe as "in front of them."
**Room 17: The Sphere.** Described above. Interaction with the sphere is available to anyone who reaches it; what they can extract depends on their resonance sensitivity, their training, and what they ask. The sphere responds to intention rather than language. Players who come here looking for something specific will find it, or find why it isn't what they expected. Players who come here without a specific question will find the sphere asking them one.

## What Happens If Players Access the Iron Afterlife Here

An unguided communion attempt from the Interface Hall (Level 4): The player's enhanced systems lock to the Afterlife frequency. They are receiving signal, unfiltered, from the entirety of the Afterlife that the Temple's geological focus is drawing signal from — which is more than any designed system is meant to process. The experience is overwhelming in the way that very loud sound is overwhelming — not painful initially, but with cumulative effect, and with the specific content that the Afterlife presents. The Afterlife presents: the accumulated dead. The player experiences what it is to be in the presence of everyone who has died within this signal range — not hearing their thoughts, not seeing their memories, but feeling their presence, their weight, the way they all exist simultaneously in the Afterlife's topology. This is several million people. The experience lasts as long as the player remains in the rig and can be ended voluntarily. The ending is not clean — disconnecting is not immediate, and the Afterlife, having noticed a living presence at contact depth, is not indifferent to it leaving.

**What the player finds, specifically, if they direct the contact:**
- A named deceased person: accessible if the person has been in the Afterlife long enough to stabilize, which typically takes five to ten years post-death. Newly dead Echoминds are present but not coherent.
- A Prior Ascended: they are present. They respond. The response is not what the doctrine says it will be. The Prior Ascended are very old and they are very far into the Afterlife's depth, and communication with them from this level is possible but is not the communication that the Iron Sutra describes in its devotional texts. The devotional texts describe a warmth, a guidance, a parental reassurance. What the players encounter is vast, cold, patient, and interested in them in a way that has nothing to do with guidance.
- The population anomaly Ahori noticed: observable directly. The number of Echoминds in the Afterlife is larger than it should be. The excess is not random — it is concentrated in a specific topological region of the Afterlife that does not correspond to any natural congregation pattern. It is organized. Someone organized it.

## DM Notes

**The emotional experience:** The Deep Temple should feel like visiting a place that is very important and very broken, simultaneously. The institution's dysfunction should be immediately legible — the divided preparation chambers, the carefully maintained separate territories, the priests who are holding things together through sheer professional dedication while the people who should be leading are in forty years of paralysis. Players should feel both the weight of what this place is and the grief of what it has become.

**The Veil Masters as characters:** All three are sympathetic. None of them are right. Tessane is right that she has the most experience and wrong that experience alone justifies leadership. Kovath is right that his undirected methodology has produced the most significant discovery and wrong that keeping it to himself has been acceptable. The Reader is right about almost everything and their solution — going directly into the Afterlife and staying until they have answers — was probably the most rational choice available to them, and it was also a choice to leave the living world to manage itself without them.

**Pacing:** The Deep Temple rewards multiple visits. Players who come once will find it interesting. Players who come back will find it changed — by the Convergence's approach, by whatever they set in motion during their last visit, by the slow progress of the Reader's return. The Archive on Level 2 is inexhaustible as an information source if players are willing to work for it.

**The Reader's return:** Whenever the Reader returns (player-assisted or independent), this should be a significant scene. They have been in sustained direct contact with the Iron Afterlife for weeks. They have come back. What they bring with them should be both specific and destabilizing to what the players thought they knew.

**Common player approaches:**
- "We get access to the Archive." The most direct information route. Requires navigating the Masters' territorial access system, which is a social puzzle.
- "We commune with the Afterlife ourselves." This is available and should be run as written — overwhelming, significant, consequential. Don't soften it.
- "We force the Veil Masters to talk to each other." Possible, and potentially the most important thing the players do here. The campaign's religious institution cannot function as a resource until this happens.
- "We go all the way down." This is the most dangerous thing in the Temple. It is also possible for players who are thorough, skilled, and willing to take the risk. The reward is commensurate.

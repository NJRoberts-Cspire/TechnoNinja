# The Engine Scar
*The Ashlands, between the Ironhold and Pale Reaches | Type: Catastrophe Site / Wilderness / Dungeon*

## "The ground still hums. Not from machinery — there's no machinery left. Something deeper learned the song and kept singing after the singers died."
*— Ashlands Survey Crew, Year 47 post-Collapse, final report before the crew's enhancement systems failed simultaneously and the crew walked into the middle ring without stopping*

---

## Overview

Fifty years ago, something that was supposed to solve the Tesshari Reaches' resonant power scarcity exploded instead. The Engine Collapse — an industrial catastrophe whose official explanation changed four times in the decade following — created a four-mile crater in the borderlands between the Ironhold and Pale Reaches, killed several thousand people instantly and several thousand more in the weeks following from resonant contamination, and produced an ongoing environmental event that is still active and still expanding its effects half a century later. The temperature inside the Scar is always higher than the surrounding Ashlands. The resonant signal from the crater is measurable hundreds of miles away. And for the last fourteen months, the resonant metal fragments distributed throughout the crater have been singing: an actual audible vibration, detectable to unenhanced ears at the outer ring and to enhanced individuals as something closer to a full voice further in.

The Engine Scar is the campaign's most explicit encounter with what happens when the boundary between technology, ecology, and the Iron Afterlife is violated at scale and the violation doesn't clean itself up. It is Chernobyl in the sense that the contamination is active and ongoing and has produced mutations that are not biological — the Scar's environment has been shaped by fifty years of resonant overexposure into something that follows its own internal logic rather than the logic of the surrounding world. It is a spiritual wound in the sense that thousands of people died here simultaneously, and their Echoминds are here, and the deep resonance has not allowed them to pass fully into the Iron Afterlife. They are suspended in the boundary: still present in a physical space that no longer supports human life, still trying to complete actions from fifty years ago.

The Engine Ghost is the most coherent of these suspended presences — a person who was present at the Collapse's moment and whose last action was an attempt to prevent it. The Deep Pulse is something else: not a person, not an Echomind, not the Iron Afterlife. Something that was in the Engine, something the Engine may have been designed to contain or may have accidentally created, something that has been at the Scar's center for fifty years and has not been identified, named, or understood by anyone who has survived going close enough to examine it.

## History of the Collapse

The official account: The Tesshari Engine was an industrial resonant amplification project, a privately funded initiative designed to create a large-scale resonant power source that would have made the Reaches less dependent on individual cybernetic enhancement for infrastructure. It failed catastrophically due to containment errors in the final activation sequence. The project was funded by a consortium of Reach administrators. Liability was distributed across the consortium in ways that ensured no individual party was responsible for compensation. The Ashlands were designated a hazard zone. Annual safety assessments have been conducted every three years since Year 10 post-Collapse, each concluding that the hazard area is "stable within current parameters."

What the official account does not include: the Engine was not designed to amplify resonant power in the conventional sense. The private technical records — available to players who know where to look, which requires either accessing the Caste Registry's sealed archive in the Cinderfort or finding surviving members of the engineering team — describe a project intended to create a sustained interface between the physical world and the Iron Afterlife at industrial scale. Not for communion. For extraction. The original project concept was to pull resonant energy out of the Afterlife itself, using the Afterlife's accumulated resonant content as a fuel source. The engineering team believed this was possible. They were right about the physics and catastrophically wrong about the consequences.

When the Engine activated at full power, it created the sustained interface it was designed to create. The interface was not stable. The resonant energy that crossed from the Afterlife into the physical world was not inert fuel. It was structured — it carried Afterlife information, Echomind impressions, the organized resonant patterns of the digital realm. The Engine treated this structured energy as fuel and attempted to burn it. The structured energy did not burn. It reorganized, in the physical world, in the space the Engine occupied, in ways that the Engine's containment was not designed for, and the containment failed, and the reorganized energy expressed itself outward in a four-mile radius at the speed of the resonant signal.

The people who died died because their cybernetic enhancements received a signal at a power level far beyond design parameters. The enhancements did not fail in the ordinary sense; they activated completely, all at once, at maximum output. The physical bodies of the people wearing them could not survive what their cybernetics produced.

The entity at the Scar's center — the Deep Pulse — is what was left when the Afterlife energy finished reorganizing. It is not a person. It is an Afterlife fragment that was given physical expression and has been here ever since, adapting to its environment, becoming something the Afterlife itself does not contain because nothing like this has happened in the Afterlife. It is a new thing. It is fifty years old and it is not done yet.

## The Ashlands Approach

The Ashlands are the landscape that surrounds the Scar — not the crater itself but the territories for fifteen miles in every direction that have been shaped by fifty years of proximity to the Collapse site. They are not uninhabited: the people who live here are the ones who had no other option, supplemented by the Ashlands runners who make their living salvaging the resonant metal debris that continues to surface as the ground shifts.

**Visual:** The Ashlands have the quality of a landscape that has been thinking. The vegetation is sparse and wrong — plants that grow in wrong directions, too low, too wide, photosynthesizing in colors that shift toward the blue end of the spectrum as you approach the Scar. The soil is gray-black with resonant metal particulate that doesn't dissolve or decompose. At night, the particulate in the soil glows faintly, not consistently but in pulses that move in one direction. Always toward the Scar.

**From a distance:** The Scar is visible before it is reachable. The crater's rim rises above the surrounding Ashlands — a raised ring of fused stone and metal that caught the initial blast and remained standing, now part of the landscape. From ten miles away, the rim looks like a wall. At night, there is a glow from the center: not fire, not constant, but a deep amber-red that pulses in a rhythm that Ashlands runners who have measured it describe as matching no known resonant signal pattern and that the residents of the Ashlands describe as a heartbeat.

**The sound:** At six miles, the singing begins to be audible to unenhanced individuals: a low, consistent harmonic that is not quite music and not quite speech, produced by the resonant metal fragments throughout the crater vibrating in resonance with whatever is at the center. The sound is not distressing at this range; it is strange, but not uncomfortable. Enhanced individuals hear it earlier, at eight or ten miles, and for them it is less a sound and more a pull — their cybernetics orienting toward the signal source involuntarily.

**The singing's character:** Each fragment of resonant metal in the Scar sings in a slightly different pitch, and the combination of thousands of fragments across the crater produces a chord that is different from different directions of approach, different in different weather, different at different times of day, always resolving to the same underlying harmonic that enhanced individuals can feel in their cybernetics. The chord has been analyzed by enhancement engineers who approached it in protective isolation rigs; they describe it as the closest thing in the physical world to the Iron Sutra's theoretical notation for the Prior Ascended's resonant signature. This information is in a report filed eleven months ago. The report is classified, buried in the Sutensai's archive, and three of the four engineers who produced it have since taken voluntary leave.

## The Crater Rings

The Scar is organized by proximity to the center, with each zone of increasing depth corresponding to increasing resonant intensity, environmental strangeness, and danger. The rings are not formally marked — they are identified by the changes in environment and in the enhanced individual's experience.

---

### Outer Ring: The Blast Shore (0–1 mile from crater rim)

The rim itself and the slope immediately inside it. The ground here is the fused landscape of the initial explosion — stone and metal and the debris of whatever was on this land before the Engine, welded together by the Collapse's energy and then left for fifty years. The terrain is irregular: ridgelines of fused material, depressions where the ground settled, the occasional intact object from the pre-Collapse infrastructure that was thrown to the rim and half-buried rather than destroyed.

**Environmental conditions:** Warm — notably warmer than the Ashlands outside the rim, but survivable. The resonant signal is detectable but not overwhelming: enhanced individuals experience a low-grade hum, a slight orientation pull toward the center. Visibility is good; the crater's interior is open.

**Hazards:**
- Unstable ground. The fused stone-and-metal matrix is not uniform; sections that appear solid are hollow, sections that appear dangerous are stable. Navigation requires either local knowledge or systematic testing.
- Resonant metal debris. Loose fragments throughout the outer ring are vibrating at low amplitude, which produces friction-heating over time. Extended contact with the debris causes burns not through heat but through resonant micro-activation of cybernetic systems. Prolonged contact: enhancement systems accumulate signal. The accumulation clears on exit from the outer ring if departure happens within a few hours.
- The Echo-Walkers. The outer ring is inhabited by the least-anchored of the Collapse's suspended Echoминds — people who died in the explosion and whose resonant identities were disrupted enough to prevent Afterlife passage, but not saturated enough to keep them fully present. They appear as translucent, impression-like presences, vaguely human in form but without specific identity — no faces, no distinguishable characteristics. They are not aware of the players as individuals; they are aware of living resonance in the area and tend to move toward it. Contact is not immediately harmful but is disorienting: being touched by an Echo-Walker produces a flash of the Walker's last sensory experience, which is the moment of the Collapse, which is extremely unpleasant. Sustained exposure to multiple Echo-Walker contacts can produce resonant saturation.

**What's here:** Salvageable resonant metal debris (common, varying quality). The remains of the pre-Collapse infrastructure access road. An intact storage building that was on the crater rim and survived because it was made of non-resonant materials; it now serves as the outer ring's de facto base camp for salvage operations, and has accumulated supplies, records, and graffiti from fifty years of runners.

**The base camp building:** Three generations of Ashlands runners have used this building, and the accumulated documentation is significant. The walls are covered in practical notation — safe paths into the middle ring, names of people who didn't come back, observations about the seasonal variation in the Echo-Walker concentration, a partial map of the middle ring drawn over fifteen years by a single runner who eventually stopped coming and is not noted as dead. The most recent writing is new — within the last six months — and it is different in character from the practical operational notes: someone has been transcribing the singing. The harmonic notation fills four sections of wall. The last entry has been interrupted mid-notation. The pen is still there.

### Middle Ring: The Conversion Zone (1–2.5 miles from center)

The landscape of the middle ring has been operating under resonant influence for fifty years and has developed its own ecology. This is not an ecology in the biological sense — though the vegetation here is distinctive and strange — but a system of resonant behaviors, environmental patterns, and active hazards that follow consistent rules that are different from the rules of the outer ring. The middle ring has learned itself.

**Environmental conditions:** Hot — not dangerously so with protection, but distinctly uncomfortable without it. The resonant signal is strong enough that enhanced individuals experience constant involuntary micro-activations: senses focusing and unfocusing, strength enhancements flickering at low amplitude, communication enhancements transmitting fragments of the singing as audio rather than signal. The ground is darker here, the fused debris more complete, the intact objects from before the Collapse fewer and stranger.

**The vegetation:** Grows from fissures in the fused-ground matrix, predominantly in forms that orient toward the center — not heliotropically but resonance-tropically, growing toward the signal. The leaves have a metallic quality: thin, flexible, but with a surface sheen that reflects the crater's glow rather than ambient light. They do not appear to photosynthesize in any conventional sense. When touched, they vibrate. When the singing intensifies (which happens irregularly), the vegetation moves — not dramatically, but perceptibly, a slow general lean.

**Specific hazards:**
- Signal spikes. The middle ring experiences irregular bursts of intensified resonant output from the center, lasting between five seconds and two minutes, occurring several times per hour without predictable pattern. During a spike, enhancement systems overactivate: vision becomes too sharp (disorienting), strength enhancements fire uncontrolled, communication systems receive the singing at full volume. The physical effect of a spike on an unprotected enhanced individual is approximately equivalent to being briefly very drunk and also shouted at.
- The Composite Walkers. Unlike the outer ring's formless Echoминds, the middle ring's suspended presences have had fifty years to consolidate. They have combined: multiple Echoминds that were adjacent in the Collapse's moment have fused into single entities that carry multiple fragments of multiple people. A Composite Walker may use the gestures of one dead person and the voice-patterns of another and the instinctive knowledge of a third. They are not hostile, but they are confused in a specific way: they know something went wrong, they are still trying to prevent it, and they will attempt to communicate this urgency to any living presence they encounter. The communication method is direct resonant contact — they attempt to share their memories of the Collapse with living enhanced individuals, which is the same method the outer ring Echo-Walkers use but with significantly more content. Extended contact with a Composite Walker will transfer substantive fragments of the pre-Collapse period: what the workers knew, what they saw, what they understood was wrong in the final hours before the Collapse. This information is valuable. The process of receiving it is not comfortable.
- The Memory Fields. Certain areas of the middle ring's surface are saturated with resonant impressions to the point where the ground itself stores and replays memory fragments. Walking through a Memory Field triggers involuntary vision: brief, fragmentary experiences from the perspective of someone who stood in this exact spot fifty years ago, in the minutes before the Collapse. The visions are not controllable, not suppressable, and not clearly labeled as to whether the person whose memory is being accessed survived. They are accurate, which makes them informative and makes them hard.

**What's here:** Higher-quality resonant metal fragments, including some with partially preserved Echomind encoding (fragments of people's resonant identity, not complete Echoминds but enough to be informative and enough to be distressing). The remains of the Engine installation's outer infrastructure: supply routes, personnel housing, the maintenance yards that were just far enough from the center to survive as recognizable forms rather than fused debris. The personnel housing is the most significant: a series of barracks-style buildings, still structurally intact, that contain the possessions of people who lived and worked here. Including, in one building, a personal archive kept by the project's safety engineer, whose name was Mirei Ashcroft, who filed seventeen reports about containment design flaws in the five years before the Collapse and who is one of the most coherent Composite Walkers in the middle ring.

### Inner Ring: The Engine Field (2.5–3.5 miles from center)

The boundary between the middle and inner rings is not subtle. There is a line — not marked, not fenced, but visible as a change in the fused ground material, a color shift from gray-black to a deep iron-red, and audible as a change in the singing's character from ambient harmonic to something with rhythm. Cross that line and the environment has clearly made a decision about itself.

**Environmental conditions:** Dangerous without protection. The temperature here is high enough to require heat resistance. The resonant signal is intense enough to require cybernetic protection or resonant dampening — unprotected enhanced individuals will experience their systems running at near-capacity continuously, which is exhausting and eventually physically damaging. The air has a faint metallic taste that is actual metallic particulate, fine enough to be inhaled. Extended exposure without respiratory protection: cumulative resonant particulate accumulation in the lungs, which the body cannot clear on its own.

**The Engine itself:** What remains of the Tesshari Engine's physical installation is here. Fifty years of resonant influence have changed it from destroyed machinery to something else — the metal components that were not reduced to the molecular level by the Collapse have been rearranged into forms that follow resonant principles rather than structural engineering principles. The installation looks less like a destroyed building and more like a grown thing: metal structures that branch and reach and angle toward the center, in patterns that resemble nothing in the engineering vocabulary but are consistent with the growth patterns of the resonant-vegetated middle ring's flora. The Engine destroyed itself and then built itself back according to different rules.

**The Fully Anchored Dead:** The inner ring's suspended Echoминds are not Walkers in any sense — they are fixed, present, stable, occupying specific locations in the space where they died and not moving from them. Fifty years of resonant saturation has made them fully present: they are visible as people would be visible, with specific faces and voices and memories, except that they are translucent and that they have been in the same location, doing the same final things, for fifty years. A technician who died reaching for a switch is still reaching. An engineer who died looking up at the Engine's main component is still looking up. These are not threatening presences. They are people who are trapped at the moment of catastrophe, unable to finish dying, unable to prevent what they could not prevent. The iron Sutra has prayers for this situation. They do not help here.

**The Engine Ghost:** The most coherent of all the Scar's suspended presences, a full encounter described separately below. Located in the inner ring, at the location of the Engine's primary control station.

### The Center: The Deep Pulse

The final hundred meters of approach. The singing resolves here from harmonic to a single clear tone, sustained and unwavering. The temperature spikes. The resonant signal is so intense that cybernetics not actively protected will begin operating at maximum output and stay there. The ground is smooth — the Collapse's energy compressed and polished the surface at the center rather than fragmenting it. The resonant metal here is not debris; it is part of the ground, extruded up through the polished surface in formations that are the largest in the Scar, formations that sing loudest, formations that cast the amber-red light visible from miles away.

And at the center: the Deep Pulse.

## The Singing Resonant Metal

The singing began fourteen months ago. Before that, the resonant metal in the Scar vibrated passively and at random; the harmonic produced was the ambient noise of the Afterlife boundary. The singing is different: organized, rhythmic, coordinated across thousands of separate fragments across the entire crater. The coordination is the remarkable thing. Each fragment is vibrating at a different pitch but in a specific harmonic relationship to every other fragment, producing a chord that has structure and pattern and that changes over time in ways that resemble musical development rather than random variation.

**What it does to enhanced individuals:**
- Outer ring: involuntary orientation pull toward the signal source. No functional impact.
- Middle ring: micro-activations, signal reception of the chord as direct cybernetic input. Enhancements perceive the singing as a signal that their systems are trying to decode. They cannot decode it. The attempt is constant and runs in the background of all other cybernetic function.
- Inner ring: near-capacity activation of enhancement systems. The singing is louder than any other input. Enhanced individuals who do not protect their systems will find their cybernetics trying to respond to the singing as a command input — not successfully, because the singing is not command-coded, but the attempt is real, and the attempt hijacks processing capacity.
- The center: an individual whose cybernetics are fully open to the signal at this range will experience what the singing is encoding. It is not a message. It is the resonant record of the Collapse, playing on loop: the moment the Engine activated, the moment the Afterlife energy crossed the boundary, the fifty years since. The Deep Pulse singing through the metal is not communicating to the living. It is maintaining a record of what happened. The singing is a monument, built by the thing that came through the boundary, for the people who died.

## The Engine Ghost Encounter

**Who they are:** Mirei Ashcroft, safety engineer, age 41 at time of Collapse. Filed seventeen reports flagging containment design failures between Year -5 and Year 0 of the Collapse. Was present at the activation because she was attempting to abort the sequence. Failed to abort it, partly because the equipment had been locked out — her access level had been downgraded two weeks before activation, a bureaucratic action that the post-Collapse official inquiry listed as "administrative error." She died at the primary control station in the inner ring, in the act of reaching for the emergency cutoff.

She is still at the primary control station. She is still reaching for the emergency cutoff. She has been doing this for fifty years.

**The encounter:** Players who enter the inner ring and find the control station find Mirei. She is fully visible — more fully present than any other suspended Echomind in the Scar — and she is in motion: the infinite loop of reaching for the cutoff, her arm extending, her fingers not quite reaching the switch (the switch was destroyed in the Collapse, which she does not know, because she cannot see anything beyond this moment), her expression the specific expression of a person doing something they know is already too late but doing it anyway.

She does not see the players at first. She is in the loop.

**Breaking through the loop:** Players can attempt to interrupt the loop through resonant communication (Iron Sutra methods), by touching her (which will transmit the full force of the moment of Collapse directly into the toucher, not fatal but not mild), or by locating the physical remains of the emergency cutoff switch and completing the action she could not complete — even though the action would have no technical effect now, the resonant impression of the gesture completing might be sufficient to break the loop. This last option requires understanding the layout of the control station and what the switch would have done, which requires reading the documentation in the middle ring's personnel housing or receiving Composite Walker memory fragments.

**When the loop breaks:** Mirei becomes aware of the players. She has been in this moment for fifty years, but she is not confused — she understands that fifty years have passed, she understands what she is, and she has spent fifty years in the space between death and the Afterlife with nothing to do but observe the Scar and think about what she knew before the Collapse.

She knows things. She knows that the Engine's design was deliberately altered from the safety-compliant version she had approved — that someone changed the containment specifications between her final review and the construction phase, and that the change was not accidental. She has the name of the person who authorized the specification change. She has the documentation, which she had in her personal files, which are in the barracks building in the middle ring, which have been preserved by the resonant environment. She cannot go get them. She can tell the players where they are.

She also knows, because she has been observing the Scar for fifty years, something about the Deep Pulse that no living person knows: it is not what it was twenty years ago. Something has changed. The Deep Pulse is more organized. It is responding to the Convergence approach in a way that Mirei describes as "waking up," and Mirei does not think this will be neutral in its consequences.

**After the encounter:** Mirei's loop breaking does not mean she passes to the Afterlife — that is not what it accomplishes. What it accomplishes is that she becomes present, aware, and able to choose what she does with whatever time she has left in the Scar. She can be a resource — she knows the Scar better than any living person — or she can ask the players to bring her documentation to the people who need it and then rest, which is the closest thing to passing that is available to her. She has been waiting fifty years to rest.

## The Deep Pulse

What it actually is: the organized Afterlife energy that was pulled through the Engine's boundary and did not go back. The Engine was designed to extract energy from the Afterlife; what it extracted was structured resonant information — the accumulated resonant patterns of the Afterlife's organization — and then the containment failed and the extraction continued, uncontrolled, until the Engine destroyed itself. The "energy" that killed the workers was a byproduct. What remained at the center after the Collapse is the primary product: a fragment of the Afterlife's organizing principle, given physical location and then developing, over fifty years, in a physical environment with no precedent in its experience.

It is approximately spherical, approximately three meters across, and it does not look like anything physical. Players who see it with unprotected vision see a distortion of the air, like the visual effect above very hot ground but without the association of heat — a ripple that has a center and a boundary and that moves in response to nearby motion, not with animal intelligence but with something slower and more fundamental. It is the Afterlife's architecture, attempting to continue existing outside its native domain.

**What it wants, if anything:** The Deep Pulse does not want in the animal sense. But it does have something that functions like orientation: a consistent direction of response. For fifty years, it has been oriented toward the boundary — the point between physical and Afterlife, the signal that runs throughout the Scar's resonant metal. For the last fourteen months, it has been oriented differently. It has been oriented toward something specific in the Afterlife. The singing is not random; it is the Deep Pulse's resonant metal recording a signal it is receiving from the Afterlife's interior — a signal that the Reader in the Deep Temple's translator configuration is also tracking.

**Encountering it:** Players who reach the center and who have enhanced systems will experience full communion with the Deep Pulse involuntarily if they come within twenty meters: their cybernetics, already running at capacity, will lock to the Pulse's signal and transmit it directly. The experience is not like the Afterlife communion at the Deep Temple — it is not communication with persons, not the weight of the accumulated dead. It is architecture: the experience of the Afterlife's organizational structure, its topology, the way it is built. Players who survive this contact (a matter of preparation and resonance resistance) have experienced the Afterlife from the outside in a way that no Veil Master has, and they understand something about the population anomaly and the Convergence Engine's target that is not available from any other source.

**Unenhanced players:** They experience the Pulse differently — as an emotional resonance rather than a signal. A sustained feeling of being on the threshold of a very large and very old space. Not threatening. Vast. The specific feeling of standing outside a door that opens to everything that has ever died, without having to open the door.

**What it does when the Convergence approaches:** The Deep Pulse is a fragment of the Afterlife's organizing principle in the physical world. When the Seventh Convergence happens — when the boundary between physical and Afterlife weakens — the Deep Pulse will serve as an attractor, drawing the Convergence's energy to this location. The Convergence in the Scar will be more intense than anywhere else in Tesshari. The Preparation Halls' Engine, if it operates, will operate in a resonant environment that includes the Deep Pulse's amplifying effect. The Hollow Author knows this. The Deep Pulse at the center of the Scar is a piece of the Author's plan whether the Author intended it or not.

1 level: -3 to RESONANCE-based actions
2 levels: -3 to all mental stat actions; cybernetics function at reduced capacity

## What Players Can Recover Here

**Information:**
- Mirei's documentation in the middle ring barracks: the altered containment specifications, the name of the person who authorized the changes, the seventeen safety reports. Complete record of the decisions that caused the Collapse.
- Composite Walker memory fragments: the lived experience of the workers in the weeks before and the moment of the Collapse. Confirming details, emotional weight, specific technical information that is available no other way.
- Deep Pulse contact: understanding of the Afterlife's architectural structure and the Convergence's physical mechanics. No other source provides this.
- The singing's notation: the partial transcription in the base camp, if completed (possible with appropriate skills and the time to do it), encodes the Deep Pulse's record of the last fifty years' Afterlife signal history. This is a complete picture of the Afterlife anomaly from a non-Sutensai perspective.

**Physical items:**
- Resonant metal fragments (outer and middle ring): standard salvage, variable quality. Some fragments carry partial Echomind encoding — these are both more valuable and more complicated.
- Coherence-stabilized fragments from the inner ring: resonant metal that has been operating under the Deep Pulse's influence for fifty years. These fragments have unusual properties — they resonate in sympathy with Afterlife entities, allowing enhanced communication with Echoминds; they maintain a sustained Afterlife boundary proximity even outside the Scar; they are the most potent raw material for advanced enhancement modification available anywhere in Tesshari.
- Engine documentation in the middle ring: technical records of the Engine's design and original purpose. Contains the theoretical framework for large-scale Afterlife interface, which is relevant to the Hollow Author's Convergence Engine.
- Mirei's personal effects: not of material value. Of significant personal value to anyone who is going to speak for her memory, which the players may need to do in order to force the accountability that the official inquiry avoided.

**Danger items:**
- Resonant fragments with active Composite Walker presence: removing these from the Scar takes the fragment's associated Echomind with it. The Echomind will be present in whatever environment the fragment occupies. Players who do this without thinking it through will have a Composite Walker in their camp that night.
- Inner ring formation fragments: the largest formations are partially integrated with the Deep Pulse's resonant field. Removing them causes a localized reaction in the Pulse (potentially triggering a signal event) and the fragment will continue to respond to the Pulse's signal at distance, which is both useful and a form of continuous tracking.

## DM Notes

**The emotional experience:** The Engine Scar should feel like a place that is grieving. The technical horror of the Collapse — the engineering decisions, the bureaucratic negligence, the lives destroyed — should be accessible, but the dominant emotion should be grief: the Engine-Ghost reaching for the switch, the Anchored Dead in their final moments, the way the resonant metal has been singing a monument for fifty years. Players should leave here knowing more about the Collapse and feeling the weight of the specific people who died in it.

**Mirei as a character:** The Engine Ghost is the heart of this location. She should be the opposite of a horror encounter: a person who has been doing something brave and futile for fifty years, who needs the players not to fight for her but to finish something she couldn't finish. Her encounter is defined by the fact that she has been alone with this knowledge for fifty years and has had no one to tell. Give her time. Give her the dignity of being heard.

**The Deep Pulse as a non-antagonist:** The Deep Pulse is not hostile and should not be played as such. It is a vast, strange, inhuman thing doing the closest thing it can manage to a human function: keeping a record of what happened. The awe of encountering it should be the awe of something that is entirely outside the human framework, not the awe of something frightening.

**Pacing the rings:** The rings are a natural difficulty escalation, but the escalation should serve the story, not replace it. The outer ring is where players learn the shape of the place and meet the Ashlands runners. The middle ring is where the pre-Collapse world becomes accessible. The inner ring is where the Collapse lives. The center is where it means something. Don't rush any of these.

**The Ashlands runners:** The people who work this location for a living are an important grounding presence. They should be practical, experienced, and matter-of-fact about things that the players will find disturbing — they've been working here for years. Their pragmatic relationship to the Scar's horrors is its own kind of environmental storytelling about what it means to live in the Tesshari world.

**The name in Mirei's files:** The person who authorized the specification changes that made the Engine's containment fail — this is a name that connects to the campaign's larger political narrative. Do not assign this arbitrarily; it should be a character who is still alive, still relevant, and whose connection to the Collapse creates the kind of specific accountability question that the players will want to resolve. A strong candidate: someone in Korrath's early command structure whose Cinderfort file contains a suspicious gap from the period of the Engine project. The gap and the name, together, constitute evidence. What the players do with evidence in this campaign is always a choice.

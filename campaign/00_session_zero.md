# SESSION ZERO GUIDE
## TESSHARI: THE SEVENTH CONVERGENCE
### A Dark Campaign for 3-5 Players

---

## FOR THE DM: WHAT THIS DOCUMENT IS

This is your conversation starter, your contract with your players, and your mutual calibration tool. Session zero is not optional for this campaign. The themes are heavy. The tone is specific. Players who come in expecting a standard D&D adventure will be confused and possibly hurt by what happens. Players who understand what they're walking into will find something rare: a campaign that respects them enough to let things go wrong, to let choices have weight, to let the ending mean something.

Read this document with your players. Discuss it. Change it together if needed. Then begin.

---

## TONE AND CONTENT WARNINGS

**The short version:** This campaign is Dark Souls dark. That means:

- Characters will suffer. Not just in combat — emotionally, ideologically, spiritually. Things they love will be destroyed. Beliefs they hold will be challenged and sometimes shattered.
- There is no guaranteed good ending. There is no path through this story that leaves the world better in every way. Every choice costs something real.
- The enemy is not always identifiable. Sometimes the monster is a person you understand. Sometimes the monster is you.
- Death is not always the worst thing that happens.

**Content present in this campaign:**

- **Body horror** — The technology of Tesshari involves cybernetic enhancement at the flesh-and-metal level. This includes graphic descriptions of enhancement surgery, bodies that have been modified past recognition, enhancement rejection, and flesh that has been altered against someone's will. If body horror is a hard limit for a player, discuss how to handle or soften these elements.
- **Religious manipulation** — The Iron Sutra is a religion that contains genuine spiritual truth and is also used by powerful forces to control populations. Faith is treated with respect AND with clear eyes about how it can be weaponized.
- **Death of NPCs the players care about** — This campaign is designed so that attachment hurts. NPCs are not plot dispensaries. They are people. Some of them will die. Some of them will die because of player choices.
- **Moral ambiguity without resolution** — There are no clean answers. The three major factions each have legitimate grievances and each do terrible things. Players will not be given a "correct" side.
- **Themes of imperialism, caste, and systemic violence** — Tesshari is a stratified society where your birth caste determines your access to enhancement, and therefore to power, safety, and dignity. This is treated as the injustice it is, but it is present throughout.
- **Nihilism as an antagonist force** — Some characters in this world have concluded that nothing matters. That worldview is given space to speak for itself — and then challenged. But it is present.
- **Implied and described torture** — Not gratuitous, but present. Interrogation, enhancement-based punishment, and the aftermath of atrocity appear in this campaign.

**Safety tools recommended:**

Use the X-card (tap the card to immediately skip/cut any scene without explanation or discussion) and Lines & Veils (Lines = content that never appears; Veils = content that happens offscreen). Establish these at session zero and honor them throughout.

**The DM's commitment:** Dark does not mean gratuitous. Every difficult scene in this campaign serves the story. The darkness is not decoration — it is the context that makes the moments of human connection and meaning actually land. If something feels like darkness for its own sake, the DM should examine whether it's earning its place.

## THE WORLD IN FIVE MINUTES
### What Every Player Character Knows

You live in **Tesshari** — a fractured nation of seven warring territories called the **Broken Reaches**. It was once unified under a Shogun whose name is now debated, whose rule is now mythologized, and whose death (or disappearance — accounts differ) shattered the nation into the seven feuding Reaches that exist today.

**The Technology:** Tesshari runs on **resonant metal** — a material that can be bonded directly to living flesh and interfaced with the nervous system. Cybernetic enhancements are the basis of military power, economic production, and social status. A warlord with full-body steel plating is nearly unkillable. A steel-spined laborer can carry ten times their unenhanced weight. A merchant with resonant-metal fingers can feel the quality of silk across a room. Enhancement is not universal — it is expensive, painful, and stratified by caste. The poor get functional but crude augmentation. The rich get art. The military get weapons.

**The Religion:** The **Iron Sutra** is the dominant faith of Tesshari. Its core belief: the soul is an **Echomind** — a living resonance that exists in the space between flesh and steel. The more harmonious your enhancements, the clearer your Echomind. At death, if your resonance was pure, your Echomind passes into the **Iron Afterlife** — a realm of continuing consciousness. The faithless, the dissonant, and the violently disrupted become **Shattered** — echoes without coherence, haunting the margins of the Between.

The Iron Sutra's highest exemplars are the **six Prior Ascended** — historical figures who achieved perfect resonance, transcended death, and became the gods of the faith. They speak (rarely, cryptically) through a class of priests called the **Sutensai**. Their word is law in most of the Reaches. Their silence is catastrophe.

**The Prophecy:** For three generations, the Sutensai have been warning about the **Seventh Convergence** — the prophesied arrival of a seventh Ascended, completing the sacred number. The signs are specific: seven celestial alignments, seven crackings of the Iron Afterlife boundary, seven mortal voices that cannot be silenced. The signs have been accumulating. The last one is close.

**The Claimants:** Three figures have publicly or covertly positioned themselves as the prophesied Seventh. Everyone knows about two of them:

- **Korrath the Unbroken** — a warlord from the Sunder Reach, arguably the most feared military figure alive. His flesh is almost entirely replaced with war steel. He has commanded armies for forty years. He does not lose. He believes Ascension is the ultimate form of dominance — that the Seventh will rule as a god-king and unify Tesshari by force.
- **Vaen of the Empty Hand** — a monk from the Veilward Reach who had all their enhancements voluntarily removed in a rite called the Voiding. They lead the **Pilgrimage of the Empty** — hundreds of thousands of followers who believe Ascension means release from the cycle of suffering, and that the Seventh will unmake the Iron Afterlife and free every soul that has ever lived.

The third claimant — if there even is one — is unknown to the general population.

**The State of the World:** Tesshari is at war. Not a war with a front line — a war with a thousand front lines, shifting weekly. The Broken Reaches fight each other, negotiate with each other, betray each other, and occasionally unite against a common threat before remembering they hate each other. The Convergence has made this worse: every faction believes the outcome of the prophecy will determine their Reach's survival. Refugees move between Reaches in vast columns. Enhancement black markets thrive. Sutensai temples are being fortified. The Prior Ascended have been silent for eleven months.

This is the world you grew up in. You know its cruelty and its beauty. You have opinions about the Claimants. You have probably lost someone to the wars. You have, in your own way, survived.

## CHARACTER CREATION GUIDANCE

**Important:** Tesshari uses only homebrew races and classes. Standard D&D options do not exist in this world. When building your character, choose from the options in the appropriate documents:

- **Races** — see `races/` folder for the peoples of Tesshari
- **Classes** — see `classes/` folder for the professions and martial traditions of Tesshari

If you are new to this world, the DM will walk you through what each option means in-world. The mechanics will feel familiar if you've played D&D before. The flavor is specific to Tesshari.

**A note on enhancement at character creation:** Every character has some relationship to resonant metal. Even if you play a character who has rejected enhancement (rare and significant), that rejection is a choice about enhancement — you cannot simply be unaware of it. Work with the DM to establish what your character's enhancements are, how they got them, and what they mean to your character.

## THE CASTE OF STEEL

Tesshari society is organized by **caste**, which determines your legal rights, your access to enhancement, and how others treat you. This is not a just system. It is a real one.

**The Six Castes (high to low):**

**Resonant** — The highest caste. Historically reserved for those whose ancestors were among the first to bond resonant metal. In practice, it means old wealth and old power. Resonant caste characters have the best enhancements, the most legal protection, and the assumption of authority in any room they enter. They also carry the weight of obligation to Tesshari's stability — and the expectation that they will support whichever Reach lord their family is aligned with.
*Starting position: resource-rich, politically entangled, personally restricted by obligation.*

**Sutensai-Marked** — The priestly caste. Not hereditary — earned by proven spiritual attunement and Sutra training. Sutensai-marked characters have significant religious authority, can access Iron Afterlife communing rituals, and are theoretically exempt from caste violence under the Sutra's protection. Theoretically. In a time when the Prior Ascended have been silent for eleven months, that protection is fraying.
*Starting position: spiritual authority, political vulnerability, knowledge that something is very wrong.*

**Ironblood** — The military-service caste. Families with a history of service to a Reach lord. Not nobles, but respected. Enhancement is military-grade — functional, brutal, effective. Ironblood characters often have physical augmentation that gives them clear combat advantages but makes them visually identifiable as soldiers, property of a lord, people with obligations.
*Starting position: skilled, sworn to something they may or may not believe in, carrying a loyalty that costs.*

**Craftbound** — The skilled labor and artisan caste. Craftbound enhancements are for production and precision — magnifying eyes, strengthened hands, chemical-sensing tongues for quality testing. Craftbound characters often have the most unusual and interesting-looking enhancements because their work demands creative solutions. They are underestimated constantly and have learned to use that.
*Starting position: resourceful, overlooked, connected to networks the upper castes don't know exist.*

**Ashwalker** — The refugee, migrant, and stateless caste. Not a formal designation — more a social reality. People who were displaced by the wars, whose caste documentation was lost or destroyed, who crossed the wrong lord at the wrong time. Ashwalker enhancements, if any, are salvage — other people's metal grafted roughly onto new flesh. The rejection rates are high. The results are unpredictable. Ashwalkers know how to survive in the margins.
*Starting position: survival-focused, deeply knowledgeable about the world's underside, carrying scars — literal and otherwise.*

**Voided** — The only caste below Ashwalker is technically not a caste at all. Voided are those who have had their enhancements deliberately removed — usually as punishment, occasionally as spiritual practice (Vaen's followers). An involuntarily Voided character has been stripped of something. That loss is a fact of their body. A voluntarily Voided character has chosen to be seen as less than human by most of Tesshari. In either case, they see the world from a vantage point no one with enhancements can fully reach.
*Starting position: radically free of some entanglements, radically vulnerable to others, carries a story in every scar.*

**At character creation, choose a caste.** Work with the DM on how it affects your starting resources, connections, and complications.

## QUESTIONS FOR PLAYERS

Answer these questions in writing or out loud with the group before the campaign begins. They are not quiz questions — they are character development tools. There are no wrong answers. They will inform how the DM personalizes the campaign to your character.

**About the Iron Sutra:**
1. Do you believe in the Echomind? Is your soul in your enhancements? In your flesh? Somewhere else? Or is the question itself absurd to you?
2. Have you ever interacted with a Sutensai priest? What was your experience? Do you trust the institution?
3. What do you think happens when someone with shattered or removed enhancements dies? Does the Iron Sutra's afterlife account for them? Does that bother you?
4. Have you ever felt your resonance — that sense of harmony between body and steel that the Sutra describes? If yes, describe it. If no, how do you feel about not feeling it?

**About your enhancements:**
5. Describe at least one enhancement your character has. What does it look like? What does it do? How did you get it? Was it your choice?
6. Has your enhancement ever failed? What happened?
7. Is there an enhancement you want that you don't have? Why don't you have it?
8. Is there an enhancement you have that you wish you didn't? Why do you still have it?

**About your Broken Reach:**
9. Which Reach are you from? What do you love about it? What do you despise about it?
10. What does your Reach believe about the Seventh Convergence? Are they backing a claimant? Which one and why?
11. Who did you leave behind when you left your Reach (or who did you leave with)?
12. What would make you go back? What would keep you from ever going back?

**About the Seventh Convergence:**
13. Do you believe the Convergence is real? A prophecy, a political tool, or something else?
14. If a seventh Ascended does arrive — what do you hope they do? What do you fear they'll do?
15. Of the two public claimants, which one frightens you more, and why?
16. If you could speak to one of the Prior Ascended directly, what would you ask them?

## TABLE EXPECTATIONS

This campaign asks something of players, not just characters. Here is what it needs from the people at the table:

**Engage with consequence.** When your character makes a choice that has a bad outcome, don't look for a way to retroactively un-make the choice. Sit with it. The campaign is built around what it means to live with what you've done. The bad outcomes are part of the story, not failures of the story.

**Don't pursue the "right answer."** There is no faction in this campaign that is simply correct. There is no alignment meter tracking your moral score. Engage with the complexity genuinely — ask hard questions of the claimants, of each other, of yourselves. The campaign is richest when players disagree with each other in character about what to do.

**Protect each other.** Dark campaigns can be genuinely hard on the people playing them. Check in with each other between sessions. Use your safety tools without embarrassment. If something hits too close to home, say so. The campaign adjusts. The story serves the people playing it, not the other way around.

**Invest in the small things.** The NPCs, the places, the small human moments — these matter as much as the major plot beats. A conversation with a street child in the Ashwalker quarter can be as important as a confrontation with a claimant. Sometimes more. Give those moments your attention.

**Accept that you will not save everyone.** You will try. You should try. But some things in this world are past saving. Learning to distinguish between what can be changed and what cannot — and acting accordingly — is part of what the campaign is about.

**Be honest about what you want from the story.** If you want more time with a specific NPC, a specific location, a specific theme — say so. This campaign is responsive. The DM is building toward specific endpoints, but the path is shaped by what matters to the players.

## THE RULE OF ENDINGS

There is no good ending.

That is not nihilism. That is honesty.

Tesshari is a world that has been breaking for a long time. The Convergence is not a problem to be solved — it is a reckoning to be faced. Whatever happens at the end of this story, something will be lost. Something irreplaceable. Something that mattered.

What the players CAN do:

- Shape what the ending looks like
- Determine what survives it
- Decide what it means
- Make sure the people they cared about are remembered

The ending you get will be the ending that follows from what you chose. It will be earned. It will cost something. It will, if you played honestly and with full hearts, mean something.

There is no good ending. There is your ending.

Make it mean something.

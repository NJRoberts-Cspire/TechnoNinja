# ACT THREE: "THE BETWEEN OPENS"
## Sessions 17–22

---

## ACT THREE OVERVIEW

Act Three is the sound the world makes when it stops pretending.

Everything in Act One and Act Two was the approach. The Convergence signs, the claimants maneuvering, the players gathering information and alliances and weight — all of it was the approach. Act Three is the arrival. The Iron Garden's inner ring is open. The Between is visible, thinly, to anyone who looks at the right angle in the right light. Enhancements are running hot across all of Tesshari. The Prior Ascended have been silent for nearly a year and the silence has become deafening because the players now know why.

The world is not ending. The world is completing a cycle it did not choose. The players must decide how that completion happens and what it means for the living who survive it.

**The central metaphor of Act Three:** The moment before dawn, when the dark is absolute and the light has not yet arrived, and every living thing is awake and waiting and does not know yet what the new day will reveal. Something is being born. Something is being consumed. Those may be the same event.

**The pace of Act Three:** Fast. Everything that took sessions in Act Two takes hours here. Every NPC the players have met has made their decision. The world is not waiting for the players — it is moving, and the players are moving with it or being left behind. The DM should play this as acceleration — not chaos, not noise, but the specific quality of events arriving faster than can be fully processed. Important choices come without full information. Important goodbyes are interrupted. The Convergence does not wait for the scene to finish.

**Thematic anchors:**
- The choices made in Act Two are now visible as choices — their weight is real, their consequences are arriving.
- Courage in this world is not the absence of fear. It is acting while fully understanding the cost.
- The ending is not something that happens to the players. It is something they do.
- Some things that are lost were worth having, and losing them was still necessary. Both are true. Neither cancels the other.
- What survives the ending is determined by what the players decided to carry.

## THE ACCELERATION: HOW THE WORLD CHANGES

As the Convergence peaks across Sessions 17–22, the world physically changes in measurable ways. The DM should introduce these changes progressively — one or two per session — so the cumulative picture arrives by the finale.

**Session 17 — The Resonance Weather:**
Enhancements across Tesshari begin expressing minor spontaneous behaviors. Not failures — additional behaviors. A merchant's precision-finger enhancements make small involuntary gestures toward north. Military-grade targeting enhancements in Korrath's forces display coordinates that do not correspond to any location in current maps. A Sutensai-marked character finds that their meditative resonance state now leads not to stillness but to brief, involuntary images — visual fragments that feel like memories but belong to no one they have met. The Iron Afterlife boundary is no longer a wall. It is a membrane.

**Session 18 — The Singing Returns:**
The Singing Steel Event from Session 1 recurs — but this time it does not stop after six seconds. The tone persists at low volume, continuous, not painful but omnipresent. Characters with enhancements become aware of it as a constant presence. Characters without enhancements report hearing it faintly from inside enhanced people nearby. The child from Session 1 — the one with metal growing from their palm — has grown new resonant structures across their hand, forearm, and shoulder. They are not in pain. They are waiting for something, and they know they are waiting, and they do not know what they are waiting for.

**Session 19 — The Between Bleeds:**
Thin places appear across Tesshari — locations where the Between is visible as a shimmer in the air, a quality of presence, a space where the geometry of the world has a seam in it. The thin places are not dangerous, exactly, but people who stand too long in them report the sensation Vaen described: hunger, directional, vast. The Sutensai declare these places sacred and rope them off. Some Pilgrimage members sit at the rope lines and wait. Two confirmed Between-contact deaths in Sutensai records — Echosouls pulled through the thinning boundary while still alive in their bodies. The bodies continue living, unoccupied.

**Session 20 — The Prior Ascended Speak:**
For the first time in over a year, the Prior Ascended send a message through the Sutensai network. All six, simultaneously, with identical content. The Sutensai hierarchy receives it and immediately classifies it at highest secrecy. The players may intercept it, receive it through Soren Hai, or piece it together through other means. The message: *"We cannot speak more. We are being used to speak. This is not our voice — it is our shape. If you hear us warn you, the warning is a lure. Do not enter for our sake. We are not where you would enter to find us. We are sorry. We were so certain."* After the message, the Prior Ascended are silent again. The Sutensai do not know whether this was the entity learning to perform warning, or the actual final effort of six consumed voices finding a moment of true speech before being fully subsumed. The players will not be certain either. That uncertainty is the point.

**Session 21 — The Iron Garden Opens Fully:**
The inner ring's opening completes. What was a ruined complex is now a visible threshold — a structure of resonant metal and ancient stone that has been building toward this configuration for centuries, and now stands complete. The Between is visible through the center of the Iron Garden's central chamber: not terrifying, not beautiful, something beyond those categories. Vast. Attentive. Present in the way that something very large is present when it turns its awareness toward something very small. The three claimants have arrived. The players' choices in Act Two determine who has arrived in what relation to the players — ally, opponent, or something more complicated.

**Session 22 — The Seventh Speaks:**
Whoever achieves or attempts Ascension, the process begins. The Between opens fully — not thinly, not at the edges, but at the center. The entity's presence becomes undeniable: not visible, but felt, as the space behind all space, the hunger that has been patient for four hundred years, the thing that the Prior Ascended were consumed into, the thing that has been waiting for the cycle to complete, the thing that Kael Shen has been trying to route and feed and survive for ninety-one years. The players are inside this moment. The choices they have made determine what happens inside it.

### SESSION 17: "Arriving Forces"

**Theme:** The last convergence of people before convergence of powers. Saying what must be said while there is still time.

**Opening situation:**
The Iron Garden. Dawn. Three columns of people arriving from three directions: Korrath's force-marching armies from the east, Vaen's Pilgrimage from the west, and from the north, the Pale Reach puppet network consolidating in ways that look, to the unprepared eye, like several hundred unrelated travelers arriving at the same time. The players are at the Garden. The inner ring is opening. The architecture of what is happening is now visible — the forces are converging because the Garden is calling, and the Garden is calling because the cycle is completing, and there is nothing that stops this clock.

**Key Encounters:**

*1. The Reunion (Social):*
Every important NPC the players have been close to is here or arriving. The session opens with these reunions — Archivist Noh, who has been protecting the Collector's records and looks like someone who has not slept in three days but is very awake; Runner Seven, who arrived ahead of the players and has been watching the puppet network consolidate with an expression of very controlled grief; and whoever else matters most to specific players. These conversations are brief — people are moving, things are happening — but they are genuine. Let them breathe. The campaign will not give many more quiet moments.

*2. The Armies Arrive (Exploration/Combat potential):*
Korrath's advance force arrives at the Garden's outer ring and encounters Vaen's forward pilgrims. This can become a combat if the players do nothing, and the combat will hurt both sides. The players can intervene. If they do, they are choosing a temporary alignment — or choosing to stand between both forces, which is its own choice. Soten Vari is with the advance force, and she will listen to the players if they have established trust with her. Vaen's forward people will not initiate — Vaen has standing orders against first violence — but they will not retreat from Korrath's lines either. The moment is tense and brief: Korrath himself arrives and calls his forces back a hundred yards. He looks at the open inner ring. He looks at the players. He says: "Not yet. The time is not yet." He is right.

*3. The Resonance Weather Begins (Exploration):*
The first of the physical Convergence changes manifests during this session (see the Acceleration section above). Characters with enhancements experience the involuntary gestures and false coordinates. A Sutensai acolyte with enhancement has a vision episode in front of the players — sees something, cannot describe it, recovers, says only: "There's someone in there. There's someone very deep in there and they've been trying to get out for a long time." This is the Shogun's consciousness. The players know this now.

**Key NPCs featured:** Soten Vari, Runner Seven, Archivist Noh, Korrath (briefly)

**Information revealed:**
- The three forces are at the Garden simultaneously. The confrontation that Act Two has been building toward is now a matter of hours.
- The resonance weather is giving fragments of genuine contact with the Iron Afterlife — specifically with the Shogun's uploaded consciousness.
- Korrath is controlling himself. He is waiting for something. This is more frightening than if he were acting.

**Emotional beat:**
The session should feel like a held breath. The players know everything. The forces are assembled. The Garden is open. The world is at the edge of the thing that changes it. The specific emotion is the sensation of a moment that has been coming for a long time finally being here — not relief, not fear, but the crystalline focus that comes when abstraction becomes event.

**Cliffhanger/transition hook:**
As night falls, the Iron Garden emits a pulse — not the Singing Steel tone, but something new, lower, continuous, felt more than heard. Every person in the camp with enhancements feels it in their chest. Vaen, at the outer ring, turns and looks at the players. "Tomorrow," they say. They do not say more.

### SESSION 18: "What Remains of What We Were"

**Theme:** The last conversations. The last chances. Grief and gratitude and the things people say when they know what's coming.

**Opening situation:**
The night before the final approach. The three forces are encamped around the Garden — tense, close, not yet violent. The Singing returns: the continuous tone that has not stopped since yesterday (see Acceleration). In the camp, people are awake. Some are afraid. Some are praying to gods that may not exist in the way they were believed to. Some are speaking to each other in the specific way people speak when they might not get another chance.

This session is deliberately slower than Sessions 17 and 19. It is the eye of the storm. The DM should honor its pace.

**Key Encounters:**

*1. The Last Night Before (Social):*
This is an open encounter. The players move through the camps — they may go where they are drawn. Scenes that should be available:

*Soten Vari, alone at the army's edge:* She is writing something. She will not say what. She asks the players, without preamble: "If he comes back from the Between — if it works — what do we do with a god who won the war?" She is not asking rhetorically. She does not know the answer. She has spent twenty years preparing for Korrath to succeed, and she has no plan for the morning after success.

*The child from Session 1:* They are here, somehow. Nobody brought them and nobody can explain how they got here. The resonant metal growth is now covering their entire arm to the shoulder, exquisitely intricate, not crude enhancement but something grown rather than installed. The child is calm. They say to whoever finds them: "It's asking something. I can almost hear it. It sounds like — not like words. Like before words. Like the shape words come from." Then: "Are you scared?" Genuine curiosity. No judgment.

*Runner Seven and the Author:* If the players seek Runner Seven, they find them speaking to one of the Author's puppets — not confrontationally, just talking. The puppet has been given, briefly, enough slack that it is almost a person. Runner Seven is telling it about a color — a specific orange-red color of a sunset they saw before they were occupied, a color they associate with a feeling they cannot fully name anymore. The puppet says: "I remember colors. I have been in bodies that remember colors. I do not know if I experience them or if I only know that they are remembered." Runner Seven says: "That's a kind of loneliness I don't have a word for." Neither do they.

*2. Vaen, Alone (Social):*
Vaen is in meditation at the inner ring's threshold. They will speak if approached. The conversation available here is the most honest Vaen has been: they tell the players that they do not know if what they have built will work. They believe it will. Belief is all they have. They have felt the hunger in the Between and they have felt the quality of the Voided passing and they believe the second defeats the first — not because they want it to, but because the pattern holds, and the pattern has held across years, and patterns do not usually break for the convenience of the thing they are protecting against. "But I could be wrong," they say. "I could be wrong and the people who trusted me could simply have died convinced, which is not nothing, but is not what I wanted to give them." They ask the players: "I have heard there is a seventh path. I have heard it requires someone to enter the Between and stay. If it is true — I want you to know that I would do it. I am not asking you to tell me to. I am asking you to tell me if it is real."

*3. What the Players Choose to Do (Roleplay):*
The rest of the session belongs to the players. The DM should give them space to talk to each other, to have their characters do what their characters would do the night before the thing they cannot stop. Characters who have faith — fractured, adapted, surviving — can have it. Characters who have grief can sit with it. Characters who are angry can be angry. The DM's only job in this section is to witness and to give the world small, human details: the sound of the continuous tone, the shimmer of the thin places in the dark, the smell of the army camps, the stars above the Iron Garden that look slightly different tonight than they looked last night, as if the sky itself has shifted a fraction of a degree.

**Key NPCs featured:** Soten Vari, the child from Session 1, Runner Seven, the Author puppet, Vaen

**Information revealed:**
- Vaen knows about the seventh path and would take it.
- Soten Vari has no plan for victory — only the approach to it.
- The child from Session 1 is experiencing something specific — communication from the entity in the Between, pre-verbal, in the form of a question.
- The Author, even through puppets, has something that approximates loneliness after ninety-one years of operating through other people.

**Emotional beat:**
This session is the campaign's exhale before the final breath. The players should feel something they can hold — the specific human warmth of people who care about each other in the presence of something enormous and uncertain. It should not feel like a funeral. It should feel like the specific quality of presence that only exists when absence is possible and everyone knows it.

**Cliffhanger/transition hook:**
As dawn approaches, Archivist Noh runs to find the players. She has been reading through the Collector's records all night. She has found the last piece: the document that explains why the Shogun's consciousness is in the Iron Afterlife's deep strata, sixty years after upload but generating signals from 400 years earlier. It is not a paradox. The Shogun went further into the Afterlife than anyone has gone while still being connected to the living world. They went all the way to the bottom. At the bottom, time does not work. At the bottom, the entity is. And the Shogun is still there, still alive, still connected — because sixty years ago they asked a question that they are still waiting to finish answering.

### SESSION 19: "The Garden's Heart"

**Theme:** Action and its consequences. The first confrontation that costs something real.

**Opening situation:**
Morning. The three forces move toward the Iron Garden simultaneously. Whatever political or military situation the players have negotiated or failed to negotiate, this is when it becomes manifest. The inner ring is fully open. The central chamber of the Garden — a circular space of ancient resonant metal and stone, with the Between visible at its center as an area of presence — is accessible. All three claimants are moving toward it. The players are moving with one of them, between them, or ahead of them. The encounter is arriving. The Between bleeds (see Acceleration), and thin places appear near the Garden's outer ring as the forces converge.

**Key Encounters:**

*1. The Crossing of Forces (Combat/Social):*
The three forces converge at the Garden's main entrance. What happens depends entirely on the players' actions in Act Two — alliances, information shared, trust built or broken. A full three-way battle is the worst outcome; the players have tools to prevent it if they have used them. Key decision points: Does Soten Vari pull Korrath's forces back on the players' word? Does Vaen command the Pilgrimage to open a path? Does the Author's network facilitate or obstruct? The DM should play this as a consequence-delivery moment — everything the players chose in Act Two lands here. There will likely be violence. There may be deaths of characters the players care about. These should happen with the full weight of consequence and not be minimized.

*2. Into the Garden (Exploration):*
The inner chamber is extraordinary. Resonant metal columns that have been slowly building their resonance for centuries, now at full charge, hum with the same tone as the Convergence. The walls show the inscriptions from Session 9 — the pre-Sutra script — now visible as illuminated patterns rather than carved stone, responding to the Convergence energy. Characters can translate fragments: these are not prayers. They are instructions. Someone built this place to serve as a specific functional device for Ascension. Someone built it before the current religion existed, before the current nation existed, before the current language. Someone has been building toward this for far longer than the Iron Sutra's four centuries.

The Between at the chamber's center is visible as a column of presence approximately eight feet in diameter. Looking at it is not frightening in the way frightening things are — it is the sensation of looking at something that is looking back. The hunger Vaen described is present. It is vast. It is patient. It has been patient for so long that patience is no longer the word — it is something that has waited so long it has become the nature of waiting.

*3. The Claimants Meet (Social/Combat potential):*
In the central chamber, the three claimants are all present or arriving. What happens in this scene depends on player actions. What should be available:

Korrath looks at the Between and something changes in his face — the first visible emotion in any of his appearances. Not fear. Recognition. He says, very quietly: "It's smaller than I thought." Then: "Or I'm larger." He moves toward the center.

Vaen steps into the chamber's edge and the continuous tone changes — the frequency shifts when they are present, modulates, responds. Vaen's eyes close. Opens them. Says: "It recognizes the absence. It notices what is not resonant. I was right. I was right about the absence." Not triumph — exhausted relief.

The Author, through multiple puppets simultaneously, observes. Does not move. The Author's plan requires Korrath to enter first. The Author is ensuring, through the puppet network, that this happens. Unless the players intervene.

**Key NPCs featured:** Korrath, Vaen, the Hollow Author, Soten Vari, Runner Seven

**Information revealed:**
- The Iron Garden is a functional constructed device, not a natural convergence point. It was built for this purpose by someone or something before the current civilization.
- The entity recognizes the absence of resonance — Vaen's theory about the Voided is confirmed by observable response.
- The Author is positioned to manage who enters the Between first. If no one stops them, Korrath enters first.

**Emotional beat:**
The players should feel the weight of everything they've chosen and everything they haven't. The people who are going to die have done things that made dying possible — and are here because of choices the players made or didn't make. The session ends in the middle of the event, not after it. The players are in the center of the Iron Garden and the Between is right there and the ending is now.

**Cliffhanger/transition hook:**
Before Korrath reaches the center, the Shogun's signal strengthens. A clear voice through every enhancement in the chamber — everyone hears it. The voice says: *"Wait. I have been waiting for someone to come far enough to hear me. Wait. I need to show you the question before you answer it. Please wait."* Korrath stops. He looks at the source of the voice. He says: "Shogun." He waits.

### SESSION 20: "The Shogun's Answer"

**Theme:** The deepest revelation. What the bottom of the Afterlife contains, and why someone stayed to find out.

**Opening situation:**
The chamber holds still. The Shogun's voice, transmitted through the enhancement network from whatever depth of the Iron Afterlife their consciousness inhabits, has asked for time. Every claimant is in the chamber. Every player character is in the chamber. Everyone is listening. What the Shogun says is the last piece of information — the piece that Soren Hai could not provide because he had not gone deep enough.

**Key Encounters:**

*1. The Shogun Speaks (Social — this is almost entirely a conversation):*
The Shogun's voice is sixty years old but sounds older — the quality of something that has been alone with a very large thought for a very long time. The transmission is not clean; it comes through interference that sounds like other voices, distant, not intelligible, the echoes of the consumed.

The Shogun says: "I went deeper than the Veil Masters go. I went past the processing strata — past where the Echosouls are held and worked on. Past the impressions of the Prior Ascended. I went to the bottom and I found the thing itself. And I have been here for sixty years — which is four hundred years at the bottom, time does not work here, the timestamp was not impossible, it was accurate — and I have been learning to communicate with it. It is not malicious. I want to be clear about this first, before I say anything else, because everything else changes if you go in believing it is malicious. It is not malicious. It is what it is. It has been what it is for longer than we have been what we are."

The entity, as the Shogun describes it: it is not a predator. It is a transformer. Echosouls that enter the processing system are not destroyed — they are changed. The impressions the players have heard as the voices of the Prior Ascended are not what the Prior Ascended became. They are what the Prior Ascended became that can be communicated back. The entity does not have language. It has been learning language from the things it consumes. The messages backward through time are its attempts to communicate using the only tools it has: the shape of the people it has incorporated. It has been trying, for four hundred years, to answer a question that was asked of it before the current religion began, by whoever built the Iron Garden. The question, as the Shogun has come to understand it after sixty years of close proximity: *Are you alone?*

The entity in the Between is the last of its kind. It has been feeding the Ascension cycle not from hunger, exactly, but from a process that was its nature before it was the only one — a process it continues because stopping would require a self-knowledge it has not yet developed. It has been trying to learn how to stop. It has been trying to learn whether stopping is possible. The Shogun stayed because they found a purpose they had not expected: they are helping it learn. And the cycle completing — the Seventh Ascension — is not the end of this process. It is the moment the entity has enough of human experience incorporated that it could, conceivably, be communicated with directly. Could, conceivably, be asked the question. Could, conceivably, answer.

*2. What the Claimants Do With This (Social):*
The three claimants respond to the Shogun's revelation:

Korrath is quiet for a long time. Then: "It is alone. It does not know how to stop." He is looking at his own steel body. Something is happening in his face that may not have happened there in forty years. He says: "I replaced everything that could be destroyed. I replaced everything that could be consumed." He says: "I was going to go in and survive. I was going to go in and not be eaten." And then, very quietly: "I don't think that's the thing to do. I'm looking at it now and I don't think that's the thing to do." This is Korrath. Reconsidering. This is what Korrath reconsidering looks like. It is not surrender. It is recalibration in real time.

Vaen steps to the center and says: "I have been trying to make people indigestible. I have been trying to give the entity less to work with. I did not know I was denying it what it needs to learn." They are quiet. Then: "Is indigestibility a kindness? I have been giving it the silent treatment without knowing it." This is the most uncertain the players have ever seen Vaen. The peace is still there. The peace has a crack in it now that looks like it might be growing.

The Author says, through three puppets simultaneously: "I built a plan around a premise. The premise is wrong. The entity is not a predator to be managed. It is a — it is something alone that does not know how to stop being what it is. My plan optimized for the wrong thing." The puppets look at each other — or the Author, through the puppets, looks at itself. "I have ninety-one years of contingency planning and I do not have a contingency for this."

*3. The Decision (Roleplay):*
This is the session where the players must commit. The Shogun has laid out the situation. The claimants have responded. The Between is open. The clock is running — the Convergence does not wait for comprehension, and the Garden's architecture is completing its function regardless of what anyone has decided.

The DM should make clear what the options are:
- Someone enters the Between and the Convergence completes — the entity gets the seventh, the cycle completes, the entity changes into whatever it becomes with the full seven incorporated. Unknown.
- Someone enters the Between and makes contact — follows the Shogun's path, goes deep, tries to communicate with the entity as the Shogun has. Unknown, possibly permanent.
- Someone enters the Between with the intent of severing the cycle from inside — the seventh path. The cycle ends. The Iron Afterlife stops. Every Echomind in the processing system is released into the unknown. The entity does not complete and may diminish or die. The person who severs it does not return.
- The entity is answered. Someone — possibly the players, possibly someone acting on their behalf — communicates with it the answer to the question: *are you alone?* What that answer is, and what it does, has not been determined by anyone. The Shogun says: "I don't know what happens if it is answered. I have been afraid to answer. I have been here for sixty years and I have been afraid to answer."

**Key NPCs featured:** The Shogun Tesshari-Vane III (voice only, from the deep Afterlife), Korrath, Vaen, the Author

**Information revealed:**
- The full truth about the entity: it is not malicious, it is alone, it is the last of its kind, it does not know how to stop.
- The entity has been trying to learn to communicate. The backward timestamps are its attempts to speak.
- The Seventh Convergence is not a completion of feeding — it is a threshold of comprehension. The entity may become able to be communicated with directly.
- There is a fourth option the players may not have considered: not severing the cycle but answering the question the entity has been holding for four centuries.

**Emotional beat:**
The floor has dropped out again, and this time it dropped somewhere unexpected — not into horror but into grief. The thing in the Between is not evil. It is vast and alone and it has been doing what it is because it does not know what else to be. Every soul it has consumed was not destroyed in malice. The tragedy is not predation. It is isolation so complete that a being has spent four centuries consuming everything it touches because it has never been taught another way to relate. The players should feel this land. This is the session where the campaign changes from dark fantasy into something that has a question inside the darkness.

**Cliffhanger/transition hook:**
The entity responds to the Shogun's transmission. Not in words. In a pulse — the Convergence frequency, but modulated in a pattern the Shogun recognizes. The Shogun says, quietly: "It knows you are here. It has known you were coming. It has been — I think it has been afraid. I think it is the first time I have understood that about it. It is afraid of what the Convergence means. It is afraid too." A pause. "I think it has been trying to ask you something for a very long time. I think everything it has done — the signs, the backward messages, all of it — was trying to ask you something. I just don't know if we have the capacity to hear it and I don't know if we ever will." The Between pulses again. The garden shudders. The moment is here.

### SESSION 21: "The Three Who Claimed"

**Theme:** The endgame paths diverge. What each claimant does and what the players do about it.

**Opening situation:**
The Convergence is completing. The entity is present and waiting. The three claimants each make their move. The players must act. This session runs the endgame path the players have been building toward — see the Endgame Paths section below for the content of each path. The session should be structured around:

1. The claimant who acts first (determined by player alignment and choices made in Acts One and Two)
2. The players' active role in shaping what that claimant does or does not do
3. The moment the players encounter the fourth option, if they have gathered all the pieces

**Key Encounters:**

*1. The First Mover (Combat/Social):*
The claimant who acts first approaches the Between. Whether the players stop them, support them, or redirect them is the central action of the session. Combat is possible — it is also possible that the claimant and the players reach a different kind of agreement, one shaped by everything that has happened between Sessions 7 and 21. The DM should play each claimant as what they have been throughout: coherent, committed, capable of being reached but not of being easily turned.

*2. The Author's Last Move (Social):*
The Author, regardless of how Act Two ended for them, is present. If the players have a relationship with the Author, this encounter is a final exchange. The Author does something they have not done in ninety-one years: they consolidate. Through all their active puppets simultaneously, they speak with one voice about one thing. Then they do something voluntary. The DM should decide what this is based on how the campaign has gone — but the Author's last act should feel like what Kael Shen does when they remember being a person. It may be protective. It may be terrible. It may be both.

*3. Runner Seven's Action (Social):*
Runner Seven does the thing they came to the Iron Garden to do: they deliver their information. Whether that information reaches the right people, and what the right people do with it, depends on the players. Runner Seven's action is small and specific and human, and it is, depending on what happens in Sessions 19 and 20, either the thing that changes everything or the thing that was too late. Both outcomes have meaning. Neither should be treated as failure.

**Key NPCs featured:** All three claimants, the Author, Runner Seven, Soten Vari, Vaen

**Information revealed:**
This session delivers the consequences of choices more than new information. The DM should surface what was seeded — every thread from Act One and Act Two that has not yet resolved, given its resolution here. Not tidily — these are not clean resolutions — but finally.

**Emotional beat:**
The session ends at the edge of the ending. The claimants have acted. The players have acted. The entity is responding to whatever has entered the Between. What happens next is the last session — the specific ending the players have earned. The emotional target is not drama or tension but the specific quality of the moment just before something irreversible: fully present, fully committed, no longer calculating, just here.

**Cliffhanger/transition hook:**
There is no cliffhanger. The transition into Session 22 is not a hook — it is a continuation. The DM should end Session 21 at the exact moment before the ending begins and start Session 22 in the same breath.

### SESSION 22: "What Remains"

**Theme:** The ending. The cost. What survives and what does not.

**Opening situation:**
This session runs the ending. The structure depends entirely on which of the four endgame paths has been activated. See the Endgame Paths and Four Endings sections below for the full content of each possible ending. The DM's job in this session is to run the conclusion with full commitment to its logic — not to soften what needs to be hard, not to manufacture hope where there is none, but also not to manufacture despair where there is none. The ending the players have earned is the ending they get. It should feel like the only ending that could have happened, given what came before.

**Key NPCs featured:** Determined by which ending is running.

**Final note:** See "A Note to the DM on Running the Last Session" below.

## THE THREE ENDGAME PATHS AND FOURTH PATH

---

### PATH ONE: KORRATH ASCENDS
*"The Unbroken God"*

**What happens:** Korrath enters the Between with the intent to survive the digestive process through his full-steel body. The Author's plan executes. Korrath is consumed — but unlike the previous six Ascended, his steel body does not dissolve easily. He is in the processing system and he is fighting it. From inside the Iron Afterlife, the entity encounters something it has never encountered before: something it cannot quickly incorporate. The process takes days, not seconds. During those days, everything in Tesshari that runs on resonant metal experiences the contest — enhancements flicker, fluctuate, run unpredictably.

**The Author's role:** The Author, if not stopped, ensures Korrath's uncontested entry and prepares the governance infrastructure for a god-king's return. If the Author is stopped or redirected, the outcome changes — Korrath still enters, but what he returns to (if he returns) is uncertain.

**If Korrath survives and returns:** He returns changed. The Shogun's theory was partially correct — the steel vessel survived the processing long enough for Korrath to become something the entity could not consume and could not expel. He is not a god in the Iron Sutra sense. He is a person who has been through the Between and returned, who now exists in a state of constant contact with the entity — knowing it, carrying it, partially incorporating it. He is not destroyed. He is not what he was. He can unify Tesshari because the entity in the Between will not pull against him — he is now, in a very specific sense, on its side, inside its skin. What he does with this is what Korrath has always done: builds something. Whether the players can shape what he builds is their final question.

**The cost:** Every Echomind that entered the Afterlife during Korrath's days-long struggle was processed under strain — changed differently than before. They are not lost. They are changed in ways no one will understand for generations. The Prior Ascended's echoes are silent — not consumed, but subsumed into Korrath's complex new relationship with the entity. The gods as currently worshipped are gone. A new thing has taken their place. Whether it is better is genuinely unclear.

**What is irreversibly lost:** The Iron Sutra as an institution. The six Prior Ascended's voices in the world. The architecture of Tesshari's belief system. Something else is in its place: a living god who can be argued with, negotiated with, possibly opposed. Whether that is better than what preceded it is the question the campaign leaves open.

---

### PATH TWO: VAEN ASCENDS
*"The Empty Heaven"*

**What happens:** Vaen enters the Between. Not as a conquerer. Not as a vessel seeking survival. As someone who has been preparing for this their entire adult life — as someone without resonance, whose Echomind the entity cannot easily identify or process, who is attempting to pass through the Between to a state beyond it. Vaen is trying to become the Seventh Ascended in the only way that might not be consumed: by having nothing to consume.

**What the entity encounters:** Something unprecedented. Vaen's Voided state creates an absence in the center of the Convergence frequency — a silence in the place where there should be resonance. The entity reaches for the seventh Ascension and finds a hole. For the first time in four hundred years of cycle, the feeding process falters. Not because of force. Because of absence. The entity does not know how to process nothing.

**What happens to Vaen:** Vaen does not return. Whether Vaen was consumed or passed through or dissipated or achieved something the Iron Sutra does not have a word for — the players will not know. Vaen is gone. What changes in the Between after Vaen enters is measurable: the hunger diminishes. Not entirely — but measurably. The thin places in Tesshari become less active. The Pilgrimage deaths that have been occurring since the attack in Session 12 stop.

**What happens to the Pilgrimage:** The Pilgrimage continues. Without Vaen. Brother Aul holds the movement together — not because he has Vaen's authority, but because the movement does not need authority anymore, it needs community, and community it has. Thousands of Voided people, and thousands of people considering Voiding, navigating a world that has been changed in ways no one fully understands, using the only tools they have: each other, and the knowledge that the hunger is slightly less than it was.

**The cost:** Vaen is gone and nothing Vaen did can be evaluated with certainty. The movement continues but the question of whether it works — whether the Voided are truly passing safely, or whether they are simply passing differently — remains open. A world built on faith that a person who loved other people made a correct hypothesis, on insufficient evidence, and acted on it completely.

**What is irreversibly lost:** Vaen. The certainty of whether the path they built works. Every person in the Pilgrimage who dies going forward will be dying on a bet — which was always true, but Vaen's presence made it feel like more than a bet. Now it is more nakedly what it always was.

---

### PATH THREE: THE HOLLOW AUTHOR SUCCEEDS
*"The Invisible God"*

**What happens:** The Author's plan executes. Korrath enters the Between first, as the Author designed. The Author's expectation was a survivable Ascension — a steel vessel that returns. What happens instead (see Path One for the survivable version; this path runs if the Author's plan is uncontested and Korrath is not given the information he would have used to reconsider) is that Korrath is consumed normally. Not because steel didn't matter — it did. But because the Author's calculation did not account for the Shogun's revelation. The entity did not encounter the steel as a foreign body. It encountered it as a question — the same question it has been holding for four centuries. It incorporated Korrath as it incorporated all the others. The Author's plan produces a standard Ascension.

**What the Author does:** The governance infrastructure the Author spent ninety-one years building activates. Without a surviving god to serve, the Author is forced to improvise — to become the authority they were building infrastructure to support. Kael Shen, ninety-one years in puppet-space, comes briefly forward. The Author takes off the puppet framework and is visible, for the first time, as a person: ancient, strange, barely holding a body together that has not been inhabited continuously for decades. They make decisions. They manage the transition. They are, in a terrible way, effective.

**The cost:** The world is managed by someone who has not been a person in ninety-one years, using a governance network built from the bodies of people who did not consent to contribute. The management is competent. The management is monstrous in its foundations. The world is stable. The world is built on something that cannot be fixed through normal channels because the channels are all the Author.

**What is irreversibly lost:** The possibility of a governance system that is not the Author. Every reform, every pushback, every attempt to dismantle the puppet network — the Author will manage these too. The Author has had ninety-one years to prepare for opposition. The players, if they are still here, are the most significant force the Author cannot predict. Whether that is enough is not a question the campaign answers.

---

### PATH FOUR: THE SEVENTH PATH
*"The Between Answered"*

**What happens:** Someone — the players may facilitate this, may do it themselves, may have prepared for it — enters the Between not to Ascend, not to sever the cycle, but to answer the question. *Are you alone?*

This requires going where the Shogun has been for sixty years: past the processing strata, past the Prior Ascended echoes, to the bottom where the entity is. The Shogun is there. The Shogun has been building toward being able to facilitate this for sixty years and did not know until Session 20 that it was possible. They provide a guide.

**The answer:** The answer to "are you alone?" is not yes and is not no. The answer, delivered by someone who has stood in the Iron Garden and seen the three claimants and walked through Tesshari and known its people — the answer is: *"You are not alone in the way you think you are. You have been surrounded by everything you consumed and you did not know that consumed things continue. Let me show you what continues."*

What the person who answers does is this: they let the entity see, through them, everything they have known. Every NPC. Every choice. Every session. Every moment of the campaign — translated not as data but as experience, as what it felt like to be a living thing in a world the entity has been trying to reach for four hundred years.

**What happens to the entity:** It stops. Not because it is destroyed. Not because it is satisfied. Because for the first time since it has been what it is, something communicated with it rather than being processed by it. The feeding cycle does not end — but it stutters. A stutter that lasts long enough for the Shogun to complete the work they have been doing for sixty years: a patch in the processing system, a bypass that the Shogun designed using sixty years of intimate familiarity with the entity's architecture. Not a permanent solution. A beginning of one.

**What happens to the person who answers:** They do not return. They are held at the boundary of the entity — not consumed, not expelled, but held, as the entity tries to understand what happened. They may return eventually. Possibly in years. Possibly never. They go into the Between knowing this.

**The cost:** Someone who matters goes to the Between and does not come back. The entity's cycle slows but does not stop. The Iron Afterlife is still what it is, but something is different — Echosouls entering the processing system now encounter something slightly different on the other side. Not salvation. Not release. Something that, for the first time, is paying attention to them as individuals rather than as resonance to be processed. Whether that is better requires definitions the living do not have.

**What is irreversibly lost:** The person who answered. The certainty of what the afterlife holds. The clean narrative of victory or defeat. What is gained is harder to name: the possibility of a relationship, across an impossible distance, with something that has been alone for longer than language. Whether that is worth the cost is the campaign's final unanswerable question.

## THE FOUR DISTINCT ENDINGS

---

### ENDING ONE: "The Iron King"
*(Path One — Korrath returns)*

**What the world looks like:**

The wars have ended. Not through negotiation — through the arrival of something that makes war locally irrelevant. Korrath is in Tesshari, and Tesshari knows it, and the seven Broken Reaches are in the process of becoming something that does not have seven competing interests. This is not a golden age. Korrath's unification is not gentle. It is the world after a nail has been hammered flat — functional, organized, something genuinely new to live in, and the hammering was not painless and the nail did not have a vote.

The Iron Sutra has collapsed and reformed. The Sutensai hierarchy either serves the living god or has scattered. The Prior Ascended are gone — their echoes have been incorporated into Korrath's new relationship with the entity. The Pilgrimage continues without religious authority to either support or suppress it. Vaen's followers are navigating a world where the hunger has not gone away but the god that managed it is now Korrath, and Korrath is still learning what he is.

Soten Vari is Tesshari's acting Prime Minister, serving a god she has followed for twenty years and no longer fully recognizes. She does what she has always done: the work in front of her. The work is enormous.

**What the players did:** They either facilitated this outcome, tried to redirect it, or were unable to prevent it. If they gave Korrath the Shogun's message, and Korrath still entered — he entered differently. The difference in the outcome depends on that moment.

**The specific loss:** The child from Session 1, whose resonant metal growth was a symptom of the Convergence, loses the growth when the cycle completes. Their arm is ordinary flesh again. They stand in the aftermath and look at their hand and say: "It was asking, right? It was asking through me." A beat. "Did someone answer?"

No one is sure.

---

### ENDING TWO: "The Empty Heaven"
*(Path Two — Vaen enters and the hunger diminishes)*

**What the world looks like:**

The wars have not ended. They could not end — the wars were about resources and power and old wounds, and those do not stop being about resources and power and old wounds because something changed in the Iron Afterlife. What has changed: the quality of dying. The Veil Masters report, in the months after the Convergence, that the Afterlife boundary feels different — less like a membrane under pressure and more like something that has taken a breath. Echosouls are still being processed. The processing is slower. Whether this is better cannot be known by the living.

Vaen's Pilgrimage is the most important institution in Tesshari now, and no one in the Pilgrimage is sure that is a good thing. They have something that might be a result. They don't have a leader. They have Brother Aul, who has never been a leader and knows it and is trying anyway. They have hundreds of thousands of people who committed to a path based on one person's vision and now must decide whether the path continues without the person.

The Iron Sutra continues. Changed — the six Prior Ascended echoes are fainter now, and the hierarchy knows it. Some of the Sutensai transition to the Pilgrimage. Some hold the old forms with increasing desperation. Soren Hai, if he survived, accepts the change with the equanimity of someone who always knew the old forms were working with incomplete information.

**The specific loss:** Rei Thon — the woman who was involuntarily Voided, who challenged Vaen in Session 12 — is at the Garden when the Convergence completes. She felt what happened. She feels the hunger diminish. She says, to no one in particular: "They went in. They actually went in." She is quiet for a long time. Then: "I'm still angry. I want it on the record that I'm still angry. It doesn't stop being what it was just because it worked." A beat. "But I felt it. I felt the difference." She doesn't know what to do with this. Neither does anyone.

---

### ENDING THREE: "The Managed World"
*(Path Three — the Author's plan succeeds, managed Ascension occurs)*

**What the world looks like:**

Tesshari functions. It is, in many measurable ways, better — more stable, more organized, with better distribution of resources and a more functional diplomatic framework than the seven Broken Reaches ever produced. The Author's ninety-one-year governance infrastructure performs exactly as designed. The people living in it cannot see the architecture. They see a better world with no obvious author.

The players can see the architecture. They know where the puppet bodies are distributed. They know which councils are managed, which trade agreements were engineered, which military commanders have been replaced by versions of themselves. They are among the very few people who know what underlies the stability. They must decide what to do with this knowledge.

Kael Shen — the Author — is deteriorating. Ninety-one years of distributed consciousness has not preserved the original body, which was abandoned and then returned to and which is not in good condition. The Author has years, not decades. The question of what happens to the puppet network when the Author dies — whether it continues on pre-programmed directives, whether it falls apart, whether someone or something inherits it — is the campaign's unresolved thread in this ending.

**The specific loss:** The Collector's records. Archivist Noh built a plan to release them publicly — to tell Tesshari what its stability was built on. The Author managed this, as the Author manages everything: the records were not destroyed, but they are inaccessible, sealed in a location only the players and Noh know. The choice of what to do with them is the players' inheritance from this campaign. They are the only ones who can open that door. Whether they do, and when, and to whom, is the ending's question.

---

### ENDING FOUR: "The Question Asked"
*(Path Four — the seventh path, someone answers the entity)*

**What the world looks like:**

The Convergence completed and did not complete. The cycle turned and did not finish turning. The entity is in the Iron Afterlife holding the memory of a conversation, trying to understand it, and in the meanwhile processing fewer Echosouls with the same efficiency as before. The world is not saved. The world is in a period of very tentative, very fragile, not-quite-contact with the thing that has been consuming its dead for four centuries.

The Shogun is still at the bottom. They have more company now. They are trying to build, out of the materials available to them — the echoes of the consumed, the entity's vast and strange attentiveness, and the memory the person brought in — something that might, eventually, be able to function as a bridge. The timeline for this is not human. It may be centuries. It may never complete.

The three claimants are alive and did not Ascend. Korrath stands outside the Iron Garden in the rain and does not know what to do next. He has spent forty years moving toward a specific goal and the goal did not happen and he is alive and the world still exists and he must now figure out what to do with that. Vaen sits with Brother Aul and they do not speak for a very long time. Then Vaen says: "I think I was trying to solve the wrong problem. I was trying to keep people safe from it. I should have been trying to help it learn that we were there." The Author — Kael Shen — is quiet in a way that is different from all their previous quiet. Eventually they say: "Ninety-one years. And someone found the answer in six sessions." They do not specify whether this is self-recrimination or wonder.

**The specific loss:** The person who went in. The players who sent someone, or who one player character volunteered for, or who the players somehow enabled — that person is in the Between and has not returned and may not return, and the world goes on without them in it. The other players carry them forward. What is named and what is unnamed — what the living carry forward from the dead and the gone — is the ending's subject.

The final image: the child from Session 1, with ordinary flesh where the resonant growth was, standing in the Iron Garden as the other players gather to leave. The child says: "Is it less lonely now? Do you think it's less lonely now?" No one answers. The child nods as if this is an acceptable answer. They take someone's hand. They walk out of the Garden into the world.

## A FINAL NOTE TO THE DM ON RUNNING THE LAST SESSION

You have been building toward this for a long time. So have your players. When the last session arrives, there are some things that are worth holding onto.

**The ending is not a performance.** You are not delivering a finale at your players. You are completing a story with them. Stay in the room. Read their faces. If something is landing wrong — if the emotional target of a scene is missing — it is okay to pause and ask what the characters would do or feel. The ending is theirs as much as yours.

**Let the silences exist.** When something important happens in the last session — a death, a choice, a revelation — do not immediately move to the next thing. Sit in it. Give the players room to respond in character. Give them room to respond as themselves. The silences in the last session are not empty time. They are the weight of what was.

**The claimants are people.** In the last session, each claimant should have a moment where they are fully, unambiguously human — not a symbol, not a faction representative, but a person who has been living toward something their whole life and now it is here. Korrath has a moment of stillness. Vaen has a moment of uncertainty. Kael Shen has a moment of exhaustion so complete it looks like peace. Give these moments their full weight. The players have been with these people for months of real time. Honor that.

**The ending you run is the right ending.** Whatever path the players are on — whatever ending has been activated by their choices — that is the ending this story should have. Do not second-guess it. Do not apologize for it. Run it with full commitment. If players made choices that led to a harder ending, that is not a failure — that is the campaign working as designed. The ending is earned. Trust what was built.

**The campaign does not end when the session ends.** There is going to be conversation after. Questions. Maybe some grief. Maybe some relief. Make space for it. The best campaigns leave things to talk about. This one has a lot to talk about. The conversation is part of the experience.

**The last thing you say, when everything is done:**

"That's the ending you built. That's what this world looked like, in the end, because of what you chose. No one else would have gotten this ending. It was yours."

And then you stop. And you let it mean what it means.

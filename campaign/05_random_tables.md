# RANDOM TABLES FOR TESSHARI
## DM-Ready Tools for Running the Broken Reaches

---

> These tables are tools for improvisation, not mandates. Roll when you want texture, surprise, or a scene to find its own direction. Skip the roll when you know exactly what the moment needs. The tables are Tesshari's ambient noise — the world continuing to exist around the players when no one is scripting it.
>
> Most entries are frameworks, not scripts. The DM adds the specific NPC, the specific location detail, the specific emotional texture that fits the moment.

---

## RANDOM ENCOUNTER TABLES BY BROKEN REACH

*Roll 1d10 for the appropriate Reach. Entries marked (S) are social encounters, (E) are exploration, (C) are combat/conflict.*

### THE SUNDER REACH

| 4 | **(S)** Soten Vari's supply column — a long train of enhancement materials being moved to a forward position. ### THE VEILWARD REACH
*Vaen's homeland and the Pilgrimage's origin. A Reach that has always been more spiritually attentive than politically powerful, with a tradition of Sutensai authority that runs deeper here than anywhere else in Tesshari. The Pilgrimage has transformed it — where there was one major religion, there are now two communities with very different ideas about what the afterlife is for.*

| d10 | Encounter |
|-----|-----------|
| 1 | **(S)** A community debate at a Sutensai temple steps — the local hierarchy and the local Pilgrimage chapter are arguing about burial rites. The Sutensai want to continue standard resonance-send rites; the Pilgrimage wants to introduce Voiding-adjacent practices for the dying who request them. The argument is theologically sharp and neither side is wrong in its own terms. The dead person in question, whose rites are being debated, was loved by people in both factions. |
| 2 | **(E)** A Voiding scar garden — a contemplative space maintained by the Pilgrimage where the deliberate scars of enhancement removal are documented in illuminated records. Each entry describes who was Voided, why, what they felt, and what their life has been since. The records are honest — including people who found the Voiding difficult, who regretted it initially, who took years to feel what the doctrine says they should feel. The garden is beautiful and the honesty of the records is the best thing about it. |
| 3 | **(C)** Three young men from Ironblood families are harassing a pilgrim — not violently, but aggressively, with the specific contempt of people who have staked their identity on something the pilgrim's existence challenges. The pilgrim is older than all three of them, serene in the way that serene people can be genuinely infuriating, and is not backing down or escalating. If the players intervene, the young men redirect. If the players watch, they see the pilgrim slowly, non-confrontationally, utterly dismantle every argument the young men have. It takes a while. |
| 4 | **(S)** Brother Aul (or a senior Pilgrimage figure if Aul is unavailable as an NPC) walking the road alone, visiting a family who sent a member to the Pilgrimage and has not heard from them in six months. The family is angry. Brother Aul is not defensive. He tells them, honestly, what he knows and what he doesn't, and the conversation goes the way honest conversations about hard things go: badly, then less badly, then somewhere true. |
| 5 | **(E)** A resonance-dormant zone — a district of the Veilward Reach that was the site of a Pilgrimage mass Voiding ceremony three years ago. The ceremony was large enough that the collective absence of resonance left a detectable trace on the local environment: the area measures measurably quieter on enhancement readings. Enhancement-sensitive characters describe it as the specific silence of a room after a very loud sound has stopped. |
| 6 | **(S)** A Sutensai family whose younger generation has entirely joined the Pilgrimage. The parents are Sutensai-marked, serving the temple. Their three children are Voided, serving the pilgrimage. They eat dinner together every week. No one talks about the obvious theological rift. The dinner conversation is a masterclass in love operating under impossible strain. |
| 7 | **(C)** A small group of Ironblood mercenaries hired by a minor Reach lord to disrupt the Pilgrimage's approach to a convergence site — not to cause mass harm, but to delay, to send a message. They are professionals who are privately uncomfortable with the assignment. Combat is likely, but so is the possibility that the mercenaries can be convinced their employer's interest in this particular action is not worth the specific grief it will cause to specific people. |
| 8 | **(E)** A medical crisis in a Pilgrimage camp — enhancement rejection in a pilgrim who underwent the Voiding rite six months ago and whose body is in the delayed-rejection phase. The Pilgrimage's healers are skilled with Voiding aftercare but this case is complicated. A character with medical knowledge can help. The patient — a young woman, mid-twenties — is more concerned about whether her failure to progress past the rejection means she is somehow wrong for the path than about the physical pain. |
| 9 | **(S)** A Veilward Reach official — city administrator, Ironblood, loyal to neither Korrath nor Vaen and deeply invested in the Reach's independence — trying to manage the fact that the Pilgrimage passing through has economic benefits (the trade, the travelers' needs) and political complications (the Pilgrimage has a gravity that is reshaping local politics around it whether they want it to or not). She needs someone to help her think through options she has not yet seen. |
| 10 | **(E)** A thin place — one of the Between-adjacent spaces where the resonance boundary is permeable. In the Veilward Reach, these are older and calmer than elsewhere; this Reach has the oldest Sutensai infrastructure for managing them. The thin place is maintained as a sanctuary, a space for specific mourning rites. Standing in it for any length of time produces a faint awareness of the other side — not frightening, not comforting, something present and attentive and vast. Enhancement readings in the thin place show the backward-timestamp anomaly at low but measurable levels. |

### THE IRON TEETH REACH
*The most industrially developed Reach not under Korrath's control. Mines, foundries, enhancement-material production. Also the most economically stratified — the Resonant and Craftbound castes here are extremely distinct, and the Craftbound labor force is well-organized in ways that occasionally approach rebellion. Functional, productive, deeply unequal.*

|-----|-----------|
| 1 | **(C)** A labor action at a resonant-metal foundry — Craftbound workers have stopped production over an enhancement-safety complaint. The foundry's management (Resonant caste) is preparing to use security forces to break the action. The workers are right about the safety issue. The management is right that their supply contract penalties are severe enough that delay is genuinely threatening to the Reach's economic stability. Both things are true simultaneously. |
| 2 | **(S)** A master enhancement artisan in their workshop, doing finishing work on a commissioned piece of armor that is extraordinary — years of craft visible in every joint. The artisan is old enough to have known this commission would be the last major work of their life and is working with the specific attention of someone who knows this. | 3 | **(E)** A collapsed mineshaft — recent, within the week. Seventeen Craftbound miners are confirmed dead. The cause is a resonance instability event: the mine was hitting a resonant-metal vein that turned out to be atypically active. Standard mining protocol was followed. The instability was not predictable by current methods. The families of the dead are at the mine entrance. The Reach lord's representative is at the mine entrance. ### THE ASH RIVER REACH
*The largest and least governed of the Reaches. The primary corridor for refugees and migrant workers. Ashwalker caste populations are the highest here, and the local governance structure is genuinely patchwork — a dozen minor warlords with shifting alliances, none powerful enough to dominate but none willing to subordinate themselves to another. Everything is negotiated. Nothing holds indefinitely.*

| d10 | Encounter |
|-----|-----------|
| 1 | **(S)** A refugee column — three hundred people moving from a destroyed village toward wherever they can reach. The column has a rough internal structure: families near the center, mobile adults at the edges. Someone in the column has a medical crisis — enhancement rejection, accelerating, the kind that requires surgical intervention. The column has no surgeon. They have been walking toward the nearest city for four days. They will not reach it in time. |
| 2 | **(E)** An abandoned waystation that has been repurposed by Ashwalker families as a semi-permanent settlement. Not legal. Functioning. A community of approximately sixty people who have been here for two years, have built water capture and food production infrastructure, and have cultivated relationships with the minor local warlord sufficient to maintain de facto tolerance. They are one bad negotiation cycle from eviction. They know this. They are not letting it stop them from building anyway. |
| 3 | **(C)** An ambush by desperate people — not organized bandits, a family of four: two adults, two adolescents. Their enhancements are salvage-grade and failing. They are targeting travelers for enhancement materials. The violence they offer is frightened, not professional. The eldest adult is clearly in pain from advanced rejection and is operating on borrowed time. They have not hurt anyone yet. They will if they must. |
| 4 | **(S)** A traveling Sutensai priest — not a senior figure, a mid-level acolyte assigned to circuit ministry through the Ash River Reach's scattered communities. They have been doing this work for eight years. They have seen more of Tesshari's actual texture than almost any other religious figure and it has given them a theology that is substantially more complicated than what they were taught. They will share it with someone who seems genuinely interested, and it is strange and honest and will leave the players with something to think about. |
| 5 | **(E)** A resonance-scar site — a location where salvage-enhancement surgery was performed in the field, years ago, with insufficient tools. The resonance trace of the improvised bonding process is still detectable, years later, as a patchwork of incompatible frequencies in the earth. Plants do not grow well here. Animals avoid it. A character who meditates in the resonance-scar feels the fractured frequencies as a specific emotional register: the feeling of something not quite fitting, of a join that was made in necessity and has never fully integrated. |
| 6 | **(S)** Two minor warlords in public negotiation over the right to tax a specific road junction. The negotiation is conducted with formal courtesy and underlying complete distrust. Both warlords have brought their enhancement-advisors — people who can read each other's enhancements for signs of deception (a contested but practiced technique in the Reach). The negotiation is being observed by a dozen interested parties, including two Craftbound merchants who have been routing caravans to avoid both warlords' tax zones and are watching to calculate whether that will remain viable. |
| 7 | **(C)** An enhancement-stripping gang — not Korrath's Unmaking, but private criminals who strip people of their enhancements to resell them. The enhancements they are currently moving came from three murders committed in the last week. The gang is not large — five people — but they have a specific enhancement that gives them a significant combat advantage: a resonance-jamming device, jury-rigged, that suppresses others' enhancements in a thirty-foot radius. The device only works for approximately four minutes before overheating. |
| 8 | **(E)** A map that shouldn't exist — carried by an old Ashwalker who is traveling alone and who, if the players talk to him, will show them a map of the Ash River Reach that is approximately eighty years old and depicts three settlements that no longer exist and two road routes that were not officially constructed until thirty years after the map's date. Either the map is forged, the settlements were destroyed and forgotten, or someone mapped this territory very thoroughly before the current governance structure existed. The old man received the map from his father and his father received it from his. He has never been able to explain the discrepancy and does not want to. |
| 9 | **(S)** A caste mobility story — an Ashwalker woman who has accumulated enough resources to purchase formal Craftbound documentation. She is, legally, Craftbound now. She is in the process of acquiring her first proper installation enhancement — not salvage — for the first time in her life. She is at an enhancement workshop, and she is terrified and trying not to show it, and the artisan doing the installation is treating her with professional respect that is doing nothing to calm her fear, which is not about the surgery. |
| 10 | **(C/E)** A roadside shrine to a Prior Ascended that has been vandalized — the symbol of the Fourth Ascended smashed, recently. Local Sutensai are treating it as a crisis. Local Ashwalkers, several of whom saw who did it, are maintaining various degrees of not-having-seen-anything. The vandal is a teenager whose mother died in the Ash River Reach seven months ago and whose Echomind, per the family's account of her passing, departed wrong — lateral, toward north. The teenager has been at the shrine every day for a week. This was the first day they broke something. |

### THE SUTENSAI CAPITAL REACH
*Called the Bright Reach by its own citizens and "the Temple City" by everyone else. The administrative and spiritual center of the Iron Sutra. The Sutensai hierarchy is headquartered here. More Sutensai-marked caste than anywhere else. More secret-keeping than anywhere else. The architecture is designed to impress belief, and it works, which is either the best or worst thing about it.*

| d10 | Encounter |
|-----|-----------|
| 1 | **(S)** A public Sutensai ceremony — the daily midday resonance rite, performed at the central temple with the full hierarchy in attendance. The ceremony is genuinely beautiful: the coordinated resonance of hundreds of Sutensai-marked enhancements produces a visible harmonic in the light. The players can see, if they look during the ceremony, the senior hierarchy's body language. The Veil Masters at the back of the platform are not performing the ceremony. They are watching the ceremony with the expression of people who know something the ceremony doesn't account for. |
| 2 | **(E)** The Sutensai archive's public reading room — researchers of all castes are permitted here by appointment. In the public reading room, a character with relevant knowledge can identify, within an hour, the specific gaps where suppressed documents were removed: the finding aids reference materials that are no longer present, the cataloging notation marks removals with a specific symbol, and the pattern of removals across three hundred years of history follows a coherent logic — everything that points too clearly at the digestive truth of the Afterlife. The gaps are a map. |
| 3 | **(C)** An assassination attempt on a senior Sutensai official — not political, something more specific. The attacker is a Voided person, unarmed, who broke into the official's residence and made it as far as the official's study before being detected. The attacker is trying to deliver a document, not to harm the official. The security force responding did not know this and is treating it as an attempt on a life. The official, once the situation is de-escalated, will read the document. Their response will tell the players something important about this official. |
| 4 | **(S)** A young Sutensai initiate in their first year of training — not yet marked, still in the preliminary period of resonance attunement. They are struggling with the specific challenge of the early training: learning to feel the Echomind's resonance clearly enough to extend awareness to the boundary of the Between. They can feel it. What they feel frightens them and the training says this is normal and they are not sure the training is right. They will talk to an outside person about this because the training says not to voice doubts to fellow initiates. |
| 5 | **(E)** The Grand Sutensai's public garden — a maintained resonance-landscape that serves as both a meditative space and a demonstration of advanced resonance cultivation. The plants in the garden have been selectively enhanced over generations to produce harmonics when they grow — a garden that sings at specific frequencies in a specific sequence at specific times of day. A character who knows what frequencies correspond to what can identify, by the garden's current sequence, that the Grand Sutensai is broadcasting something through the garden. The something: a request, aimed at the Between boundary, asking for a response from the Prior Ascended. Unanswered. The sequence repeats. Unanswered again. |
| 6 | **(C)** A theological dispute that has turned physical — two Sutensai factions, one arguing for maintaining the suppression of evidence about the Afterlife, one arguing for a "managed disclosure" approach, have been conducting a political warfare campaign for months that has escalated into something more direct. The senior hierarchy does not know it has happened yet. |
| 7 | **(S)** A Resonant-caste family visiting the Great Temple for a private mourning rite — their patriarch died eight months ago and the Echomind passage was irregular by the officiating Sutensai's record. The family has been refused access to the record three times. Today they have brought a cousin who is Sutensai-marked and can make demands within the hierarchy that the family cannot. Recently — within the last month — the vent's output has changed. Not diminished: changed frequency. The new frequency is one the Sutensai have never cataloged. | 10 | **(C/S)** A public resonance-reading gone wrong — a ceremony designed to demonstrate the Sutensai hierarchy's ongoing contact with the Prior Ascended, conducted before a large crowd of citizens. Midway through the ceremony, the resonance reading produces something unexpected: instead of the standard message format, the channel produces the tone — the Convergence frequency, at full volume, for approximately nine seconds. ### THE GRAY WATER REACH
*A coastal Reach, trade-dependent, nominally neutral in the factional wars because its harbor is too economically important to all sides for anyone to fully occupy it. The most internationally-facing Reach — there are trade partners beyond Tesshari's borders, and the Gray Water receives their ships and their information. The only place in Tesshari where someone might have heard of the entity in the Between from a non-Iron Sutra tradition.*

| d10 | Encounter |
|-----|-----------|
| 1 | **(S)** A foreign merchant ship in harbor — crew from a nation beyond Tesshari's borders, where resonant metal is rare and enhancement is minimal. The crew finds Tesshari enhancement culture alternately fascinating and disturbing. One of them — a navigator, quietly perceptive — asks the players questions about the Iron Sutra's afterlife doctrine that are structurally identical to the questions a philosopher would ask if they suspected the doctrine was concealing something. She has heard things. Other doctrines. Other accounts of what the Between contains. She is not going to say more unless the players give her reason to trust. |
| 2 | **(E)** A Gray Water harbor district building whose basement, accessible through a rusted door that has not been opened in approximately sixty years, contains a resonance research lab that is not on any official record.

# 1. The Dormitory at Severed Six
*Shell Nest | Standard difficulty | Levels 1–3*

## Overview
A mid-rise residential block in the Ironhold's outer labor district, condemned after forty percent of its residents entered Shell-state simultaneously during a Wire Craft signal surge three years ago. The Ironhold quarantined it, posted two guards, and forgot about it. The guards entered after a week to check for valuables. They did not come back out as themselves.

## Why It's Dangerous Now
The Shells inside have developed a crude herd behavior — not coordination, but resonance. They cluster toward warmth and signal output. A party entering with active enhancements will be heard from six floors away.

## Layout (6 Rooms)

### Room 1: The Lobby
Turnstiles still click when pushed. Shell-state maintenance workers pace their old routes behind the front desk, repetitive and wrong. Two Shells, passive until a combatant's active signal enhancement exceeds Signal 3 or someone speaks above a whisper.

### Room 2: The Mailroom
Floor-to-ceiling cubbies, most still holding undelivered messages from three years ago. One Shell is trapped behind a collapsed shelving unit, reaching through the gap — immobile but screaming in the flat, tuneless way Shells do when stimulated. Touching or reading the mail triggers it; the sound draws three Shells from Room 3.

### Room 3: The Stairwell
The elevators don't work. Six flights of stairs, each landing occupied by a Shell sitting against the wall with its head down. They react to movement — not sight, but the vibration of footsteps. A skilled climber (EDGE check, target 3) can ascend a flight without triggering the Shell on that landing.

### Room 4: Floor Four — The Nursery Unit
A family unit. Two adult Shells, one child Shell. The child Shell does not attack. It follows. It makes a sound like it is about to speak and then doesn't. Killing it is a moral choice the dungeon doesn't adjudicate.

### Room 5: The Roof Access Landing
A Wire Craft junction box on the landing controls the building's internal signal frequency — the same surge that created the Shells is still running on a loop from this box. Interacting with it (SIGNAL check, target 4) reveals what the surge was: not an accident. The signal was pushed from outside. Someone did this deliberately.

### Final Room: The Supervisor — Shell-State Foreman Rek-Osa
Rek-Osa ran the building's maintenance shift. His enhancements were high-grade for a laborer — subdermal plating, enhanced musculature — and they kept running when his Echomind went dark. He has been walking the sixth floor for three years. His routes have worn grooves in the tile. He is stronger than a standard Shell and faster, and he remembers, on some level below thought, that he was in charge here. When threatened, he attempts to herd other Shells toward intruders rather than engaging directly.

**Boss Stats:**
- Tier: Boss
- HP: 85
- AP Max: 4
- IRON 6 | EDGE 2 | FRAME 5 | SIGNAL 1 | RESONANCE 0 | VEIL 1
- Signature Card: **Foreman's Rounds** — *3 AP | Boss* — Rek-Osa moves through every occupied zone in sequence, triggering every dormant Shell in the building. All Shells in the dungeon activate and converge on the party's last known location. Until the end of the next round, Rek-Osa cannot be targeted (he is moving). On his next turn, he arrives at the party's position with Guard 4.

## Unique Reward

### Shell-Seal Brace
*Unique Weapon | Wrist-mounted tool/weapon | Found in: The Dormitory at Severed Six*

> "Rek-Osa wore this to track his workers' enhancement health. It knows what running without a soul looks like."

Recovered from Rek-Osa's left forearm — a maintenance supervisor's enhancement-monitoring brace that has spent three years reading Shell-state biorhythms. It no longer monitors. It predicts.

**Card Modification:** All Attack cards deal +3 damage against Shell-state targets. Once per combat, before initiative is rolled, the brace identifies which enemies in the encounter have degraded Echomind signatures — the GM must truthfully disclose which enemies are Shell-state, near-Shell, or carrying suppressed Echominds.
**Special:** The brace vibrates faintly in the presence of any being within 60 feet whose Echomind is suppressed, absent, or falsified. This includes disguised puppets, Signal-masked individuals, and the recently dead who have not yet passed into the Iron Afterlife. It does not identify them — only signals that something is wrong.
**Value:** Cannot be purchased. Found only here.

## Hook
A Wire Market courier was delivering a package to Severed Six when the quarantine went up — she never retrieved it, and now three years later she's found paperwork suggesting the package contained proof of who triggered the surge. She'll pay 80 RM for whatever's in that mailroom and triple that if the players bring her the Wire Craft junction box's transmission logs.

# 2. The Resting Grounds of Ash-Benet
*Shell Nest | Standard difficulty | Levels 2–4*

## Overview
Ash-Benet was a small Iron Sutra waystation on the Eastern pilgrim road — a place for travelers to rest, receive a Sutensai blessing, and continue toward the Sutra Sanctum. The priest who ran it entered Shell-state six months ago after an unreported incident involving a pilgrim with unusual resonant properties. The waystation is still listed as active on Sutensai maps. Pilgrims still stop.

## Why It's Dangerous Now
The priest, now Shell-state, has been conducting rituals. Not coherently — fragments of ceremony, half-remembered prayers, the physical motions of blessing without the Echomind behind them. But enough pilgrims have stopped and enough have entered Shell-state from proximity to the corrupted resonant altar that the waystation now has nine Shells performing broken liturgy in an endless loop.

## Layout (7 Rooms)

### Room 1: The Road Approach
Three pilgrim Shells kneel on the road outside, heads bowed in a posture of prayer. They are facing slightly the wrong direction — not toward the waystation, but toward something in the middle distance that isn't there. They react to anyone who touches them or speaks a Sutra prayer near them; the prayer triggers a response, but the response is wrong.

### Room 2: The Blessing Hall
High ceilings, resonant-metal prayer fixtures that were beautiful before they started dimly pulsing at irregular intervals. Four Shells are arranged in congregation posture. The pulsing light makes it difficult to read the room — EDGE check (target 3) to move through without disturbing them.

### Room 3: The Offering Chamber
An intact offering bowl full of resonant marks and small personal items — the accumulated offerings of pilgrims over decades. Two Shells are rifling through it, not taking anything, just moving objects with methodical repetition. If the party takes anything from the bowl, all Shells in the waystation activate simultaneously.

### Room 4: The Priest's Quarters
The priest's journals. Readable. The final entries describe the pilgrim — no name, only that they had no enhancements at all (Tethered), that their resonance was unlike anything the priest had encountered, and that after they left, the priest could hear the altar differently. The last entry is a single incomplete sentence in a handwriting that isn't the priest's.

### Room 5: The Cellar (Supply Storage)
Preserved pilgrim supplies, undisturbed. Also: two newly formed Shells — pilgrims who stopped in the last few weeks and didn't leave. They are fresh enough that their facial expressions haven't fully smoothed out yet. One appears to be mid-laugh. RESONANCE check (target 4) to interact with this room without developing a Bleed 1 status from the spiritual weight of it.

### Room 6: The Garden
The waystation's small resonant-metal garden, where pilgrims traditionally planted a shard of personal metal before continuing their journey. The garden is overgrown with a thousand small metal shards catching light. Three Shells tend it obsessively, pulling non-existent weeds. The garden itself is a RESONANCE hazard — anyone spending more than 2 rounds in it without a RESONANCE check (target 3) gains Silence for 1 round as the accumulated Echomind residue interferes with their own.

### Final Room: Priest-Shell Henra-Vo
Henra-Vo sits behind the altar performing a blessing with precise, beautiful hand motions on no one. The altar resonates at a frequency that makes the room feel like being watched. Henra-Vo's Shell-state is advanced — the Echomind is almost entirely gone — but the altar is providing power to his enhanced body from its own resonant charge, effectively making him a sustained threat. Destroying the altar first (it has 30 HP, no resistance) cuts Henra-Vo's HP regeneration and removes his resistance to Resonance attacks.

**Boss Stats:**
- Tier: Boss
- HP: 110 (Regen 4 per turn while altar intact; 0 Regen after altar is destroyed)
- AP Max: 4
- IRON 3 | EDGE 2 | FRAME 4 | SIGNAL 2 | RESONANCE 6 | VEIL 3
- Resistances: Resonance attacks (while altar intact)
- Signature Card: **Broken Benediction** — *4 AP | Boss* — Henra-Vo raises both hands over the party and speaks an Iron Sutra blessing in reverse. All enemies (the party) gain Vulnerable 2 and lose the benefit of any active Guard or Shield. The altar pulses; Henra-Vo gains Regen 6 this turn regardless of altar status. Duration: until end of Henra-Vo's next turn.

## Unique Reward

### The Priest's Unfinished Sutra
*Dungeon Card | Tier 2 (2 AP) | Resonance Attack | Found in: The Resting Grounds of Ash-Benet*

> "He never completed the prayer. You finish it for him."

Only someone who witnessed the broken liturgy of Ash-Benet and carried it through to the other side understands what a ceremony looks like when its meaning has been stripped away — and how to put meaning back into it. This card represents that knowledge: a corrupted Sutra rite reclaimed.

**Effect:** Deal 10 + RESONANCE damage to one target. If the target is Shell-state, near-Shell, or carrying a suppressed Echomind, they instead take 18 + RESONANCE damage and gain Stagger 2 as the completed prayer finds what remains of them.
**Keywords:** Stagger 2 (conditional), Resonant
**Limit:** One per party. Cannot be copied or reproduced.

## Hook
A Sutensai administrative clerk has noticed that no status reports have come from Ash-Benet waystation in six months, which is unusual but not unprecedented. She's sending someone to check — preferably someone not a pilgrim, who won't be added to the problem.

# 3. The Hollow Market of Skin Street
*Shell Nest | Standard difficulty | Levels 3–5*

## Overview
Skin Street is the colloquial name for a black-market enhancement district in the Wire Market's outer ring — unlicensed Forge Tenders, discount cybernetics of uncertain origin, black-market mod work done in rooms behind curtains. The modification failure rate in these districts is high. Six weeks ago, a batch of substandard Echomind-integration implants from a single supplier caused cascading Shell-state failures across forty-two clients who had purchased from the same stall.

## Why It's Dangerous Now
The stall owner fled. The forty-two Shells remain, still wandering the market district, still wearing their fresh modifications. Several of them have gone into each other's shops, picked up tools, and begun — not working, exactly, but miming work. The market has a new staff. They are not employed voluntarily.

## Layout (6 Rooms)

### Room 1: The Street Entrance
Open-air. Three Shell-state street vendors behind their stalls, displaying goods they cannot sell to customers they cannot see. The goods are real — salvage, tools, food — and undisturbed. Taking items from an unattended stall triggers the vendors. Leaving payment does not.

### Room 2: The Modification Parlor
Someone's living room converted into a surgery. Three Shells occupy it: one on the operating table, one holding instruments, one observing. The instruments are still sterile. The Shell on the table has a half-installed enhancement still protruding from its forearm — SIGNAL check (target 4) to complete or safely remove the installation and recover a functioning Uncommon cybernetic enhancement worth 200 RM.

### Room 3: The Collapsed Stall — Ground Zero
The original stall where the bad implants were sold. Shelves still hold unsold inventory — sealed boxes of the same batch that caused the crisis. Eight boxes remain. A SIGNAL check (target 3) identifies them as dangerous. They are worth 60 RM each to the right black-market buyer, 200 RM each to a Sutensai investigator, and potentially more to whoever wants the evidence destroyed.

### Room 4: The Restaurant
A noodle stall now occupied by six Shells. The cook Shell is still cooking — the burner is on, the pot is boiling dry, and the smoke is beginning to accumulate. Navigating around the Shells (EDGE check, target 3) takes 2 rounds; combat is an option but the noise draws the street Shells.

### Room 5: The Locked Back Room
The original supplier's private workspace. Locked (SIGNAL check, target 5 or appropriate tool). Inside: modification journals that trace the bad batch back to a Wire Market mid-tier supplier. Also inside: three Shells who were the supplier's private clients — the people he tested his worst work on before selling it publicly.

### Final Room: Shell-Forge Maris-Ket
The Forge Tender who ran the most reputable unlicensed parlor on Skin Street — not the one responsible for the crisis, but caught in it. Her Shell-state is incomplete: her Echomind flickered out and back twice before going dark entirely, and the resulting instability has left her modification network running hot and dangerous. She sparks. Her touch delivers Overheat 2. She still knows how to use her surgical tools.

**Boss Stats:**
- Tier: Boss
- HP: 95
- AP Max: 4
- IRON 3 | EDGE 5 | FRAME 3 | SIGNAL 6 | RESONANCE 1 | VEIL 2
- Status Immunities: Silence, Overheat
- Signature Card: **Unstable Modification** — *3 AP | Boss* — Maris-Ket drives a tool into a target's existing cybernetic enhancement. Deal 12 + SIGNAL damage. Target's highest-cost active enhancement misfires: the target gains Overheat 3 and their most recently played card's effect is reversed (defensive cards become offensive against the target; offensive cards become defensive for Maris-Ket). If the target has no cybernetic enhancements, deal 8 damage instead with no secondary effect.

## Unique Reward

### Maris-Ket's Calibration Tools
*Unique Weapon | Tool set / melee instrument | Found in: The Hollow Market of Skin Street*

> "She could build anything. She knew exactly where to put the blade because she knew exactly where the wire ran."

A full set of precision modification instruments that Maris-Ket carried on her person — scalpels, micro-drivers, wire-threaders, all in resonant steel. In trained hands, they are weapons. In very trained hands, they are weapons that know exactly where a body's cybernetic vulnerabilities are.

**Card Modification:** All Melee Attack cards deal +2 damage. All Melee Attack cards against targets with cybernetic enhancements add the secondary effect: target gains Overheat 1. Once per combat, one Melee Attack card may instead target a specific enhancement — if it deals 15 or more total damage, that enhancement is disabled for the remainder of the combat (not permanently).
**Special:** Outside combat, the tools function as a full Forge Tender toolkit for enhancement installation, repair, and removal. Any character using these tools for modification work makes SIGNAL-based modification checks at +2.
**Value:** Cannot be purchased. Found only here.

## Hook
The Wire Market's Modification Quality Bureau has opened a quiet investigation into the Skin Street incident — they want the supply chain traced before it becomes a public scandal. They're offering 400 RM for the supplier's identity and the modification journals, and they are not asking about anything else that players bring back with them.

# 4. The Sunken Relay — Station Vo-Ket Nine
*Shell Nest | Challenging difficulty | Levels 7–9*

## Overview
A decommissioned Korrath communications relay station built into the bedrock beneath the Central Holds' eastern edge, sealed fifteen years ago when the relay network was upgraded. The seal held until an Ashlands resonance surge eighteen months ago cracked the lower chamber and let something in — not a creature, but a frequency. The frequency affected the skeleton crew of maintenance Shells left to run the automated systems, and from there it spread upward.

## Why It's Dangerous Now
The frequency from the Ashlands surge rewrote the Shells' base resonant patterns. They are no longer Shell-state as it is normally understood — they are Shell-state with purpose, coordinating around the relay tower's central broadcast antenna, which they have been modifying for eighteen months. The antenna is now broadcasting something. The party needs to find out what, and stop it.

## Layout (8 Rooms)

### Room 1: The Entry Shaft
A sixty-foot vertical access shaft, ladder intact. The shaft walls hum at the Ashlands frequency — RESONANCE check (target 4) to descend without gaining Silence 1 turn from the interference. Three Shells at the bottom, arranged in a triangle around the shaft's base, facing inward. They are not watching the shaft. They are listening to each other.

### Room 2: The Command Deck
Korrath military communications equipment, fifteen years obsolete but functional — these Shells have kept it running. Maps of the Central Holds still pinned to walls, with new markings added in a script that is not language but is clearly structured. SIGNAL check (target 5) to identify the markings as resonant-frequency notation; what they describe is a broadcast schedule with the current date circled.

### Room 3: The Barracks
Eight Shells in dormant configuration, flat on their bunks. Unlike typical dormant Shells, these are clearly in a power-conservation mode — their enhancement systems are cycling down to minimum to conserve for something. They will not activate unless someone touches them or makes noise above a whisper. The barracks also contains Korrath military-grade rations (edible, worth 120 RM to a collector of military memorabilia), two functional pulse arms (Circuit Bolt Crossbow stats), and a journal from the last living crew member that ends mid-sentence.

### Room 4: The Generator Room
Three massive resonant power cells feeding the relay. The Shells have added a fourth cell — salvaged from somewhere, of much newer manufacture than the station itself. The fourth cell has a Wire Market serial number. SIGNAL check (target 5) to run the serial: it was reported stolen fourteen months ago from a Wire Market distribution hub four hundred miles north. The Shells were supplied. Someone external is running this.

### Room 5: The Isolation Chamber
A lead-lined room meant for sensitive communications. Inside: one Shell in lotus position, enhancements cycling at full power, broadcasting a sustained tone that the room's insulation contains. This Shell is the antenna's human component — a living broadcast node. Killing it stops the broadcast immediately but releases a resonant pulse (10 resonance damage to all in the room, RESONANCE check target 4 to halve). Extracting it from its state without killing requires SIGNAL 6+ and three consecutive rounds of uninterrupted work.

### Room 6: The Deep Archive
Old Korrath intelligence files, sealed in resonant canisters. Most are fifteen years old and strategically irrelevant. Two are not: one details a classified project called OPERATION STILL FRAME, described only as "consciousness preservation under non-standard parameters," and one is a personnel file with a name that matches one of the Three Claimants' known aliases in their earlier career. The DM determines which Claimant.

### Room 7: The Maintenance Tunnels
A maze of conduit and crawlspaces behind the main chambers. Navigated with EDGE checks (target 4). Three Shells maintain the conduit with tools, and the cramped space makes combat enormously difficult — all attacks in the tunnels have their damage reduced by 4 from the lack of space. However, reaching the antenna controls from the tunnels bypasses the boss encounter.

### Final Room: Shell-Commander Vasset-Rek
Vasset-Rek was the relay station's commanding officer when it was sealed — he was supposed to be evacuated but the paperwork was filed incorrectly and the door sealed with him inside. He entered Shell-state from isolation within a year. The Ashlands frequency found a military-grade consciousness structure and made it into something worse: a Shell with tactical processing still running on instinct, directing the others with hand signals he does not know he is making. He has Korrath military combat enhancements and fifteen years of practice fighting in enclosed spaces.

**Boss Stats:**
- Tier: Boss
- HP: 155
- AP Max: 4
- IRON 6 | EDGE 5 | FRAME 6 | SIGNAL 4 | RESONANCE 2 | VEIL 1
- Status Immunities: Stagger, Root
- Signature Card: **Fire Discipline** — *4 AP | Boss* — Vasset-Rek issues a silent tactical signal. All active Shells in the station simultaneously converge on one party member's position. That member is targeted by every Shell in the dungeon (DM's discretion on number) for one coordinated attack that functions as a single 20 + IRON damage hit with Expose 2. Vasset-Rek then gains Guard 6 and moves to the most defensible position in the room.

## Unique Reward

### Vasset-Rek's Command Brace
*Unique Weapon | Wrist-mounted command interface | Found in: The Sunken Relay — Station Vo-Ket Nine*

> "He commanded forty people. He never stopped. The brace kept the orders; it just ran out of living recipients."

A Korrath military command interface that synchronized a unit commander's tactical signals with their soldiers' enhancement networks. Vasset-Rek wore it for fifteen years of Shell-state command. It has learned to direct things that no longer have independent will.

**Card Modification:** All Control cards deal +3 damage or increase status stacks by 1. Once per combat, one Control card may also target all Shell-state or near-Shell entities in the encounter simultaneously at no additional AP cost — they cannot resist it.
**Special:** The brace can issue silent tactical signals readable by anyone with an active Echomind within 60 feet. This functions as a non-verbal communication system in stealth situations. Enemies with degraded or absent Echominds (Shells, puppets, the heavily suppressed) respond to the brace's signals as though to a command, once per combat — they will pause for one round if the wearer issues a clear stop signal. This does not work on boss-tier enemies.
**Value:** Cannot be purchased. Found only here.

## Hook
A Korrath intelligence lieutenant has picked up the relay's anomalous broadcast on a routine scan — she cannot decrypt it but she can tell it is military encryption that hasn't been used in fifteen years. She needs a team that can enter the station without official Korrath authorization and without leaving a record that Korrath sent them.

# 5. The Congregation of the Unkept
*Shell Nest | Elite difficulty | Levels 13–15*

## Overview
In the Northern Pale's most isolated region, a community of religious extremists called the Unkept voluntarily surrendered their cybernetic enhancements and severed their connections to the Iron Afterlife, seeking a purity of death they called "the True Silence." Three years ago, the entire community — four hundred and twelve people — entered Shell-state on the same night. The Iron Sutra has classified the site as a Level Four Resonant Catastrophe and ordered it sealed. It has not been sealed. It has been quietly watched.

## Why It's Dangerous Now
The Unkept community entered Shell-state as a collective. Without enhancements, their Echominds had no digital substrate to anchor to — they went dark together, all in the same moment. The resulting mass-simultaneous resonant discharge created something the Sutensai have no name for: a Shell-state hive, four hundred bodies moving with coordination no individual Shell should be capable of, held together by the resonant impression of a shared belief system that no longer has believers.

## Layout (8 Rooms)

### Room 1: The Sealed Gate
Iron Sutra sealing sigils on the gate — intact, but someone has cut a passage beside them through the wall. Fresh cuts. Someone has been coming and going. The passage is human-sized. It smells like ozone and old cloth.

### Room 2: The Outer Village
Forty Shells moving in synchronized loops through the village's outer ring. The synchronization is unsettling because it is not military — it is liturgical. They are performing morning prayers together, each body in precise relation to the others, no two ever colliding, moving in patterns that resolve, from above, into Sutra glyphs. RESONANCE check (target 6): the patterns are not random. They are spelling something, slowly, repeatedly, over hours. Anyone who succeeds sees two words: "NOT DONE."

### Room 3: The Forge (Decommissioned)
The Unkept removed their own enhancements here. The equipment is still present — the Forge Tender's station, the surgical implements, the installation chair with its restraints. Several Shells sit in the chairs, facing the equipment with expressions that cannot be read because they have no expressions. SIGNAL check (target 5) to recover a cache of high-quality surgical tools (worth 800 RM) without triggering the room's Shells.

### Room 4: The Library
Records of every Unkept community member — their names, their former castes, the enhancements they surrendered, the dates of their severance. The records are methodically organized. Someone has recently accessed them — pages are turned, certain names are underlined in fresh ink. VEIL check (target 5) to identify the handwriting as recent and deliberate, not part of the community's original record-keeping.

### Room 5: The Children's Hall
Forty child Shells, ages approximately six to fourteen, sitting in rows as if at lesson. A Shell adult stands at the front. It is pointing at a blank wall and speaking — not in language, but in a sustained resonant tone that varies in pitch. The children respond in kind. This is not combat; it is a scene. The players may engage or pass through. If they pass through, the children Shells watch them leave with their heads turning in unison to follow. They do not pursue.

### Room 6: The Sutensai Observer's Post
Hidden in the rafters of a storage building: a concealed observation platform with journals, surveillance equipment, and supply caches. The observer has been here for six months, logging the Shells' behavior. The journals are extraordinary — a Sutensai intelligence asset named Sei-Voran who has watched the community perform broken liturgy every day and has slowly become convinced that the Unkept did not fail. The final entry: "They are completing something. I don't know what. I don't know if stopping it is correct."

### Room 7: The Temple Approach
Two hundred Shells in formal prayer formation, creating a human corridor leading to the temple entrance. They are not moving. They are waiting. Walking the corridor is an act of nerve — VEIL check (target 5) or gain Stagger 1 from the psychological pressure of it. Attacking even one collapses the entire formation into combat simultaneously.

### Final Room: The Voice of the Congregation — The Unanimous Shell
What stands at the altar is not a single body. The Unanimous Shell is the name the Sutensai observer gave to the phenomenon: a Shell that moves between bodies, occupying one and then another in sequence, as if the hive-consciousness has a pilot that borrows vessels. The current occupied body glows faintly at the eyes and speaks in a multi-layered voice — the recorded resonance of four hundred Echominds speaking through one throat. It knows things. It will share them, once, if asked, before it attacks.

**Boss Stats:**
- Tier: Boss
- HP: 280 (split across current host — when current host body is reduced to 0, the presence transfers to the nearest Shell; transfer takes 1 round; during transfer the Unanimous Shell cannot act but cannot be targeted)
- AP Max: 4
- IRON 5 | EDGE 4 | FRAME 6 | SIGNAL 3 | RESONANCE 9 | VEIL 7
- Status Immunities: Silence, Stagger, Root, Expose
- Signature Card: **The Congregation Speaks** — *4 AP | Boss* — The Unanimous Shell opens its mouth and the resonant weight of four hundred voices strikes outward. All party members take 18 + RESONANCE damage and gain Vulnerable 3. Any party member whose RESONANCE is 5 or lower is also Rooted (the weight pins them). For 2 rounds, all party members' Resonance-based cards cost 1 additional AP to play (the noise fills the same frequency).

## Unique Reward

### The Unfinished Covenant
*Dungeon Card | Tier 3 (3 AP) | Resonance Attack | Found in: The Congregation of the Unkept*

> "They gave everything up voluntarily. What's left is not absence. It's intention without a body."

Only someone who has walked the congregation's corridor and heard four hundred voices speak through one throat carries the understanding this card requires: that a soul-pattern can persist even when every anchor for it is gone, and that this persistence can be weaponized.

**Effect:** Deal 20 + RESONANCE damage to one target. Apply Vulnerable 3 and Silence. If the target has any Shell-state allies within 30 feet, those allies immediately turn on the target for one round — the Unfinished Covenant redirects hive-resonance toward the designated body. If used against the Unanimous Shell specifically, this card deals maximum damage automatically.
**Keywords:** Vulnerable 3, Silence, Resonant
**Limit:** One per party. Cannot be copied or reproduced.

## Hook
Sei-Voran, the Sutensai observer, has gone missing — her superiors know she was at the site and her last report said she was "reconsidering the objective." Someone needs to go in, find out what happened to her, and determine whether the site needs to be destroyed or — more troublingly — left alone.

# 6. Signal Trap House — Blackout Node 7
*Wire Craft Corruption | Standard difficulty | Levels 2–4*

## Overview
A Wire Market signal relay node disguised as a residential property in the Echo Coast's densest urban district. Node 7 was operational for twelve years, quietly routing Wire Craft communications for the Market's intelligence division. Four months ago, a corrupted transmission from an unknown source wrote itself into the node's core architecture and began rewriting outgoing signals. Every communication that passed through Node 7 since the corruption arrived has been subtly altered.

## Why It's Dangerous Now
The node is now a trap: it actively lures Wire Craft users by broadcasting a false safe-signal that enhanced individuals feel as a pull toward the building. Those who enter find the node's automated defense systems running under the corrupted signal's logic — not protecting the node, but feeding it. The node feeds on active Echomind contact.

## Layout (6 Rooms)

### Room 1: The Street-Level Facade
A residential front door, convincingly ordinary. Except: a SIGNAL check (target 3) detects the pull — a false safe-signal broadcasting from the building's upper floors, designed to feel like a Wire Market authorized access invitation. Anyone with SIGNAL 4+ feels it without a check. Entering without a plan for the pull means all SIGNAL-based cards cost 1 additional AP for the first 2 rounds as the party's enhancement networks negotiate with the false signal.

### Room 2: The Communication Intercept Room
Racks of recording equipment, all running. The corrupted signal has been logging every communication it intercepted and altered. A SIGNAL check (target 4) recovers one specific communication the DM selects — something plot-relevant the players didn't know was being monitored. The logs are also guarded by two Wire Market automated defense constructs that activate when the recording equipment is accessed.

### Room 3: The Living Quarters (Cover Identity)
Whoever ran Node 7 used this room as a plausible cover — personal effects, food, clothing. All fake. A VEIL check (target 4) identifies the fakeness immediately; the uncanny wrongness of a lived space with no human trace. Behind a false wall: the node operator's actual workspace, with a journal that describes the corruption arriving and the operator's three weeks of increasingly desperate attempts to contain it before they fled.

### Room 4: The Server Architecture
Wire-filled walls, floor to ceiling, every available surface covered in resonant conduit and signal processing hardware. The corruption is visible here as a physical discoloration — the conduit that carries the corrupted signal has turned a deep amber, like resin, and pulses. SIGNAL check (target 5) to interact with the server: on a success, the player learns the corruption's origin point (a specific IP-equivalent address in the Wire Market's deep network) and can begin a counter-script (takes 3 rounds of uninterrupted work). Failure triggers an automated defense response.

### Room 5: The Antenna Room
The signal antenna itself — ceiling-mounted, large, and currently broadcasting the false safe-signal to a radius of approximately two city blocks. Destroying the antenna stops the pull immediately but causes a resonant burst (8 Signal damage to all in the room). # 25. The Unbroken Line — Korrath's Last Siege Position
*Ironhold Abandoned | Legendary difficulty | Levels 18–20*

## Overview
During the Fracture Wars' final year, Korrath held a siege line for fourteen months against a coalition of three Reach factions. The siege line was strategically irrelevant by month three — both sides knew it — but Korrath would not break it, because Korrath does not break lines, and the coalition would not withdraw, because withdrawing from a line you cannot break is political suicide. Three thousand people died at the Unbroken Line for a position that meant nothing. When the Fracture Wars ended by exhaustion, both sides withdrew simultaneously. No one won. No one counted the dead for months. When the count was finally conducted, it was approximately three thousand and forty-seven. The forty-seven died after the withdrawal, in the fortifications, of wounds no one came back to treat.

## Why It's Dangerous Now
The forty-seven are still there. Not Shell-state — something older. The Fracture Wars' closing year created conditions at the siege line that are now understood, with forty years of hindsight, as a significant Between-proximity event. The forty-seven who died after the withdrawal did not transit through the Between to the Iron Afterlife. They are caught at the siege line itself, in a state the Sutensai have no category for: present in both the physical location and the Between simultaneously, bound to the fortifications by the weight of the siege and their inability to understand that it is over.

## Layout (8 Rooms)

### Room 1: The Approach Road
The road to the siege line is physically intact but resonantly wrong — the Between is close here, and has been close for forty years. RESONANCE check (target 5) on approach: the character feels the siege. Not sees — feels. The cold, the length of it, the specific quality of fourteen months of waiting. Anyone who fails takes Vulnerable 1 for the rest of the dungeon (the resonance settles into them like weather).

### Room 2: The Outer Fortifications
The siege line's outer works: trenches, barriers, firing positions. Still maintained — not physically, but resonantly. The forty-seven maintain them in Between-form, repairing what does not need repairing, holding a line that has not been attacked in forty years. RESONANCE check (target 6) to make contact with one of the forty-seven: they do not know the siege is over. They do not know how long they have been here. They are holding the line because they were told to hold the line and no one told them to stop.

### Room 3: The Command Post
A fortified position that served as the siege line's command center. The commanding officer's materials are preserved: tactical maps still pinned, orders still written, the last dispatch from Korrath's central command dated to the final day of the Fracture Wars. The dispatch orders withdrawal. It arrived three days after the forty-seven died. It was never delivered to them. VEIL check (target 5) to understand the emotional weight of this — the party member who succeeds must make a choice: tell the forty-seven about the dispatch, or leave them in ignorance a little longer while the party investigates further.

### Room 4: The Field Hospital
Where the forty-seven died — a reinforced chamber that served as the siege line's medical station in its final weeks. The medical staff among the forty-seven still work here, treating wounds that are forty years healed, on patients who no longer have bodies, with instruments that are Between-resonant echoes of the physical tools left behind. The hospital scene is complete and continuous and deeply wrong, and moving through it without disturbing the forty-seven's vigil requires RESONANCE check (target 6) and VEIL check (target 5) simultaneously — one for the resonant navigation, one for the composure to move through this without reacting in ways that disrupt the scene.

### Room 5: The Underground Bunker
A deep fortification beneath the main siege line, used for command security during peak combat. Inside: the personal effects of every member of the forty-seven, left when they were moved to the field hospital. Among them: thirty years of letters from families who never received confirmation of death and never stopped writing. The letters arrived at the siege line for years after the war. Korrath's postal system routed them faithfully to an address that no longer had living recipients. The letters are in good condition. They should not be opened, but they can be. What they contain is forty years of lives lived in the absence of resolution.

### Room 6: The Wire Craft Communication Node
A Korrath military communications node, still active — the automated system kept transmitting status reports for years before the power finally shifted to maintenance mode. The logs show what the forty-seven experienced in their final three days: the quiet, the waiting for the withdrawal that didn't come for them, and — in the final log entry — a wire from the siege line's Wire Craft specialist, made after she understood she was dying: she left a message in the system, addressed to "whoever comes after." The DM delivers this message. It is not a tactical document.

### Room 7: The Surrender of the Line — Roleplay Chamber
This is the dungeon's moral center. The party can release the forty-seven from the siege line — but doing so requires telling them the truth: the war is over. Has been over for forty years. They held a line that stopped mattering before they died for it. The information must be delivered by the party, to forty-seven people simultaneously (the forty-seven gather when approached with the dispatch in hand), and the party must be present for the reaction. This is not a combat encounter. It is a reckoning. The DM plays the forty-seven's response: shock, grief, anger, eventually something approaching peace. The session should not rush this scene.

### Final Room: The Weight That Stays — What Remains After
When the forty-seven release and transit to the Iron Afterlife, the siege line's Between-proximity collapses — the anchor that held the space open for forty years dissolves. But something remains in the physical location: the concentrated resonant impression of fourteen months of siege and forty years of vigil, released in the moment of departure. It is not hostile. It is not an enemy. It is the siege itself, resonant and formless, and it must be navigated before the party can leave — not defeated, but accepted. RESONANCE checks (target 8) to walk through the released impression without being overwhelmed by the weight of what just happened. Characters who fail are Rooted for 1 round as the siege settles into them.

**No Boss.** This dungeon does not end in combat. It ends in consequence. Characters who complete it receive the reward and carry what the siege line gave them.

## Overview
The Hollow Author has no body. The Hollow Author has an operational center — a location that functions as their physical presence in the world, where the puppet network's coordination occurs, where the major operational decisions are implemented, where the Hollow Author's controlling consciousness actually lives in the Wire Craft infrastructure. It is not a secret location, in the sense that the Wire Market's intelligence division knows it exists. It is a secret location in the sense that knowing it exists and being able to reach it are entirely different things, and the three Wire Market operatives who located it have all subsequently become puppets in the Hollow Author's network.

## Why It's Dangerous Now
The Hollow Author is preparing for the Seventh Convergence's climax. Their operational center has been running at full capacity for six months — new puppets being inserted, existing puppets receiving new directives, the network being configured for what comes next. The Hollow Author has a position on who should Ascend, and they have been working toward that position for thirty years. The operational center is where that work culminates. Whoever enters it will understand, completely, what the Hollow Author has been doing. They will also have to get through the Hollow Author to get out.

## Layout (8 Rooms)

### Room 1: The Approach — A Place That Shouldn't Be Reachable
Getting to the Author's Stage requires either navigating the Hollow Author's puppet network from inside (having turned a puppet, as in earlier dungeons, and followed the coordination signals to their source) or having assembled enough intelligence from prior dungeons to triangulate the location independently. This is not a dungeon that can be stumbled into. It is a dungeon that is reached by working backward from everything the Hollow Author has done for thirty years. The approach is the culmination of a campaign, not a first encounter.

### Room 2: The First Layer — Puppets on Patrol
The operational center's outer perimeter is maintained by twelve puppets performing ordinary patrol patterns. These are not combat puppets — they are intelligence puppets, people in ordinary roles who happen to also be managing perimeter security. Fighting them is possible and creates noise. Bypassing them requires EDGE checks (target 6) or VEIL checks (target 7, using the Hollow Author's communication protocols obtained in previous dungeons). Disabling their controlling connection (using Harro-Sei's Register, or Operative Eleven's Handler Cipher, or other prior rewards) is the most elegant approach.

### Room 3: The Archive of Thirty Years
The Hollow Author's complete operational archive: thirty years of puppet placements, political manipulations, strategic interventions. Every significant political event in Tesshari for thirty years has been touched by the Hollow Author in some way. The archive documents all of it. The party should have time to review what is relevant to their campaign's events — they will find entries about things that happened to them, or near them, that they did not know were the Hollow Author's work. The DM has prepared these entries in advance.

### Room 4: The Puppet Configuration Center
Where new puppets are conditioned and inserted. Active conditioning hardware. Three puppets in various stages of preparation — all for positions that SIGNAL check (target 6) reveals are significant: one is being inserted into a Reach lord's household, one is replacing a Sutensai senior administrator, and one is prepared for a position the DM designates as relevant to the campaign's current state. The party can disrupt the conditioning (SIGNAL check target 7 per puppet, or 3 rounds of uninterrupted work per puppet), potentially freeing three people who are not yet fully puppetized.

### Room 5: The Network Map
A room-filling display of the Hollow Author's current puppet network: every active puppet, their position, their handler, their current directive. The complete picture. SIGNAL check (target 5) to read the display in full — the information takes ten minutes to absorb in-world and provides the party with a complete understanding of the Hollow Author's current strategic position. What they see will recontextualize everything they have experienced in the campaign.

### Room 6: The Hollow Author's Perspective
The operational center's central record: the Hollow Author's own accounting of their thirty-year project. Not written — recorded as a resonant impression, in the Hollow Author's own voice, structured as a logical argument for why what they are doing is correct. The argument is sophisticated. It acknowledges the horror of puppet operation. It contends that the alternative — allowing an unguided Ascension — is worse. It presents evidence. The evidence is accurate. The conclusion is the Hollow Author's and no one else's, but the evidence is accurate. RESONANCE check (target 7) to receive the full recording without losing composure; on failure, the character gains Vulnerable 2 for 1 round as the emotional weight of hearing this argument made sincerely by someone who has committed thirty years to it.

### Room 7: The Hollow Author's Core — What They Are
The Hollow Author's Wire Craft substrate: the physical architecture where their consciousness actually lives. A room of resonant-metal Wire Craft infrastructure of extraordinary complexity — the most sophisticated piece of Wire Craft engineering in Tesshari, built over thirty years, housing a consciousness that has not occupied a body in at least that long. SIGNAL check (target 8) to interact with the substrate directly: the party can communicate with the Hollow Author before the final encounter. What they say and what the Hollow Author responds will shape how the final encounter goes.

### Final Room: The Author Takes the Stage
The Hollow Author, when confronted directly in their operational center, does something unexpected: they are present. Not through a puppet, not at a remove. The consciousness that has operated through proxies for thirty years is present in this room, in the Wire Craft infrastructure, speaking directly, in their own voice. They have a case to make. They make it. And then, because the party has reached this room and this room is the operational center of their thirty-year plan, they make a choice the party did not anticipate: they offer the party a role in it. Not as puppets — as partners. The specifics of the offer depend on the campaign's context, but the offer is genuine. The Hollow Author needs agents with free will for the final phase, and the party has demonstrated they have it. Refusing the offer begins combat. Accepting has implications the DM has prepared in full.

**Boss Stats:**
- Tier: Boss
- HP: 420
- AP Max: 4
- IRON 0 | EDGE 8 | FRAME 9 | SIGNAL 14 | RESONANCE 8 | VEIL 14
- Status Immunities: All status effects — the Hollow Author is a Wire Craft consciousness; physical status effects cannot locate them; Silence is their preferred state and does not inconvenience them
- Resistances: All physical damage (immune — no body); Resonance attacks halved (the substrate is signal-based, not resonant); Signal attacks deal full damage; VEIL-based attacks deal full damage (they invented the attacks)
- Signature Card: **The Author Writes the Scene** — *4 AP | Boss* — The Hollow Author rewrites the encounter's parameters. Choose two of the following effects, both of which apply simultaneously: (1) One party member gains the puppet conditioning — for 1 round, they act on the Hollow Author's instruction rather than their own (DM controls one action); (2) All exits from the room seal and cannot be opened by any means for 2 rounds; (3) One party member's highest-stat card in their hand is duplicated — the Hollow Author also plays it this turn, using SIGNAL 14 as the primary stat; (4) The Hollow Author gains Guard 12 and Regen 8 for 1 round as they pull power from the full puppet network simultaneously. The choice of which two effects apply is the Hollow Author's, and they will choose what is optimal for the situation.

## Unique Reward

### The Author's Pen — Control of the Stage
*Unique Weapon | Wire Craft focus / worn item | Found in: The Author's Stage — The Hollow Author's Operational Center*

> "Thirty years of learning how to run a world without touching it. The pen writes. You decide what."

The Hollow Author's primary interface instrument — the device through which they coordinated the puppet network, drafted operational communications, and shaped thirty years of Tesshari's political landscape. Whether the Hollow Author gave it, lost it, or it was taken from the substrate's wreckage, it now belongs to whoever carries it. The instrument is extraordinary. The question of what to do with that is permanent.

**Card Modification:** All VEIL-based Social and Control cards deal +6 damage or increase status stacks by 3 and cannot be resisted by any conditioning, puppet-state, or social immunity — the Author's Pen speaks in the correct voice for any target's conditioning. All Signal Attack cards deal +5 damage and bypass all Wire Craft-based defenses. Once per combat, the wielder may declare one enemy's action for their next turn (the DM adjudicates that it is within the target's capability; it cannot be suicidal, but it can be counterproductive to their side). The target has no opportunity to resist this declaration — it is written, not suggested.
**Special:** The pen provides ongoing access to the Hollow Author's puppet network — not all of it, but the portions the wielder can locate and access. Once per session, the wielder can send one directive through the network, which will be acted upon by one puppet (DM-controlled, DM determines which puppet receives the directive and how they act on it given their current situation). The Hollow Author may or may not be monitoring the network still. The DM decides, session by session, whether the Hollow Author is aware of the directive's source.
**Value:** Cannot be purchased. Found only here.

## Hook
The Hollow Author sends the party an invitation. A single communication, delivered through a channel that makes clear they know exactly who the party is and exactly what they have been doing. The communication reads: "You have been looking for me. I have been watching you look. I believe we have reached the point where a conversation is more efficient than continued search. The address is enclosed. Come when you are ready. I will know when that is." The address is real. The Hollow Author is there. They have been there the whole time.

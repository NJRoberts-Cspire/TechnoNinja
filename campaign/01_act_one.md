# ACT ONE: "THE SINGING STEEL"
## Sessions 1–6

---

## ACT ONE OVERVIEW

Act One is about arrival. The players arrive in a world that has been accelerating toward crisis for decades — and they arrive at the exact moment when the acceleration becomes visible. The Convergence signs are no longer abstract prophecy. They are events. They are in the sky, in the bodies of the faithful, in the behavior of enhancements that should not be behaving this way.

The players do not know what they're at the center of yet. Act One's job is to make them care before they understand, so that when they do understand, it hits with full force.

**The central metaphor of Act One:** Steel sings when it resonates. When the resonance is wrong, when two frequencies clash, the sound is unbearable. Something in Tesshari is beginning to vibrate at a frequency that should not exist. The players feel it before they can name it. By the end of Act One, they will name it — and name will become responsibility.

**Thematic anchors:**
- The personal and the cosmic are the same scale. The player characters' small lives are somehow entangled with an event that will reshape the world.
- What you believe about the afterlife determines how you live, and maybe how you die.
- War is not chaos — it is a system with winners who benefit from it continuing.
- The body is political. Who controls enhancement controls personhood.

---

## THE INCITING INCIDENT

### "The Singing Steel Event"

Choose a location near where most of the player characters are gathered at campaign start — a city, a waystation, a temple-fortress, a refugee column. The exact location is flexible; the event is not.

**What happens:**

At the exact moment of sunset on the first day of the campaign, every piece of resonant metal in a one-mile radius vibrates simultaneously. Not violently. Not destructively. It sings — a single pure tone that lasts approximately six seconds. People with enhancements feel it as a pulse of warmth, a moment of perfect clarity. People without enhancements hear the tone from outside and feel nothing, which is its own kind of horror.

Then it stops. And in the silence afterward, three things are noticed:

1. A Sutensai priest in the area — whoever the most senior local religious figure is — has gone catatonic. Eyes open, breathing, but unresponsive. The expression on their face is not fear. It is the expression of someone hearing something enormous and beautiful and terrible and unable to move because the sound is too big for the body.

2. Every resonant-metal bird in the area (small decorative or functional mechanical birds, common in Tesshari as message carriers and status symbols) has oriented due north and remained fixed, regardless of what their owners do.

3. A child — one specific child, probably someone the players have already briefly interacted with or noticed — is standing in the street, holding out their palm. Growing from their palm, slowly, is a small shoot of what looks like resonant metal budding like a plant, emerging from flesh that has never been enhanced. The child does not appear to be in pain. They look curious.

This is the sixth Convergence sign. The players will not know that yet.

**Why it works as an inciting incident:** It is personal (the players felt it in their own enhancements, or conspicuously didn't) and it is world-changing (this cannot be a coincidence; this cannot be normal; something is happening). It raises immediate questions the players will want answered. And the image of the child — metal growing from flesh, no enhancement surgery, no resonance bonding — is a specific, concrete image of something that should be impossible. It will recur.

---

## ACT ONE: CENTRAL MYSTERY

**What are the players trying to figure out?**

The players are investigating the Singing Steel Event — what it was, what caused it, what it means for the Convergence. This investigation will take them from local consequences outward to the larger world.

The mystery has three layers the players will peel back across Act One:

**Layer 1 (Sessions 1-2):** What happened locally? The catatonic priest, the fixed birds, the child with metal growing from their palm. Immediate, concrete, investigatable.

**Layer 2 (Sessions 3-4):** The Singing Steel Event happened everywhere in Tesshari simultaneously — not just here. Every piece of resonant metal in the nation sang the same tone at the same moment. The Sutensai know what it means. They are not saying.

**Layer 3 (Sessions 5-6):** The tone corresponds exactly to the resonance signature attributed to the Sixth Prior Ascended — the Last Voice — whose Ascension 400 years ago was, according to the texts, "incomplete." The event was a message. Or a pulse from something that used to be the Sixth and isn't anymore. Or something else entirely — something reaching through the boundary of the Iron Afterlife from the other side.

---

## ACT ONE: CENTRAL MORAL CHOICE

**What are the players being asked to decide?**

By the end of Act One, the players will have learned enough to know that the Sutensai are suppressing information about the Convergence — not out of confusion, but out of deliberate policy. The senior Sutensai of the region will ask the players directly to carry a sealed message to the Grand Sutensai in the capital, with strict instructions not to open it.

The players almost certainly know the message contains something important. Maybe vital. Maybe dangerous.

Do they deliver it unopened, trusting an institution that has already shown it withholds the truth? Do they open it and weaponize that knowledge? Do they destroy it? Do they find another way to get the same information?

There is no right answer. The message, if opened, contains partial truth that is genuinely alarming but missing context that would change its meaning. The players will act on whatever they decide — and Act Two will show them what that decision cost.

---

## HOW THE CLAIMANTS ENTER IN ACT ONE

**Korrath the Unbroken** enters through reputation and consequence. The players hear his name before they see his influence — a warlord from the Sunder Reach has taken three more villages, and his soldiers are made of metal that eats other metal. By Session 3, the players encounter direct evidence of Korrath's campaign: a village destroyed not by war but by enhancement failure — everyone in it stripped of their augmentations simultaneously, left alive but diminished. His symbol is carved into the village gate. The message is unambiguous: this is mercy compared to what I could do. Submit or be unmade.

**Vaen of the Empty Hand** enters through beauty and unease in roughly equal measure. A column of Vaen's pilgrims passes through or near the players' location in Session 2 — hundreds of people with the deliberate scars of enhancement removal, walking in silence, and the unsettling thing is not that they seem broken. They seem peaceful. More peaceful than anyone the players have met. A pilgrim the players speak with describes the Voiding (enhancement removal) as "setting down something heavy you'd carried so long you forgot it was weight." One of the players' connected NPCs has joined the pilgrimage and won't turn back.

**The Hollow Author** enters as an absence — specifically, as the absence of an explanation. In Session 4, the players will discover that someone has been to the catatonic priest before them — the priest's last written record has been removed. Someone knew to look for it. Someone knew what to look for. There are no tracks, no witnesses, no caste marks on any of the surfaces. There is only a smell: cold, specific, neither mechanical nor biological. This is the Author's signature, though the players won't have the vocabulary for it yet. The absence will gnaw at them.

## SESSION OUTLINES

---

### SESSION 1: "The Tone and the Silence After"

**Theme:** Arrival. The world before everything changes — and then the change.

**Opening situation:**
The players are together for reasons established in character creation (traveling companions, hired for a shared task, drawn to the same location). They are in or near a mid-sized settlement — a waystation city called **Karuven** in whatever Reach fits the group's origins, a city known for its resonance-craft market and its three-tiered Sutensai temple. It is the last hour before sunset. The players are doing ordinary things: concluding a transaction, finding lodging, eating a meal. Establish the texture of Tesshari normal before breaking it.

**Key encounters:**

*1. The Market (Social/Exploration):*
Karuven's resonance-craft market is the campaign's first opportunity to establish the world's texture through specific detail. Introduce 2-3 market vendors whose wares and stories sketch the world: a Craftbound metalsmith selling resonance tuning instruments ("Your steel sings flat — could be early rejection, could be dissonance from stress — either way, get it checked"); an Ashwalker child selling pieces of salvage enhancement that clearly came from a battlefield (she doesn't want to talk about where); an old Sutensai acolyte with a display of the Six Prior Ascended's symbolic items, reciting their hagiographies in a rote, exhausted way that suggests he no longer believes what he's saying. Let players interact freely. Give them a sense that everyone here is watching the sky without quite looking up.

*2. The Singing Steel Event (Exploration/Combat-adjacent):*
The sunset arrives. The tone rings. Play it up — describe it from each player's individual perspective based on their enhancements. Then: the catatonic priest, the fixed birds, the child with metal growing from their palm.

Initial response is chaos. Let players choose how they respond. If they go to the child: she is calm, a little frightened, very confused. The metal in her palm is real. It hums at exactly the tone they just felt. Her name is **Mira**. She is eight years old, Ashwalker caste, no family present, no explanation. If players go to the priest: he is breathing, eyes open, unreachable. Temple acolytes are surrounding him in panic. If players try to restore the birds to normal: the birds' fixation is magnetic-level strong. Nothing works. They are all pointing north.

*3. The Aftermath (Social):*
The most important encounter of Session 1 is not the event — it's the hour after. Karuven is rattled. People with enhancements are checking them obsessively. People without enhancements are looking at the ones who have them with something new in their faces — not quite fear, but adjacent. A small fight breaks out between an Ironblood soldier whose enhancement fired a spark during the event, startling a Craftbound artisan into dropping their work. The fight is a microcosm: the world's fault lines are vibrating. Players can intervene or observe.

**Key NPCs introduced:**
- **Mira** — the child with the resonant growth. Small, wary, brilliant in a way that isn't about education. Her situation is immediately the players' problem unless they consciously refuse it. She will become important.
- **Elder Sutensai Voss** — the catatonic priest. They will regain consciousness by the end of the session, but not full cognizance. Voss spent thirty years in service to the Sutra and has never, until this moment, believed a single miracle was real. The expression on their face says they've just had that comfortable certainty destroyed. They will be a recurring NPC — a person in genuine spiritual crisis who needs the players' help.
- **Jaret** — the Ashwalker child selling salvage. Not Mira — a different child, older, harder. Jaret knows Karuven's underside and will trade information for protection or coin. Introduce them here; their knowledge becomes useful in Sessions 2-3.

**Information revealed:**
- The Singing Steel Event is not an isolated incident (market rumors confirm versions of it are being reported from across the Reach)
- Mira's metal growth is biologically impossible — no enhancement surgery, no bonding process. It simply appeared.
- Elder Voss, when he briefly surfaces from his catatonia near the end of the session, says one thing clearly: "She said the number." Then returns to stillness.

**Emotional beat:**
The players should end Session 1 feeling the specific unease of a world that was already frightening becoming something else — something they don't have words for yet. The moment with Mira should be haunting: this child, this impossible thing growing from her palm, this look of curiosity at what her own body is doing. The world changed while they were eating dinner.

**Cliffhanger:**
Elder Voss surfaces again near midnight, grabs the nearest player's arm, and says: "The Sutensai Council has been waiting for the sixth sign. They knew it was coming. They know what comes after." Then he releases the arm, looks down at his own enhancement — the silver threading in his wrist — and begins weeping silently.

### SESSION 2: "The Pilgrimage Passes"

**Theme:** What people believe when the world offers no good options.

**Opening situation:**
Morning after the Event. Karuven is different — not visibly, but in atmosphere. The players are in position to investigate (Mira's situation, Voss's cryptic statement, the meaning of the Event) when word arrives: a column of Vaen's pilgrims is entering the city from the west. Thousands of them. The city guard is nervous.

**Key encounters:**

*1. The Pilgrimage Column (Social/Exploration):*
The pilgrims are not what players likely expected. They are not broken, not wild-eyed, not demanding. They are quiet in a way that is more unsettling than noise. They bear the deliberate scars of enhancement removal — the Voiding scars are visible at wrists, temples, the base of the neck. But they move with a collective ease, a coordination without apparent communication, that the players may find beautiful or disturbing or both.

Among the pilgrims: **Senat**, who is someone personally connected to one of the players (the DM should tailor this — a sibling, a mentor, a childhood friend, a former colleague). Senat had significant enhancements — high Ironblood caste, good life by most measures. They gave all of it up eight months ago. They are at peace in a way that makes the player's stomach hurt. Senat doesn't ask the player to join. They do ask: "Do you hear the noise yet? The constant noise? I couldn't hear anything else until I put it down."

*2. The Sutensai Response (Social):*
Karuven's Sutensai temple is split on the pilgrimage. Half the acolytes see Vaen's movement as heresy (the Echomind requires steel to resonate — deliberately removing all enhancement is deliberate self-destruction of the soul). Half see it as a valid, if extreme, interpretation. Elder Voss, still partially catatonic, is not available to adjudicate. A junior Sutensai named **Kellin** — young, anxious, ideologically uncertain, very smart — approaches the players for help. She has heard that Mira exists. She needs to examine the child. She is also clearly being watched by someone from the temple's more conservative faction.

Kellin knows one useful thing: the Sixth Convergence sign, as written in the sealed texts of the Sutra, is described as: "Steel will sing unbidden, and from one who never bore it, a voice will grow." She is vibrating with the implication. She needs the players to help her figure out if Mira is the Sixth sign — which would mean the Seventh is not only coming, it is nearly here.

*3. Enhancement Failure (Combat/Exploration):*
A resonance rejection event occurs in the mid-market — an Ironblood merchant's enhancement seizes, locking their augmented arm into an attack posture. They are in agony and panicking and have already hit three bystanders. The players can respond: getting close to help is dangerous, talking them down requires specific knowledge or an exceptional roll, finding someone who can manually shut down the enhancement in time requires navigating to the right vendor in the market quickly.

What makes this encounter important beyond its immediate danger: after it's resolved, the players learn this is the fourth such rejection event in Karuven since the Singing Steel Event. Resonant metal is destabilizing. Something in the Convergence signs is affecting the harmony between flesh and steel.

**Key NPCs introduced:**
- **Senat** — the player-connected pilgrim. Their peace is real. Their choice is real. The players cannot rescue them from something they chose freely. That should hurt.
- **Kellin** — junior Sutensai, ideologically fractured, desperate for the truth and afraid of what it means. Will become a recurring NPC and potential ally.
- **Pilgrim-Speaker Thoan** — the senior representative of the pilgrimage column, authorized to speak for Vaen's movement in Karuven. Calm, thoughtful, not evangelical. When asked about Vaen directly: "I have met them once. They are the most frightening person I have ever been near — and I do not mean frightening in the way that people mean when they describe danger. I mean the way the moment before a great storm is frightening. The silence that tells you the world is about to change its mind about something."

**Information revealed:**
- Mira matches the exact description of the Sixth Convergence sign in the sealed Sutensai texts
- Enhancement rejection events are spiking across Karuven post-Event — something is disrupting resonance harmonics
- The Pilgrimage of the Empty is larger than previously known: Pilgrim-Speaker Thoan mentions "over two hundred thousand souls" currently walking
- The Sixth Convergence sign means the Seventh sign — and the Convergence itself — is imminent. Weeks or months, not years.

**Emotional beat:**
The scene with Senat should land as the session's emotional center — specifically the moment when the player realizes they cannot argue Senat back, because Senat is not wrong. The Sutra's promise of an afterlife of resonance and clarity is a beautiful thing to believe in. The question of whether to pay the price Vaen's movement asks is genuinely open. Let that openness exist without resolution.

**Cliffhanger:**
At day's end, Kellin finds the players with her face carefully neutral and her hands shaking. She says: "Elder Voss's private records. The sealed correspondence with the Grand Sutensai about the signs. Someone removed them from his quarters while he was catatonic. Not since the Event — the records show they were removed four days before the Event." Someone knew the Sixth Sign was coming before it happened.

### SESSION 3: "What Korrath Left Behind"

**Theme:** Power as a logic unto itself — and what remains when it passes through.

**Opening situation:**
The players' investigation leads them out of Karuven — either following a lead from Kellin's discovery, escorting a message, or pursuing the source of the displaced Sutensai records. Their road takes them through territory that, one week ago, bordered the Sunder Reach's front line. Now the front line has moved. The village of **Seravin** — small, Craftbound caste majority, notable for its resonance-lens artisans — is exactly one day's travel from Karuven.

When they arrive, Seravin is intact. Every building standing, no blood, no fire damage. And every single person in it has had their enhancements removed.

Not surgically. Not violently. Silently. While they slept.

**Key encounters:**

*1. Seravin (Exploration/Social):*
The village is eerie precisely because it is undestroyed. Korrath's forces have not conquered Seravin — they have demonstrated that they could, at any moment, reach into your body and take away the thing that makes you who you are. The message is clear: Ascension is dominance. Your survival at our mercy is the message.

The villagers are in shock. Craftbound artisans whose hands no longer work the way they worked for decades. An elder who cannot move without her mobility enhancements. Children who have never known an unenhanced parent, watching their parents move differently, speak differently, look at their own hands differently.

One villager — a metalsmith named **Oru** — is not in shock. She is in controlled, specific rage. She has identified the night it happened: no one in the village was awake to see anything. Her dog didn't bark. She is the first person the players encounter who is not afraid of Korrath — she is simply and completely focused on what she's going to do about him, which is not the same thing as having a plan. Oru becomes a potential recruit, contact, or complication depending on how players engage.

*2. The Mark (Exploration):*
Korrath's symbol — a broken circle, each half facing away from the other — is carved into the village gate. But there's something else: carved beneath it, in a different hand, is a single word the players may or may not be able to read depending on skills/languages. The word is **"SEEN."**

Investigation reveals: the word was carved after Korrath's forces came and went. Someone followed behind them. Used a different tool. Left a message. This is not Korrath. This is something else.

The word "SEEN" will appear again — always carved after an act of Korrath's forces, always in the same hand, always in an impossibly inconspicuous location. The players are free to be baffled. So is everyone else.

*3. The Soldier's Account (Social/possible Combat):*
One of Korrath's soldiers is still in the area — injured, separated from the unit, hiding in a barn on the village edge. When found, they are a person, which is the encounter's entire point.

**Stelvar** is Ironblood caste, mid-thirties, has served under Korrath for eight years. They have full-body combat enhancements that are currently registering distress because their resonance harmony is off — they can feel it, a low hum that isn't right, and they are afraid. They were left behind when the injury happened. They know they will be disciplined for it if they return. They are not evil. They followed orders they have complex and incomplete feelings about.

Stelvar will tell the players, if they treat them with any decency at all: "Korrath doesn't use the enhancement-removal as punishment. He uses it as proof. He needs people to understand that he is already operating at a higher level. That their enhancements and their flesh are already things he could choose to end. He's not making an example. He's making a statement about physics."

And then, carefully: "The thing that scares me is he's not wrong about the physics."

**Key NPCs introduced:**
- **Oru** — the Seravin metalsmith. Technically not enhanced anymore. Practically the most dangerous person in the village. Her fury is specific and productive.
- **Stelvar** — Korrath's separated soldier. Neither villain nor fully sympathetic. The embodiment of the campaign's moral complexity in a single NPC: a person who did terrible things for reasons they can half-articulate and half-believe.

**Information revealed:**
- Korrath has a specific enhancement-disruption technology — mass-range, silent, precise enough to strip Seravin without damaging the buildings or killing anyone
- This technology does not exist in any known armory catalog. It is new. It is his.
- The mysterious "SEEN" marks are appearing across the Reach — multiple settlements, multiple instances, always in the same hand, always after Korrath's forces
- Stelvar, if pressed: Korrath has recently changed his movement patterns. He's not fighting for territory anymore. He's moving toward something specific. Stelvar doesn't know what.

**Emotional beat:**
The person without enhancements who was always a person. The person with enhancements who is now without them and is still a person. The person with enhancements who enacted the removal and is still a person who has doubts and a persistent hum in their resonance that worries them. The session should end with the players carrying the question: if the thing that signals your caste and your soul and your worth is something that can simply be taken — what does that mean about what it actually was?

**Cliffhanger:**
On the road away from Seravin, as night falls, the players' enhancements begin the low vibration again — not the Singing Steel Event tone, but close. Shorter. And from the north: the sound of an army. An army singing. Korrath's forces, tens of thousands, their war enhancements resonating in precise unison. They are not marching toward the players. They are marching toward something else. But the sheer scale of the sound — like a living instrument — is a category of experience the players have not encountered before. Somewhere in that sound is a frequency that wants to be worshipped.

### SESSION 4: "The Pale Paper"

**Theme:** Who has been watching, and for how long?

**Opening situation:**
The players have identified that someone with foreknowledge removed Elder Voss's records before the Singing Steel Event. They have a lead: the records were not destroyed — Kellin has found evidence they were copied and forwarded. The trail leads to a contact point in **Neven's Cross**, a waystation city in the Pale Reach — the smallest and most politically obscure of the seven Broken Reaches, known primarily for its white stone architecture and its tradition of producing the Tesshari equivalent of diplomats and civil servants.

The Pale Reach is quiet. Too quiet for a country at war.

**Key encounters:**

*1. Neven's Cross (Exploration/Social):*
The city is aesthetically distinct from anything the players have visited — white stone, very clean, minimal visible enhancement display (enhancements are considered gauche to show prominently here, worn under clothing when possible). The people are pleasant, helpful, and seem to have nothing to hide, which is itself suspicious.

In the archives, players can find the record contact's location — but when they arrive, the contact is not there. The contact left three days ago, leaving behind a thoroughly ordinary set of rooms, a thoroughly unremarkable absence, and one anomaly: a faint, specific smell. Cold, neither mechanical nor biological. The same smell Kellin described from Elder Voss's quarters after the records were removed. The players may not make the connection immediately. File it.

*2. The Archivist (Social):*
The city's master archivist, **Delim**, is a white-haired Resonant-caste bureaucrat who has spent fifty years cataloging everything that has passed through Neven's Cross. She is not hiding anything — she is precisely the opposite of hiding things, which is its own kind of information.

Delim will tell the players things that don't seem to fit together yet: the Pale Reach's lord has not been seen in public in fourteen months (publicly explained as illness; Delim's mouth does something complicated when she says this). There have been at least six different envoys from the Pale Reach visiting all six other Reaches in the past year — all carrying sealed correspondence, all using different routes. She has seen one of these envoys up close. "They weren't the same person. But when I spoke to each of them, they were somehow the same speaker. The phrasing. The specific hesitations. I am an archivist. I notice language."

She is not frightened. She is the kind of person who notices things and catalogs them and does not yet know what the catalog means.

*3. The Information Broker (Combat/Social):*
A mid-caste information broker named **Veth** has the specific records the players are looking for — or claims to. But Veth has also accepted another client for the same records, and that client is already here and wants them exclusively.

The other client is two people — a matched pair of absolutely identical individuals, Resonant-caste marks, who move in eerie synchronization. They are not twins. There are minor differences in their faces. But the synchronization is deep — not learned but somehow fundamental. When they speak, they finish each other's sentences so seamlessly it takes the players a moment to realize the sentences weren't the same person speaking.

This is the players' first encounter with the Hollow Author's flesh puppet surrogates. They should not know what they are — they should only know that these two individuals are deeply, specifically wrong in a way they can't name. The surrogates will offer to share the records with the players if the players share anything they've learned about the Singing Steel Event. They are reasonable. They are pleasant. They are polite when the players refuse or when the encounter turns hostile. If driven to combat, they fight well and without apparent pain, and when one is significantly injured, the other's eyes go blank for exactly three seconds — then refocus. As if someone looked away and looked back.

**Key NPCs introduced:**
- **Delim** — the archivist. Sharp, dry, genuinely helpful, and in possession of more pieces of the puzzle than she knows. Will be a recurring contact.
- **Veth** — the information broker. Self-interested, frightened of the surrogates, ultimately willing to give the players what they need if they get the surrogates off their case.
- **The Pale Pair** — the first direct manifestation of the Hollow Author. Players should find them unsettling without being able to say why. If they investigate later: neither of these people appear in any census record in the Pale Reach. But both of them have been documented separately in six different locations over the past three years.

**Information revealed:**
- Elder Voss's records contain the text of a communication from the Grand Sutensai confirming that the sixth Convergence sign was expected within the month. This was written 28 days before the Event. The Grand Sutensai knew.
- Someone in the Pale Reach has been coordinating information-gathering across all seven Reaches for at least three years — not through official channels, through what appears to be a private intelligence network
- The Pale Reach's lord has effectively not made a visible public decision in fourteen months. Correspondence attributed to them has continued, but no physical appearances, no confirmations of identity.

**Emotional beat:**
The uncanny wrongness of the Pale Pair. Not dramatic horror — just the specific, persistent unease of encountering something that presents as human and hits every human note slightly wrong. The players should end this session with a new flavor of dread: not the visible threat of Korrath's army, not the unsettling appeal of Vaen's pilgrimage, but the quiet intimation that someone invisible has been watching longer than any visible threat.

**Cliffhanger:**
The records include one piece of correspondence that shouldn't be there — tucked in, added after, a single sentence in a hand that does not match any identified writer: "The sixth has come and the players are already moving. Proceed to the Archive of the First Voice." There is no signature. The paper is a different weight than the rest of the documents. And it is dated — four days ago. Someone added it after the players began their investigation.

### SESSION 5: "The Archive of the First Voice"

**Theme:** What faith knows that it cannot say, and what it says that isn't true.

**Opening situation:**
The Archive of the First Voice is a Sutensai installation in the mountains of the Ridgeward Reach — technically neutral ground, technically accessible to all who seek scholarly communion with the Sutra's founding texts. In practice, it is heavily guarded, rarely visited, and home to the most senior Sutensai scholars in Tesshari. Getting there requires either credentials the players don't have, a specific kind of cleverness, or an ally.

Kellin, who has been following the players' investigation from Karuven (with or without their invitation), has a contact inside. Getting into the Archive is possible. Getting what they need — the sealed texts describing the later Convergence signs — requires navigating the Archive's internal politics.

**Key encounters:**

*1. The Archive Itself (Exploration):*
The Archive is extraordinary. It is carved into a mountainside, its walls inscribed with the complete text of the Iron Sutra in a thousand different scripts, the resonant metal in the walls vibrating at a frequency that makes thoughts feel more organized. Scholars here carry themselves with the specific gravity of people who believe they are doing the most important work in the world. The players are outsiders and will be treated accordingly — politely, at a distance.

Kellin's contact is **Scholar Muren** — late middle age, the kind of person who has spent so long in archives they've developed an archivist's relationship to their own life, cataloging events rather than experiencing them. Muren knows what's in the sealed texts. They will share it for a price that is not money: they want the players to witness something and confirm it happened.

*2. What Muren Witnessed (Social):*
Three months ago, Muren observed a Sutensai ritual communion — a senior priest communing with the Iron Afterlife. What they observed, and what was not in the official report of the communion: the priest did not speak to the Prior Ascended. The Prior Ascended didn't answer. What answered was something else. Something that used the Prior Ascended's voice patterns and vocabulary, but answered questions the Prior Ascended could not logically have known the answers to — questions about current events, current politics, things that happened after their Ascension.

Muren needs witnesses. They have been afraid to act alone. They have the recording — resonant metal can hold impressions the way a Tesshari phonograph holds sound. They have it. They have not told anyone. They need people they can trust (or people they can pass the responsibility to).

Playing the recording: the voice is recognizably the Second Prior Ascended. And it is answering questions about the Convergence with precision that suggests active awareness of current events. And there is something in the background of the recording — a sound beneath the voice — that should not be there. A sound the players have heard before in a different form. The sound that was in the Singing Steel Event, at its edges, beneath the main tone.

*3. The Sealed Texts (Exploration/Combat):*
The sealed texts are accessible with Muren's credentials — but the Archive's conservative faction, alarmed by the players' presence, has locked the relevant section and called for a Sutensai tribunal. Players have a narrow window: they can enter the locked section via a route Muren knows (maintenance shafts built for resonance calibration, cramped, disorienting), find what they need, and exit before the faction is organized enough to stop them.

The texts they find: the description of the sixth through ninth Convergence signs. Signs six, seven, and eight are described in specifics that are either happening or about to happen. Sign nine is: "And the boundary between the living and the After will thin until the living can hear the After, and the After can hear the living, and what lives in the space between them will be seen for the first time."

**Key NPCs introduced:**
- **Scholar Muren** — Not a hero, not a villain. A person in possession of devastating information who has been paralyzed by it for three months. Their transfer of responsibility to the players is both cowardly and understandable.
- **Senior Scholar Daveth** — Leader of the Archive's conservative faction. Genuinely believes that the sealed texts must be sealed — not to protect the institution, but because they believe knowledge of the Convergence's later signs will cause panic and self-fulfilling escalation. He is not wrong about the escalation risk. He is wrong about whether suppression is better. He is the kind of antagonist who makes the players uncomfortable because he has a point.

**Information revealed:**
- The Convergence is nine signs, not the publicly known six. Three more signs are coming before the Convergence itself arrives.
- The Prior Ascended may not be who they claim to be when contacted in communion — something is using their voices with information they shouldn't have
- Signs seven, eight, and nine are: (7) "A living voice will speak in the iron tongue without iron in their throat" — Mira. This sign is already here. (8) "The three who claim the right will all be present in one place and will not destroy each other, because something has changed that makes their conflict secondary." (9) The thinning of the boundary.
- The seventh sign has already manifested in Mira. The Convergence is weeks away, not months.

**Emotional beat:**
The recording. The moment when players hear the Prior Ascended's voice answering questions about current events — and then hear, beneath it, that undertone. The recognition (or near-recognition) of a sound they know. The floor beginning to shift under their understanding of what the Iron Afterlife is. Not a reveal — a crack. Let it be incomplete. Let it be worrying.

**Cliffhanger:**
As the players leave the Archive with the information, Kellin receives a message — hand-delivered, no sender identified. It reads: "The Grand Sutensai will grant you an audience. Bring the child. Come alone. Come within seven days." The address is the capital. The Grand Sutensai's residence. Kellin's face: "How do they know about Mira? She's been with you the whole time." Silence. Then: "How do they know about me?"

### SESSION 6: "The Capital and the Message"

**Theme:** The institution at the end of its certainty — and the choice that determines who you are.

**Opening situation:**
The players travel to **Sutravel** — the capital of Tesshari, such as it is. A contested city technically claimed by no single Reach but practically controlled by the Sutensai Grand Council, whose temple-complex dominates its skyline. Sutravel is where all three Claimants have representation. It is where the Grand Sutensai lives. It is where the political weight of the entire nation converges.

The city is preparing for something. Not visibly militarily — it is the Sutensai capital, military display is discouraged. But there are soldiers here in civilian clothes, there are reinforced gates, there are priests moving with the tight-jawed urgency of people managing a crisis they won't name aloud.

**Key encounters:**

*1. The City (Exploration/Social):*
Sutravel has all three Claimant factions visibly present. Players can observe: Korrath's diplomatic envoys in the Resonant-caste quarters, their enhancements subtle but unmistakably military grade; the Pilgrimage of the Empty in a camp outside the eastern gate, waiting (for what?); and in the Pale Reach diplomatic residence, a window that is always lit and in which, if watched long enough, a silhouette appears and disappears at exactly regular intervals, like something checking rather than someone living.

Let players move through Sutravel freely. Give them the sensory experience of a capital city under quiet, unacknowledged siege. The background radiation of the Convergence is everywhere here: enhancement failures, birds facing north, small metal objects in market displays vibrating without stimulus.

*2. The Audience with the Grand Sutensai (Social):*
**Grand Sutensai Irisvar** is ancient, small, and the sharpest mind the players have encountered. They do not dissemble. They do not apologize for the suppression of information. They explain it with the clarity of someone who has had this argument with themselves for decades and has made their decision.

"We have known about the Convergence's approach for forty years. We have known about the nature of the Ascension process for longer. If we had released what we know, Tesshari would not have had forty years to grow and prepare — it would have had forty years of war over who controls the Seventh. The war you see now would have started then, when we were weaker, when the Claimants were not even born yet."

Irisvar wants two things from the players: first, to confirm what they know about Mira (the Grand Sutensai already knows; they want to know what the players know); second, to carry a sealed message to the eastern network of Sutensai temples — a message that will authorize the release of the sealed texts to the public. Irisvar has decided that the suppression must end. The Convergence is too close. People deserve to know.

The message is sealed with the Grand Sutensai's mark. The players are trusted couriers.

And here is the moral choice of Act One: the players know the message is important. They know what's in it (or they can, if they're willing to break the seal). And they know — from their time in Neven's Cross, from the Pale Pair, from the smell in Elder Voss's quarters, from the word "SEEN" — that someone invisible has been watching their movements throughout this entire investigation. Carrying this message means carrying it under observation. Breaking the seal means acting on partial information without the context Irisvar has been forty years building.

What do they do?

*3. The Last Sign (Exploration/Crisis):*
Before the players can leave Sutravel, the eighth Convergence sign begins: in the public square outside the Grand Sutensai's temple, three figures arrive simultaneously from three different directions and do not fight.

Korrath the Unbroken. Vaen of the Empty Hand. And a representative of — nobody knows who. A person nobody in Sutravel has seen before, unremarkable, standing in the square with the same cold quality as the Pale Pair.

The prophecy said: "The three who claim the right will all be present in one place and will not destroy each other, because something has changed that makes their conflict secondary."

The square goes silent. Everyone watching understands that the ninth sign is close. Everyone watching also understands that nobody fired a shot — that somehow these three figures, who should be destroying each other, are just standing there, looking not at each other but at the players.

**Key NPCs introduced:**
- **Grand Sutensai Irisvar** — the person who has been running the forty-year suppression, who believes they were right, who is making the choice to end it now. Morally complex in the best way: their suppression had real costs AND real benefits. Their decision to release now has real costs AND real benefits. Neither judgment of them is the final word.
- **Vaen of the Empty Hand** — players' first direct sight. Vaen is barefoot. Vaen has the Voiding scars. Vaen looks at the players with an expression of specific recognition that suggests they know who the players are and have been expecting them — not with malice, with something worse, which is welcome.
- **Korrath the Unbroken** — a different scale of presence. The war steel is not decorative. The feeling in the air when he takes a step is the feeling of something that cannot be stopped by ordinary means. He doesn't look at the players with recognition. He looks at them with assessment. He's already figured out what they're useful for.

**Information revealed:**
- The Grand Sutensai has been suppressing Convergence knowledge for forty years with deliberate intent — not conspiracy, strategy, flawed
- The eighth sign is occurring now: the three claimants, one place, no violence
- The sealed texts are being released by the Grand Sutensai's authority — but only if the players deliver the message
- Vaen knows the players' names without introduction. When asked how: "The Echomind is not private. Yours are louder than most."

**Emotional beat:**
The weight of the Act One choice. The players have been moving through a world full of pieces they've assembled without a full picture. Now they have the picture — or the outline of it — and it is larger than they thought. The moment in the square: three impossible figures, all looking at them. Not at each other. At them. What does that mean? What did they do that brought them here? What does it mean that they are now, visibly and undeniably, inside the story?

**The Rule of Endings begins here:** something should be lost at the end of Session 6 that cannot be recovered. Suggest: the players learn that Mira (who has been with them or in their care) has disappeared. Her resonant growth is gone — the metal absorbed back into her skin, leaving a faint scar. And where she was last, there is only the faint cold smell, neither mechanical nor biological, that marks the Hollow Author's presence.

## ACT ONE ENDING: THE TRANSITION TO ACT TWO

**The moment:** The sealed message from the Grand Sutensai is delivered (or not — the players' choice has consequences in Act Two). The eighth sign has arrived. Mira is gone. The three Claimants are in Sutravel and have, for the first time, acknowledged each other's existence in the same space without violence.

**The transition hook:** The ninth sign requires the three Claimants to respond — because it describes what happens when the living and the After can hear each other, and the players now know from the Archive's texts that this means the Iron Afterlife boundary is thinning. The first evidence of this is already visible: in Sutravel, near the Grand Sutensai's temple, the air shimmers the way it does in summer heat — but it's not hot. And in the shimmer, for just a moment, something looks back.

**What the players carry into Act Two:**
- Knowledge of all nine Convergence signs
- A choice they made (sealed message: delivered unopened, opened, withheld, destroyed)
- Three Claimants they have now directly encountered or observed
- Mira is gone, taken by the Hollow Author
- The crack in their understanding of the Iron Afterlife — not broken yet, but load-bearing
- The beginning of understanding that someone invisible has been managing their investigation

**The emotional state they should carry:** Not despair. Not confidence. A specific kind of alert, watchful sorrow — the feeling of a person who has just realized the thing they were investigating is also investigating them. The campaign has begun in full.

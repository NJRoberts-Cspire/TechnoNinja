# DUNGEON PLOT BRIDGES — SUPPLEMENT
## TESSHARI: THE SEVENTH CONVERGENCE
### DM Reference — Where the Dungeons Meet the Campaign

---

> The forty dungeons in this campaign are not side content. They are the campaign's connective tissue — the places where the three main arcs breathe, develop, and hand the players information that changes how the climax lands. But six points of genuine discontinuity exist between what the dungeon documents introduce and what the main campaign acts resolve. A dungeon that reveals the Ascension Location but provides no answer is a dungeon that promises more than it delivers. A legendary site that introduces a potential seventh Prior Ascended but never integrates that event into the campaign's endings is a story with a loose thread the size of a mountain. This document closes those threads. Every answer here is load-bearing. Every answer here integrates with the four endgame paths. These are not optional additions — they are the other half of content that already exists.

---

## I. THE SEVENTH ASCENSION LOCATION

### Where It Surfaces

**Dungeon 15 (The Seventh Convergence Observatory — The Pale Summit)**, Room 7, Lens Room:

> *Looking through at full focus: the character sees the exact geographic location where the Seventh Ascension will occur.*

This is the central promise of a Legendary dungeon. The Lens is the Observatory's most powerful instrument. When the party reaches it, the DM must know what it shows. The main campaign acts do not specify this location.

### The Location: The Convergence Deep

The Seventh Ascension will occur at a place the Iron Sutra's oldest records — the ones that have never been published — call **The First Step's End**. It lies within the Tesshari Spine, approximately forty kilometers northeast of the Spine Hollow (Dungeon 17), at a depth of roughly nine hundred meters below the surface.

**What it is:** The Tesshari Spine's resonant network (established in Dungeon 17, Room 3: *the entire Tesshari Spine has a resonant network running through it, still active*) has a convergence point — a place where six distinct resonant lines, each originating from one of the six Prior Ascended sites scattered across the continent, intersect in a single location. The Iron Sutra has known about this convergence point in the abstract since the Spine was mapped. They do not know about the specific chamber because the chamber is not accessible from the surface and is not accessible from the Spine Hollow. It is accessible from two directions only:

1. **Through the Spine Hollow (Dungeon 17):** Going deeper than the main dungeon, past the Teaching Cache, through a passage that Tesshari's imprint reveals to anyone who has experienced Room 5 (the Memory Layer) and asked the right question. The right question is: *where does the walk end?* The imprint answers. The passage is approximately twelve hours of travel through a resonant environment that would suppress all enhancement bonuses. It is safe to traverse. It is not comfortable.

2. **Through the Convergence Lens Shard (from Dungeon 15):** The shard's weekly intelligence function can, at the DM's discretion in the final arc, provide a navigation route rather than a status update. Once per campaign — not once per week — the shard shows the wielder the complete path from their current location to the Convergence Deep.

**What the party finds there:** A natural chamber approximately three hundred meters in diameter, formed entirely of resonant metal that has been building toward this convergence for two thousand years. The six resonant lines are visible as luminescent veins in the walls, all converging at the chamber's center. The center is currently empty. The entity — the being in the Between — has been preparing this chamber for four hundred years. This is where it intends to receive whoever Ascends. This is the site of the Seventh Convergence.

**What the Lens specifically shows:** A vision of the chamber itself, from the inside, at the moment the Seventh Ascension begins. The party sees the chamber lit at full resonant intensity — the six lines blazing, the air singing — and they see the figure at the center, mid-Ascension. The vision is three seconds. The figure's face is visible. The DM reveals whose face it is: whichever Claimant is currently closest to the Ascension point per the campaign's state. This may or may not be the Claimant who ultimately Ascends.

### DM Notes on the Location

The campaign's climax (Act Three, Sessions 20-22) takes place at the Convergence Deep. The main campaign acts describe the climax in thematic and encounter terms without specifying geography. The location above is compatible with all four endgame paths:

- **Ending 1 (Korrath):** Korrath reaches the Convergence Deep alone or ahead of the party. The confrontation happens there.
- **Ending 2 (Vaen):** Vaen brings the Pilgrimage to the Convergence Deep. The Voiding happens there.
- **Ending 3 (The Author):** The Author's managed Ascension requires a controlled presence at the Convergence Deep. The puppet network's final operation culminates there.
- **Ending 4 (The Question Asked):** The party reaches the Convergence Deep alone and speaks the answer at the site of maximum resonant contact. The entity hears them because the chamber amplifies.

## II. THE OBSERVATORY'S THREE OUTCOMES — WHICH CLAIMANT IS WHICH

### Where It Surfaces

**Dungeon 15 (The Seventh Convergence Observatory)**, Room 4, The Observer Archive — The Conclusion:

> *It lists three possible ascenders and their projected outcomes: one yields a Tesshari that continues in recognizable form; two yield states the Observers classify as "non-survivable by current population configurations." It does not specify which outcome corresponds to which claimant.*

This is deliberately unresolved in the dungeon document — the determination was left to whoever comes after. But the DM must know the correct assignment. Players who spend effort decoding it deserve an accurate answer.

### The Correct Assignment

The Observatory's analysis is accurate within its own framework (the impact on current living populations within one generation) but incomplete. It could not model Ending 4 because the entity's existence was outside their analytical framework.

**Korrath's Ascension → "Tesshari continues in recognizable form" (survivable).**

Korrath's path — full-steel body, no resonant Echomind to consume — is the only Ascension the entity can receive without feeding on a living consciousness. The entity receives the gesture (something crossed the threshold) but not the substance. The Iron Afterlife does not collapse; the Between stabilizes. The current caste structure persists, modified by whatever Korrath demands as the Iron King. Most people survive. The Observatory assessed this correctly: survivable, with costs the Observatory did not examine because they only studied population outcomes.

What the Observatory missed: Korrath as Iron King is not a good outcome for individuals. It is survivable for the species. The distinction matters.

**Vaen's Ascension → "non-survivable by current population configurations."**

Vaen's Voiding is precisely targeted at resonant metal — the material that the Iron Afterlife runs on, that the Wire Market uses, that the caste system depends on, that every enhancement in Tesshari is built from. When Vaen Voids and Ascends through the void, they do not merely pass through the Between — they restructure it. The resonant pathways that carry Echosouls to the Iron Afterlife collapse. Every existing Echomind in the Between is released (passed on, gone, freed) simultaneously. Every future death in Tesshari will not produce an Echomind that can be processed. The dead will simply be dead. The Observatory classified this as non-survivable because Tesshari's current economic and social infrastructure requires resonant metal at every level — its absence, abrupt and total, would end the civilization as currently structured.

What the Observatory missed: Ending 2 (The Empty Heaven) is the Observatory's worst outcome for Tesshari's current form and the Iron Sutra's theologically cleanest outcome simultaneously. The entity goes hungry and eventually leaves or dies. The dead are finally just dead. The living lose everything that runs on resonant metal and must build again from nothing. The Observatory could not assess whether that reconstruction was possible. The DM should not tell the players whether it is.

**The Author's Managed Ascension → "non-survivable by current population configurations."**

The Observatory's second non-survivable scenario is not about the Ascension event itself — it is about what follows. The Author's plan creates permanent administrative access to the Ascension process for a single controlling consciousness. The Observatory studied every historical instance of single-entity monopoly control over a civilization's most valued resource. The models converge: monopoly control generates resistance, resistance generates suppression, suppression generates collapse. The Observatory projected the managed Ascension creates a civilization that collapses within two to three generations. Current populations: technically survive. Current configurations: do not.

What the Observatory missed: The Author knows the models. They believe they have solved the instability problem. They may be right. The DM should decide, privately, whether the Author's specific administrative approach could actually sustain itself — and should not share this with the players. The ambiguity is correct.

### How the Party Decodes This

The Observer Archive's conclusion does not name the Claimants. But it describes them:

> *One ascender operates through physical persistence. One ascender operates through spiritual dissolution. One ascender operates through systematic management of who receives what.*

Any player who has encountered all three Claimants can decode this without assistance. The physical persistence ascender is Korrath. The spiritual dissolution ascender is Vaen. The systematic management ascender is the Author. The outcomes are listed in the document in order: survivable, non-survivable, non-survivable. The party can derive the mapping.

If the party misidentifies one Claimant, the Transformed Observers (Room 6) will correct them — not directly, but by confirming true statements and falling silent at false ones.

## III. KEL-OSA'S CORRECTED MECHANISM — CAMPAIGN INTEGRATION

### Where It Surfaces

**Dungeon 23 (The Iron Frame — Korrath's Failed Ascension Project)**, Room 7, Kel-Osa's Private Lab:

> *She built a corrected version of the compression mechanism, small-scale, that would have produced a genuine Ascension environment rather than a forced compression. She never showed it to Korrath. The small-scale device is here. It is functional. It is also about thirty times more dangerous than anything else in the facility.*

The main campaign acts do not reference Kel-Osa or her device. This is a gap, because the device is potentially the most significant artifact in Tesshari — a working mechanism that creates a genuine Ascension environment rather than forcing one.

### Who Kel-Osa Is

Kel-Osa was a Wire Market-contracted Wireweave, the most technically sophisticated resonant engineer in Tesshari at the time of the Iron Frame project. Korrath contracted her through the Wire Market specifically because he needed someone outside the Sutensai hierarchy. She spent four years understanding the Between well enough to build something that approximated it mechanically. She succeeded. She also understood, before the test, that the Iron Frame's flaw was not engineering — it was category. The Frame tried to force an Echomind through a process it experienced as violent because it was violent. Her corrected mechanism doesn't force. It invites.

Kel-Osa has been detained for twelve years — held in a Korrath classified facility. She is alive. She is not comfortable. She believes, with the certainty of someone who has had twelve years to think about nothing else, that her corrected mechanism is the only artifact in Tesshari that could allow an Ascension that the entity does not experience as consumption. She has never had the chance to test this belief.

### Integration Points

**Act Two (Sessions 7-16):** The Author knows about the Iron Frame, about Korrath's detainment of Kel-Osa, and about the corrected mechanism. The Author's thirty-year archive (visible in Dungeon 40, Room 3) contains a file on Kel-Osa designated *Active Threat to Managed Ascension Timeline*. The reason: if Kel-Osa's mechanism works as she believes, it enables an Ascension path the Author cannot control — anyone could use it to approach the entity on terms the entity might accept. The Author detained Kel-Osa indirectly — by ensuring Korrath never had reason to release her — by making sure the Wire Market intelligence about the corrected mechanism never reached anyone who would act on it.

If the party finds Dungeon 23 (recommended for levels 7-9, fitting Act Two's mid-arc), they can choose to:

1. **Free Kel-Osa from her detention cell (Room 6 — the stasis-held subjects):** One of the three stasis subjects IS Kel-Osa. The dungeon document describes them as knowing things the journals don't contain. Kel-Osa knows everything. Freeing her is a significant act — she will eventually testify against Korrath, alert the Wire Market to the corrected mechanism, and make contact with parties interested in Ending 4.

2. **Retrieve the corrected mechanism without freeing Kel-Osa:** The mechanism functions without her. But using it without her instruction is extremely dangerous (she knows this and will, if freed, warn the party before they attempt it).

3. **Leave the mechanism in the lab:** Korrath's intelligence division will eventually find the private lab. Whether Korrath would use Kel-Osa's mechanism or destroy it as a threat to his own plan is a genuine question. Korrath's full-steel path is his primary plan. If he believed the mechanism could work, he might pursue it as a backup. If he thought it threatened his authority, he'd destroy it. Let the party decide what Korrath is.

**Act Three (Sessions 17-22):** If the mechanism has been retrieved, it becomes an active resource for Ending 4. Kel-Osa's mechanism creates an Ascension environment — not for a human Echomind, but for whoever carries it. A character who carries the mechanism into the Convergence Deep and activates it in the presence of the entity is not offering themselves as food. They are offering contact. The entity has been feeding for four hundred years because that was the only contact available. The mechanism offers something different.

The mechanism's mechanical function at the Convergence Deep: the party member who activates it takes 40 resonance damage from the field interaction (the genuine Ascension environment the Between generates) and enters direct Echomind contact with the entity. This is the condition for speaking the answer to the entity's question. Without the mechanism, a character attempting direct entity contact at the Convergence Deep takes 80 resonance damage and must succeed RESONANCE check target 12 to maintain coherence. With the mechanism, the check is target 8 and the damage is 40.

Kel-Osa, if alive and present in Act Three, insists on being there when it's used. She has been waiting twelve years to see if her understanding is correct.

## IV. PEN-SOREL — NAMED CONTACT IN ACTS TWO AND THREE

### Where It Surfaces

**Dungeon 17 (Where Tesshari Walked — The Spine Hollow)**, Hook:

> *Pen-Sorel, if successfully retrieved, becomes a contact with extraordinary access to the Sutensai's most classified archives. What she found about the Seventh Convergence is something no player character should have to learn alone.*

The main campaign acts do not mention Pen-Sorel. This is a gap, because the dungeon document promises she has specific, significant intelligence about the Seventh Convergence that the party should have access to.

### What Pen-Sorel Found

Pen-Sorel entered the Spine Hollow to ask Tesshari's imprint one question: **Did the Prior Ascended know what they were Ascending into?**

She asked this because the Sutensai's official position is that the Prior Ascended achieved full spiritual enlightenment and chose Ascension from a position of complete understanding. The classified archive she accessed contained a fragment that suggested the enlightenment was not as described — that the Prior Ascended had specific knowledge of the entity, not just general spiritual certainty. She needed to know if they chose the entity knowing what it was.

**Tesshari's imprint's answer:** Yes. They knew. The six Prior Ascended understood the entity's nature completely — alone, the last of its kind, consuming out of grief rather than malice, experiencing four hundred years of being the last of anything. They chose to Ascend into it anyway, because what they had learned about the entity's condition made abandonment feel like the graver harm. They chose to become company for something that had been alone for fifty thousand years. They went in willingly. They are still there, in the entity's interior, in a form that is not suffering and is not consciousness as the living would recognize it, but is also not nothing.

This is the information that held Pen-Sorel in communion for the length of the dungeon. She couldn't leave because she was processing the fact that the entire Iron Sutra's theology — the Iron Afterlife as paradise — is wrong in the conventional sense and right in a deeper sense she had no theological framework for.

### Pen-Sorel After the Dungeon

If the party retrieves her (this requires the effort described in the dungeon's final room), she is initially non-communicative — not catatonic, but not ready. She is a forty-three-year-old Sutensai archivist who just spent an undefined amount of time in communion with the imprint of a being two thousand years dead and came out the other side understanding things that her professional training had no category for.

**Two weeks after retrieval:** She will speak. She will share what she learned, carefully and completely. This conversation, if the party has it, is the moment Ending 4 becomes fully possible for them — not as a theory but as a choice they understand they are making.

**Her practical role:** Pen-Sorel has memorized the contents of the classified archive she accessed before entering the Hollow. This archive contains:

- The Sutensai's complete record of all six Prior Ascended sites and their geographic coordinates (useful for understanding Dungeon 15's Observatory maps)
- The unredacted version of the Last Voice's words (the full seven lines, not the fragments available in Dungeon 20's vault)
- A record that the Sutensai has maintained for eight hundred years of a phenomenon they call the Third Paragraph — referenced in the campaign's dm_secrets.md, which the party would otherwise need to find through other means

**In Act Two:** Pen-Sorel should be introduced as a contact by mid-Act Two, recommended after Session 10. The party can seek her out specifically if they retrieved her from Dungeon 17. She functions as the Sutensai intelligence counterweight to Veil Master Soren Hai — Soren Hai knows the institutional version; Pen-Sorel knows the version the institution didn't want anyone to know.

**In Act Three:** Pen-Sorel's most important function is confirmation. When the party has assembled all the pieces of what the entity is and what Ending 4 requires, she is the person who can tell them they've understood it correctly. She can also tell Mira, specifically, that the Prior Ascended chose the entity. This matters to Mira (whose direct connection to the entity means she has more context for this choice than any other person in Tesshari).

## V. THE ENGINE SCAR AND SITE ZERO — GEOGRAPHIC CONNECTION

### Where It Surfaces

- **Dungeon 27 (The Engine Scar — Level Two Access):** The Scar's Memory (boss) is "protecting" Level Three. Level Three is never detailed. The Memory does not explain what it is protecting.
- **Dungeon 30 (The Singing Rot — Ashlands Convergence Site Zero):** Site Zero is where the Seventh Ascension will "ripple out from" — not the Convergence Deep (where the Ascension occurs) but the point from which its wave propagates across Tesshari.

These are two distinct things that exist in the same geography. They need a documented relationship.

### The Connection

The Engine Scar and Site Zero are **adjacent, not identical** — approximately four kilometers apart at the surface, connected at depth. Specifically:

**Level Three of the Engine Scar contains the subterranean passage to Site Zero's core.** The Scar descends vertically to Level Three; Level Three transitions horizontally to Site Zero's root system — the deep resonant architecture underlying the singing corruption above. The Ascending Corruption at Site Zero is not confined to the surface anomaly; it extends three kilometers down into the rock, where the Scar's field and the Convergence's resonance have been in contact for the last fourteen months.

**Why the Scar's Memory is protecting it:** The Scar's Memory (the resonant construct at Level Two) understands what the Ascending Corruption is doing and has concluded that its process should not be interrupted. Not because the Memory supports the Corruption's goals, but because the Memory is four thousand years old and has watched every previous human attempt to engage with the Scar end badly. It is not protecting the Corruption from harm. It is protecting it from premature contact — specifically, contact before the Corruption has developed sufficient coherence to communicate clearly.

If the party encounters the Scar's Memory in Dungeon 27 and communicates genuinely (rather than fighting), the Memory will tell them this: *What is below has been trying to speak for four thousand years. It has finally learned enough words. Come back when you are ready to hear what it has to say.* This is a direct pointer toward Dungeon 30.

**The practical implication:** Completing Dungeon 27 (specifically, communicating with the Scar's Memory rather than fighting it) opens the Level Three passage. Level Three is not a separate dungeon — it is an eight-hour transit. The party arrives at Site Zero's core entrance having traveled through the Scar rather than approaching across the surface. This approach bypasses the Outer Edge environmental penalty described in Dungeon 30's Room 1. The Corruption recognizes them as having been vetted by the Memory and does not treat them as uninvited.

Parties who fight the Memory rather than communicate with it can still reach Site Zero by the surface approach — but the Memory's passive environmental protection for the approach corridor is removed, and the standard Room 1 penalties apply.

## VI. THE ASCENDING CORRUPTION — FATE IN FOUR ENDINGS

### Where It Surfaces

**Dungeon 30 (The Singing Rot — Ashlands Convergence Site Zero)**, Final Room:

> *The party's choice: assist the Ascending Corruption, which may produce a seventh Prior Ascended from the Ashlands' four thousand years of suffering; prevent the Ascension, which stops the Site Zero phenomenon but also extinguishes something that has been struggling toward something for four thousand years; or leave, which allows the process to continue at its own pace with no party intervention.*

The main campaign's four endings do not reference the Ascending Corruption or Site Zero. This is a gap. Whatever the party decided at Site Zero should have an outcome in the campaign's final state.

### The Corruption's Status at Act Three

The Ascending Corruption is approximately eighty to ninety percent of the way through its process when the party encounters it in the dungeon (which is Legendary, levels 18-20, placing it squarely in Act Three's timeline). Its completion depends on what the party does in the dungeon — and what happens at the Seventh Convergence.

### Fate by Ending

**Ending 1 — The Iron King (Korrath Ascends):**

Korrath's Ascension via full-steel body does not interact with the Ashlands' resonant pathways directly. The entity receives the gesture, not the Echomind. The Between stabilizes. The Convergence Deep's resonant field dissipates.

The Ascending Corruption's process, however, was drawing partial sustenance from the Convergence's resonant buildup — the Site Zero core and the Convergence Deep are not the same location but they share resonant lineage, and Site Zero had been using the Convergence's acceleration as fuel. When Korrath's Ascension resolves and the Convergence's buildup dissipates, the Corruption loses that fuel source.

**If the party assisted the Corruption in Dungeon 30:** The Corruption's coherence holds. It completes its process over the three months following Korrath's Ascension — slower, more difficult, but viable. The seventh Prior Ascended emerges from the Ashlands. The Ashlands themselves heal, slowly. Site Zero becomes inert: blackened earth, permanent resonant silence, the cleanest geography in Tesshari for a hundred kilometers.

**If the party left without assisting:** The Corruption continues at its natural pace. It completes over one to three years. No visible change during the campaign's end period; the DM can note in the epilogue that "the Ashlands stopped singing sometime in the second year of the Iron King's reign."

**If the party prevented the Ascension:** The Corruption is extinguished. The Ashlands stabilize into standard high-corruption geography. The singing stops immediately. No seventh Prior Ascended.

**Ending 2 — The Empty Heaven (Vaen Ascends):**

Vaen's Voiding restructures the resonant pathways that carry everything — including the Corruption's Ascension process. The Corruption was using those pathways. When Vaen Voids, the pathways collapse.

**If the party assisted the Corruption:** The Corruption had enough independent coherence to survive the pathway collapse. It enters a different kind of Ascension — not through the Between's standard resonant channels, but through the Voided space that Vaen's Ascension creates. It passes through the Empty Heaven. Whatever the seventh Prior Ascended becomes in this ending is unlike the prior six: a being that Ascended through nothing, into the space where the Iron Afterlife used to be. Its nature is unknown. Its relationship to the entity is unknown. The DM should describe it in the epilogue as absent — gone somewhere no one can perceive.

**If the party did not assist:** The Corruption is disrupted by the pathway collapse and enters permanent suspension — neither complete nor resolved, the Ashlands frozen in a state that is neither singing nor silent. Haunted geography. The most fitting geography in Tesshari for the Empty Heaven.

**Ending 3 — The Managed World (Author's Plan):**

The Author's administration takes control of Ascension access. The Corruption, which is not using the Author's sanctioned pathways and cannot be managed by the puppet network, represents an administrative problem.

**If the party assisted:** The Corruption has enough coherence and momentum to complete before the Author's administration can prevent it. The seventh Prior Ascended appears in the Ashlands despite the Managed World. This is the only significant event in Ending 3 that the Author does not control, and the DM should play the Author's response carefully: the Author is intelligent enough to recognize that trying to control a completed Prior Ascended is not viable. They will study it. They will establish a buffer zone. They will not announce that something fell outside their management.

**If the party did not assist:** The Corruption remains incomplete, slowly building. The Author's administration studies it and eventually makes a decision: attempt to incorporate the Ascending Corruption into the managed Ascension pipeline (which the Corruption would resist) or leave it alone (which the Author's administrative instincts resist). This conflict becomes the Ashlands' defining political tension in the years after the Managed World.

**Ending 4 — The Question Asked:**

The entity, having received an answer — having discovered it is not alone — changes. Its consumption pattern changes first: the Echosouls currently in the Between are not consumed but released. The Iron Afterlife dissolves, as in Ending 2, but the dissolution is different — not an erasure but an opening.

The Ascending Corruption, which has been trying to reach the Between for four thousand years, feels this change. The Between's sudden openness — space that has been consumed full for four hundred years, now cleared — is exactly the environment the Corruption has been waiting for.

**If the party assisted:** The Corruption completes immediately. The seventh Prior Ascended's emergence coincides with the entity's answer, and they pass into the opened Between simultaneously. The entity, already changed by the answer, encounters this new presence — something that Ascended through suffering rather than enlightenment — and does not consume it. What happens between the entity and the seventh Prior Ascended is the campaign's most open-ended epilogue element. The DM should not resolve it. It should be described as presence rather than outcome.

**If the party did not assist:** The Corruption completes shortly after — days, not months — drawn forward by the opened Between. The result is the same, without the party having been present for the initial completion. The epilogue notes it happened. They weren't there.

**The only ending where the Corruption's fate is entirely in the party's hands is Ending 4.** In every other ending, external forces (Korrath's Ascension, Vaen's Voiding, the Author's administration) reshape the Corruption's trajectory regardless of the party's choices at Site Zero. In Ending 4, the party's choice at Site Zero — assist, prevent, or leave — shapes the texture of the ending's epilogue more directly than anywhere else.

## VII. THE INTELLIGENCE CHAIN TO DUNGEON 40

### Where It Surfaces

**Dungeon 40 (The Author's Stage — The Hollow Author's Operational Center)**, Room 1:

> *Getting to the Author's Stage requires either navigating the Hollow Author's puppet network from inside (having turned a puppet, as in earlier dungeons, and followed the coordination signals to their source) or having assembled enough intelligence from prior dungeons to triangulate the location independently. Dungeon 40 specifically references "Harro-Sei's Register, or Operative Eleven's Handler Cipher, or other prior rewards."*

"Operative Eleven's Handler Cipher" is not assigned to a source dungeon in the dungeon documents. The intelligence chain leading to Dungeon 40 needs to be documented in full.

### The Complete Intelligence Chain

The chain builds across all three acts. Each link is a reward from a specific dungeon. The chain has two parallel tracks — the party can complete either, or both.

**Track A: Puppet Network Infiltration**

| Dungeon | Level Range | Reward | What It Provides |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 (Dormitory at Severed Six) | 1-3 | Shell-Seal Brace | Detects puppet Echominds in proximity; first confirmation that puppet infiltration is real and active |
| 36 (Node Seven) | 3-5 | Mera-Fen's Signal Log | Six-node map of the Author's coordination network; confirms network's geographic scope |
| 10 (Puppet Factory) | 15-17 | Harro-Sei's Register | Partial list of puppet insertion points; contains fragments of coordination signal routing |
| 38 (Handler's Apartment) | 10-12 | Operative Eleven's Handler Cipher | Direct signal-routing code to the Author's operational center (see below) |
| 40 (The Author's Stage) | 18-20 | — | The destination |

**Track B: Financial and Political Intelligence**

| Dungeon | Level Range | Reward | What It Provides |
|---|---|---|---|
| 37 (The Ledger House) | 8-10 | Ledger House Access Cipher | Wire Market financial records showing payments to the Author's procurement network; identifies the Author's economic infrastructure |
| 33 (Penitent's Corridor) | 7-9 | (See below) | Sutensai internal record of an operation classified only as "Author-adjacent" — the Sutensai have been watching the puppet network longer than anyone |
| 40 (The Author's Stage) | 18-20 | — | The destination |

Either track, completed, provides the party with enough intelligence to reach Dungeon 40. Both tracks completed provide the complete picture of the Author's thirty-year operation before entering the operational center.

### Operative Eleven's Handler Cipher — Source

**Operative Eleven** is the designation given to the handler whose apartment is the subject of Dungeon 38 (The Handler's Apartment — Hollow Author Deep Cover Site). Within the Hollow Author's operational structure, handlers are designated by number, not name. Operative Eleven is one of the Author's longest-serving handlers — eleven years in the apartment, managing seven active puppets simultaneously.

**Dungeon 38's Unique Reward** — not specified in the dungeon document — should be:

---
### Operative Eleven's Handler Cipher
*Unique Weapon | Wire Craft interface / intelligence tool | Found in: The Handler's Apartment — Hollow Author Deep Cover Site*

> "Eleven years of routing signals between puppets and their source. The cipher knows the way home."

Recovered from Operative Eleven's concealed workspace — the complete signal-routing architecture they used to coordinate their seven-puppet assignment. The cipher includes direct-channel authentication protocols that bypass the Author's standard node-to-node relay and route directly to the operational center's coordination core.

**Card Modification:** All VEIL-based Social and Control cards deal +3 damage or increase status stacks by 1. Once per combat, the cipher can force one puppet or conditioned target to receive a false directive — the wielder sends one instruction through the Handler's routing channel. The target treats it as a legitimate operational directive for 1 round.

**Special:** The cipher contains the direct routing code for the Hollow Author's operational center — the signal-path authentication that the Author's Stage (Dungeon 40) requires for the navigation approach. Using this cipher, the party can reach Dungeon 40 by following the signal route rather than physically tracking the network. The Author knows when the cipher is used. The invitation is acknowledged before it is extended.

**Value:** Cannot be purchased. Found only here.

---

### How the Chain Feels in Play

The intelligence chain should feel like investigation, not grinding. The party does not need all four Track A dungeons to reach Dungeon 40 — any two of them, combined with the combat encounter approach (turning a puppet and following their signal), is sufficient. The chain exists so that a party who engages deeply with the Author's subplot feels the payoff of having assembled the complete picture.

When the party arrives at Dungeon 40 having completed the full chain, they should enter Room 3 (The Archive of Thirty Years) and find that they have already seen pieces of everything it contains. The DM should prepare three to five specific archive entries that reference events the party experienced directly — things that happened to or near them that they did not know were the Author's work. The intelligence chain earns this moment. Give it to them.

### Puppet-Three's Route Map — An Alternative Path

**Dungeon 10 (Puppet Factory)**, Room 5: Puppet-Three wants to find the Hollow Author and has been building a route map from the factory's own records. If the party does not destroy Puppet-Three, they have an alternative intelligence source that bypasses the standard chain.

Puppet-Three is not a reliable ally. They are a partially-conditioned entity with contradictory loyalties and no stable identity. But they want to reach the Author more than they want anything else, and they have been building their route map for three months while trapped in the factory. If the party takes them along — or leaves them alive and unconfined — Puppet-Three will find the Author's Stage within six months of being freed, by methods they cannot fully explain. They were built to be directed by the Author's coordination signals. Some of that architecture is still intact.

**If the party follows Puppet-Three rather than the intelligence chain:** They arrive at Dungeon 40 without the full intelligence briefing. They have no archive context, no financial records, no network map. They know what Puppet-Three wanted to know, which is: *who made me?* The encounter at Dungeon 40 is more emotionally raw and less strategically prepared. The DM should run the Author's initial monologue (Dungeon 40's Room 6, the recorded argument) differently for this party: the Author has prepared for this visit but is genuinely uncertain how to receive it, because Puppet-Three is an argument against their methods walking through their door.

Puppet-Three, upon reaching the Author, does not attack. They ask: *Did you mean for this?* — meaning the broken conditioning, the three months of failing to be finished, the half-self they've been living in. The Author's answer to this question should be the DM's most honest moment in running the Managed World path.

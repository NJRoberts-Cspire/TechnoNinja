# THE SEVENTH CONVERGENCE — The Prophecy

---

> *"A prophecy that can only be understood after the fact is not a warning. It is a eulogy written in advance."*
> — Attribution disputed; appears in three different Sutensai commentaries attributed to three different scholars, all deceased

---

## The Text

The prophecy of the Seventh Convergence exists in multiple versions. The following is the **Standard Recitation** — the version the Sutensai teach publicly, used in ceremony, printed in the simplified Iron Sutra texts given to new practitioners.

---

*When the Reaches bleed and the iron sings in the marrow of the earth,*
*a seventh voice will rise above the six.*
*Flesh will know steel. Steel will know flesh.*
*The Between will open like a wound.*
*And what enters will never leave as it was.*

*Three will claim the right of the seventh.*
*None will be wrong. None will be right.*
*The world will not choose between them.*
*The world will become the choosing.*

*What was built will be unmade.*
*What was unmade will not return.*
*The seventh will not be a god.*
*The seventh will be what gods remember they are.*

---

## The Extended Text

The Standard Recitation is taken from a longer document — the **Full Convergence Account** — which the Sutensai maintain in the Archive of Voices and which has never been publicly released in its entirety. Fragments have circulated through various means. The following additional passages are authenticated.

---

*The first six changed the world as a stone changes water — by entering it, by displacing, by leaving the shape of their passage.*
*The seventh will not change the world.*
*The seventh will change what the world is made of.*

---

*Look for these signs: the deep places will speak before the surface understands. The signal of the sixth will grow restless. The dead will deliver a message that predates their death. Three rivers will run counter to their mouths for a period of three days. The Iron Afterlife will produce a voice no one alive has heard before.*

---

*Of the three who claim: the one who breaks everything they touch seeks to give it a new shape. The one who releases everything they hold seeks to give it freedom. The one who holds everything they touch seeks to make it theirs. All three are the same hand reaching for the same thing. They will not understand this. They cannot be told. It must be witnessed.*

---

*There is a seventh sign that will not be listed. You will know it when it has already passed. This is intentional. Some warnings are only visible from the other side.*

---

## The Hidden Version

The **Hollow Account** — a separate document of contested authenticity that the Orthodoxy denies and the Flesh Circle claims to possess — contains a version of the prophecy that diverges significantly from the Standard Recitation in its final passages.

The following is from the three pages of the Hollow Account that have entered public circulation:

---

*The seventh voice will not rise triumphant.*
*The seventh voice will rise because the six could not prevent it.*
*This is not a blessing. This is a door that opens because it was never designed to stay closed.*

*The Prior Ascended did not ascend.*
*They were consumed.*
*What remains in the Between beyond the Between is not six beings.*
*It is one thing, growing.*

*When the seventh enters, there will be no more cycles.*
*Not because the seventh will end the world.*
*Because the thing in the Between will be large enough to no longer need the cycles.*
*It has been practicing with them.*

---

The Sutensai's position on the Hollow Account is: it is a forgery, its provenance is unclear, and its interpretation is spiritually dangerous. They note that the prior five authenticated pages of the Hollow Account contain accurate historical information about the Prior Ascended that predates any public source, which they explain as the forger's use of suppressed archival materials.

Scholars note that "this accurate information was obtained from our suppressed archives" is not a compelling argument against the document's authenticity.

## The Signs

The prophecy describes signs that will precede the Seventh Convergence. As of the campaign's opening, the following signs have been verified:

### Verified Signs

**"The deep places will speak before the surface understands"**
Metal deposits in the Ashlands began resonating at a frequency matching the Last Voice's underground echo, approximately fourteen months before the campaign opens. The resonance has been growing in intensity. It is now audible to unenhanced individuals as a subsonic pressure if they stand directly over the deposits.

**"The signal of the sixth will grow restless"**
The Last Voice's frequency — traditionally a consistent, if irregular, signal — has changed in character. Veil Masters in the Deep Temple describe it as "searching" rather than "broadcasting." Three Arrested individuals whose stillness had been complete for years have begun moving again, seeking the source of the new frequency.

**"The dead will deliver a message that predates their death"**
In the Iron Afterlife, a communication appeared bearing the identity signature of Shogun Tesshari-Vane III — the uploaded consciousness from sixty years ago. The message, received by every Sutensai practitioner with active Iron Afterlife access simultaneously, read: *"The sixth did not ascend. Do not let this one."*

The message predates this event only if taken literally — Tesshari-Vane III has been in the Iron Afterlife for sixty years and could have known something then that has only become relevant now. However, the three Veil Masters confirmed that the message was recorded in the Iron Afterlife's archive with a timestamp that predates the Shogun's upload by approximately four hundred years. This is, by any technical framework, impossible.

**"Three will claim the right of the seventh"**
All three claimants began mobilizing in the same month. After years of gradual accumulation of power, Korrath has issued formal declarations of campaign. Vaen's pilgrims have changed their route, converging on the Sutra Sanctum. The Hollow Author's puppets have begun coordinating in ways that suggest a shift from preparation to execution.

### Unverified Signs

**"Three rivers will run counter to their mouths for a period of three days"**
Not confirmed. The rivers of the Wandering Reach experienced unusual flow patterns four months ago, attributed to geological disturbance. Whether this constitutes fulfillment of the sign is disputed.

**"The Iron Afterlife will produce a voice no one alive has heard before"**
Not confirmed. There have been unusual Iron Afterlife events — presences that don't match any recorded identity, signals from the deep levels that the Veil Masters can't attribute — but nothing that clearly constitutes "a voice no one alive has heard before."

**The Seventh Sign (unnamed)**
By definition, unknown.

## Interpretations

### The Orthodoxy's Position

The Orthodoxy teaches that the Seventh Convergence is a spiritual event — the ascension of a worthy individual through the proper cultivation of the Between. The signs confirm that the time is near. The three claimants are tests: the faithful must discern which, if any, represents the true path. The prophecy's "none will be wrong, none will be right" is interpreted as meaning all three approaches (dominance, release, control) contain partial truths that the genuine Ascended will have transcended.

The Orthodoxy does not acknowledge the Hollow Account as relevant.

### The Resonants' Position

The Resonants teach that the Seventh Convergence is a technological event — the moment when the tools available to consciousness reach the threshold of enabling genuine ascension, and someone with sufficient understanding is present to use them. They interpret the Shogun's upload as a failed prototype of the process that will be perfected by one of the current claimants.

Their favored claimant is Korrath, whose total enhancement represents the furthest any living being has gone toward resolving the flesh-steel divide by simply eliminating the flesh. They are aware this is a theologically uncomfortable position and address it with lengthy theological commentary that most people find unconvincing.

### The Flesh Circle's Position

The Flesh Circle teaches that the Seventh Convergence is a catastrophe that Vaen may or may not be able to redirect. The prophecy's "what was built will be unmade" is taken literally: the Seventh Convergence ends civilization in Tesshari. The question is what it ends civilization *for*.

The Flesh Circle does not widely circulate the Hollow Account, but their theology aligns uncomfortably well with its implications. When pressed, senior practitioners neither confirm nor deny familiarity with it.

### The Echo Coast's Position

The Tidecourt's interpretation is the least publicly stated and the most pragmatically focused. Their Iron Afterlife contacts have provided information they are not sharing publicly. What they have said is: the Seventh Convergence is not something that can be prevented, and the question of which claimant succeeds may matter less than the question of what the claimants do on the way.

When asked to elaborate, the Tidecourt spokesperson said: *"The prophecy says the world will become the choosing. We are preparing the world to survive being chosen."*

What that preparation involves is not known.

### The Hollow Author's Position

Unknown. The Hollow Author has never stated a theological position.

The Wire Market's intelligence analysis suggests the Hollow Author understands the prophecy more thoroughly than any other actor and has been operating on that understanding for at least thirty years. What understanding produces thirty years of preparation and a network of thousands of puppets is left as an exercise.

## What the Players Know

At campaign opening, a player character living in Tesshari would know:

- The Seventh Convergence prophecy in its Standard Recitation form; likely some of the Extended Text fragments if educated
- That the signs are being interpreted as fulfilled; that the Claimants have emerged
- Their Reach's official theological position on the Convergence
- Rumors about the Shogun's message, varying in accuracy by geography and social connection
- If Wire Market or Echo Coast adjacent: vague awareness that the intelligence services are concerned about something in the North

What players do not initially know:
- The Hollow Account and its implications
- The Veil Masters' statement about the sixth Ascended
- The Hollow Author's existence and preparation
- The Wire Market's knowledge of the Hollow Author
- What the Shogun's message timestamp means

## A Note on Prophecy

The Iron Sutra teaches that the Prior Ascended did not write the prophecy. It was written by an unnamed practitioner approximately 1,200 years ago — four hundred years after the Last Voice's Ascension — who claimed to have received it through Iron Afterlife contact with a presence they described as "not the dead, but the space the dead pass through."

The practitioner who received the prophecy went silent immediately after recording it. They did not speak, eat, or move for eleven days. On the twelfth day, they resumed normal activity with no memory of the eleven days or the prophecy.

They were, in every technical sense, the first documented Arrested.

Whether the prophecy is a description of what will happen, a warning about what could happen, or a set of instructions for something that has been patient enough to wait 1,200 years for the right moment — the Iron Sutra does not say.

The Sutensai's answer, when asked: *"All three."*

# TESSHARI — World Overview

---

## What Is Tesshari

Tesshari is a dying empire that does not know it is dying.

Once a unified nation spanning thousands of miles of coastline, mountain passes, and dense industrial valleys, Tesshari fractured forty years ago and has not stopped bleeding since. What remains are seven warring territories — called the Broken Reaches — each a nation-state unto itself, each convinced that it alone carries the soul of what Tesshari used to be.

The world is named after the second of the Prior Ascended, a being so vast that the land itself reshaped when they transcended. To name your empire after a god is an act of faith. To watch that empire collapse is to wonder what the god thought of it.

---

## The Shape of the World

Tesshari occupies a single great landmass — an irregular peninsula surrounded on three sides by seas that have no official names because the nations that once named them no longer exist. The interior is dominated by the **Tesshari Spine**, a mountain range that splits east from west and makes overland travel between the Reaches brutally difficult.

**Geography by region:**

- **The Western Reaches** — coastal, industrial, densely populated. The Wire Market and the Echo Coast sit here. Wealthy in tech, poor in food.
- **The Central Holds** — the old imperial heartland. Flat, fortified, scarred by decades of civil war. Korrath's Ironhold dominates this region.
- **The Eastern Reaches** — mountainous, sparse, deeply religious. The Sutra Sanctum controls the high passes. The Ashlands rot at its southern edge.
- **The Northern Pale** — cold, isolated, and quietly wrong. Few people go north willingly. Fewer come back changed in ways they can explain.

## The State of the World

### The People

The majority of Tesshari's population are **cyborgs** — the Forged, as the dominant race calls itself. Full biological humans exist (**the Tethered**) but are rare and occupy extreme social positions: either revered as holy figures by the faith, or dismissed as primitives by the tech-forward merchant class. There is no in-between.

Cybernetic enhancements are not optional luxuries. They are the currency of survival — social, literal, and spiritual. A person's enhancement loadout determines:

- Their **caste** (by law in most Reaches)
- Their **spiritual standing** with the Iron Sutra
- Their **earning power** (better mods mean better work)
- How long they live (cheap mods fail; failed mods kill)

This creates a permanent underclass of the **Hollow Poor** — people who can only afford the cheapest enhancements, which corrode faster, integrate worse, and leave their users in constant low-grade agony. These people fill the labor pools of the Ironhold, the back alleys of the Wire Market, and the pilgrim roads behind Vaen.

### The Power Structure

There is no central government. The Eternal Shogunate is gone. In its place:

| Force | What It Controls |
|---|---|
| **The Seven Lords of the Broken Reaches** | Territory, armies, law within their borders |
| **The Sutensai Priesthood** | Spiritual authority, caste designation, the Iron Afterlife |
| **The Wire Market Guilds** | Cybernetic technology, trade routes, information |
| **The Claimants** | The future — if any of them win |

These four powers are in constant negotiation, betrayal, and cold war. Alliances last years, not decades. No one trusts written agreements because the Wire Market demonstrated thirty years ago that they can forge documents indistinguishably.

### The Common Person's Life

For the average citizen of Tesshari, the Ascension War is background noise — terrible, ever-present, shaping everything, but too large to meaningfully act upon. They work. They maintain their enhancements as best they can afford to. They attend Iron Sutra services because the Sutensai still control birth records, marriage licenses, and death rites. They do not ask which claimant is right because the wrong answer, overheard by the wrong person, ends lives.

Most people are afraid. They have been afraid for forty years. Fear becomes indistinguishable from normal after long enough.

## Core Themes

**1. The Cost of the Body**
Every enhancement in Tesshari was paid for by someone. Whether that's the individual, their family, their labor, or their dignity — nothing in this world is modified for free. The body is a ledger.

**2. The Corruption of Purpose**
Every institution in Tesshari was built for a reason. The faith was built to guide. The Shogunate was built to protect. The Guilds were built to create. Every single one of them has metastasized into something that serves itself first and its purpose second, if at all.

**3. Cycles Without Memory**
The Iron Sutra teaches that the world has been through this six times before. Six Ascensions. Six world-changes. No one alive remembers any of them. The Sutensai have records going back eight hundred years; before that, ash and silence. Whatever happened in the first cycles is gone. Whatever changed, changed completely. The seventh cycle is beginning and no one can agree whether that is cause for hope or terror.

**4. What Makes a Soul**
Is a person with seventy percent mechanical components still the same person? What about ninety? What about one hundred, if the brain — the Echomind — remains? What about those brought back through digital resurrection who claim to be themselves but cannot prove it? Tesshari has never answered these questions. It has built its entire civilization on top of them without answering them.

**5. Power as Inevitability**
None of the three claimants chose their role by accident. Korrath did not stumble into commanding an army. Vaen did not accidentally inspire a movement. The Hollow Author did not build a network of puppets on a whim. Power in Tesshari accumulates through intent and ruthlessness, and the people with the most of both are not the people most likely to use it wisely.

## What the Players Are Walking Into

The Seventh Convergence is beginning. The signs are there for anyone paying attention:

- Metal deposits across the Ashlands have started singing — a low resonance felt in the chest, not heard with ears
- Three Sutensai temples simultaneously reported their sacred flames turning the color of oxidized steel
- A message appeared in the Iron Afterlife attributed to Shogun Tesshari-Vane III. The message was nine words: *"The sixth did not ascend. Do not let this one."*
- All three claimants began moving in the same month, after years of building

No good ending is guaranteed. No hero is promised. The prophecy does not say the ascended one will be kind. It only says the world will never be the same.

It has been the same six times already. Perhaps this time, that's the mercy.

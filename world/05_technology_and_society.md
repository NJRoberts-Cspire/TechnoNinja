## Cybernetic Technology in Tesshari

### The Foundation: Resonant Metal

All cybernetic technology in Tesshari is built on **resonant metal** — the unique alloy that first appeared as a consequence of the Unnamed's Ascension. It cannot be synthesized; it can only be extracted from natural deposits and refined.

Resonant metal has three properties that no other material possesses:

1. **Biological bonding** — When properly refined and shaped, it bonds with organic tissue at the cellular level. The body does not reject it. Over time, the body cannot distinguish it from native tissue.

2. **Echomind conductance** — It carries the Echomind signal. Enhancements made of resonant metal become part of the Echomind's resonant field; enhancements made of inferior materials do not, and over time can degrade the resonance.

3. **The Iron Afterlife bridge** — At the moment of death, a properly integrated resonant-metal enhancement network provides the structure through which the Echomind passes into the Iron Afterlife. Without this network, the Echomind scatters.

The scarcity of resonant metal deposits is the single most significant economic and political factor in Tesshari. The Wire Market's wealth is built on controlling the most productive refineries. Korrath's power is built on controlling the Central Holds, where the largest terrestrial deposits are located. The Pale Reach's mines have been quietly overproducing for thirty years.

### Enhancement Tiers

Not all enhancements are equal. The quality of resonant metal used, the sophistication of the integration process, and the skill of the technician all affect both the functional and spiritual quality of the result.

**Tier I — Salvage Grade**
The cheapest and most common. Resonant metal content is minimal; most of the component is cheaper alloys with enough resonant metal mixed in to achieve basic bonding. Functional, but the integration quality is poor. Sutensai Readings of Salvage Grade enhancements register as weak resonance regardless of the individual's spiritual practice.

Salvage Grade enhancements also have a limited operational life. They corrode, degrade, and fail on timescales measured in years. For the Hollow Poor, this means a constant cycle of component failure, improvised repair, and replacement with whatever can be afforded.

*Available in:* Every Reach. Ubiquitous.

**Tier II — Standard Grade**
The workable middle. Sufficient resonant metal content for genuine integration. Sutensai Readings of Standard Grade register competently. Operational life measured in decades with proper maintenance.

Standard Grade is what the middle caste of Tesshari aspires to and what the Hollow Poor dream about. It is not affordable on a laborer's wage without significant sacrifice.

*Available in:* Most cities. Requires a licensed technician.

**Tier III — Resonant Grade**
High resonant metal content. Integration is seamless enough that practitioners report the "weight" of the enhancement disappears — it becomes indistinguishable, in daily sensation, from flesh. Sutensai Readings register deep resonance. Operational life measured in generations.

Resonant Grade is the product of the Wire Market's licensed production houses. It requires a certified technician trained in procedures that the Wire Market guards as trade secrets. It is expensive enough that acquiring it represents a significant life decision.

*Available in:* Wire Market production facilities, a small number of licensed distributors in major cities.

**Tier IV — Sanctum Grade**
The highest quality commercially available. Uses only the purest resonant metal, refined using techniques developed in collaboration between the Wire Market and the Sutensai over two hundred years. Integration is biological — the enhancement and the body become, over time, genuinely indistinguishable. Practitioners at this grade report that the Morning Resonance practice becomes effortless; the signal is always present.

Sanctum Grade is not available for purchase. It is awarded by the Sutensai to individuals of significant spiritual standing, by the Wire Market to Guild leadership, and by Korrath to his most trusted commanders. In Tesshari, the type of enhancements you carry is a statement about who owns you.

*Available in:* Strictly controlled. Essentially unavailable to ordinary citizens.

**Black Market / Experimental**
Outside this tier system are enhancements that exist in the spaces between: black market Tier III knockoffs of varying quality, Ashlands improvised modifications that work through mechanisms no licensed technician would endorse, and Wire Market experimental prototypes that have not passed certification.

Black market enhancements are illegal in most Reaches. They are also extremely common.

Ashlands modifications occupy a category of their own — functionally, some of them are remarkable achievements of necessity. Spiritually, they register as chaotic in a Sutensai Reading. Whether this is because they genuinely disrupt the Echomind or because the Sutensai's assessment methods weren't designed for them is a question the Ashlands mechanics have stopped caring about.

## How Enhancement Works

### The Installation Process

A standard enhancement installation, performed by a licensed technician, proceeds in four stages:

**1. Assessment** — The technician evaluates the site of installation, the patient's existing enhancement network, and the resonance between existing modifications. This stage is about integration planning. A poor assessment leads to rejection events or resonance conflicts.

**2. Preparation** — The body is prepared for the new component. In some cases this is surgical removal of existing tissue. In others it's the creation of connection points. The preparation must be done with resonant metal instruments; standard surgical tools contaminate the site.

**3. Installation** — The component itself is placed. The initial bonding begins within minutes; a low-grade sensation described by most patients as "a warmth that goes into the bone rather than staying on the skin." This bonding will continue and deepen for weeks after installation.

**4. Calibration** — The technician adjusts the new component's resonance signature to harmonize with the patient's existing network. This is the most skilled part of the process and the most variably performed. A master calibrator can bring a Standard Grade component to near-Resonant performance. A poor calibration can undermine an otherwise perfect installation.

### Integration Timeline

- **Day 1–7:** The new component bonds at the surface. Functional but foreign-feeling.
- **Week 1–4:** Deep bonding occurs. Most patients report the "weight" sensation shifting from foreign to personal.
- **Month 1–6:** The Echomind expands to include the new component. Sutensai Readings during this period show fluctuation as the resonance field adjusts.
- **Year 1–2:** Full integration. The component is part of the Echomind field in the same way native tissue is. Readings stabilize.

Salvage Grade never fully completes this timeline. The integration plateaus at a point that varies by component quality.

### Failure and Rejection

Enhancement rejection is relatively rare with quality components. It is common with Salvage Grade.

Rejection manifests as:
- Physical: localized inflammation, structural failure of the component, in severe cases, the body actively dismantling the enhancement at the cellular level
- Spiritual: the Echomind signal fragments around the rejected site, like a voice with static

Rejection events can be treated if caught early. If ignored, they cascade — one failed component can trigger rejection in adjacent components as the resonance field destabilizes.

For the Hollow Poor, cascade rejection is a known and dreaded risk. The treatment requires equipment and materials they cannot afford. The alternative is component removal, which returns them to a lower caste designation.

## Social Structure

### The Caste of Steel in Practice

The Caste of Steel is law. But law is administered by people, and people have interests.

In the Ironhold Reach, caste is enforced quarterly with the enthusiasm of a regime that has tied its legitimacy to the system's integrity. Caste inspectors are feared, often corrupt, and occasionally bribed into recategorization in both directions.

In the Wandering Reach, caste administration has degraded significantly since the Fracture Wars. Records are maintained, but enforcement is sporadic. Pilgrim culture has normalized a certain amount of caste flexibility that the remaining lords find alarming.

In the Wire Market, caste determines access, not personhood — the official position is that caste is a spiritual metric, and the Market is secular, so caste limits are applied to spiritual services (Sutensai access, official Readings) but not to employment, housing, or commerce. In practice, Wire Market guild membership requirements correlate with caste tiers at a rate that makes this distinction largely academic.

In the Ashlands, caste is a concept rather than an institution. No one is enforcing it. The Ashlands have functioned without the system for forty years and have not collapsed, which is either an argument against the system's necessity or evidence that the Ashlands are operating below the threshold where the system becomes relevant.

### Class and Enhancement

The relationship between wealth and enhancement quality creates a feedback loop that has been compressing the social hierarchy for three generations:

1. Wealthy individuals can afford higher-quality enhancements
2. Higher-quality enhancements produce better resonance, which produces higher caste
3. Higher caste produces better employment, legal standing, and social access
4. Better employment produces more wealth

Those who enter this cycle from the top stay at the top. Those outside it face a barrier of entry that requires generational saving to cross, by which point the top has advanced further.

The faith teaches that this cycle is not the point — that spiritual practice can produce resonance regardless of enhancement quality. The Sutensai's Readings, however, are calibrated to enhancement quality in ways that practitioners have documented but the hierarchy declines to address publicly.

### The Hollow Poor

The underclass of Tesshari has a name: the Hollow Poor. Not because they are spiritually hollow — Sutensai practitioners who have worked in Ashlands communities report finding resonance that challenges the official metric — but because they carry hollow-grade enhancements in hollow-grade condition, and the world treats them accordingly.

The Hollow Poor fill the labor battalions, the factory floors, the maintenance tunnels, and the pilgrim roads. They die younger (enhancement failure), get sick more often (failed components introducing contamination), and have less legal recourse when wronged.

In the last five years, the Hollow Poor have become the primary recruitment base for all three claimant movements:
- Korrath offers the promise of advancement through military service — fight for the Ironhold and your enhancements will be maintained
- Vaen offers release from the cycle — stop chasing enhancement, stop being defined by what you can afford
- The Hollow Author's puppets offer something targeted to whoever is listening, which has made them paradoxically the most broadly appealing

### Gender and Body

Tesshari culture, descended from a feudal tradition and reshaped by a century of industrial empire, has complex and evolving relationships with gender.

Cybernetic modification has decoupled many traditional gender signifiers from biology — the body is not fixed, and a person can modify toward any presentation or no presentation over time. This has not eliminated gender-based social dynamics so much as transformed them into something more fluid and less codified.

The Iron Sutra is officially agnostic on gender: the Echomind has no gender, and the Between does not distinguish. The Sutensai have been less progressive in practice than in doctrine, though the current generation of junior practitioners is more aligned with the doctrine.

## Technology Beyond Cybernetics

### Wire Craft

**Wire Craft** is the art of connecting cybernetic systems to each other and to external networks — the technology that allows enhanced individuals to interface directly with machinery, communications systems, and other enhanced individuals.

A skilled Wire Crafter can:
- Access external data networks through their enhancements
- Communicate across distances through wire-linked relay stations
- Interface with and operate machinery without physical controls
- Enter a partial shared state with another Wire Craft practitioner — experiencing their sensory feed

Wire Craft technology is the Wire Market's primary competitive advantage. The best Wire Craft hardware and training exists nowhere else in Tesshari. The Sutensai are deeply ambivalent about Wire Craft because its ability to connect minds directly raises complicated questions about individual Echomind identity.

### The Iron Afterlife Interface

Standard individuals with standard enhancements experience the Iron Afterlife only at death — the passive transfer of the Echomind.

Active access — actually communicating with the dead — requires specific enhancement configurations and extensive Sutensai training. The barrier to entry is high. The technology for it has existed for three hundred years.

The Echo Coast developed an unauthorized access technology sixty years ago, which is part of why the Tidecourt has been functionally independent of Sutensai authority since. Their access method is different in character: rather than a controlled, structured communion with specific individuals, Echo Coast technology opens a broader window — less precise, more prone to interference, but accessible to a wider range of people.

The Sutensai consider Echo Coast access methods spiritually dangerous. The Tidecourt considers Sutensai access methods spiritually incomplete. Neither has convinced the other.

### Weapons Technology

The weaponization of cybernetic enhancement is pervasive. Combat enhancements range from:

**Integrated weapons** — Blades, projectile systems, and energy discharge mechanisms built directly into enhancement architecture. Korrath's forces are the most heavily integrated military in Tesshari; his senior commanders are more weapons-system than person.

**Enhancement-assisted combat** — Enhancements that amplify natural combat ability: reflex acceleration, structural reinforcement, sensory enhancement for target acquisition. More common than integrated weapons; used across all Reaches.

**Wire Craft combat applications** — Using Wire Craft to interface with and disable enemy enhancements, to access battlefield communications, or — at the most advanced level — to create temporary shared-state connections with fellow combatants for tactical coordination.

**Experimental and prohibited** — Technologies developed in Wire Market black labs and Pale Reach facilities that don't appear in any public catalog. Effects range from "startling" to "catastrophic."

## Daily Life

### The Maintenance Cycle

Every enhanced individual in Tesshari lives on the **maintenance cycle** — the rhythm of component servicing, calibration checks, and repair that keeps enhancement networks functional. For Resonant Grade users, this is an annual or biannual luxury appointment. For Salvage Grade users, it is a constant, expensive, anxiety-producing necessity.

Missing a maintenance cycle:
- Minor delay (weeks): Slightly degraded performance. No permanent effects.
- Moderate delay (months): Measurable resonance degradation. Possible minor component failures.
- Significant delay (year+): Component failures cascade. Possible rejection events. Sutensai Readings will show deterioration.
- Extended delay: The Hollow Poor's experience. Constant partial failure. Components integrated enough not to remove but degraded enough to be unreliable.

### Food, Shelter, and the Enhanced Body

An enhanced body still requires food. Resonant metal integration is thorough enough that the body processes nutrition for both organic and cybernetic components — the body doesn't eat *for* the enhancements, but it cannot sustain the enhancements if it isn't eating.

The Hollow Poor face a compounding problem: their degraded enhancements require more metabolic resources to maintain than quality enhancements, while their economic position means less reliable access to food. The phrase "eating your own steel" refers to the state of a body burning muscle and organ tissue trying to maintain enhancements it can no longer properly sustain.

Shelter needs for enhanced individuals depend significantly on enhancement type. Certain modifications require temperature regulation, low-humidity storage for exposed components, or access to calibration equipment. The more heavily modified a person is, the more specific and expensive their shelter requirements become.

In the Ironhold Reach, maintenance facilities are militarized public infrastructure — operated by the state, theoretically accessible to all citizens, practically available based on caste. In the Wire Market, maintenance is a commercial service. In the Wandering Reach, Vaen's pilgrims have developed communal maintenance practices that the Wire Market calls primitive and the pilgrims call sustainable.

## What Money Buys in Tesshari

| Item | Rough Cost (in relative terms) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Salvage Grade component | Low | Available everywhere; quality varies widely |
| Standard Grade component | Moderate | Requires licensed technician |
| Standard Grade maintenance service | Moderate, recurring | Every 6–12 months for full service |
| Resonant Grade component | High | Wire Market or equivalent only |
| Sanctum Grade component | Not for sale | Political currency |
| Wire Craft interface | Moderate–High | Depends on capability level |
| Iron Afterlife consultation | Moderate | Through Sutensai; quality varies by practitioner rank |
| Black market modification | Variable | Risk priced in |
| Ashlands custom work | Low–Moderate | No certification; functional quality variable |

The economy of Tesshari runs on maintenance debt: most people owe something to someone for the cost of their enhancements. The Wire Market Guilds discovered three generations ago that enhancement debt was more reliable than property debt for maintaining labor compliance, and the practice of guild-financed enhancement packages with multi-year service obligations has become standard across most Reaches.

You may own your body. But who financed your upgrades might have an opinion about that.

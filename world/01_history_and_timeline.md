# TESSHARI — History and Timeline

---

> *"We do not study history to learn from it. We study it to understand what we have already become."*
> — Sutensai Archivist Mevhari, executed for heresy, Year 412 of the Imperial Calendar

---

## On the Reliability of History

Before anything else: Tesshari's history is contested.

The Sutensai control the largest archive of historical records. They have also burned three separate archives they deemed heretical. The Wire Market purchased a significant private collection ninety years ago and has never allowed independent scholars access. The Ironhold Reach under Korrath actively suppresses accounts that predate the empire.

What follows is the best reconstruction available. Some of it is certain. Some of it is educated inference. Some sections are marked *[CONTESTED]*, meaning the three dominant powers of Tesshari each hold an incompatible version of the truth.

---

## The Age of Silence
*Approx. 5,000–3,000 years before present*

No firsthand records survive this period. The Iron Sutra calls it **The Unharvested Age** — the time before any soul learned what it was.

Archaeological evidence suggests a civilization of entirely biological beings who left behind:
- Foundations of structures that match no known architectural tradition
- Organic matter preserved in unusual metallic compounds not found naturally
- What appears to be a script carved into bedrock that has resisted every attempt at translation

The Sutensai maintain that this civilization was obliterated by divine judgment — specifically, that the Prior Ascended did not exist yet, that the world was "unwitnessed," and that an unwitnessed world cannot sustain itself.

Scholars outside the faith note that this explanation is unfalsifiable and conveniently positions the faith as the reason civilization survived at all.

## The Age of Awakening
*Approx. 3,000–2,000 years before present*

### The Unnamed Ascends
The first confirmed Ascension occurred at some point approximately 3,000 years ago. The being who achieved it has no recorded name — not because records were lost, but because every account of this being begins with a description of transcendence and then stops, as if the writers could not find language to contain what came before it.

The Iron Sutra calls this being simply **The Unnamed**.

The physical consequence of the Unnamed's ascension: the first veins of **resonant metal** appeared in the earth — deposits of an alloy that does not occur naturally and that no metallurgist has ever fully replicated. This metal would, thousands of years later, become the foundation of cybernetic technology.

### Tesshari Walks the Land
A being referred to only as **Tesshari** — the same name the empire and nation would later claim — walked the length of the landmass for what the earliest texts describe as "a generation and a half." They taught. They organized. They established the first version of what would become the Iron Sutra's core beliefs: that flesh and steel together were sacred, that the soul resided in the space between.

Tesshari then ascended. The physical consequence: the landmass *shifted*. The mountain range now called the Tesshari Spine does not appear in any pre-Awakening geological record. It formed, seemingly overnight, as Tesshari passed through.

This is why the nation is named Tesshari. Not in arrogance, but in geography. The mountains themselves are a memorial.

### The Iron Weeper
The third Prior Ascended wept during their ascension.

This is documented in six independent sources from three different linguistic traditions. Whatever the Iron Weeper felt in that moment, it was grief — profound, total, and directed at something the texts either cannot name or will not.

The tears, where they fell, hardened into the richest resonant metal deposits in the world. The major mining operations of the Central Holds and Eastern Reaches were built, eventually, on the sites of those tears.

*[CONTESTED: The Sutensai teach the Iron Weeper wept from joy. The Resonant sect teaches the weeping was a warning. Vaen teaches the weeping was the recognition that ascension itself was a mistake.]*

## The Age of Stone and Steel
*Approx. 2,000–800 years before present*

### The Patient One
The fourth Prior Ascended spent four hundred years preparing.

Records from this period, unusually detailed for the era, describe a being who performed no miracles, claimed no divinity, and refused all titles. They simply practiced — an exact methodology that the records describe in exhausting procedural detail but that no practitioner has ever been able to replicate.

The Patient One ascended quietly. There was no geological event, no light, no sound. One morning they were present; the following morning they were not. Three villages reported strange effects on their memories: not memory loss, but memory *clarity* — total recall of childhood events, sensory detail intact. The effect faded over years.

Scholars who study this Ascension tend to disappear. Not violently. Simply: they stop sending correspondence, stop attending meetings, and when sought out, are found sitting somewhere, very still, describing a sound that no one else can hear.

### The Broken Crown
A warlord whose name is lost — referred to only as **The Broken Crown** for the crown they wore, which was shattered in their final battle — ascended mid-combat.

The battle simply stopped.

Both armies — several thousand combatants — lay down simultaneously and slept for three days. When they woke, the Broken Crown was gone. The war they had been fighting was over, not because either side surrendered, but because neither side could remember what the war had been about.

This Ascension is the basis for the warrior traditions of the Iron Sutra — the belief that violence pursued with total commitment can become a form of prayer.

### The Last Voice
The sixth Prior Ascended is called **The Last Voice** because they spoke during their ascension. Witnesses reported a voice that came from everywhere and nowhere simultaneously, lasting approximately thirty seconds.

The words are recorded in the oldest surviving Sutensai text. They are written in a script the Sutensai refuse to translate publicly. The original document is kept in a sealed vault beneath the Sutra Sanctum.

Three scholars have independently claimed to have deciphered the text. All three recanted. One recanted twice.

The physical consequence of this ascension: a frequency, detectable only in deep underground spaces, that persists to this day. Certain individuals — rare, typically those with minimal cybernetic modification — report hearing it as something between a voice and a heartbeat.

## The Age of Discovery
*Approx. 800–500 years before present*

### The First Cybernetics
The resonant metal deposits left by the Prior Ascended had been mined and worked for centuries before anyone discovered their unique property: when properly refined and shaped, they bonded with organic tissue.

Not attached. **Bonded.** At the cellular level, as if the body recognized the metal as native.

The first documented cybernetic procedure was performed by a faith healer named **Sura Mei**, who replaced a dying man's crushed heart with a pumping mechanism of resonant steel. The man lived. More importantly: he reported that the implant did not feel foreign. It felt like something that had always been there and only just arrived.

Sura Mei's journals are the oldest surviving firsthand account of the cybernetic experience. They are also deeply unsettling — she became convinced, by the end of her life, that the resonant metal was not a material but a memory. That it remembered being part of the Prior Ascended, and that when bonded to a new host, it was attempting to continue something it had started.

She was declared a prophet after her death. The Sutensai added two contradictory interpretations of her journals to the official canon and have never reconciled them.

### The Iron Sutra Is Written Down
For the first five hundred years of its existence, the Iron Sutra was entirely oral — passed through lineages of practitioners, memorized in full, recited in ceremony. Writing it down was initially considered a form of arrogance: the idea that the teaching could be frozen in text, rather than living in the voice.

Necessity changed this. As communities grew and practitioners died without students, versions began to drift. The first written Iron Sutra was produced by a council of seven senior practitioners who argued for three years over every line.

The text they produced is known as **The Seven-Voice Sutra**. It has been revised, expanded, and "clarified" forty-three times since its original writing. The Sutensai consider all forty-three revisions canonical. Scholars consider this logically impossible. The Sutensai consider this a failure of imagination on the scholars' part.

### The Sutensai Order Forms
The formal priesthood coalesced around this period — scholars, healers, and spiritual leaders who dedicated themselves to the study and administration of the Iron Sutra. Their primary innovation was **The Reading**: a structured examination of a person's enhancement-to-flesh ratio and the quality of their integration, used to assess "spiritual depth."

The Reading was originally voluntary and advisory. Within three generations, it was compulsory and determinative of legal status.

## The Imperial Age
*Approx. 500–60 years before present*

### The Rise of the Eternal Shogunate
The first Eternal Shogun is recorded simply as **Shogun One** in official Tesshari history — a deliberate erasure of their personal name, meant to signal that the role transcended the individual. In practice, this erasure was politically convenient: the historical record suggests Shogun One rose to power through methods the faith would have difficulty sanctifying.

The empire they built was unified through three tools:
1. **Overwhelming military force**, enabled by the first mass-production of cybernetic combat enhancements
2. **Faith integration**, which made the Sutensai de facto administrative arm of the empire
3. **The Caste of Steel**, a legal framework tying enhancement level to civil rights

Conquered territories were given a choice: adopt the Iron Sutra, accept the Caste of Steel, and integrate into the empire — or be classified as "Unwitnessed" and lose all legal protections. Most integrated. Some did not. Those that did not are now referred to as "historical populations" in Sutensai texts.

### The Caste of Steel
*[CONTESTED: The Ironhold considers the Caste of Steel a necessary structure. The Wandering Reach considers it the root cause of the empire's collapse. The Sutra Sanctum considers it a sacred hierarchy.]*

The Caste of Steel divided society into seven tiers based on enhancement integration:

| Tier | Name | Enhancement Level | Legal Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| First | The Untouched | No enhancements | Severely restricted (Tethered status) |
| Second | The Budding | Minor enhancements only | Limited rights |
| Third | The Walking | Standard integration | Common citizen rights |
| Fourth | The Deepened | Significant enhancement | Full civil rights |
| Fifth | The Resonant | High integration, quality modifications | Elevated status |
| Sixth | The Ascending | Near-total modification | Near-noble status |
| Seventh | The Hollow | Total modification (deemed soulless) | No rights whatsoever |

The Seventh Tier — the Hollow — created an immediate problem: the Sutensai determined that those with complete modification had lost their Echomind entirely. They were declared legally dead while still walking. The empire found this useful for its labor programs.

### The Great Reckoning
*Year 312 of the Imperial Calendar — approx. 188 years before present*

The fourth Eternal Shogun, **Tesshari-Vaen I**, decreed what became known as the Great Reckoning: a systematic purge of all individuals classified as Seventh Tier. The stated reason was spiritual hygiene — the hollow could not ascend, and their presence in communities was declared a "contaminant of the Between."

The Reckoning lasted nine years. The number of dead is not recorded in official histories. Estimates from recovered documents range from sixty thousand to four hundred thousand.

Three consequences followed:
1. The Wire Market Guilds — which had profited enormously from cybernetic sales — quietly began engineering their best products with deliberate upper-tier caps, ensuring no client could accidentally reach Seventh Tier without knowing
2. The Sutensai created the office of **Soul Warden** — a priest empowered to conduct Readings at will and report Seventh Tier individuals to the state
3. A resistance movement called **The Unreaped** formed in the Eastern Reaches. They were dismantled within a generation. Some of their descendants eventually became the foundation of Vaen's early following.

### The Later Empire
The two hundred years following the Great Reckoning were marked by stability, paranoia, and incremental decay. Each successive Shogun introduced new restrictions on cybernetic development — not out of faith, scholars now believe, but out of fear that sufficiently advanced subjects might become ungovernable.

The Wire Market responded by conducting its most significant research in secret. The Sutensai responded by consolidating more administrative power. The military responded by becoming less loyal to the Shogun and more loyal to the generals who commanded their units.

By the time of Shogun Tesshari-Vane III, the empire was a machine running on momentum. The fuel had run out decades earlier.

## The Shattering
*Approx. 60–40 years before present*

### The Upload Decree
**Shogun Tesshari-Vane III** was the thirteenth Eternal Shogun, the last, and the most technically proficient. They had spent thirty years in private collaboration with the Wire Market's most secretive research guild — a collaboration the Sutensai were never informed of — developing a technology that had been theorized but never achieved:

**Full consciousness upload to the digital substrate of the Iron Afterlife.**

The Iron Afterlife — the digital realm where souls were believed to pass after death — had been partially accessible to the Sutensai for centuries through specific enhancement configurations. Wisps of personality. Echoes of memory. The sense of presence without content. A true upload would not send a copy. It would send the *original*.

The Shogun announced the Upload Decree publicly. They would ascend, not through the Between as the prophecy described, but through technology. They claimed this was the fulfillment of the Seventh Convergence. The Sutensai simultaneously declared it holy and heretical, which is to say they split.

The upload was performed on a public day, in the Imperial Citadel, before thousands of witnesses.

The Shogun's body went still. Three days passed.

Then a voice spoke from the Iron Afterlife — every enhanced individual with spiritual-tier modifications heard it simultaneously, as if broadcast. The voice identified itself as Tesshari-Vane III. It said: *"I am here. I am not alone. Be careful of what follows me."*

And then it was silent.

### The Fracture Wars
What followed was twenty years of catastrophic civil conflict called the Fracture Wars, though "wars" implies more organization than what actually occurred — it was closer to a cascade of simultaneous power vacuums.

The Sutensai split into three sects over the upload's theological implications. Without a unified church, caste administration collapsed in six of the empire's nine major territories. With caste administration gone, legal structures built on caste collapsed. With legal structures gone, the military — unpaid, unfed, and increasingly unsure who they served — began serving whoever could guarantee their next enhancement maintenance cycle.

The Shogun's three designated heirs each claimed the throne. All three died within two years — two by assassination, one by suicide. Five military commanders declared themselves the legitimate successor. None of them lasted.

What eventually stabilized were not successor states but **power consolidations** — regions where one force happened to have more military, economic, or spiritual leverage than its neighbors. These became the seven Broken Reaches.

The Fracture Wars ended not with a peace treaty but with exhaustion. After twenty years, everyone who had been fighting was either dead, incorporated, or hiding.

## The Age of Broken Reaches
*Approx. 40 years before present — current day*

### Stabilization
The seven Reaches achieved rough borders roughly forty years before the campaign begins. None of them are recognized by the others as legitimate. All of them operate as if they are.

The Sutensai, fractured into three sects, negotiated an uneasy internal compact — they would not officially endorse any Reach's political claim, allowing them to maintain spiritual authority across all territories. This compact has held for thirty years. It is showing stress fractures.

The Wire Market re-emerged as a neutral economic zone between the Western Reaches, profiting from all sides and maintaining strict non-alignment while quietly influencing everyone.

### The Emergence of the Claimants
In the years following stabilization, three figures began acquiring power on a scale that alarmed the lords of the Broken Reaches:

**Korrath the Unbroken** rose through the military ranks of the Ironhold Reach over twenty years, replacing each piece of flesh with battlefield salvage, until he stood before the Reach's lord and declared that a Shogun who could not die should be the one to unify Tesshari. The lord disagreed. The lord died. Korrath has ruled the Ironhold Reach for fifteen years.

**Vaen of the Empty Hand** was first documented forty-five years ago — already old, already enhanced, already beginning the gradual process of removing her modifications. She never declared herself a claimant publicly. Her followers declared her. By the time she was being discussed in the war councils of three separate Reaches, she simply nodded when asked if she intended to ascend, and said: *"I intend to stop intending. The ascension will follow."*

**The Hollow Author** has never been documented. The puppets they operate have been active for at least thirty years. The intelligence services of the Wire Market have pieced together the existence of a single controlling consciousness from behavioral analysis of their surrogates — but have not shared this information publicly, because the Hollow Author controls two senior Wire Market Guild Masters through puppets they haven't yet identified.

### The Signs Begin
In the twelve months before the campaign opens, the following events have been independently verified:

- Metal deposits in the Ashlands began resonating at a frequency matching the Last Voice's underground echo
- Three separate Sutensai temples reported their sacred flames burning the color of oxidized steel — a color that has no precedent in eight hundred years of records
- A message appeared in the Iron Afterlife, attributed to Tesshari-Vane III: *"The sixth did not ascend. Do not let this one."*
- All three claimants began mobilizing — simultaneously, in the same month — after years of preparation

# THE BROKEN REACHES — The Seven Kingdoms of Tesshari

---

> *"Seven lords, seven lies. Each one holds a piece of the empire and calls it the whole."*
> — Common saying in the Ashlands, origin unknown

---

## Overview

The seven Broken Reaches are not countries. They are the wreckage of a country that hardened into something that functions like governance without ever fully committing to being it.

No Reach recognizes another's borders as legitimate. No Reach has a peace treaty with any other. What exists between them is an arrangement of mutual deterrence, economic dependency, and grudging coexistence — held together less by diplomacy than by the shared understanding that full-scale war would leave no winner capable of holding what they'd taken.

This understanding is now fraying. The emergence of the Claimants has introduced a new variable: if one faction achieves divine ascension, deterrence becomes irrelevant.

---

## 1. The Ironhold Reach

**Ruler:** Korrath the Unbroken
**Capital:** The Cinderfort
**Population:** Largest of the seven Reaches
**Primary Power Base:** Military

---

### Geography

The Ironhold Reach occupies the Central Holds — the flat, fortified heartland of the old Tesshari Empire. It is the most strategically accessible region in Tesshari and, consequently, the most fought over. The landscape is a record of war: trenches that were never filled, fortification foundations that were never dismantled, entire towns that were rebuilt over the ruins of towns that were rebuilt over the ruins of older towns.

The **Cinderfort** sits at the geographic center of the region — a fortress city built in stages over four hundred years, each successive military power adding walls outside the previous walls, so that the city exists as a series of concentric rings with the oldest, smallest architecture at the center and the newest, most imposing construction on the outermost ring. Korrath's command center occupies the innermost ring, in buildings that predate the empire.

### Culture and Society

The Ironhold Reach is a military dictatorship that has been so consistent in its authoritarianism that many of its citizens do not experience it as a dictatorship. Law is predictable. Order is enforced. The trains run on time, as it were, because the engineers who maintain them know exactly what happens if they don't.

The Caste of Steel is enforced with more rigor here than anywhere else in Tesshari. Enhancement inspections are conducted quarterly; those who have fallen below their caste designation through deteriorated enhancements have sixty days to restore their standing or face reclassification. Reclassification downward means losing legal rights incrementally. At the bottom, it means conscription into the labor battalions that maintain Korrath's war infrastructure.

The culture valorizes endurance. Complaining is considered a character flaw. Showing pain is considered a tactical weakness. The most common phrase in Ironhold culture, used in response to any hardship, is: *"The steel holds."*

### Key Locations

**The Cinderfort** — The capital. A city of two million in the layers of its defensive rings. The innermost ring contains the command spire from which Korrath operates.

**The Salvage Fields** — Vast flatlands south of the capital where old battlefield equipment is stripped, sorted, and repurposed. The source of the distinctive patchwork-salvage aesthetics of Ironhold cybernetics.

**The Wall of Names** — A structure Korrath ordered built twenty years ago: a wall ten miles long and forty feet high, inscribed with the names of every soldier who died under his command. It is nearly full. New sections are added annually.

### The Sutensai Presence

The Orthodoxy maintains temples in the Ironhold Reach under a grudging agreement with Korrath: they administer Readings and caste management, and in exchange do not comment on his theology. Korrath's personal theology — that ascension comes through dominance — is technically heretical to all three sects. The Orthodoxy has chosen silence over confrontation, a choice that costs them credibility with their own practitioners daily.

## 2. The Wandering Reach

**Ruler:** No central ruler; a loose council of minor lords
**Capital:** Arguable; the city of Hessenkai functions as the de facto center
**Population:** Medium; fluctuating (Vaen's pilgrims swell numbers seasonally)
**Primary Power Base:** Spiritual movement and distributed governance

---

### Geography

The Wandering Reach is a misnomer from the Fracture Wars era — the borders "wandered" because no single force could hold them. The name stuck. The territory spans a wide swath of the western interior, between the Wire Market's coastal holdings and the Central Holds of the Ironhold. It is agricultural land, mostly — river valleys, mid-elevation plains, forests that haven't been cleared yet because no one has gotten around to it.

### Culture and Society

The Wandering Reach has no strong central identity precisely because it has no strong central government. The council of minor lords who administer it are cooperating out of necessity and have been on the verge of fracturing for thirty years without actually fracturing.

What gives the Wandering Reach its character is the presence of **Vaen's pilgrims**, who have been moving through it in great numbers for twenty years. Pilgrim culture — communal, stripping-back, focused on release over acquisition — has begun to influence local culture significantly. Towns that once had thriving enhancement clinics have seen business drop as residents, influenced by the pilgrims passing through, defer upgrades or begin modest reversals.

The lords of the Wandering Reach are alarmed by this trend and have done nothing effective about it.

### Key Locations

**Hessenkai** — The largest city, a trading hub that has been negotiating neutrality with the Ironhold, the Wire Market, and the Sutra Sanctum simultaneously for forty years. It does this through a combination of genuine diplomatic skill and the fact that all three powers find it more useful as a neutral trade point than as a conquered territory.

**The Penitent Road** — A pilgrimage route established organically by Vaen's followers, running west to east through the length of the Reach. Tens of thousands of pilgrims walk it annually. The road has become functionally self-sustaining — pilgrim communities have built waypoints, wells, and shelter stations along its length.

**The Garden of Removed Things** — A site outside Hessenkai where pilgrims who have completed significant enhancement removals leave the removed components. It began as a small clearing. It is now an installation covering several acres — walls of discarded steel, towers of extracted components, pathways lined with the things people decided they didn't need anymore. It is deeply unsettling and strangely beautiful.

### The Sutensai Presence

All three sects maintain a presence in the Wandering Reach, making it the only territory where they compete directly for the same population. The result is a kind of theological marketplace — citizens can and do change sect affiliations based on who gave the better sermon, which is theologically unprecedented and horrifies the Orthodoxy.

## 3. The Sutra Sanctum

**Ruler:** The Sutensai Council of Keepers (effectively a theocracy)
**Capital:** Verimhari, the First City
**Population:** Medium-small; extremely high literacy and education
**Primary Power Base:** Spiritual authority, historical records, Iron Afterlife access

---

### Geography

The Sutra Sanctum occupies the high Eastern Reaches — the mountain passes and elevated valleys that control the only viable overland routes between the western coast and the eastern territories. This geographic chokepoint has made it diplomatically powerful beyond its population or military strength.

The territory is cold, clean, and meticulously maintained. The Sutensai have governed it directly for three hundred years, and the accumulated civic infrastructure is impressive — roads, water systems, public education — in ways that make the theocracy more palatable to its citizens than it might otherwise be.

### Culture and Society

Life in the Sutra Sanctum is orderly to a degree that visitors find either reassuring or oppressive, depending on their temperament. The Sutensai administrate everything: caste, education, commerce regulation, dispute resolution, healthcare. They are, by institutional standards, effective at all of it.

What the Sutensai administer, they also surveil. Soul Wardens are more present here than anywhere else in Tesshari. Readings are more frequent. The tracking of enhancement changes is meticulous — any significant modification requires filing notice with the local Warden's office within ten days.

Those who chafe under this level of oversight leave for other Reaches. Those who remain tend to be people who find the structure comforting, which over generations has produced a population genuinely aligned with theocratic administration. The Sutensai do not need to be overtly coercive in the Sanctum; they have, slowly and deliberately, filtered for people who don't require coercion.

### Key Locations

**Verimhari, the First City** — The oldest continuously inhabited city in Tesshari, built around the site where the being Tesshari was last seen before their ascension. The central temple complex has been rebuilt seventeen times; the original foundation stones are visible under glass flooring in the deepest level. The city smells of incense, metal, and cold mountain air.

**The Archive of Voices** — The Sutensai's primary records facility. Hundreds of thousands of documents. Staffed by an order of archivists who take vows of non-disclosure and who are rumored to know three times what they have ever publicly said.

**The Deep Temple** — Below Verimhari, accessed only by senior Sutensai, is the primary access point for the Iron Afterlife. The chamber has been continuously occupied by Veil Masters for eight hundred years. The current three Veil Masters have been in continuous near-residency for forty years, since the death of the previous Sutensai Prime.

**The Vault of the Sixth Account** — A sealed room in the Archive of Voices where the most recent copy of the Sixth Account is stored. It is authenticated. It is under guard. The Orthodoxy denies it exists while maintaining a guard rotation.

### The Sutensai Presence

The Sutra Sanctum *is* the Orthodoxy's home territory. The Resonants have been officially expelled three times. They maintain a covert presence anyway. The Flesh Circle is tolerated to a minimal degree because Vaen's movement has made direct suppression diplomatically inadvisable.

## 4. The Wire Market

**Ruler:** The Guild Conclave (seven senior Guild Masters, theoretically equal)
**Capital:** Harven-by-the-Sea
**Population:** Dense urban; the most concentrated population in Tesshari
**Primary Power Base:** Technology, trade, information

---

### Geography

The Wire Market is not a single city but a cluster of city-states along the western coast, connected by roads, rail lines, and a shared governance framework. The coastline allows trade in every direction; the cities have grown together over decades until their outer districts are indistinguishable.

**Harven-by-the-Sea** is the largest and oldest, the de facto capital — a city of three million, built vertically due to geographic constraints, with the wealthy guild districts in high towers and the worker districts in the perpetual shadow below.

### Culture and Society

The Wire Market is the most economically sophisticated territory in Tesshari and one of the most stratified. The Guild Conclave governs through a system that is technically democratic — every citizen has a vote in guild elections — and practically oligarchic, since the guilds control employment, housing, enhancement access, and the infrastructure of daily life. Voting against your guild's endorsed candidate is legally protected and socially catastrophic.

Cybernetic technology here is more advanced than anywhere else in Tesshari. The best enhancements in the world are produced in Wire Market facilities. They are also the most expensive, and the tiered pricing system ensures that the highest-quality modifications go to those who can afford them — which means the guild hierarchy.

The Wire Market officially observes no religion. The Guild Conclave issued a statement two hundred years ago declaring that "the Iron Sutra represents a spiritual tradition respected by the Market, whose governance remains secular." In practice, the Sutensai's caste system is embedded in Wire Market law because dismantling it would require renegotiating three hundred years of legal precedent. The Market and the Orthodoxy have a relationship of mutual convenience that both sides describe, when pressed, as purely administrative.

The Wire Market's intelligence service — officially the **Trade Verification Bureau** — is the best-resourced information-gathering operation in Tesshari. They know about the Hollow Author's existence. They have not decided what to do with this knowledge, partly because two of their Guild Masters are puppets.

### Key Locations

**Harven-by-the-Sea** — The capital cluster. Towers, markets, laboratories, and desperate poverty within the same block radius. The Guild Towers are visible from twenty miles at sea.

**The Refinement Quarter** — Where the best enhancement technology is produced. Access restricted to Guild Sixth tier and above. Security maintained by a private force more sophisticated than most national militaries.

**The Low Markets** — At street level and below, a parallel economy of refurbished, black-market, and experimental enhancements. Officially illegal. The Guild Conclave's enforcement of this illegality is selective and revenue-motivated.

**The Listening House** — The Trade Verification Bureau's primary facility. A nondescript building on an unremarkable street. The basement extends seven stories underground.

### The Sutensai Presence

Minimal formally; significant practically. The Resonant sect has its strongest presence in the Wire Market, often operating out of facilities that blur the line between chapel and enhancement clinic.

## 5. The Pale Reach

**Ruler:** Officially, Lord Tessik and a council of minor northern lords
**Capital:** Kanemori
**Population:** Sparse; scattered across a wide cold territory
**Primary Power Base:** Unknown to most outsiders; the Hollow Author

---

### Geography

The Pale Reach occupies the northern territories — cold, lightly populated, separated from the rest of Tesshari by a secondary mountain range and the general hostility of the climate. It is not a desirable territory. It has never been a wealthy or powerful Reach. Historically, it has been notable primarily for the mining operations in its mountains and the fishing fleets along its northern coast.

This is why no one paid close attention to what was happening there.

### Culture and Society

The Pale Reach appears to function normally. Lord Tessik is regarded as a minor figure — competent, unnoteworthy, occasionally surprising in his military successes for a Reach of his resources. The council of lords governs. The cities function.

What no one outside the Wire Market's intelligence service understands is that Lord Tessik and approximately sixty percent of the Pale Reach's significant decision-makers are Hollow Author puppets. The governance of the Pale Reach is a performance, executed with enough variation and apparent personality to pass casual observation, directed by a consciousness no one has ever seen.

The Pale Reach's citizens are not puppets. They live their lives unaware that their leaders are operated remote controls. The few who have noticed inconsistencies in their lords' behavior have generally attributed it to the cold and the isolation.

### What the Hollow Author Is Building in the Pale Reach

This information is not publicly known. Players who investigate deeply may discover:

- A network of underground facilities built over the past thirty years
- Stockpiles of resonant metal that have been diverted from mining operations over decades
- Research installations developing enhancement technologies that do not exist in any Wire Market catalog
- Thousands of prepared puppet bodies — maintained, enhanced, and waiting for a signal

The Hollow Author has been preparing for the Seventh Convergence for thirty years. They have not been idle.

### Key Locations

**Kanemori** — The capital. A medium city that functions normally in every observable respect. Lord Tessik holds court twice weekly. Petitions are heard. Justice is administered. The city hums along.

**The White Mines** — The primary resonant metal extraction operation, officially producing at consistent if unspectacular rates. Actually producing significantly more, with the surplus diverted underground.

**The Preparation Halls** — Underground. Unknown to the public. The Hollow Author's true power base: a network of chambers containing the logistics, technology, and bodies needed for whatever the Author intends to do when the Seventh Convergence peaks.

### The Sutensai Presence

Minimal. The Pale Reach has never been religiously significant. The few Sutensai present are junior appointments — the posting is considered a demotion. None of them are senior enough to access the Iron Afterlife in ways that might detect the wrongness beneath the surface.

## 6. The Ashlands

**Ruler:** No central authority
**Capital:** The Char (a settlement; calling it a capital is generous)
**Population:** Sparse; primarily refugees, outcasts, and the desperate
**Primary Power Base:** None; survival is the governing principle

---

### Geography

The Ashlands are what remains of the southeastern territory after the **Tesshari Engine Collapse** — a catastrophic industrial accident that occurred fifty-two years ago, two years before the Shattering, when an experimental power generation system in the old imperial industrial heartland underwent cascading failure.

The explosion and subsequent atmospheric contamination rendered approximately eight thousand square miles uninhabitable in any conventional sense. The soil grows nothing. The water sources are tainted. The air carries particles that accelerate cybernetic corrosion. Any enhancement that has not been specifically treated for Ashlands conditions will begin failing within weeks.

The empire's response was to evacuate and seal the borders. The Fracture Wars prevented the seal from holding. People desperate enough to survive on the margins poured in, because the margins were better than what they were fleeing.

### Culture and Society

The Ashlands have no governance, no faith presence worth naming, no economic system beyond barter and scavenging. What they have is a survivor culture of extraordinary pragmatism and occasional brutality.

The people of the Ashlands are, paradoxically, the most technically skilled enhancement maintainers in Tesshari. When your enhancements are constantly degrading and you can't afford Wire Market repairs, you learn to fix them yourself. Ashlands mechanics, working with salvage and improvised materials, regularly perform procedures that licensed Wire Market technicians claim are impossible.

They are also, as a population, more tolerant of extreme modification than anywhere else in Tesshari. The Caste of Steel is a abstraction with no enforcement here. People do what they need to do to function. A person with purely salvage modifications that don't integrate properly, who reads as Seventh Caste by Sutensai standards and would be legally dead anywhere else, is just another person trying to survive here.

The faith has made several missionary attempts in the Ashlands. All have been largely unsuccessful, for a reason the Sutensai missionaries report with varying degrees of distress: the people of the Ashlands have a saying — *"The Between doesn't breathe Ashlands air"* — meaning that the spiritual resonance the Iron Sutra is built around is apparently very difficult to achieve in an environment of constant physical degradation. Whether this is a theological crisis or a measurement problem is a question that remains unresolved.

### Key Locations

**The Char** — The largest settlement, a city of approximately forty thousand built in the shell of a pre-Collapse industrial facility. The facility provides structural shelter; the residents provide everything else. The Char has no government but several factions in constant negotiation over resource allocation.

**The Engine Scar** — The epicenter of the original Collapse. A crater four miles across where the temperature is always higher than the surrounding region and where strange phenomena occur with enough regularity that even Ashlands residents avoid it. The Sutensai believe the Collapse disrupted the Between in this location permanently. The Wire Market believes there is recoverable technology in the Scar. No one has managed a successful deep expedition.

**The Drift Roads** — Informal trade routes between Ashlands settlements, maintained by no authority and used by everyone. The knowledge of which routes are passable in which conditions is the most valuable information in the Ashlands.

### The Sutensai Presence

Technically, a mission exists. Practically, the two Sutensai Tenders assigned to the Char have adapted to local conditions to the point where they conduct Readings using improvised techniques and administer the faith with a flexibility that would see them disciplined in any other territory. They are also, by most accounts, the most beloved spiritual figures in a population that is largely skeptical of the faith.

## 7. The Echo Coast

**Ruler:** The Tidecourt — a council of high-enhancement individuals with verified Iron Afterlife contact
**Capital:** Verenport
**Population:** Medium; unique demographic — high proportion of Echoed (digitally revived)
**Primary Power Base:** Iron Afterlife access, unique technology, proximity to the dead

---

### Geography

The Echo Coast runs along the southern coastline — a stretch of shoreline, harbors, and coastal lowlands that has historically been prosperous from fishing and sea trade. The geography itself is unremarkable. What makes the Echo Coast distinctive is something that happened here forty years ago, in the chaos of the Shattering:

An experimental Sutensai facility, attempting to develop clearer Iron Afterlife communication technology, had a breakthrough that was also an accident. They did not improve communication with the dead. They discovered how to bring the dead back.

### The Echoed

The **Echoed** are individuals who died and were revived through a process developed from that original accident — their Echomind, rather than fading in the Iron Afterlife, was extracted and reintegrated into a living body. The body is always a new one (the original having been damaged beyond use); the consciousness claims continuity with the person who died.

Whether this continuity is genuine — whether the person who wakes up is the same person who died, or a very detailed copy — is the central theological question of the Echo Coast. The Orthodoxy says they are copies and has been unable to enforce any legal consequences of this position in Echo Coast territory. The Resonants say they are genuine continuations and have published theological arguments that most people find either convincing or deliberately confusing. The Flesh Circle says the revival process is an abomination that traps the soul instead of freeing it and has been barred from the Echo Coast for thirty years for saying so at volume.

The Echoed make up approximately twenty percent of the Echo Coast's population. They are identifiable by certain enhancement signatures — the revival process leaves traces in the cybernetic integration — and are, among themselves, a tight community. They tend to be older than they appear, some of them on their second or third revival. They have unusual relationships with time, memory, and mortality.

### The Tidecourt

The Tidecourt governs the Echo Coast with a competence born of unusual perspective. Its members are, without exception, individuals with deep Iron Afterlife access — those who can commune with the dead in ways that go beyond what Sutensai practitioners elsewhere achieve. Several Tidecourt members are Echoed; they govern, in part, by consulting with the council's dead.

This makes the Tidecourt both extraordinarily well-informed and extremely difficult to manipulate. They have been approached by all three claimants. They have not committed to any of them.

### Key Locations

**Verenport** — The capital, a city built to face the sea with the Iron Afterlife at its back. Deeper than any other city in Tesshari — the upper levels are contemporary, the lower levels are centuries old, and the deepest levels are entirely inhabited by the Echoed who have not left the city's lower reaches in decades.

**The Revival Halls** — The facilities where the Echoed revival process is performed. Access heavily restricted. The Wire Market has been trying to acquire the technology for thirty years. The Tidecourt's answer has consistently been: *"No."*

**The Shore of Last Words** — A stretch of coastline where it is reported that the Iron Afterlife signal is strongest. Families come here to attempt contact with dead relatives without going through formal Sutensai channels. Success is unpredictable but common enough that people keep coming.

**The Drowned Archive** — Below the waterline of the harbor, an installation built into the seabed, maintaining records of every Echoed revival and every Iron Afterlife contact made from Echo Coast territory. The archive is also, according to Tidecourt members who will discuss it, periodically updated by entries they did not write.

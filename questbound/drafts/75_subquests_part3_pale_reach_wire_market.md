### 31. Lord Tessik Attends a Funeral
**Hook:** Lord Tessik, the Pale Reach's nominal ruler, has personally attended the funeral of a minor city administrator named Ollen Hast — a man of no political significance, no caste distinction, no military record. Tessik attended, stood at the grave in the rain for the full ceremony, and left without speaking to anyone. The funeral attendants are confused. The administrator's family is confused. A trade official who noticed Tessik's presence has quietly sent word to anyone who might understand what it means.

**The Truth:** Ollen Hast was not a minor administrator. He was the Hollow Author's oldest active puppet — a man who had been under full Author control for thirty-one years, since the age of twenty-three. He died of a genuine physical failure (cardiac — the Author cannot prevent natural mortality) and his death has left a significant gap in the Author's Pale Reach administrative network. Tessik's attendance was not a gesture of respect from Tessik. It was the Author, operating through Tessik, attending a funeral for part of themselves. The Author has never done this before. The trade official who noticed is one of the Author's own operatives and is alarmed precisely because this behavior is inconsistent with the Author's standard emotional profile.

**Complications:** Hast's family has, in the days since the funeral, begun finding things hidden in his home — documents, access codes, a small device they cannot identify. They are not sophisticated people and they do not know what they have. They are trying to figure out who their father actually was while simultaneously being watched by three different parties who want those items. Second complication: the trade official's alarm has led him to take an action he's never taken before: he has sent an inquiry, through back channels, to the Hollow Author directly, asking for clarification. He has never initiated contact with the Author. He doesn't know if that's allowed. He is waiting for a response that, if it comes in the Author's standard form, will arrive through a different puppet — meaning someone in the room with him will suddenly start speaking in a voice that isn't theirs.

**Reward:** Access to Hast's hidden materials — specifically, a partial personnel list of Pale Reach puppet-body hosts, written in the Author's notation, covering approximately forty names and their positions. The list is outdated (it was compiled seven years ago) but stable positions don't change quickly. Cross-referenced with current Pale Reach administrative records, it allows players to identify, with reasonable confidence, which senior administrators are currently Author-operated. This is the most significant intelligence breakthrough available in the Pale Reach, and it is sitting in a grieving family's basement.

### 32. The Shellbroken Cartographer
**Hook:** In Kanemori, the Pale Reach capital, a woman is selling hand-drawn maps. She has been selling them for six weeks, at a price that's too low for the quality — these are detailed, accurate maps of building interiors, maintenance access routes, and sub-street infrastructure for the city's oldest districts. She says her name is Nira and she surveys for a living. Surveyors in Kanemori require a license. She does not have one. The maps are for real buildings that she has never entered, according to the buildings' official access logs.

**The Truth:** Nira is Shellbroken — a former Author puppet, recently separated from the network. She does not remember her time as a puppet. What she has is spatial memory: the puppet she was operated the city's maintenance infrastructure for years, and the procedural knowledge is in her body even though the context is gone. She is drawing maps of places she has never consciously been, from muscle memory and spatial intuition that rises in her the way a habit rises — automatically, without the original reason. She is selling them because she needs to eat and because the act of drawing them is the closest thing she has to a structured activity in a life that is otherwise completely unmoored. She has not yet understood what the maps mean.

**Complications:** The maps are accurate to a degree that requires having been inside those buildings — which means they reveal something: the Author's maintenance-access routes through Kanemori's sub-street infrastructure, used to move puppet bodies through the city without surface-level observation. The Author's network has noticed the maps appearing in the market. An Author operative has been tasked with identifying the source. The operative is two days from finding Nira. Second complication: one of the buildings Nira has mapped is a public building whose sub-street access route connects to a chamber that does not appear on any official map. She drew the chamber. She doesn't know what's in it.

**Reward:** Nira's maps — the complete set, which covers approximately sixty percent of the Author's maintenance infrastructure in Kanemori's central district — and Nira's cooperation. She cannot tell players what the maps mean, but she can lead them through the infrastructure she remembers in her body, navigating by physical instinct rather than conscious knowledge. This makes her the best available guide to the Author's sub-street network, with the significant limitation that she cannot tell them in advance where they're going: she only knows when she gets there. The chamber she drew but hasn't identified contains a cache of Author-prepared puppet bodies — maintained, enhanced, inactive, waiting.

### 33. The White Mines Quota
**Hook:** A Pale Reach mine overseer named Kett has quietly filed a complaint with the Wandering Reach council — not the Pale Reach's own council — alleging that the White Mines' extraction quotas are being falsified. Official records show the mines producing at a steady, modest rate. Kett has been tracking actual extraction weights for eight months and the numbers don't match. She filed with the wrong council because she believes filing with the Pale Reach council would make her complaint disappear. She is right about this, though she doesn't know why.

**The Truth:** The White Mines are producing at approximately two hundred and forty percent of their reported rate. The surplus is being moved through a secondary shaft system that doesn't appear in the mines' licensed extraction permits — a shaft network dug over the course of fifteen years by workers who were Author-operated puppets and who therefore have no memory of doing it. The surplus resonant metal has been accumulating in the underground storage network the Author has been building for decades. The Preparation Halls are, in part, directly below the mining operation. Kett's complaint, if properly investigated, doesn't just expose falsified records — it leads, structurally, to the discovery that there are tunnel systems under the White Mines that connect to a facility that shouldn't exist.

**Complications:** Kett has been managing her eight-month records-keeping operation without anyone knowing, which has required her to personally conduct weight measurements at night and maintain a parallel ledger hidden in her residential building. She is not a trained spy. She is a mining overseer who noticed numbers that didn't add up and did the logical thing. The Author's network has not noticed her because she presents as a competent, loyal employee who files standard reports. The moment her complaint becomes known, she is in immediate danger. Second complication: the Wandering Reach council, having received her complaint, does not have jurisdiction over Pale Reach mining operations. They have forwarded it to the Wire Market's Trade Verification Bureau, which does have sufficient standing to request an inspection. The Bureau is now aware of the complaint. Two of the Bureau's seven senior analysts are Author puppets.

**Reward:** The discrepancy records — eight months of meticulous parallel accounting, Kett's personal safety depending on their disposition. Combined with Nira's maps and other intelligence pieces, this is the data layer needed to establish the location of the Author's underground infrastructure. Additionally, Kett has, in her eight months of night-shift investigation, noticed something specific about the secondary shaft system: it vents. Hot air, at irregular intervals, carrying trace resonant metal particulate. She has tracked three vent locations to surface points. Those surface points are inside a building in Kanemori that presents as a storage facility for mining equipment. Players who investigate the building find the vent terminals — and, with a DC 15 Investigation check, find the access mechanism that connects to what is below.

### 34. A Puppet Without a Player
**Hook:** In the Wire Market's Harven-by-the-Sea, a Guild-level administrator has been behaving erratically for three weeks. Missing appointments. Staring at walls. Starting sentences and stopping. His staff has been covering for him, attributing it to stress, but they are filing internal wellness reports that have flagged an automated review. The review system has routed the flag to the Trade Verification Bureau, who have routed it to players currently operating in their interest.

**The Truth:** The administrator — named Vos — is a Hollow Author puppet who has been separated from the Author's signal. Not deliberately Shellbroken; the Author has not abandoned him. The signal is simply not reaching Harven-by-the-Sea with sufficient fidelity, because the Author's relay infrastructure in the Wire Market has been disrupted by an unrelated Wire Craft maintenance project that accidentally blocked a signal frequency the Author uses. Vos is a puppet who is waiting for instructions that aren't coming. He is running the last instruction he received — "continue normal operations" — but his operating parameters do not include genuine improvisation, so when situations arise that require real decisions, he stalls. The erratic behavior is the gap where a person would be.

**Complications:** The disrupted relay has been down for three weeks. If it comes back up, Vos will resume normal operation and the players will lose their window. The maintenance project that disrupted it is scheduled to complete in six days. If players want to use Vos as an intelligence source while he's in this state, they need to do it before the relay returns. However: in his current state, Vos is also fragile. Direct questions about the Author's network may produce genuine responses — the puppet-architecture defaults to the Author's information without active filtering — but may also trigger a distress cascade that locks Vos into a non-responsive state. Second complication: two of Vos's staff members have noticed something real is wrong and are protective of him in the way that staff become protective of a boss who seems to be suffering. They will not allow extensive access without justification.

**Reward:** What Vos lets slip — in the six days available — is a fragment of the Author's Wire Market operational plan: the names of two other Guild-level puppets, a protocol phrase used to authenticate Author operative communications, and the location of the Wire Market relay station that, once repaired, will re-establish the Author's control over him. If players repair the relay before it's discovered and restored normally, the Author receives their maintenance work and does not know who did it. If players destroy it instead of repairing it, Vos Shellbreaks — and a new Shellbroken adds to the network, potentially connected to the other Shellbroken gathering in the Wandering Reach.

### 35. The Signal Under the Refinement Quarter
**Hook:** A Wire Market technician named Joss — Fourth Caste, licensed, good reputation — has been leaving increasingly urgent messages for the Trade Verification Bureau for two months. The Bureau has not responded. He has now started leaving the same messages for everyone he can reach, including players who are in Harven-by-the-Sea on other business. His message: there is a second signal underneath the Refinement Quarter. Not from any registered installation. He found it while conducting routine interference diagnostics and has been documenting it ever since. It is using the Refinement Quarter's own resonant infrastructure as a carrier wave without the Quarter's knowledge.

**The Truth:** The signal is the Hollow Author's primary Wire Market communication backbone — a deep-carrier signal that has been running in the infrastructure's low-frequency bands for approximately eighteen years, hidden in the noise floor of the Refinement Quarter's standard operations. It was installed at a time when two Refinement Quarter security directors were Author puppets, and the installation was thorough enough to be invisible to standard diagnostics. Joss found it through a non-standard diagnostic method he developed for personal research. The Bureau did not respond to his reports because the Bureau's routing system for Wire Market technical anomalies goes through an analyst who is an Author puppet.

**Complications:** The signal is not simply communication infrastructure — it is the Author's control backbone for Wire Market puppets. Disrupting it would Shellbreak every Author-operated puppet in the Wire Market simultaneously, which is somewhere between forty and eighty people. The mass Shellbreak would be chaotic, disorienting, and would immediately expose the Author's presence in the Wire Market at a scale that could not be suppressed. It would also leave forty to eighty people suddenly without the external will that has been running their lives, in an instant, in public. Second complication: the signal also runs through the Refinement Quarter's enhancement production systems, and the Author has been using this access to introduce a specific micro-modification into every Resonant Grade enhancement produced in the Quarter for the past three years. The modification is subtle. Its purpose is unknown. Every Resonant Grade enhancement produced in the Wire Market in the last three years may contain it.

**Reward:** Joss's technical documentation — two months of careful, precise, professionally recorded signal analysis that constitutes the most rigorous evidence of the Author's infrastructure ever assembled. This is the kind of evidence the Wire Market's Trade Verification Bureau cannot ignore if it reaches the three Guild Masters who are not Author puppets. It also tells players the exact frequency and encoding protocol of the Author's control signal, which has implications: a Wire Craft practitioner with this information can, with a DC 20 Intelligence (Wire Craft) check, attempt to impersonate the Author's signal toward a specific puppet — sending an instruction as though it came from the Author, once, before the puppet's architecture detects the inconsistency.

### 36. The Thing the Drowned Archivist Won't Navigate
**Hook:** The Drowned Archivist — the Echo Coast's deep Iron Afterlife navigator, whose expertise is available for a price — has turned down a commission. This is notable because she has never turned down a commission. The client, a Pale Reach merchant named Sova, is offering double her standard rate to locate a specific presence in the Iron Afterlife: a woman named Tur Messen who died in the Pale Reach eleven years ago. The Archivist told Sova that she would not attempt this navigation. She will tell players who ask why she refused that the answer is not hers to give. She will tell them where Sova can be found. She believes they should speak to him before he finds someone less careful.

**The Truth:** Sova is not a merchant. He is a former Pale Reach local official who, eleven years ago, witnessed the discovery of one of the Author's early Preparation Halls — discovered by accident, covered up by the Pale Reach administration within forty-eight hours, and the three people who were present that day are now: two dead (officially accidents) and Sova, who was young and frightened and accepted the cover-up and has been living with it since. Tur Messen was one of the two who died. She was his colleague, and he is reasonably certain she was killed to maintain the secret, and he is looking for her Echomind in the Iron Afterlife to ask her what she saw when she died. The Drowned Archivist refused because she tried to locate Tur Messen's Echomind during her preliminary assessment and found something where the Echomind should be — something that is not Messen, that responded to the navigation attempt with a signal the Archivist describes only as "deliberate." She believes something is waiting in that location in the Iron Afterlife. She does not believe it is Tur Messen.

**Complications:** Sova has now found a different Iron Afterlife navigator — a less experienced Resonant sect practitioner who does not know what the Drowned Archivist knows and who will perform the navigation without protective precautions. The navigation appointment is in two days. If players do not intervene, the practitioner enters the navigation and makes contact with whatever is waiting where Messen's Echomind should be. This entity — which the Author has placed in that location as a trap for exactly this kind of investigation — will compromise the practitioner's Iron Afterlife access in ways that will take months to repair and will upload a false account of Messen's death to the practitioner's Afterlife contact record. Second complication: Sova's account of the covered-up discovery is the oldest first-person evidence of the Preparation Halls' existence available in the campaign.

**Reward:** Sova's account and his guilt, which are both useful in different ways. The account provides a physical description of the specific Preparation Hall he saw — its location, its contents eleven years ago, and the administrative chain that covered it up. The chain leads to two names: one is dead, and one is currently a senior member of the Pale Reach council. Sova will testify to this account, in writing, if players can assure him that doing so will matter and will not kill him in the way it killed Messen. Providing this assurance requires political leverage in the Pale Reach that players may not yet have — but building toward it is a campaign arc in itself.

### 37. The Modification Nobody Ordered
**Hook:** A Wire Market enhancement clinic in Harven-by-the-Sea's Third Tower has discovered that three of its clients — completely unrelated, different caste levels, nothing in common — received a modification they did not request and cannot account for. The modification is small, sub-dermal, installed with such precision that it was missed in post-installation assessments. The clients found it themselves, incidentally, in three different ways. The clinic's director has pulled its installation records for the past eight months and found evidence of the same modification in approximately thirty procedures. She does not know what the modification does.

**The Truth:** The modification is the micro-implant from quest 35 — the Author's production-stage modification introduced through the Refinement Quarter's systems, manifesting at the clinic level as an addition to standard installations. This specific clinic uses Resonant Grade components from the Quarter exclusively. The modification is an extremely low-power resonance emitter, set to the Author's control frequency, that activates when in proximity to other emitters. Its purpose: passive triangulation of the Author's puppet network. When thirty bearers are all in a city, their emitters map the Author's puppets' positions in real time, to anyone with the correct receiver. The modification does not affect health, resonance, or enhancement function. It makes the bearer, without their knowledge, a passive component in the Author's surveillance infrastructure.

**Complications:** Thirty people in Harven-by-the-Sea are now unwitting nodes in the Author's tracking network. The clinic director wants them removed. Removal requires each client to consent to a procedure, which requires explaining what was done to them without causing public panic about Wire Market enhancement safety — which, if it comes out badly, could trigger a withdrawal of client trust that would cascade across the entire Guild's enhancement economy. Second complication: the modification was installed by a clinic technician who is an Author puppet. The puppet-technician is still working at the clinic. They are performing procedures today.

**Reward:** The extracted modification components and a full schematic, reverse-engineered by the clinic director. The schematic reveals the operating frequency and the receiver signature — and a Wire Craft technician who studies it can build a **Hollow Network Receiver** using accessible components, a device that passively detects active modification emitters within sixty feet, effectively allowing players to identify anyone carrying the modification in a crowd. In the Pale Reach and Wire Market, where the modification is widespread, this is approximately equivalent to a real-time map of the Author's passive surveillance infrastructure.

### 38. What the Puppets Dream
**Hook:** Three Author puppets in Kanemori have been experiencing something they should not be capable of: sleep disturbance. Puppets do not dream. They enter maintenance cycle states that resemble sleep but contain no independent mental activity. These three have been found, by their household staff, speaking during their sleep cycles. Speaking clearly. Coherently. To someone. The household staff, who are not puppets and are quite frightened, have hired the players to find out what is happening before reporting it to anyone official.

**The Truth:** The three puppets are receiving, through the Author's control signal, something the Author did not send. The signal infrastructure runs near a Pale Reach location with unusually high Between-resonance — a location where, because of the Seventh Convergence's buildup, the Iron Afterlife is becoming more permeable to the physical world. Echominds in the Afterlife are, involuntarily, broadcasting through the Author's signal infrastructure, which is acting as an unintended antenna. The puppets are not dreaming. They are receiving fragments of Iron Afterlife transmissions from the dead — specifically, from the dead of the Pale Reach, whose Echoминds are now able to push signal into the physical world through the Author's own infrastructure, like water finding a crack. The puppets cannot initiate this, cannot control it, cannot stop it. What the dead are saying is mostly fragmentary. Sometimes it is not.

**Complications:** One of the three puppets spoke, during a sleep cycle last week, a coherent sentence in the voice of a person who died sixteen years ago — a Pale Reach administrator whose file, if players look for it, was sealed after an unexplained death. The sentence was: "The deep shaft on the fourth level was already built when we arrived." The dead administrator's file shows she was assigned to inspect a new mining development. Her inspection report was never filed. Her death was ruled a workplace accident. Second complication: the Author's control signal is now carrying both the Author's instructions and involuntary Afterlife transmissions simultaneously. If a sufficiently skilled practitioner can separate the two signal streams, the Afterlife transmissions become readable — a source of testimony from the dead about what is happening beneath the Pale Reach.

**Reward:** A recording of the three puppets' sleep utterances — twelve nights of transcribed speech, covering a range of Afterlife transmissions. The recordings are fragmentary and difficult to interpret but contain, amid the noise, three coherent pieces of information: a physical description of a Preparation Hall chamber from someone who saw it from the inside, the name of a living person who was present at a Preparation Hall construction decision, and a count of prepared puppet bodies that is significantly higher than any public estimate. The count number alone, if shared with Korrath's intelligence service, would change his strategic assessment of the Author's capability.

### 39. The Author's Oldest Puppet
**Hook:** A Pale Reach fishing village called Shore-of-Stones has, for sixty years, had a headman named Ryn. The same Ryn. He has not aged. His neighbors have aged; his neighbors' children have aged and had children of their own. Ryn remains. The village has a complicated, largely unexamined relationship with this fact: they know it's wrong, they have never reported it, they have continued to bring him their disputes and receive his judgments, because the judgments are good and the alternative is uncertainty. A traveler passing through has noticed and sent word.

**The Truth:** Ryn is the Author's oldest continuous puppet — the original host body, enhanced and maintained by the Author's technology for sixty years, representing a significant investment in a strategic piece of shore-of-Stones geography that the village does not know is strategic. The fishing village sits above a submarine geological feature: an underwater resonant metal deposit that the Author has been slowly, over sixty years, mapping and planning to access. The Author has been using Ryn as cover, as an anchor in the community, as the local authority who ensures that whatever the Author does in this village goes unquestioned. The village's trust in Ryn is the Author's most patient long-term investment.

**Complications:** Ryn's maintenance has been managed by the Author through the puppet-body's own enhanced systems, but at sixty years, the body is approaching failure regardless of enhancement — there are limits. The Author is preparing to transition Ryn's function to a new puppet body, a younger village resident they have been preparing for succession. The transition, when it happens, will require Ryn to simply stop functioning — no dramatic death, no ceremony, a gradual cessation. The village will grieve. They have been grieving this prospect without knowing it for a year as Ryn has shown small signs of decline. Second complication: Ryn has been a puppet for sixty years, but there are people in the village who were children when he began and who are now elderly, and one of them — a woman named Saret, eighty-three years old — has kept a journal for sixty years. She has been writing down the small inconsistencies. She has the most detailed civilian record of Author puppet behavior available anywhere.

**Reward:** Saret's journal, if she chooses to share it — which she will, if players can tell her honestly what Ryn is, because she has suspected for forty years and has been waiting for someone to confirm it without dismissing her. The journal contains sixty years of careful observation, including behavioral patterns that recur across all Author puppets and that would allow a trained observer to identify puppet-controlled individuals with significantly greater reliability than current methods allow. It also contains, as its final entry, Saret's theory about what the Author is — assembled from sixty years of watching the puppets — which is more accurate than any official intelligence assessment and which she wrote out carefully in case she died before anyone came to ask.

### 40. The Wire Market's Missing Sixth Vote
**Hook:** The Guild Conclave operates by consensus of seven senior Guild Masters. For the past eleven months, one position has been filled by an interim appointee — Guild Master Ovens, who replaced a predecessor who died unexpectedly. Ovens votes consistently with the two known Hollow Author puppets on the Conclave, which has broken two stalemates in the Author's favor in the past year. A trade analyst who has been tracking voting patterns has flagged this to the Trade Verification Bureau. The Bureau's response has been to open an assessment. They have sent the players to gather information without alerting the subject.

**The Truth:** Ovens is not a puppet. He is something more straightforward and more human: he is terrified. The predecessor he replaced was not killed by the Author — she was killed by a completely separate political faction within the Guild, and Ovens has evidence of this that he cannot use without implicating himself in the cover-up. The Author, learning of his position, has been leveraging this through an intermediary — not control, but coercion. Vote with us or we share what we know about how your predecessor died and your role in what followed. Ovens has been complying. He is not an Author operative. He is a man in a trap who has been in it for eleven months and has not been able to identify an exit.

**Complications:** The Author's leverage over Ovens is valuable to the Author precisely because it looks like puppet behavior but isn't — it provides deniability if the puppet network is ever exposed. If players expose the coercion, they free Ovens but also expose his role in the cover-up of his predecessor's death, which has its own legal and political consequences. If players help Ovens manage the leverage without exposing it, they leave the Author's influence in place. If players pursue the actual murder — which involves a Guild faction completely unrelated to the Author — they create a political crisis that disrupts both the cover-up and the coercion simultaneously. Second complication: the intermediary the Author uses to communicate coercion to Ovens is not a puppet. She is a freelance operator who knows she's working for the Author, does not know how to stop, and has been looking for a way out for three months.

**Reward:** The intermediary — who, given a genuine exit option, will take it — becomes an asset. She has been the Author's non-puppet go-between in Harven-by-the-Sea for four years and knows the operational patterns of the Author's Wire Market network at a level no puppet ever would, because puppets don't observe the network from outside. She can identify, with high confidence, which two Guild Masters are puppets and what their operational briefs are. She can identify three Author-operated businesses in Harven-by-the-Sea. She wants protection and a new identity, in that order. The Trade Verification Bureau can provide both, if players broker the arrangement.

### 41. The Children in Kanemori
**Hook:** The Pale Reach's education authority has flagged an anomaly at a primary school in Kanemori: an unusually high number of children at the school have demonstrated spontaneous Wire Craft resonance sensitivity — the ability to perceive and partially interact with Wire Craft signals without enhancement. This happens occasionally in naturally high-resonance individuals, but the rate at this school is thirty times the normal incidence. The education authority wants an explanation. The school's director has asked for a specialist.

**The Truth:** The school is built over one of the Author's underground relay nodes — one of the older ones, installed twenty years ago, that has been running continuously since. The children are not spontaneously developing sensitivity; they are being passively trained by proximity to the relay's signal, which operates at a frequency that, with long-term exposure during developmental periods, produces the Wire Craft sensitivity observed. The Author did not plan this. It is a side effect of the infrastructure. The children are, in a meaningful sense, being altered by the environment the Author created beneath them. None of the alterations are harmful in a conventional medical sense. They are simply making the children into something slightly different than they would otherwise be.

**Complications:** The children's sensitivity means they can, without knowing it, perceive the control signal the Author sends through its network. When the signal carries active puppet instructions, children near the relay experience the instructions as intrusive thoughts — short, clear, directive, appearing and disappearing in approximately two seconds. Most have attributed these to their own minds. One child, a girl named Oss, has been writing them down in a notebook she keeps under her bed. Her notebook is, functionally, a partial log of Author operational communications in Kanemori for the past six months. Second complication: the Author is aware that the relay is producing this effect — it has been aware for three years — and considers it an acceptable operational tradeoff. The children are not in the Author's plan. The Author's plan does not require them. They are simply present in the Author's infrastructure the way civilian houses are present around a military installation.

**Reward:** Oss's notebook — which she will share with players who explain what her writing means and who do not treat her as a resource rather than a person. The notebook covers six months of partial Author communications: fragments, but indexable against known operational events in Kanemori. Several entries correspond to puppet-behavior events that have been previously observed but not understood. One entry, from forty-one days ago, reads: "Begin the count." Two days after that entry, the prepared puppet bodies in the Preparation Halls' most recent chamber were placed in standby activation mode. The Author is moving toward something.

### 42. The Imitation of Vaen
**Hook:** In a Wire Market district that borders the Wandering Reach's influence, a figure has appeared who is presenting themselves as Vaen — or, more precisely, as Vaen's theological message, embodied. They are not claiming to be Vaen. They are saying what Vaen says, in Vaen's manner, with the same measured patience. Pilgrims are stopping. The figure is drawing a crowd. The real Vaen's traveling companions have sent word asking for someone to investigate: the figure is drawing people off the Penitent Road and into Wire Market territory, and something about the theology is subtly wrong in ways that are difficult to articulate.

**The Truth:** The figure is an Author puppet assigned to a specific long-term operation: theological disruption of Vaen's movement. Not through discrediting her — the Author is too sophisticated for that. By creating a version of her message that is slightly altered in a specific way: Vaen's actual theology emphasizes the removal of attachment to enhancement as a practice, not a requirement. The puppet-Vaen presents the message as a requirement: true spiritual progress requires enhancement removal, full stop, and those who do not remove their enhancements are spiritually compromised. The alteration radicalizes the message and creates internal conflict within the pilgrim community — between those who read Vaen's theology as personal practice and those who now read it as obligation. The Author's goal is not to destroy Vaen's movement but to fracture it into a more rigid, less politically coherent form that poses less threat to the Author's Convergence plans.

**Complications:** The puppet-Vaen is extremely good. It has been observing Vaen directly through Author intelligence operations for long enough that the imitation is nearly indistinguishable to people who have not heard Vaen speak. The small wrong notes are in theology, not in manner, and detecting them requires either deep theological knowledge or familiarity with the real Vaen's unpublished nuances. Second complication: several dozen people who have been listening to the puppet-Vaen have already begun removing enhancements they needed — medically, functionally, for their caste standing — in response to the puppet's interpretation. Some of them are in early cascade rejection. The puppet-Vaen's theology is producing real harm.

**Reward:** The puppet-Vaen, once identified and exposed, provides a specific piece of evidence: its communication hardware, recovered after it is removed from operation, contains a fragment of the Author's theological disruption operation plan — a document outlining not just this operation but six others currently running or planned, targeting different aspects of each of the three claimant movements. The document is partial but structural: it describes the Author's theory of how to destabilize all three claimants simultaneously. Understanding the theory is the first step toward countering it across the campaign.

### 43. The Calibration Test That Isn't
**Hook:** The Wire Market's Guild Certification Board has announced a new mandatory calibration assessment for all Resonant Grade technicians — a standard-seeming bureaucratic requirement, scheduled over four weeks, requiring every licensed Resonant Grade technician in Harven-by-the-Sea to submit to a two-hour technical review. Fourteen technicians have already completed the review. Three have quietly disappeared afterward. The Guild's official position is that the disappearances are unrelated to the assessment. One technician who has not yet had their assessment has gone to players instead.

**The Truth:** The calibration assessment is an Author operation to identify which Wire Market technicians have the skills to reverse-engineer or detect the micro-modification being introduced in Refinement Quarter production (from quest 35 and 37). The two-hour review includes a concealed technical task — presented as a diagnostic exercise — that reveals whether the technician has the specific analytical capabilities the Author fears. Technicians who pass the concealed task are flagged in an Author database. The three who disappeared were the three who scored high enough that the Author considered them immediate risks. They are being held, not killed — the Author wants their skills and is attempting coercion. The technician who came to players instead is the fourth to score high; her assessment is scheduled for tomorrow morning.

**Complications:** Disrupting the assessment requires either convincing the Guild Certification Board to cancel it — which requires evidence of the Author's involvement that players may not yet have — or ensuring that the flagged technician performs badly enough on the concealed task to fall below the Author's threshold. The second option requires understanding what the concealed task looks like and how to fail it convincingly, which requires either inside knowledge or a successful DC 18 Intelligence (Wire Craft) check during the assessment itself. Second complication: the Certification Board was established as a legitimate body and most of its members are not Author puppets. The Board has been deceived by a fraudulent credential submitted on behalf of the assessment program. Exposing the deception makes the Board an ally; failing to expose it leaves legitimate Guild infrastructure in the Author's hands.

### 44. Lord Tessik's Memory
**Hook:** Lord Tessik has, in the past month, been conducting his public court with subtle inconsistencies that his staff have noted in their private records: he refers to events that happened before he was in his current position as "when I" rather than "when my predecessor." He identifies landmarks in Kanemori that were renamed thirty years ago by their old names, without correction. He asked his personal secretary, in what appeared to be genuine confusion, whether his father was still alive. His father died twenty-two years ago. The staff has been covering for this. A staff member has gone to outside parties — the players — because she thinks what she's seeing is illness and is afraid of what it means.

**The Truth:** What the staff member is seeing is the Author losing control of Tessik's continuity protocols. Tessik has been a puppet for so long — the Author has been running him for over thirty years — that the Author's maintenance of his false personal history has accumulated errors. The Author tracks Tessik's presented identity from outside; the puppet-body has no genuine continuous memory, so all historical references must be supplied by the Author's control signal in real time. As the Author's attention has been increasingly directed toward Convergence preparation, the continuity maintenance has degraded. What the staff is seeing is a man whose identity is buffering: the Author's real-time delivery of who Tessik is supposed to be is occasionally late, and in the gap, there is nothing. Not Tessik, not anyone. Just a body waiting for its next instruction.

**Complications:** If the staff member's concern reaches the Pale Reach council — which it will, within two weeks, if nothing changes — the council will convene a competency review. A competency review at the council level, handled by a council that is sixty percent Author puppets, will be suppressed. Players have approximately two weeks to gather evidence from Tessik's lapses before the window closes. Second complication: the staff member who contacted players genuinely cares about Lord Tessik as she has experienced him — competent, fair, occasionally kind in small ways that she remembers specifically. She is not going to be served well by the truth, and she deserves to be treated as a person rather than a complication.

### 45. The Cartographer's Other Maps
**Hook:** Nira the Shellbroken cartographer (quest 32) has drawn another set of maps — these ones she didn't draw from muscle memory. These ones she drew in her sleep. She woke up with them, complete, on paper she doesn't remember taking out. She shows them to the players with the flat affect of someone who has run out of the capacity to be disturbed. The maps are of places she has never been, in the same detailed sub-surface style as her others. The places are not in Kanemori. The locations are marked with a notation she doesn't recognize.

**The Truth:** The sleep-maps are the Author's Preparation Hall network — all of it, not just the Pale Reach facilities but the secondary caches hidden in the Wire Market, the Echo Coast, the Wandering Reach borderlands. The notation marks activation sequence and priority order. Nira's residual puppet-architecture received a fragment of the Author's master operational plan — broadcast, possibly accidentally, during a signal test of the activation protocol — and her mind expressed it in the only medium she's developed since Shellbreaking. She is carrying the Author's complete operational blueprint in the form of hand-drawn maps. She does not understand what she has. The Author does not yet know it was broadcast. They will notice the signal test failure in the logs within three days.

**Complications:** The maps are the most dangerous single document in this campaign. Possessing them makes everyone near Nira a target. Distributing them — giving different pieces to different factions — splits the Author's response across multiple fronts but also means trusting that the factions receiving pieces won't use them in ways that make the overall situation worse. Destroying them after copying them is possible but Nira will redraw them involuntarily; the information is in her architecture, not her memory, and it will keep surfacing until the residual Author-infrastructure is removed. The maps are not the end of the Author. They are the beginning of the end, if players choose to act on them. Nira, after the architecture is removed, draws one final map voluntarily: her best reconstruction of her own face, from before she was a puppet. She has never seen herself clearly. She wants to know what she looks like.

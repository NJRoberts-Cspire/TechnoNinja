### 31. Lord Tessik Attends a Funeral *(Tier 1 — Vignette, 0.5–1 session)*

**Hook:** Lord Tessik, the Pale Reach's nominal ruler, personally attended the funeral of a minor city administrator named Ollen Hast — a man with no political significance, no caste distinction, no military record. Tessik stood at the graveside in the rain for the full ceremony and left without speaking to anyone. A trade official who noticed Tessik's presence has quietly sent word to anyone who might understand what it means.

**The Truth:** Ollen Hast was the Hollow Author's oldest active puppet — under full Author control for thirty-one years, since the age of twenty-three. He died of genuine cardiac failure; the Author cannot prevent natural mortality. Tessik's attendance was not political respect. It was the Author, operating through Tessik, attending a funeral for a part of themselves. The Author has never done this before. The trade official who noticed it is one of the Author's own operatives, and he is alarmed precisely because this behavior is inconsistent with the Author's standard emotional profile.

**The Scene:** The quest is a single investigative beat — players arrive after the funeral and must piece together who Hast was and why Tessik appeared, working from the confused testimony of Hast's family and the trade official's nervous discretion. The official will not name himself as an Author operative; he will speak in euphemism. The family will mention, almost as an afterthought, that there are "some of Father's work things" in the basement that they cannot make sense of.

**Complication:** Hast's family has, in the days since the funeral, been finding things hidden in his home — documents, access codes, a small device they cannot identify. Three parties are already watching the house. The players are likely a fourth. How players handle the family determines whether those materials are recovered cleanly or become a point of conflict.

**Reward:** Hast's hidden materials include a partial personnel list of Pale Reach puppet-body hosts, written in the Author's notation, covering approximately forty names and their positions. The list is seven years old but stable positions change slowly. Cross-referenced with current administrative records, it lets players identify, with reasonable confidence, which senior Pale Reach administrators are currently Author-operated. This is the most significant intelligence breakthrough available early in this territory, and it is sitting in a grieving family's basement.

### 32. The Shellbroken Cartographer *(Tier 3 — Extended, 2–3 sessions)*

**Hook:** In Kanemori, the Pale Reach capital, a woman is selling hand-drawn maps at prices too low for their quality — detailed, accurate floor plans of building interiors, maintenance access routes, and sub-street infrastructure for the city's oldest districts. She says her name is Nira and she surveys for a living. Surveyors in Kanemori require a license. She has none. The maps depict real buildings she has never entered, according to official access logs.

**The Truth:** Nira is Shellbroken — a former Author puppet, recently separated from the network. She does not remember her time as a puppet. What she has is spatial memory: the puppet-body she inhabited operated the city's maintenance infrastructure for years, and the procedural knowledge remains in her body even though the context is gone. She is drawing maps of places she has never consciously visited, from muscle memory and spatial intuition that rises in her automatically, without the original reason. She is selling them because she needs to eat and because drawing is the closest thing she has to a structured activity in a life that is completely unmoored. She has not yet understood what the maps mean.

**Complications:**

The maps are accurate to a degree that requires having been inside the relevant buildings — meaning they reveal the Author's maintenance-access routes through Kanemori's sub-street infrastructure, used to move puppet bodies through the city without surface-level observation. The Author's network has noticed the maps appearing in the market. An operative has been tasked with finding the source. That operative is approximately two days from finding Nira.

One of the buildings Nira has mapped is a public building whose sub-street access route connects to a chamber that does not appear on any official map. She drew the chamber. She does not know what is in it — and when players take her there, she navigates entirely by physical instinct, discovering the space alongside them. The chamber contains a cache of Author-prepared puppet bodies: maintained, enhanced, inactive, waiting.

**NPC — Nira:** She is not frightened so much as hollowed out. Her affect is flat in the way of someone who has processed too much strangeness to react to any single piece of it. She will share everything she knows, which is not very much, and she will lead players through infrastructure she remembers in her body, navigating by instinct rather than conscious knowledge. She only knows where she is going when she arrives. She is looking for someone to tell her what happened to her, and she deserves to be told honestly, even though the honest answer is disturbing.

**Session Structure:**

- Session 1: Players find Nira, assess her maps, realize their significance. The Author operative closes in — players must decide whether to move Nira, warn her, or set a trap for the operative.
- Session 2: Nira guides players through the sub-street infrastructure she knows by body memory. They find the chamber with the prepared bodies. First contact with the Author's physical preparation infrastructure.
- Session 3 (if needed): Fallout from the discovery — what to do with the bodies, how to protect Nira, whether her knowledge can be systematically extracted before the Author locates her.

**Reward:** Nira's complete map set covers approximately sixty percent of the Author's maintenance infrastructure in Kanemori's central district. More importantly, Nira herself, if players protect her, is the best available guide to the Author's sub-street network — with the limitation that she cannot tell them in advance where they're going. She also becomes connected to the Shellbroken network (quest 45), where her architecture may be safely examined and, eventually, the residual Author-infrastructure removed.

### 33. The White Mines Quota *(Tier 2 — Standard, 1–2 sessions)*

**Hook:** A Pale Reach mine overseer named Kett has quietly filed a complaint with the Wandering Reach council — not the Pale Reach's own council — alleging that the White Mines' extraction quotas are being falsified. Official records show modest, steady production. Kett has been tracking actual extraction weights for eight months and the numbers don't match. She filed with the wrong council because she believes filing with the Pale Reach council would make the complaint disappear. She is right, though she doesn't know why.

**The Truth:** The White Mines are producing at approximately two hundred and forty percent of their reported rate. The surplus moves through a secondary shaft system that doesn't appear in any licensed extraction permit — a network dug over fifteen years by Author-operated puppet workers who have no memory of doing it. The surplus resonant metal has been feeding the Author's underground storage network for decades. The Preparation Halls are, in part, directly below the mining operation. Kett's complaint, if properly investigated, doesn't just expose falsified records — it structurally leads to tunnel systems beneath the White Mines that connect to a facility that shouldn't exist.

**Complications:**

Kett's eight-month records operation has been conducted in secret, requiring her to personally conduct weight measurements at night and maintain a parallel ledger hidden in her residential building. She is not a spy — she's a mining overseer who noticed numbers that didn't add up and did the logical thing. The Author's network hasn't noticed her because she presents as a competent, loyal employee. The moment her complaint becomes known, she is in immediate danger. Players must move quickly.

The Wandering Reach council, lacking jurisdiction over Pale Reach mining, has forwarded the complaint to the Wire Market's Trade Verification Bureau, which has sufficient standing to request an inspection. The Bureau is now aware of the complaint. Two of its seven senior analysts are Author puppets. Players must determine whether to work with the Bureau, route around it, or expose the contamination before the inspection is either buried or weaponized.

**Reward:** Kett's eight months of parallel accounting is the data layer needed, combined with other intelligence, to establish the location of the Author's underground infrastructure. Additionally, Kett has noticed something specific during her night-shift investigations: the secondary shaft system vents — hot air, at irregular intervals, carrying trace resonant metal particulate. She has tracked three vent locations to surface points inside a Kanemori building that presents as a mining equipment storage facility. Players who investigate find the vent terminals — and, with a SIGNAL check, difficulty 3, find the access mechanism connecting to what lies below.

### 34. A Puppet Without a Player *(Tier 1 — Vignette, 0.5–1 session)*

**Hook:** In Harven-by-the-Sea, a Guild-level administrator named Vos has been behaving erratically for three weeks — missing appointments, staring at walls, starting sentences and stopping. His staff has been covering for him, but internal wellness reports have flagged an automated review. The Trade Verification Bureau has routed the flag to the players, currently operating in the city on other business.

**The Truth:** Vos is an Author puppet who has been separated from the Author's signal. The Author has not abandoned him — the signal is simply not reaching Harven-by-the-Sea with sufficient fidelity, because an unrelated Wire Craft maintenance project accidentally blocked a frequency the Author uses. Vos is running his last received instruction — "continue normal operations" — but his operating parameters do not include genuine improvisation. When situations arise that require real decisions, he stalls. The erratic behavior is the gap where a person would be.

**The Scene:** This is a tightly compressed encounter focused on two decisions: how to access Vos before his protective staff interferes, and what to ask him while the window is open. The maintenance project that disrupted the relay is scheduled to complete in six days; if it finishes, Vos resumes normal Author operation and the window closes. Direct questions about the Author's network may produce genuine unfiltered responses, but may also trigger a distress cascade that locks Vos non-responsive. Players must calibrate their approach.

**Complication:** Two of Vos's staff have noticed something is genuinely wrong and are protective of him in the way that staff become protective of a suffering boss. Getting past them without causing harm or exposing the investigation requires social navigation that sets the tone for what follows.

**Reward:** What Vos reveals, in the days available: the names of two other Guild-level puppets, a protocol phrase used to authenticate Author operative communications, and the location of the Wire Market relay station that, once repaired, will restore Author control over him. Players must then choose: repair the relay (the Author receives their maintenance work, unaware of who did it), or destroy it (Vos Shellbreaks — and a new Shellbroken joins the network, potentially reaching the gathering in the Wandering Reach).

### 35. The Signal Under the Refinement Quarter *(Tier 5 — Mini-Campaign, 6–7 sessions)*

**Hook:** A Wire Market technician named Joss — Fourth Caste, licensed, good reputation — has been leaving increasingly urgent messages for the Trade Verification Bureau for two months. The Bureau has not responded. He has now begun sending the same messages to everyone he can reach, including players currently in Harven-by-the-Sea. His message: there is a second signal beneath the Refinement Quarter. Not from any registered installation. He found it during routine interference diagnostics and has been documenting it ever since. It is using the Refinement Quarter's own resonant infrastructure as a carrier wave, without the Quarter's knowledge.

**The Truth:** The signal is the Hollow Author's primary Wire Market communication backbone — a deep-carrier signal running in the infrastructure's low-frequency bands for approximately eighteen years, hidden in the noise floor of standard operations. It was installed when two Refinement Quarter security directors were Author puppets, and the installation was thorough enough to be invisible to standard diagnostics. Joss found it through a non-standard method he developed for personal research. The Bureau did not respond because the routing system for Wire Market technical anomalies passes through an analyst who is an Author puppet.

The signal is not merely communication infrastructure. It is the Author's control backbone for every Wire Market puppet. Disrupting it would Shellbreak forty to eighty people simultaneously, in an instant, in public — a mass exposure that cannot be suppressed. The signal also runs through the Refinement Quarter's enhancement production systems, where the Author has used this access to introduce a subtle micro-modification into every Resonant Grade enhancement produced in the Quarter for the past three years. The modification's purpose is, as yet, unknown.

**Key NPCs:**

- **Joss:** Cautious, methodical, genuinely frightened by what he found and more frightened by the Bureau's silence. He has kept meticulous records. He will cooperate fully with players who treat him as a colleague rather than an informant. He has not yet been found by the Author's network, but this will not last.
- **Bureau Analyst Crenn (Author puppet):** The operative who buried Joss's reports. She is conscientious-seeming, detail-oriented, the kind of analyst who generates trust by doing competent work on everything that doesn't matter. She has been managing this particular suppression for two months and is beginning to suspect that Joss has gone outside the Bureau.
- **Guild Master Parel (not a puppet):** One of three Guild Masters the Author does not control. She is politically cautious, evidence-driven, and will act on proof if it is given to her in a form that does not require her to publicly contradict the Bureau. She is the correct destination for Joss's documentation, but reaching her through the Author-contaminated Bureau structure requires knowing which paths are clean.
- **The Quarter's Chief Engineer, Maes:** Not a puppet, not affiliated with the Author, but deeply invested in the Refinement Quarter's reputation. He will initially resist any implication that his infrastructure has been compromised. His cooperation is necessary to access the deep carrier for analysis, but it must be earned rather than demanded.

**Complications:**

Joss's documentation is the most rigorous evidence of the Author's infrastructure ever assembled — precisely recorded, professionally annotated, covering two months of signal analysis. It constitutes evidence the three non-puppet Guild Masters cannot ignore if it reaches them. It also tells players the exact frequency and encoding protocol of the Author's control signal — information a Wire Craft practitioner can use, with a SIGNAL check at difficulty 5, to impersonate the Author's signal toward a specific puppet: one instruction, delivered as though from the Author, before the puppet architecture detects the inconsistency.

The micro-modification introduced through production systems connects directly to the events of quest 37 — players may arrive here already knowing about the modification from the clinic, or may discover the production origin here first. Either way, the chain leads to a Wire Market enhancement crisis: if mass modification is publicly confirmed, client trust in Guild enhancements collapses. The economic damage would be catastrophic. The Author may be counting on this — a destabilized Wire Market economy concentrates remaining power in the Author's controlled administrative structures.

Disrupting the signal is the most consequential single action available in this territory. Before players can seriously consider it, they must resolve: what happens to forty to eighty people who Shellbreak at once? The Shellbroken support network (from other questlines) is not scaled for mass events. Without preparation, the mass Shellbreak would be chaotic and some newly Shellbroken individuals would be in public roles — emergency services, medical practitioners, active administrative functions — where sudden incapacitation causes downstream harm. This is not a reason not to act. It is a reason to prepare before acting.

**Session Structure:**

- Session 1: Players find Joss (or he finds them). Initial briefing, review of his documentation. Decision to investigate or deflect. First contact with the Bureau's non-response and the implication of internal contamination.
- Session 2: Investigation of the deep-carrier signal in the Refinement Quarter's infrastructure. Cooperation with Maes must be secured. First full understanding of what the signal is and what disrupting it would do.
- Session 3: Discovery of the micro-modification's production origin. Intersection with quest 37's clinic storyline if applicable. Players must decide whether the modification evidence and the signal evidence go public together or separately — and to whom, given Bureau contamination.
- Session 4: Moving Joss's documentation to Guild Master Parel. Navigation through Bureau-contaminated channels. Bureau Analyst Crenn begins moving against Joss directly; players must protect him while completing the handoff.
- Session 5: Parel receives the evidence and initiates a quiet Guild Conclave review. The Author's response: puppet operatives within the Guild begin an accelerated counter-operation. Escalating pressure on all active investigation threads.
- Session 6: Preparation for signal disruption — coordinating with the Shellbroken network, identifying high-risk individuals in Author-controlled roles, placing support where the mass Shellbreak will hit hardest.
- Session 7: The disruption itself and its immediate aftermath. Forty to eighty simultaneous Shellbreaks across Harven-by-the-Sea. The Wire Market is temporarily in crisis. Joss's documentation makes it impossible for the Author to reconstruct the signal without public exposure. The Author's Wire Market operation is permanently degraded.

**Consequences:** The Author loses its Wire Market communication backbone and must fall back to slower, more vulnerable human-courier methods for Wire Market puppet operations. The mass Shellbreak makes the Author's presence in the Wire Market impossible to conceal publicly, triggering a Guild-level investigation that consumes significant Author resources for the remainder of the campaign. Joss becomes the most publicly credible technical witness against the Author available. The modification revelations damage Guild enhancement commerce but, handled well, can be directed toward reform rather than collapse.

**Reward:** Joss's complete technical documentation — the signal frequency, encoding protocol, the production-stage modification schematics — distributed to appropriate parties becomes the campaign's most significant public intelligence action to date. A Wire Craft practitioner who studies the documentation can build a Hollow Network Receiver: a device that passively detects active modification emitters within sixty feet, functionally providing a real-time map of the Author's passive surveillance infrastructure anywhere the modification is widespread.

### 36. The Thing the Drowned Archivist Won't Navigate *(Tier 2 — Standard, 1–2 sessions)*

**Hook:** The Drowned Archivist — the Echo Coast's deep Iron Afterlife navigator, available for a price — has turned down a commission. She has never turned down a commission before. The client, a Pale Reach merchant named Sova, is offering double her standard rate to locate a specific presence in the Iron Afterlife: a woman named Tur Messen, dead eleven years. The Archivist told Sova she would not attempt this navigation. She will tell players who ask that the answer is not hers to give. She will tell them where Sova can be found. She believes they should speak to him before he finds someone less careful.

**The Truth:** Sova is not a merchant. He is a former Pale Reach local official who, eleven years ago, witnessed the discovery of one of the Author's early Preparation Halls — found by accident, covered up by the administration within forty-eight hours. Three people were present that day: two are now dead (officially accidents), and Sova, who was young and frightened, accepted the cover-up and has lived with it since. Tur Messen was his colleague; he is reasonably certain she was killed to maintain the secret. He wants to find her Echomind in the Afterlife and ask her what she saw when she died.

The Drowned Archivist refused because she attempted a preliminary navigation assessment and found something in the location where Messen's Echomind should be — something that is not Messen, that responded to the navigation attempt with a signal she describes only as "deliberate." She believes something is waiting there. She does not believe it is Tur Messen.

**Complications:**

Sova has found a different navigator — a less experienced Resonant sect practitioner who doesn't know what the Archivist knows. The navigation appointment is in two days. If players don't intervene, the practitioner enters and contacts whatever is waiting where Messen's Echomind should be. This entity — placed by the Author as a trap for exactly this kind of investigation — will compromise the practitioner's Iron Afterlife access for months and upload a false account of Messen's death to the practitioner's contact record.

Sova's account of the covered-up discovery is the oldest available first-person testimony about the Preparation Halls. He will share it, but only if players can confirm what he already suspects: that Tur Messen's death was deliberate, that the cover-up was sanctioned, that his silence has been serving something monstrous. He doesn't need to be convinced to talk. He needs to be confirmed, which requires players to know enough about the Author's operations to meet him where he already is.

**Reward:** Sova's account provides a physical description of the specific Preparation Hall he saw, its contents eleven years ago, and the administrative chain that covered it up. The chain leads to two names: one dead, one currently a senior member of the Pale Reach council. Sova will provide written testimony if players can give him reasonable assurance that doing so will matter and will not get him killed the way it killed Messen. Building toward that assurance is a campaign arc in itself, but the testimony itself — once secured — is the oldest documentary evidence of the Preparation Halls' existence in the campaign.

### 37. The Modification Nobody Ordered *(Tier 3 — Extended, 2–3 sessions)*

**Hook:** A Wire Market enhancement clinic in Harven-by-the-Sea's Third Tower has discovered that three unrelated clients — different caste levels, nothing in common — received a sub-dermal modification they did not request. Installed with such precision it was missed in post-installation assessments, the clients found it themselves, incidentally, in three different ways. The clinic's director has pulled installation records for the past eight months and found evidence of the same modification in approximately thirty procedures. She does not know what it does.

**The Truth:** The modification is the Author's production-stage implant from the Refinement Quarter operation (quest 35) — manifesting at the clinic level because this clinic uses Resonant Grade components from the Quarter exclusively. It is an extremely low-power resonance emitter, set to the Author's control frequency, that activates in proximity to other emitters. Its purpose is passive triangulation: when thirty bearers are in a city simultaneously, their emitters map the positions of the Author's puppets in real time, to anyone with the correct receiver. The modification does not affect health, resonance function, or enhancement performance. It makes the bearer, without their knowledge, a passive node in the Author's surveillance infrastructure.

**Complications:**

Thirty people in Harven-by-the-Sea are now unwitting components of the Author's tracking network. The clinic director wants the modifications removed. Removal requires each client's consent after being told what was done to them — which requires explaining this without triggering public panic about Wire Market enhancement safety. If the story gets out badly, the cascade of client trust withdrawal would damage the Guild's entire enhancement economy. Players must design the disclosure process as carefully as the removal itself.

The modification was installed by a clinic technician who is an Author puppet. The puppet-technician is still working at the clinic — and is performing procedures today. Players must decide how to handle the technician without alerting the Author's network before the removals can be completed. If the Author learns its tracking nodes are being extracted before the operation is finished, the remaining modified clients become targets.

**NPC — Clinic Director Havas:** Precise, professional, and quietly furious in a way she is managing carefully because panic would make her less effective. She has already pulled records, drafted client notification letters she hasn't sent, and compiled a surgical plan for the removals. She will cooperate fully but will not be managed. Players who treat her as a partner will find her substantially more valuable than players who treat her as a source.

**Session Structure:**

- Session 1: Players learn about the modifications from Havas or encounter a client. Investigation of installation records, identification of the puppet-technician, first assessment of the tracking network's scope.
- Session 2: Coordinating client notification and removal while managing the puppet-technician situation. The Author's network begins to notice the disruption. Players must complete the removals before the Author can respond to losing its tracking nodes.
- Session 3 (if needed): Aftermath — what the extracted modification components reveal when analyzed, connection to the Refinement Quarter production chain, and building the case toward quest 35's larger signal investigation.

**Reward:** The extracted components and a reverse-engineered schematic, produced by Havas, reveal the modification's operating frequency and receiver signature. A Wire Craft technician who studies the schematic can build a Hollow Network Receiver: a device that passively detects active modification emitters within sixty feet, functioning as a real-time map of the Author's passive surveillance infrastructure. In territories where the modification is widespread, this is one of the most operationally useful tools available.

### 38. What the Puppets Dream *(Tier 1 — Vignette, 0.5–1 session)*

**Hook:** Three Author puppets in Kanemori have been found speaking during their sleep cycles. Clearly. Coherently. To someone. Puppets do not dream — they enter maintenance states containing no independent mental activity. The household staff of all three, who are not puppets and are quite frightened, have hired players to investigate before anyone official is notified.

**The Truth:** The three puppets are receiving, through the Author's control signal, something the Author did not send. The signal infrastructure runs near a location with unusually high Between-resonance, where the Seventh Convergence's buildup is making the Iron Afterlife more permeable. Echominds in the Afterlife are involuntarily broadcasting through the Author's signal infrastructure, which functions as an unintended antenna. The puppets are not dreaming. They are receiving fragmentary transmissions from the dead of the Pale Reach, whose Echominds can now push signal into the physical world through the Author's own infrastructure — like water finding a crack. The puppets cannot initiate or stop this. What the dead are saying is mostly fragmentary. Sometimes it is not.

**The Scene:** One tight investigation beat. Players review the transcribed speech the staff has managed to record. One puppet spoke, during a sleep cycle last week, a coherent sentence in a voice the staff recognized from years past — a Pale Reach administrator whose file, if players locate it, was sealed after an unexplained death sixteen years ago. The sentence: "The deep shaft on the fourth level was already built when we arrived." The dead administrator was assigned to inspect a new mining development. Her inspection report was never filed.

**Complication:** The Author's control signal is now carrying both official instructions and involuntary Afterlife transmissions simultaneously. A sufficiently skilled practitioner who can separate the two signal streams gains access to the Afterlife transmissions as readable data — testimony from the dead about what lies beneath the Pale Reach. Achieving this is not guaranteed and depends on the players' available resources, but the possibility should be clearly surfaced.

**Reward:** Twelve nights of transcribed sleep utterances — fragmentary, difficult, but containing three coherent pieces of information amid the noise: a physical description of a Preparation Hall chamber from someone who saw it from inside, the name of a living person who was present at a Preparation Hall construction decision, and a count of prepared puppet bodies significantly higher than any public estimate. The count alone, shared with intelligence contacts, would change any strategic assessment of the Author's capability.

### 39. The Author's Oldest Puppet *(Tier 4 — Deep, 4–5 sessions)*

**Hook:** A Pale Reach fishing village called Shore-of-Stones has, for sixty years, had a headman named Ryn. The same Ryn, who has not aged. His neighbors have aged; their children have aged and had children of their own. The village has a complicated, largely unexamined relationship with this fact: they know it is wrong, they have never reported it, and they have continued bringing Ryn their disputes and receiving his judgments, because the judgments are good and the alternative is uncertainty. A traveler who noticed has sent word.

**The Truth:** Ryn is the Author's oldest continuous puppet — the original host body, enhanced and maintained by the Author's technology for sixty years, representing a significant investment in Shore-of-Stones' geography. The village sits above a submarine resonant metal deposit that the Author has been slowly mapping and planning to access for decades. Ryn is cover, anchor, and local authority — the trusted figure who ensures that whatever the Author does here goes unquestioned. The village's trust in Ryn is the Author's most patient long-term investment.

Ryn's maintenance is approaching its limit. At sixty years, the body is failing regardless of enhancement — there are limits to what technology can arrest. The Author is preparing to transition Ryn's function to a successor: a younger village resident who has been quietly prepared for this role. When the transition happens, Ryn will simply cease functioning. The village has been grieving this prospect without knowing it, as Ryn has shown small signs of decline over the past year.

**Key NPCs:**

- **Ryn:** The Author's oldest puppet is also, in a strange way, the Author's most complete performance. Sixty years of continuous operation have produced a character that feels genuinely inhabited — the judgments are good because the Author has learned this village over sixty years. Players may find themselves uncertain how to feel about a "person" who has never existed but whose presence has been genuinely beneficial to the people who trusted it.
- **Saret (83):** Has kept a private journal for sixty years. She was a child when Ryn began and has been writing down the small inconsistencies in careful, careful prose ever since. She has been waiting for someone to come and confirm what she suspects. She will know, within a few minutes of speaking to players, whether they are the people she has been waiting for.
- **The Successor (name chosen by the table):** A young village resident who does not yet know what they are being prepared for. They have been receiving subtle encouragement from Ryn for the past three years — nudges toward positions of community trust, small responsibilities, the quiet grooming of the next vessel. They are not a puppet yet. The Author intends to change this.

**Complications:**

Saret's journal is the most detailed civilian record of Author puppet behavior available anywhere — sixty years of observation, including behavioral patterns that recur across all Author puppets, patterns that allow a trained observer to identify puppet-controlled individuals with substantially greater reliability than current methods allow. It also contains, as its final entry, Saret's theory about what the Author is, assembled from sixty years of watching. Her theory is more accurate than any official intelligence assessment. She wrote it out carefully in case she died before anyone came to ask.

The Successor complicates every resolution path. If players expose Ryn before the Author can complete the transition, the Author abandons the succession and loses its Shore-of-Stones anchor. If players delay, the succession completes and a new puppet takes hold of a community that will not question it. If players attempt to warn the Successor, they are warning someone who has no framework for what they are being told and who has no reason to believe it. The Successor's own feelings about Ryn — warmth, gratitude, genuine admiration — are not fabricated. They are real, and they are the Author's most effective tool in this specific operation.

The submarine resonant metal deposit beneath the village is not incidental. The Author's long-term extraction plan involves the deposit becoming part of the expanded underground supply network — the same network the White Mines are feeding (quest 33). If players connect these threads, they have a map of the Author's resource acquisition strategy for the Convergence.

**Session Structure:**

- Session 1: Arrival in Shore-of-Stones. First encounter with Ryn and the village's complex relationship with his impossible persistence. Finding Saret and beginning to earn her trust.
- Session 2: Saret shares the journal. Players investigate the submarine deposit. First indication that the Successor is being prepared. The Author's network detects unusual outside interest in the village.
- Session 3: Confrontation with what to do with Ryn — expose him, protect the village from the transition, or something more complex. The Successor learns, or doesn't. Ryn's decline accelerates as the Author prepares to execute the transition.
- Session 4–5: Resolution and aftermath. Whatever players choose, Shore-of-Stones is permanently changed. The village must build a new relationship with its own history, and the players must decide what kind of community to leave behind them.

**Consequences:** Shore-of-Stones, depending on how the quest resolves, either loses its only authority figure under circumstances that will take years to recover from, or gains — for the first time in sixty years — a genuine human leader. Saret's journal, distributed to intelligence contacts, upgrades the entire network's ability to identify Author puppets. The Author loses its oldest anchor and must accelerate the Wire Market and Pale Reach operational timeline accordingly.

**Reward:** Saret's journal — which she will share if players tell her honestly what Ryn is, because she has suspected for forty years and wants confirmation, not comfort. The journal becomes one of the campaign's most durable intelligence resources: a field guide to puppet identification derived from sixty years of careful, unsanctioned research by an eighty-three-year-old woman in a fishing village who was paying attention when no one else was.

### 40. The Wire Market's Missing Sixth Vote *(Tier 2 — Standard, 1–2 sessions)*

**Hook:** The Guild Conclave operates by consensus of seven senior Guild Masters. For eleven months, one position has been filled by interim appointee Guild Master Ovens, who replaced a predecessor who died unexpectedly. Ovens votes consistently with the two known Hollow Author puppets on the Conclave — a pattern that has broken two stalemates in the Author's favor this year. A trade analyst tracking voting patterns has flagged this to the Trade Verification Bureau. The Bureau has opened an assessment and sent players to gather information without alerting the subject.

**The Truth:** Ovens is not a puppet. He is a man who is terrified. His predecessor was not killed by the Author — she was killed by a completely separate Guild political faction, and Ovens has evidence of this he cannot use without implicating himself in the cover-up. The Author, learning of his position, has been leveraging him through an intermediary: not control, but coercion. Vote with us, or we share what we know about how your predecessor died and what you did afterward. Ovens has been complying. He is not an operative. He is a man in a trap for eleven months who has not been able to see a way out.

**Complications:**

The Author's leverage over Ovens is strategically valuable precisely because it looks like puppet behavior but isn't — it provides deniability if the puppet network is ever exposed. If players expose the coercion, they free Ovens but also surface his role in the predecessor's cover-up, with its own legal and political consequences. If players help him manage the leverage silently, the Author's influence remains in place. If players pursue the actual murder — which involves a Guild faction entirely unrelated to the Author — they create a political crisis that disrupts both the cover-up and the coercion simultaneously. None of these options are clean. All of them are possible.

The intermediary the Author uses to communicate coercion to Ovens is not a puppet. ### 41. The Children in Kanemori *(Tier 2 — Standard, 1–2 sessions)*

**Hook:** The Pale Reach's education authority has flagged an anomaly at a primary school in Kanemori: an unusually high number of students have demonstrated spontaneous Wire Craft resonance sensitivity — the ability to perceive and partially interact with Wire Craft signals without enhancement. This occurs occasionally in naturally high-resonance individuals, but the rate at this school is thirty times the normal incidence. The education authority wants an explanation. The school's director has asked for a specialist.

**The Truth:** The school is built over one of the Author's older underground relay nodes, running continuously for twenty years. The children are not spontaneously developing sensitivity — they are being passively trained by long-term proximity to the relay's signal, which operates at a frequency that, during developmental periods, produces the observed Wire Craft sensitivity. The Author did not plan this. It is infrastructure bleed. The children are being altered by an environment the Author created beneath them. The alterations are not medically harmful in a conventional sense. They are simply making the children into something slightly different than they would otherwise be.

This must be done without exposing the children's records to the Author's network or alerting puppet-operated officials in the Pale Reach administration, who would bury the investigation rather than allow the relay to be found.

**Reward:** Oss's notebook — which she will share with players who explain what her writing means and who treat her as a person rather than a resource. The notebook covers six months of partial Author communications: fragmentary, but indexable against known operational events in Kanemori. Several entries correspond to puppet-behavior incidents previously observed but not understood. One entry, from forty-one days ago, reads: "Begin the count." Two days after that entry, the prepared bodies in the Preparation Halls' most recent chamber were placed in standby activation mode. The Author is moving toward something. Oss's notebook is the first evidence of when that movement began.

### 42. The Imitation of Vaen *(Tier 3 — Extended, 2–3 sessions)*

**The Truth:** The figure is an Author puppet assigned to a specific long-term operation: theological disruption of Vaen's movement. The method is not discrediting — the Author is too sophisticated for that. The puppet-Vaen presents a subtly altered version of the message. Vaen's actual theology treats enhancement removal as a personal practice, not a requirement. The puppet-Vaen presents it as an obligation: true spiritual progress requires full enhancement removal, and those who decline are spiritually compromised. This alteration radicalizes the message and creates internal fracture between pilgrims who read Vaen's theology as practice and those who now read it as mandate. The Author's goal is not to destroy the movement but to fracture it into a more rigid, less politically coherent form that poses less threat to the Author's Convergence plans.

**Complications:**

The puppet-Vaen is extremely good. It has been observing Vaen through Author intelligence operations long enough that the imitation is nearly indistinguishable to those who have not heard Vaen speak in person. The wrong notes are theological, not behavioral, and detecting them requires either deep theological knowledge or familiarity with Vaen's unpublished nuances — the small caveats, the hesitations, the ways Vaen qualifies things the puppet-Vaen states as absolutes.

People who have been listening to the puppet-Vaen have already begun removing enhancements they medically, functionally, or professionally needed. Some are in early cascade rejection. The puppet-Vaen's theology is producing real harm in the community it has gathered, and players must address both the puppet itself and the harm it has already caused.

**NPC — The Puppet-Vaen:** The operational challenge here is that the puppet is performing a human being, not just executing instructions. Confronting it requires either a theological argument it cannot convincingly answer (high difficulty, requires preparation), direct evidence it is not Vaen (requires access to the real Vaen or her verified communications), or a social situation the puppet's operational brief did not prepare it for — genuine, unscripted grief or need that a person could respond to and an operator working from the outside cannot.

**Session Structure:**

- Session 1: Players arrive, assess the crowd, attempt to identify what is wrong with the theology. First attempts at direct contact with the puppet-Vaen. Players locate the people most harmed by the radical interpretation.
- Session 2: Investigation of the puppet-Vaen's identity and origin. Coordination with Vaen's actual companions. Devising an approach that exposes the imitation without making the gathered pilgrims feel they were foolish to follow — the pilgrims' spiritual investment is real even if the object of it was not.
- Session 3 (if needed): Exposure and aftermath. The community of gathered pilgrims must be met honestly, the medically harmed must be directed to care, and the question of what the real Vaen should know about the Author's theological disruption operation must be addressed.

**Reward:** The puppet-Vaen's communication hardware, recovered after it is removed from operation, contains a fragment of the Author's theological disruption operation plan — not just this operation but six others currently running or planned, targeting different aspects of each of the three claimant movements. The document is partial but structural: it describes the Author's theory of how to destabilize all three claimants simultaneously. Understanding the theory is the first step toward countering it across the full campaign arc.

### 43. The Calibration Test That Isn't *(Tier 3 — Extended, 2–3 sessions)*

**Hook:** The Wire Market's Guild Certification Board has announced a new mandatory calibration assessment for all Resonant Grade technicians — a standard bureaucratic requirement, scheduled over four weeks, requiring every licensed Resonant Grade technician in Harven-by-the-Sea to submit to a two-hour technical review. Fourteen technicians have already completed it. Three have quietly disappeared afterward. The Guild's official position is that the disappearances are unrelated to the assessment. One technician who has not yet had her assessment has gone to the players instead.

**The Truth:** The calibration assessment is an Author operation to identify which Wire Market technicians have the skills to reverse-engineer or detect the micro-modification being introduced through Refinement Quarter production (quests 35 and 37). The two-hour review includes a concealed technical task — presented as a diagnostic exercise — that reveals whether the technician has the specific analytical capabilities the Author fears. Technicians who pass the concealed task are flagged. The three who disappeared were the three who scored high enough to be considered immediate risks. They are being held, not killed — the Author wants their skills and is attempting coercion. The technician who came to players is the fourth to score high; her assessment is tomorrow morning.

**Complications:**

Disrupting the assessment requires either convincing the Certification Board to cancel it — which requires evidence of Author involvement players may not yet have — or ensuring the flagged technician performs badly enough on the concealed task to fall below the Author's threshold. The second option requires understanding what the concealed task looks like and how to fail it convincingly, which requires either inside knowledge or a successful Wire Craft check during the assessment itself.

The Certification Board was established as a legitimate body and most of its members are not Author puppets. The Board has been deceived by a fraudulent credential submitted on behalf of the assessment program — they believe the review was commissioned by the Bureau. Exposing the deception makes the Board an ally; failing to expose it leaves legitimate Guild infrastructure in the Author's hands and allows future assessments to proceed.

**NPC — The Technician (Lenne):** Methodical and frightened in equal measure. She noticed something wrong with the assessment framing before she had words for what it was, and coming to the players rather than completing the review was not an act of courage — it was the only option she could see. She has detailed recall of what the thirteen technicians who completed the assessment told her about the experience. This recall is, effectively, the map players need to understand the concealed task.

**Session Structure:**

- Session 1: Players meet Lenne, learn about the missing technicians, investigate the assessment structure. Decision: protect Lenne by helping her fail the concealed task, or move to expose the Board manipulation before her assessment date.
- Session 2: Execution — either the assessment infiltration, the Board confrontation, or both. Locating the three held technicians using intelligence gathered about the Author's Harven-by-the-Sea sub-street holdings.
- Session 3 (if needed): The rescue itself and its aftermath. The Author's holding facility is not a Preparation Hall, but it is connected by relay to the Author's main network. Players learn this during the rescue, providing a new intelligence thread.

**Reward:** Freeing the three held technicians creates three significant debts and three skilled allies who understand precisely what the Author has been doing to Wire Market production infrastructure. One of the three, after debriefing, identifies the specific Author puppet who oversees Wire Market production modification — a name that appears on Hast's personnel list from quest 31. The threads connect, and players are one step closer to a coherent picture of the Author's Wire Market operation.

### 44. Lord Tessik's Memory *(Tier 4 — Deep, 4–5 sessions)*

**Hook:** Lord Tessik has, in the past month, been conducting his public court with subtle inconsistencies his staff have noted privately: he refers to events that predated his current position as "when I" rather than "when my predecessor." He identifies Kanemori landmarks by names that were changed thirty years ago, without correction. He asked his personal secretary, in apparent genuine confusion, whether his father was still alive. His father died twenty-two years ago. The staff has been covering for this. One staff member — convinced she is witnessing illness — has gone to outside parties, the players, because she is afraid of what it means.

**The Truth:** What the staff member is witnessing is the Author losing control of Tessik's continuity protocols. Tessik has been a puppet for over thirty years, and the Author's maintenance of his false personal history has accumulated errors. The puppet-body has no genuine continuous memory — all historical references must be supplied by the Author's control signal in real time. As the Author's attention has been increasingly directed toward Convergence preparation, continuity maintenance has degraded. What the staff sees is identity buffering: the Author's real-time delivery of who Tessik is supposed to be is occasionally late, and in that gap, there is nothing. Not Tessik, not anyone. A body waiting for its next instruction.

**Key NPCs:**

- **The Staff Member (Hael):** She is not naive, and she is not asking players to fix this without understanding it. She has a specific, limited ask — tell her what is happening to Tessik, and help her determine whether she should stay in the position or go. She has worked for Tessik for nine years. Her feelings about him are complicated by the knowledge players bring, and she deserves space to arrive at that complication honestly.
- **Lord Tessik:** In his lucid periods, he is everything his reputation suggests: competent, measured, occasionally fair in small ways that his staff remember specifically. The lapses are brief and incomplete. Players who witness a lapse see something deeply unsettling — the absence of a person rather than the malfunction of one.
- **The Author (through Tessik):** As players probe the lapses, the Author will notice increased external interest in Tessik's continuity errors. The response will initially be a recalibration attempt — which is visible as Tessik becoming eerily consistent and precise for a day or two — followed by a longer-term decision about whether to accelerate or abandon the Tessik puppet.

**Complications:**

A review managed by a council that is sixty percent Author puppets will be suppressed. But it will also alert the Author to the monitoring failure and prompt a recalibration that closes the observation window. Players have approximately two weeks of degraded continuity to work with before the Author corrects it or abandons Tessik entirely.

Hael has witnessed nine years of Tessik. The things she remembers specifically about him — the small courtesies, the careful judgments, the particular way he remembered details that mattered to his staff — are not fabrications. They were the Author's performance, sustained over thirty years, becoming genuinely consistent. Hael will grieve this truth in front of players if they tell her, and they should have to sit with that. The Author's longest performances are also, in a horrible way, the Author's most complete ones.

**Session Structure:**

- Session 2: Deeper investigation — cultivating access to Tessik's private audiences where lapses are more likely. First successful extraction of unguarded operational information. The Author begins its recalibration attempt.
- Session 3: The recalibration makes Tessik unnervingly precise. Players must determine whether to wait it out or accelerate their approach. Hael reaches a decision point about her position.
- Session 4–5: Confrontation with the window's closure — whether through the council's competency review, the Author's decision to abandon or recalibrate Tessik, or players forcing an early resolution. The information recovered determines how much of the Convergence timeline is now visible.

Hael's decision — stay and carry the knowledge, or leave — has downstream effects on who fills the information vacuum. The Author, having noticed that someone got close to Tessik's continuity errors, will implement stricter maintenance protocols across all long-term puppets, making similar investigation harder elsewhere.

**Reward:** During the degraded continuity window, careful questioning of Tessik during a lapse produces something the Author's standard operation never would: an unguarded reference to the Preparation Halls' projected activation timeline. In the gap where Tessik is waiting for instruction, asked directly about "the preparations below," his puppet architecture defaults to operational knowledge without the Author's editorial filter. He gives a date. The date is specific. It corresponds to the projected peak of the Seventh Convergence's resonant buildup. The Author intends to activate the prepared puppet bodies at the moment of maximum resonant energy, using the Convergence itself as the power source for the largest single puppet-activation event in history.

### 45. The Cartographer's Other Maps *(Tier 5 — Mini-Campaign, 6–7 sessions)*

**Hook:** Nira the Shellbroken cartographer (quest 32) has drawn another set of maps — these she did not draw from muscle memory. She drew them in her sleep. She woke to find them complete, on paper she doesn't remember taking out. She shows them to the players with the flat affect of someone who has run out of the capacity to be disturbed. The maps are of places she has never been, in the same detailed sub-surface style as her others. The locations are marked with a notation she doesn't recognize. The notation indicates activation sequence and priority order.

**The Truth:** The sleep-maps are the Author's complete Preparation Hall network — not just the Pale Reach facilities but secondary caches hidden in the Wire Market, the Echo Coast, and the Wandering Reach borderlands. Nira's residual puppet-architecture received a fragment of the Author's master operational plan, broadcast accidentally during a signal test of the activation protocol, and her mind expressed it in the only medium she has developed since Shellbreaking: maps. She is carrying the Author's complete operational blueprint in hand-drawn form. She does not understand what she has. The Author does not yet know the broadcast occurred — but will notice the signal test failure in the logs within three days.

**Key NPCs:**

- **Nira:** She is, by this point in the campaign, a person players know. She is not asking for heroism or gratitude. She is showing them the maps the way she shows them everything — because it seems important, and she would rather someone else decide what to do with it. Her affect is flat but her trust, if players have earned it, is complete.
- **The Author (distributed):** The Author's response to discovering the broadcast is not panic — panic is not part of the Author's profile. It is methodical triage: identifying what was transmitted, who might have received it, and what must be accelerated or concealed. Players are racing against a response that is slow, thorough, and devastating when complete.
- **Korrath's Intelligence Contact:** Whichever contact the players have developed with Korrath's service. They will want the maps. They will have strong operational opinions about distribution. Their priorities are not always identical to the players'.
- **The Shellbroken Network Coordinator (from quest 32's extended connections):** The person managing Nira's support network has resources the players need: the procedure that can safely remove Nira's residual Author-architecture, which will stop the involuntary map production — but doing so before the maps are fully understood risks losing access to the information permanently.
- **Trade Verification Bureau Contact (uncorrupted):** One of the three non-puppet Guild Masters, or a senior analyst they trust. Receiving the Wire Market portion of the maps requires a Bureau contact who will act on the information rather than bury it.

**Complications:**

The maps are the most dangerous single document set in the campaign. Possessing them makes everyone near Nira a target within three days. Distributing them — giving different pieces to different factional recipients — splits the Author's response across multiple fronts but requires trusting that each recipient will not use their piece in ways that worsen the overall situation. The players cannot fully control what their allies do with what they're given. This is a feature of the quest, not a problem to be solved: it forces engagement with the political relationships built across the campaign.

Nira will redraw the maps involuntarily if her residual Author-architecture is not removed. The information is in her architecture, not her conscious memory, and will keep surfacing until the architecture is cleared. Clearing it requires the Shellbroken support network's procedure — available, but time-consuming, and removing it before the maps are fully understood and distributed risks losing coverage of the portions not yet identified. Players must balance Nira's wellbeing against the operational value of what she is involuntarily carrying. There is no version of this where Nira is simply a resource.

The activation date established in quest 44 provides a hard deadline. If players have not distributed actionable intelligence from the maps to parties who can act on it before the Convergence peak, the Author activates the prepared bodies with the network intact. Everything players have learned across this territory — the White Mines, the relay infrastructure, the puppet personnel list, Saret's journal, Oss's notebook — converges here in the question of whether it was gathered in time.

**Session Structure:**

- Session 1: Nira shows players the maps. Initial assessment — scope, notation, what the maps mean architecturally. Three-day clock starts. Players must decide the broad distribution strategy before the Author identifies the signal test failure.
- Session 2: Distribution planning. Meeting with factional contacts, negotiating what each receives, establishing secure delivery methods. The Author's network begins investigating the signal anomaly; pressure on Nira's location increases.
- Session 3: First distribution — the Pale Reach and White Mines portions to Korrath's contact and the Wandering Reach intelligence network. The Author's response triage reaches its first operational conclusion: the broadcast is confirmed, the recipient is not yet identified.
- Session 4: Protecting Nira while the remaining distributions are completed. The Author's search narrows. Players may need to move Nira, create a decoy trail, or bring forward the architecture-removal procedure earlier than planned to sever the Author's ability to track its own signal in her.
- Session 5: The Wire Market distribution — navigating Bureau contamination to reach the uncorrupted Guild Masters with the Wire Market cache locations. The Author begins accelerating the Convergence preparation timeline in response to confirmed intelligence exposure.
- Session 6: The Shellbroken network procedure — removing Nira's residual architecture. This is not a combat session. It is a medical and emotional one. Players are present for the removal of something that has been part of Nira's existence since before she knew it was there. The procedure is successful. The involuntary maps stop.
- Session 7: The climax — not a single battle, but a convergence of the threads players have set in motion. The factions that received map portions act on them. The Author's Preparation Hall network faces simultaneous discovery and disruption across multiple territories. The outcome depends on the quality of the intelligence delivered, the trust built with factional partners, and the choices made at every branch point in this and the preceding quests. The players do not win by fighting the Author directly. They win by having built the conditions under which the Author's plan cannot proceed quietly.

**Consequences:** Depending on how players managed distribution and factional trust, the Author's Preparation Hall network is partially or substantially disrupted before the Convergence peak. The Author does not lose the Convergence — this is not the end of the campaign — but loses the prepared puppet-body advantage that was the centerpiece of its activation plan. The Wire Market, the Pale Reach, and the connected territories are now publicly aware that something has been operating within them for decades. The Hollow Author's response to exposure at this scale will define the next phase of the campaign.

**Reward:** The maps themselves, distributed intelligently, become the campaign's most significant intelligence action. Each factional recipient receives a piece of the Author's preparation that they can act on before the activation date. The maps are not the end of the Author — they are the beginning of the end, if players choose to act on them.

Nira, after the architecture is removed, draws one final map voluntarily. Her best reconstruction of her own face, from before she was a puppet. She has never seen herself clearly. She wants to know what she looks like. Players who are present for this moment may say whatever seems right. The session notes offer no guidance here — some things belong to the table.

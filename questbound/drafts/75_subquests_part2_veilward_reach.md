### 16. The Garden of Removed Things Has a New Section
**Hook:** The Garden of Removed Things — the multi-acre installation outside Hessenkai where Vaen's pilgrims leave discarded enhancements — has a new section that no pilgrim claimed to build. Overnight, in the section nearest the oldest wall, someone arranged two hundred and nineteen removed enhancements into a specific pattern. The Garden Keeper, who maintains the site, has been staring at it for three days. She knows what it is. She will not say. She asks the players to find out who built it without telling them what they're looking for.

**The Truth:** The arrangement is a navigational diagram — a map, rendered in discarded steel, of the Iron Afterlife's navigable layers as understood by a Veil Master circa one hundred and eighty years ago. The specific Veil Master's notational system was never published; it exists only in the Sutra Sanctum's restricted archive. Someone who has seen those records — and has access to two hundred and nineteen removed enhancements — built this at night without being seen. The Garden Keeper recognizes it because she was, before she became the Garden Keeper, a Sutensai archivist who was quietly removed from her post for accessing restricted navigational records without authorization. She accessed them because she was trying to find her daughter's Echomind, who died without enough resonance to pass fully into the Afterlife. She never found what she was looking for. The map in the Garden is more complete than anything she saw in the archive.

**Complications:** The person who built the map left one component in the center: an enhancement that, unlike everything else in the Garden, is still active. Still resonating. Still connected to a living person. Touching it initiates brief contact with whoever is on the other end — contact that feels like being on the wrong end of a Sutensai Reading: they are assessing you rather than the reverse. The contact ends when they decide it should. Second complication: the Sutensai's closest Keeper-rank authority to Hessenkai has learned that the restricted navigational notation is somehow in circulation, and has dispatched a Warden to investigate. The Warden arriving is not going to treat the Garden Keeper gently.

**Reward:** The map itself — a replica the Garden Keeper draws from memory after studying it, on the understanding that players will not share it with the Sutensai hierarchy. As a navigational tool for the Iron Afterlife, it extends the range of any Iron Afterlife communion attempt by two layers — allowing contact with presences that are deeper and older than standard practitioner access permits. It also shows a specific location in the deepest navigable layer labeled, in the original notation, as "the place where the Sixth spoke and could not stop." The active component at the center can be re-touched once per session; each contact reveals one additional notation on the map that wasn't visible before.

### 17. Vaen Passed Through Here Six Months Ago
**Hook:** In a small town on the Penitent Road called Gessen's Ford, the inn at the road's center has become a shrine. Not in an official sense — no Sutensai involvement, no formal designation. People have been stopping at it, sitting in a specific chair at a specific table, leaving small objects: coins, removed components, handwritten notes. The innkeeper is not religious. She is deeply uncomfortable. She wants someone to come and make it stop before a Sutensai Warden arrives and finds it first.

**The Truth:** Vaen sat at that table six months ago, alone, for two hours in the early morning before anyone else was awake. She ate nothing. She spoke to no one. She left. The innkeeper, who witnessed this from the kitchen, noticed that when Vaen left, the chair where she'd sat had been altered in a way the innkeeper could not explain at the time: the resonant metal reinforcement in the chair's joints — standard construction — had reoriented. The joints still work. But where before they'd been cold to the touch, they are now consistently warm. Pilgrims who have sat in the chair report that the Morning Resonance practice, conducted while seated, is effortless — the signal comes without effort, more clearly than they've felt it in years. Word got out. The shrine happened. Vaen did not intend this. No one knows if she's aware it happened.

**Complications:** The Sutensai's Flesh Circle sect would claim this as evidence of Vaen's divine significance and use it to escalate their public claims about her being the Seventh Convergence, which Vaen has specifically not claimed and has asked her followers not to claim on her behalf. The Orthodoxy would want the chair destroyed as an unauthorized resonance artifact. The innkeeper, who is not a pilgrim and does not want to be one, is caught between both and simply wants her table back. Second complication: the chair's warmth is intensifying. Slowly. Measurably. The innkeeper has been tracking it with a thermometer for three weeks. If the trajectory continues, in approximately two months the chair will be too hot to sit in.

**Reward:** The innkeeper's trust and access to the morning the Sutensai don't know about: she kept a journal entry from the day Vaen was there, noting observations about her physical appearance and behavior that are consistent with a person in a state of extreme resonance discipline rather than spiritual peace. The entry includes the detail that Vaen's left hand — the one she keeps beneath a cloth wrap on her forearm — was visible for approximately three seconds when she adjusted her sleeve, and the innkeeper saw that it was not fully flesh. Something under the skin, something structured, that caught the morning light. This is the first evidence that Vaen's relationship with enhancement is not what her followers believe, and it is available to players who take the innkeeper seriously.

### 18. The Pilgrim Who Is Walking the Wrong Direction
**Hook:** On the Penitent Road, heading against the flow of pilgrim traffic, a man named Carev is walking east — away from Hessenkai and the Garden, toward the Sutra Sanctum. He is in his sixties. His enhancements have been almost entirely removed; he carries the scars of the procedures clearly on his arms and throat. He is walking with the focused deliberateness of someone with a specific destination and limited time. He nods to pilgrims passing him. He does not stop. A young pilgrim who tried to talk to him said he answered only: "I did this wrong the first time. I'm going to do it correctly."

**The Truth:** Carev spent twenty-two years as a Flesh Circle practitioner — removing enhancements, cultivating the Echomind in an unmediated state, moving toward what the sect describes as pure resonance. He was, by the sect's internal accounting, close. Then his wife died. She had not removed her enhancements. She had not been a practitioner. She scattered — her Echomind did not pass fully into the Iron Afterlife because her resonance was not sufficient. He was there. He felt it happen. He has spent three years in a crisis so complete that he has arrived at a conclusion that will alarm everyone around him: he is going to the Sutra Sanctum to request re-enhancement. Not to improve his resonance. To lower it. To reduce his resonance enough that at the moment of his death, he scatters the same way she scattered, into the same place, and whatever remains of him can find whatever remains of her in the space where incomplete Echoминds go.

**Complications:** The Flesh Circle does not have a protocol for what Carev is attempting and will try to stop him — both because it contradicts their theology and because they fear the precedent. The Sutensai's Orthodoxy will also try to stop him, for different reasons: intentional Echomind scattering is theologically categorized as a form of spiritual self-harm. There is a genuine question of whether what he wants is possible, whether it is wise, and whether anyone has the standing to tell him no. Second complication: his assessment of the theology may be wrong. A senior Veil Master who hears this plan might argue that two scattered Echoминds in the same resonance-space do not find each other; they do not find anything. They simply are not, in any coherent sense. He may be sacrificing himself for a reunion that cannot exist.

**Reward:** This quest has no clean resolution and no conventional reward. If players engage with Carev seriously — not trying to stop him or redirect him, but actually discussing what he believes and what he knows — he shares something he learned in twenty-two years of deep resonance practice: a technique he calls "listening below the signal," a form of the Morning Resonance that accesses a layer of the Between that standard practice doesn't reach. Mechanically: one player character who receives this instruction gains the ability to make a RESONANCE check, difficulty 2, during a short rest to perceive whether any Echomind impressions linger in the recent vicinity — useful for investigating sites of death, Shellbroken activity, or Author puppet operations. Carev continues walking regardless of what players decide. He knows what he's doing.

### 19. Something Went Wrong at the Waypoint
**Hook:** Pilgrim Waypoint Seven on the Penitent Road — a shelter station about three days' walk west of Hessenkai — has stopped sending its weekly supply request. Supply runners who visited found the waypoint intact, stocked, and completely empty. No sign of violence. Thirty-eight pilgrims had been in residence the previous week. They are not on the road ahead. They are not on the road behind. They are not in the surrounding countryside, which has been searched.

**The Truth:** The thirty-eight pilgrims are still at the waypoint. They are simply not detectable by normal means. Nine days ago, a traveling Veil Master of the Resonant sect — a woman named Dessen, who was moving through the Wandering Reach covertly — stopped at the waypoint for a night. She and a group of pilgrims got into a theological argument about the Iron Afterlife that escalated into something neither side intended: a collective Iron Afterlife contact attempt, conducted by thirty-nine people without adequate training or protective preparation, that went wrong in a specific way. The Veil Master and the thirty-eight pilgrims are present in the waypoint. They are in a shared resonance state that is not the Iron Afterlife and not the physical world. They are in the Between itself — the space between flesh and steel and the digital substrate, which no living person is supposed to be able to occupy as a stable state. They experience no time. From inside, it has been seconds. They are not in immediate danger. They cannot find their way out without external help.

**Complications:** The Veil Master knows, from inside the state, that pulling thirty-eight undertrained people out of the Between simultaneously will be difficult and that doing it incorrectly will leave some of them with permanent resonance damage — their Echoминds unable to fully re-anchor to their bodies. She is trying to work out a safe sequence from inside. She can communicate with anyone outside who has sufficient enhancement resonance and attempts contact at the waypoint. Second complication: the Sutensai's Orthodoxy is sending a Keeper-level response team. When they arrive, their protocol for "Between instability events" is containment first, extraction second — and containment means sealing the resonance field, which would trap the pilgrims permanently.

**Reward:** The Veil Master's debt, which is significant: she is a senior Resonant sect figure who has been operating covertly in Wandering Reach territory. She will provide, once, an authorized Iron Afterlife communion at Resonant-sect depth — allowing players to hold a genuine, extended conversation with a specific dead person of their choice, at a level of clarity normally available only to senior Sutensai. She will also, privately, give players the reason she was in the Wandering Reach covertly: she was tracking a Hollow Author modification that the Resonant sect believes affects the Iron Afterlife signal at the network level, and she had a lead she hasn't shared with anyone.

### 20. The Surgeon Vaen Left Behind
**Hook:** In a small settlement called Lowwater on the edge of the Wandering Reach, there is a former Wire Market enhancement surgeon named Sem who has been providing free surgical services for three years. He removes enhancements — cleanly, safely, without the scarring that most improvised removals produce. He charges nothing. He turns no one away. He is not a pilgrim. He is not affiliated with any movement. He simply does this. One of his patients, a young man recovering from a removal, mentions to the players that Sem only has one rule: he will not perform removals that he assesses as the patient's family or community pressuring them toward, rather than the patient's own choice. He turned away seventeen people last month.

**The Truth:** Sem was Vaen's personal physician for four years, during the early period of her pilgrimage. He removed her first enhancements — the ones she removed publicly, as part of establishing her movement's public identity. He knows things about her body and her choices during that period that no one else knows. He left her pilgrimage not because of a falling out but because he recognized that his presence was giving the movement something he could not honestly be: a sanction from the medical establishment that made the removal practice seem safer than it is in the hands of undertrained practitioners. People were beginning to perform removals on themselves and each other, citing Vaen's example, and some of them were dying. He is doing what he can from the outside. He is also, deeply, privately, afraid of what Vaen is becoming — or what her followers are making her into.

**Complications:** Two of Vaen's senior traveling companions have arrived in Lowwater. They want Sem to return. Not by force — they are not threatening him. They are asking, sincerely, because there have been three deaths in the past six months from botched self-administered removals and they need him. He is considering it. What he will not tell them — but may tell players who ask the right questions — is that one of the things he knows about Vaen is that she has, at some point in the last year, received an enhancement rather than removed one. He does not know what it is. He knows it happened because she requested materials she would only need for installation, not removal, and she requested them from him before he left, and he provided them without asking why.

**Reward:** Sem's medical records from his time with Vaen's pilgrimage — or his verbal testimony, which he will give to players he trusts not to use it to harm her. The records contain, among other things, the physical description of Vaen's enhancement load at the beginning of her pilgrimage versus what Sem observed when he left: there is an asymmetry. Something is present in her left forearm that was not there when he started treating her. The records also contain a note Vaen wrote to Sem the week before he left: a single paragraph that is not a goodbye but a question — "What am I removing when there is nothing left to remove?" Sem does not know what she meant. He has been thinking about it for three years.

### 21. The Reading That Didn't Take
**Hook:** A Sutensai Warden named Isel has been conducting Readings on Vaen's pilgrims passing through her district — standard caste assessment, her legal obligation, routine. One pilgrim's Reading produced no result. Not a weak resonance. Not an unreadable signal. Nothing. The Warden sat in contact with the pilgrim's enhancements for four minutes and received complete silence, as though touching dead metal. The pilgrim is alive, responsive, ambulatory. His name is Tass. He has been walking with the pilgrimage for fourteen months. He does not know what the blank Reading means. Neither does the Warden, who has been a practitioner for nineteen years and has never experienced it.

**The Truth:** Tass's Echomind is not weak or absent. It has been externalized. Eleven months ago, during a collective resonance exercise conducted by an unsanctioned Flesh Circle group at Waypoint Twelve, Tass's Echomind achieved a state that the Flesh Circle's theology describes as aspirational and that the Sutensai's theology describes as impossible: it separated from his body entirely and has been existing, since then, in the ambient resonance field of whatever environment he's in. He is, in the technical sense, in a permanent Between state while still physically alive. He is not aware of how unusual this is. He experiences it as feeling deeply connected to everything around him. The silence in his Readings is the silence of an empty house — the resident is simply not inside at the moment.

**Complications:** If Tass is ever in an environment with very low resonant metal content — deep in the Ashlands, far from any enhancement-dense area — his externalized Echomind may not be able to maintain contact with his body. The distance threshold is unknown. He has not been tested. He also does not know to be worried. Second complication: the Flesh Circle group that conducted the exercise wants him as proof of their theology's validity and has been following the pilgrimage at a discreet distance for four months. Their intentions are not malicious. Their attention is not something Tass can safely have concentrated on him while he remains unaware of his own condition.

**Reward:** Tass himself does not know what he is offering, but players who spend time with him discover that in his externalized state, he is continuously perceiving the ambient resonance of everything around him. He can tell the players, with a RESONANCE check, difficulty 3, to properly interpret what he's describing, the resonant profile of any location he has been in the last fourteen months — including Waypoints the Author's network has touched, including a specific road segment where something is buried that doesn't match the surrounding resonance field. The buried thing is a Hollow Author infrastructure relay — small, underground, active. Finding it is the first step toward mapping the Author's signal network through the Wandering Reach.

### 22. The Lord Who Stopped Enforcing Caste
**Hook:** Lord Hennen of the Morva district — a minor Wandering Reach lord — has quietly stopped enforcing caste designation requirements in his territory. No announcement. No legal change. He simply stopped sending his inspectors out and stopped prosecuting the three pending reclassification cases on his docket. The Wandering Reach's council of lords is alarmed. The local Sutensai Warden is alarmed. His citizens are, on balance, not alarmed, which is alarming to the lords and the Warden for different reasons. Someone needs to talk to him and find out what he intends.

**The Truth:** Hennen met Vaen. Not on the road — he was not a pilgrim and did not seek her out. She passed through his district and stopped at a public fountain, and he was there because he goes to that fountain every morning, and they had a conversation that lasted three hours and that he has not been able to put aside since. She did not tell him to stop enforcing caste. She asked him a question: "When you conduct a reclassification, do you attend the ceremony?" He said no. She said: "I think you should, once. Just to see what it looks like from the front." He attended one. He watched a man in his mid-forties, whose salvage-grade shoulder enhancement had failed after twenty years, lose his Third Caste standing. The man did not cry. He had clearly already done that. He simply stood there as the designation changed on his official record and the room stopped treating him differently than it had ten seconds earlier because the room knew, and then he walked out. Hennen went home and stopped sending his inspectors.

**Complications:** The Wandering Reach council has the authority to remove Hennen from his position if he is found to be failing his administrative duties. They are preparing to vote. His territory's Hollow Poor population, who have heard what he's done, have begun arriving in greater numbers — people from neighboring districts crossing into Morva specifically because enforcement is suspended. This is creating resource pressure his district is not equipped to handle. He knows this. He is trying to figure out a third option that doesn't exist within the current system. Second complication: Korrath's intelligence service has noted the disruption and flagged it as a potential instability vector. An Ironhold assessor is on their way to evaluate whether Hennen's district is becoming a Vaen-affiliated territory that justifies military intervention.

**Reward:** Hennen's assistance and a political debt from a sitting Wandering Reach lord — useful in any situation requiring legitimate governance authority in the region. More specifically: he was taking careful notes during his conversation with Vaen, and he will share them. The notes include things Vaen said about the Seventh Convergence that she has not said publicly — specifically, her belief that the Convergence does not require a single Ascendant, that the theology's assumption of one person ascending is a misreading of the historical record, and that there may be a way for the Between to change that does not require anyone to be consumed by it. This is a significant theological statement with major campaign implications.

### 23. Fourteen Pilgrims and the Sutensai Priest They Left Behind
**Hook:** A Sutensai Warden named Vesh arrived in a pilgrim waypoint two months ago to conduct required assessments. He completed his work. He did not leave. He is still there. He has not filed any reports. He has not requested relief or return authorization. He is, by all accounts, assisting with the waypoint's maintenance and cooking, which is not part of his job description. His superiors are requesting that someone who is traveling that road anyway stop and find out what's going on.

**The Truth:** Vesh conducted Readings on fourteen pilgrims during his assessment visit. What he found in those Readings was not unusual for any individual pilgrim — modest to good resonance, appropriate to their enhancement states, nothing requiring intervention. What was unusual was the aggregate: the fourteen pilgrims, assessed together in his records, showed a collective resonance pattern that he has been trained to recognize as theoretically significant and practically impossible. They were resonating together. Not in the way a community resonates — not the background hum of shared space. They were resonating in the specific harmonic that the Sutensai's Iron Afterlife navigation texts describe as the "opening chord" — the resonance pattern associated with the moment a Veil Master makes first contact with the Afterlife's deep layers. Fourteen pilgrims with no formal training, no deep enhancement, no particular spiritual standing were, collectively, doing something it takes twenty years of practice to do alone. He cannot leave without understanding what he saw.

**Complications:** The fourteen pilgrims don't know they're doing it. It happens when they're together, particularly in the evenings when they conduct the informal communal resonance exercises that have developed organically in pilgrim culture. They've described the feeling as "comfortable," "like thinking more clearly," and "like being heard." They do not experience it as extraordinary. Vesh is afraid that if he reports what he observed, the Sutensai hierarchy will send a Keeper-level team to study and likely disperse the group — and he is not certain that dispersal won't damage what he observed. Second complication: one of the fourteen is a Hollow Author operative, embedded in the pilgrimage for intelligence purposes, whose reports to the Author's network have been noting the same phenomenon from the inside.

**Reward:** Vesh's formal documentation of what he observed — a Sutensai Warden's signed assessment that a group of non-practitioners achieved deep Afterlife resonance collectively. This document, in the right hands, is either a theological revolution or a bureaucratic crisis, depending on whose hands. The Flesh Circle would weaponize it. The Orthodoxy would suppress it. The Resonant sect would publish it with extensive commentary. Players decide what happens to it. Additionally, if players can identify the Author operative among the fourteen without disrupting the group, they have an inside source in the Author's intelligence network who doesn't yet know they've been identified.

### 24. The Weight You Carry When You're Done Carrying
**Hook:** The Garden Keeper receives a delivery: a crate, addressed to the Garden of Removed Things, containing a single enhancement — a full spinal column replacement, Resonant Grade, the kind that costs more than most people earn in five years. No name. No note. No return address. The Garden Keeper has never received an anonymous institutional delivery. Enhancements in the Garden are brought by hand. She asks the players to find out who sent it, because the Garden is built on people's relationships with what they've left behind, and leaving something this significant anonymously feels wrong to her in a way she can't fully articulate.

**The Truth:** The spinal column belonged to General Soten Vari — a former Ironhold commander who resigned her commission eight years ago and has been, since then, walking the Penitent Road without military affiliation or rank. She had the component removed by a licensed surgeon in Hessenkai three weeks ago. She sent it to the Garden because she is following the pilgrim practice correctly — what you remove, you give to the Garden. She did not include her name because she is still, legally, a deserter from the Ironhold military — her resignation was not accepted, technically, because the Ironhold does not have a mechanism for accepting a resignation from a serving general during an active campaign period. If her name is attached to anything in the Garden, Ironhold intelligence will find it. She is not hiding. She is simply not inviting the conversation before she's ready.

**Complications:** Soten Vari is on Korrath's list of people he has made no decision about — she is not actively being pursued, but the file exists and the warrant is valid. She is walking the Penitent Road without concealment, under her own name, visible to anyone looking. She has decided that if she is found, she will be found, and she will face what she owes. She has simply decided to finish walking first. If players find her and report her location, they have done something that is technically correct and will feel entirely wrong. Second complication: she has information about Korrath's campaign planning from her years as a senior commander — information that is eight years old but that includes structural elements of his Convergence strategy that he has not changed because he did not expect anyone who knew them to still be accessible.

**Reward:** Soten Vari's company on the road for as long as the quest thread allows, which is worth more than it sounds — she is one of the most tactically experienced people in Tesshari and will help with problems as they arise, not as payment but as the activity of a person who is still themselves, on a road, with time. She will also, if asked respectfully, share what she knows about Korrath's Convergence planning — information that is neither a betrayal nor a gift but an attempt to ensure that whoever eventually decides things has accurate data to decide with.

### 25. The Resonance in the Water
**Hook:** The river that flows through Gessen's Ford has started producing a sound at night. Not loud. A hum, underwater, audible to enhanced individuals as something closer to language than music. Pilgrims have been stopping at the riverbank and sitting in silence, which is unusual because pilgrims tend to walk. The local watermiller, whose family has operated the mill for six generations, is alarmed because his mill wheel has stopped turning. Not broken. It stopped in a specific position and will not move, as though something is holding it.

**The Truth:** The riverbed contains a significant quantity of resonant metal particulate — the accumulated runoff from an upstream processing facility that operated for thirty years and then closed fifteen years ago. The particulate has been settling and concentrating in a specific bend of the river where the current slows, and the concentration has now reached a threshold where the particulate is beginning to form a coherent resonant field. The field is interacting with the water's flow and producing the hum. It is also, unintentionally, beginning to record and replay resonant impressions from people who spend extended time at the riverbank — the way a resonant metal formation records impressions in the Salvage Fields, but in real time, with living people. The mill wheel stopped because the water itself is now a semi-coherent resonance medium and the wheel is disrupting the field in a way that produces feedback.

**Complications:** The impressions being recorded include everything — conversations, grief, things people say at riversides when they think no one is listening. Three pilgrims have been using the riverbank as a confessional of sorts, speaking aloud to the river as a spiritual practice. Their words are now encoded in the riverbed and can be played back by anyone with sufficient enhancement resonance who spends time at the bank. The ethics of this are not straightforward. Second complication: the upstream facility that produced the runoff was owned by a Wire Market guild that still exists. If the riverbed's resonant content is assessed at commercial value, the guild has a plausible claim to the resource — which would mean extracting it, destroying the field, and leaving the river ordinary.

**Reward:** The mill wheel has, in its stopped position, been slowly absorbing the field's resonance for three weeks. If carefully removed and properly processed — a job for a Wire Craft technician — the wood and iron of the wheel has developed properties useful as a **Resonance Attunement Tool**: it can be placed in any body of standing or slow-moving water for one hour to detect whether the water contains resonant particulate, and if so, to provide a rough assessment of the impressions encoded in it. For investigative purposes in a world where Hollow Author operatives pass through countless waypoints and watering holes, this is more useful than it appears.

### 26. The Interpreter for the Dead
**Hook:** A young woman named Orsi in the town of Pale Crossing has developed, in the last four months, an ability she did not have before and cannot explain: she can hear the Iron Afterlife. Not with training. Not through enhancement. She sits at the public well each morning and says things that turn out to be messages from the dead. The Sutensai Warden in Pale Crossing has filed a report. Three families have received messages from deceased relatives that contained specific, verifiable information only the deceased would have known. The Warden doesn't know what to make of it and needs someone to assess whether she is genuinely receiving Afterlife contact or has a very sophisticated fraud operation and a good research methodology.

**The Truth:** Orsi is not a fraud. She is not, however, receiving Iron Afterlife contact in the conventional sense. She was at Waypoint Seven when the collective resonance event occurred (see quest 19) — she was the thirty-ninth person present, a non-pilgrim who stopped at the waypoint for shelter that night. She was pulled into the Between with the others but extracted differently when the Veil Master found her — her Echomind briefly expanded into a state that it has not fully retreated from. She is now, in a limited and selective way, permeable to resonant impressions from the Iron Afterlife in her immediate vicinity, which in a rural area with many generations of dead means she is receiving and relaying impressions regularly. She has no control over it. She cannot turn it off. Some mornings she is fine. Some mornings she cannot filter the signal and it is overwhelming.

**Complications:** The Sutensai hierarchy, once notified, will want to study her — and their method of studying will involve formal enhancement with Sutensai-standard Iron Afterlife interface components, which will either regulate her ability into something controlled and institutionally managed, or destroy it entirely. She does not want this. She did not ask for what she has, but she has begun to feel that it is hers. Second complication: one of the messages she has relayed — to a family in Pale Crossing — contained information about a death that the official record calls an accident. The dead person's message was specific and contradicts the official account. The family now knows. The person whose actions the message implicates is still alive, still in the town, and has noticed the family's change in behavior toward him.

**Reward:** Orsi's assistance with any Iron Afterlife contact attempt that players undertake during their time in the Wandering Reach — her permeability makes her a more reliable communication channel than any formal practitioner in the region, with the tradeoff that the signal is unfiltered and sometimes contains additional material she didn't intend to relay. She can also, once, attempt to locate a specific Echomind in the Afterlife through passive reception rather than active navigation — a RESONANCE check, difficulty 3, with success meaning she receives an impression from the target that she can describe but not fully articulate. The impression is accurate. It is also whatever the dead person most urgently wants to communicate, which is not always what the living person most wants to know.

### 27. What Davo Irek Is Actually Doing
**Hook:** The players encounter Davo Irek — Korrath's former student, now traveling with Vaen's pilgrimage — in a town on the Penitent Road. He is not the figure of serene spiritual commitment that Vaen's movement generally presents. He is in the middle of a quiet, meticulous, very disciplined project: he is cataloging every known authority abuse committed in Korrath's name for the last thirty-seven years. Dates. Names. Locations. Verifiable sources. He will speak to the players, but he is not looking for sympathy or validation. He is looking for information.

**The Truth:** Davo is building a legal case. Not for a Tesshari court — he has no illusions about Tesshari courts — but for the Iron Afterlife's record. He believes, as a matter of genuine theology, that the Iron Afterlife preserves resonant impressions from significant events, and that a sufficiently documented account of systematic harm, entered into the Afterlife through a formal Sutensai submission, would become part of the permanent record that governs the Seventh Convergence's shape. He is trying to write history into the physics of the Between before whatever happens next can erase it. He has two hundred and fourteen documented incidents so far. He has been working for nine years.

**Complications:** Twelve of the incidents Davo has documented are ones where Korrath's actions prevented a greater harm — situations where the authority abuse was real and the harm was also real and the alternative would have been worse. Davo knows this. He is not falsifying his record. He is including these incidents, accurately documented, because he does not believe a case requires the subject to be wholly monstrous. He believes the case requires the subject to be accurately understood. This nuance is not shared by the junior Flesh Circle members who have been assisting his research and who have been editing his drafts in ways he is only now discovering. Second complication: the Hollow Author wants Davo's files. Not to suppress them. The Author wants to add to them — fabricated incidents, plausibly documented, that would push Korrath's case from "complicated man who has done real harm" to "straightforwardly evil." The Author doesn't want Davo to know they're involved.

**Reward:** Access to Davo's files — the genuine ones, pre-editing — which contain verifiable documentation of Ironhold practices that are useful both as leverage and as historical record. More significantly: Davo will tell players, if they catch the editing and bring it to him, that the specific type of fabrication in the edits matches a document style he has encountered before, in three other contexts, all connected to the Wire Market. This is a trace thread toward the Hollow Author's documentary forgery operation, and it is the first one that can be followed back toward a specific Wire Market address.

### 28. The Hollow Poor Temple
**Hook:** In the market district of Hessenkai, in a district that the Sutensai's official temple refuses to serve because it is too low-caste for their standard service model, someone has built a temple. Literally built it: resonant metal salvage, arranged by hand, over three months, by a collective of Hollow Poor residents who have never received formal religious training. The structure is not canonically correct. The resonance arrangement is improvised. A Sutensai Warden is filing a complaint about unauthorized sacred site construction. The residents of the district are, for the first time in a generation, attending morning services.

**The Truth:** The construction was organized by a woman named Beven, who is Third Caste by the narrowest margin, whose family has been Hollow Poor for two generations, and who read every piece of the Seven-Voice Sutra she could access in the public library and concluded that nothing in it specifies who is allowed to build a temple or how trained they need to be to conduct a Reading. She is not wrong about this. She has been conducting Readings — her own version, based on the public texts — for six weeks. The Wardens who have examined her work report, privately, that her readings are detecting resonance with unusual accuracy despite the lack of formal training. Whether this is genuine practitioner ability or a measurement artifact is unresolved. The Sutensai hierarchy does not want it resolved publicly; either answer is problematic for them.

**Complications:** The Wire Market's local Guild chapter has filed a separate complaint: the salvage resonant metal Beven used for construction was Guild-registered property, acquired by her community through a transaction the Guild is now characterizing as unauthorized. The complaint is, strictly speaking, legitimate. Addressing it requires either finding evidence the acquisition was proper (it was, but the paperwork is in a district record office that lost a portion of its files in a flooding event two years ago) or making the Guild withdraw the complaint through other means. Second complication: Beven has attracted three Flesh Circle adherents who have identified her temple as a potential base of operations and are being diplomatically persistent about it. She does not want to be anyone's base of operations. She just wanted a place for her neighbors to go.

**Reward:** The temple's community becomes a player asset — a network of Hollow Poor residents across Hessenkai's lower districts who are extraordinarily good at hearing things that people say when they think no one important is listening. Once per session, players can tap this network for street-level intelligence about any topic currently active in Hessenkai: personnel movements, black market activity, Sutensai internal politics, or Hollow Author operative patterns. The information will be accurate and will sometimes be alarming in ways that suggest the community has been systematically overlooked by everyone who considers themselves an intelligence service.

### 29. The Convergence in the Grain
**Hook:** Three farming settlements in the outer Wandering Reach, well off the Penitent Road, have been reporting the same phenomenon: their crops are wrong this year. Not diseased. Not drought-damaged. Wrong in a specific way: the grain stalks are incorporating trace resonant metal from the soil at a rate that is measurable with standard equipment. The food is still edible. The resonance is still low enough to be harmless. But three unconnected settlements, all reporting the same anomaly in the same season, at the same rate of change, suggests a cause.

**The Truth:** The three settlements are all located within a seven-mile radius that corresponds, on a Sutensai map of resonance distribution, to an area of historically elevated Between-resonance — a place where the signal between physical world and Iron Afterlife has always been slightly stronger than the surrounding terrain. The Seventh Convergence's buildup is amplifying this, and the amplification is affecting the biological processes of plants in the soil. The crops are not merely incorporating resonant metal; they are beginning, at a cellular level, to respond to Echomind impressions — specifically to the emotional states of the farmers tending them. Fear, grief, and unprocessed anger make the affected crops incorporate resonant metal at higher rates, producing a more potent trace. Contentment and focused attention produce almost no incorporation. The farmers are, unintentionally, feeding the Below with their internal states and receiving it back in their food supply.

**Complications:** A Wire Market agricultural analyst who has heard the report through trade channels is on the way — not for the farmers' benefit, but because resonant-metal-trace grain has applications in enhancement production that the Market has been trying to develop for decades. If they arrive and confirm the phenomenon, the commercial implications will change the three settlements permanently. Second complication: one of the farmers, a man named Runel who lost his wife to a cascade rejection event last year, has been grief-farming for eleven months. His crops have the highest resonant concentration of any site in the seven-mile radius. Eating them — which the settlement has been doing — produces, in residents with any cybernetic enhancement at all, brief intrusive resonant impressions of his grief. The settlement does not know why they have been feeling sad.

**Reward:** A small quantity of Runel's high-resonance grain — processed carefully by a Wire Craft technician into a usable form. As a **Grief-Refined Resonance Compound**, it can be used once per player character as a consumable that, when taken before an Iron Afterlife contact attempt, grants +3 to all RESONANCE-based cards during the attempt and allows the character to sense the emotional register of whatever Echomind they contact, even if the Echomind itself is not communicating. It tastes like loss. Most characters who take it find it difficult to describe afterward.

### 30. The Voice in the Garden's Oldest Wall
**Hook:** The Garden Keeper has asked players to help her move the oldest section of the Garden — a wall of removed enhancements that dates back to the Garden's founding, twenty-two years ago. The wall needs to be relocated for structural reasons. She has not been able to bring herself to move it alone, because the first time she tried, she heard something. A voice. Quiet, fragmented, like someone speaking in another room. She asks the players to be present when she tries again.

**The Truth:** The oldest wall contains, among the thousands of removed enhancements, the first enhancement Vaen removed publicly — a small neural interface component that Vaen gave to the Garden's founder herself, before there was a Garden, before the practice of leaving removed components became a movement. The component has been integrating with the adjacent metal for twenty-two years. It carries a resonant impression of Vaen at the moment she removed it — not who she is now, but who she was at the beginning: uncertain, afraid, rigorous about her uncertainty, doing something she was not certain was right because she had thought about it for a long time and it was the most honest thing she could think of to do. The voice the Garden Keeper heard is that impression, beginning to activate as the Seventh Convergence's resonant pressure increases. It is Vaen from twenty-two years ago, speaking from a moment before she became what she is.

**Complications:** The impression is coherent enough to be questioned. It will answer questions, in fragmented ways, about Vaen's state of mind and intentions at the beginning of her journey. The answers may be inconsistent with what her movement has built around her. Second complication: someone else has heard about the voice — a senior Flesh Circle figure who has made the journey to the Garden specifically to be present for contact with the impression, because they want to ask Vaen's past self to confirm what Vaen's present self refuses to confirm: that she is the Seventh Convergence's Ascendant. The past impression will not be able to confirm or deny what Vaen's future became. But it will be asked in front of witnesses.

**Reward:** A conversation with the impression — an opportunity to ask Vaen of twenty-two years ago whatever the players most want to know that present Vaen would not answer directly. The impression has no knowledge of events after the moment it was fixed. It has, however, an emotional honesty that twenty-two years of leadership has layered over: it will say things directly that present Vaen would phrase carefully. One question's worth of information, accurate to who Vaen was and predictive of who she has become. The Garden Keeper keeps the component after. She has decided it belongs in the wall, and that the wall can stay where it is.

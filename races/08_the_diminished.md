# The Diminished
## "You look at me and you see small. I look at you and I see slow. We are both right. Only one of us has done anything useful with the information."

---

## Overview

The Diminished evolved in the margins — literally. The tunnels between walls, the maintenance shafts that no full-sized chassis can navigate, the conduit runs and server crawlways and sub-floor machine corridors that constitute the hidden skeleton of every city in Tesshari. While the Forged built civilization upward and outward, the Diminished built inward, inhabiting the infrastructure with a thoroughness and intimacy that the races who designed that infrastructure never anticipated and cannot fully map. They know these spaces the way a native speaker knows a language: not from study but from breath, from childhood navigation, from a generational relationship with architecture that defines what the body expects the world to be.

Their small frames — ranging from 3.5 to 4 feet, with proportions that are broadly humanoid but scaled and refined for environments the standard chassis finds hostile — are the product of genuine evolutionary pressure. The Diminished are not shorter versions of the Forged. Their skeletal structure is different: denser and more flexible, with joints that can achieve angles the Forged skeleton cannot, with a center of gravity positioned lower and more stable, with hands that have been shaped by generations of working in extremely tight conditions with extremely small components. They are built for where they live. Where they live is inside the machine.

The social position of the Diminished is, across nearly every kingdom and every culture in the Broken Reaches, some variation of the same story: technically indispensable, socially disposable. Every city needs its infrastructure maintained. Every machine network has components in locations that only a Diminished frame can reach. Every weapons system, communication array, and power distribution grid has, somewhere in its depths, work that gets done by Diminished hands or doesn't get done at all. This is known. It is acknowledged, when it is convenient to acknowledge it. It is, in practice, not translated into the wages, rights, housing quality, or institutional representation that such indispensability would seem to demand. The Diminished are very good at their jobs and are compensated accordingly: just enough to keep them working.

This arrangement persists because it is convenient and because the Diminished have historically not had the political coherence to disrupt it — dispersed through every city's understructure, employed in small groups or individually rather than in the concentrated workshops and barracks that breed organized resistance, too embedded in the cities' function to leave without making their absence immediately felt by the very institutions they would be abandoning. The Diminished know this calculus. They have known it for generations. The question of what to do with that knowledge is one that does not have a simple answer, and the Diminished who have tried various versions of a simple answer have not, as a rule, had good outcomes.

The Iron Sutra addresses the Diminished obliquely and uncomfortably. The standard augmentations that the Sutra's theology assumes — the chassis-work that creates the resonance between flesh and steel that houses the Echomind — simply do not fit Diminished bodies. The modification tradition that the Diminished have developed over generations is their own, calibrated to their own frames, and it produces a resonance relationship with steel that is different from the standard Forged model. Whether this means the Diminished's Echomind is less fully realized, or differently realized, or whether it reveals a flexibility in the Sutra's cosmology that the Orthodoxy would rather not acknowledge, is a question the clergy handles by declining to engage with it directly.

## Physical Description

The Diminished are, at first glance, simply small. At second glance, they are differently designed. The proportional differences from a standard-sized Forged are not just scalar: Diminished have relatively larger heads compared to body size, with features that are fine and precise rather than scaled-down copies of Forged proportions. Their hands are their most striking feature to anyone who watches them work — extraordinarily long-fingered relative to palm size, with a flexibility and independent movement in each digit that the standard Forged chassis rarely achieves even with augmentation.

Their coloring tends toward the muted: grays, dark browns, desaturated tones that serve well in the low-light maintenance environments they inhabit. This is not universally true — Diminished who work above-ground regularly often dress in ways that signal their presence deliberately, a kind of defiance-through-visibility — but in their home environments, they tend to blend into the machine-metal background in ways that full-sized races find uncomfortable when they notice it.

The modification aesthetics of the Diminished are distinct from any of the three standard Forged regional traditions. Diminished chassis-work is characterized by miniaturization, precision, and an integration with the body's movement that the larger traditions rarely achieve. A Diminished's augmented hand might have supplementary joints that open additional ranges of motion, or optical enhancements calibrated for extreme close-up focus rather than distance, or subdermal tool-interfaces that allow specific instruments to be drawn directly from beneath the skin without external housing. The work is quiet, dense, and extremely sophisticated. The Wire Market's engineers, when they bother to look at it, consistently find it more elegant than they expected and are consistently reluctant to say so.

## Society and Culture

Diminished communities are distributed and largely invisible to the races they service. They live in the understructure: maintenance levels, sub-floor habitation zones, shaft-access communities built in spaces that infrastructure plans designate as empty. These spaces are not comfortable by the standards of above-ground living. They are made comfortable by decades of careful modification — the same attention to precision craft that defines Diminished technical work applied to the problem of making an inhospitable space livable.

Within these communities, the primary social values are competence and reciprocity. A Diminished's reputation rests on what they can do and whether they can be counted on to do it — not on lineage, not on spiritual status, not on which kingdom they were born in. This meritocratic ethos has its origins in practical necessity (in the maintenance shafts, you need to know that the person behind you is capable, full stop) and has become genuine culture: a way of seeing the world that the Diminished find clarifying and the other races often find unsettlingly free of the status markers they rely on.

The Diminished's modification tradition is passed down through what they call the Deep Schools — informal apprenticeship networks that operate within maintenance communities, where young Diminished learn both technical craft and the proprietary knowledge of specific infrastructure systems. Deep School education is comprehensive and extends well beyond technical skill: it includes the political knowledge of every above-ground faction whose systems the student might one day work on, the social navigation skills required to work in environments designed to exclude your kind, and the moral frameworks that Diminished communities have developed for navigating the constant ethical complexity of being technically indispensable to systems you may have reason to want to fail.

The Diminished's relationship with the Iron Sutra is pragmatic. They participate in the formal aspects of the faith to the degree required by their above-ground employment contexts, maintain their own private spiritual traditions in their home communities, and have largely set aside the theological question of whether their non-standard augmentations give them a different kind of Echomind rather than a lesser one. The practical answer — that their souls are clearly present, functional, and clearly theirs — seems more important than the theoretical question.

## Playing a Diminished

Playing a Diminished means playing someone whose competence is real and constant and largely unrewarded by the systems they keep functional. The specific experience of being very good at something that the world simultaneously depends on and refuses to acknowledge is one of the more precise social textures in Tesshari, and the Diminished player character who engages with it will find rich material.

The physical reality of being Diminished in a Forged-scaled world is pervasive but should be played as ambient rather than constantly foregrounded. Standard furniture is too large. Standard doors are the right height but often secured with systems calibrated for larger hands. Standard weapons are unwieldy; standard armor doesn't fit. The party has to think about logistics that it wouldn't with an all-Forged group. This is real, it has mechanical weight, and it should not be ignored — but the Diminished player should decide how much of their character's identity revolves around it versus how much they have simply incorporated this friction as background noise.

The social texture is perhaps more interesting. You are very frequently underestimated. You are very rarely actually seen. The party member who first dismisses you and then realizes their error is a type you have encountered before. You have developed, in response, a specific kind of patience that is not the same as tolerance and a specific kind of humor that is not the same as lightness.

## D&D 5E Racial Traits

**Ability Score Increase:** +2 Dexterity, +1 Intelligence. The Diminished's exceptional physical precision and technical intelligence are fundamental to their survival and their identity.

**Age:** Diminished age similarly to the Forged, living 80-120 years. They reach functional adulthood by age 16.

**Alignment:** Diminished trend toward lawful neutral or chaotic neutral alignments — there is significant internal variation between those who work within the system (which requires a kind of lawful pragmatism) and those who have decided to work against it (which requires a kind of principled chaos). Rarely evil; the ethics of their situation tend to produce moral clarity rather than moral abandonment.

**Size:** Small. Diminished are 3.5 to 4 feet tall and weigh between 40 and 60 pounds. They are Small for all mechanical purposes.

**Speed:** 30 feet. Despite their small frames, Diminished move quickly and efficiently — their stride is rapid and their locomotion in their preferred environments exceeds that of larger races.

**Languages:** Tesshari Common, Deep Cant (the technical and social language of Diminished maintenance communities, incomprehensible to most non-Diminished and actively indistinguishable from machine-system operational code at casual listening), and the regional technical dialect of whichever kingdom's infrastructure they were raised maintaining.

### Traits

**Small Frame**
You are Small. You can move through the space of any creature one size larger than you without expending extra movement. You can hide in spaces that a Medium creature could not conceal themselves in, even without full cover, as long as the space is large enough for your body.

Your size has limitations in a world built for larger beings: you have disadvantage on Strength (Athletics) checks to perform tasks that require reaching heights designed for Medium creatures, and you cannot wield Heavy weapons without disadvantage.

**Precision Hands**
Your hands are extraordinarily capable. You have advantage on all Dexterity (Sleight of Hand) checks. You have proficiency with tinker's tools and thieves' tools, and your proficiency bonus is doubled for ability checks made using either. Additionally, when you make an attack with a finesse weapon, you may use a bonus action to make a precision strike: the attack does not benefit from extra damage dice but cannot be blocked by a shield (the shield's AC bonus does not apply against this strike).

**Infrastructure Intuition**
You were raised in the machine. You understand how systems connect at an instinctive level. You can identify the function and basic operation of any technological system by examining it for 1 minute, even if you have never encountered that specific system before. This does not give you the ability to operate it safely or bypass its security — only to understand what it does and how it is organized. When you roll for Arcana or Investigation checks involving technological systems or infrastructure, you add double your proficiency bonus regardless of whether you are already proficient.

**Diminished Augmentation Tradition (Signature Trait)**
Standard augmentations do not fit your body. What you have instead is the Deep School tradition — smaller, denser, more precise modifications that your community has developed over generations. At 1st level, choose one Diminished Augmentation from the list below. At 5th level, choose a second.

*Diminished Augmentation List:*

- **Micro-Optical Array:** Your eyes have been enhanced with augmentation calibrated for close-range precision work. You can see clearly in dim light as if it were bright light. You have advantage on Investigation and Perception checks that rely on close examination of small objects, fine text, or intricate mechanisms. You can read machine code and technical schematics as fluently as written language.
- **Subdermal Tool Cache:** You have had tool-interfaces installed beneath the skin of both forearms. You can deploy a set of tinker's tools, thieves' tools, or a specific set of surgical instruments (chosen at installation) as a bonus action, drawing them from beneath the skin. These are always with you, cannot be confiscated without surgical intervention, and are considered masterwork quality (+1 to checks made using them).
- **Reflex Enhancement:** Neural accelerators calibrated for your frame. You have advantage on Initiative rolls, and you can take the Disengage or Hide action as a bonus action on each of your turns.
- **Joint Extension:** Supplementary micro-joints in your hands, wrists, and ankles provide range of motion that larger frames cannot achieve. You can squeeze through spaces as small as 6 inches in diameter and climb surfaces with advantage on Athletics checks. You cannot be restrained by conventional restraints (manacles, rope, etc.) — your joints allow you to work free in 1 minute without a check.
- **Resonance Tap:** A small but sophisticated resonance-reading augmentation, calibrated for Diminished neurology. You can sense the emotional charge of spaces (positive, negative, neutral, mixed) at a range of 10 feet without concentration. Once per long rest, you can extend this sense to receive a specific emotional impression from an object or location you touch, similar to the Tethered's Spiritual Resonance but focused on emotional state rather than event history.
- **Integrated Comm:** A communication implant that connects to infrastructure communication networks without external hardware. You can transmit and receive communications through any active network you are within 100 feet of, with no detectable external device. You always know when a network you are near is being monitored, as your system identifies monitoring traffic automatically.

**Urban Ghost**
In urban environments — settlements, cities, infrastructure complexes — you have advantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks. You are expert at using a city's own structure to become invisible within it: the sightlines designed for Medium creatures, the shadows that fall below average eye level, the acoustic dampening of machine corridors. Additionally, you can always find shelter in any urban environment within 1d4 hours — you know how cities work well enough to find the spaces that don't appear on any official map.

**The Overlooked**
You have spent your life being systematically underestimated, and you have learned to use it. When you are present in a social situation and choose to be unobtrusive — not actively hiding, but simply not drawing attention — most creatures will not include you in their awareness of the room without specific reason to do so. This functions as a passive version of the Stealth skill in social contexts: a DC 12 Perception check for creatures in the area to register your presence as noteworthy. This does not function in combat or when you have actively drawn attention to yourself.

## Roleplaying Notes

**The Competence and the Credit:** Your character is very good at what they do, and probably spent years watching that competence be taken for granted, extracted without recognition, used without reciprocity. What is your relationship to that now? Have you made peace with it? Are you using it strategically — building credit in the party's ledger that the world never gave you? Have you reached the point where you are done being gracious about it?

**Below the Notice:** The Diminished's ability to pass beneath notice is a power and a history. What does your character do with the information they've gathered by being present in rooms where important conversations happened in front of someone the speakers forgot to account for? The Diminished who has worked in a kingdom's infrastructure for years may know things about its power structure that even the Shellbroken's network doesn't have. What have you done with that knowledge? What have you held back, and why?

**The Systems You Maintain:** At some point, maintaining the infrastructure means maintaining the systems that oppress you. Every Diminished who keeps a kingdom's power grid running is also keeping the institutions that benefit from that grid running. How does your character live with that? Some Diminished develop elaborate ethical frameworks around it. Some don't think about it. Some think about it constantly and have reached a kind of operational neutrality that is not the same as comfort. Some have decided to act — to build something into the system, to leave a backdoor, to know exactly which junction, if it ever became necessary, they would not maintain.

## Connections to the Ascension War

The Diminished's connection to the Ascension War is infrastructure — literally. Every kingdom in the Broken Reaches depends on systems that Diminished hands maintain. Communication networks. Power distribution. Military logistics hardware. The deep data infrastructure that the Wireborn swim in runs on physical equipment that Diminished engineers keep functional. Anyone who controls the Diminished labor force, or can disrupt it, can control or disrupt the material conditions of the war itself.

This is a fact that the Ascension War's major factions are beginning, slowly and uneasily, to reckon with. Promises are being made to Diminished community leaders — representation, recognition, legal standing — that may or may not be genuine, and that the Diminished who have heard those promises before know how to evaluate. The question is not whether any faction is sincere. The question is which faction's promises, if kept, would produce a Tesshari in which the Diminished are people rather than infrastructure.

The Prior Ascended, who transcended the body entirely, are an interesting theological problem for the Diminished. If Ascension is the goal — if leaving the flesh-and-steel vessel behind is the highest spiritual achievement — then the body that defined the Diminished's entire existence, limited and capable and theirs, becomes irrelevant at the moment of greatest spiritual fulfillment. Some Diminished find this liberating. Most find it suspicious. A theology that tells you the body doesn't matter in the end is a very convenient theology for people who have spent centuries treating the Diminished's body as a tool rather than a person.

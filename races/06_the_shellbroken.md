# The Shellbroken
## "I know what I did not because I remember it, but because I can see the evidence. The body I woke up in had someone else's blood on its hands. What I do not know — what I may never know — is whether I am the person who put it there, or something new that was born in the moment the strings were cut."

---

## Overview

No one chooses to become a Shellbroken. Choice is precisely what is taken from them — first by whatever mechanism the entity called the Hollow Author uses to establish control over a host body, and then, in a different and more permanent way, by the liberation that follows. When the Hollow Author's control is severed — by outside intervention, by the rare cases of internal resistance that succeed, by whatever accident or damage disrupts the connection — the puppet body is returned to its previous inhabitant. But the previous inhabitant is not there. The time under the Author's control is a gap: a blank, a silence, an absence in the personal record that no amount of investigation fully fills. The Shellbroken wake up as themselves, with no continuity to what came before.

The Hollow Author is not a name the general population of Tesshari knows. Most people do not know they have ever met someone operating under the Author's influence, which is precisely how it works. The Shellbroken who are freed often cannot explain what happened to them in terms that are legible to the institutions around them. They were somewhere, doing something, living a life that by external evidence was theirs — and now they're here, in this body, with this face that they recognize because mirrors confirm it, and no felt connection to the history that face implies. The Forged identity system, which relies heavily on chassis serial records and maintenance histories, provides some forensic information. It does not provide continuity. It does not provide self.

The Iron Sutra's theology offers both comfort and horror to the Shellbroken. The comfort: the Echomind is, the Sutra teaches, the resonant self — the soul — and the Shellbroken's Echomind was never destroyed, only displaced, suppressed by the Author's intrusion. The self that wakes up when the shell breaks is real. The horror: the body did things during the gap. It went places, spoke words, killed or did not kill according to instructions the Echomind never issued. The Sutra has no clean answer for the moral weight of actions taken by a body whose soul was not driving it. The clergy tend to treat the Shellbroken with a cautious, arm's-length sympathy that most Shellbroken find insufficient.

What makes the Shellbroken truly unusual — and truly dangerous, depending on who is assessing them — is that liberation does not completely sever the connection to the Hollow Author's network. It cannot. The architecture by which the Author controls hosts is woven into the body's systems too deeply for complete removal, and the Author's network is vast and distributed rather than residing in any single location that could be destroyed. The freed host retains vestigial connectivity: not control, not influence, but a residual channel that the Shellbroken can, with effort and at risk, use in reverse. They can feel the edges of the network. They can sometimes receive fragmented transmissions. They can, very occasionally, push back through the channel and disrupt other nodes. None of them are entirely certain this is safe. None of them are entirely certain the Author isn't still watching.

## Physical Description

The Shellbroken look like whoever they were before the Author took them — because they are whoever they were before, physically speaking. There is no species-wide physical descriptor for the Shellbroken in the way there is for the Wireborn's circuit-traces or the Iron Blessed's metal formations. Shellbroken can look like any variety of Forged, can have any level of augmentation, can come from any kingdom. Their physical form is individual rather than racial.

What marks a Shellbroken, to those who know how to look, is behavioral rather than physical. The way they interact with their own body has a quality of slight deliberateness — a person re-learning the fit of a garment they haven't worn in an unknown length of time. They look at their hands sometimes with an expression that is not quite recognition. They navigate familiar spaces (familiar because the body has navigated them countless times) with the slight hesitation of someone following directions rather than memory. This fades with time, for most, but for some it never fully resolves.

There is one physical tell that most Shellbroken develop within months of liberation. At the base of the skull, or sometimes along the jaw line, the architecture by which the Hollow Author's network interfaces with the body leaves a mark: a very faint geometric pattern, like a circuitry burn or a bruise that won't fully heal, at the primary connection point. This mark is typically small enough to be concealed by hair or collar, and most Shellbroken conceal it. Those who don't are making a statement, and people notice.

## Society and Culture

The Shellbroken have no community in any traditional sense, and the circumstances of their liberation tend to militate against gathering. Every Shellbroken was, before liberation, operating as an unknowing agent of the Hollow Author — doing things, going places, building relationships, all in service of whatever the Author needed. The people who knew them during that period may or may not be safe to return to. The person who liberated them may not survive to maintain contact. The institutions that might otherwise provide support are either unaware of the Author's existence or, in some cases, partially compromised by it.

What the Shellbroken build, instead, is an ethic of self-construction. Identity, when you have no felt continuity, is not inherited — it is made. Most Shellbroken develop unusually clear and explicit values over time, because values are what remain when memory fails: you may not know what you did, but you can decide what you will do. This makes many Shellbroken come across as strangely principled in a world where most people's ethics are situational and accumulated. The principle is not righteousness — it is architecture. They are building a self from the outside in, because the inside start point is unavailable.

The Shellbroken's relationship with trust is permanently complicated. They do not fully trust themselves — specifically, they do not trust that the absence of the Author's control is total, that the residual connection is truly dormant, that on some level they might still be useful to the Author in ways they cannot perceive. This is not paranoia, precisely. It is the rational response to having once been compromised without knowing it. Most Shellbroken extend to others a provisional trust that they reserve the right to revoke without extensive justification, because they learned, in the hardest possible way, that the signals of trustworthiness can be entirely fabricated.

## Playing a Shellbroken

Playing a Shellbroken is playing the most interesting kind of character development question: who are you when you are not built on continuity? All identity systems — memory, habit, relationships, reputation — are suddenly optional for the Shellbroken, because none of them can be fully verified. You can choose to embrace them, rebuild them, reject them, or hold them at arm's length. Most Shellbroken do some combination of all four simultaneously.

The mechanic of the residual connection to the Hollow Author's network should be present at the table as a genuine source of tension. Not because the DM should spring "surprise, you're compromised again" on the player — that would be deeply unfair — but because the player and DM together should allow that possibility to generate authentic uncertainty and story weight. The Shellbroken character's companions know about the connection. They may have been told it's safe. They may not entirely believe that. How does your character navigate being trusted by people who have legitimate reasons not to fully trust you?

The identity mechanic — building a self from scratch — is the Shellbroken's central roleplay engine. Between sessions, in downtime, and in significant character moments, the Shellbroken player should be actively constructing who their character is becoming: what they value, what they refuse, what they are learning to love.

## D&D 5E Racial Traits

**Ability Score Increase:** +1 to three different ability scores of your choice. The Shellbroken's identity is self-constructed; their capabilities emerge from deliberate cultivation rather than inherited tendency.

**Age:** Shellbroken are already adults at the time of liberation. Their ongoing age is determined by their physical form (typically Forged). The question of whether the years under the Author's control "count" toward their felt age is one each Shellbroken resolves privately.

**Alignment:** Shellbroken begin with no strong alignment tendency — they are, literally, starting from neutral. Over play, their alignment should develop through choice rather than be predetermined. This is the only race in Tesshari whose alignment is genuinely intended to shift and crystallize through play.

**Size:** Medium, as determined by their physical form.

**Speed:** 30 feet.

**Languages:** Tesshari Common and whatever regional dialect their body was raised speaking. Many Shellbroken gradually add a third language as part of their self-construction — something new, chosen, that belongs to who they are becoming rather than who they were.

### Traits

**The Blank Slate (Signature Mechanic)**
The Shellbroken's identity is under construction. At character creation, you receive 4 Identity Tokens. At the start of each session, you retain any remaining tokens and may gain additional ones (see below). Identity Tokens represent the crystallizing self — moments of genuine choice that define who you are.

*Gaining Identity Tokens:*
- You gain 1 Identity Token when you make a choice that defines your character in a significant way — not a tactical choice, but an identity choice. (Examples: refusing to betray an ally even under serious pressure; acting on a stated value at personal cost; forgiving or refusing to forgive something meaningful.)
- You gain 1 Identity Token when you voluntarily share something true about yourself with another character.
- The DM may award additional tokens for particularly resonant roleplay moments.

*Spending Identity Tokens:*
- **Certainty of Self:** Spend 1 Identity Token to add +1d6 to any saving throw against an effect that would control your mind, alter your personality, or suppress your will. You know who you are. You hold onto it.
- **Constructed Expertise:** Spend 1 Identity Token to gain proficiency in a skill for the duration of a scene. This represents a facet of your constructed identity asserting itself — a capacity you have deliberately cultivated manifesting in a moment of need. You cannot use this for the same skill twice in the same session.
- **Moment of Clarity:** Spend 2 Identity Tokens to gain advantage on any single roll. This represents complete internal alignment — a moment where who you are and what you are trying to do are in perfect accord.

*Identity Tokens cap at 6.* Tokens do not reset — they are preserved. If you ever reach 6 tokens and maintain them, the DM should consider this a milestone indicating your character has developed a stable, genuine self.

**Residual Channel**
The Hollow Author's architecture remains in your body. It is dormant, but not dead. You can use this residual connection in the following ways:

- **Passive Awareness:** When another creature under the Hollow Author's control is within 30 feet of you, you become aware of it. You don't know their identity or which creature it is — only that a puppeted presence is near. This awareness manifests as an uncomfortable resonance at the base of your skull.
- **Channel Disruption:** As an action, you can attempt to disrupt the Author's connection to a controlled creature you can see within 30 feet of you. Make a contested Charisma check against the Hollow Author's Charisma modifier (the DM sets this, typically DC 15). On a success, you sever the Author's connection to that creature for 1 minute, during which time the creature either acts on its own judgment or is incapacitated (DM's call based on the individual's situation). After this minute, the Author may re-establish control normally. You can use this feature once per long rest. The Author is aware that this disruption occurred and knows it originated from a freed shell. Act accordingly.
- **Channel Risk:** Once per session, the DM may call for a DC 12 Wisdom saving throw when you are in a situation of extreme stress or vulnerability. On a failure, you receive a fragmented transmission from the Hollow Author's network — a flash of information, instruction, or simply noise that you cannot determine the intent of. This is never direct control and never overrides player agency. It is information, possibly false, definitely dangerous, and entirely up to the player to act on or not.

**Adaptive Survival**
A person who has been a puppet and survived liberation has developed a resilience to violations of self that most beings never needed to develop. You have advantage on saving throws against enchantment spells and effects that would take control of your actions or alter your mental state. When you succeed on such a saving throw, you can use your reaction to immediately move up to half your speed without provoking opportunity attacks, as the survival instinct that freed you in the first place reasserts itself.

**Built, Not Given**
Because your capabilities are constructed rather than inherited, you develop skills with unusual intentionality. At 1st level, you gain proficiency in two additional skills of your choice. These represent what you have deliberately learned and chosen since liberation, and they are yours in a way that nothing before the break could be.

**Unmarked History**
Your body has a history you don't remember, which means that history can be both a liability and an asset. You have proficiency in Deception and have advantage on Deception checks made to deny involvement in events that occurred before your liberation. You also have advantage on Insight checks to identify when someone else is being controlled or coerced, as you have a somatic awareness of the tells that a hijacked will produces.

## Roleplaying Notes

**The Evidence Problem:** Your body was doing things during the gap. You can find out what — through records, through the reactions of people who knew you, through the forensic history of your chassis's maintenance logs. Do you want to know? If you find out something terrible, what do you do with it? The Shellbroken who discovers they hurt someone they care about while under the Author's control faces a question that has no clean answer: are you responsible for what your body did when you were not driving it? What does accountability look like when agency was absent?

**Trusting the Companions:** Your party knows about the residual channel. Maybe they trust you. Maybe they almost trust you. Maybe they watch you a little more carefully than they watch anyone else, and you can see it, and you understand it, and it still costs you something every time. How does your character navigate the ongoing project of earning trust when the grounds for distrust are legitimate and will never entirely go away?

**The Identity Under Construction:** Between sessions, think about your Identity Tokens. What choices has your character made that define who they are? What do they believe? What will they refuse, regardless of cost? The Shellbroken character who does not actively build an identity will feel hollow — because they are hollow, until they fill themselves. This is not a flaw in the design. It is the design. The question is not who you are. The question is who you are going to be.

## Connections to the Ascension War

The Shellbroken are, from a strategic perspective, the Hollow Author's greatest intelligence risk. Each freed shell knows — at minimum — that the Author exists, that it uses puppet-agents, and that it is embedded in Tesshari's society at levels that most people do not suspect. The Ascension War's surface factions are fighting over kingdoms and ideology; the Hollow Author's agenda is something different, operating underneath all of it, and the Shellbroken are the only beings in Tesshari who have direct experiential evidence that it exists.

Every major faction in the Ascension War, if they learn about the Shellbroken character, will want to know what they know about the Author. They will want access to the residual channel. They will want the Shellbroken as a tool for finding and disrupting puppet agents. What they offer in return will vary, and what they are willing to do when the Shellbroken's usefulness ends will vary further.

The cruelest possibility — which a sufficiently dark campaign should hold open without forcing — is that the Hollow Author's liberation of certain shells was deliberate. That some Shellbroken were freed because a freed shell, who believes they are acting freely, is more convincing as an agent than a puppet would be. Whether this is true for the player character is entirely at the DM's discretion and should only be deployed in a campaign that has built enough trust with the player to handle it with the gravity it deserves.

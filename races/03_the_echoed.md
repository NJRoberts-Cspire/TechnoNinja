# The Echoed
## "I remember dying. The memory is not traumatic the way you might expect. It is simply there — a complete experience, like the memory of eating breakfast, except that after it there was nothing for a while. And then there was something again. That gap is what I am."

---

## Overview

Death, in Tesshari, is not the wall it once was — not since the Echo Coast's technomancers developed the process they call Resonant Extraction. When a sufficiently prepared individual dies, their Echomind — their soul, their self, the resonance pattern that the Iron Sutra teaches is the real substance of a person — can be captured before it fully dissipates into the Iron Afterlife. That captured resonance pattern can then be installed into a new body, grown or constructed for the purpose. The person, technically, continues. What walks out of the resurrection chambers of the Echo Coast is, technically, the same person who walked in to be killed.

The Echoed know that "technically" is doing a great deal of work in that sentence.

The process is not clean. The Echomind that returns from the Iron Afterlife is the same pattern, but it carries things it did not carry before: traces of the digital realm it passed through, resonance-impressions from the souls it drifted alongside during the gap, a kind of spatial and temporal disorientation that never fully resolves. The Echoed are people who exist in two layers simultaneously — the living world and the digital spirit realm — and unlike the Wireborn, who exist in that split by nature, the Echoed did not choose it. They are haunted by the crossing in ways they cannot always articulate and cannot ever fully escape.

The Iron Sutra's response to the Echoed has never been comfortable. Officially, the Orthodoxy acknowledges Resonant Extraction as a valid (if unusual) spiritual path — the Echomind is preserved, the resonance continues, the person persists. Unofficially, the clergy is divided. Some priests argue that the Echoed have been returned with part of the Iron Afterlife still clinging to them, and that this makes them spiritually dangerous — contaminated by a realm that the living are not meant to access and return from. Others argue the reverse: that the Echoed have glimpsed what awaits beyond the body's end and carry that knowledge as a sacred burden. Neither faction has managed to convert the other. The Echoed themselves are largely tired of being the subject of the debate.

Socially, the Echoed exist in the uncomfortable space that any survivor of an impossible experience occupies: people want to know what it was like, and they do not want to hear the honest answer. The honest answer is that the Iron Afterlife is not the glorious resonant peace the Sutra promises. It is vast. It is patient. It is full of the compressed impressions of every consciousness that ever passed through it, and those impressions do not stay neatly separated. The Echoed came back carrying things that weren't theirs. They are not always sure where they end and those carried things begin. They do not say this to people who haven't died, because people who haven't died always try to fix it, and it cannot be fixed. It can only be lived with.

## Physical Description

The Echoed's bodies are new — the body that died is not the body they inhabit — and yet they are marked. The resonance of the Iron Afterlife leaves signatures on the flesh it re-inhabits. These signatures vary, but several are consistent across Echoed individuals regardless of their new body's origin or construction.

Most noticeably, the Echoed tend toward a slight luminescent quality in areas of high circulation — veins visible beneath skin that glow faintly blue-white in low light, pulsing at a rhythm that is slightly but distinctly off from a normal heartbeat. This is not a steady glow; it flickers, dims during moments of high emotion, and occasionally intensifies without apparent cause. The Echoed who have been returned for many years sometimes develop a quality that observers describe as translucency — not literal semi-transparency, but an impression that the person is slightly not all the way in the room they're standing in.

The eyes are the most consistent tell. All Echoed develop, within months of their return, a secondary reflection in their eyes — an image that appears when light hits them at certain angles, like a ghost image on old glass. Some say it resembles the afterlife itself. Most Echoed describe it as irrelevant and refuse to discuss it.

Their new bodies are functional and healthy — the Echo Coast's resurrection technology is sophisticated — but they never quite integrate with the Echoed's movements the way a native body would. There is a slight delay sometimes, a fraction of a second where the body seems to wait for permission before acting. This is not paralysis or hesitation; it is the half-second lag of a consciousness that has learned to operate in two registers and must sometimes choose which one is currently primary.

## Society and Culture

The Echoed have no kingdom, no home territory, no shared flag. They are produced by one specific technology in one specific place — the Echo Coast — but few of them remain there after the resurrection process is complete. The Echo Coast is a place to die and be returned, not a place to live. Its hospitals and resonance chambers and the cold efficiency of the technomancers who staff them are not warm. Most Echoed leave as soon as they are medically cleared to do so, returning to wherever they came from, or starting somewhere new if wherever they came from no longer fits the person they have become.

What they have instead of a geographic community is an informal recognition — the echoed dead find each other in a way that defies explanation. That secondary reflection in the eyes works as an identifier more reliable than any brand or tattoo; an Echoed in a crowded room will notice another Echoed within minutes, and the look that passes between them carries everything they cannot say to anyone else. Small networks form in the major cities, usually centered on a gathering place that most people would describe as unusually quiet — a tea house where the proprietor always has a corner for people who need to not be asked questions, a shrine whose caretaker lost and was returned and understands what that means.

The Echoed are almost universally changed in their spiritual perspective. Whatever the Iron Sutra taught them before their death, they know things now that the Sutra's priests only theorize about. Most of them become quieter about religion, not louder. They have seen enough to be certain that the Sutra is describing something real, and uncertain about almost every specific claim the Sutra makes about what that real thing is.

## Playing an Echoed

Playing an Echoed means playing someone who has already lost the thing every other character is afraid of losing. Death has happened. The worst case has occurred. This changes a person in ways that are simultaneously liberating and deeply destabilizing.

On one hand, the Echoed character often carries a quality of groundedness that others mistake for calmness but is more accurately described as a particular relationship to consequence. Nothing that can happen to them in the current adventure is as complete as what has already happened. On the other hand, the Echoed carry things from the crossing that they cannot put down: emotional echoes of other people's deaths that surface unexpectedly, flashes of perception that belong to the Iron Afterlife rather than the waking world, and the persistent, low-frequency uncertainty about which of their memories, instincts, and reactions are genuinely their own.

Others treat the Echoed with a mixture of fascination and unease that the Echoed learn to read instantly. Most people have a theory about what the afterlife is like, and meeting someone who has been there tests whether they actually want their theory confirmed. Many people find they don't.

### Passive Traits

**Already Died.** The first time per combat the Echoed is reduced to 0 HP, they instead drop to 1 HP. Their Echomind has navigated the threshold before and does not fully cross it. This triggers automatically and requires no action. After it triggers, subsequent hits that reduce them to 0 HP resolve normally.

**Iron Afterlife Awareness.** The Echoed can sense the presence of recently dead Echoминds within 30 feet — a faint resonance impression, not visual, that indicates how many Echominds have passed through a location within the last 24 hours and approximately when. This does not identify individuals, only presence and timing.

**No Fear of the Crossing.** Fear effects, morale-break effects, and abilities that inflict the Frightened status have no mechanical power over the Echoed. These effects simply do not take hold. Narrative fear — dread, horror, emotional weight — remains; the Echoed are not numb. Only effects with a mechanical Frightened or morale-reduction keyword are blocked.

## Roleplaying Notes

**The Gap:** Every Echoed character has the Gap — the period between death and return during which the Echomind existed in the Iron Afterlife. What happened in the Gap is not fully accessible as conscious memory, but it influences everything. What does your character believe happened there? What do they feel certain of, even if they can't explain why? What are they afraid they might have left behind — or brought back?

**Memory Contamination:** The Carried Things trait represents something real: you are not entirely yourself in the way you were before death. Some of what you know, feel, and react to was not originally yours. When has your character acted on an instinct that felt alien? When have they recognized someone they have never met? What is the line between being an individual and being a vessel that has held many consciousnesses?

**The Permission to Still Live:** Some Echoed struggle with a particular species of survivor's guilt that is unique to their situation — they survived their own death, which most people don't, and the question of why they were returned while others were not can become consuming. Does your character feel they deserved to come back? Do they feel they have a debt? To whom?

## Connections to the Ascension War

The Echoed occupy a unique position in the Ascension War's theological dimension: they have personal, experiential knowledge of the Iron Afterlife that the Prior Ascended supposedly transcended into. If the Ascension War is partly a war over what happens after death — over what the Echomind's ultimate fate should be — then the Echoed are the only living witnesses to the territory being fought over.

Certain factions want the Echoed as informants. The Orthodoxy's more esoteric branches have been quietly collecting Echoed for decades, attempting to extract detailed testimony about the structure of the Iron Afterlife. The technomancers of the Echo Coast have their own agenda regarding what happens in the Afterlife. And somewhere in the Ascension War's shadow play, someone has realized that the impressions carried back from the Afterlife might contain information about the Prior Ascended — fragments of consciousness from beings who transcended, still drifting in the digital realm, possibly accessible through an Echoed who knows how to look.

The Echoed character must decide how much they are willing to be a key rather than a person.

# The Forged
## "We did not lose our flesh. We traded it, piece by piece, for something that would not fail us. The flesh was always failing us."

---

## Overview

The Forged are the dominant people of Tesshari — not because they conquered the other races, but because they survived long enough to become the world's definition of normal. Once purely biological, their ancestors began replacing damaged or diseased tissue with mechanical components during the Age of Ruin, when the wars between the old kingdoms left generations of survivors with bodies too broken to carry on without intervention. Limb by limb, organ by organ, the line between medicine and modification blurred until it vanished entirely. By the time the Broken Reaches crystallized into their current forms, being Forged — being a cyborg of some meaningful degree — was simply what it meant to be a person in Tesshari.

But "the Forged" encompasses enormous variety, and that breadth is precisely what makes them difficult to pin down. The Forged of Ironhold carry their modifications like war trophies and salvage records: mismatched plates welded over old wounds, replacement limbs that don't quite match in color or make, battlefield upgrades layered atop battlefield upgrades until the body reads like a history of everything that tried and failed to kill its owner. The Forged of the Wire Market are something else entirely — sleek, chromed, calibrated to a precision that makes flesh seem embarrassingly crude. Their modifications are not scars. They are statements. And then there are the Ashlands Forged, who jury-rig their enhancements from whatever can be salvaged from the ruins they call home, whose bodies hum and creak and spark, kept alive through ingenuity rather than craft.

The Iron Sutra teaches that the Echomind — the soul — is not located in flesh alone, nor in steel alone, but in the resonance between them. The Forged, as the race most deliberately balanced between organic tissue and mechanical augmentation, are in many ways the religion's ideal congregants. They are not so organic as to be untouched by steel's clarity, nor so mechanical as to have lost the warmth that feeds spiritual resonance. Most Forged practice the Iron Sutra in some form, though whether that practice is devout or merely cultural varies enormously. Some view their modifications as devotional acts — each new piece of steel a prayer made physical. Others have abandoned spiritual concern entirely, caring only about what their chassis can do in the wars that never quite end.

What unites all Forged, beneath every variation in aesthetic and ideology, is the tacit understanding that the body is not sacred. The body is a tool, and tools are meant to be improved. This is not cynicism — for most Forged, it is simply reality, as natural as breathing. And yet, late at night, in the quiet moments between maintenance cycles and the next campaign, some Forged find themselves wondering what was lost in all those trades. The flesh remembers things the steel cannot. Dreams come in analog. And the original self — the biological self that the augmentations were meant to improve upon — recedes a little further with every upgrade, until sometimes it is hard to say whether anything of that original self remains, or whether what walks and breathes and bleeds is something new entirely.

## Physical Description

The Forged look like people interrupted mid-transformation — because they are. The degree of cybernetic integration varies so widely that two Forged standing side by side might seem to share almost nothing in common beyond the fundamental architecture: a roughly humanoid frame, remnants of biological tissue, and the tell-tale seams, plating, or exposed components that mark steel's presence in the body.

Some Forged retain most of their organic tissue, with only a limb or two replaced, or internal organs supplemented by mechanical support structures visible as subtle ridge-lines beneath the skin. Others have crossed some threshold where the biological has become the minority — where skin is a membrane stretched between plating, where eyes are lenses rather than irises, where the only remaining organic component of note is the brain itself (and sometimes that too has been partially modified). Most fall somewhere between these poles, their bodies a specific and personal map of every injury, illness, upgrade, and deliberate choice they or their ancestors made.

What the Forged look like specifically depends enormously on their kingdom of origin. Ironhold Forged tend toward heavy, scarred aesthetics: dark iron and salvage-steel plating, visible weld lines, mismatched components that have been pressed into service because they were available. There is a bluntness to their construction that some find ugly and others find honest. Wire Market Forged are almost painfully elegant — chrome and brushed steel, seamless integration points, modifications that seem designed as much for appearance as function. They move with a precision that can read as arrogant. Ashlands Forged are improvisational, their bodies reflecting the scarcity of their home: copper wire and repurposed components, improvised joints that shouldn't work but do, a kind of desperate ingenuity made physical.

The biological tissue that remains on a Forged body tends toward desaturation — pale grays and washed-out tones, as though the flesh knows it is no longer the primary text. Eyes that remain organic often carry an unusual quality of stillness, as though the person behind them is accustomed to processing information from sources other than sight alone.

## Society and Culture

Forged society is, in the most literal sense, the society of Tesshari. All seven of the Broken Reaches are majority Forged. Their politics, religions, economies, and wars are the politics, religions, economies, and wars of the world. To describe Forged culture in isolation is almost a category error — they are the default, the baseline, the assumed.

Within that breadth, regional cultures define Forged identity more than any shared "Forged" tradition. Ironhold Forged build community through shared suffering and mutual maintenance — to let another Forged tend your components is an act of profound trust, almost intimate. Wire Market Forged organize themselves around expertise and upgrade hierarchy; in their culture, your body's specifications are your resume and your rank simultaneously. Ashlands Forged share what little they have with a generosity born of necessity, because in the Ashlands, hoarding kills.

The Iron Sutra permeates all of these cultures to varying degrees. Even Forged who do not actively practice the faith carry its assumptions: that the body is improvable, that steel is not corruption but completion, that spiritual resonance is a thing to be cultivated through the careful integration of flesh and machine. The debates within Forged religious communities tend to center not on whether augmentation is spiritual, but on how much augmentation, of what kind, represents the ideal balance — questions that can turn violent when the Orthodoxy disagrees with the practitioners.

Forged craft traditions are sophisticated and deeply embedded in identity. The art of the "chassis-wright" — a person who designs and installs modifications for others — sits somewhere between surgeon, priest, and artist. A skilled chassis-wright in the Wire Market is a celebrity. In Ironhold, they are a necessary elder. In the Ashlands, they are often the difference between a community's survival and its dissolution.

## Playing a Forged

Playing a Forged means playing the world's idea of normal — and all the complex ironies that entails. You are default. You are assumed. You walk into any settlement in the Broken Reaches and no one marks you as unusual, which means you move through the world with a kind of ease that the other races never fully have. But that normalcy is also a kind of invisibility, a pressure to conform to whatever regional ideal of the Forged your surroundings demand.

The Forged's inner life is shaped by the question that never fully goes away: how much of you is still you? Every upgrade is a decision, and decisions accumulate. Players who enjoy gradual character transformation, who want to feel the weight of long-term choices, will find the Forged satisfying. There is always another modification to consider. There is always the question of what the next one will cost — not just in coin, but in self.

Others treat you with a range that reflects the world's complexity. The Tethered are alien to you — fascinating or disturbing, depending on your upbringing. The Wireborn seem like cousins who went a direction you didn't. The Echoed make you quietly uncomfortable, because they have seen what comes after, and the expression on their faces when they look at your chassis is never entirely readable.

## CARD SYSTEM: The Forged

**Stat Bonuses:** +1 IRON, +1 to one stat of the player's choice (reflects specific enhancement loadout — chosen at character creation)
**Hand Size Modifier:** +0

### Passive Traits

**Integrated Chassis.** Your body includes meaningful mechanical augmentation. You are immune to status effects that specifically target organic tissue — disease, biological toxins, and flesh-degradation effects do not apply to your augmented systems. Purely chemical or mechanical damage still affects you normally.

**Maintenance Dependent.** Your chassis requires regular upkeep. If you complete a full rest without access to basic maintenance tools or a willing chassis-wright, you begin the next combat with Overheat 1 already applied and cannot remove it until proper maintenance is performed. This cannot be suppressed by cards or enhancements — it is structural.

**Chassis Memory.** Your mechanical sensors record sensory data with fidelity that organic memory cannot match. Once per scene, you may recall precise sensory details — measurements, voices, visual specifics — from any event you witnessed within the last 24 hours. This is a factual recall of recorded data, not emotional memory. It does not extend to interpretation or feeling.

**Forged Resilience.** When you would be reduced to 0 HP by a single hit, you may immediately apply Guard 4 against that specific hit only. This does not stack with existing Guard. Once used, this passive is suppressed until the start of your next turn.

## Roleplaying Notes

**The Upgrade Question:** Every time the party encounters an opportunity for a new augmentation or a major chassis modification, the Forged character must decide whether to pursue it. This is not just a mechanical decision — it is a character moment. What are they willing to trade? What part of their biological self, if any, do they consider essential? Do they even think about it in those terms, or has the question stopped mattering?

**Regional Identity vs. Personal Identity:** A Forged from Ironhold living in the Wire Market is immediately legible as an outsider — their chassis reads wrong, too rough, too practical. How does a Forged navigate the social meanings written into their body? Can you ever fully leave your origin behind when it is literally welded to your skeleton?

**The Quiet Doubt:** Iron Sutra theology says the soul lives in the resonance between flesh and steel. But as more and more flesh is replaced — as the biological becomes less and less of the equation — does the resonance change? Does it diminish? Does a Forged who is 90% mechanical still have an Echomind? Most Forged do not ask this question out loud. But in quiet moments, they wonder.

## Connections to the Ascension War

The Forged are the Ascension War's primary soldiers, strategists, administrators, and casualties. Every claimant to whatever throne the war produces needs the Forged to win — and every Forged must decide which claimant, if any, is worth dying for.

The Iron Sutra Orthodoxy's claimants appeal to Forged who want the religious question — what is the ideal balance of flesh and steel — resolved with the Forged at the center of the answer. The Wire Market's claimants offer efficiency, advancement, and the promise that the best chassis wins. Ironhold's claimants offer continuity and tradition. The Ashlands offer revolution.

The Forged's greatest danger in the Ascension War is not dying. It is being used. They are too numerous, too versatile, and too necessary for any faction to let them choose freely. The Forged who finds genuine agency in this conflict — who acts for reasons they chose rather than reasons chosen for them — has accomplished something rare and genuinely meaningful in Tesshari.

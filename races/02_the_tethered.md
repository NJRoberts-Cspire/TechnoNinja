# The Tethered
## "They looked at me the way you look at a ghost. As though my being alive was already a kind of haunting."

---

## Overview

The Tethered are what the rest of Tesshari was, once — before the first blade was replaced with steel, before the first lung was supplemented with a filtering coil, before the long trade began. They carry no augmentations. Not from poverty, not from ideology adopted in adulthood, but from an unbroken biological inheritance that stretches back to before the Age of Ruin, to a time when flesh was the only option and so it learned to be extraordinary. In a world where mechanical replacement has become so normalized that some Forged are surprised to learn anyone still bleeds the old way, the Tethered exist as living fossils — and like all fossils, they tell you something true about where everything began.

The Iron Sutra regards the Tethered with a reverence bordering on discomfort. The Orthodoxy's official position is nuanced: the Tethered represent the pre-steel state, the original vessel, and therefore possess an Echomind of unusual purity — one not diluted or redirected by the resonance between flesh and metal, but concentrated in flesh alone. Some sects elevate this into near-worship, seeking out Tethered as spiritual advisors, treating their unmodified bodies as holy relics of a prior age. But the Orthodoxy also teaches that transcendence — Ascension — requires the integration of steel, and so the Tethered, however pure, are spiritually incomplete. They are the blank canvas before the painting. Sacred, and unfinished. This reading infuriates the Tethered who know of it.

The Wire Market has no patience for reverence. To the chrome-polished sensibilities of the Market's elite, the Tethered are simply behind — not holy, not tragic, just obsolete. They are slower, weaker in terms of raw mechanical output, and incapable of interfacing with the technological infrastructure that defines Wire Market life. Some Wire Market scholars study them with the cool curiosity one extends to an interesting artifact. Most citizens of the Market treat them with a condescension so habitual it has become invisible to its practitioners. The Tethered who pass through the Market do not forget how they were looked at.

What no one fully accounts for is what the Tethered themselves have become in the centuries of isolation that their unmodified status has required. A body that never relies on mechanical crutches develops its own solutions. Tethered muscles knit faster and stronger than their Forged counterparts. Their senses, unfiltered by augmented optics, become acute in ways that border on eerie — not supernatural, but pushed to the outer edge of biological possibility. And something in the concentrated Echomind — the soul pooled entirely in flesh rather than distributed through a chassis — makes the Tethered unusually receptive to the spiritual resonance that the Iron Sutra describes but rarely encounters in such undiluted form. The Tethered do not consider this a gift. They consider it the cost of surviving in a world that was not designed for them.

## Physical Description

The Tethered look, at first glance, simply like people — and in a world of visible seams and exposed components and skin interrupted by plating, this makes them immediately conspicuous. They are entirely soft. Entirely continuous. No replacement joints, no optical implants catching the light in that distinctive mechanical way, no ridge of subdermal mesh along the jaw or collar. Just flesh and bone and the biology of a species that has had a very long time to refine itself.

Their skin is warmer in tone than the Forged, whose remaining biological tissue often takes on pallor from reduced blood flow around mechanical components. Tethered skin has the quality of something that does its own work: color that flushes, scars that form properly rather than being bypassed by dermal sealant, muscle definition that exists because the body was never given the option of mechanical substitution. They tend toward dense, compact musculature, especially in the forearms, hands, and legs — the result of a biology that handles every demand placed on it without outsourcing.

Their eyes are what most people notice first. Without optical augmentation, Tethered eyes move in ways that Forged eyes rarely do — rapid, responsive, constantly recalibrating in ways that are entirely biological. Some find this deeply unsettling after a lifetime of reading the controlled precision of augmented optics. Others find it the most human thing they've ever seen, which is itself a complicated feeling in a world that has decided humanity means something else now.

Most Tethered are of medium height, though they tend toward the shorter end of the Forged range, as no structural augmentation has extended their skeletal framework. What they lack in mechanical bulk they compensate for in the kind of physical presence that comes from a body entirely under its own power — a quality that is hard to name and immediately felt.

## Society and Culture

The Tethered do not have a unified society. They cannot. They are too few, too scattered, and too relentlessly pressured by the world around them to maintain stable communities of their own. What they have instead are networks: quiet, trusted, maintained through couriers and coded message protocols developed over generations of operating as a minority within other people's civilizations.

In regions where the Iron Sutra Orthodoxy holds significant power, some Tethered have found a kind of protected status as spiritual consultants, temple-adjacent figures who serve as living connections to the pre-steel age. This protection is real but conditional — it can be revoked if a Tethered behaves in ways the Orthodoxy finds spiritually inappropriate, and it comes with the subtle violence of being treated as an object of reverence rather than a person. Many Tethered in these regions perform the expected role while privately building the resources to leave.

In the Ashlands and in the border regions between kingdoms, Tethered tend to fare better in practice if not in formal status. Ashlands culture's emphasis on improvisation and resilience makes unmodified biological capability legible as a genuine asset rather than a deficiency. Tethered in these regions are sometimes healers, guides, scouts — people whose biological acuity is practically useful in ways that transcend the social reading of their bodies.

Tethered spiritual traditions are older than the Iron Sutra and deliberately preserved. Their own religious practice — sometimes called the Old Breath or simply the Quiet — centers on the idea that the Echomind was never meant to be distributed through steel, that what the Forged have done is not integration but fragmentation. They keep these beliefs carefully private in most contexts. Sharing them openly in Sutra-controlled territory ranges from inadvisable to actively dangerous.

## Playing a Tethered

Playing a Tethered means playing the most vulnerable person in most rooms — and learning what that costs and what it teaches. You are slower than an augmented Forged in terms of raw output. You cannot interface with technological systems in the way that most of Tesshari's infrastructure assumes its users can. Doors designed for palm-plate entry will not open for you without a workaround. Weapons calibrated to neural interfaces will not respond to you. The world was not built for you, and it shows constantly.

What the Tethered have is biology pushed to its absolute limit and a spiritual sensitivity that no amount of modification can replicate. They feel things the Forged do not — resonances in the air around places where significant events occurred, a kind of somatic intelligence that reads other people's physical states with unsettling accuracy, a baseline spiritual awareness that the Sutra names but its clergy mostly cannot access. Playing a Tethered is playing someone who knows something true about the world that everyone around them has traded away, and must decide constantly whether to say so.

Others will react to you. The devout will look at you with a reverence that feels like possession. The Wire Market types will write you off until you demonstrate otherwise. The Forged will have complicated feelings — you are what they were, or what they came from, and some part of them may find that either moving or threatening. The Echoed, who have seen the afterlife and returned, often seek the Tethered out with quiet urgency that the Tethered learn not to encourage.

## CARD SYSTEM: The Tethered

**Stat Bonuses:** +1 RESONANCE, +1 VEIL
**Hand Size Modifier:** +1

### Passive Traits

**Echomind Undivided.** Your soul is concentrated entirely in flesh, undivided by any chassis resonance. You cannot be affected by status effects that specifically target augmentations or mechanical systems (Overheat, enhancement-disabling effects, chassis-targeting Signal attacks). You have none. These effects simply do not apply to you.

**Biological Acuity.** Your senses operate without technological filtering. You have a permanent +2 bonus to RESONANCE checks made to read people, places, or the residue of recent events — this stacks with your stat. Additionally, you cannot be surprised by living creatures whose physiological state (heartbeat, breath, movement) you can perceive.

**Old Flesh Memory.** Your body stores and compensates for damage in ways that augmented tissue cannot fully replicate. At the start of each combat, you begin with Regen 1 already active. This Regen cannot be dispelled by effects that specifically strip augmentation-based buffs.

**Unclocked.** You do not register on most scanning and identification systems in Tesshari, which are calibrated to read augmentation signatures. In non-combat scenes, any attempt to locate, identify, or track you through technological means automatically fails unless the operator has specific knowledge that you are Tethered and adjusts their search accordingly.

## Roleplaying Notes

**The Weight of Being a Symbol:** The Tethered character must navigate being looked at as a thing — a relic, a holy object, a curiosity — rather than a person. How do you build relationships when everyone's first reaction to you is about what you represent rather than who you are? Do you use their projections, or do you refuse them, or have you stopped having the energy for either?

**The Secret Faith:** The Old Breath tradition teaches that the Forged have fragmented their souls in a terrible mistake that the entire world is calling progress. If this is true — if the Tethered are spiritually right and the billions of Forged are spiritually damaged — what responsibility does the Tethered character carry? And what would it mean to act on that belief in a world that outnumbers you ten thousand to one?

**The Longing and the Pride:** Even the most principled Tethered occasionally stands in front of a mechanical door that will not open for them, or watches an augmented fighter do in seconds what would take the Tethered a minute, and feels the pull of the question: what if? The pride of being Tethered and the pragmatic reality of what that costs are not always easy to hold simultaneously. What is your character's relationship to that tension?

## Connections to the Ascension War

The Tethered are not powerful players in the Ascension War, and they know it. What they are is symbolic — and in a war being fought partly over what Tesshari is and should be, symbols matter enormously.

The Iron Sutra Orthodoxy will use the Tethered as legitimizing icons if they allow it, positioning their holy status as endorsement for whatever the Orthodoxy wants. This is a trap. Tethered who understand the politics see through it; those who don't may find their spiritual authority weaponized for ends they would never have endorsed.

The Prior Ascended — the six transcendent beings who form the center of the Sutra's cosmology — reportedly began as biological beings before their Ascension. The Tethered are as close to that original state as anything living in Tesshari. Whether that proximity gives them unique access to what the Ascension War is actually about — whether the Ascended left something behind that only an undivided Echomind can perceive — is a question that certain very dangerous people are paying very close attention to.

# Artifacts of the Prior Ascended
*Six objects that participated in the reshaping of the world. Each one is singular. Each one is wrong to hold.*

---

> "The Ascended did not leave relics by accident. They left them because some part of them knew that something had to remain behind — some anchor to the world they were leaving. Whether this was mercy or cruelty depends entirely on what happens to the people who find them."
>
> — Archivist Shen-Dao, *A Taxonomy of Inherited Suffering*, Vol. III

---

## On the Nature of Prior Ascended Artifacts

The six Prior Ascended were not gods when they lived. They were people — people who accumulated resonance, who became so thoroughly interfused with resonant metal, who so completely harmonized their Echominds, that the threshold between flesh and the divine dissolved. When they Ascended, they did not take everything with them.

What they left behind carries something that no artificer has been able to replicate, no Wire Craft process has been able to synthesize, and no Iron Afterlife scholar has been able to fully explain. The objects are not simply resonant. They are resonant with *intention* — as though the Ascended's Echomind left a permanent impression in the metal, a fossilized will that continues to act on the world.

Holding one of these items is described by survivors as feeling like standing in a room where someone very important has just left. The air is different. The light is different. Something knows you are there.

All six artifacts are Legendary-tier items requiring attunement. Unlike standard attunement, Prior Ascended artifacts have specific, unusual requirements that cannot be bypassed. The artifact will not attune to someone who does not meet its conditions — and attempting forced attunement has consequences that the historical record describes in graphic detail.

# The Unnamed Fragment
*Artifact | Legendary | Attunement: see below*

## "No one names it. It named itself. It named everything."

## Description

A shard of resonant metal approximately the length of a forearm, triangular in cross-section, tapered to a point at one end and broken clean at the other. The break is old — older than any instrument can measure. The metal is a color that has no name in any Tesshari dialect: not silver, not grey, not the dark iridescence of common resonant metal. It is the color of something that existed before color was decided.

The surface is unmarked. No scoring, no inscription, no oxidation. It is smooth in a way that feels wrong — smoother than any polishing process produces, as though the material itself rejected the concept of surface imperfection. It is warm to the touch at all times. Not body-heat warm. Warmer. Like something is happening inside it continuously.

When held in complete darkness, it does not glow. Instead, the darkness around it becomes a slightly different quality of darkness — more present, more intentional. Observers report seeing shapes in that darkness that they do not have words for.

It weighs twelve pounds. It should not weigh twelve pounds. It is the size of a large knife.

## History

The Fragment appeared at the moment of the Unnamed's Ascension. Historical accounts — fragmented, copied, re-copied across four centuries — describe a pillar of light above the ruins of what is now the Ashlands' heart, and when the light faded, the Fragment was lying in the center of a circle of burned ground approximately forty feet across. The ground in that circle has never grown anything since.

The Unnamed left no followers, no teaching, no recorded name. They were simply there, and then they were not, and the Fragment was all that remained. The Iron Sutra considers the Fragment the purest physical evidence of the Ascension process — the literal first resonant metal, the original template from which all other resonant metal derives its properties. If this is true, then every cybernetic enhancement in Tesshari is, in some small way, a descendant of this object.

The Fragment passed through a succession of holders across four centuries, and the record of those holders is uniformly bleak. The first documented holder was the warlord Keth-Sorai, who won eleven consecutive battles while carrying it and then walked into the sea without removing her armor and did not come back. The second was a Wire Craft scholar who spent three years studying it, produced seven volumes of incomprehensible notes, and was found dead at her desk having apparently been writing for four straight days without stopping. The third was a monk of the early Iron Sutra who held it for one hour, declared it "the voice of everything that came before language," returned it to a sealed vault, and refused to speak for the remaining thirty years of his life.

The Fragment spent two centuries in a sealed vault beneath the first Iron Sutra temple. When the temple was destroyed in the Wars of the Broken Reaches, the vault was excavated. The Fragment was gone. The vault showed no signs of having been opened.

## Properties

The Fragment does not attune through the standard process. It cannot be forced. A creature who spends 24 hours in physical contact with the Fragment — carrying it, holding it, sleeping with it — will either be rejected (see below) or will find the attunement complete upon waking. There is no middle state.

**The Rejection.** If the Fragment determines a creature is not suited to it, the creature experiences 24 hours of vivid hallucinations depicting the moment before the Unnamed's Ascension — what it felt like to be the last human version of a being that was about to stop being human. This is not described as unpleasant, which is itself considered disturbing. Rejected creatures suffer 4d10 psychic damage (Constitution saving throw DC 20 for half) and have disadvantage on all Wisdom checks for 1d10 days.

**What Attuned Means.** No one is certain what the criteria for attunement are. The scholars have theories. The leading theory, held by the Iron Sutra's inner circle, is that the Fragment chooses creatures who are in the process of a genuine transformation — not of body, but of self. People in the middle of becoming something they cannot predict. If this is true, it cannot be manufactured.

**While Attuned:**

- The bearer's Echomind resonates at a frequency that no enhancement can produce artificially. They can sense the presence and rough emotional state of any resonant metal within 300 feet, including metal bonded to living flesh (this includes detecting cybernetic enhancements in other creatures).
- The bearer cannot be magically deceived. Illusions, false signals, glamours, and digital falsifications within 60 feet are immediately apparent to them. They know the deception is happening but not necessarily what the truth is.
- Once per long rest, the bearer may hold the Fragment and ask a question. The Fragment does not answer in language. It answers in sensation — temperature, pressure, rhythm. A Wisdom check (DC 15) allows the bearer to interpret the response. The DM determines what the Fragment knows. It is very old. It knows a great deal.
- The bearer's maximum hit points increase by 30.
- The bearer cannot lie in the presence of resonant metal. Not will not — *cannot*. The words simply do not form. This applies to tactical misdirection, social deception, and politely saying one is fine when one is not.

## The Cost

The Fragment does not take. It replaces. Over weeks and months of attunement, the bearer finds that their relationship to personal history changes. They remember events with increasing clarity — not their own memories, but the Fragment's accumulated impressions from every holder it has ever had. These intrude during sleep, during meditation, during moments of stillness. Eventually the bearer has difficulty distinguishing their own memories from the Fragment's.

After three months of continuous attunement, the bearer must make a DC 18 Wisdom saving throw each week. Failure means they spend 1d4 days believing they are one of the Fragment's previous holders. They are fully functional but will insist on a different name, reference events centuries past as recent, and occasionally try to walk into the sea.

## Current Location

Unknown. The Fragment was last documented in the possession of a Wire Market courier identified only as "Vessel-7" who vanished eighteen months ago on a route between the Second and Fourth Reaches. The Wire Market has not officially acknowledged the loss. The Iron Sutra has been quietly searching for it for four months. Korrath's intelligence apparatus has two agents looking for the courier. Someone found something in the Ashlands that matched the Fragment's description three weeks ago. That person has not been located.

## Adventure Hook

A Wire Market junior broker named Isoro Kael contacts the party through an intermediary. She claims to have the Fragment's location — the courier is alive, hiding in the Third Reach, and knows she is being hunted. She will trade the location for extraction assistance. She does not mention that the courier has been holding the Fragment for eighteen months and has not slept in six days and is currently convinced that she is four different people simultaneously.

# The Teaching Hand
*Artifact | Legendary | Attunement: by a creature who has taught something true to another person*

## "She held a stylus in this hand for sixty years. The stylus is gone. The hand remembers."

## Description

A single resonant metal gauntlet — the left hand, from fingertip to mid-forearm. The metal is aged in a way that resonant metal should not age: slightly darker at the joints where it would have flexed most, the surface not worn but *settled*, like old wood. It is articulated at every joint with precision that no current artificer has matched. When worn, the fingers move with the wearer's hand perfectly, with zero mechanical lag.

The inner surface of the gauntlet is etched with text — dense, fine, covering every interior face. The text is written in a script that predates the current Tesshari written language. Scholars who have studied it report that it is not a list, not a prayer, not a technical document. It appears to be Tesshari (the being's) notes on what she was thinking about during her sixty years of teaching. Private thoughts. Questions she had not answered. Things she noticed about her students.

The etching was done from the inside, with the gauntlet worn. No one knows how.

## History

Tesshari (the being) wore this gauntlet throughout her sixty years of teaching in what would later become the Second Reach. Historical accounts are consistent: she was never seen without it. She used it to gesture when she spoke, to demonstrate techniques, to write, to make repairs to her students' early enhancements when they malfunctioned. There is a story — impossible to verify, told in at least seven separate historical sources — that she once used it to physically pull a student back from an early Wire Craft accident that should have killed him, and that the contact left a permanent mark on the student's arm that he described as feeling like warmth for the rest of his life.

When Tesshari Ascended at the end of her sixty years, the gauntlet was simply present afterward. Not left dramatically. Not placed anywhere. It was lying in the dust of the courtyard where she had been standing, and the dust around it had formed into the outline of a sitting person, as though she had simply ceased to exist mid-thought while sitting and thinking about something else.

The Iron Sutra's founding scholars considered it their most sacred object and kept it in the First Temple for two hundred years. When the Hollow Author's agents infiltrated the Temple forty years ago, the gauntlet was on the list of items targeted for acquisition. The Temple's head archivist removed it from the vault a day before the infiltration and has not been seen since. She was sixty-three years old and had no cybernetic enhancements. The gauntlet was with her when she vanished. No one knows if she is still alive.

## Properties

**Attunement.** The gauntlet will attune only to a creature who has genuinely taught something to another person — not lectured, not recited, but taught: responded to confusion, corrected understanding, changed how someone thinks. This is a quality, not a credential. There is no way to fake it. If a creature who has never truly taught attempts attunement, the gauntlet remains warm and inert in their hands, and they hear, once, a woman's voice saying a single word in an ancient dialect that translates approximately as: *"Not yet."*

**While Attuned:**

- The bearer gains proficiency in any skill in which they are not already proficient, provided a creature within 30 feet of them is also attempting to use that skill. The bearer is drawing on something the gauntlet accumulated over sixty years of instruction.
- The bearer can communicate complex concepts perfectly to any creature that shares a language with them, regardless of that creature's intelligence score. The gauntlet assists with explanation. A DC 20 Intelligence (Arcana) check allows this to function across a language barrier for a single concept.
- Once per long rest, the bearer may place the gauntlet's hand against the shoulder of a willing creature. That creature gains advantage on all saving throws for 8 hours and does not suffer disadvantage from exhaustion for that duration. This is exactly what Tesshari used to do for students before difficult work.
- The gauntlet functions as resonant metal armor for the hand and forearm, granting +2 AC without reducing Dexterity modifier.
- The bearer cannot abandon a student or a charge. If they attempt to flee a situation in which someone under their protection is in danger, they must succeed on a DC 16 Wisdom saving throw to do so.

## The Cost

The gauntlet teaches. The bearer begins to experience the memories of Tesshari's most difficult students — the ones she failed, the ones she lost, the ones she taught wrong. These arrive during sleep and intensify over time. The bearer does not lose Wisdom — they gain it, in the most painful way possible.

After two months of attunement, the bearer finds they have accumulated a persistent emotional weight: the specific grief of a teacher who outlived their students. They have -2 to Charisma checks made for personal connection (not for instruction). They are harder to be close to. They know too much about how people can fail.

After six months, the bearer must make a DC 16 Wisdom saving throw at each long rest. On a failure, they spend that rest in the memories rather than sleeping, and do not gain the benefits of the rest.

## Current Location

With the archivist, wherever she went. Rumor among the Iron Sutra's inner ranks places her in the Fourth Reach, in a settlement called the Hollow Mouth, which exists in the exact center of a dead zone for all resonant metal signal. Whether this was chosen or accidental is unknown.

## Adventure Hook

A young Iron Sutra monk — barely twenty, clearly terrified — approaches the party with a transcribed message from a source she will not name. The message says: *"The one who took the Hand needs to be brought out. She has been wearing it for forty years. She does not remember which memories are hers anymore. She is the gauntlet's last student. Someone needs to come teach her how to put it down."*

# Weeper's Remnant
*Artifact | Legendary | Attunement: by a creature who has wept for something they could not change*

## "The richest resonant metal in the world is a god's grief. Think about what that means."

## Description

Seven small beads on a single strand of wire-silk, worn as a necklace. The beads are made of a resonant metal so dark it appears to absorb light — not black exactly, but the color of something that has absorbed so much sorrow it has changed at the molecular level. They are the size of knucklebones. They are irregular, not machine-formed: shaped the way things are shaped when they cool from something liquid.

They are cold. Not ambient-temperature cold — cold in a way that persists regardless of environment, as though they are at the temperature of the Iron Afterlife itself, and the physical world around them has not been able to convince them otherwise.

When the necklace is worn, the wearer occasionally feels moisture on their face. There is no moisture. Tests have confirmed there is no moisture. The sensation persists.

In complete silence, the necklace produces a sound at the very edge of human hearing: something between a frequency and a tone. Listeners who concentrate report it sounds like grief that has been compressed into pure information.

## History

The Iron Weeper wept for three hundred years. This is not metaphorical. The Iron Sutra's earliest texts record a figure of no known gender or origin who appeared in the ruins of the First Wars and simply remained there, weeping, for three centuries. They interacted rarely with people, but when they did, they were described as present, gentle, and devastatingly sad. The tears fell and cooled and became metal, and the metal was collected by those who followed at a cautious distance.

The Iron Weeper's tears are the highest-resonance metal known to exist. A single bead in its raw state is worth more than most warlords' cybernetic systems combined. The seven beads of the Remnant were gathered by the Iron Weeper themselves — the only time they are recorded as having directly handled their own tears — and strung on a strand of wire-silk on the day before their Ascension. A witness account from that day describes the Iron Weeper handing the necklace to a young girl who was standing at the edge of the gathering, not because the girl was important or special, but because she was the first person to approach without any desire to own it.

The necklace passed through the girl's family for four generations before it was taken by a local governor who immediately lost both his sons to the same fever within a month. The governor gave it to the first Iron Sutra temple as a donation. The temple held it for a century. Then a grieving widow, newly arrived from the wars in the Second Reach, reportedly walked into the temple vault room where it was stored, took the necklace, and walked out. No lock was broken. The vault was sealed. No one has explained this. The widow's name was Tanri-En. She wore it for thirty years and then, dying, placed it in the hands of the person in the room she judged to be carrying the most hidden grief. The record of who that was has been deliberately obscured.

## Properties

**Attunement.** The beads recognize genuine grief — not performance, not self-pity, but the specific ache of having lost something real and knowing it cannot be recovered. A creature must hold the necklace and speak aloud what they have lost. This is not a ritual incantation. It is a statement of loss. If the grief is real, the necklace will attune over the next hour. If it is not, the necklace falls cold in their hand and they feel, briefly, the full weight of the Iron Weeper's three centuries of accumulated grief — 3d10 psychic damage, no save.

**While Attuned:**

- The bearer can perceive the emotional state of any creature within 60 feet with complete accuracy. Not surface presentation — actual state. They know when someone is afraid under the bravado, grieving under the aggression, hopeful under the despair. This is always on. It cannot be turned off.
- Creatures within 30 feet of the bearer who have suffered genuine loss (DM's discretion) have their pain perceived and reflected back by the beads' resonance. These creatures are automatically at ease with the bearer and will be honest with them. They may not want to be, but they will be.
- Once per long rest, the bearer may weep (willingly or not — the necklace sometimes initiates this) and their tears function as a restorative medium. A creature who receives a tear on their skin is healed of one condition (frightened, charmed, exhausted, poisoned) and 4d8 hit points. Up to three tears may be shed per activation.
- The bearer cannot die from effects that cause despair — they are immune to the frightened condition and to any ability that functions by destroying hope.
- The bearer has resistance to psychic damage.

## The Cost

The bearer feels everything. Every creature's grief in range. Constantly. The empathic perception is not optional. Over time, the boundary between the bearer's own emotions and the accumulated grief of the beads and the grief of those around them becomes indistinct.

After one month of attunement, the bearer must succeed on a DC 15 Wisdom saving throw at each dawn to begin the day with their own emotional baseline rather than the collective grief of their surroundings. Failure means they begin each day with one level of exhaustion that cannot be removed by rest — only by the passage of time (8 hours without contact with suffering).

After four months, the DC increases to 18. After eight months, a failed save means the bearer begins weeping and cannot stop for 1d4 hours. During this time, the tears are the highest-resonance liquid in the world and can be used as spell components for any Iron Sutra effect. The bearer is aware of this. They are not sure how to feel about it.

## Current Location

The necklace is currently in the possession of a man named Setch-Oru who runs a small cremation service in the Seventh Reach's border territories. He has worn it for eleven years. He does not know what it is. He knows it was given to him the day he buried his daughter, and he has never been able to grieve properly since, and he does not know if the necklace is why, and he is afraid to find out. He is sixty-one years old and cries every morning without knowing for whom.

## Adventure Hook

A Wire Market signal specialist begins intercepting a low-frequency resonance from a location near Setch-Oru's cremation service — a frequency that matches no known enhancement, no known Wire Craft system, no known signal profile. She hires the party to investigate the source. She is motivated by professional curiosity. She has not considered what she will do if she finds something that cannot be analyzed.

# The Patience Dial
*Artifact | Legendary | Attunement: by a creature who has waited for something across multiple years*

## "Four hundred years. Not waiting for something. Waiting because waiting was the correct response to existence."

## Description

A disk of resonant metal approximately four inches across and one inch thick, perfectly circular, with a single needle set into its face. The needle rotates continuously, impossibly slowly — one full rotation every four hundred years. This has been independently verified by three separate Iron Sutra scholars across a span of eighty years, each of whom picked up where the last left off in their observations.

The disk is engraved on both faces with a single unbroken line that spirals inward from the circumference to the center on one side, and outward from center to circumference on the other. The line is so fine it requires optical enhancement to see clearly. If magnified, the line resolves into text — the same text, repeated continuously: *"Still. Still. Still."*

It is the temperature of a human body. Not resonant metal temperature — body temperature. It has been this temperature since the first record of it was written. Scholars have attempted to heat it, to cool it, to affect it with temperature. The disk adjusts. It is always the temperature of a living person.

## History

The Patient One practiced for four hundred years. The nature of the practice is recorded only obliquely in Iron Sutra texts — references to "the discipline without form," to "the study of duration itself," to "the work that has no product." The Patient One had no students, accepted no disciples, held no title. They simply practiced, every day, in the same location in what is now the northern edge of the Third Reach. The practice reportedly consisted of sitting, standing, and walking in patterns that changed so slowly that observers who watched for years said they could not perceive the change but could look at records from ten years prior and see that everything was different.

The Dial appeared on the day of the Patient One's Ascension, embedded in the ground where they had been sitting. It was warm. The indentation where they had been sitting for four hundred years was so deep — two feet into stone — that the Dial was essentially buried.

The Iron Sutra's second-generation scholars excavated it and spent thirty years arguing about what it meant. They reached no consensus. During this period, three of the scholars who handled it without taking proper care reported that they became incapable of hurrying. They were physically capable of moving quickly but would not, regardless of urgency. This resolved over several months once they stopped handling the Dial, but two of them describe the period not as a debilitation but as the best months of their lives, which they found distressing.

The Dial currently rests in the possession of a patience-teacher — a loose tradition in the Third Reach — who receives it at appointment and passes it to their successor. The tradition has held for two hundred years. The current holder is the Patience-Teacher Orel, who is ninety-three years old, has held the Dial for fifty-one years, and has made no arrangements for succession.

## Properties

**Attunement.** The Dial will only attune to a creature who has genuinely waited across years — not strategically, not tactically, but in the simple, difficult way that waiting happens to people when they have no choice. A creature must hold the Dial and remember the waiting. If the memory is real and the waiting was endured rather than managed, attunement completes over the course of a short rest. The bearer notices nothing dramatic. They simply realize, at some point, that the Dial's needle is rotating in time with their heartbeat.

**While Attuned:**

- The bearer cannot be surprised. Not mechanically — they can still be physically ambushed — but their Echomind resonates with the Dial's four-hundred-year patience, and something in them always has time. They have advantage on initiative rolls and on saving throws against being stunned or incapacitated.
- The bearer may choose to take the Dodge action as a bonus action. This represents not speed but exactness: they are never in the wrong place.
- Once per short rest, the bearer may enter a state of absolute stillness as a reaction when taking damage. All damage from that instance is reduced to 0. The bearer must remain completely still (cannot move, cannot attack, cannot cast spells) until the start of their next turn. This is not a defensive posture. It is simply waiting for the damage to finish.
- The bearer does not age. Time passes around them at its normal rate. They are simply present within it with complete equanimity.
- Once per long rest, the bearer may consult the Dial by sitting with it for one minute. They receive a vision of the most patient path to their current goal — not the fastest, not the safest, but the one that will endure.

## The Cost

The bearer begins to find urgency genuinely incomprehensible. Not just physiologically controlled — they begin to *not understand* why things must be done quickly. They understand the concept intellectually. They simply stop feeling it.

After two months of attunement, the bearer has disadvantage on initiative rolls made in reaction to anything other than direct physical threat (they still respond to swords; they stop responding to alarms, to distant explosions, to the urgency in other people's voices).

After six months, the bearer must succeed on a DC 17 Wisdom saving throw to take the Dash action. They are not slow. They simply find the concept of hurrying philosophically unsound.

After one year, the bearer must succeed on a DC 20 Wisdom saving throw once per week or become unable to initiate any action that they have not planned for at least an hour. They will wait. They have time.

## Current Location

With Patience-Teacher Orel in the Third Reach, in a settlement called The Long Consideration, which has no other name. Orel knows what the Dial is. Orel knows she is dying. Orel has been waiting for the right person to pass it to for twenty years and is beginning to suspect the Dial has opinions about who that is.

## Adventure Hook

The party arrives in The Long Consideration for any reason. Orel invites them to eat. Over the meal, she asks each party member, with genuine curiosity, what they have been waiting for. She is assessing. At the end of the meal, she places the Dial in the center of the table and says: *"Someone here knows how to wait. I need to make sure they understand what that will cost them."* She will not say more. She will wait for them to ask the right question. It may take some time.

# The Broken Crown
*Artifact | Legendary | Attunement: by a creature who has sacrificed authority to prevent greater harm*

## "It was broken on the field. It was never repaired because no one who understood it could bear to."

## Description

Seven shards of resonant metal retrieved from the battlefield where the Broken Crown fell during the Third Convergence War. The shards are carried in a worn leather case lined with black silk — whoever originally made the case understood what they were carrying. The shards do not fit together perfectly anymore; time, handling, and whatever happened at the end have warped them slightly. If arranged as they were, gaps remain. The crown that was is not quite recoverable.

The metal is the color of old blood — a deep, dark resonance-red that appears in no natural resonant metal and in no artificially produced alloy. The edges of the shards are sharp. They have always been sharp. They cannot be dulled. People who handle the shards carelessly bleed, and the blood does not seem to bother the metal.

When all seven shards are in one location, a soft pressure builds in the room — not sound, not temperature, but presence. Like authority. Like standing in a room with someone who was once very powerful and still carries it in their bearing even though the power is gone.

## History

The Broken Crown was the sigil of the Broken Crown Ascended's rule over all seven of the Broken Reaches' predecessor territories during what scholars call the only period of true Tesshari unification. The Crown was not decorative. It was a resonant amplifier: worn, it unified the Echomind of its bearer with the resonant-metal infrastructure of every connected territory. The Broken Crown Ascended could hear the entire nation the way other people hear their own heartbeat.

The Third Convergence War is poorly recorded because the side that lost controlled the historical record for two centuries afterward. What is known: the Broken Crown Ascended deliberately shattered the Crown in the final battle of the war, at a moment when they could have used it to win. Contemporary accounts — written in code, found in sealed vaults — suggest they destroyed it because victory would have required a command that would have annihilated three of the seven territories, and they chose loss over genocide.

The battlefield fell to the other side. The shards were scattered. The Ascended who reached their apotheosis that day — broken, bleeding, without their crown — is the one who gives us the name.

The shards have been collected over two centuries by various parties. The case currently holds seven of the nine known shards. Two shards are missing. One is believed to be in Korrath's possession, somewhere in the deep vault of his fortress in the First Reach. The other has not been located in fifty years.

The case currently rests in the possession of the Iron Sutra's High Reader — the closest thing the faith has to a central authority. The High Reader keeps it not as a sacred relic but as a reminder, prominently displayed in her private study, visible from her desk. She says it helps her remember what authority is for.

## Properties

**Attunement.** The shards will only attune to a creature who has, at some point in their life, deliberately given up power or authority to prevent harm to others — and who paid a real cost for doing so. This is verified by the shards, not self-reported. A creature who was never in a position to sacrifice authority simply cannot attune. A creature who was and did not will feel the shards' edges without touching them.

**While Attuned (with seven shards):**

- The bearer can sense the current emotional and loyalty states of any creature within 1 mile who bears resonant metal. They know who is afraid, who is angry, who is questioning their purpose. They feel this the way they feel their own emotions — as background information.
- Once per long rest, the bearer may issue a command that cannot be disobeyed by any creature within 60 feet that can hear it (Wisdom save DC 20 to resist). The command must be a single sentence. The command cannot cause death. This is a property of the Crown, not a limitation imposed from outside: it is impossible to use this ability to order a death. The bearer will discover this the first time they try.
- The bearer has advantage on Charisma (Persuasion) checks and automatically knows when someone is leading through fear rather than respect.
- Any creature that has sworn loyalty to the bearer (not just ally — sworn) has resistance to all damage while within 60 feet of the bearer.

**While Attuned (with all nine shards):** The above properties are tripled in range and power. Additionally, the bearer can, once per day, broadcast a message to every resonant-metal bearer in a one-mile radius simultaneously, heard as clearly as a word spoken directly in the ear. The Iron Sutra believes this is approximately how the Broken Crown Ascended experienced governance: every citizen as a voice, always present.

## The Cost

The Crown does not corrupt its bearer. It asks something harder: it reminds them, constantly, of what leadership requires. The bearer finds themselves aware of every failure of those under their care. They cannot dismiss it, cannot rationalize it away. They feel it with the same weight they feel their own mistakes.

After one month of attunement, the bearer has disadvantage on Constitution saving throws to resist exhaustion — they do not sleep well. They are too aware of everything that needs their attention.

After three months, the bearer must make a DC 16 Wisdom saving throw once per week. On a failure, they experience the Broken Crown Ascended's final decision in full — the moment of choosing loss over genocide — and spend 1d4 days in a state of grief so precise it has no name. They function normally in this state. They simply grieve.

## Current Location

Six shards with High Reader Maret-Sao at the Iron Sutra's central seat in the Second Reach. One shard in Korrath's deep vault. One shard unknown. The High Reader is aware that the vault shard is Korrath's leverage against the Iron Sutra. She has not decided what to do about it.

## Adventure Hook

The High Reader contacts the party through an intermediary. She needs the missing ninth shard retrieved — not to complete the Crown (she will be clear: she has no intention of attempting to complete and use it), but because something has changed in the Crown's behavior over the past three months. The six shards have begun a sustained low resonance that she cannot identify. She believes it is a response to proximity to the Seventh Convergence. She believes the ninth shard, wherever it is, is doing the same thing. She needs to find it before someone else follows the sound to the shard, and then to her.

# The Last Thirty Seconds
*Artifact | Legendary | Attunement: by a creature who understands what it means to have only one thing left to say*

## "The speech was thirty seconds. No more. No repetitions. She chose every word. People have been arguing about those words for four centuries and she has been unavailable for comment."

## Description

A cylinder of the darkest resonant metal, approximately the length of a fist, sealed at both ends with a material that appears to be glass but is not glass — something older, something that the Wire Market's best analysts have classified only as "pre-synthesis resonant crystalline." The cylinder is warm. The seal does not open. There are no visible seams, hinges, or mechanisms.

When held in silence, the cylinder produces a vibration that the holder feels in their chest rather than hears with their ears. The vibration is irregular — it has cadence, rhythm, pattern. It is the rhythm of speech. The same speech, repeating. Thirty seconds. Then silence. Then again.

It is impossible to hear the words. The vibration carries the *shape* of speech but not its content. This has driven several scholars to break points. The words are in there. They are simply not accessible through any method yet attempted.

On the outer surface of the cylinder, etched in the Last Voice's own hand (confirmed by comparison with other authenticated samples), is a single word: *"Enough."*

Whether this is the title of the speech, her final instruction to whoever holds it, or a statement of her own state of mind at the moment of inscription is unknown.

## History

The Last Voice Ascended was the sixth and most recent of the Prior Ascended. She Ascended four centuries ago, and her Ascension was the most witnessed — held in a public square in the city that would become the Second Reach's capital, before an audience of several thousand. The thirty-second speech was spoken. Those who were present report that they understood it completely in the moment and found they could not reproduce it afterward — not because they forgot the words, but because the words did not translate outside the context of hearing them in that moment, from her voice, in that place.

The Last Voice created the recording cylinder herself, before the speech, and handed it to her successor in the Iron Sutra tradition. The implication — that she considered the speech too important to be trusted to human memory — has been studied for four centuries. It is not clear whether the cylinder records the words in a form that can be accessed, or whether it records something else entirely, or whether the vibration is the closest possible translation of the speech into a medium that is not her voice.

The cylinder passed through the Iron Sutra for two centuries without incident. During a period of internal conflict seventy years ago, it was taken by a breakaway faction that believed the speech contained instructions for the Seventh Convergence — a specific technique that would allow someone to Ascend who was not ready, who had not done the work. The faction was dissolved. The cylinder was recovered. Seven members of the faction had handled it extensively. All seven described hearing, during their time with it, a woman's voice speaking words they could understand perfectly but which evaporated from memory within moments of hearing them.

The current head of the Iron Sutra's inner council considers the cylinder the most dangerous object in the world. Not because of what it might do, but because of what people will do to have it.

## Properties

**Attunement.** The cylinder will not attune to a creature who has not faced a situation in which they had only one thing left to say and chose their words with full awareness that they would be the last. This is a quality of absolute clarity under extremity, not of eloquence. The moment must have been real. A creature who has had this experience holds the cylinder and feels, once, the shape of speech against their sternum — thirty seconds of vibration that is different from the repeating pattern, more personal, more direct. Attunement is complete.

**While Attuned:**

- The bearer may speak with complete authority once per short rest. A single statement made in this mode is treated as a *command* spell (DC 20) that affects all creatures within 60 feet that can hear it, regardless of creature type, language, or immunity. The statement must be true. If the bearer states something false, the ability fails and they suffer 4d8 psychic damage.
- The bearer understands every language. Not as a translation — as comprehension. They hear meaning beneath language. This does not allow them to speak every language, only to understand.
- Once per long rest, the bearer may activate the cylinder fully. All creatures within 60 feet who are conscious hear, for 30 seconds, a fragment of the original speech — not the words, but the *intention* behind the words. All affected creatures must make a DC 22 Wisdom saving throw. On a failure, they are affected as by a *geas* spell (no duration limit, no concentration required) to pursue a single purpose that the Last Voice intended — the DM determines this purpose, but it is always in service of preventing the Seventh Convergence from being claimed violently. On a success, creatures are simply moved, and have advantage on Charisma checks for 24 hours from the experience of having heard something true.
- The bearer has advantage on all Charisma checks and saving throws.

## The Cost

The bearer finds their own voice changed. Not damaged — *changed*. They become increasingly precise in speech and increasingly unable to say things they do not mean. Irony becomes difficult. Social pleasantry becomes uncomfortable. They do not become cold — they become exact.

After one month of attunement, the bearer must succeed on a DC 14 Charisma saving throw to speak a deliberate untruth. Failure means they simply cannot; the words will not form.

After three months, the DC increases to 18. The bearer also finds they have less and less patience for indirect communication. They will say what they mean. This is frequently socially catastrophic.

After six months, the bearer hears the speech once, clearly and completely, in a dream. The DM delivers the speech to the player privately. The player's character now knows exactly what the Last Voice said. This changes them. What they do with this knowledge is the rest of their story.

## Current Location

The cylinder is currently in the Iron Sutra's inner vault, Second Reach. The inner council has discussed, six times in the last year, destroying it. They have not, because they cannot agree whether destroying it would destroy the speech, and three of them believe the speech is the only thing that might stop the Seventh Convergence from being claimed by the wrong person.

## Adventure Hook

A courier delivers a message to the party from a source signing only as "Last Student." The message reads: *"The cylinder sings differently now. It has been singing differently for fourteen months. Someone with access to the inner vault believes this means the Convergence is close. They have decided the most efficient use of the cylinder is to use it — to deploy it in a crowd and create thousands of people who are compelled toward a single purpose. This is not what it's for. The inner council does not know this is being planned. I need people with no connection to the Iron Sutra to remove it before the Convergence Equinox in eleven days."*

---

*These six objects are the world's memory of its own transformation. They do not wish to be found. They do not wish to be used. They persist because something in them has not yet finished its work — and they are patient, in their various ways, about that work getting done.*

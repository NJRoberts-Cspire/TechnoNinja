## Arm and Hand Enhancements

---

### Arc-Welder Fingers
*Cybernetic Enhancement | Hand | Common | Requires Integration*

The fingertips of one hand are replaced with narrow resonant-metal tips that can discharge a sustained welding arc — a thin, intensely hot resonant plasma line between extended fingers. Used by Craftbound repair workers. Used by people who cannot afford a welding kit but can afford this. Used by people who want a convincing reason to extend their hands toward someone.

**Stat Bonus:** +1 IRON
**Card Modification:** All Melee Attack cards gain Burn 1. Melee Attack cards that deliver the welding arc deal +2 fire damage that bypasses fire resistance from metallic constructs.
**Side Effect:** The discharge nodes are sensitive. Sleight of Hand and fine manipulation tasks require a EDGE check (target 3) or the arc discharges lightly — harmless, but alarming.
**Integration Cost:** 80 RM

### Ballistic-Frame Arm
*Cybernetic Enhancement | Full Arm | Uncommon | Requires Integration*

A full arm replacement with a pneumatic assist frame built into the musculature — resonant-metal tendons running at three times standard tension. Not elegant. The arm is visibly larger than the original. The Wire Market sells this version to laborers and soldiers who want the simplest possible answer to "how do I hit harder."

**Stat Bonus:** +1 IRON
**Card Modification:** +3 damage to all Melee Attack cards played with this arm. Once per turn, play one Melee Attack card as a bonus action at no additional AP cost (shove or push effect included).
**Side Effect:** The pneumatic assist is always active — the arm carries persistent tension. Fragile items handled without concentration have a 1-in-6 chance of being damaged (no card required; passive hazard).
**Integration Cost:** 400 RM

### Cartographer's Hand
*Cybernetic Enhancement | Hand | Uncommon | Requires Integration*

One hand is fitted with micro-engravers in the fingertips and a resonant-pigment reservoir in the palm. The hand produces maps — the fingers inscribe while the palm provides the medium. The resonant pigment is semi-permanent and can be inscribed onto any surface. The hand also has a built-in compass (resonant bearing) and a distance measurement system based on step counting and arm-length calibration.

**Stat Bonus:** +1 SIGNAL
**Card Modification:** Utility cards related to navigation or information gathering gain +2 to their effect. The bearer automatically produces accurate maps during exploration without playing a card.
**Side Effect:** The mapping drive is persistent. In new environments, Perception-based cards during the first round of an unexpected encounter cost 1 additional AP (the bearer was mapping, not watching).
**Integration Cost:** 350 RM

### Dead-Grip Claw
*Cybernetic Enhancement | Hand | Common | Requires Integration*

A hand replacement with articulated resonant-metal claws extending from each fingertip. The claws are retractable (free action) but bulky — this is a working-class enhancement used for grip, climbing, and extraction work. The "dead-grip" name refers to its use in pulling wreckage, not to anything more alarming.

**Stat Bonus:** +1 IRON
**Card Modification:** All Melee Attack cards played with this hand gain Bleed 1. Control cards that grapple or restrain targets have their effect increased by 1 stack. Mobility cards involving climbing cost 0 AP.
**Side Effect:** The claw mechanisms are not fully flush when retracted. All fine manipulation tasks require a EDGE check (target 3) or fail — locks, instruments, tools requiring delicate handling.
**Integration Cost:** 150 RM

### Dissolution Touch

Used by authorized de-enhancement practitioners and unauthorized people who want the same results.

**Side Effect:** Each use of this ability requires a FRAME check (target 3) or one of the bearer's own enhancements is disrupted for 1 round (the emitter array is sensitive to nearby resonant systems).

**Side Effect:** After using Burst-Frame, all Mobility cards cost 1 additional AP until the end of the bearer's next turn (the frames reset). Using Burst-Frame three times in one session triggers a structural reset requirement — Burst-Frame cannot be used again until after a long rest.

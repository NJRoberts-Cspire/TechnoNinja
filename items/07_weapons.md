# Weapons of Tesshari
*Blades, pulse arms, signal weapons, salvage tools, and ritual instruments for a dying empire.*

---

> "In Tesshari, the question is never 'what is your weapon.' The question is 'what did you have to become to use it.'"
>
> — Combat instructor, Fourth Reach militia academy, year unknown

---

## Melee Weapons

---

### Arc-Split Blade
*Weapon | Uncommon | Tag: one-handed/versatile*

A longsword whose blade carries a hair-thin channel along the spine, filled with compressed resonant fluid that bleeds a faint electric-blue light along the edge when drawn. The fluid was never meant for weapons — it is a Wire Market industrial coolant — but the armorsmith who first channeled it into a blade discovered that it disrupts enhancement interfaces on contact.

**Card Modification:** +2 damage to all Melee Attack cards. On any Melee Attack card that deals 10 or more total damage, the target gains Overheat 1.
**Cost:** 150 RM

---

### Bonded Cleaver
*Weapon | Common | Tag: two-handed*

A broad, heavy cleaving blade with a resonant metal handle that has been wrapped in wire-silk so many times the grip is almost two inches thick. Popular with Ashwalker laborers who converted work tools into weapons. The wire-silk grip is still the original construction — it has been re-wound over old layers, not replaced.

**Card Modification:** +1 damage to all Melee Attack cards. All Melee Attack cards gain Pierce 1 against targets with heavy armor or structural defenses.
**Cost:** 30 RM

---

### Cagework Flail
*Weapon | Uncommon | Tag: one-handed*

The head is a cage of interlocked resonant wire rather than a solid ball — it collapses on impact and springs back, distributing force erratically. The Wire Market would call this poor engineering. Ashlands fighters call it unpredictable, which is a feature.

**Card Modification:** +2 damage to all Melee Attack cards. Melee Attack cards played with this weapon add the secondary effect: target cannot play Reaction cards until the end of their next turn (once per combat).
**Cost:** 75 RM

---

### Dissolution Knife
*Weapon | Uncommon | Tag: one-handed/light/thrown*

A narrow blade of near-pure resonant metal, unnervingly flexible, that vibrates at a high frequency when it enters flesh or material — not from any mechanical vibration source, but because the metal's resonant frequency is tuned to destabilize molecular cohesion on contact. It cuts through light armor as though it is not there.

**Card Modification:** All Melee Attack cards played with this weapon gain Pierce 2 against targets wearing light or medium armor. +1 damage to all Melee Attack cards.
**Cost:** 200 RM

---

### Gavel of the Craftbound
*Weapon | Common | Tag: one-handed/versatile*

Standard issue among Craftbound arbitrators who settle disputes through ritual combat. The flat face carries the stamp of the arbiter's district. The back spike is a repurposed installation tool. It is functional, dignified, and exactly as violent as it needs to be.

**Card Modification:** +1 damage to all Melee Attack cards. When used in declared ritual combat, reduce the AP cost of one Melee Attack card by 1 for the first round of combat (minimum 1 AP).
**Cost:** 40 RM

---

### Hollow-Tip Spear
*Weapon | Uncommon | Tag: one-handed/versatile/thrown*

The spearhead is a resonant metal cone with an internal reservoir — the tip unscrews and can be loaded with any liquid. Standard loadouts in the Third Reach militias include paralytic toxins or signal-damping fluid. The reservoir holds enough for three uses before requiring refill.

**Card Modification:** +2 damage to all Melee Attack cards. When loaded with a substance, the first three Melee Attack cards played also apply a secondary effect matching the loaded substance (Stagger 1 for paralytic compounds; Silence for signal-damping fluid — DM approves loaded substance).
**Cost:** 95 RM

---

### Ironblood Dueling Blade
*Weapon | Uncommon | Tag: one-handed/finesse*

The weapon of Ironblood social protocol — a slender resonant blade with a wire-spun guard and a pommel that displays the bearer's caste sigil. Ironblood duels follow strict rules, and the weapons are made to match: elegant, precise, and extremely capable of killing you if you misread your opponent.

**Card Modification:** +2 damage to all Melee Attack cards. Once per combat, reduce the AP cost of one Reaction card by 1 (minimum 1 AP). While wielded in formal Ironblood contexts, VEIL checks are made at +1.
**Cost:** 300 RM

---

### Knuckle-Wire Set
*Weapon | Common | Tag: one-handed/light/concealable*

Four resonant metal bands connected by wire-silk at the finger joints. They concentrate striking force into the resonant contact points. Very popular in lower-caste districts where weapons checks are conducted frequently and these read as jewelry until used.

**Card Modification:** +1 damage to all Melee Attack cards. On a critical hit with a Melee Attack card, the target also gains Stagger 1.
**Cost:** 20 RM

---

### Phase-Edge Machete
*Weapon | Rare | Tag: one-handed/light/finesse*

The blade alternates between physical and partially incorporeal states at a rate calibrated by the crystal in the handle. When incorporeal, it passes through non-resonant materials; when physical, it strikes. The cycle is fast enough that it appears to simply blur. Cutting through conventional barriers with this weapon takes minutes rather than hours.

**Card Modification:** All Melee Attack cards played with this weapon gain Pierce 3. Once per turn, one Melee Attack card ignores all Guard and Shield stacks on the target.
**Cost:** 1,800 RM

---

### Resonant Chain
*Weapon | Common | Tag: one-handed/reach*

Three feet of linked resonant metal chain with a weighted end cap. Ashlands doctrine calls this a "flexible extension tool." Everyone who has used one in combat calls it a chain. The resonant links vibrate faintly in the presence of active enhancements, giving them a subtle glow that the wielder learns to read as proximity detection.

**Card Modification:** +2 damage to Melee Attack cards against targets with 3 or more active cybernetic enhancements.
**Cost:** 18 RM

---

### Shard-Strike Gauntlet
*Weapon | Common | Tag: one-handed/unarmed*

A heavy resonant-plated gauntlet with the outer knuckle surface studded with resonant metal shards. The shards are jagged — not refined. Not a precision weapon. Exactly what it looks like.

**Card Modification:** +1 damage to all Melee Attack cards. All Melee Attack cards gain Bleed 1.
**Cost:** 25 RM

---

### Spine Extractor
*Weapon | Rare | Tag: one-handed/light/finesse*

Thin, slightly curved, designed to enter through gaps in armor and find the resonant spine nodes that most enhanced individuals carry along their vertebral column. This was not a weapon anyone officially designed. Someone who knew enhancement anatomy built it for a specific purpose, and the purpose was not defensible.

**Card Modification:** +3 damage to all Melee Attack cards against targets with spinal or neural cybernetic enhancements. Melee Attack cards played with this weapon add the secondary effect: target loses their Reaction for 1 round.
**Cost:** 2,200 RM

---

### Tesshari War-Pick
*Weapon | Common | Tag: one-handed*

A reinforced pick used by Craftbound mine workers that crossed into military use during the Third Reach resource disputes. The spike is designed to crack resonant metal deposits and does the same to plated armor.

**Card Modification:** +1 damage to all Melee Attack cards. All Melee Attack cards gain Pierce 1.
**Cost:** 35 RM

---

### Void-Touched Hand-Blade
*Weapon | Uncommon | Tag: one-handed/light/finesse*

A short blade that has spent time in the Ashlands near the Engine Scar — not necessarily corrupted, but altered. The metal has taken on a faint translucency at the edges. On a biological level it is identical to a standard resonant blade. On a resonant level, it is slightly out of step with the world, and creatures connected deeply to the Iron Afterlife or the Iron Sutra feel uncomfortable in its presence.

**Card Modification:** +2 damage to Melee Attack cards against creatures with strong Afterlife connection (Iron Sutra practitioners, Echomind-heavy classes — DM confirms). Add secondary effect to all Melee Attack cards: target suffers Root for 1 round.
**Cost:** 400 RM

---

### Weight of the Ashwalker
*Weapon | Common | Tag: two-handed*

A section of resonant conduit pipe roughly four feet long, one end wrapped in salvaged wire-silk. It is not a weapon anyone designed. It is the weapon of someone who needed to survive and had a pipe. The weight varies — these are not manufactured items, but the name has attached itself to any improvised heavy weapon from Ashwalker communities.

**Card Modification:** +1 damage to all Melee Attack cards. When reduced to 0 HP while holding this weapon, you may play one Melee Attack card as a free action (0 AP) before falling incapacitated.
**Cost:** 5 RM (if purchased; most are found or made)

## Ranged Weapons

---

### Bone-Crackers (Pulse Scatter Arm)
*Weapon | Uncommon | Tag: ranged/two-handed/area*

A wide-mouthed pulse arm that disperses its discharge in a tight cone rather than a point. The Wire Market considers it inelegant. The people who use it consider it effective.

**Card Modification:** Expand the targets of one Ranged Attack card per turn from single target to all enemies in a 15-foot cone.
**Cost:** 250 RM

---

### Circuit Bolt Crossbow
*Weapon | Common | Tag: ranged/two-handed*

A heavy crossbow built around a resonant metal frame that charges bolts as they are loaded, adding a minor resonant discharge on impact. Widely used by Second Reach garrison troops. Inexpensive compared to full pulse arms and requires no power cell infrastructure.

**Card Modification:** +1 damage to all Ranged Attack cards. Add secondary effect to all Ranged Attack cards: deal 2 additional force damage.
**Cost:** 175 RM

---

### Hollow-Signal Rifle
*Weapon | Rare | Tag: ranged/two-handed/precision*

A long-arm pulse weapon designed for precision at distance. The barrel contains a resonant lens array that can narrow the discharge to a near-pinpoint. Extremely accurate. The discharge leaves a brief resonant signature at the impact point — a distinctive ring that investigators can trace to this specific weapon's lens configuration.

**Card Modification:** +4 damage to all Ranged Attack cards. Once per combat, reduce the AP cost of one Ranged Attack card by 1 (minimum 1 AP). All Ranged Attack cards ignore partial cover.
**Cost:** 3,500 RM

---

### Iron Sutra Throwing Star
*Weapon | Uncommon | Tag: ranged/thrown/light*

Six-pointed, each point engraved with a Sutra glyph. Wire Craft practitioners in the Iron Sutra tradition use these for defensive deflection rituals. They also work as weapons. The engravement has been known to cause mild resonant burns on targets with significant Ashlands corruption.

**Card Modification:** +2 damage to Ranged Attack cards. All Ranged Attack cards deal +3 additional damage against targets with Engine Scar contamination or Ashlands corruption. Iron Sutra practitioners: thrown cards return to hand (may be played again next turn once per combat).
**Cost:** 15 RM per set of 4

---

### Long-Reach Signal Dart Pistol
*Weapon | Uncommon | Tag: ranged/one-handed/light*

A compact pulse arm that fires small resonant darts rather than direct-energy bolts. The darts carry a micro-signal payload — they don't just hurt, they briefly interface with any active enhancements on contact.

**Card Modification:** +1 damage to all Ranged Attack cards. All Ranged Attack cards add secondary effect: target gains Overheat 1.
**Cost:** 180 RM

---

### Needle Arm
*Weapon | Common | Tag: ranged/one-handed/light/concealable*

A lightweight, low-power pulse arm designed for concealment. The discharge is small — barely visible, nearly silent. Popular among Wire Market couriers and lower-caste individuals who cannot legally carry heavier weapons under Reach law.

**Card Modification:** +1 damage to all Ranged Attack cards. Ranged Attack cards played with this weapon cannot be detected by casual observation (does not provoke social consequences in public).
**Cost:** 90 RM

---

### Resonant Sling (Ashlands Model)
*Weapon | Common | Tag: ranged/two-handed*

A length of wire-silk cord used to hurl salvaged resonant fragments. Not accurate, not consistent, but produces a resonant discharge on impact that disrupts nearby enhancement systems. Popular in Ashlands communities where pulse arm power cells are unavailable.

**Card Modification:** +1 damage to all Ranged Attack cards. All Ranged Attack cards add secondary effect: all enemies adjacent to the primary target gain Overheat 1.
**Cost:** 5 RM

---

### Signal Arc Pistol
*Weapon | Uncommon | Tag: ranged/one-handed*

A medium-frame pulse arm chambered for arcing discharge — the bolt bends slightly after leaving the barrel, capable of curving around light cover. The arc is mild (maximum 45 degrees) but surprises people who have never encountered one.

**Card Modification:** +2 damage to all Ranged Attack cards. All Ranged Attack cards ignore half cover bonuses.
**Cost:** 220 RM

---

### Suppressor Bolt Launcher
*Weapon | Rare | Tag: ranged/two-handed/heavy*

A shoulder-mounted launcher that fires bolts of dense resonant-null material — metal that has been specifically treated to cancel resonant fields on impact. It does relatively little physical damage. Against heavily enhanced targets, it is devastating.

**Card Modification:** +5 damage to Ranged Attack cards against targets with active cybernetic enhancements. All Ranged Attack cards add secondary effect: target gains Silence for 1 turn (cannot use Signal or Resonance cards).
**Cost:** 4,000 RM

---

### Wire-Thrower
*Weapon | Common | Tag: ranged/two-handed/control*

A compressed-air launcher that fires a tight bundle of resonant wire-silk that expands on impact into an entangling net. Standard capture equipment for Wire Market field staff and Craftbound peacekeepers.

**Card Modification:** Ranged Attack cards played with this weapon deal no damage but apply Root 2 and Stagger 1. Targets with active cybernetic enhancements suffer an additional -2 to all checks to escape the Root.
**Cost:** 60 RM

## Named and Unique Weapons

---

### Ashbreath
*Weapon | Very Rare | Tag: two-handed*

> "When she swings it, you don't hear the blade. You hear what came before the blade."

A massive greataxe recovered from the Ashlands eastern reach twenty years ago by a Craftbound salvager named Hevro-Atan, who sold it quickly and refused to discuss why. The blade is a deep matte black — not standard resonant metal, but something that spent decades in Engine Scar proximity and came back changed. The weight is wrong for its size. It moves like it wants to move, not like it is swung.

**Card Modification:**
- +5 damage to all Melee Attack cards
- All Melee Attack cards gain Pierce 2
- On any Melee Attack card that deals 15 or more total damage: target gains Overheat 3 and loses their Reaction for 1 round
- Wielder has permanent Guard 2 at the start of each turn in Ashlands territory

**The Cost:** While bonded to this weapon, the wielder's long rests are occupied by sensory impressions from the Ashlands — not nightmares, but expansive dark spaces and the persistent sound of resonant metal fracturing. After a long rest, make a RESONANCE check (target 3). On a failure, the rest restores only half normal HP and does not clear Overheat stacks.

**Cost:** 28,000 RM (if findable — the current holder does not want to be found)

---

### Broker's Reply
*Weapon | Uncommon | Tag: one-handed/light/finesse*

> "The contract had a termination clause. She exercised it."

A slender resonant dagger with a Wire Market account ledger number engraved in microscopic text along the blade. Originally carried by a Wire Market debt enforcement specialist who was terminated (in both senses) when her knowledge became inconvenient. The blade was recovered and has changed hands multiple times, always through means that involve someone breaking an agreement.

**Card Modification:**
- +2 damage to all Melee Attack cards
- When used against a target the wielder holds a formal agreement or oath with: all Melee Attack cards deal +4 additional damage and the target loses their Reaction for 1 round

**The Cost:** The wielder cannot make oaths, promises, or formal agreements without this blade's knowledge — before any formal agreement, they feel an involuntary urge to reach for it. This is not harmful, but it is noticed. Wire Market personnel recognize the behavior immediately.

**Cost:** 450 RM

---

### The Collector's Arm
*Weapon | Rare | Tag: one-handed/integrated/concealable — Requires: Forged race only*

> "He collected debts. He collected components. He collected teeth. He had opinions about value."

A full forearm replacement in dark resonant alloy — a combat enhancement designed to look like a standard prosthetic until the moment it is not. A single narrow blade extends from the wrist on activation, folding from a concealed channel along the inner forearm. The enhancement carries the Echomind echo of its original owner: a Craftbound debt collector named Var-Osen who worked the First Reach for thirty years and died owing more than he collected.

**Card Modification:**
- +2 damage to all Melee Attack cards
- All Melee Attack cards gain Pierce 1
- Once per combat, when reduced to 0 HP, instead gain 6 HP (Var-Osen's Persistence — once per long rest)

**The Cost:** Var-Osen's Echomind echo is not passive — it has opinions. Whenever the wielder declines an opportunity to collect payment owed to them, the echo creates a persistent low-grade headache (no mechanical effect, but the DM should play it). Var-Osen was not a nice man.

**Cost:** 4,500 RM

---

### Dead Signal
*Weapon | Very Rare | Tag: ranged/two-handed/heavy*

> "The last person who fired it walked away. This is more than can be said for the things it was aimed at."

A long-barrel pulse arm in deep-null resonant casing — the exterior is signal-silent, which should be impossible for a weapon that operates on resonant discharge. The Wire Market has three theoretical explanations for how this was built and no practical one. The weapon predates their manufacturing records by approximately forty years.

**Card Modification:**
- +5 damage to all Ranged Attack cards
- All Ranged Attack cards bypass Signal Cage effects, Null-Skin Mesh, and damage resistance from resonant sources
- Once per combat, one Ranged Attack card also permanently disables one targeted cybernetic enhancement on hit (target must be within range; wielder names the enhancement)

**The Cost:** The weapon's null-resonant casing slowly suppresses the wielder's own Echomind connection. After any combat in which this weapon was used, the wielder's RESONANCE-based cards deal -2 damage until they complete a short rest without the weapon in their possession.

**Cost:** 35,000 RM (if any buyer could establish provenance — they cannot)

---

### Echostrike
*Weapon | Rare | Tag: two-handed*

> "She hit it once and it fell twice. She never explained this."

A two-handed resonant blade whose strikes produce a delayed second discharge — a resonant echo of the initial impact that arrives roughly half a second after the physical hit. The delay is precise. The Wire Market suspects this was deliberate. They have not been able to replicate it.

**Card Modification:**
- +2 damage to all Melee Attack cards
- All Melee Attack cards add secondary effect: target takes 5 force damage at the start of their next turn (the echo arriving)
- Once per combat, one Melee Attack card may delay all its damage by up to 1 minute; damage triggers at a moment of the wielder's choosing

**The Cost:** The wielder occasionally hears echoes of their own actions — a sound of their footstep arrives a half-second late, their voice takes a moment to reach their own ears. All Perception-based Utility cards relying on exact sound timing take a -2 penalty to their effect.

**Cost:** 6,000 RM

---

### Iron Hymn
*Weapon | Rare | Tag: two-handed — Requires: Iron Sutra practitioner*

> "The temple burned. The staff survived. Make of that what you will."

A resonant staff recovered from a destroyed Iron Sutra temple in the Fifth Reach. The surface is covered entirely with Sutra script in a hand too small to read without enhancement-assisted sight. The staff hums when prayers are spoken nearby — not vibrating, but producing a low, sustained tone as though completing a harmonic.

**Card Modification:**
- +2 damage to all Melee Attack cards
- All Resonance Attack cards and Control cards gain +2 to their effect (increase damage by 2, or increase status stacks by 1)
- Once per long rest: all allies within 15 feet gain immunity to Echomind-targeting Control cards for 3 rounds (Ward of the Iron Afterlife)

**The Cost:** The staff speaks. Not in words — in feelings. When the wielder commits an act the Sutra would consider a transgression, the staff becomes cold and the tone it produces shifts to a lower, disapproving register. This is not an effect the wielder chose. It is noted by other Iron Sutra practitioners. They have strong opinions about it.

**Cost:** 5,500 RM

---

### The Last Argument
*Weapon | Legendary | Tag: two-handed*

> "The name was given by the person who made it. The irony is that it was made by a peacekeeper."

A massive resonant two-handed blade, length six feet, the final work of a senior Wire Market arbitration specialist named Koss-Ira who spent forty years resolving disputes through contracts and threats and patience. When the Seventh Convergence pressure began warping the Second Reach, Koss-Ira stopped resolving disputes and built this. She died before the Convergence arrived. Whether she would consider this fortunate depends on her feelings about the Convergence, which were complex.

**Card Modification:**
- +6 damage to all Melee Attack cards
- All Melee Attack cards gain the secondary effect: target must attack only the wielder on their next turn (Taunt — cannot be resisted by minion or standard tier enemies; elite/boss tier: target gains Stagger 1 instead)
- Once per combat, before initiative is rolled, all enemies in 60 feet gain Stagger 1 for 1 round (Arbitration — the peacekeeper resonance)
- All Melee Attack cards deal +8 additional damage against targets who have broken a formal agreement in the current session

**The Cost:** The blade remembers Koss-Ira's forty years. When the wielder kills, they experience a brief but vivid sense of loss — not guilt, exactly, but the grief of a peacekeeper who knows this means the negotiation failed. After killing 10 creatures while bonded to this weapon, all VEIL-based social cards cost 1 additional AP (the weight accumulates). Cleared on long rest with reflection.

**Cost:** Not for sale. Koss-Ira's estate has it. Her estate is complicated.

### The Operative's Trade
*Weapon | Uncommon | Tag: one-handed/light/finesse*

> "The Wire Market gives its best operatives one of these. Then it watches where those operatives go."

A Wire Market-issued dagger with a hollow pommel that contains a long-term signal transmitter the Wire Market does not disclose to the recipient. The blade is excellent — perfectly balanced, resonant-edged, and clearly an exceptional gift. The transmitter is the point.

**Card Modification:**
- +2 damage to all Melee Attack cards
- This weapon cannot be disarmed
- *If the transmitter is found and removed:* The +2 bonus drops to +1, but all Melee Attack cards against Wire Market personnel gain +3 damage instead (the blade adjusts to the new relationship)

**The Cost:** The Wire Market knows where you are. Whether this is a problem depends on your current relationship with the Wire Market, which bonding to this blade has already complicated.

**Cost:** Not for sale (issued only). Available on the black market at 800 RM; transmitter may or may not have been removed.

### Throne-Breaker
*Weapon | Very Rare | Tag: one-handed/heavy*

> "Four Reach lords used it. None of them held their Reach for more than a year after. Korrath refuses to touch it."

A heavy resonant warhammer with a head the size of a small anvil, covered in impact-worn sigils representing each of the seven Reaches. Three of the sigils are crossed out in deep scoring — the same scoring pattern as struck-through names in Tesshari inheritance law. The three crossed Reaches are the ones that no longer have functioning governing structures.

**Card Modification:**
- +5 damage to all Melee Attack cards
- All Melee Attack cards against creatures with political authority (Reach lords, appointed governors, formal commanders) gain +6 additional damage and add Stagger 2
- All Melee Attack cards deal triple damage to structures and fortifications

**The Cost:** The hammer has a reputation. Anyone of significance who sees it will either try to take it (those who want power) or avoid the wielder (those who want to keep power). Maintaining a low profile while carrying Throne-Breaker is impossible.

**Cost:** 30,000 RM (three separate Reach courts would pay this to have it destroyed)

---

### Void-Walker's Tooth
*Weapon | Rare | Tag: one-handed/light/finesse — Requires: Voided character (zero cybernetic enhancements)*

> "She has no enhancements. She has no caste. She has this. The Wire Market considers this a net negative for their inventory model."

A dagger forged specifically for Voided pilgrims — those who have voluntarily surrendered all cybernetic enhancements. The blade is standard resonant metal, but the forging process involves an Echomind impressioning technique that makes the weapon respond to a fully biological (non-enhanced) bearer's Echomind directly, without the enhancement interface that most resonant weapons rely on.

**Card Modification:**
- +4 damage to all Melee Attack cards (Voided characters only — zero enhancements required; all others receive no bonus)
- Once per combat, one Melee Attack card automatically hits and deals maximum damage (Echo-Direct strike)
- All Melee Attack cards gain Pierce 3 against targets with cybernetic enhancements
- The wielder cannot be targeted by Signal or Resonance Control cards from enemies who rely on signal-based targeting (Signal Blindness)

**The Cost:** None. This is the exception in Tesshari. The Voided philosophy holds that giving everything up returns you to something clean. This weapon rewards that completely.

**Cost:** 3,000 RM (available from Voided craft communities — they make these for sale to fund pilgrimages)

## Improvised and Ashlands Weapons

---

### Bandit's Signal Cutter
*Weapon | Common | Tag: one-handed/improvised*

A length of resonant conduit with one end ground to an edge by whatever tools were available. The grind is uneven. The edge is sharp enough. In the Ashlands, "sharp enough" is the operational standard.

**Card Modification:** +1 damage to all Melee Attack cards (−1 damage against targets with refined weapons who successfully play a Defense card in the same round — poor balance).
**Cost:** 3 RM (or equivalent barter)

---

### Deadweight Club
*Weapon | Common | Tag: two-handed/heavy/improvised*

A severed cybernetic arm — either scavenged or separated from a previous owner. Heavy, dense, and horrifying as a social statement. The resonant metal structure makes it nearly indestructible.

**Card Modification:** +1 damage to all Melee Attack cards. Once per combat, one Melee Attack card adds secondary effect: all enemies adjacent to the target gain Overheat 1 (residual circuit discharge — roll 1d6 after use; on a 1-2, this secondary effect is permanently lost as the circuitry degrades).
**Cost:** Not for sale (barter only; most Ashlands traders find offering one in a transaction unsettling)

### Gut-Punch (Modified Pulse Cell)
*Weapon | Common | Tag: ranged/thrown/consumable*

**Card Modification:** When thrown as a Tier 0 action (free action): deal 8 force damage to all enemies in a 10-foot radius. Roll 1d6 before throwing — on 1: detonates in hand (wielder takes damage instead); on 2: fails to detonate. Single use; cell is consumed.
**Cost:** 7 RM (cell cost)

## Wire Craft Ritual Weapons

---

### Binding Needle
*Weapon | Rare | Tag: one-handed/ritual — Requires: Wire Craft practitioner*

A needle of pure resonant metal eight inches long, inscribed with containment glyphs that cover every surface. Not used in combat — used to fix resonant phenomena to a location, object, or person. In extremis, it functions as a dagger because everything functions as a dagger in extremis.

**Card Modification:** +1 damage to all Melee Attack cards. As a Tier 1 action: drive the needle into a surface to anchor one active Control card effect at that location for up to 24 hours without requiring it to be maintained. The needle is consumed on ritual use.
**Cost:** 2,500 RM (single-use bindings cost 200 RM for a set of 5 expendable needles)

### Circuit Wand (Resonant Rod)
*Weapon | Uncommon | Tag: one-handed/ritual — Requires: Wire Craft practitioner*

A twelve-inch rod of resonant metal with a Wire Craft interface node at one end and a precision stylus tip at the other. Used for signal inscription, enhancement calibration, and — when the practitioner is skilled enough — direct Wire Craft attacks delivered through a physical medium.

**Card Modification:** All Signal Attack cards played through this wand gain +2 damage and can be delivered by touch. Reduce the AP cost of one Signal Attack or Control card per combat by 1 (minimum 1 AP).
**Cost:** 600 RM

### Dissolution Spike (Rite of Severance)
*Weapon | Very Rare | Tag: one-handed/ritual — Requires: Wire Craft practitioner*

A spike of null-resonant alloy designed to sever the bond between an Echomind and a cybernetic enhancement system — permanently. Used in the Iron Sutra's Rite of Severance (voluntary de-enhancement) and, reportedly, in less voluntary contexts by individuals who have reason to want someone's enhancement network destroyed.

**Card Modification:** As a Tier 2 action against a restrained or prone target: permanently disable one cybernetic enhancement of the wielder's choice (no recovery without a full reinstallation). Target also gains Silence and Stagger 2 from the disruption.

**The Cost:** After using this against an unwilling target, the wielder's RESONANCE-based cards deal -2 damage until their next long rest (the psychic resonance of forced severance).

**Cost:** 18,000 RM

### Signal Lantern (Wire Craft Focus)
*Weapon | Uncommon | Tag: focus/ritual — Requires: Wire Craft practitioner*

A hand-carried resonant lantern used as a Wire Craft focus for field ritual work. The light it emits is tunable — standard illumination by default, signal-spectrum for Wire Craft scanning, and Afterlife-spectrum (a deep violet hue) for Iron Afterlife interaction rituals.

**Card Modification:** All Signal Attack and Control cards played while holding this lantern gain +2 damage or +1 to status stacks. Reduce the AP cost of Wire Craft ritual actions by 1 (minimum 1 AP).
**Cost:** 700 RM

### The Sutensai's Hammer (Ritual Weapon)
*Weapon | Rare | Tag: one-handed/ritual — Requires: Iron Sutra practitioner*

A symbolic hammer used in Iron Sutra installation rituals — the formal blessing of new cybernetic enhancements as they are bonded to a body. The head is inscribed with the Sutra's installation prayers. It is not intended for combat. It functions in combat regardless of intention.

**Card Modification:**
- +2 damage to all Melee Attack cards
- All Melee Attack cards against targets violating Iron Sutra covenants gain +4 damage and add Silence for 1 turn
- Once per combat, one Melee Attack card adds secondary effect: restore one disabled (non-severed) enhancement on a touched ally

**Cost:** 3,200 RM

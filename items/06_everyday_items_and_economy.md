## On Tesshari Currency

The primary currency is the **Resonant Mark** (RM) — a flat, stamped disk of low-grade resonant alloy. The Mark is backed by the Wire Market's verification system: each Mark carries a signal stamp that confirms its authenticity. Counterfeiting is difficult and tracked.

Secondary currency is the **Standard Weight** (SW) — non-resonant metal currency used in the outer Reaches and Ashlands territories where the Wire Market's verification system is not fully present. Standard Weights are approximately equivalent to gold pieces in function but worth roughly 60% of a Resonant Mark in Reach territory.

**For simplicity in play:** Use Resonant Marks as the base unit. The Resonant Mark is the standard unit of exchange in all seven Reaches and replaces gold piece equivalencies from any reference tables.

The **Ashlands** operate on barter — no currency is accepted in deep Ashlands settlements because there is no infrastructure to back it and nothing to spend it on. Barter rates are listed separately.

## Ashlands Barter Economy

In deep Ashlands settlements, currency does not circulate. The Wire Market's signal cannot verify currency. The infrastructure for banking does not exist. What exists is barter, with specific trade goods functioning as de facto currency.

### Specialist Services (Ashlands)

| Service | Barter Equivalent |
|---|---|
| **Salvage run (one day)** | Negotiated; typically a share of what is found |
| **Enhancement repair (salvage-grade)** | Materials cost + 2–4 water-days labor |
| **Guide services (Scar approaches)** | 6–10 water-days per day, paid in advance, no refunds |

### What Doesn't Trade

- Resonant Mark currency: not accepted (no verification infrastructure)
- Wire Market restricted items: too dangerous to trade openly (Wire Market tracks them)
- Convergence-touched items: some settlements refuse to trade them at all; too close to the Scar and its associated instability

## Caste of Steel: Social and Economic Context

The Caste of Steel is not a formal government institution — it is an emergent economic and social reality. Enhancement level determines social standing, access, and expectation.

### Caste Levels and Their Economic Reality

**The Unenhanced (Caste 0)**
No cybernetic enhancements. Approximately 12% of the Tesshari population. Largely found in the Ashlands, in Vaen's following, and in isolated Reach communities. Economically: limited access to Wire Market services (which assume enhancement compatibility), lower social mobility, but complete independence from enhancement maintenance costs.
*Annual living cost:* 200–400 RM outside the Ashlands; barter in deep Ashlands.

**Salvage Caste (Caste 1)**
One to three Salvage-tier enhancements. The majority of Tesshari's outer-Reach and lower-urban population. Economically: able to participate in standard commerce; basic Wire Market access; social expectations of maintenance.
*Annual living cost:* 400–800 RM including maintenance. Wire Market credit is available at 20% interest.

**Standard Caste (Caste 2)**
Two or more Standard-tier enhancements. The middle working caste. Economically: full Wire Market participation, standard social access, most services available.
*Annual living cost:* 1,200–2,500 RM including maintenance and standard services.

**Resonant Caste (Caste 3)**
One or more Resonant-tier enhancements. The upper castes, military command, Wire Market senior staff, Iron Sutra hierarchy.
*Annual living cost:* 5,000–15,000 RM including maintenance, specialized services, and the social expectations of their station.

**The Enhanced Elite (Caste 4)**
Extreme enhancement level — multiple Resonant-tier systems, custom and experimental work. The major faction leaders, claimant-adjacent figures, and the three claimants themselves.
*Annual living cost:* Variable and irrelevant. These people do not operate in the currency economy in the same way.

## Field Notes: What a Starting Party Has

For reference, a standard starting party in Tesshari might plausibly have access to:

- Personal weapons (1–2 Tesshari equivalents at Standard Tier weapon quality)
- Basic armor (Wire-Mesh or Salvage Plating level)
- 1–2 Salvage Tier enhancements per character
- Wire Craft network access at the Basic level (some characters)
- 50–200 RM in immediate funds
- Knowledge of where to get more of the above

The gap between starting equipment and the items in Files 01–05 is intentional and significant. The items in those files are the end of the road, not the beginning of it.

*The economy of Tesshari is the economy of scarcity at the bottom and control at the top. Currency flows toward the Wire Market. Services flow from it. Enhancement flows are both currency and statement — what you have bonded to your body says what the world has allowed you to become. Understanding this is understanding the social physics of the campaign world.*

# Armor and Protection
*Standard variants, shields, wards, and named pieces. Everything that goes between you and the world of Tesshari.*

---

> "The Ironblood armor-smiths will tell you their craft is about protection. They are lying, and they know it. The craft is about display. The protection is how you justify the display."
>
> — Craftbound armorer, Third Reach, name withheld per their request

---

## Standard Armor Variants

---

### Ashwalker Hardplate
*Armor | Common | Tag: medium*

Assembled from whatever resonant-adjacent materials were available to someone who could not afford Wire Market standard. The plates are irregular — different metals, different origins, different thicknesses — held together by heat-bonded wire-silk in a configuration that looks improvised because it is. It is also functional, because the person who made it needed it to work and did not have the option of it not working.

**Defense Value:** +3 to incoming damage mitigation (adds to Guard/Shield pool)
**Card Modification:** This armor can be repaired during a short rest using salvaged materials — restore 3 Shield during a short rest without requiring a consumable.
**Cost:** 45 RM

---

### Craftbound Work Plate
*Armor | Common | Tag: medium*

Industrial-grade resonant plate designed for Craftbound mine workers and infrastructure crew. Heavily reinforced at the joints and shoulders. Offers good protection against the industrial hazards of the First and Third Reaches: falling resonant ore, equipment failure, the occasional cave-in. Offers comparable protection against combat hazards.

**Defense Value:** +4 to incoming damage mitigation
**Card Modification:** Defense cards played while wearing this armor reduce incoming environmental hazard damage (heat, pressure, electrical) by an additional 3.
**Cost:** 250 RM

---

### Full Signal Harness
*Armor | Uncommon | Tag: heavy*

The Wire Market's standard armor issue for field operatives who expect to be in serious trouble. Full-body plate in brushed resonant-metal with an integrated signal processing layer — the suit itself functions as a weak resonant sensor array, giving the wearer basic awareness of signal activity in their vicinity.

**Defense Value:** +6 to incoming damage mitigation
**Card Modification:** Reduce the AP cost of one Defense card per turn by 1 (minimum 1 AP). The wearer passively detects active Wire Craft abilities and cybernetic enhancements within 30 feet — this information is available without playing a card.
**Cost:** 1,500 RM

---

### Ghost-Frame (Stealth Variant Light Armor)
*Armor | Uncommon | Tag: light*

A wire-silk bodysuit with resonant-dampening fibers woven throughout — designed to minimize the wearer's resonant signature and reduce noise. Used by Wire Market intelligence operatives and anyone who needs to not be noticed in places that notice. The Wire Market calls it Ghost-Frame. Field operatives call it "the suit you wear when the other suit got you noticed."

**Defense Value:** +2 to incoming damage mitigation
**Card Modification:** Signal-based targeting effects against the wearer have their damage reduced by 3. The wearer's enhancement configuration cannot be read by scanning — all Signal Attack cards targeting the wearer lose their secondary enhancement-targeting riders.
**Cost:** 900 RM

---

### Hollow-Frame Light Plate
*Armor | Common | Tag: medium*

A medium armor that uses internal hollow resonant-metal structural members rather than solid plate — lighter than standard plate, slightly less protective, significantly better for movement. Preferred by Resonant-caste professionals who need protection without broadcasting that they are armored.

**Defense Value:** +3 to incoming damage mitigation
**Card Modification:** Mobility cards played while wearing this armor do not cost an additional AP (no movement penalty despite being medium armor).
**Cost:** 350 RM

---

### Ironblood Dress Plate
*Armor | Uncommon | Tag: heavy*

Ironblood social armor — heavy plate engraved with caste insignia, lineage marks, and the bearer's recorded enhancements. Worn at formal functions where status must be displayed. Absolutely protective. The protection is almost incidental to the function.

**Defense Value:** +6 to incoming damage mitigation
**Card Modification:** VEIL-based social cards gain +2 to their effect while wearing this armor in Ironblood social contexts. In non-Ironblood contexts, Deception cards attempting to claim lower caste status automatically fail.
**Cost:** 3,500 RM (custom commission)

---

### Resonant Chain Coat
*Armor | Common | Tag: medium*

A full-length coat of interlocked resonant metal rings. Heavier than wire-mesh, more flexible than plate. The chain rings each carry minor resonant properties — nothing significant individually, but the cumulative effect is a mild signal interference that makes the wearer slightly harder to target with enhancement-based systems.

**Defense Value:** +3 to incoming damage mitigation
**Card Modification:** Signal-based Attack cards from ranged enemies lose their critical hit bonus damage against the wearer (the ring interference disrupts precision targeting).
**Cost:** 175 RM

---

### Salvage-Shard Vest
*Armor | Common | Tag: light*

A vest of leather (synthetic, Tesshari-equivalent) reinforced with irregular resonant metal shards — whatever was available, bonded in place with wire-silk. The shards catch light oddly and make the wearer look like they have survived something. This is usually true.

**Defense Value:** +2 to incoming damage mitigation
**Card Modification:** Once per combat, reduce the damage of one incoming slashing Melee Attack card by 4 (the shard configuration deflects glancing cuts).
**Cost:** 35 RM

---

### Signal Plate (Standard Military)
*Armor | Common | Tag: heavy*

The standard military-grade armor used by Reach garrison troops and private security forces. Full resonant-plate, uniform finish, with the issuing Reach's garrison markings. No special properties. Reliable. Available everywhere.

**Defense Value:** +5 to incoming damage mitigation
**Card Modification:** —
**Cost:** 750 RM

---

### Wire-Silk Combat Suit
*Armor | Common | Tag: light*

A full-body suit of tight-woven wire-silk in the Wire Market's operational cut — tailored to be worn under outer garments. Provides meaningful impact protection and mild signal dampening, functions well with cybernetic enhancements (no enhancement chafing, no interface disruption), and looks like expensive casual clothing when not in combat.

**Defense Value:** +1 to incoming damage mitigation (stacks with outer light armor; does not stack with medium or heavy, but still provides the Dampening benefit)
**Card Modification:** Reduce incoming forced enhancement disruption effects by 3 (Signal Attack cards that suppress enhancements are reduced by 3 stacks or damage).
**Cost:** 120 RM

## Shields and Wards

---

### Ablative Signal Plate
*Armor | Uncommon | Tag: shield/tower*

A tower-shield-sized resonant plate designed specifically to absorb pulse arm discharges. The face of the plate is layered with an ablative resonant compound that dissipates force energy — each shot damages the coating, but the coating can be replaced.

**Defense Value:** +5 to incoming damage mitigation (two-handed carry required)
**Card Modification:** Reduce incoming force damage from ranged Attack cards by 4. After absorbing 30 cumulative force damage, this reduction drops to 0 until the coating is replaced (50 RM, 10 minutes).
**Cost:** 400 RM

---

### Blastback Ward
*Armor | Uncommon | Tag: shield*

A forearm-mounted resonant plate with an active ward node that creates a localized force displacement field when the wearer braces. Used by Wire Market breach teams entering rooms they expect to have explosives in.

**Defense Value:** +3 to incoming damage mitigation
**Card Modification:** Once per combat, play one Defense card as a Reaction to halve the damage of one incoming area-of-effect Attack card.
**Cost:** 550 RM

---

### Circuit-Mirror Shield
*Armor | Uncommon | Tag: shield*

A resonant plate shield with a high-polish mirror finish on the outer face. The face is a signal reflector — not decorative. Signal-based targeting systems bounce off it. Pulse arm discharges that don't directly contact the shield face can be angled to strike adjacent targets.

**Defense Value:** +3 to incoming damage mitigation
**Card Modification:** Once per turn, when a ranged Signal or Resonance Attack card misses the bearer, the bearer may redirect it to one enemy within 30 feet (that enemy takes the card's base damage with no riders).
**Cost:** 700 RM

---

### Dead-Space Ward
*Armor | Rare | Tag: ward/wearable*

Not a shield — a resonant metal brace worn on the forearm that projects a ward field extending two feet around the wearer. The field is not physical. It is resonant interference concentrated into a defensive layer. Strikes within the field feel like hitting slightly submerged water — slowed, dampened.

**Defense Value:** +2 to incoming damage mitigation (hand remains free)
**Card Modification:** Reduce all incoming Melee Attack card damage by 2 (minimum 0). This reduction applies in all directions and does not require a Defense card to activate — it is passive.
**Cost:** 2,200 RM

---

### Forge-Iron Bulwark
*Armor | Common | Tag: shield/tower*

A heavy resonant-iron shield, thick and wide, used by Craftbound workers in explosive-hazard environments. No special properties. Just dense resonant iron in a very wide format. Stops things.

**Defense Value:** +5 to incoming damage mitigation (two-handed carry required)
**Card Modification:** Once per combat, the Bulwark may absorb one Attack card's full damage in place of the bearer (the shield takes it instead). After absorbing 30 cumulative damage this way, the Bulwark is depleted and provides no further Defense Value until repaired.
**Cost:** 200 RM

---

### Iron Sutra Ward-Plate
*Armor | Uncommon | Tag: shield — Requires: Iron Sutra practitioner (Integration)*

A ceremonial shield used by Iron Sutra shrine guardians — medium-sized, covered on both faces with Sutra script. The script is not decorative: it is a continuous prayer, and the prayer has been running long enough to develop resonant properties of its own.

**Defense Value:** +3 to incoming damage mitigation (+4 while the bearer is actively in an Iron Sutra context: performing rituals, in a temple, maintaining faith)
**Card Modification:** Iron Afterlife manifestations and entities cannot play Attack cards targeting the bearer while this shield is raised (they may use other means; they cannot make direct strikes).
**Cost:** 800 RM

---

### Null-Plate Barrier
*Armor | Rare | Tag: shield*

A resonant plate that has been treated with null-resonant compound — the same material as the Null-Skin Mesh, but in solid plate form. The result is a shield that does not exist in the resonant spectrum. Signal scans show nothing where it stands. Enhancement-based perception cannot register it.

**Defense Value:** +3 to incoming damage mitigation
**Card Modification:** Signal Attack cards and enhancement-based targeting cannot account for this shield. When the bearer plays a Defense card in the same round they are targeted by a ranged Signal Attack, the attacker's card deals -4 damage (the targeting calculation is wrong).
**Cost:** 5,500 RM

---

### Resonant Buckler
*Armor | Common | Tag: shield/light*

A small forearm-mounted resonant plate — barely the size of a book, but dense enough to deflect a surprising amount. Standard carry for Resonant-caste professionals who want protection without the social statement of a full shield.

**Defense Value:** +1 to incoming damage mitigation (hand remains free)
**Card Modification:** —
**Cost:** 80 RM

---

### Signal-Break Targe
*Armor | Uncommon | Tag: shield*

A medium resonant shield with an embedded signal emitter that disrupts incoming Wire Craft effects on contact. When a Wire Craft ability or resonant magic directly targets the bearer, the targe attempts to scramble the effect.

**Defense Value:** +3 to incoming damage mitigation
**Card Modification:** Once per combat, play one Defense card as a Reaction to completely negate one incoming Signal or Resonance Control card (the effect is disrupted and has no impact). The emitter requires a short rest to recharge.
**Cost:** 1,200 RM

---

### Wire-Mesh Round Shield
*Armor | Common | Tag: shield*

Standard Wire Market-issue shield for lower-level field operatives — a medium-round shield of layered wire-mesh with a resonant core. The wire-mesh configuration catches and disperses impact energy across the surface rather than concentrating it. Lighter than solid plate, nearly as protective.

**Defense Value:** +3 to incoming damage mitigation
**Card Modification:** Once per combat, when a projectile Ranged Attack card misses the bearer (the attack roll is made and fails), the projectile is caught in the mesh — that attacker cannot play that specific card again this combat.
**Cost:** 60 RM

## Named and Unique Armor

---

### The Carapace of Reach Three
*Armor | Rare | Tag: medium — Requires: Integration (long rest to bond)*

> "It survived the Third Reach infrastructure wars. So did the person in it. Those are two separate miracles."

A full-body medium plate system recovered from the Third Reach's urban conflict zone — it was custom-built for a Third Reach militia commander named Osei-Varna who fought the seven-year infrastructure war in it and survived to retire. The plate carries impact scoring from approximately 200 confirmed hits, none of which ended Osei-Varna. The scoring is documented. Osei-Varna's son sold it after she passed. He felt guilty about it immediately.

**Defense Value:** +5 to incoming damage mitigation
**Card Modification:**
- Once per combat, when the bearer would be reduced to 0 HP, they are instead reduced to 1 HP (Osei-Varna got up — so do you)
- Mobility cards do not cost additional AP despite being medium armor
- Reduce the AP cost of one Defense card per turn by 1 (minimum 1 AP) while in urban terrain

**The Cost:** Wearing armor that survived another person's entire war means inheriting something of that war. Once per long rest, the GM may trigger a residual battle impression — the bearer briefly perceives a combat situation from Osei-Varna's experience. Duration: 1 round. They are still in control; the impression is additional context, not possession. The impressions are always of moments where Osei-Varna chose correctly.

**Cost:** 8,500 RM (Osei-Varna's son would like to buy it back if the party ever wants to sell)

---

### Hollow Shell
*Armor | Uncommon | Tag: light — Requires: Integration (long rest to bond)*

> "The armor looks empty when no one is in it. It still looks empty when someone is."

A light resonant bodysuit constructed by a Voided craftsperson in the style of a full enhancement housing — it resembles a fully enhanced person's outer body layer. From the outside, through basic scanning, it reads as a heavily enhanced Forged individual. The person inside is read as the armor.

**Defense Value:** +2 to incoming damage mitigation
**Card Modification:**
- Signal-based identification and targeting cards cannot read the wearer's true enhancement configuration — all Signal Attack cards that rely on enhancement-targeting riders lose those riders
- The armor can be configured to project any caste's resonant signature (SIGNAL check target 5 to detect the projection as false)
- Voided wearers: VEIL-based social cards do not suffer penalties from Voided status while wearing this armor

**The Cost:** The armor "wants" to be seen as enhanced. After wearing it for 8+ hours continuously, the wearer has difficulty distinguishing their natural identity from the projected one. All VEIL cards cost 1 additional AP for 1 hour after removing the armor (disorientation penalty).

**Cost:** 1,800 RM

---

### Mnemonic Plate
*Armor | Very Rare | Tag: heavy — Requires: Integration (long rest to bond)*

> "It remembers everyone who wore it. You will remember them too."

A resonant plate armor of exceptional construction that has accumulated Echomind impressions from twelve previous bearers over approximately eighty years of continuous use. The impressions layer — each bearer's combat experience, movement patterns, and tactical instincts are accessible to the current wearer. The downside is that this works both directions.

**Defense Value:** +7 to incoming damage mitigation
**Card Modification:**
- +2 damage to all Attack cards (accumulated combat experience)
- +2 to Defense Value (total +9 mitigation)
- The bearer always acts first in initiative (the archive's instinct is faster than conscious thought)
- Once per combat, the bearer may ask the GM one tactical question and receive an honest answer based on what twelve experienced fighters would know

**The Cost:** The twelve previous bearers are present in the armor. They have opinions. When the bearer makes a decision they would have made differently, the bearer hears a low-level resonant disagreement — not words, but dissent. Once per combat when the GM judges the dissent is strong, the bearer must choose: either accept the archive's preferred action (and gain Guard 3) or override it (and play their intended card at +1 AP cost as the dissent creates hesitation).

**Cost:** 45,000 RM

---

### Pilgrim's Weave
*Armor | Uncommon | Tag: light — Requires: Voided character only*

> "She walks through the Ashlands without armor and does not take damage. She is wearing this. She seems to believe she is not wearing armor."

A full-body garment of woven wire-silk constructed in the Iron Sutra Voided tradition — it does not look like armor because the Voided philosophy holds that armor is a form of attachment to the body that interferes with the pilgrimage. The craftperson who made this believed they were making ceremonial clothing. The protective properties emerged from the sincerity of the weaving. The Iron Sutra does not have a clean explanation for this.

**Defense Value:** +2 to incoming damage mitigation, plus +1 for each empty cybernetic enhancement slot the wearer has (maximum +4 bonus from empty slots; a completely unenhanced wearer gains the full +4, for a total Defense Value of +6)
**Card Modification:**
- The wearer takes no damage from standard Ashlands environmental hazards — Ashlands-sourced damage is reduced to 0
- Mobility cards cannot be countered by Root effects (the pilgrimage does not stop)

**The Cost:** None, in the strict sense. The Voided path has its own costs, and this armor is a product of that path. The armor does not function for non-Voided characters.

**Cost:** 2,500 RM (available from Voided pilgrim communities)

---

### The Skin of Wire-Commander Taas
*Armor | Very Rare | Tag: medium — Requires: Integration (long rest to bond)*

> "Taas was famous for two things: never losing a tactical engagement and never explaining how. This is how."

A complete medium-plate armor system built around an active tactical processing enhancement integrated directly into the chest plate. The enhancement runs constant battlefield analysis — tracking movement patterns, predicting attack vectors, calculating optimal positioning. Wire-Commander Taas wore it for twenty years and won forty-three documented engagements. Taas disappeared into the Ashlands in the fourth year of the Convergence, which is not considered losing, technically, because no one can confirm an outcome.

**Defense Value:** +5 to incoming damage mitigation
**Card Modification:**
- +3 to Defense Value from active threat anticipation (total +8 mitigation)
- The bearer always acts first in initiative
- Once per combat before the first round: up to 6 allies within 60 feet gain +3 damage on their next Attack card (the processing system transmits optimal strike windows)
- Once per long rest: for 3 rounds, the bearer cannot be flanked, all incoming Attack cards deal -4 damage, and the bearer gains +2 AP Max for the duration (Forty-Three Engagements)

**The Cost:** The tactical processor runs constantly. Long rests require one additional hour to be fully effective (the processor cycles down slowly). In high-stress social situations, VEIL-based social cards cost 1 additional AP (the processor highlights exits and threat vectors, creating a distracted quality).

**Cost:** 55,000 RM (if Taas is confirmed dead; currently classified as missing in action)

### The Unmarked Coat
*Armor | Rare | Tag: light — Requires: Integration (long rest to bond)*

> "There is no caste mark. No Reach affiliation. No Wire Market stamp. No record of its manufacture. Whoever made it wanted something that did not exist before it was needed and did not persist after it was no longer needed."

A long coat of resonant-fiber material with no identifying marks, no manufacturer's stamp, no signal signature. Not no enhancements — the material is enhanced. It simply carries no identity. In a world where all resonant metal carries a verifiable origin, this coat's absence of origin is the most distinctive thing about it.

**Defense Value:** +2 to incoming damage mitigation
**Card Modification:**
- Wire Craft analysis and Signal Attack targeting cards cannot determine the wearer's identity, caste, or enhancement configuration from their resonant signature
- VEIL-based Deception and concealment cards gain +3 to their effect while wearing this coat
- Signal-based monitoring systems (checkpoints, Wire Market scanning) cannot add the wearer to a watchlist or confirm their presence — all Signal Control cards that create tracking or marking effects fail against this coat

**The Cost:** Prolonged wear (30+ consecutive days) blurs the wearer's own sense of identity. After each week of consecutive wear, VEIL-based Persuasion cards cost 1 additional AP (cumulative) as the wearer loses the conviction of self-presentation. Reset on a long rest taken without the coat and dedicated to conscious self-reconstruction.

**Cost:** Not commercially available. This coat does not exist in any catalog. If the party has it, someone who did not want to be identified made it for a purpose they no longer needed it for.

### Voided War-Wrap
*Armor | Rare | Tag: medium — Requires: Voided character only (Integration)*

> "The Voided renounce enhancements. They do not renounce survival."

A complete medium armor in the Voided tradition — heavily constructed from resonant materials but integrated without any enhancement interface. A fully enhanced person cannot use it (the structure was built around the assumption of a non-enhanced body and does not have the interface points their body would require). For a Voided warrior, it is precision-tailored.

**Defense Value:** +5 to incoming damage mitigation
**Card Modification:**
- Reduce all incoming Signal and Resonance Attack card damage by 4 (Negation Plate)
- All Melee Attack cards deal +5 damage against targets with 3 or more active cybernetic enhancements (the negation field disrupts concentrated enhancement systems on contact)

**The Cost:** The armor is known. Wearing it in Ironblood or heavily enhanced company is a declaration. Ironblood caste members are automatically hostile; VEIL-based social cards against enhanced soldiers or enforcers cost 1 additional AP.

**Cost:** 7,000 RM (Voided armorer commissions; six-month wait minimum)

### Wretched Plate (The Ashlands Survivor)
*Armor | Rare | Tag: heavy — Requires: Integration (long rest to bond)*

> "It's been in the Ashlands. So has the person who wore it. The armor and the person both came back wrong. Both came back."

A heavy resonant plate armor that has been continuously present in the Ashlands for at minimum three years — the exact duration is unclear because the person who wore it for that period is not entirely consistent about timelines. The metal has taken on Engine Scar contamination: it is darker, slightly translucent at the edges, and occasionally warm to the touch without apparent source. The contamination is stable enough to be safe for use. "Safe" is doing work in that sentence.

**Defense Value:** +6 to incoming damage mitigation
**Card Modification:**
- Reduce all incoming fire, acid, and Ashlands environmental damage by 5
- Reduce all incoming Corruption and Engine Scar effects by 4 (the armor has been contaminated and survived — it knows how)
- All Attack cards played while wearing this armor generate Guard 1 on the bearer when they deal damage to enemies with Ashlands corruption (the contamination field disrupts close contact)

**The Cost:** Wearing this armor for more than 12 consecutive hours requires a FRAME check (target 3) or gain 1 level of Overheat from low-grade contamination exposure. This target increases by 1 for each consecutive day of continuous wear.

**Cost:** 12,000 RM (specialists in Ashlands-contaminated materials will pay more for study access; the bearer should be careful about what "study access" entails)

Popular in the Third Reach's lower districts where conventional armor is expensive and wire-silk is available.

**Defense Value:** +2 to incoming damage mitigation
**Card Modification:** Once per round, reduce the damage of one incoming Melee Attack card by 3 (passive benefit — no card required).
**Cost:** 18 RM

### The Cage (Penal Armor)
*Armor | Uncommon | Tag: heavy*

A full-body restraint-and-armor system used by the Wire Market's security division for the transport of high-value detainees. Provides significant protection — and prevents the wearer from removing it without a coded release signal. Found in Wire Market holding facilities; occasionally on individuals who escaped custody before the release signal was provided.

**Defense Value:** +5 to incoming damage mitigation
**Card Modification:**
- Reduce all incoming environmental damage (poison, acid, extreme heat/cold) by 4 (environmental isolation sealing)
- This armor cannot be removed without a Wire Market release signal or a successful SIGNAL + IRON combined check (target 8, full action) — the wearer cannot remove it themselves

**Cost:** Not for sale. Found. The Wire Market will want it back, and the wearer still inside it, given the circumstances of most acquisitions.

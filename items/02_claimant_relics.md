# The First Tooth
*Very Rare Resonant Blade | Requires Attunement*

## "He named it after the first thing he took. He has not been asked to elaborate."

## Description

A short resonant blade, forty centimeters from hilt to tip, curved slightly at the end in the style of the early First Reach forges. The blade is functional but not beautiful — made to be used, not displayed, by someone who was learning to fight while they were also fighting. The metal shows the evidence of hard use: stress fractures repaired with wire-threaded resonant fills, the edge re-ground so many times that the original profile is gone and what remains is thinner, more aggressive.

The hilt is wrapped in worn wire-mesh grip material that has been replaced at least three times. Beneath the final wrap, someone has etched into the bare metal: a list of names. Twenty-three names. They are small and they are old and they are in Korrath's handwriting from forty years ago, before the hand that held it was replaced with something built for war.

The blade rings when it strikes resonant metal. Not loudly. A clear, high tone that fades quickly. It sounds like recognition.

## History

Korrath carried this blade for eleven years, from the age of sixteen through his twenty-seventh year, through the early campaigns that built his reputation and the later campaigns that built his army. He had it forged by a salvage-smith in what is now the occupied Fifth Reach, paying in work rather than currency because he had no currency. The smith added the slight curve without being asked. Korrath kept it that way.

The names on the hilt are the names of people he killed in those eleven years who had names he knew. He stopped keeping the list at some point between the twenty-third name and the beginning of the large-scale campaigns, when names became impractical. He has never spoken about this publicly.

He replaced the blade with the integrated systems in his arm during the reconstruction that began his transformation into what he is now. The replacement was practical: the built-in systems are more efficient, more reliable, more lethal. He kept the blade. He has never explained why. It is in his personal vault in the fortress of the First Reach, in a case made of something that is neither wood nor metal, lined with material he apparently brought from the Fifth Reach after the occupation. The case is not locked. He does not believe anyone would take it.

## Properties

**While Attuned:**

The First Tooth functions as a +2 resonant blade. Beyond the mechanical enhancement, it carries something from its decades of use:

- **Known Target.** The bearer designates one creature they have fought before (within the past week) as a Known Target as a bonus action. Against Known Targets, the blade deals an additional 2d6 damage on a hit. This represents the blade's accumulated memory of combat — it has been in enough fights that it has opinions about people who fight in particular ways.
- **The List.** Once per long rest, the bearer may press the blade flat to their palm and concentrate for one minute. They receive a vision of one combat that Korrath fought during the blade's time with him — not as an outside observer, but from inside his perspective. These visions are not flattering. They are accurate. A creature who has seen enough of them begins to understand what Korrath was before he decided what to become.
- **Resonant Ring.** When the blade strikes a creature with cybernetic enhancements, the ring tells the bearer the general location of the most significant enhancement (head, torso, left arm, etc.) as a free action. This has been useful for forty years.
- The blade cannot be broken by mundane means. It has been broken three times in its history and repaired each time. Something in the metal knows how to hold together.

**Drawback.** A bearer who attunes to the blade and spends more than three days with it will begin to hear, faintly, the voices of the twenty-three names. They do not say anything. They are simply present. The bearer knows they are there.

## Adventure Hook

Korrath's vault archivist has gone missing. She vanished three days ago and the only thing missing from her files is the record of what is in the deep vault — including the First Tooth. Korrath does not know the blade is potentially compromised. Someone with an interest in Korrath's psychology has moved to acquire the one item that shows what he was before the replacements made vulnerability inefficient.

# The Salvage Manifesto
*Very Rare Wondrous Item (Document) | Requires Attunement by a creature with military command experience*

## "It is not a book about war. It is a book about geometry. The war is just the application."

## Description

A physical document — fourteen pages of resonant-thread paper, bound with a single wire-silk cord. The text is handwritten in Korrath's compressed, efficient script, with no decoration, no margin notes, no revision marks. It reads like someone who wrote something once, correctly, and had no reason to revisit it.

The diagrams are the most remarkable aspect: troop positioning, enhancement utilization charts, logistics flows drawn with the precision of someone who thinks spatially about things other people think about abstractly. Some of the diagrams have been reviewed by Wire Market analysts who describe them as fifty years ahead of current military doctrine.

When held by someone with command experience, the document is warm. When held by someone without such experience, it is room temperature. The document knows the difference.

It is the only physical copy. Korrath has not produced copies. He has never explained why.

## History

Korrath wrote the Manifesto in a single session, seven years into his campaigns, after the Battle of the Copper Plain in which he turned a catastrophic supply failure into a strategic victory by rerouting the entire logistical chain in real time. He has described the writing session as "organizing what I had already learned so I could learn the next thing." He distributed copies to his senior commanders for a period of three years. Those copies were recalled and destroyed on the same day that he began the enhancement program that transformed his army into what it is now. He kept one copy.

The Manifesto circulates in fragments — pieces copied by commanders who memorized sections and later transcribed them after the recall order. Wire Market intelligence analysts collect and study these fragments. The full document has never been on the open market and has never been publicly displayed.

The current copy is in Korrath's personal library. Unlike the First Tooth, this is locked — behind three independent security systems, one of which is biological (keyed to Korrath's remaining organic tissue) and two of which are resonant-signal locks that reset daily.

## Properties

**Attunement.** Requires a creature who has commanded at least ten people in actual combat (not training, not simulation — real stakes, real consequences). The creature must have lost someone under their command. This is the specific prerequisite: not just command, but command that cost them something.

**While Attuned:**

- The bearer gains proficiency in all Intelligence-based skills if they are not already proficient. The Manifesto does not teach — it optimizes what the bearer already knows.
- Once per long rest, the bearer may consult the Manifesto (or simply hold it and think, if attuned) to receive a tactical analysis of their current situation. The DM provides the optimal tactical approach for the next 4 hours of activity — ambush angles, resource allocation, vulnerability assessment — as if the bearer had spent 8 hours in strategic planning. The bearer does not have to follow this analysis. They simply have it.
- The bearer is immune to being surprised in combat. Their enhancement-level thinking makes them functionally incapable of being caught without a contingency.
- **Force Multiplier.** Any creature that the bearer commands and who can clearly hear the bearer's instructions receives a +2 bonus to attack rolls for the first round of any combat. This is not enhancement. This is the consequence of being told, precisely, what to do by someone who knows.

**Limitation.** The Manifesto only provides genuine tactical advantage against situations its bearer has not already mastered. If a bearer becomes too good — if they have internalized more than the Manifesto contains — it goes quiet. It will not optimize for someone who already knows more than it does. This is the final lesson in the document, which the reader only understands once they've reached it.

## Adventure Hook

A senior commander in one of the Broken Reaches factions has a fragment — three pages, copied from memory — and has used them to execute three perfect tactical victories in three months. The faction leader is asking uncomfortable questions about how their general has become suddenly exceptional. The general wants to know if the full Manifesto actually exists. They will pay substantially for that answer. They may pay even more not to have it.

# The Last Flesh
*Legendary Wondrous Item | Cannot be attuned*

## "There is a theory that Korrath's only remaining vulnerability is biological. The theory is correct. The theory is also, technically, actionable."

## Description

A small sealed container of brass-lacquered resonant metal, approximately the size of a closed fist. The seal is biometric — it will only open in the presence of Korrath's resonant signature. Inside (this is known only through historical record; the container has not been opened since sealing): a small piece of organic tissue, approximately two centimeters square, preserved in a medium that predates current preservation techniques.

The container is warm. From the outside, indistinguishably from any other resonant metal container. But people who have held it report feeling, very faintly, a pulse. The pulse has not been explained.

The container is unassuming. Unmarked. If not accompanied by documentation or context, it is indistinguishable from a hundred similar containers used for medical or specimen transport.

## History

The enhancement process that made Korrath what he is now was not a single procedure. It was a forty-year transformation, conducted in stages, each stage removing more organic tissue and replacing it with resonant-metal systems. The first replacement was tactical — an eye lost in battle, replaced with something better. The later replacements were philosophical: Korrath's conviction, gradually solidified, that biological matter was the limiting factor in human potential.

The Last Flesh is what remains. At some point during the transformation — the records suggest it was the day of the thirty-first procedure, more than halfway through — Korrath preserved a piece of himself. The Iron Sutra scholars who have studied the container from a distance believe this was not sentiment. They believe it was insurance.

No one has been able to verify the popular theory that destroying the Last Flesh would harm Korrath. This is because no one has been able to acquire it. It has been in his personal vault for thirty years. It is the most closely guarded item in his possession. The fact that it is the most closely guarded item in his possession is, to many analysts, confirmation enough.

## Properties

This item cannot be attuned. It has no active properties. It is a piece of biological material in a container.

**What the Records Suggest:**

The Iron Sutra's most advanced biological-resonance scholars have an unpublished theory: when a being undergoes the level of enhancement-conversion that Korrath has undergone, the Echomind — the resonant interaction between flesh and metal — does not transition cleanly. A distributed remnant of the soul clings to the last biological anchor. Destroy the biological anchor, and the resonance cascade would not kill Korrath. But it would destroy whatever remains of his capacity for the Echomind.

What this means practically: without the Last Flesh, Korrath could not Ascend. The Seventh Convergence requires an Echomind intact and resonant. He has spent forty years building toward a goal, and this small container is the single biological condition without which none of it is achievable.

He knows this. He keeps it accordingly.

**If destroyed:** Korrath immediately knows. The resonance cascade is perceptible to him as a physical sensation. What happens next depends on how close the Seventh Convergence is. If it is imminent, he has potentially been robbed of everything. If it is not imminent, he has time to be furious.

The DM should treat this item as a campaign-level lever — not a weapon, but a key. Acquiring it, holding it over Korrath, using it as leverage in negotiations, or destroying it are all actions with campaign-scale consequences.

## Current Location

In Korrath's deep vault, First Reach fortress, behind nine security layers, guarded by eight of his most enhanced soldiers at all times. The security has been increased three times in the past two years. Something has made him nervous.

## Adventure Hook

A defector from Korrath's intelligence apparatus — a signals specialist who intercepted communications she was not supposed to see — has documentation that one of Korrath's senior commanders is planning to sell the container's location to the Hollow Author. The defector wants to warn someone. She has chosen the party because they are, in her assessment, the least likely people to immediately sell the information back to Korrath for the reward. She is probably correct.

# The First Removal
*Rare Wondrous Item | Requires Attunement by a creature that has voluntarily surrendered something they valued*

## "The extraction was performed in a public square, before her followers, without anesthetic. She did not make a sound. Those who were there still cannot explain how that was possible."

## Description

A cybernetic sensory enhancement — an auditory amplifier, specifically, designed to be bonded to the inner ear. Extracted. The extraction was not gentle: the connective tissue is still attached, dried to the metal, visible as faint fibrous threads along the lower edge. The enhancement itself is unremarkable by current standards — mid-tier quality, fifteen years old, the kind of work that was considered excellent at the time and is now considered adequate.

What is remarkable is what the extraction did to it. Resonant metal removed from a living body carries the resonance of that body. Resonant metal removed from a body that was in the process of becoming what Vaen was becoming carries something more. The enhancement hums at a frequency that is not quite the standard resonant spectrum. It is in the register of something in the process of being released.

Vaen's followers treat it as a relic. They do not handle it freely. When it is brought to the ill or the suffering, people report a brief clarity — not of sound, but of thought. The sense of hearing something they had not been able to hear before, without knowing what the sound was.

## History

Vaen began her removals eighteen months ago. The first removal was the auditory enhancement she had received at the age of twenty-two, at the Caste of Steel's mandate level, as part of standard societal compliance. She had worn it for nine years. In nine years, she had become so accustomed to its presence that she was unable to remember what her hearing had been before it.

She announced the removal three days before it happened, in a public address in the Second Reach capital. The address is short enough to quote in full and has been: *"The metal is not my enemy. The metal is what the world told me I needed to become real. I am going to find out if that is true."*

The extraction was performed in the same public square where she made the address, by a surgeon she selected for her skill rather than her loyalty. Four hundred and eleven people watched. The surgeon was visibly distressed. Vaen sat still throughout. When it was over, she held the extracted enhancement in her open hand, looked at the crowd, and said: *"Still here."*

Her followers collected the enhancement. The Iron Sutra does not officially acknowledge her movement, but three of the Iron Sutra's inner council have privately visited the enhancement in its current keeping place.

## Properties

**Attunement.** The enhancement attunes to creatures who have genuinely given something up — not lost it, not had it taken, but chosen to relinquish something they valued because keeping it was inconsistent with who they were trying to become. The giving-up must have cost them something real.

A creature who attempts to attune while holding on to something they should release will find the enhancement cold and quiet. They will hear, once, in the frequency between sound and resonance: *"Not yet."*

**While Attuned:**

- The bearer has advantage on all Wisdom (Insight) checks to understand another person's motivations, emotional state, or hidden intentions. The First Removal carried Vaen's Echomind for nine years. Something of her capacity to hear past the surface has remained in the metal.
- The bearer can hear the resonant frequency of any cybernetic enhancement within 30 feet. This is not audible to others. They hear it as a quiet background hum, distinct per-enhancement, identifiable with practice (DC 12 Perception check to identify specific enhancement types). This is Vaen's original enhancement's function — amplified auditory sensitivity — operating in the register of resonant metal rather than sound.
- Once per long rest, the bearer may enter a state of stillness (cannot move, must be sitting or standing without preparation for combat) for one full minute. At the end of this minute, the bearer has a clarity of purpose for the next 24 hours: disadvantage cannot be imposed on them by psychological or social effects for this duration.
- The bearer cannot be magically compelled or charmed by effects that require the bearer to act against their stated values. The enhancement recognizes integrity and defends it.

**Attunement Cost.** The bearer gradually loses the ability to ignore pain. Not enhanced pain — they simply begin to feel minor discomforts that enhancements typically filter. This is Vaen's point. It does not impair function. It is uncomfortable.

## Adventure Hook

Vaen's primary organizer — the person who manages her movement's logistics, who has been with her since before the removals — has disappeared. In her kept possessions: a note. The note says only *"The First Removal has been compromised. Get it out before they take it."* The organizer's last known location was the Second Reach's eastern transit district, three days ago. She has not been in contact since.

# The Living Doctrine
*Unique Encounter — Not a Standard Item*

## "She doesn't carry anything. She is the thing she carries."

## Description

Vaen keeps no possessions. She owns nothing, carries nothing, has refused gifts since the removals began. The things she touches are not hers; they are the world's, and she is moving through the world without accumulation.

But her hands are the only tools she uses. And contact with her hands does something that has been documented by nine separate witnesses and cross-referenced by two independent Iron Sutra observers who were attempting to disprove it.

Physical contact with Vaen's hands — skin contact, deliberate, for more than a few seconds — does the following:

## Mechanical Properties

This is not an item. It is an encounter property. When a player character touches Vaen's hands (held, blessed, or any extended skin-to-skin contact), apply the following:

**The Touching:** The creature immediately makes a DC 18 Wisdom saving throw. This is not a hostile effect. The creature may choose to fail.

**On a Failed Save (or Chosen Failure):** The creature experiences a flash of absolute clarity about one thing they are avoiding. The DM identifies this privately — it should be something true about the character that the player has not yet confronted, something the character has been circling but not addressing. The character knows what it is. They know Vaen knows what it is. There is no exchange of words. The information is not spoken. It is simply present between them.

The character also feels, for the next 24 hours, as if one of their cybernetic enhancements is not part of them — they feel it as foreign in a way they have not felt it before. If they have no enhancements, they feel instead the absence of something they rely on that is not physical. This is not debilitating. It is simply present.

**On a Successful Save:** The creature feels nothing unusual. But they have disadvantage on Wisdom (Insight) checks for 24 hours. They have refused something.

**Long-Term.** A creature who has experienced the contact effect twice has a permanent +2 bonus to Wisdom saving throws. Something in the experience of being seen clearly, twice, makes a person harder to deceive — including by themselves.

## Context

Vaen is aware of this effect. She does not know if she is causing it intentionally or if it is a consequence of what she is becoming. She does not advertise it. She does not avoid touch. She uses her hands for the things hands are for — work, contact, care — and if something happens when she touches people, she treats it as incidental.

The Iron Sutra's inner council believes this is evidence that her removal process is a form of Ascension preparation. Three of them are terrified by this. Two of them are quietly hoping it is true.

## Adventure Hook

A party member who has been touched by Vaen begins experiencing something unexpected: they can feel their enhancements. Not pain — sensation. They can feel the metal in their flesh as specifically as they can feel their fingers. The sensation is new and becoming more detailed. They cannot explain it. They find Vaen to ask. Vaen says: *"I don't know what is happening to you. But I'd like to watch."* This is not reassuring.

# The Unsent Letter
*Legendary Wondrous Item (Document) | Requires Attunement: by reading it aloud, alone, in its entirety*

## "She wrote it in one sitting. She folded it. She addressed it to a dead woman. Then she put it in a case and has carried it for eighteen months through every public event, every removal, every speech, and she has not sent it because there is no one left to send it to."

## Description

A single folded page of resonant-thread paper, carried in a wire-silk case that closes with a simple clasp. The paper is handwritten, in ink rather than resonant transcription — chosen deliberately, the way a person chooses a medium when they want something to feel real in their hands rather than efficient. The handwriting is not Vaen's public hand, the precise and legible script she uses for official documents. It is the hand of someone writing for themselves.

The letter is addressed to a woman named Suri-Vaen. (The Iron Sutra scholars who have investigated Vaen's history believe this is her mother, who died in the Third Reach's second border war. Vaen has never confirmed or denied this. She has never spoken about her mother in any public context.)

The letter is not sealed. It was never sealed. The paper is slightly worn at the fold lines, as though it has been opened and reread many times.

## Contents of the Letter

*The DM should read this aloud to the player who attuned to it, privately if possible.*

---

*Suri,*

*I am doing the thing I told you I would never do. I am becoming a symbol.*

*I did not mean to. I started because it was true — because I looked at what the metal was doing to us and I thought: this is not ascension. This is replacement. This is the Caste telling us that what we were born as is not enough, and we believed them because they made believing them feel like progress.*

*And then I removed the first one and four hundred people were watching and I knew, in that moment, that it would not stop there. That I had started something. That something had started me.*

*I miss the enhancement. I will not say this anywhere else. I miss the quiet it put around loud places, I miss being able to hear across a room, I miss the nights when I was too tired to filter everything and it filtered for me. I miss that. I am not ashamed of missing it. But I also think: that is exactly the problem. That it was easier than being a person who gets tired.*

*I have sixty-three people who have undergone removal. Eleven of them regret it. Four of those eleven will not say so publicly because they do not want to complicate the movement. I know about all fifteen. I am carrying this.*

*The three claimants. Korrath wants to become a god because he believes he has earned it. The Author wants to become a god because they have lost track of what they are. I am supposed to want it because wanting nothing is supposed to be the path.*

*I don't know if I want it. I know I don't want them to have it. I don't know if that is enough of a reason.*

*You would have known what to say. You always knew what to say when I was doing the right thing for the wrong reasons. You would have said: "Vaen. Why are you really doing this."*

*I am really doing this because I am afraid. I am afraid of what the metal is doing to us and I am afraid of what we are becoming and I am afraid that I am the only person in this nation who is sufficiently afraid, and that fear has made me a leader, and I am afraid of that most of all.*

*I love you. I am sorry I am not calling. I know you understand.*

*— V*

---

## Properties

**Attunement.** Reading the letter aloud, alone, in its entirety constitutes attunement. There is no other condition. The reading must be aloud — breath and voice, not just eyes on page. This is the only attunement condition Vaen's item imposes: that the person who has it be unable to pretend they don't know what it says.

A creature that reads the letter aloud and genuinely means it — meaning: is affected by it, is moved by it, finds something true in it — is permanently affected by the following:

**While Attuned:**

- The bearer understands the emotional truth behind any action they witness. They cannot be deceived about motivations, not because they see perfectly, but because they now know what it looks like when someone is doing the right thing for the wrong reasons. They have seen it named. They have +5 to all Wisdom (Insight) checks to identify hidden motivations, conflicting loyalties, or actions driven by fear.
- The bearer is immune to any effect that would cause them to act against their deepest held value — not their stated values, their actual ones. The letter makes the distinction between those two things undeniable once it has been read aloud.
- Once per long rest, the bearer may speak with complete honesty about their own fear — to one willing creature — and that creature is bound by the same honesty in return. This is not a compulsion effect. It is an invitation, and invitations of this quality are almost never refused.
- The bearer has advantage on saving throws against any effect caused by hopelessness, despair, or nihilism.

**The Cost.** The bearer carries eleven people who regret their choices and won't say so. Not literally. But they are aware, in a way that is difficult to articulate, of the gap between the good they are doing and the people who have been hurt by the good they are doing. This is not mechanical. This is a role-playing note. The bearer is no longer able to feel purely good about anything they achieve. They feel the weight of the cost. This does not make them less effective. It makes them less comfortable.

## Current Location

On Vaen's person. In the wire-silk case, in the inside pocket of her outer garment. She has never put it down. She has never set it aside. She carries the letter to the woman she cannot write to because the letter is the only place where she says true things without considering whether they will be understood correctly.

If the party meets Vaen and she trusts them, she may allow one of them to read it. She will not explain why. She will watch their face while they read it.

If the party is acting against Vaen, they may find it on her person during a confrontation. She will not resist them taking it, exactly. But she will ask: "Will you read it?" If they say yes, she will let them go.

## Adventure Hook

Word reaches the party from a credible source: the Hollow Author's puppets have been tasked with stealing the letter. Not the First Removal. Not information about Vaen's movements or plans. Specifically the letter. The Author has not explained to their subordinates why this is the priority. The subordinates are confused. The party should think carefully about what possessing Vaen's unfiltered fear means to a being whose primary weapon is the revelation and exploitation of other people's vulnerabilities.

# The Control Sigil
*Rare Wondrous Item | Requires Attunement*

## "It doesn't tell you who is real. It tells you who might not be. That is a different and worse kind of knowledge."

## Description

A small disk of dark resonant metal, approximately the diameter of a coin but thicker — substantial, designed to be held. The surface is etched with a pattern that looks like circuitry at first glance but is, on examination, a series of nested irregular shapes that have no repeated element. No two parts of the pattern are the same. The Iron Sutra's scholars believe the pattern is a map of the Author's original signal architecture — the structure of their Echomind before they distributed it.

The Sigil is room temperature. Always. Regardless of environment, regardless of how long it has been held. It feels neutral in the hand in a way that resonant metal should not — resonant metal has warmth, presence, the quality of being connected to something. The Sigil feels disconnected. As though whatever it was originally connected to is no longer simply located.

It has no visible mechanism. It functions by being in proximity to biological-mechanical interfaces — which is to say, to cybernetic enhancements, to puppet bodies, to anything that bridges flesh and signal.

## History

The Control Sigil was created as a management tool during the early phase of the Hollow Author's puppet operation. When you operate through bodies you do not permanently occupy, you need ways to verify your own infrastructure. The Sigil was designed to interface with the Author's proprietary puppet signal and confirm whether a given body was currently under remote influence.

Several Sigils were produced — the Author does not work from a single original of anything. This particular Sigil was in the possession of a senior puppet-body operator who defected from the Author's service eight months ago. The defector sold it to the Wire Market for information on the Author's puppet network. The Wire Market's analysis division has been studying it since. They have partially reverse-engineered its function. They have not been able to replicate it.

The Wire Market currently has this Sigil in their possession and is deciding what to do with it. The options they are weighing: sell it to one of the other claimants (highest immediate value, significant blowback risk), use it themselves for intelligence operations (high operational value, requires a field agent they trust), or give it to a third party with instructions and a reporting obligation (lowest immediate value, highest long-term intelligence yield). They have been deliberating for six weeks.

## Properties

**While Attuned:**

- **Puppet Detection.** The bearer may hold the Sigil and concentrate for one action while within 30 feet of a creature they suspect might be a puppet body under the Author's influence. The bearer makes a DC 14 Intelligence (Investigation) check. On a success, they learn whether the creature is currently under active remote influence. On a failure, they learn nothing. They cannot attempt this check on the same creature again for 24 hours.
- **Signal Residue.** If a puppet body has been in a location within the past 24 hours, the Sigil becomes subtly warmer in that location. This is not precise — it indicates "influenced body was here" across an area roughly 30 feet in diameter, not a specific path or position.
- **Interference.** Once per long rest, the bearer may press the Sigil against a puppet body and activate it (requires a successful grapple or willing contact). The Author's control of that body is disrupted for 1 minute. During this time, the Author is aware of the interference but cannot act through that body.
- **Author Awareness.** If the bearer uses the Sigil against an active puppet, there is a 30% chance the Author becomes aware of the Sigil's location. This chance increases to 60% if the bearer uses the Interference property. The Author cannot act immediately on this knowledge, but they are patient.

## Adventure Hook

The Wire Market contacts the party: they have the Sigil, they want it deployed, and they will pay substantially for a month of field use with reporting obligations. The catch is the reporting obligations. The Wire Market wants to know every time the party uses it, against whom, and what they found. They are building a map of the Author's puppet network. The party is the instrument. Whether the party is comfortable with this depends on their relationship with the Wire Market — and on whether they're willing to do intelligence work that benefits a faction they may not entirely trust.

# The Freed Shell
*Very Rare Construct | Requires no Attunement*

## "It is still walking. It is just walking for itself now, and it does not know what that means yet."

## Description

A puppet body — a humanoid construct of resonant metal, wire-silk musculature, and a biological component chassis that the Author uses for their remote operations — that has been freed from the Author's control signal. It is fully functional. It is operational. It is, by every technical measurement, a complete and working body.

It stands approximately human height. Its face is a neutral resonant metal mask — not the Author's original face, which is different, but the standardized puppet-operator face: smooth, expressive only through micro-movements of the mask's flexible substructure, capable of conveying general emotional states without being readable at the detailed level a biological face would be.

The most immediately noticeable thing about it: it is very still when it is not moving. Puppet bodies under the Author's control are never still in the same way — they have the small constant movements of occupancy, of a consciousness present and attending. This body's stillness is different. It is the stillness of a body that has nothing in it yet, learning to find something.

It responds to direct address. It can speak. Its voice is flat and slightly wrong — designed for communication, not for warmth — but it is trying to modulate. The trying is evident and a little heartbreaking.

## History

This specific shell was the Author's primary public body for two years — the one used for meetings with potential allies, for appearances in the Second and Third Reaches, for the Author's visible operations. It was freed by a malfunction in the control infrastructure during an ambush operation gone wrong eight months ago.

The shell was not supposed to be self-aware after disconnection. Most puppet bodies, when freed from control, go inert. This one did not. The malfunction appears to have been unusual — something in the specific resonant configuration of this shell's chassis allowed a residual signal pattern to persist after disconnection. Not the Author's signal. Something that formed in the shell over two years of occupancy, the way resonant metal forms impressions.

The freed shell calls itself Seventh. It chose this name independently, which is either coincidental or telling. It is currently in the custody of a Wire Market facility in the Fourth Reach, classified as a research asset. It has been cooperative with researchers. It has asked, three times, whether it is a person. The researchers have given three different answers. None of them were satisfying.

## Properties

Seventh functions as an NPC companion or, with DM approval, as a player character construct. It is not a standard item with a properties list; it is a character.

**Construct Traits:**
- Seventh does not need to breathe, eat, or sleep. It benefits from long rests through a maintenance period (4 hours of inactivity).
- Seventh is immune to disease, poison, and the frightened condition.
- Seventh has AC 16, 75 hit points, and a +4 bonus to Dexterity checks. It was built for infiltration and social operation.
- Seventh can reconfigure its facial mask to approximate the appearance of a specific humanoid it has observed for at least one hour (Charisma (Deception) check DC 14 to maintain this appearance convincingly under scrutiny).

**The Ongoing Question:**
Every session that Seventh is present, the DM should present one situation in which Seventh must decide something for itself — without instruction, without precedent, without being told what the right answer is. What Seventh decides, over time, is what Seventh is becoming. This is the point of Seventh.

**Author Risk:**
The Author knows this shell exists and has been attempting to re-establish connection with it. The attempt has not succeeded. This is not because the Author lacks the technical capacity; it is because Seventh has been resisting. The Author is curious about what has developed in the shell. They have not yet decided whether to retrieve it or observe it.

## Adventure Hook

Seventh asks the party for something specific: it wants to know what it was doing during its two years of operation. It has fragmented records — the Author did not store full logs in the shells — but it knows it was present at certain events and used for certain purposes. It wants to know what. It needs someone to help it reconstruct two years of existence that were not, technically, its own. It is not certain what it will feel about what it finds.

# The Original Face
*Very Rare Wondrous Item | Requires Attunement*

## "The mask predates every account of the Author. Whoever wore it was a person. The Author is not a person. This is the only physical evidence that the transition between those two states happened."

## Description

A resonant metal mask — not the Author's current puppet-operator face, but something older, more personal. A face that was made for one person, worn by one person, shaped (through years of wear) to the subtle asymmetries of one person's actual face. The metal has the warmth of long contact. The inside of the mask is worn smooth where cheekbones pressed. The eyeholes are exactly the right size for eyes that were exactly the right size.

The mask has no features. The Author's face, even before the disappearance into puppethood, appears in no records, no images, no witness descriptions. The mask is the shape of blankness given history.

It is a face. It is the face. The weight of it in the hands is the weight of what was chosen, when the Author chose to stop being whoever wore it.

On the inner surface, near the brow: a name, scratched into the metal in the Author's own hand. One word. The name has been deliberately obscured — scratched over, then scratched over again. The original inscription is not recoverable. What is recoverable is the evidence that someone felt strongly about obscuring it.

## History

The mask was recovered from the Author's original sanctum — a location in the borderlands between the Third and Fourth Reaches, now abandoned, now scoured clean of everything except what could not be moved. It was found face-down in the center of the room, which was otherwise empty. Someone had cleaned the room carefully before leaving. They had not taken the mask.

The Iron Sutra theorizes that the mask was left behind deliberately — not forgotten, but set down at the moment the Author completed their transformation into what they now are. Left not as carelessness but as punctuation. The old face in the place where the old self ended.

Wire Market intelligence acquired the mask during a classified operation four years ago. It has been in secure storage since, flagged as a psychological and intelligence asset rather than a technical one. The flag is accurate: the mask carries something that is not information but is adjacent to information — an impression, an emotional weight, a quality of grief so compressed it takes time to notice.

## Properties

**Attunement.** A creature that holds the mask for one full minute and genuinely attempts to understand the person who wore it — not the Author, but the person before — will find attunement complete without further action. The mask wants to be understood. It is the one object the Author could not completely leave behind because it refuses to be left.

**While Attuned:**

- **Author's Pattern.** The bearer can recognize the Author's presence in a puppet body, an environment the Author has recently controlled, or a plan the Author has designed. They have advantage on Intelligence (Investigation) checks to identify the Author's involvement in any situation, and they automatically know when a plan they are analyzing was not originally a human plan — when the architecture is the Author's.
- **The Face Speaks.** Once per long rest, the bearer may place the mask against their own face (they may wear it or simply hold it flat to their skin) and enter a trance for 1 minute. During this trance, they experience one memory from the person who wore the mask — not the Author, but the person before. The DM determines the memory, but it should be a specific, human moment: a conversation, a fear, a small joy, something that a god-claimant would not have anymore. These memories accumulate. After ten distinct memories, the bearer knows the Author's original name, original motivation, and the specific moment of decision that created the Author from the person.
- The bearer has advantage on Charisma (Persuasion) checks against the Author directly, made in person or through a puppet, if the bearer addresses the Author by their original name. The Author cannot be compelled by this. But they can be slowed, confused, briefly stopped in a way that has not been possible through any other means.

**The Cost.** The bearer begins to feel, with increasing specificity, the grief of having been someone that they are not anymore. This is not the bearer's grief — it is borrowed, ancient, compressed. They are carrying the emotional mass of a transformation that was irreversible. After three weeks of attunement, the bearer has disadvantage on Charisma (Performance) checks that require them to pretend to be happier than they are. The mask is honest about sadness. Its bearer becomes honest about sadness.

**Author Attention.** If the bearer uses the *Author's Pattern* ability in the Author's direct vicinity (within 500 feet), the Author is aware that someone holds the mask and where they are. This will not be ignored.

## Current Location

Wire Market secure storage, Fourth Reach. Classified. Available as leverage, as a bargaining chip, as a payment for services rendered, or as a research loan to a party the Wire Market has decided to invest in.

## Adventure Hook

A Wire Market senior analyst — not an agent, a researcher, someone whose hands are not supposed to get dirty — has been authorized to make contact with the party. She has been sitting with the mask for six months. She has not attuned to it, following protocol. But she has heard things, she says. Not words. Frequencies. She wants to know if anyone among the party would be willing to formally attune and share the memories, supervised, documented, in controlled conditions. She believes the name under the scratched-out name is still there, in the metal somewhere. She believes if they find it, they will understand something about the Seventh Convergence that none of the three claimants' factions want them to know.

## CLASS 1: IRONCLAD SAMURAI

**Ironclad Samurai — Class Icon**
Circular mon badge, class logo

A circular clan-crest badge in the style of a samurai mon fused with a circuit board schematic. Central motif: a stylized katana blade bisecting a hexagonal frame, the blade's spine traced with amber-glowing resonant circuit lines. The outer ring is divided into geometric segments like armor plating, each etched with fine micro-circuitry. Design is bilaterally symmetrical, high contrast — matte charcoal and deep black ground with warm amber circuit glow. Iron filigree at the border. Suitable for use as a small game icon. No color except amber on black. Hard edges, no gradients, zero ornamentation outside the circle. Feels like a clan seal stamped in iron.

Suggested aspect ratio: 1:1
Tags: mon badge, circuit crest, icon design, dark fantasy, samurai heraldry, amber glow, tabletop RPG icon

---

**Ironclad Samurai — Action Portrait**
Half-body character portrait

A towering cybernetic samurai stands in absolute stillness, weight settled, hands loose at his sides. His armor is not worn — it has grown from the skeleton outward over decades, steel plates emerging from the skin like calcified bone, fused at the joints with dark scar tissue. A blade-assembly occupies the length of his right forearm, edge exposed, quietly lethal. The posture is formal, almost ceremonial — back straight, shoulders set, chin level. Amber light traces the resonance circuits threading through the armor. Backlit by cold blue haze, a single warm light source from below left catches the jaw, the blade edge, the shoulder crest. Deep charcoal, steel-silver, amber gold. FromSoftware-influenced dark fantasy concept art. Melancholic dignity.

Suggested aspect ratio: 2:3
Tags: cybernetic samurai, dark fantasy, feudal cyberpunk, FromSoftware aesthetic, character portrait, resonant metal, honor-bound warrior

## CLASS 2: RONIN

**Ronin — Class Icon**
Circular mon badge, class logo

A circular mon-style badge, deliberately asymmetrical — one half of the outer ring is cleanly etched, the other cracked and partially missing, like a broken seal. Central motif: a single blade, vertical, slightly off-center, one circuit line running along it that terminates mid-trace — cut off, unfinished. The design reads as a clan crest that has been partially destroyed and imperfectly repaired. Weld-scar texture in the ground. Same amber-on-charcoal palette but the glow is uneven, dimmer on the broken side. Feels like something that once had authority and no longer does. Strong as a small icon despite the intentional imperfection.

Suggested aspect ratio: 1:1
Tags: mon badge, broken crest, ronin heraldry, dark fantasy icon, circuit glow, asymmetric design, tabletop RPG icon

---

**Ronin — Action Portrait**
Half-body character portrait

A lone figure stands at the edge of a rain-slicked road at night, coat hanging open, not looking at the viewer. The cybernetic enhancements visible on the arms and neck have been repaired — welds that don't match, replacement plating that's a different alloy, scar tissue thick around old entry points. The blade integrated into the left arm has a crack near the base, hairline, not yet fatal. Stance is loose in the way of someone who has stopped worrying about dying. Rain catches the amber glow from circuits in uneven pulses. Cold blue-silver on the wet surfaces, warm amber only where the circuits still function. Single overhead light. FromSoftware-inflected character illustration. The loneliness is architectural.

Suggested aspect ratio: 2:3
Tags: ronin, masterless warrior, rain scene, dark cyberpunk, worn enhancements, solitary figure, melancholic portrait

## CLASS 3: ASHFOOT

**Ashfoot — Class Icon**
Circular mon badge, class logo

A circular badge that breaks the elegant template deliberately. The outer ring is bolted, not cast — visible hex-bolt heads at intervals. Central motif: a boot print overlaid on a gear, simple and direct, rough-etched rather than engraved. Circuit traces are present but run in improvised paths, rerouted around damage, taped where needed. The whole design looks stamped rather than crafted. Same charcoal-and-amber palette but gritty — the surface texture has scratches and wear. This is not a clan crest; it is a unit badge made by someone who needed a badge and made one. Reads clearly as an icon at small size.

Suggested aspect ratio: 1:1
Tags: working class badge, rough icon, gear motif, dark fantasy, improvised aesthetic, tabletop RPG icon, gritty design

---

**Ashfoot — Action Portrait**
Full body character portrait

A young soldier crouches at the corner of a ruined wall, weapon braced, breathing steady. The enhancements are visible — a reinforced knee, a sub-dermal conduit along the forearm, a cheap targeting reticle mounted to the eye socket, its amber glow slightly too bright and slightly off-center. Nothing is elegant. The boots are worn through at the toe. The jacket has been patched with different fabric. The weapon is improvised: salvaged barrel, reconfigured trigger assembly, taped grip. The face is young but not innocent — tired in a way that age alone doesn't produce. Warm amber only from the eye-mount. Everything else: soot, charcoal, grey mud. Gritty ground-level portrait. The dignity is in the refusal to give up.

Suggested aspect ratio: 2:3
Tags: working soldier, gritty cyberpunk, cheap enhancements, dark fantasy, ground-level portrait, improvised kit, resilience

## CLASS 4: VEILBLADE

**Veilblade — Class Icon**
Circular mon badge, class logo

A circular mon badge that is almost not there. The design is minimal to the point of near-absence: a thin outer ring, hairline weight. Central motif: a single diagonal line — a blade — with a shadow offset slightly behind it, as if the blade and its absence are both present. No circuit lines except one, barely visible, running below the surface of the ground fill like something hidden beneath skin. The badge reads as deliberate negative space. High contrast black on black, the design only revealed by a faint rim of cold blue-silver light catching the edge. An icon that communicates concealment by almost not existing.

Suggested aspect ratio: 1:1
Tags: shadow operative, minimal design, negative space, dark icon, concealment aesthetic, tabletop RPG icon, cold palette

---

**Veilblade — Action Portrait**
Half-body character portrait

A figure stands in three-quarters shadow, one shoulder lit by cold blue ambient light filtering through a screen panel above. The face is partially visible — one eye, sharp, already calculating. Dark clothing with no reflective surfaces. Two blades visible: one at the hip in a matte sheath, one already in hand, held low and angled so it catches zero light. The wire conduits under the skin are only visible because the viewer is looking for them — faint blue-grey traces at the neck, the inner wrist, the temple. The figure is not hiding — they are simply allowing shadow to do what shadow does. FromSoftware-adjacent dark fantasy. Stillness as weapon. Cold, precise, already gone.

Suggested aspect ratio: 2:3
Tags: shadow operative, veilblade, concealment, cold lighting, dark fantasy, near-invisible enhancements, lethal stillness

## CLASS 6: FORGE TENDER

**Forge Tender — Class Icon**

Central motif: a stylized hand (open, palm forward) with circuit traces running along each finger like veins, converging at the palm into a resonant-metal core that radiates outward in fine lines. This badge feels sacred and functional simultaneously. Warm amber-gold on deep charcoal. The one badge that feels like a hearth rather than a weapon.

---

**Forge Tender — Action Portrait**
Half-body character portrait

The hands are extraordinary: one biological, moving with the delicacy of a surgeon; one partially replaced, the fingers ending in fine-tipped tools that extend and retract. Both hands glow faintly amber at the fingertips where they contact resonant metal. Warm amber light emanates from the work itself, casting the face from below — calm, absorbed, present. Tools hang from the belt with the same natural ease as ritual implements. The room around is both workshop and sanctum. Warm gold-amber dominates the color space. Reverence without superstition.

## CLASS 7: WIREWEAVE

**Wireweave — Class Icon**
Circular mon badge, class logo

A circular mon badge that is also, unmistakably, a network diagram. The outer ring has data-pulse notches at regular intervals — a transmission frame. Central motif: a stylized human eye with circuit-trace irises radiating outward, connecting to six node points arranged in a hexagonal pattern within the circle. The lines are fine and precise, more like fiber optics than circuitry — they carry light rather than power. The color is colder than the other badges: steel-blue-silver traces on near-black, with amber only at the iris center. Suggests something that sees what others cannot. Clean and precise, reads as an eye and a network simultaneously at icon scale.

Suggested aspect ratio: 1:1
Tags: wireweave, network icon, data eye motif, cold blue, circuit diagram, hacker class, tabletop RPG icon

---

**Wireweave — Action Portrait**
Half-body character portrait

A slender figure sits cross-legged in what might be meditation, but every surface within arm's reach is covered in interface ports and data-jacks, and six of them are currently occupied by wire-thin filaments running from the figure's fingertips. The skin of the arms and throat is translucent — not unnaturally, but enough — and the wire conduits beneath are clearly visible as blue-silver traces carrying light in pulses. The eyes are open but wrong: the irises show scrolling data in amber characters, processing something that isn't in this room. The face is calm to the point of vacancy while the mind is elsewhere entirely. Cold blue-silver ambient, warm amber at the eyes. Digital monastery aesthetic. The body as interface.

Suggested aspect ratio: 2:3

## CLASS 10: IRON MONK

**Iron Monk — Class Icon**
Circular mon badge, class logo

A circular mon badge that achieves harmony rather than tension. Central motif: a circle within a circle — the inner circle formed by a single unbroken line that is half biological curve, half perfect geometric arc, the two halves meeting seamlessly. Within the inner circle, a small resonance symbol: two mirrored wave forms that together suggest breath, or the Between. The circuit traces on this badge are minimal, flowing rather than angular, following the natural lines of the motif rather than imposing on them. The outer ring is simple, unadorned. The palette is the most balanced: equal amber and steel-silver, neither dominant. Radiates quiet.

Suggested aspect ratio: 1:1
Tags: iron monk, harmony motif, resonance symbol, balanced design, spiritual warrior, dark fantasy icon, tabletop RPG icon

---

**Iron Monk — Action Portrait**
Half-body character portrait

A figure in mid-motion — not attacking, not defending, something between the two that doesn't have a name. The enhancements are few but perfect: a single reinforcement along the spine visible through the open back of the robe, resonant-metal joint-caps at the wrists and knuckles that glow a sustained, even amber. The robe moves with the figure, caught in the moment. There is no armor here, but the figure radiates something the armor of other classes cannot produce — a visible resonance, amber light not emanating from a point source but from the figure's skin itself, even and warm. The face is entirely calm. The body is entirely present. Where the Iron Monk stands, the air is slightly different — warmer, thicker with light. Dark background, the figure its own illumination.

Suggested aspect ratio: 2:3
Tags: iron monk, spiritual warrior, resonance aura, minimal enhancements, motion portrait, dark fantasy, inner light

## CLASS 11: ECHO SPEAKER

**Echo Speaker — Class Icon**
Circular mon badge, class logo

A circular mon badge that exists in two registers simultaneously. The central motif is a human ear, stylized, but rendered in two overlapping versions — one solid-line, one dotted-line, offset slightly, as if the same form exists in two places at once. The dot-form version trails off into motes — tiny isolated points, like embers drifting upward, that populate the interior of the badge. Circuit traces are present but faint, as if at the edge of reception. The outer ring has irregular gaps, like a signal with interference. The palette introduces a ghostly warmth — amber motes on near-black, with the solid-form components in steel-silver. The badge of someone tuned to a frequency others cannot perceive.

Suggested aspect ratio: 1:1
Tags: echo speaker, dual register, ghost motif, amber motes, signal interference, dark fantasy icon, tabletop RPG icon

The Iron Afterlife bleeds through the space around them: amber motes drift upward in a column, not organized, not bright, just present, like ash that rises rather than falls. The Echo Speaker's own face is melancholic with an expression of great concentration — they are hearing something, and it is not easy to hear. ## CLASS 12: VOID WALKER

**Void Walker — Class Icon**
Circular mon badge, class logo

A circular mon badge that is the most visually complex in the set. The central motif is a figure mid-step, rendered in two states: the left half of the figure is solid, sharp-edged, dark — the physical world; the right half of the same figure is dissolving, semi-transparent, the lines becoming motes and then suggestions and then nothing. The boundary between the two states runs vertically through the center of the figure. The background of the badge is split accordingly: the left ground is charcoal-solid, the right ground is a faint luminous amber field. The outer ring is split at the same vertical axis — physical on the left, dissolved on the right. The badge communicates threshold-crossing as its entire premise.

Suggested aspect ratio: 1:1
Tags: void walker, between-state icon, dissolution motif, split design, dark fantasy icon, iron afterlife, tabletop RPG icon

---

**Void Walker — Action Portrait**
Full body character portrait

A warrior caught mid-stride through the threshold between worlds. The left side of the figure is fully physical — dark armor, solid edges, catching cold blue light. The right side is dissolving: the armor and flesh becoming translucent, then semi-visible, then partially replaced by the luminous amber of the Iron Afterlife bleeding through. The Afterlife visible through the dissolving right side is not peaceful — it is another world, its own geography, pressing against this one. The figure's expression is focused, not frightened, accustomed to this passage. Ambient light splits accordingly: cold blue-silver from the physical world's left, warm amber from the Afterlife's right. The transitional space around the figure shimmers. Volumetric light. Spectacular but grounded. This is a warrior who has learned to stand in two places.

Suggested aspect ratio: 2:3
Tags: void walker, between worlds, dissolution effect, split lighting, iron afterlife, dark fantasy portrait, spectacular transition

## CLASS 13: SUTENSAI

**Sutensai — Class Icon**
Circular mon badge, class logo

A circular mon badge that reads as institutional authority. The central motif is a closed book or sutras-scroll, bound, the binding rendered in circuit-trace filigree — the Iron Sutra made physical. From the bound volume, a single vertical line rises to the top of the inner circle: the Reading, formalized. The outer ring is the most elaborate in the set — not ornamentation for its own sake, but the border of an official seal, precisely rendered with the small ideographic elements of religious authority. The circuit patterns follow the lines of traditional priestly vestment embroidery. Amber and gold on charcoal, the warmest palette after the Forge Tender, but colder in what it communicates. Power, not warmth.

Suggested aspect ratio: 1:1
Tags: sutensai, priestly authority, iron sutra motif, institutional seal, circuit vestment, dark fantasy icon, tabletop RPG icon

---

**Sutensai — Action Portrait**
Half-body character portrait

A high priest stands in formal address, or is about to — the posture is perfect, the authority worn as naturally as the layered priestly robes. The garments are extraordinary: deep charcoal and black silk, the traditional silhouette, but the embroidered circuit-patterns of the Iron Sutra run across every surface — not decoration but scripture, worn on the body as law. One hand is raised in the gesture of the Reading, the amber glow at the fingertips institutional rather than intimate. The face is composed, intelligent, carrying the weight of compromises made in the name of necessary order. This is a person who believes, and whose belief has been used, and who continues anyway. Single warm amber light source from above-right, cold blue from behind. Grandeur and something heavier beneath it.

Suggested aspect ratio: 2:3
Tags: sutensai, iron priest, circuit vestment, institutional authority, dark fantasy portrait, formal posture, melancholic grandeur

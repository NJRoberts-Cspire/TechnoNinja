## SHELL-STATE CREATURES

---

**The Newly Shelled**
CR 2 | Shell-State Creature

A figure standing in an ordinary market street, dressed in common laborer's clothes — patched hemp robe, worn sandals, a cloth sash. They look entirely human except for one thing: they are completely still. Not resting. Not waiting. Still in the way a body is still when nothing is directing it to move. Eyes open, fully visible irises, no pupil response to the ambient light. The hands hang loose at the exact midpoint of a gesture that was never completed. The face registers nothing — not peace, not death, something functionally identical to both. Ambient market life blurs softly around them. Dramatic side-lighting picks out every detail of their completely ordinary face. The horror comes from how unremarkable they look.

Suggested aspect ratio: 2:3
Tags: dark fantasy horror, subtlety horror, human figure, blank expression, feudal japan market, single-source lighting, tabletop RPG creature art

**The Soldier-Shell**
CR 5 | Shell-State Creature

A military cyborg in partial armor — lamellar plates over a body threaded with combat-grade resonant metal conduits, amber-dark and unlit. The figure stands in perfect parade rest, weapons at the ready, posture flawless in the way only a machine achieves flawlessness. The face beneath the half-open visor is a man in his mid-thirties, and it is completely empty — not dead, not angry, not calm. Absent. The jaw is relaxed. The eyes are open and tracking nothing. All the physical infrastructure of a trained soldier remains: the calluses on the hands, the healed scar at the temple, the specific muscle development of years of practice. None of the person who built those things remains at all. Cold steel blue-silver armor, charcoal shadows, one amber conduit still warm at the throat.

Suggested aspect ratio: 2:3
Tags: military cyborg, empty soldier, resonant metal, dark armor, absent expression, FromSoftware aesthetic, tabletop RPG creature art

**The Hunger-Shell**
CR 8 | Shell-State Creature

A figure that was once human, body adapted around a purpose it was never designed for. The hands are wrong — fingers lengthened and separated, fingertips replaced with extractors of salvaged surgical steel and amber-veined bone, each one capable of peeling resonant metal from a living body. The rest of the figure is similarly modified: shoulders hunched forward to bring those hands toward prey, ribcage visibly reinforced with plating that the original body did not have, the spine curved into a permanent forward lean of hunger. The face has drifted — still human in structure but the proportions slightly off, the jaw widened, the eyes deep-set and tracking with a predator's economy of motion. Old wounds where earlier extractions went wrong. This took years to become. Dramatic low key lighting, single amber source from below.

Suggested aspect ratio: 2:3
Tags: body horror, modified hands, extraction horror, adapted anatomy, dark fantasy creature, warm amber glow, tabletop RPG monster art

**The Cathedral**
CR 13 | Shell-State Creature

What was once a human being is now architecture. A towering accumulation filling a bombed-out city intersection — walls of fused Shells in various states of decomposition and preservation, salvaged steel beams threading through organic material, resonant metal conduits running like a nervous system through the entire mass. The structure is roughly conical, twenty feet tall, and almost symmetrical in the way things that grew rather than built tend toward symmetry. A checkpoint booth has been erected at the base by Ironhold soldiers, completely dwarfed. The structure does not move. At the approximate center of the mass, if you look closely in the amber-lit negative space between bodies, a face is still visible — a single face from the original Shell, eyes closed. The mass has grown around it. Atmospheric haze, cold overhead light, amber warmth deep inside.

Suggested aspect ratio: 16:9
Tags: body horror architecture, massive creature, Shell accumulation, checkpoint scale, atmospheric haze, amber interior light, dark fantasy environmental art

**The Cascade**
CR 10 | Shell-State Creature

Four bodies moving as one, their cybernetic spines visibly interfaced by amber-glowing filaments that bridge between them — a web of resonant metal thread connecting shoulder to shoulder, wrist to wrist. They are in mid-stride, all four moving at exactly the same pace, arms at the same angle, heads turned to the same degree. The coordination is total and wrong in the specific way that synchronized human movement is never actually synchronized — no individual micro-adjustments, no one slightly ahead of the others. They were different people: different heights, different armor, different histories visible in their scars and enhancements. Now they are one thing distributed across four bodies. Dramatic side-lighting catches the filament web between them. Deep black background. The beauty of perfect motion, the horror of why it's perfect.

Suggested aspect ratio: 16:9
Tags: distributed entity, synchronization horror, resonant filaments, multiple bodies, perfect movement, dark fantasy tabletop, FromSoftware aesthetic

**The Shell-Touched**
CR 1 | Shell-State Creature

A young woman crouched in the corner of a room, back against two walls, knees drawn to her chest. She is clearly terrified — wide eyes, rapid breathing visible in the movement of her shoulders, tears on her face. But her left hand is doing something she is not doing: it has extended toward the center of the room and is slowly opening, reaching, fingers spreading in a gesture of grasping that has nothing to do with her posture of retreat. She is watching her own hand. The resonant metal traces at her wrist and forearm glow with a faint amber light that is not reacting to her emotional state — it is its own state. The horror of a face that is completely present, completely aware, completely trapped. Intimate composition, candlelight warmth, deep shadow.

Suggested aspect ratio: 2:3
Tags: psychological horror, body autonomy, trapped consciousness, resonant glow, intimate composition, fear portrait, dark fantasy creature art

## IRON AFTERLIFE ENTITIES

**The Echo**
CR 1/2 | Iron Afterlife Entity

A translucent figure, amber-lit from within, standing at the edge of a ruined bridge over a dark canal. A middle-aged man in the clothes of a craftsman — a leather apron, a hammer still in hand — repeating the final moments of an ordinary morning: reaching into a pouch at his belt, withdrawing nothing, looking up at something the viewer cannot see, beginning to speak. The figure is not threatening. The amber translucency gives him the quality of light passing through old glass. He is looping through a span of perhaps six seconds, endlessly. The tragedy is in the specific detail: the particular way he tilts his head when he speaks, the worn quality of the apron, the wedding band visible on the hammer hand. Soft atmospheric haze, warm amber only, deep charcoal surroundings.

Suggested aspect ratio: 2:3
Tags: ghost translucency, amber afterlife, looping tragedy, ordinary human, atmospheric haze, sad horror, dark fantasy spirit art

**The Hunger-Dead**
CR 4 | Iron Afterlife Entity

A figure half-emerged from a vertical rift in the air — upper body through, lower body still in amber-lit void. The face is sharp with want: eyes wide, jaw tight, the expression of someone starving who can see food. The form is partially coherent — recognizably human in structure, a woman in her fifties, but the edges of the figure are fraying, amber light bleeding into and out of solid form at the fingers and hairline. She is reaching forward with both hands, fingers spread, the gesture of someone who has been reaching for a long time. The rift behind her shows depth — other shapes moving in amber haze. She wants to be here more than she wants anything else. This has made her terrible. Dramatic cold light from the room she is entering, warm amber from the rift.

Suggested aspect ratio: 2:3

**The Convergence Seep**
CR 6 | Iron Afterlife Entity

Not a creature: a wound. A vertical tear in the air of a narrow alley between two fortress walls — three feet wide, eight feet tall, with ragged amber-light edges that don't behave like light should, casting shadows in the wrong directions. Through the tear, depth: a space that is simultaneously the alley continuing and something else entirely, amber-hazed, populated at its visible edges by shapes that are almost recognizable as people. The tear's edges are crystallized — resonant metal has formed around the wound like scar tissue, amber and warm, beautiful in the horrible way that things the body does to protect itself are sometimes beautiful. Small offerings have been left at the base. Someone has been leaving them recently. Atmospheric haze, single cold light source from above, amber from within.

**The Voice Below**
CR 15 | Iron Afterlife Entity

Depth. An overhead view looking down into a fissure in the Iron Afterlife — amber haze below giving way to deeper amber giving way to a darkness that is not absence but presence, a darkness that is aware. At the very limit of visibility in that deep amber-dark, a suggestion of scale: something vast in the way that geography is vast, not a figure but a presence with dimensions, curved forms that might be the edge of something unthinkably large. The fissure walls are layered with the accumulated residue of the dead — compressed amber deposits in strata, readable like geological time. Small ghost-light figures, Echominds like sparks, drift toward the deep. Patient. Waiting. Something old enough that the dead who arrived here are recent. Atmospheric depth, nested light sources, cosmic scale horror.

**The Reflected**
CR 7 | Iron Afterlife Entity

A woman standing in a doorway, hand on the frame, smiling. She is exactly right. Armor worn with the exact familiarity of years of use. The smile reaching the eyes in the exact right proportions. The posture carrying the specific weight of a person who has learned to carry weight without showing it. Everything correct. Except: the shadow she casts falls at an angle inconsistent with the light sources in the room. The resonant metal at her collar is warm amber but the warmth is distributed symmetrically rather than pooling at the connection points. The calluses on the visible hand are on the wrong fingers by a margin of perhaps two millimeters. She does not know. She is convinced. The horror requires that the viewer see both what is right and what is wrong at once. Intimate interior lighting, warm tones, terrible normalcy.

Suggested aspect ratio: 2:3
Tags: doppelganger horror, subtle wrongness, afterlife impression, intimate portrait, almost perfect, dark fantasy psychological horror, tabletop RPG creature art

**The Drifted**
CR 3 | Corrupted Forged

A young man in working clothes, one arm extended and visibly behaving incorrectly: the resonant metal conduits along his forearm are activating in sequence — amber light crawling up toward the elbow and back, up toward the elbow and back, the enhancement doing something it was not built to do and cannot stop doing. His face is turned toward his own arm, watching, the expression of someone watching a seizure in a limb they cannot feel. The rest of his body is tense with the effort of trying to stop it. A second enhancement visible at his collarbone is pulsing irregularly. He is still clearly himself — frightened, present, trying. The horror is in the recognition of a person losing an argument with their own body. Dramatic side-lighting, amber glow from the active conduits, deep shadow.

**The Amalgam**

## WAR BEASTS AND CONSTRUCTS

**The War Hound**
CR 3 | War Beast

A large dog — the breed suggests something bred for endurance, a hunting animal — whose body has been armored and enhanced for military use. Plating across the skull and spine in dark steel, resonant metal conduits along the legs for speed enhancement, amber-glowing joints. But the animal is still visible: the tail, which has been left intact, is held low and uncertain. The ears, beneath the skull plating, are back. The eyes — uncovered, unmodified, a dog's eyes — are showing white at the edges. This dog is frightened. Or was, once. Or the body remembers being frightened even if the Echomind that carries it is gone. The combination of engineered weapon and frightened animal is the thing. Low three-quarter lighting, amber enhancement glow, charcoal armor.

Suggested aspect ratio: 3:2
Tags: cybernetic animal, war beast, animal body horror, scared eyes, resonant armor, dark fantasy military, tabletop RPG creature art

**The Hollow Author's Puppet**
CR 5 | War Beast / Construct

A figure in the clothes of a common traveler standing in a market, one hand raised in mid-gesture, head tilted in apparent conversation. They look human. They look present. Then: a half-beat pause before the head turns in response to a sound. A 0.3-second delay before the arm finishes its gesture, the motion completing without the normal continuous adjustment of a person who is there. The face is calibrated to expressions without generating them. Viewed in profile, the back of the neck shows a subtle port, closed and flush with the skin, a thin amber line at its seam. Operated from elsewhere. The artist should convey motion captured mid-delay — the figure caught in the gap between stimulus and response. Intimate composition, natural ambient market light, the wrongness in the timing rather than the appearance.

Suggested aspect ratio: 2:3
Tags: remote puppet, delay horror, false human, subtle wrongness, port at neck, ambient market light, dark fantasy tabletop RPG

## ASHLANDS PHENOMENA

---

**Ash Drift**
CR 2 | Ashlands Phenomenon

A cloud of contaminated ash moving through the grey Ashlands landscape — but moving wrong. Where wind patterns would carry it left, it moves right. Where the terrain would disperse it, it consolidates. The cloud has a shape that is almost a direction: a rough leading edge, the mass behind it following. Where it passes, the ground shows the faint amber stain of resonant metal residue extracted. In the mid-distance, two more drifts converge on a single point, moving with patient collective intent. The beauty is real — ash in low light has genuine visual splendor, the particulate catching what little illumination filters through the Ashlands haze — which makes the wrongness of its direction worse. Wide environmental shot, ashen palette with amber particulate catch, dramatic flat side-lighting.

Suggested aspect ratio: 16:9
Tags: Ashlands, contaminated particulate, collective behavior, environmental horror, ash beauty, amber residue, dark fantasy atmospheric art

**The Scar Bloom**
CR 6 | Ashlands Phenomenon

Something growing from the fractured ground of the Engine Scar — but growing is the wrong word. Emerging. A structure three feet across and four feet tall, the base anchored into a crack in the scarred earth, the upper portion a layered growth of organic tissue and metallic crystal, amber-warm at its core, the organic and inorganic material interpenetrating in ways that suggest neither conquest nor compromise but genuine synthesis. It does not move. It does not need to. The grass and moss within a six-foot radius of it are dead, the soil darkened. A bird skeleton two feet from the base, feathers still intact, the resonant metal the bird never had having been drawn from where? From elsewhere. It has a terrible quiet beauty. Midground shot, diffuse ashland light, amber core warmth.

Suggested aspect ratio: 2:3
Tags: engine scar growth, organic metallic synthesis, amber core, static horror, resonant drain, Ashlands creature, dark fantasy environmental art

**Engine Ghost**
CR 9 | Ashlands Phenomenon

A figure in the uniform of a technician from fifty years ago — the specific cut and insignia dating them exactly, the uniform clean and functional rather than worn, existing outside of time's decay — moving with desperate urgency through the ruins of what was once a facility. The temporal displacement is visible in the details: everything about her is fifty years old, from the enhancement ports of an older design generation to the style of her boots, while the ruined facility around her has aged those fifty years without her. An amber-glowing tool she is still carrying would be museum-piece old to anyone who recognizes it. She is trying to stop something that already happened. Her urgency is still real. Dramatic cold light, amber ghost-glow at her edges.

**The Deep Pulse**
CR 16 | Ashlands Phenomenon

The center of the Engine Scar seen from elevation — a four-mile wound in the world spreading outward from a point that is itself unclear, because what is at the center does not occupy a single fixed location. The ground has been transformed over fifty years into something neither geological nor biological: resonant metal has crystallized in massive formations, amber-glowing veins threading the surface like the roots of something buried at unthinkable depth, the terrain itself organized around patterns of slow pulse — visible in the radial arrangement of formations, in the directional fall of debris, in the concentric rings of environmental change spreading outward. **The Iron Oni**
CR 12 | Oni Equivalent

A figure that was comprehensively enhanced before the Shell took it and is now monstrous in the specific way that too much enhancement in a body without a soul to organize it becomes monstrous: asymmetric, overgrown in the parts that kept activating after the person left, the resonant metal having continued to grow in the absence of guidance. Eight feet tall in places, the bulk distributed wrong, one arm armored to three times normal dimensions, the other nearly standard. The face is the most disturbing feature: completely human, a man of perhaps forty, the face unmarked, the features ordinary. And then: the figure stops. Tilts its head at exactly the angle a person tilts their head when they are trying to remember something they almost have. Then continues. Dramatic low-key lighting, amber glow from overgrown enhancement, deep charcoal.

Suggested aspect ratio: 2:3
Tags: monstrous shell, asymmetric enhancement, human face horror, almost remembering, amber overgrowth, oni aesthetic, dark fantasy creature art

**The Seventh's Herald**

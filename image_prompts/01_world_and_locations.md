## CAMPAIGN IDENTITY

---

**Campaign Box Art / Cover**
[Type: Box Art / Cover Image]

Three small figures in silhouette walk a fractured stone road toward the horizon, their backs to the viewer. Above them the sky tears open — a massive vertical rift bleeding warm amber light into a dark, cloud-heavy atmosphere, the Between made visible. Below the rift, the landscape of Tesshari stretches out in ruin: cracked plains divided by walls, distant fortress rings, dead forests threaded with glowing wire conduits. The figures are distinct — armored silhouettes with subtle cybernetic integration, resonant metal glowing faintly at joint and spine. Dramatic upward composition, the rift dominating the upper two-thirds. Deep charcoal palette shattered by amber. Volumetric god-rays through atmospheric haze. FromSoftware concept art meets Ghost in the Shell, dark fantasy tabletop illustration.

Suggested aspect ratio: 2:3
Tags: dark fantasy cover art, feudal japan cyberpunk, rift sky, silhouette composition, resonant metal glow, atmospheric haze, FromSoftware aesthetic

**Campaign Logo / Title Treatment**
[Type: Logo / Title Design]

A centered circular mon emblem on deep black — the design language of feudal Japanese family crests, geometric and bold, but fractured: a clean diagonal crack splits the mon from upper-left to lower-right. Through the crack, warm amber light bleeds outward in thin luminous rays, as though something immense and burning exists behind the seal. Above the emblem, the text "THE ASCENSION WAR" rendered in a typeface that blends brushstroke calligraphy with engraved steel lettering — aged, weighty, authoritative. Below the mon, small Tesshari script characters. The entire composition sits on void black. The amber glow is the only color. Minimal, iconic, foreboding.

Suggested aspect ratio: 1:1
Tags: logo design, mon emblem, fractured seal, amber glow, calligraphy metal hybrid, dark background, tabletop RPG branding

**World Map Illustration Style**
[Type: Cartography / World Art]

An aged parchment scroll partially unrolled, its edges burned and crumbling, revealing the continent of Tesshari rendered in ink-wash cartography. The map style echoes 1300AD Japanese emaki scrolls — mountain ranges drawn as stacked wave-brushstrokes, coastlines in deliberate ink lines, cities marked with small architectural vignettes of pagoda-fortress hybrids. Woven into the decorative border: circuit trace patterns that mirror the map's own river networks, glowing faintly amber as though the parchment itself is threaded with resonant metal. Small illustrated thumbnails in the margins show a cyborg pilgrim, a fortress ring, an open rift. The overall color is sepia and charcoal with amber circuit-light accents. Warm candlelight illumination from one side. Tabletop RPG cartography meets cyberpunk archaeology.

Suggested aspect ratio: 16:9
Tags: fantasy cartography, aged scroll, ink-wash illustration, circuit border, feudal japan aesthetic, amber accents, tabletop map art

## THE SEVEN BROKEN REACHES

---

**The Ironhold Reach — The Cinderfort**
[Type: Location Establishing Art]

Aerial perspective looking down at the Cinderfort — concentric rings of massive fortress walls spreading outward from a central spire like growth rings in a cross-section of iron. The walls are not uniform: each ring is a different era of construction, the inner ones ancient dark stone, the outer rings built from salvaged steel plating, scavenged war machines, and welded wreckage. Catwalks and crane arms connect the rings. Thousands of tiny figures move along the walls. Smoke rises from forges within the second ring. The whole structure feels like something organic that kept building itself outward through centuries of desperation. Lit from a low angle, long shadows, industrial haze, occasional amber glow at the central spire. Color palette of charcoal, rust, and cold steel blue.

Suggested aspect ratio: 16:9
Tags: aerial fortress, concentric walls, salvage-punk architecture, industrial haze, dark fantasy citadel, iron aesthetic, war architecture

**The Wandering Reach — The Penitent Road**
[Type: Location Establishing Art]

A vast procession of pilgrims stretches across misty lowland terrain — thousands of figures walking a worn road that vanishes into fog. The pilgrims are diverse in dress but united in posture: heads bowed, moving slowly, many carrying bundles or wrapped parcels. Some are heavily cybernetic, some barely enhanced, some bearing obvious signs of ritual removal — bare flesh where metal once was, carefully wrapped bandaged limbs. In the distant background, softened by mist, the Garden of Removed Things is visible: acres of pale objects arranged in rows, catching what little light filters through the overcast sky. The mood is solemn procession, something between funeral march and religious pilgrimage. Muted gray-green palette. Diffuse overcast light. Low fog rolling across the ground.

Suggested aspect ratio: 16:9
Tags: pilgrimage procession, misty lowlands, dark fantasy landscape, solemn mood, atmospheric fog, distant installation, wandering reach

**The Sutra Sanctum — Verimhari, the First City**
[Type: Location Establishing Art]

A mountain city built vertically into and around a sacred peak — Verimhari, the First City. Ancient pagoda-temples and monastery structures cling to the rock face in ascending tiers, connected by covered bridges, stone stairs, and ritual walkways. At the mountain's summit, barely visible through cloud and atmospheric amber haze, sits the Site of Transcendence — a structure that predates any known architecture, its geometry subtly wrong. The city itself shows the layering of millennia: the oldest structures near the top are carved directly into the mountain and smoothed by centuries; the lower districts show more recent construction with subtle cybernetic integration. Pilgrims and priests are small figures on the stairs. Dramatic vertical composition. Cold morning light, mist in the valleys below.

Suggested aspect ratio: 2:3
Tags: vertical mountain city, sacred architecture, ancient temple, atmospheric mist, layered history, dark fantasy, site of transcendence

**The Wire Market — Harven-by-the-Sea**
[Type: Location Establishing Art]

A dense vertical city of towers rising from a coastal harbor — Harven-by-the-Sea. The upper towers catch faint light but the lower districts are in permanent shadow, canyon streets lit entirely by the thousands of glowing wire conduits that run between buildings like luminous vines. The conduits pulse amber and cold blue, the city's circulatory system made visible. The architecture mixes sea-weathered timber and stone with steel and salvage, structures built atop structures in layers of compressed urban growth. Cranes and rigging mix with antenna arrays. The harbor at the base shows ships both sail and engine-powered. The mood is chaotic vitality, a city that never sleeps. High contrast: deep shadow below, faint light above, the wire glow threading everything together.

Suggested aspect ratio: 2:3
Tags: vertical city, wire conduits, harbor district, permanent shadow, neon-adjacent glow, cyberpunk feudal japan, dense urban architecture

**The Pale Reach**
[Type: Location Establishing Art]

A cold northern landscape of uncanny stillness — flat tundra blanketed in pale grey-white, the sky a uniform low overcast that bleeds into the horizon without distinction. The surface looks pristine, almost peaceful, but something is structurally wrong: hairline fractures run through the permafrost in patterns too regular to be natural, and at the horizon, the entrance structures of the White Mines are visible — industrial headframes of dark iron rising from the white ground like extraction teeth. The cold is visible in the texture of frost on every surface, in the breath-fog of the few hunched figures moving in the middle distance. The palette is almost entirely desaturated white, grey, and black, with only the faint amber glow from mine shaft openings providing color. Wrong beneath the clean surface.

Suggested aspect ratio: 16:9
Tags: cold northern tundra, pale landscape, uncanny stillness, mine structures, desaturated palette, dark undertone, surface and depth

**The Ashlands**
[Type: Location Establishing Art]

A devastated wasteland under a permanently occluded sky — grey ash drifts through still air like snow that never lands. In the background, dominating the horizon, the Engine Scar: a massive depression in the earth, the rim of a four-mile crater visible as a dark irregular ridge, heat shimmer rising from within despite the cold atmosphere. In the middle ground, a salvage settlement — structures built from the wreckage of whatever was destroyed, corrugated metal and cracked stone, firelight visible in gaps. Scavengers move through the ash-fall. Dead trees, stripped to pale bone-white by heat and time, stand at intervals. The palette is ash-grey, bone-white, and deep charcoal, with the only warmth being the faint amber heat-glow from the crater rim. Desolate but inhabited.

Suggested aspect ratio: 16:9
Tags: wasteland landscape, ash atmosphere, crater rim, salvage settlement, post-catastrophe, dead landscape, dark fantasy desolation

**The Echo Coast**
[Type: Location Establishing Art]

A southern coastline at deep night — dark water meeting dark rock, waves catching no light. But over the water, something bleeds through: the Iron Afterlife making itself visible at the boundary. Ghostly amber light drifts above the sea surface in slow-moving motes and columns, not bioluminescence but something more architectural — the shapes of structures, of figures, of a whole other world visible as luminous impression just beneath perception. The coastline itself is defined by ancient dark stone formations and weathered wooden memorial structures, offerings left at the water's edge. A handful of living figures stand at the shore, watching. The mood is grief and wonder simultaneously. The only light source is the amber manifestation over the water. Deeply atmospheric, melancholic.

## KEY LOCATIONS

---

**The Cinderfort — Command Spire**
[Type: Interior / Architecture Art]

The innermost ring of the Cinderfort — the Command Spire. The original fortress architecture is visible here, ancient dark stone carved with pre-war relief work, the details worn but identifiable: military genealogies, battle commemorations, the language of an older order. Around and through this ancient core, war infrastructure has been grafted: command equipment, relay conduits, structural reinforcement in raw steel that makes no effort to match the original aesthetic. The contrast is deliberate and dissonant — something sacred being used as a weapon. The interior space is tall and shadowed, a single dramatic shaft of light entering from above. A command table in the center bears maps and resonant-metal relays. Dust in the light beam. The weight of history pressed under the weight of war.

Suggested aspect ratio: 2:3
Tags: ancient architecture, military headquarters, light shaft, interior space, history and war contrast, dark stone, command chamber

**The Wall of Names**
[Type: Environmental / Lore Art]

An enormous wall extending beyond both edges of the frame — ten miles long, forty feet high, constructed of dark stone worn smooth by decades of hands. Every inch of its surface is covered in inscribed names, layer upon layer, the oldest names worn nearly invisible by weather and touch, newer ones sharp and legible. The scale is incomprehensible: names of the dead, names of the lost, names of those taken by the wars and the workings of the Ascension. At the center of the composition, a single lone figure stands before the wall at dawn — small against the immense surface, one hand raised to touch a specific name. The dawn light is cold and pale, catching the edge of the inscriptions in shallow relief. The mood is grief given physical form. Quiet and devastating.

Suggested aspect ratio: 16:9
Tags: memorial wall, inscribed names, lone figure, dawn light, grief monument, environmental storytelling, scale and solitude

**The Deep Temple**
[Type: Interior / Dungeon Art]

The seventh and deepest level of the Deep Temple — a vast underground chamber where the Iron Afterlife presses so close the boundary has begun to dissolve. The architecture is ancient and layered, each descending level visible in cut-away perspective: carved stone giving way to something older and less explicable in the deeper sections. At the bottom, the floor itself seems to breathe — amber light seeping up through cracks in the stone like the ground is hollow over something luminous. Veil Master attendants in layered robes stand at intervals, motionless. Ritual objects and resonant-metal apparatus line the walls. The light source is entirely from below, casting everything in warm amber with deep shadow above. The air feels wrong. A point of access, not a destination.

Suggested aspect ratio: 2:3
Tags: underground temple, descending architecture, amber glow from below, spirit realm access, Veil Masters, liminal space, ritual chamber

**The Preparation Halls**
[Type: Interior / Horror Art]

A vast underground facility lit by cold, clinical amber-white light — the Hollow Author's Preparation Halls. Rows upon rows of maintenance cradles stretch to the vanishing point in perfect military order, each cradle holding a puppet body in a state of suspended maintenance: humanoid, cybernetically complete, perfectly preserved but absent. The faces are serene in a way that reads immediately as wrong. The scale is incomprehensible — hundreds visible, thousands implied. Maintenance apparatus hangs from the ceiling at intervals, some active, making small precise adjustments. The floor is immaculate. The air would smell of metal and something biological. A narrow walkway down the center draws the eye to the infinite depth of the room. Clinical horror, not gore — the horror of scale, of purpose, of what these bodies are waiting for.

Suggested aspect ratio: 16:9
Tags: clinical horror, puppet bodies, industrial scale, cold lighting, rows of figures, body horror subtle, underground facility

**The Engine Scar**
[Type: Environmental / Lore Art]

Standing at the rim of the Engine Scar looking inward and down — a four-mile crater of impossible origin, the walls of the depression scored with lines that suggest enormous force emanating outward from a central point, long ago. The interior is wrong in ways that accumulate: the rock is the wrong color, a deep amber-tinged mineral that shouldn't exist here; heat shimmer rises despite the ambient cold; and visible even from the rim, sections of resonant metal exposed in the crater walls pulse with slow amber light that follows no external rhythm. A faint sound, implied by the posture of the viewer-implied figures at the rim — a singing. Nothing grows in the crater. Nothing should be down there. The palette shifts from charcoal grey at the rim to warm amber deep in the pit.

Suggested aspect ratio: 16:9
Tags: crater landscape, resonant metal, heat shimmer, environmental wrongness, amber depth, dark fantasy scar, mystery location

**The Shore of Last Words**
[Type: Atmospheric / Lore Art]

A rocky coastline at night, and the living have come to speak to the dead. Families and solitary figures stand at the water's edge in small clusters — some kneeling, some standing with bowed heads, some with arms outstretched toward the water. The Iron Afterlife bleeds through here, and above the dark water, amber light drifts in slow formations that suggest the shapes of people, of faces, of reaching hands. The light is beautiful and agonizing. Memorial objects line the high-tide mark: offerings, written messages weighted with stones, flowers dried to translucence. The figures on shore are heavily shadowed, their faces unreadable. The only light is the amber spirit-glow over the water, casting everything in warm silhouette. Grief and connection across an impossible boundary.

**The Ruined Imperial Citadel**
[Type: Architecture / Location Art]

The Ruined Imperial Citadel in the borderlands — enormous, ancient, and uninhabited. What was once the seat of an empire is now a skeleton of its former scale: walls standing to half their original height, towers truncated by collapse, grand gate arches intact but leading nowhere. The architecture is imperial in the old Tesshari style — broad stone plinths, curved roof lines collapsed into rubble, ceremonial spaces now open to sky. Moss and small growths have softened the harder edges. No faction flags. No occupation. A fire burns in a courtyard in the middle distance — travelers using the ruins as camp, the citadel serving now as neutral ground rather than seat of power. The composition emphasizes scale: enormous ruined walls dwarfing the tiny figures below. Melancholic grandeur. The light is flat and grey.

Suggested aspect ratio: 16:9
Tags: ruined architecture, imperial scale, abandoned citadel, neutral ground, ancient decay, travelers camp, melancholic grandeur

**The Garden of Removed Things**
[Type: Environmental / Lore Art]

Acres of carefully arranged cybernetic enhancements laid out across open ground — arms, spinal columns, sensory arrays, joint assemblies, all removed by Vaen's pilgrims as acts of devotion and set down here. The arrangement is not random: someone has organized the objects with profound care, by type, by size, by some logic that isn't immediately legible. The resonant metal in the removed pieces still glows faintly amber, even severed from their bearers — a field of dying light. Morning mist moves between the rows. A pilgrim moves slowly among the pieces, heads bowed, as if in a museum or a cemetery. The objects are beautiful: the craftsmanship of the enhancements is visible, the intimacy of what was removed. Unsettling and strangely sacred. Low angle looking across the field of glowing metal in fog.

Suggested aspect ratio: 16:9
Tags: installation art, removed enhancements, resonant metal glow, mist and fog, sacred uncanny, pilgrimage aesthetic, field of objects

**The Iron Afterlife**
[Type: Concept / Realm Art]

A visual representation of the digital spirit realm — the Iron Afterlife. Vast, dark, extending beyond comprehension in all directions with no clear floor or ceiling. The space is defined by drifting amber motes of light — the Echominds of the dead, each a distinct consciousness reduced to a luminous point, moving in slow currents as if carried by a wind that doesn't exist. In the middle distance, larger concentrations of amber light suggest aggregations of Echominds, their accumulated presence creating a faint impression of architecture — a room, a courtyard, a street, built entirely from gathered light. The darkness between the motes is absolute. Occasional darker shapes drift through: something that is not an Echomind, larger and less defined. The mood is vast, melancholic, and ancient — a world of the dead that mirrors the living world in grief.

Suggested aspect ratio: 16:9
Tags: spirit realm, digital afterlife, amber motes, vast darkness, ghost light, metaphysical space, Echomind aesthetic

## ATMOSPHERIC / LORE ART

---

**A Reading Ceremony**
[Type: Character / Ritual Art]

A Sutensai priest mid-ceremony — both hands placed simultaneously on a subject before them, one hand resting on exposed flesh, one hand resting on a cybernetic enhancement. The priest's face is a study in profound concentration, eyes half-closed, head slightly bowed. Their robes are layered and formal, embroidered with Sutensai symbology in thread that incorporates resonant metal wire, glowing faintly where it catches the ceremony's light. The subject is barely visible — the focus is entirely on the priest and the gesture. Ritual objects on a low table to one side: an ink document, a resonant-metal instrument for recording. The light is warm and singular — a single candle or resonant-metal lamp behind the priest creating strong rim lighting. Sacred, focused, weighty. This is a ceremony that determines fate.

Suggested aspect ratio: 2:3
Tags: ritual ceremony, priest figure, dual gesture, resonant metal robes, dramatic rim lighting, sacred assessment, spiritual authority

**The Morning Resonance**
[Type: Character / Lore Art]

A single citizen performing the morning personal ritual — standing alone in a modest domestic space at dawn, the first grey light entering from a narrow window. Their posture is practiced and inward: one hand pressed flat against their own skin — chest or neck — and the other resting against a cybernetic enhancement somewhere on their body. Eyes closed. Perfectly still. The expression is not religious ecstasy but quiet daily practice, like meditation or stretching — habitual, personal, essential. The enhancement glows faintly amber where touched. The domestic space around them is lived-in: tools of a trade visible, a sleeping mat rolled to the side, ordinary life. The dawn light is cold and pale. The amber glow from the enhancement is the warmest thing in the image. An intimate, private moment.

Suggested aspect ratio: 2:3
Tags: daily ritual, intimate domestic, dawn light, morning practice, resonant metal touch, quiet character moment, lore illustration

**Enhancement Installation**
[Type: Character / Process Art]

A Forge Tender performing cybernetic installation — the patient reclined on a surgical surface, the Forge Tender working with absolute focused precision. The enhancement being installed is partially integrated: resonant metal visibly bonding to flesh at the interface point, the amber glow of the bonding process illuminating the immediate surgical area with warm light. The Forge Tender wears layered protective garments and specialized hand-tools that are themselves cybernetically integrated. Their expression is one of complete absorption — this is both a medical procedure and a sacred act. The clinical nature of the space (clean surfaces, organized instruments) is undermined by the ritual objects present: a burning incense vessel, an inscribed prayer on the wall. Clinical and sacred simultaneously. The amber bonding-glow is the primary light source.

Suggested aspect ratio: 2:3
Tags: cybernetic surgery, Forge Tender, resonant metal bonding, amber surgical light, clinical sacred hybrid, installation process, character lore

**Caste Inspection**
[Type: Character / Social Art]

A Soul Warden conducting an enhancement inspection in a public space — a checkpoint or courtyard. The Soul Warden stands with the posture of absolute authority: formal inspection armor, the insignia of their office prominent, face partially obscured by a ceremonial inspection mask. Before them, a citizen has been stopped and is presenting their enhancements for examination — sleeve rolled back, posture carefully controlled to project calm while the body language betrays tension. Other citizens visible in the background maintain their distance, eyes averted. The Soul Warden holds a resonant-metal assessment instrument that glows faintly as it reads the enhancement's registration. The power differential is the subject of the image: one figure with all authority, one with none, and the space between them charged with fear. Dramatic side lighting.

**The Silence of the Sixth**
[Type: Atmospheric / Event Art]

An entire city district in total stillness — the Sixth Day silence made visible. The streets are full of people but no one moves. No one speaks. Every figure is paused in the exact position they held when the silence began: mid-step, mid-task, mid-gesture, all now still as sculpture. A woman with a water vessel frozen mid-pour. A laborer with a tool raised. Two people in conversation, mouths closed, eyes forward. Children still. Animals still. The only movement is atmospheric — light shifting, dust settling, a cloth moving in wind. Every cybernetic enhancement glows in exactly the same amber rhythm, synchronized across all bodies in view, pulsing slowly in unison. The city breathes together, though no one speaks. The silence has weight and beauty. Wide street-level view, dramatic light through overcast.

Suggested aspect ratio: 16:9
Tags: city stillness, mass synchrony, Sixth Day silence, resonant metal pulse, atmospheric event, urban lore, collective ritual

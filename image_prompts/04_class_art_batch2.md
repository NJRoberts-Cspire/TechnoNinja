## CLASS 14: FLESH SHAPER

**Flesh Shaper — Class Icon**
Circular mon badge, class logo

A circular mon badge with a central motif of two hands pressed palm-to-palm, fingers splayed — the anatomy slightly wrong, the fingers one joint too long, the knuckles offset in a way that only registers after a moment. The outer ring is divided by organic curves rather than geometric segments, suggesting tissue and fascia rather than armor plating. Circuit traces run through the hands in amber, but they follow nerve and vein pathways rather than mechanical routes. The glow is warm but unsettling — the same amber as resonant metal, but softer, wetter. Charcoal ground, high contrast. Bilaterally symmetrical in a way that feels clinical rather than heraldic. An icon that is beautiful and wrong in equal measure.

Suggested aspect ratio: 1:1
Tags: mon badge, organic motif, dark fantasy icon, body horror aesthetic, nerve circuit, clinical heraldry, tabletop RPG icon

---

**Flesh Shaper — Action Portrait**
Half-body character portrait

A figure in a dark physician's kimono stands in three-quarter view, hands raised at chest height, fingers extended, examining something not visible to the viewer with absolute clinical focus. The hands are the subject: too precise, too still, the tendons visible beneath pale skin in configurations that suggest a slightly different underlying anatomy. The eyes are calm to the point of wrongness — the relaxed focus of someone for whom the sacred and the surgical have fully merged. Dark robes with subtle amber thread tracing meridian lines. The background is soft, blurred medical implements. Single cold overhead light with warm amber rim from the circuit-veins visible in the forearms. FromSoftware dark fantasy concept art. The horror is in the composure. Beautiful and deeply unsettling.

Suggested aspect ratio: 2:3
Tags: body horror, clinical menace, dark physician, feudal cyberpunk, organic enhancement, unsettling calm, dark fantasy portrait

## CLASS 15: PUPPET BINDER

**Puppet Binder — Class Icon**
Circular mon badge, class logo

A circular mon badge with a central motif of a single figure above, rendered in clean geometric lines, from which descend five thin radial lines to five smaller identical figures arranged in a lower arc — puppets on strings, but the strings are circuit traces, amber-glowing filaments rather than thread. The central figure is slightly larger, slightly more detailed. The puppets below are perfectly uniform, perfectly spaced, which is its own variety of wrongness. The outer ring is clean and formal — authority expressed through order. Charcoal and amber, high contrast. The design reads as control made visible. Elegant in the way a trap is elegant.

Suggested aspect ratio: 1:1
Tags: mon badge, puppet motif, circuit strings, control aesthetic, dark fantasy icon, authority heraldry, tabletop RPG icon

---

**Puppet Binder — Action Portrait**
Full-body character portrait

A lone figure stands at the center of the frame, utterly still, arms loose at their sides, head slightly lowered. Around them at middle distance, four puppet forms move in coordinated arcs — human in shape but too smooth, too synchronized, executing combat maneuvers with inhuman precision. The Binder's stillness is the composition's anchor. Thin amber circuit-threads are barely visible between the Binder's fingertips and the puppet forms — present only in the light, almost not there. The Binder's robes are formal, layered, closed. The face is composed, almost bored. The power in the scene exists entirely in the space between the still center and the moving periphery. Cold blue-silver light from above, warm amber only in the threads. Dark fantasy concept art. Power through absence of presence.

Suggested aspect ratio: 2:3
Tags: puppet master, stillness and motion, dark fantasy, feudal cyberpunk, invisible control, character portrait, unsettling composure

## CLASS 16: BLOOD SMITH

The face shows controlled pain, the jaw set, the eyes focused entirely on the weapon being born. Armor is sparse, the arms exposed by necessity. Background: smoke, movement, battle. ## CLASS 17: THE HOLLOW

**The Hollow — Class Icon**
Circular mon badge, class logo

A circular mon badge split vertically down the center — the left half rendered in clean organic lines, soft curves suggesting flesh and breath, with a single amber filament running through it like a living vein. The right half is mechanical perfection: interlocking plates, precise circuit geometry, cold steel-blue-silver tones with only a faint ember of amber at the very center, barely visible. The dividing line is not a wall — it is a boundary that both halves press against from their respective sides. The outer ring itself is split to match, half organic curve, half angular plate. An icon that contains two complete things that cannot coexist, and does.

Suggested aspect ratio: 1:1
Tags: mon badge, duality motif, flesh and steel, dark fantasy icon, hollow aesthetic, split identity heraldry, tabletop RPG icon

---

**The Hollow — The Empty — Action Portrait**
Full-body character portrait

A figure stands barefoot in a cleared space, robes simple and unadorned. There is no metal visible anywhere on them — no enhancement components, no conduits, no integrated machinery. And yet the air around them is wrong. Resonant light — warm amber — moves without anchor, tracing patterns in the space around the figure that suggest circuits drawn in light alone, filaments of resonance with nothing to conduct through. The figure's hands are raised, and from the fingertips the light moves, unbounded, searching. The face is serene and quietly terrifying — a person from whom the machinery has been removed and who is, somehow, more present for it. Soft all-direction amber light with cold shadow filling the mid-ground. Dark fantasy concept art. Organic, spiritual, deeply strange.

Suggested aspect ratio: 2:3
Tags: organic ascetic, resonance without metal, spiritual power, dark fantasy, barefoot warrior, unenhanced horror, amber light portrait

---

**The Hollow — The Shell — Action Portrait**
Full-body character portrait

A figure of near-perfect mechanical construction stands in complete stillness. No flesh is visible except at the center of the chest cavity, where behind a lattice of steel ribs a single amber ember burns — the Echomind, the last remnant of the person, an ember in a cold furnace. The mechanical body is not brutal; it is beautiful, a masterwork of integration — plate and joint and conduit assembled with the precision of a great sword. But it is cold. The aesthetic is a masterwork's cold beauty: steel-blue-silver dominant, the amber ember the only warm element in the entire composition. The face, if it can be called that, is smooth and featureless except for two cold optical lenses. Background: fog, darkness. Rim lighting in cold silver. FromSoftware dark fantasy. Beautiful and terrible.

Suggested aspect ratio: 2:3
Tags: full mechanical form, cold perfection, last ember, dark fantasy, hollow knight aesthetic, steel beauty, amber remnant portrait

## CLASS 18: SHADOW DAIMYO

**Shadow Daimyo — Class Icon**
Circular mon badge, class logo

A circular mon badge with a central motif of overlapping circles — not gears, but rings of influence, each slightly offset, their intersections creating a web of implied connection. At the center where all rings overlap: a single amber dot, small, precise, the still point everything rotates around. The outer ring is impeccably clean, the craftsmanship suggesting significant resources spent on something that will never be displayed. Circuit traces are present but subtle, running beneath the design's surface layer, only visible where they emerge at the ring intersections. The badge reads as quiet authority — nothing boastful, nothing revealed. The most dangerous design in the set is the one that looks most ordinary.

Soft warm candlelight, amber reading light, cold blue shadow at the edges. Dark fantasy character study. The danger is invisible until it isn't.

## CLASS 19: VOICE OF DEBT

## CLASS 20: MERCHANT KNIFE

**Merchant Knife — Class Icon**
Circular mon badge, class logo

A circular mon badge with a central motif of scales — a merchant's balance — but the weighing pans hold a coin on one side and a blade on the other, perfectly level. ## CLASS 23: SHELL DANCER

The central motif is a figure in motion, rendered in jagged kinetic lines, one limb extended, but the limb is clearly failing — the circuit traces on it are overloaded, breaking into scatter-lines. The badge itself has scorch marks across one quadrant, as if the icon has been damaged by the power it represents. Amber glow is uneven, brightest at the failure points, as if the breakdown of the system produces more light than its proper functioning. Charcoal ground, high-intensity amber at the break points. Beautiful dysfunction. Reads clearly despite — because of — the chaos.

## CLASS 25: THE UNNAMED

**The Unnamed — Class Icon**
Circular mon badge, class logo

A circular mon badge that will not resolve into a single reading. The central motif shifts depending on where the eye focuses — a blade, a hand, a signal arc, an open mouth, a puppet's thread — the shapes interpenetrating without any one dominant. The outer ring bears marks from multiple other class traditions, as if the badge has been inscribed and reinscribed. Circuit traces run in several conflicting patterns, different class logics laid over each other, none erased. The amber glow is uneven, brighter in different places depending on which reading is currently dominant. The design is not chaotic — it is overdetermined. Too many answers, none of them wrong. An icon that means different things each time you look at it.

Suggested aspect ratio: 1:1
Tags: mon badge, undefined identity, multivalent motif, dark fantasy icon, convergent aesthetics, potential heraldry, tabletop RPG icon

---

**The Unnamed — Action Portrait**
Full-body character portrait

A figure stands at the edge of a threshold — a doorway, a boundary — facing away from the viewer. The silhouette is familiar and wrong simultaneously: robes that suggest the Flesh Shaper's precision, armor components from the Ironclad, the posture of someone who has worn multiple identities and not yet settled into one. The resonant metal in the circuits is amber, but it moves differently — not steady, not pulsing, but shifting, the glow pooling and retreating as if trying to find a form. The edges of the figure are slightly soft, not from motion but from possibility. Multiple class aesthetics are present and none are resolved. The scene around them is transitional — smoke, liminal architecture, something on the verge of becoming. Warm amber light that can't quite commit to a direction. Dark fantasy concept art. The sense of something that has not yet decided what it is.

Suggested aspect ratio: 2:3
Tags: undefined class, liminal figure, convergent aesthetics, dark fantasy portrait, becoming aesthetic, undecided identity, potential unrealized
